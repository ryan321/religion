### Lesson 1.1 -- What Jesus Left Behind

Have you ever built something really important and then had to leave it in someone else's hands? That's exactly what Jesus did. Before He returned to His Father in Heaven, He didn't just leave behind good ideas or nice stories. He left behind a **living church** -- an organized group of believers with real authority to act in God's name.

Think about it this way: Jesus didn't write a book and say, "Good luck figuring this out!" Instead, He called **twelve apostles** and gave them something incredibly important -- **priesthood authority**. That means they had the actual power and permission from God to lead His church, perform sacred **ordinances** like baptism, and receive **revelation** to guide the members.

You might recognize this pattern. In The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints today, we have apostles, priesthood authority, ordinances, and continuing revelation. That's not a coincidence! The Church Jesus established during His earthly ministry had all of these things first.

Jesus spent about three years teaching His apostles. He didn't just teach them lessons -- He **ordained** them and gave them the authority to carry on His work. In John 15:16, Jesus told them, "Ye have not chosen me, but I have chosen you, and ordained you." That word **ordained** is important. It means He officially gave them power and responsibility through the priesthood.

The apostles could baptize people, give the gift of the Holy Ghost by the **laying on of hands**, bless the sick, and organize congregations of believers. They could also receive revelation from God about how to lead the Church. Peter, as the senior apostle, held the **keys of the priesthood** -- the right to direct how that authority was used throughout the whole Church.

Jesus also established sacred ordinances. **Baptism by immersion** was the way people entered the Church. The **sacrament** (what some call communion or the Lord's Supper) helped members remember Jesus and renew their covenants. These weren't just nice traditions -- they were essential acts performed by those with proper authority.

So when Jesus ascended into heaven (Acts 1:9), He left behind something real and organized: apostles with priesthood authority, ordinances, and the promise of continuing revelation through the Holy Ghost. It was a church built to be guided by God -- not just by human opinions.

The big question going forward in history is: What happened to this church after Jesus left?
