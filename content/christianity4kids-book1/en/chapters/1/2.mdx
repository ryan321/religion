### Lesson 1.2 -- Day of Pentecost

Have you ever been to a meeting where something so powerful happened that everyone there was changed forever? That's what the **Day of Pentecost** was like for the early Church.

After Jesus ascended into heaven, the apostles stayed in Jerusalem, just as He told them to. About ten days later, during the Jewish festival of **Pentecost**, something amazing happened. Acts chapter 2 tells us that the apostles were gathered together when suddenly a sound like a rushing mighty wind filled the house. Then what looked like small flames of fire appeared above each of their heads. They were all filled with the **Holy Ghost** and began to speak in different languages so that people from many nations could understand them (Acts 2:1-4).

Now, Jerusalem was packed with Jewish visitors from all over the Roman Empire for the festival. When they heard these simple Galilean men speaking in their own languages, they were amazed. Some people made fun of the apostles and said they must be drunk. But **Peter**, the senior apostle, stood up boldly and said, "These men are not drunk!" (Acts 2:15). Then he preached one of the greatest sermons in history.

Peter testified that Jesus of Nazareth was the promised **Messiah**. He told the crowd that Jesus had been crucified, but that God raised Him from the dead. Peter and the other apostles were **witnesses** of these things. The Holy Ghost was now being poured out just as the prophets had foretold.

The people listening felt the Spirit powerfully. Their hearts were touched, and they cried out, "What shall we do?" (Acts 2:37). Peter answered clearly: "**Repent, and be baptized** every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost" (Acts 2:38).

Does that sound familiar? It should! That's the same message Latter-day Saint missionaries share today: faith, repentance, baptism, and receiving the Holy Ghost.

About **3,000 people** were baptized that very day (Acts 2:41). Think about that: three thousand! The Church of Jesus Christ went from a small group of apostles to a thriving community in a single day. The Holy Ghost had launched the Church into action.

Pentecost showed that God was with the apostles. They had authority, they had the Spirit, and they had a message the world needed to hear.
