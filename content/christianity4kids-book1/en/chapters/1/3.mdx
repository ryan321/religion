### Lesson 1.3 -- How the Early Church Worked

If you could travel back in time and visit the Church that Jesus established, what would you find? You might be surprised at how familiar it would feel.

The early Church wasn't just a group of people who believed in Jesus. It was an **organized church** with leaders, offices, ordinances, and meetings, much like what we have today in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

At the top were the **apostles**. They were the ones Jesus had called and ordained personally. They held the highest authority and made decisions for the whole Church. When disagreements came up or new questions arose, the apostles could seek **revelation** from God to find the answer. Peter, as the senior apostle, presided over the others.

But the apostles weren't the only leaders. Paul wrote in Ephesians 4:11 that Christ "gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers." The Church also had **bishops**, **elders**, **deacons**, and **teachers**, all organized to help care for the members and spread the gospel. Does that list of offices sound familiar? It should!

Early Christians didn't have big church buildings. They met in **homes**. Acts 2:46 says they gathered "from house to house" to worship, learn from the apostles, and break bread together. These small home gatherings were where members shared meals, prayed, and supported one another.

The ordinances of the early Church were clear and specific. **Baptism** was performed by **immersion**, meaning the person went completely under the water, just as Jesus was baptized by John the Baptist in the Jordan River (Matthew 3:16). The **sacrament** (or Lord's Supper) was shared regularly so members could remember Jesus's sacrifice and renew their promises to follow Him.

Another important ordinance was the **laying on of hands**. When someone was baptized, authorized priesthood holders would place their hands on the person's head to give them the **gift of the Holy Ghost** (Acts 8:17). This same method was used to ordain people to offices in the Church and to give blessings to the sick (James 5:14).

Everything in the early Church was done through proper **authority**. It wasn't enough to just believe; you needed someone with the priesthood to perform ordinances. This is an important point to remember as we study what happened later in history.
