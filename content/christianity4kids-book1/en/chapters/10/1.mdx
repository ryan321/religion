### Lesson 10.1 -- The Crusades

Have you ever heard of a war fought over a city? For almost two hundred years, European Christians launched military campaigns called the **Crusades** to try to capture the **Holy Land**, the region around Jerusalem where Jesus had lived and walked.

It started in **1096** when Pope Urban II called on Christians across Europe to march to the Middle East and take Jerusalem back from **Muslim** rulers. Thousands of knights, soldiers, and even ordinary people answered the call. Some were genuinely motivated by their **faith** and believed they were doing God's work by reclaiming sacred places. Others had very different reasons. Many went hoping to gain **land, wealth, and power**. Some younger sons of noble families knew they wouldn't inherit anything at home, so conquering territory in the East seemed like their best opportunity.

The First Crusade actually succeeded in capturing Jerusalem in **1099**. But what happened there was horrifying. The crusaders massacred thousands of Muslim and Jewish inhabitants of the city: men, women, and children. They claimed to be fighting for Christ, but their actions were as far from the teachings of Jesus as you can imagine.

Over the next two centuries, there were **several more Crusades**. Historians usually count at least seven or eight major ones. Some were aimed at the Holy Land, while others targeted different regions. The crusaders set up small Christian kingdoms in the Middle East, but they were constantly under threat. Muslim leaders like **Saladin** eventually recaptured Jerusalem in 1187, and by **1291** the last crusader stronghold fell.

The Crusades left behind a terrible **legacy of bitterness**. Muslims remembered the violence and cruelty of the crusaders for centuries. The wars also damaged relationships between Western and Eastern Christians. During the Fourth Crusade, crusaders actually attacked the Christian city of **Constantinople** instead of going to the Holy Land!

From a Latter-day Saint perspective, the Crusades show what happens when people claim to act for God but don't have His priesthood authority or His Spirit to guide them. Jesus taught us to love our enemies, not slaughter them. These wars remind us why a **restoration** of true authority and true gospel principles was so desperately needed.
