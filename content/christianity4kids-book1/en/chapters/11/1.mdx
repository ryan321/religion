### Lesson 11.1 -- People Who Questioned

Have you ever felt like something was wrong, even when everyone around you said it was fine? Throughout the medieval period, **courageous individuals** felt exactly that way about the Church. They couldn't fix everything (they didn't have the **priesthood authority** to restore what had been lost), but they recognized that something was seriously wrong, and they were brave enough to say so.

These questioners came from different countries and different centuries, but they shared common concerns. They saw Church leaders living in luxury while ordinary people struggled. They saw people being told to **pay money for forgiveness** instead of being taught to repent and come to Christ. They saw the Bible locked away in Latin, a language most people couldn't read. And they believed this wasn't what Jesus had intended.

One of the earliest groups to push back were the **Waldensians**, followers of a man named Peter Waldo in the 1100s. Waldo was a wealthy merchant in France who gave away everything he owned to follow Jesus, much like the apostles in the New Testament. He and his followers translated parts of the Bible into the language ordinary people spoke and went out **preaching in the streets**. The Church condemned them as heretics.

The **Cathars** in southern France also rejected Church authority and lived simple lives of devotion. The Church launched an actual military crusade against them, killing thousands. Other groups in England, Italy, and across Europe raised similar concerns at different times. Almost all of them were **persecuted, imprisoned, or killed**.

What these people wanted most was to get the **Bible into people's hands** and into their own language. They believed that if ordinary Christians could read the scriptures for themselves, they would see how far the Church had drifted from what Jesus actually taught. They also opposed **indulgences** and the idea that the Pope had unlimited authority over every Christian's soul.

These early questioners didn't restore the gospel. They couldn't, because that wasn't their role. But they **planted seeds**. Their courage and sacrifice kept alive the idea that something better was possible, that the Church as it existed wasn't the final word. From a Latter-day Saint perspective, we can see God's hand at work, stirring the hearts of honest seekers, preparing the world step by step for the day when the fullness of the gospel would be restored.
