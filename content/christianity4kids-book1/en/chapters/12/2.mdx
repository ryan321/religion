### Lesson 12.2 -- Luther's Key Ideas

Martin Luther didn't just complain about indulgences. He developed powerful ideas about what Christianity should actually look like. And here's something interesting: many of his ideas were actually **closer to the original Church** that Jesus established than what the Catholic Church was teaching at the time.

Luther's most important idea was about **salvation**. The Catholic Church taught that people were saved through a combination of faith and works, including things like buying indulgences, going on pilgrimages, and performing acts of penance. Luther studied the writings of the Apostle Paul and came to a different conclusion. He believed that **salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ**, not through paying money or performing rituals to earn God's favor. Paul had written in Romans 1:17, "The just shall live by faith," and Luther believed this meant that trusting in Christ's grace was the foundation of salvation.

Luther's second major idea was about **authority**. The Catholic Church taught that the Pope was the supreme authority on earth in matters of faith, and that whatever the Pope declared was as good as scripture. Luther rejected this. He declared that the **Bible alone** was the ultimate authority. He called this principle **"sola scriptura"**, a Latin phrase meaning "scripture alone." If a teaching couldn't be found in the Bible, Luther argued, Christians didn't have to follow it.

His third revolutionary idea was the **"priesthood of all believers."** The Catholic Church taught that ordinary people needed priests to serve as middlemen between them and God. Luther said no. **Every baptized Christian has direct access to God** through prayer and through reading the scriptures. You didn't need a priest to translate God's word for you or to stand between you and your Heavenly Father.

To put this idea into practice, Luther **translated the entire Bible into German** so ordinary German-speaking people could read it for themselves. This was an enormous project, and Luther's translation became one of the most influential books in the German language.

As Latter-day Saints, we can see that Luther was reaching back toward truths that had been lost. Faith in Christ, the importance of scripture, direct access to God: these are principles we believe in too. Luther didn't have the fullness of the gospel, but he was moving in the right direction. God was using him to **break open the doors** that had kept people from the truth for over a thousand years.
