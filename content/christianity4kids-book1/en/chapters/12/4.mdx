### Lesson 12.4 -- Protestantism Is Born

When Martin Luther nailed his 95 Theses to the church door, he hoped the Catholic Church would listen and fix its problems. Instead, the Church tried to **silence him**. And in trying to shut Luther down, Church leaders accidentally helped create the biggest split in the history of Western Christianity.

The Pope demanded that Luther take back everything he had written. Luther refused. In **1521**, he was called before a gathering of political and Church leaders called the **Diet of Worms** (that's "Worms" the German city, not the creatures!). Standing before the emperor and powerful officials, Luther reportedly declared, "Here I stand. I can do no other." He would not deny what he believed the Bible taught.

The Church **excommunicated** Luther, officially kicking him out. The emperor declared him an outlaw, meaning anyone could arrest or even kill him without punishment. But something unexpected happened: **German princes** who were fed up with the Pope's power and interference stepped in to **protect Luther**. A prince named Frederick the Wise had Luther secretly taken to a castle called the **Wartburg**, where Luther hid for months and spent his time translating the New Testament into German.

Luther's followers began organizing their own churches, separate from Rome. They became known as **Protestants**, a name that came from the word "protest." They were protesting against what they saw as the errors and corruption of the Catholic Church.

The movement spread with incredible speed. **Entire nations** began leaving the Catholic Church. Parts of Germany, Scandinavia, Switzerland, the Netherlands, and eventually England all broke away. New Protestant leaders emerged: **John Calvin** in Switzerland, **Huldrych Zwingli** in Zurich, and eventually **King Henry VIII** in England, who created the Church of England for reasons that were more political than religious.

What had started as one monk's list of complaints had **shattered the religious unity** of Western Europe. Christendom, which had been united under the Catholic Church for over a thousand years, was now split into Catholic and Protestant camps. This division would lead to wars, persecutions, and conflicts that lasted for centuries.

From a Latter-day Saint perspective, the Reformation was a necessary step. It broke the monopoly the Catholic Church held over Christianity and opened the door for **religious freedom** and **individual Bible study**. But Protestantism, despite its good intentions, still lacked priesthood authority. The Reformation prepared the way, but only the **Restoration** through Joseph Smith could bring back what had truly been lost.
