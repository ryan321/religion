### Lesson 13.4 -- What the Reformation Fixed and Didn't Fix

The Reformation was one of the most important events in Christian history. Brave men and women risked their lives to challenge corruption and bring people closer to the Bible. But from a Latter-day Saint perspective, we can see that the Reformation, as important as it was, could only go so far.

Let's start with what the Reformation **did fix**. First, it put the **Bible into people's hands**. Before the Reformation, most people couldn't read scripture for themselves. Thanks to the printing press and translators like William Tyndale and Martin Luther, ordinary families could own and study the word of God. This was a tremendous blessing.

Second, the Reformation **challenged corruption** in the Church. The selling of indulgences, the extreme wealth of church leaders, and the abuse of power were exposed and rejected. Reformers demanded that Christian leaders live honestly and serve their people instead of enriching themselves.

Third, the Reformation encouraged an **individual relationship with God**. Instead of relying entirely on priests to connect them with God, people learned they could pray, study scripture, and develop their own faith. This was a powerful and true principle.

But now let's look at what the Reformation **didn't fix**, and couldn't fix.

No reformer restored **priesthood authority**. Luther, Calvin, Zwingli, Knox: none of them claimed that God or angels had given them the priesthood. They were scholars and preachers, but they didn't have the authority that Christ had given to His apostles through the laying on of hands.

No reformer restored **temple ordinances**, the sacred ceremonies that bind families together forever and connect us to God through covenants. No reformer called **living apostles or prophets** to lead the Church through continuing revelation. No reformer taught the **true nature of God**: that the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are three separate beings. And no reformer understood the full **plan of salvation** as revealed through modern prophets.

The Reformation created **hundreds of new churches**, each interpreting the Bible differently. But none of them had the authority to act in God's name. More churches didn't solve the problem; it actually proved it. Without prophets and revelation, sincere people reading the same Bible came to very different conclusions.

So what was the Reformation? Latter-day Saints see it as a **necessary preparation**, not the Restoration itself. God used brave reformers to break the chains of the Great Apostasy and prepare the world for something much bigger. The Reformation cracked open the door. The Restoration would open it wide.
