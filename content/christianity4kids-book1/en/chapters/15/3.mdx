### Lesson 15.3 -- What Was Still Missing

By the early 1800s, Christianity had come a long way. The Bible had been translated into many languages. Millions of sincere believers worshipped God every Sunday. Beautiful churches dotted the landscape of Europe and America. Good people lived good lives, served their neighbors, and taught their children about Jesus Christ.

But from a Latter-day Saint perspective, after more than **1,800 years** since the death of Christ's original apostles, something essential was still missing. In fact, many things were missing -- things that no amount of Bible study, good intentions, or church-building could bring back.

**Priesthood authority** was gone. None of the reformers -- Luther, Calvin, Zwingli, Knox, or anyone else -- claimed that God or angels had appeared and given them authority to act in His name. They were preachers and scholars, not ordained servants holding the same power Christ gave to Peter, James, and John. Without priesthood authority, no ordinance -- no baptism, no confirmation, no ordination -- carried God's official approval.

**Living prophets** were absent. The original Church of Jesus Christ had apostles and prophets who received direct revelation from God. They could settle disputes, organize the Church, and guide members through changing times. For eighteen centuries, no one held that role. Churches were led by educated men, not by men called and authorized by God.

**Continuing revelation** had stopped. The heavens seemed closed. People had the Bible, but no new scripture, no new direction from God, no voice saying "this is the way -- walk in it." Every question about doctrine had to be answered by human interpretation of ancient texts, which is why hundreds of churches existed, each with different answers.

**Temple ordinances** were completely unknown. The sacred ceremonies that bind families together for eternity, the baptism for the dead that offers salvation to those who lived without hearing the gospel, the endowment that teaches the plan of salvation -- none of these existed in any Christian church on earth.

The **true nature of God** had been lost. The creeds adopted during the Apostasy described God as a mysterious, incomprehensible being without body, parts, or passions. The simple truth that the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are three separate beings -- that God the Father has a glorified body of flesh and bones -- was nowhere to be taught.

The **fulness of the gospel** was missing. The plan of salvation -- where we came from, why we're here, and where we're going -- was incomplete in every Christian denomination.

Good people had done their best. But these things couldn't be recovered through reform. They had to be **restored by God Himself**.
