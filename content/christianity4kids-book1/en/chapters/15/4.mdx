### Lesson 15.4 -- The Need for Restoration

Step back for a moment and look at the big picture. Everything we've studied in this book has been leading to this moment. From the very beginning, God has been preparing the world for the Restoration of His Church.

Think about the **printing press**. When Johannes Gutenberg invented it around 1440, he made it possible for ordinary people to own Bibles and read God's word for themselves. Without the printing press, the Reformation couldn't have happened. Without people reading the Bible, they never would have questioned the changes that had crept into Christianity over the centuries.

Think about the **Reformation**. Martin Luther, John Calvin, William Tyndale, and dozens of other courageous men and women stood up against corruption and demanded change. They broke the stranglehold of a single all-powerful church. They taught people to study scripture, to think about their faith, and to seek their own relationship with God. The Reformation didn't restore Christ's Church, but it cracked the door open.

Think about **America**. God guided faithful people across the ocean to a new land. Pilgrims, Puritans, Quakers, and countless others risked everything to worship according to their conscience. They built communities, established traditions of self-government, and planted the seeds of something the world had never seen before.

Think about the **First Amendment** and religious freedom. For the first time in history, a major nation declared that the government would not control how people worship. God raised up wise founders who enshrined this principle in law. Without religious freedom, no restoration could survive.

Every single step was necessary. Remove any one of them, and the Restoration couldn't happen. It's as if God was building a road, one stone at a time, over centuries, leading to a grove of trees near **Palmyra, New York**.

And now, in the spring of 1820, a fourteen-year-old farm boy was about to read a verse of scripture that would change his life and the history of the world. **James 1:5**: "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him."

Joseph Smith lacked wisdom. He was confused by the competing claims of the churches around him. And so he decided to do exactly what the Bible said: **ask God directly**.

He walked into the trees. He knelt down. He prayed.

And God answered.

The answer would change the world forever. After 1,800 years of apostasy, after centuries of preparation, the heavens were about to open again. Prophets would be called. Priesthood would be restored. Temples would rise. The fulness of the gospel would return to the earth.

**The stage is set.** The story of the Restoration is told in *Restoration for Kids*, and it picks up right where this book ends.
