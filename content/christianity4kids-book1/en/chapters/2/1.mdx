### Lesson 2.1 -- Paul: Persecutor to Apostle

Have you ever met someone who completely changed? Maybe they used to be unkind, but then something happened and they became one of the nicest people you know. The story of the Apostle Paul is one of the greatest change stories in all of history.

Before he was called Paul, his name was **Saul of Tarsus**. Saul was a devout Jew, a **Pharisee**, and extremely well educated in Jewish law. He was also completely convinced that the followers of Jesus were dangerous and needed to be stopped. Saul didn't just disagree with Christians -- he actively **persecuted** them. He went from house to house, dragging believers off to prison (Acts 8:3). He was even there when Stephen was stoned to death, holding the coats of the men who threw the stones (Acts 7:58).

Saul was so determined to destroy the Church that he asked the high priest for letters authorizing him to travel to **Damascus** (a city in modern-day Syria) to arrest any Christians he found there. But on the road to Damascus, something happened that changed everything.

A brilliant light from heaven suddenly shone around Saul, and he fell to the ground. Then he heard a voice say, **"Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me?"** Saul asked, "Who art thou, Lord?" And the answer came back: **"I am Jesus whom thou persecutest"** (Acts 9:4-5). Saul was struck blind and had to be led by the hand into Damascus.

For three days Saul couldn't see, and he didn't eat or drink. Then the Lord sent a disciple named **Ananias** to find Saul. Ananias was nervous -- he knew Saul's reputation! But the Lord told him that Saul was a "chosen vessel" who would carry the gospel to the Gentiles, to kings, and to the children of Israel (Acts 9:15). Ananias laid his hands on Saul, and immediately Saul could see again. He was baptized and received the Holy Ghost.

From that moment on, Saul -- who became known as **Paul** -- was a completely different person. The man who had tried to destroy the Church became its most tireless and **prolific missionary**. He traveled thousands of miles, started churches across the Roman Empire, wrote letters that became part of the New Testament, and endured incredible hardships for the gospel.

Paul's conversion shows us that no one is beyond the reach of Jesus Christ. If the Lord could change Saul the persecutor into Paul the apostle, He can change anyone.
