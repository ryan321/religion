### Lesson 2.2 -- Paul's Missionary Journeys

What if someone asked you to walk hundreds of miles across deserts, mountains, and seas to tell people about Jesus, knowing that some of those people might beat you up or throw you in jail? That's exactly what the Apostle Paul did, not once, but over and over again.

After his conversion, Paul dedicated his life to spreading the gospel throughout the **Roman Empire**. He made **three major missionary journeys** that took him thousands of miles across the ancient world. These journeys are described in the book of Acts, and they show us just how hard Paul worked to share the message of Jesus Christ.

On his **first journey**, Paul and his companion **Barnabas** traveled to the island of **Cyprus** and then into **Asia Minor** (modern-day Turkey). They preached in Jewish synagogues and to Gentile audiences. Some people believed and were baptized, but others tried to stop them. In one city, Paul was **stoned** and left for dead, but he got back up and kept going (Acts 14:19-20). That's dedication!

On his **second journey**, Paul traveled even further. He crossed over into **Greece**, bringing the gospel to Europe for the first time. He preached in famous cities like **Philippi**, **Thessalonica**, **Athens**, and **Corinth**. In each place, he established small congregations of believers and appointed local leaders to care for them.

On his **third journey**, Paul revisited many of the churches he had started, strengthening the members and correcting problems. He spent about three years in **Ephesus**, one of the largest cities in the empire.

Throughout all of these journeys, Paul faced incredible hardships. In 2 Corinthians 11:23-28, Paul himself described what he went through: he was beaten with rods, whipped, **shipwrecked** three times, stoned, robbed, and constantly in danger from rivers, bandits, and even false believers. He went without food, sleep, and shelter. Yet he never gave up.

Why did Paul endure all of this? Because he knew the gospel was true. He had seen the risen Lord on the road to Damascus, and nothing could shake his testimony. Paul established **churches** across the empire, trained local leaders, and laid the groundwork for Christianity's spread throughout the ancient world.

Paul's missionary journeys remind us that sharing the gospel sometimes requires real sacrifice, but the blessings are worth it.
