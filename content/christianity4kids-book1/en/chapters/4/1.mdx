### Lesson 4.1 -- One by One

Imagine a team where the coach picks twelve players, trains them personally, and then gives them everything they need to win. Now imagine those players being taken off the field one by one, and nobody is allowed to replace them. How long before the team falls apart?

That's what happened to the apostles Jesus chose. After Jesus ascended to heaven, the apostles spread out across the known world to teach the gospel. They baptized, organized congregations, and guided the Church through revelation. But the world didn't welcome them. Almost every one of them was killed for their faith.

**James**, the brother of John, was the first apostle to die. King Herod had him killed with a sword around 44 AD (Acts 12:2). **Peter** was crucified in Rome, tradition says upside down because he didn't feel worthy to die the same way as his Lord. **Paul**, though not one of the original Twelve, was a powerful apostle who was beheaded in Rome. One by one, the apostles were imprisoned, beaten, stoned, or executed by those who hated the message they carried.

By the end of the first century, all of the original apostles were gone except **John**. Latter-day Saints understand from modern revelation that John did not die but was **translated**, meaning he was changed so he could remain on the earth to continue his ministry (see Doctrine and Covenants 7). But even though John remained, he was no longer leading a visible, organized Church with fellow apostles at his side.

Here is the critical point: when the apostles died, there was **no mechanism to replace them**. Earlier, when Judas betrayed Jesus and died, the remaining apostles had chosen Matthias to fill his place (Acts 1:26). But as persecution scattered and killed the apostles, that process stopped. Nobody with the proper authority was left to call and ordain new apostles.

Think of it like a chain. Each link connects to the next. The apostles were the links that connected the Church to Jesus Christ and His authority. As those links broke, one by one, the chain of **priesthood authority** that connected believers to God began to fall apart. The people still believed, but the authority Jesus had given was disappearing from the earth.
