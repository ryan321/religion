### Lesson 4.2 -- What Was Lost

When the apostles were gone, it wasn't just a group of leaders that disappeared. Something much bigger was lost: the things that made the Church truly the Church of Jesus Christ.

First, **revelation for the whole Church** was lost. The apostles didn't just share their opinions about what God wanted. They actually received messages from God, real revelation, to guide every member. Peter received a vision that the gospel should go to the Gentiles (Acts 10). Paul received instructions from the Lord about how to organize congregations. When questions came up, the apostles could pray and receive answers that had real authority behind them. Without apostles, there was no one on earth authorized to receive that kind of revelation for the entire Church.

Second, the **authority to ordain** was lost. The apostles had the power to lay their hands on someone's head and give them priesthood authority. They could call new leaders, set apart missionaries, and organize the work. When they were gone, who could pass on what they had received? You can't give something you don't have. It would be like trying to hand someone a key that no longer exists.

Third, the **priesthood keys for temple ordinances** were lost. In the Latter-day Saint understanding, certain sacred ordinances require specific keys of authority. This means not just general priesthood power, but the right to direct how those ordinances are performed. The apostles held those keys. When the last authorized key-holder was gone, those ordinances could no longer be performed with God's approval.

Think of it like a ship. Jesus built the ship and appointed a captain and officers. The ship had charts (the scriptures), a compass (the Holy Ghost), and a crew (the members). But when the captain and officers were all gone, what happened? The crew still sailed. They still loved the ship. They still tried to follow the charts. But without a captain who could read the stars and receive instructions from the ship's owner, the ship began to drift off course.

That's what happened to the early Church. The members were sincere. Many were incredibly brave. But without living apostles holding priesthood keys, the Church Jesus organized could not stay on the course He had set. Something essential was missing, and it would stay missing for a very long time.
