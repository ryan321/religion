### Lesson 5.4 -- Loss of Simple Truths

Some of the most precious truths of the gospel were lost during the centuries after the apostles. Today, many people think these teachings were invented by Joseph Smith or are unique to Latter-day Saints. But the truth is, these doctrines existed in the earliest days of Christianity. Joseph Smith didn't **invent** them. He **restored** them.

**God has a body.** The Bible teaches that humans were created "in the image of God" (Genesis 1:27). Moses spoke with God "face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend" (Exodus 33:11). Stephen, just before he was killed, looked up and "saw the glory of God, and Jesus standing on the right hand of God" (Acts 7:55-56). The earliest Christians understood that God the Father and Jesus Christ had real, glorified bodies. But as Greek philosophy took over, this plain teaching was rejected and replaced with the idea of an immaterial, bodyless God.

**Humans can become like God.** This might sound surprising, but early Christians actually taught this. The idea is sometimes called **theosis** or **deification**. The Apostle Peter wrote that we can become "partakers of the divine nature" (2 Peter 1:4). Paul taught that we are "heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" (Romans 8:17). Several early Church Fathers even wrote about it. Irenaeus said, "God became man so that man might become God." But over time, this beautiful truth was pushed aside and eventually rejected by most of mainstream Christianity.

**We lived before we were born.** The idea of a **premortal existence** (that our spirits lived with God before coming to earth) can be found in passages like Jeremiah 1:5, where God told Jeremiah, "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee." This concept faded from Christian teaching as Greek ideas about the soul replaced the earlier understanding.

**Baptism for the dead.** Paul mentioned this practice plainly in 1 Corinthians 15:29: "Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if the dead rise not at all? Why are they then baptized for the dead?" This verse shows that early Christians performed baptisms on behalf of people who had died. The practice was eventually abandoned and dismissed.

Each of these truths was part of the original gospel. Each was lost during the apostasy. And each was brought back through the Prophet Joseph Smith, not as something new, but as something beautifully, wonderfully old.
