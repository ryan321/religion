### Lesson 9.4 -- What the Schism Shows

The Great Schism of 1054 is one of the saddest chapters in Christian history. Two enormous branches of Christianity, both claiming to follow Jesus Christ, both filled with sincere believers, both possessing centuries of tradition, split apart and never came back together. What does this event teach us?

First, it shows what happens when there is **no prophet to unify** the Church. Jesus Christ established His Church with apostles who held priesthood keys. They had the authority to receive revelation, settle disputes, and keep the Church united across different cultures and languages. When the apostles were gone and that authority was lost, there was no one left who could speak for God to the whole Church.

Imagine if, in 1054, there had been a living prophet -- someone like Peter, who held the keys of the priesthood. The disagreements between East and West could have been brought to God in prayer. A prophet could have received revelation and said, "This is what the Lord says." Both sides would have had a reason to stay together, because they would have been following God's direction, not just arguing over human opinions.

Second, the Schism shows how **disagreements become permanent divisions** when there is no way to resolve them with authority. The arguments about the filioque, priestly marriage, and bread had been going on for a long time. In a church with revelation, these could have been settled. Without revelation, they just festered and grew until the relationship couldn't hold.

Third, we should remember that **both sides were sincere**. The Catholic bishops in Rome genuinely believed they were defending the truth. The Orthodox patriarchs in Constantinople genuinely believed the same thing. Neither side was simply being stubborn for no reason. They all loved God and wanted to serve Him. But sincerity alone is not enough. Without **authority** and **revelation**, even the most sincere people will eventually disagree and divide.

Finally, the Schism reminds us that the Apostasy didn't just change **doctrine**. It **fractured the body of Christ**. Jesus prayed "that they all may be one" (John 17:21). The Great Schism was a visible, painful sign that this prayer was not being fulfilled. The Church Jesus built was no longer one Church. It was splitting into pieces.

The Restoration is God's answer to this brokenness. Through the Prophet Joseph Smith, God restored priesthood authority, continuous revelation, and a Church that can be guided and unified by the voice of the Lord, not by the arguments of men. The Great Schism shows us exactly why the Restoration was needed.
