# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing History of Christianity for Kids. You've just covered 1,800 years of history -- from the apostles to the eve of the Restoration.

You saw the church Jesus built: twelve apostles, priesthood authority, revelation, and ordinances. You watched it grow across the Roman Empire through the courage of Paul, Peter, and the other apostles. You stood with the early Christians who died rather than deny Christ.

Then you watched what happened when the apostles were gone. Doctrine changed. Greek philosophy replaced simple truths. Councils voted on the nature of God. The simple church became an empire. Christianity split into Catholic and Orthodox. The medieval Church grew powerful but corrupt. And brave reformers -- Wycliffe, Hus, Luther, Calvin -- tried to fix what they could, even though they didn't have the authority to restore what had been lost.

You followed the Pilgrims across the ocean. You watched America become the first major nation to protect religious freedom. And you stood in the Burned-over District as a fourteen-year-old boy tried to figure out which of all the churches was right.

Now you understand why the Restoration was necessary. Not because everyone before Joseph Smith was foolish or evil -- many were courageous and sincere. But because priesthood authority, living prophets, temple ordinances, and the fulness of the gospel had been lost. They couldn't be reformed back into existence. They had to be restored by God.

And that's exactly what happened. If you haven't already, pick up The Restoration for Kids and continue the story. Everything you've learned in this book is the prelude to what happened in a grove of trees near Palmyra, New York, in the spring of 1820.

The stage was set. The Restoration was about to begin.
