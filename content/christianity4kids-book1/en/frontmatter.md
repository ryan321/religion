## Introduction

What happened to the church Jesus built?

After the Savior ascended into heaven, His apostles carried the gospel across the Roman Empire. They baptized thousands, organized churches, wrote letters, and performed miracles. But one by one, the apostles were killed. And when they were gone, something essential was missing: the authority to receive revelation and lead the Church.

This book tells the story of what happened next -- 1,800 years of Christian history, from the apostolic church to the conditions that led to the Restoration. You'll learn how doctrine changed when there were no prophets to correct it. You'll see how Christianity went from a small, persecuted movement to the official religion of the Roman Empire. You'll watch the Church split into Catholic and Orthodox, and then shatter again during the Reformation into hundreds of denominations.

But you'll also see the good. Monks who preserved the scriptures. Reformers who risked their lives to challenge corruption. Pilgrims who crossed an ocean for the right to worship freely. And a country -- America -- where religious freedom was protected by law for the first time in history.

By the end of this book, you'll understand why the Restoration was necessary. Not because everyone before Joseph Smith was wrong, but because something had been lost that no amount of human effort could bring back. It had to be restored by God Himself.

## How to use this book

This book is designed as a workbook for self-study or guided by a parent or teacher. The book covers 15 weeks, one chapter per week, four lessons per chapter, with a test on the fifth day.

Each lesson is about 15-20 minutes. Historical references are included so you can explore further if you want.

## Note to parents

This book covers the history of Christianity from a Latter-day Saint perspective. It teaches that the Great Apostasy was real and that priesthood authority was lost after the apostles died. At the same time, it treats other Christian traditions with respect. Many brave, sincere people did the best they could with the light they had.

Some topics (the Crusades, the Inquisition, persecution) are handled honestly but at an age-appropriate level. The book ends where The Restoration for Kids begins -- the Second Great Awakening and the religious confusion that led Joseph Smith to the Sacred Grove.
