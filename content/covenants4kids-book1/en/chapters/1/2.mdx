### Lesson 1.2 -- What Is a Covenant?

In the last lesson, you learned that a law is a rule from God that applies to everyone. Now let's talk about something different -- something that requires *your* choice.

A **covenant** is a two-way promise between you and God. It's not just God telling you what to do. It's an agreement where both sides make promises. You promise to do certain things, and God promises to bless you when you do. Both of you are part of the deal.

Think about it like this: imagine your parent says, "If you take care of the family dog every day -- feed him, walk him, and give him water -- I will pay for your summer camp." That's a two-way promise. You agree to do something, and your parent agrees to do something in return. A covenant with God works in a similar way, except God's promises are even bigger and more wonderful than summer camp.

Here is what makes a covenant so special: **it requires your choice**. Nobody can force you to make a covenant. God will never make you promise something you don't want to promise. When you choose to be baptized, for example, you are choosing to enter into a covenant with God. You promise to take upon yourself the name of Jesus Christ, always remember Him, and keep His commandments. And God promises to give you the gift of the Holy Ghost (see Mosiah 18:8-10).

Can you see how this is different from a law? A law applies to you whether you chose it or not. But a covenant is something you *choose* to enter. That choice makes it personal. It creates a **relationship** between you and God, not just a set of rules.

Think of it this way: laws are like the rules of a school. Everyone in the school follows the same rules. But a covenant is like a friendship. You choose to be in it, and both people care about each other and make promises to each other. God wants more than for you to just follow rules -- He wants a real relationship with you. Covenants are how He builds that relationship.

King Benjamin taught his people about this. When they made covenants with God, they were "called the children of Christ" because they had willingly entered into a promise with Him (Mosiah 5:7). They chose to belong to God, and God claimed them as His own.

That's the beauty of a covenant -- it's personal, it's chosen, and it connects you to God in a way that nothing else can.
