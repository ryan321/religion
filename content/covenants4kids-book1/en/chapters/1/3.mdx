### Lesson 1.3 -- How Laws and Covenants Work Together

Now that you know what laws and covenants are, let's see how they fit together. They aren't the same thing, but they work side by side like two important parts of the same plan.

**Laws** tell you what is right and what is wrong. They are God's standards, the truth about how the universe works. For example, the law of tithing teaches that you should give one-tenth of your increase to the Lord. The law of the Sabbath teaches that you should keep the Sabbath day holy. These laws are true for everyone.

**Covenants** are what bind you and God together in a promise. When you make a covenant, you aren't just agreeing to follow a law. You are entering into a relationship with God where He promises to bless you for your obedience. The covenant adds something personal and powerful on top of the law.

Here's a way to think about it: imagine a trail through the mountains. The law is like the trail markers -- they show you which way to go so you don't get lost. The covenant is like having a guide who walks with you, who promises to help you along the way as long as you stay on the trail. The markers (laws) tell you the right path, and the guide (covenant) gives you a partner for the journey.

One of the most important scriptures about this comes from Doctrine and Covenants 82:10: "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise." This means that when you keep your part of a covenant, God is **bound**, meaning He *must* keep His part. That is an incredible promise. The Creator of the universe has committed Himself to bless you when you are faithful.

But notice the other half: "when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise." The blessings are tied to your choices. God's laws don't change, and His covenants don't disappear, but the blessings come when you do your part.

So laws and covenants work together like this: laws teach you what God expects, and covenants create a binding agreement where God promises to bless you for living those laws. You need both. Laws without covenants would just be rules with no relationship. Covenants without laws would be promises with no direction. Together, they make up God's plan for bringing you back to Him.
