### Lesson 10.1 -- What Tithing Is

Have you ever earned money by doing chores, selling lemonade, or getting a birthday gift from Grandma? Now imagine someone told you to give back one piece out of every ten you earned. That might sound like a lot, but that is exactly what God asks in the law of tithing.

**Tithing** means giving one-tenth of your income to the Lord. If you earned ten dollars, you would give one dollar back to God. Doctrine and Covenants 119:4 calls it "one-tenth of all their interest annually." It's a simple fraction, but it carries a powerful meaning. It's a way of showing God that everything you have really belongs to Him, and you trust Him enough to give some of it back.

Tithing didn't start with the modern Church. It goes all the way back to the Old Testament. In Genesis 14:20, we learn that **Abraham** paid tithes to **Melchizedek**, a great high priest and king of Salem. Abraham had just won a battle and received a large amount of goods, and the first thing he did was give a tenth to the Lord's servant. That tells you something about Abraham's heart. He knew that his blessings came from God.

Today, tithing is one of the requirements for receiving a **temple recommend**. When you meet with your bishop, one of the questions he asks is whether you are a full tithe payer. That doesn't mean tithing is a ticket you buy to get into the temple. It means that keeping the law of tithing shows you are serious about your covenant relationship with God. If you aren't willing to give back one-tenth, are you really willing to give Him your whole heart?

So what happens with the tithing money once you pay it? It goes to The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and is used to build **temples** and **meetinghouses**, print scriptures and lesson materials, support missionary work, run Church schools, and fund **humanitarian projects** around the world. When you drop that tithing envelope into the donation box, your dollar might help build a temple in Africa, print a Book of Mormon in Japanese, or provide clean water to a village that has never had it.

Tithing is not a tax and it's not a fundraiser. It is a **law of God**, an ancient, sacred practice that connects you to Abraham, to Melchizedek, and to every faithful person who has ever trusted the Lord with their increase. And it starts with just one-tenth.
