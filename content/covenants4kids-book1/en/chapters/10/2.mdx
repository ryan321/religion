### Lesson 10.2 -- Malachi's Promise and What Tithing Teaches

In the entire Bible, there is one place where God says something truly remarkable. He looks at His children and basically says, "Go ahead, test me." That's not something God says very often. But in Malachi 3:10, He does exactly that:

"Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house, and **prove me now herewith**, saith the Lord of hosts, if I will not open you the **windows of heaven**, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it."

Think about that. God is inviting you to put Him to the test. He's saying, "Pay your tithing and then watch what I do." This is one of the very few places in all of scripture where God asks you to **prove** Him -- to try it and see for yourself whether He keeps His word.

Does that mean if you pay tithing you'll suddenly get rich? Not exactly. The "windows of heaven" don't always pour out money. Sometimes the blessings come as peace in your home, health in your body, unexpected help at just the right moment, or wisdom to make good choices with the money you do have. Many faithful tithe payers will tell you they can't explain the math. Somehow, ninety percent with God's blessings goes further than one hundred percent without them.

But tithing is about much more than the blessings you receive. It's about what it **teaches** you. When you pay tithing, you are practicing something that matters more than money. You are practicing **trust**. You are saying, "God, I believe You will take care of me even when I give part of my income away." That is an act of faith, plain and simple.

Tithing also teaches you to **put God first**. It's easy to say God is the most important thing in your life, but tithing makes you prove it with your actions. When you pay tithing before you buy toys, snacks, or games, you are showing, not just saying, that God comes first.

The world will tell you to hold on tight to every penny. God says the opposite. He says, "Give, and I will give back to you more than you can contain." Tithing is one of the simplest and clearest ways to learn that God always keeps His promises, and that trusting Him is never a mistake.
