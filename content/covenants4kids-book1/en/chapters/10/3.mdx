### Lesson 10.3 -- The Law of the Fast

Have you ever been really hungry and then finally sat down to eat? That first bite tastes amazing, doesn't it? Now imagine choosing to skip eating on purpose -- not because you forgot, but because you wanted to draw closer to God. That is what **fasting** is all about.

The **law of the fast** means going without food and water for two consecutive meals. In the Church, members typically fast on the first Sunday of each month, called **fast Sunday**. You skip breakfast and lunch (or two other meals), and then you break your fast with the next meal. It's not about starving yourself or suffering. It's about setting aside something physical so you can focus on something spiritual.

But fasting isn't just about skipping meals. The real power of fasting comes when you combine it with **prayer**. When you fast, you should have a **specific purpose** in mind -- something you are praying about or seeking help with. Maybe you are praying for a sick family member, asking for guidance about a hard decision, or seeking strength to overcome a weakness. Fasting adds power to your prayers. It's like turning up the volume on your conversation with God.

There is also a second part to the law of the fast: **fast offerings**. When you skip two meals, you save the money you would have spent on that food. You then donate at least that amount -- and more if you can -- as a fast offering. This money goes directly to help people in your ward and stake who are struggling with food, rent, or other basic needs. Your bishop uses fast offering funds to care for the poor and the needy.

The prophet Isaiah described the blessings of fasting in Isaiah 58:6-11. He taught that a true fast would "loose the bands of wickedness," "let the oppressed go free," feed the hungry, and clothe the naked. Then he gave the promise: "Then shall thy light break forth as the morning, and thine health shall spring forth speedily... and the glory of the Lord shall be thy rearward." In other words, when you fast the right way -- with a humble heart and a willingness to help others -- God promises light, health, protection, and answered prayers.

Fasting is not always easy, especially when you're young. But even small efforts to fast teach you that your spirit is stronger than your body, and that God hears the prayers of those who sacrifice to seek Him.
