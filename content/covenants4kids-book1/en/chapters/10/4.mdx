### Lesson 10.4 -- What Fasting and Tithing Have in Common

At first glance, tithing and fasting might seem like two completely different things. One is about money and the other is about food. But when you look deeper, you'll find that these two laws are built on the same foundation -- and they teach you the same lessons.

The first thing tithing and fasting have in common is that both require you to **sacrifice something physical** in order to **gain something spiritual**. With tithing, you give up money. With fasting, you give up food. In both cases, you are letting go of something your body wants so your spirit can receive something far more valuable -- closeness to God, answered prayers, spiritual strength, and blessings you can't buy at any store.

The second connection is that both laws **help other people**. Tithing funds build temples, meetinghouses, and support humanitarian work all over the world. Fast offerings go directly to feeding the hungry and helping families in need. When you pay tithing, your money might help print scriptures in another language. When you donate fast offerings, your sacrifice might put food on a family's table that very week. Both laws turn your personal sacrifice into a blessing for someone else.

Third, both tithing and fasting teach you to **put God and others before yourself**. The natural instinct is to keep your money and eat when you're hungry. There is nothing wrong with either of those things. But God asks you to pause, step back, and choose something higher. He asks you to trust that giving is better than keeping, and that serving is better than being served. That doesn't come naturally -- it has to be learned. Tithing and fasting are two of the best teachers.

Finally, both of these laws are **acts of faith**. When you pay tithing, you are trusting God to take care of you even though you now have less money. When you fast, you are trusting that God will hear your prayers and bless you for your sacrifice. Neither law makes logical sense to the world. Why give away money? Why skip meals? But faith isn't about logic -- it's about trust. And every time you practice these laws, your faith in God grows stronger.

Tithing and fasting are not punishments. They are not burdens. They are invitations from a loving Father to learn -- through real experience -- that when you put Him first, He will always take care of you.
