### Lesson 11.1 -- The Word of Wisdom

In 1833, the early members of the Church used to gather in a small room above a general store in Kirtland, Ohio, for what was called the **School of the Prophets**. These were grown men studying the gospel together, and many of them smoked tobacco and chewed it during class. By the time they were done, the room was so filled with smoke and the floor so covered with tobacco spit that it was disgusting. Joseph Smith's wife, **Emma**, complained about having to clean up the mess. Joseph thought about it, took the question to the Lord, and received one of the most well-known revelations in the Church: **Doctrine and Covenants 89**, the Word of Wisdom.

The Word of Wisdom is God's law of health. It tells us what to avoid and what to use to take care of our bodies. Here's what it prohibits:

- **Alcohol** (called "strong drinks" in the revelation)
- **Tobacco** in any form
- **Coffee and tea** (called "hot drinks," which prophets have clarified means coffee and tea)
- **Illegal drugs** and any harmful substance

But the Word of Wisdom isn't just a list of "don'ts." It also tells you what is **good** for your body. It encourages eating **fruits and vegetables**, which it says are "ordained for the use of man." It recommends **grains**, calling them "the staff of life." And it says that **meat** should be eaten "sparingly," used with thankfulness, not wasted or overused.

Think about what an unusual revelation this was for 1833. At that time, most people thought tobacco was harmless or even healthy. Doctors actually recommended it for certain ailments. Coffee and alcohol were considered completely normal. Nobody was talking about eating more fruits and vegetables and less meat. God gave this law long before medical science caught up.

The Word of Wisdom started as "a principle with promise, adapted to the capacity of the weak and the weakest of all saints" (D&C 89:3). At first it was advice, not a commandment. But over time, Church leaders made it a requirement. Today, living the Word of Wisdom is necessary for baptism and for holding a temple recommend. God gave it as gentle counsel, and His people eventually embraced it as binding law.
