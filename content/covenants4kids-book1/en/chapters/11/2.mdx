### Lesson 11.2 -- The Promises and Why It Matters

God doesn't give commandments without giving reasons. And the Word of Wisdom comes with some of the most specific promises in all of scripture. Doctrine and Covenants 89:18-21 spells them out clearly for anyone who obeys this law:

"And all saints who remember to keep and do these sayings... shall receive **health** in their navel and marrow to their bones; and shall find **wisdom** and great treasures of knowledge, even hidden treasures; and shall **run and not be weary**, and shall walk and not faint. And I, the Lord, give unto them a promise, that the **destroying angel shall pass by them**, as the children of Israel, and not slay them."

Four promises: health, wisdom, energy, and protection. That last one is especially powerful. God compares it to the Passover in Egypt, when the angel of death passed over the homes of the Israelites who had obeyed the Lord. Obedience to the Word of Wisdom is a form of spiritual protection.

Here's something remarkable to consider. When God gave this revelation in 1833, **tobacco was considered harmless**. People smoked it, chewed it, and even gave it to children. It took more than a hundred years for medical science to discover that tobacco causes **cancer**, heart disease, and lung disease. Today, tobacco kills millions of people every year. God knew that in 1833. Science didn't catch up until the 1950s and 1960s.

The same pattern holds for alcohol. We now know that alcohol damages the liver, impairs judgment, destroys families, and is one of the leading causes of preventable death in the world. God warned against it long before modern research confirmed how destructive it is.

This is why the Word of Wisdom matters. It's not just a health code. It's an **act of faith**. When you choose to follow the Word of Wisdom, you are saying, "I trust that God knows what is best for my body, even when the world says otherwise." You don't need to wait for a scientist to prove God right. You can trust Him now.

The world will tell you that a drink here or there won't hurt, that everyone experiments, that these things are no big deal. But God gave you a body as a sacred gift, and He gave you a law to protect it. The promises attached to that law (health, wisdom, strength, and protection) are worth far more than anything the world offers in a bottle or a cigarette.
