### Lesson 11.4 -- Living These Laws

The Word of Wisdom and the law of chastity might seem very different from each other. One is about what you eat and drink, and the other is about relationships and intimacy. But when you step back and look at them together, you'll see they are built on the same truths.

First, both laws are about **respecting the body God gave you**. Your body is not an accident. It is a gift -- a temple, as the apostle Paul called it (1 Corinthians 6:19). The Word of Wisdom teaches you to protect your body by keeping harmful substances out of it. The law of chastity teaches you to protect your body by honoring the sacred creative power within it. Both laws say the same thing: your body matters to God, and it should matter to you.

Second, both laws require you to **say no to things the world accepts**. The world says that drinking alcohol is normal, that experimenting with drugs is no big deal, and that sexual boundaries are outdated. Following God's laws means standing apart from those messages. That's not easy. It takes courage to be different, especially when friends, classmates, or popular culture push back. But every time you choose God's standard over the world's, you grow stronger.

Third, both of these laws **prepare you for temple covenants**. When you go to the temple, you will be asked whether you live the Word of Wisdom and the law of chastity. These are not trick questions or technicalities. They are real requirements because the temple is the most sacred place on earth, and God asks you to be living His laws before you enter His house. Keeping these laws now -- even as a young person -- is preparation for the covenants you'll make later.

Finally, it's worth saying plainly: **the world's standards are not God's standards**. The world changes its mind constantly about what is acceptable. God does not. His laws have been the same from the beginning, and they will be the same forever. Sometimes living by God's standards feels lonely or difficult. But the blessings are real -- peace of mind, a clear conscience, physical health, spiritual strength, and the quiet confidence that comes from knowing you are living the way God asked you to live.

The blessings of obedience are always worth it. Always.
