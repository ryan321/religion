### Lesson 12.1 -- What Happens in the Temple

Throughout this book, you've learned about laws and covenants, from the basics of what a covenant is, all the way through tithing, fasting, the Word of Wisdom, and the law of chastity. Every single one of those lessons has been leading somewhere. They've been leading you to the **temple**.

The temple is the most **sacred building** on earth. It is literally the **house of the Lord**, a place where God's presence can dwell and where the most important ordinances and covenants are performed. The Lord commanded the Saints to build a temple so He could "reveal mine ordinances therein" and endow His people "with power from on high" (D&C 124:40-42). Churches and meetinghouses are wonderful places to worship, but the temple is different. It is set apart. It is holy ground.

So what actually happens inside a temple? Two of the most important things are the **endowment** and **sealings**.

The **endowment** is a ceremony where you receive sacred instruction and make the highest covenants with God. The word "endowment" means "a gift," and that's exactly what it is. God gives you knowledge, promises, and power that you can't receive anywhere else. During the endowment, you learn about God's plan for His children, and you make specific promises to live according to His laws. In return, God promises you the greatest blessings He has to offer.

**Sealings** are ordinances that bind families together forever. When a husband and wife are sealed in the temple, their marriage doesn't end at death. It lasts for eternity. Children can be sealed to their parents, creating a family bond that reaches beyond this life and into the next. This is one of the most beautiful truths of the gospel: families are not temporary. Through temple sealings, they are forever.

The temple is the **culmination of the covenant path**. Every covenant you've learned about in this book (baptism, the sacrament, the priesthood, and more) leads you toward the temple. It's the destination. It's where the most sacred promises are made and where the greatest blessings are given.

You may not be old enough to go through the temple yet, but everything you are doing now (keeping the commandments, learning the gospel, honoring your covenants) is preparing you. The temple is waiting for you. And when you walk through those doors one day, you'll understand why everything you've learned matters.
