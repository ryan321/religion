### Lesson 12.2 -- The Five Covenants of the Endowment

When you receive the endowment in the temple, you don't just make one promise. You make **five specific covenants** with God. Each one builds on the last, and together they cover every part of how God wants you to live. Here is a brief look at each one.

**The Law of Obedience.** This is the foundation. You covenant to **obey God's commandments**. Not just the easy ones or the ones you agree with. All of them. Obedience is where the covenant path begins because nothing else works if you aren't willing to follow God's direction. Every other covenant depends on this one.

**The Law of Sacrifice.** You covenant to **sacrifice to support God's work** and to put your personal interests secondary to His purposes. This doesn't mean God wants you to be miserable. It means that when there's a conflict between what you want and what God needs, you choose God. Abraham was willing to sacrifice Isaac (Genesis 22). Jesus sacrificed everything. After Christ's Atonement, the sacrifice God asks is "a broken heart and a contrite spirit" (3 Nephi 9:20). This covenant asks you to follow their example in your own life.

**The Law of the Gospel.** You covenant to **live the gospel of Jesus Christ**, to accept His teachings, follow His example, and apply His Atonement in your life. This includes having faith, repenting, keeping the commandments, and being guided by the Holy Ghost. The gospel isn't just a set of beliefs. It's a way of life, and this covenant commits you to living it fully.

**The Law of Chastity.** You've already learned about this law in Chapter 11. In the temple, you formally covenant to live it, to keep sexual intimacy within the bounds the Lord has set, between a husband and wife. Making this covenant in the temple elevates it from a commandment you keep to a **sacred promise** you've personally made to God in His house.

**The Law of Consecration.** This is the highest covenant. You promise to **dedicate everything you have** (your time, talents, and resources) to building God's kingdom. Nothing is held back. Everything you are and everything you own belongs to the Lord. This covenant is the ultimate expression of trust and devotion.

These five covenants are not meant to weigh you down. They are meant to lift you up. Each one connects you more deeply to God, and together they form the framework for a life of purpose, power, and eternal joy.
