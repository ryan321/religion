### Lesson 12.3 -- The Law of Consecration in Detail

Of the five temple covenants, the law of consecration is the **highest** and the most demanding. It asks more of you than any other covenant, and it promises more in return. The Lord revealed this law in Doctrine and Covenants 42:30-31, commanding the Saints to "consecrate of thy properties" to care for the poor and build up the Church.

To **consecrate** means to set something apart as holy, to dedicate it entirely to God. When you make the covenant of consecration, you are promising to dedicate everything (your **time**, your **talents**, and your **resources**) to the building of God's kingdom on earth. Not just ten percent like tithing. Everything. Your whole life becomes an offering to the Lord.

The early Saints actually tried to live this law in a very literal way. In the 1830s, Joseph Smith organized what was called the **United Order**, where members pooled their property and resources together and shared communally. Everyone contributed what they had, and everyone received what they needed. It was a beautiful idea, but the Saints struggled to live it perfectly. Over time, the Lord adjusted how this law was practiced.

Today, the law of consecration looks different on the outside, but the principle is the same. You live it through:

- **Church callings**: giving your time to serve as a teacher, a leader, or a helper without being paid.
- **Tithing and offerings**: giving your money to support God's work.
- **Temple work**: spending your time performing ordinances for people who have died and can't do it for themselves.
- **Using your gifts and talents**: whatever you are good at, you use it to bless others and build God's kingdom. If you can sing, you sing in the choir. If you can teach, you teach. If you can serve, you serve.

The law of consecration changes the way you think about your life. Instead of asking, "What do I want?" you start asking, "What does God need from me?" Instead of living for yourself, you live for God and for others. That doesn't mean you give up happiness. It means you find a deeper kind of happiness, the kind that comes from knowing your life has purpose and that you are doing exactly what God created you to do.

Consecration is not about losing everything. It's about giving everything to the One who gave everything to you.
