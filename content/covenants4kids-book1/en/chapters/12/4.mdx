### Lesson 12.4 -- Ordinances vs. Covenants

Throughout this entire book, you've heard two words over and over: **ordinances** and **covenants**. You might think they mean the same thing. They don't. Understanding the difference between them is one of the most important things you can learn.

An **ordinance** is a sacred physical act performed by someone who holds **priesthood authority**. The Lord taught that certain ordinances are essential for salvation: "Except a man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God" (John 3:5). Baptism is an ordinance; someone with the priesthood immerses you in water. The sacrament is an ordinance; someone with the priesthood blesses the bread and water. The endowment and sealings in the temple are ordinances. In every case, an ordinance involves something you can see and touch: water, bread, oil, hands on your head. It is a physical, outward act.

A **covenant** is the promise you make during the ordinance. When you are baptized, the ordinance is being lowered into the water. The covenant is your promise to take upon yourself the name of Christ, keep His commandments, and always remember Him. The ordinance is what happens to your body. The covenant is what happens in your heart.

Here is the important part: **you can go through an ordinance without truly making the covenant**. Imagine someone who gets baptized but doesn't really intend to follow Christ. They went under the water -- the ordinance happened. But did they make a covenant? Not really. Their body got wet, but their heart wasn't in it. The same is true of the sacrament. You can eat the bread and drink the water every Sunday without actually thinking about Jesus or renewing your promises. The ordinance happens either way, but the covenant only happens if your heart is sincere.

This is why **the covenant is what matters most**. The ordinance is essential -- God requires it, and it must be done by proper authority. But the ordinance is the container, and the covenant is what goes inside it. A beautiful wrapped gift box with nothing inside is just an empty box. An ordinance without a real covenant is the same thing.

When you understand this, everything changes. You stop just going through the motions and start making real promises with real intent. You don't just get baptized; you commit your life to Christ. You don't just take the sacrament; you genuinely renew your covenant. You don't just attend the temple; you offer your whole soul to God.

The ordinance opens the door. The covenant is you choosing to walk through it.
