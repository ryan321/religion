### Lesson 13.1 -- The Sealing Power

In 1836, something incredible happened in the Kirtland Temple. The prophet Elijah -- the same Elijah from the Old Testament who was taken up to heaven in a chariot of fire -- appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery. He gave them a very special power called the **sealing power**. This is recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 110:13-16.

But why was this such a big deal? Because hundreds of years earlier, the prophet Malachi had promised that Elijah would return before the great day of the Lord. Malachi 4:5-6 says Elijah would "turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers." That prophecy was fulfilled right there in the Kirtland Temple.

So what exactly is the sealing power? It is the power to **bind on earth and in heaven**. That means when something is sealed by this power, it doesn't end when you die. It lasts forever -- through death, through the resurrection, and into eternity. This is the same power Jesus talked about when He told the Apostle Peter, "Whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall be bound in heaven" (Matthew 16:19).

Think about it this way: imagine tying a knot in a rope. A regular knot might come undone over time. But a knot tied with the sealing power is permanent. Nothing -- not even death -- can untie it, as long as you stay faithful to your covenants.

The sealing power was given to the **prophet**, who is the only person on earth who holds all of its keys at any one time. The prophet then delegates this authority to worthy men who serve as **temple sealers**. These sealers perform sealing ordinances in the temple, but they can only do so because the prophet has authorized them. It all flows from one source -- from Jesus Christ, through Elijah, to the prophet, and then to the sealers.

This power is sacred. It is not used casually or for small things. It is reserved for the most important ordinances in God's plan -- especially the sealing of husbands and wives, and the sealing of children to their parents. Without this power, no family relationship would last beyond the grave.

The restoration of the sealing power is one of the main reasons the gospel was restored in the last days. God wants His children sealed together as families -- forever. That is what Elijah came to make possible.
