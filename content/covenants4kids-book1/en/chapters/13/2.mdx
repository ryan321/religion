### Lesson 13.2 -- Celestial Marriage

In the world, when two people get married, the ceremony usually ends with the words "till death do you part." That means the marriage is only meant to last during this life. When one person dies, the marriage is over. But Heavenly Father had something much greater in mind.

Doctrine and Covenants 131:1-4 teaches that in order to receive **exaltation** -- the highest degree of the celestial kingdom -- a person must enter into "the new and everlasting covenant of marriage." This is what we call **celestial marriage**, and it can only be performed in the temple by someone who holds the sealing power.

Celestial marriage is different from any other wedding. It is not "till death do you part." It is **for time and all eternity**. That means the marriage covenant lasts forever: through this life, through death, through the resurrection, and into the eternities. A husband and wife sealed in the temple are promised that their relationship can continue in the celestial kingdom if they stay faithful.

This is the **highest ordinance** in the gospel. Every other covenant and ordinance (baptism, the sacrament, priesthood ordination, the temple endowment) all lead up to this one. Celestial marriage is the capstone, the very top of the whole structure. Without it, a person cannot receive the fulness of what God has prepared for His children.

Why is it called "the new and everlasting covenant of marriage"? Because it was **new** when it was restored through Joseph Smith in the last days; the world had lost it during the apostasy. And it is **everlasting** because it lasts forever and has been part of God's plan since the very beginning. Adam and Eve were the first to receive it.

Some people wonder why marriage matters so much in God's plan. The answer is that God Himself lives in a family relationship. He is our Heavenly Father. The whole point of the plan of salvation is to help us become like Him, and that includes living in eternal family relationships. Celestial marriage makes that possible.

This doesn't mean you need to worry about marriage right now. You are young, and that covenant is ahead of you on the path. But it's important to know that it exists, that it's real, and that it is worth preparing for. Every good choice you make today is preparing you for that sacred day in the temple.
