### Lesson 13.3 -- Families Are Forever

One of the most beautiful parts of Heavenly Father's plan is this: **no one is left out**. The sealing power doesn't just connect husbands and wives -- it connects entire families across generations.

When a husband and wife are sealed in the temple, their children who are born after the sealing are automatically **born in the covenant**. That means those children are sealed to their parents without needing a separate ordinance. They are already linked to their family for eternity. Children who were born before their parents were sealed can be sealed to their parents in a special temple ordinance too. Either way, every child can be part of an eternal family.

But what about people who lived and died without ever hearing the gospel? What about your great-great-great-grandparents who never had the chance to be baptized or sealed? Does God just forget about them?

Absolutely not. This is where **temple work for the dead** comes in. Members of the Church can research their family history, find the names of their ancestors, and then go to the temple to perform ordinances on their behalf. Baptisms, confirmations, endowments, and sealings can all be done by proxy -- meaning a living person stands in for the person who has died. The deceased person, who is now in the spirit world, can then choose to accept or reject those ordinances.

Think about how fair and loving this is. God doesn't say, "Too bad you were born at the wrong time or in the wrong place." Instead, He created a way for **every single person** who has ever lived to receive every ordinance and every covenant. That is why Elijah restored the sealing power -- to turn the hearts of the children to their fathers, linking every generation together.

This means your family tree is not just a chart on paper. It is a real, living chain of covenants stretching back through time. And you are part of it. When your family does temple work for your ancestors, you are helping to build an eternal family that reaches across centuries.

President Russell M. Nelson taught that the gathering of Israel on the other side of the veil is just as important as the gathering on this side. Every name you take to the temple is a person -- a real son or daughter of God -- who has been waiting for that ordinance.

Families are not just meant to last for a few decades. They are meant to last forever. And because of the sealing power, they can.
