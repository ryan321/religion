### Lesson 13.4 -- Why Sealing Is the Culmination

Have you ever built something step by step? Maybe a tower of blocks, a puzzle, or a LEGO set? Each piece matters, but the whole point is to finish the complete picture. The covenants of the gospel work the same way. Every single one leads somewhere, and that somewhere is the **sealing ordinance**.

Think about the path. It starts with **faith** in Jesus Christ and **repentance**. Then comes **baptism**, your first covenant with God, where you promise to take upon yourself the name of Christ, keep His commandments, and always remember Him. After that, you receive the **gift of the Holy Ghost**, which gives you a constant companion to guide you.

Each week, the **sacrament** renews your baptismal covenant and keeps you connected to the Savior. Young men receive the **priesthood**, giving them authority to act in God's name. Then, in the temple, you receive the **endowment**, sacred instruction and covenants that prepare you with knowledge and power from on high.

And then comes the **sealing**. A husband and wife kneel across a sacred altar in the temple and are sealed together for time and all eternity. This is not just one more step. This is what every other step has been building toward. The sealing is the **culmination**, the highest point, the purpose behind everything else.

Why? Because the whole plan of salvation is about **bringing families home to God together**. Not alone. Together. Heavenly Father doesn't want you to return to Him by yourself. He wants you to come back with your family: your spouse, your children, your parents, your grandparents, and your ancestors stretching back to the beginning.

Every covenant you have made or will make is a piece of that grand design. Baptism starts you on the path. The sacrament keeps you on it. The priesthood gives you power to serve. The endowment gives you knowledge and protection. And the sealing ties it all together into one eternal family, bound by the power of God.

This is what it's all for. This is why God gave laws and covenants in the first place: not to burden you, but to bring you home. Every commandment you keep, every covenant you honor, is one more step toward that beautiful moment when your family is sealed together forever.

The path is real. The destination is real. And the God who set it all in motion is waiting for you at the end with open arms.
