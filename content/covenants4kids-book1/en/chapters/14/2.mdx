### Lesson 14.2 -- Repentance: The Way Back

If breaking a covenant is like unplugging a lamp, then repentance is plugging it back in. The light returns. The warmth comes back. And the connection to God is restored.

**Repentance** is one of the most hopeful words in the entire gospel. It means you can change. It means your mistakes don't define you. It means that no matter how far you've wandered from the path, there is always a way back -- and that way was paid for by Jesus Christ.

The **Atonement of Jesus Christ** is what makes repentance possible. In the Garden of Gethsemane and on the cross at Calvary, Jesus suffered for every sin ever committed by every person who has ever lived. He took upon Himself the pain, the guilt, and the consequences of all our wrong choices. Because He already paid the price, we can be forgiven when we sincerely repent.

So how do you repent? It starts with **recognizing** that you did something wrong. Then you feel **godly sorrow** -- not just feeling bad because you got caught, but truly feeling sad that you hurt your relationship with God and maybe with other people. Next, you **confess** to God in prayer, and for serious sins, you talk to your bishop, who is there to help you -- not to judge you. Then you **forsake** the sin, which means you stop doing it and try your best not to do it again. Finally, you **make things right** with anyone you may have hurt.

Here is the beautiful part: the **sacrament** is a weekly renewal mechanism built right into the gospel. Every Sunday when you take the bread and water, you are renewing your baptismal covenant. You are telling God, "I'm trying. I want to keep my promises. Please forgive me for the ways I've fallen short this week." And He does.

Is there any sin too big for the Atonement? The scriptures teach that there is only one sin that cannot be forgiven -- **denying the Holy Ghost** after receiving a perfect knowledge. That is something extremely rare, and it is not something you need to worry about. For every other sin -- every single one -- the Atonement is big enough, deep enough, and powerful enough to cover it.

Repentance is not a punishment. It is a **gift**. It is the Savior saying, "I already carried this for you. Let me help you set it down."
