### Lesson 14.3 -- Dispensations: Covenants Lost and Restored

Throughout history, there has been a pattern -- and once you see it, you'll notice it everywhere. God gives His children covenants. For a while, people keep them faithfully. Then, over time, people drift away. They forget. They rebel. They change the ordinances and ignore the prophets. Eventually, the covenants and priesthood authority are **lost**. This is called **apostasy**.

But here's the hopeful part: God never leaves things broken forever. When apostasy happens, God calls a **new prophet** and restores His covenants all over again. Each time He does this, it is called a **dispensation**.

Let's walk through the pattern. **Adam** was the first prophet. He received covenants and priesthood authority directly from God. Over time, his descendants fell away. Then God called **Noah** and started again. Noah received covenants -- including the rainbow as a sign. Later, people fell away again, and God called **Abraham**, giving him the great Abrahamic covenant. After Abraham, the covenant continued through Isaac and Jacob, but eventually the people fell into bondage in Egypt. So God raised up **Moses** and gave the children of Israel the law at Mount Sinai.

When Israel rejected the prophets over and over, God sent His own Son. **Jesus Christ** came and established His Church, called apostles, and gave them priesthood authority. But after Jesus and His apostles were killed, apostasy crept in again. The priesthood was lost. Ordinances were changed. Truths were corrupted. This led to centuries of what we call the **Great Apostasy**.

Then, in 1820, God called **Joseph Smith** -- a young boy, just a teenager -- and began the final restoration. This is our dispensation: the **dispensation of the fulness of times** (D&C 112:30). It is called "the fulness of times" because everything that was ever given in any previous dispensation has been restored in this one. Every covenant, every key, every ordinance -- all gathered into one final dispensation that will never be taken from the earth again.

You are living in the most important dispensation in all of history. The covenants you make today are part of a restoration that God has been working toward since the very beginning.
