### Lesson 14.4 -- God Never Breaks His Covenant

Here is something you can always count on, no matter what: **God never breaks His covenant**.

You might break yours. You might stumble, fall, forget, or even choose to walk away for a time. But God? He stays. He waits. He keeps every single promise He has ever made. Doctrine and Covenants 82:10 puts it simply: "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise." God is **bound** by His own word. When you keep your covenants, He is absolutely guaranteed to keep His.

But even when you break your side of the covenant, God doesn't tear up the agreement and walk away. He stands at the door. His arms stay open. He keeps calling you home through the Holy Ghost, through the scriptures, through the words of prophets, through the kindness of other people, and through the quiet feelings in your heart that remind you who you really are.

Think about a parent who loves their child. If that child makes a mistake -- even a big one -- does the parent stop loving them? No. A good parent might be sad. They might let the child experience consequences. But they never stop loving, and they never stop hoping the child will come back. Heavenly Father is the perfect version of that parent. His love for you is **unconditional** -- it doesn't depend on your performance. He loves you at your best and at your worst.

Now, that doesn't mean breaking a covenant has no consequences. It does. Blessings are paused. The Spirit withdraws. Growth stops for a time. But the **door of mercy never closes** -- not as long as you are willing to turn around and come back.

This is the heart of every covenant: **mercy**. God didn't create the covenant system to trap you or to set you up for failure. He created it because He knows you will stumble, and He wanted to build a way back into the plan from the very beginning. Repentance wasn't an afterthought. It was part of the design from day one. The Atonement of Jesus Christ was planned before the world was even created.

So if you ever feel like you've gone too far, remember this: you haven't. God is still there. His covenant is still in force. His arms are still open. And He will never -- not ever -- give up on you.
