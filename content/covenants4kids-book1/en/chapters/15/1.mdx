### Lesson 15.1 -- One Path, Many Covenants

President Russell M. Nelson has taught about something he calls the **covenant path**. It's a simple but powerful idea: there is one path that leads back to Heavenly Father, and it is marked by covenants.

The path follows a clear sequence, and each step builds on the one before it. You don't skip steps, just like you don't skip rungs on a ladder. Each covenant gives you the power and preparation you need for the next one.

It begins with **faith** in Jesus Christ. Faith isn't a covenant, but it's the starting point -- you have to believe before you can commit. Next comes **repentance** -- turning away from sin and toward God. These two steps prepare your heart to make your first covenant.

Then comes **baptism**. This is your first formal covenant with God. You promise to take upon yourself the name of Jesus Christ, keep His commandments, and always remember Him. In return, God promises you the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost. Right after baptism, you are **confirmed** and receive the **gift of the Holy Ghost** -- a permanent guide and comforter.

From there, young men receive the **priesthood**, which gives them the authority to act in God's name. This is both a privilege and a covenant responsibility. The next major step is the **temple endowment**, where you receive sacred instruction, make additional covenants, and are given power from on high to help you through the rest of your life.

And finally, the path leads to **sealing** -- celestial marriage in the temple, where a husband and wife and their family are bound together for eternity. This is the highest covenant and the destination the whole path has been leading toward.

Notice something important: each step **prepares** you for the next. Baptism teaches you to make and keep promises. The sacrament gives you weekly practice in renewal. Priesthood service teaches you to act with God's authority. The endowment gives you knowledge and power. And the sealing ties it all together.

This is not a checklist to rush through. It is a **way of living**. Each covenant changes you, strengthens you, and draws you closer to becoming like Heavenly Father. The path is steady, and it is sure. And every step you take on it brings you closer to home.
