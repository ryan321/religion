### Lesson 15.2 -- The Gathering of Israel

Thousands of years ago, God made a promise to Abraham. He told Abraham that through his descendants, **all the families of the earth** would be blessed (Genesis 12:3, Abraham 2:11). That promise, the Abrahamic covenant, is not just an old story from the scriptures. It is happening right now, and **you are part of it**.

The **gathering of Israel** is the process of bringing God's children back to Him through covenants. Israel was the name God gave to Abraham's grandson Jacob, and his descendants became the house of Israel. Over the centuries, the house of Israel was scattered across the whole earth. People lost their connection to the gospel, to the priesthood, and to the covenants. But God promised that in the last days, He would **gather** them back.

That gathering is happening today. Every time a missionary teaches the gospel and someone chooses to be baptized, that is gathering. Every time a family is sealed in the temple, that is gathering. Every time someone does temple work for a deceased ancestor, that is gathering on the other side of the veil. The gathering of Israel is the **biggest thing happening on the earth right now**, as President Nelson has taught.

And here's the amazing part: if you are a baptized member of the Church, you are part of the house of Israel, whether by blood or by adoption through baptism. The Abrahamic covenant flows through you. When you share the gospel with a friend, invite someone to church, or help with family history work, you are literally fulfilling the promise God made to Abraham thousands of years ago.

Think about that for a moment. You -- right now, at your age -- are the Abrahamic covenant in action. You are not too young to be part of God's greatest work. Every act of faith, every testimony shared, every name taken to the temple is part of the gathering.

The prophets have taught that this is the **dispensation of the fulness of times**, the final dispensation before the Savior returns. The gathering must happen before He comes. And you are not a spectator. You are a participant. God is counting on you to help gather His children home.
