### Lesson 15.4 -- Your Place on the Path

If you have been baptized and confirmed, then you are already **on the covenant path** right now. You are not waiting for it to begin someday. It has already begun, and you are walking it.

Every Sunday when you take the sacrament, you are renewing your baptismal covenant. You are telling Heavenly Father that you still want to follow Jesus Christ, that you still want to keep His commandments, and that you still want to remember Him. And every Sunday, He renews His promise to you, the promise that His Spirit will always be with you.

Every commandment you keep is a way of **keeping your side of the covenant**. When you are honest, you are keeping your covenant. When you are kind, you are keeping your covenant. When you pray, read your scriptures, serve others, pay your tithing, obey the Word of Wisdom, and honor your parents, you are keeping your covenant. It's not just about the big moments in the temple. It's about the everyday choices that show God you are serious about your promises.

Ahead of you on the path are the **temple endowment** and the **sealing**. These are sacred covenants you will make when you are older and ready. But you are preparing for them right now, today, with every choice you make. The way you live now is building the foundation for everything that comes next.

The covenant path is not a **checklist**, something you rush through and check off. It is a **way of life**. It is how you live every single day. It is waking up and choosing to follow Christ. It is falling down and getting back up through repentance. It is serving others, growing in faith, and becoming a little more like Heavenly Father each day.

And where does this path lead? To **eternal life**, what Doctrine and Covenants 14:7 calls "the greatest of all the gifts of God." Eternal life means living forever in the presence of Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ, with your family, having received a fulness of joy.

That is what every law and covenant has been pointing toward from the very first chapter of this book. That is what God wants for you. And the path to get there is already under your feet.

You are on it. Keep walking.
