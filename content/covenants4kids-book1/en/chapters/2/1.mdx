### Lesson 2.1 -- Adam and Eve: The Fall and the First Covenant

Let's go back to the very beginning -- the Garden of Eden. This is where God's plan of laws and covenants started, and it's one of the most important stories in all of scripture.

God placed Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden, a beautiful place where everything was provided for them. But God gave them a choice. He told them they could eat of every tree in the garden except one -- the tree of knowledge of good and evil. If they ate from it, they would be cast out of the garden and become mortal, meaning they would experience pain, hard work, and death.

Here's something many people don't understand: the Fall was **necessary**. It had to happen. The prophet Lehi explained this clearly: "If Adam had not transgressed he would not have fallen, but he would have remained in the garden of Eden ... they would have had no children; wherefore they would have remained in a state of innocence, having no joy, for they knew no misery" (2 Nephi 2:22-23). Without the Fall, none of us would be here. Adam and Eve could not have had children, and no one would have the chance to learn, grow, and become like God.

Lehi went on to say, "Adam fell that men might be; and men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25). The Fall was part of the plan all along.

After Adam and Eve left the garden, God did not abandon them. He gave Adam the **law of sacrifice** -- a commandment to offer the firstlings of their flocks as an offering to God. Adam obeyed, even though at first he didn't fully understand why. Then an angel came and explained: "This thing is a **similitude of the sacrifice of the Only Begotten** of the Father" (Moses 5:7). Every sacrifice Adam offered pointed forward to Jesus Christ.

This was the beginning of the first covenant. God taught Adam what to do (the law), Adam chose to obey (his part of the covenant), and God promised to send a Savior to redeem Adam and all of his children (God's part of the covenant). Right from the very start, everything pointed to Jesus Christ.

The pattern was set: God teaches, we obey, and He blesses. This is the pattern of every covenant that would ever follow.
