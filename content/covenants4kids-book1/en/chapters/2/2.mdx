### Lesson 2.2 -- What Adam Was Taught

Some people think that Adam and Eve were left on their own after the Fall, stumbling around trying to figure out how to worship God. But that's not what happened at all. God taught Adam the **fulness of the gospel** from the very beginning.

This is one of the most amazing truths in the restored gospel: Adam didn't have a watered-down version of God's plan. He had the whole thing. The scriptures in Moses chapters 5 and 6 tell us exactly what Adam was taught, and it might surprise you how much he knew.

Adam was taught about **baptism**. That's right. Baptism didn't start with John the Baptist or even with Moses. It started with Adam. God commanded Adam to be baptized in water, and Adam was baptized. "And he heard a voice out of heaven, saying: Thou art baptized with fire, and with the Holy Ghost" (Moses 6:66). Adam received the **Holy Ghost**, just like you can after you are baptized and confirmed.

Adam was also given the **priesthood**. He held the authority to act in God's name, to perform ordinances, and to teach his family the gospel. He was ordained "after the order of him who was without beginning of days or end of years" (Moses 6:67), meaning the priesthood of the Son of God, which we now call the Melchizedek Priesthood.

Think about what this means: from the very first generation of people on the earth, God made sure that His children had access to **baptism, the Holy Ghost, the priesthood, and the full plan of salvation**. These weren't inventions that came later. They were there from day one.

This also sets a pattern for every covenant that follows. The pattern is: God reveals His gospel, He offers ordinances (like baptism and sacrifice), He gives His children the priesthood to perform those ordinances, and He promises blessings to those who are faithful. Every single covenant in the scriptures, from Adam all the way to you, follows this same basic pattern.

Adam was the first, but he certainly wasn't the last. God's plan was to repeat this pattern with every generation, teaching His children the same truths and offering them the same blessings. And that's exactly what He did, as you'll see with Noah in the next lesson.
