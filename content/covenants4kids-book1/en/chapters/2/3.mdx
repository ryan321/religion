### Lesson 2.3 -- The Covenant with Noah

After Adam, many generations came and went. Some of God's children were faithful, but many turned away from Him. By the time of Noah, the earth had become so wicked that the Lord said, "The end of all flesh is come before me; for the earth is filled with violence" (Genesis 6:13). God decided to cleanse the earth with a great flood.

But Noah was different. The scriptures say Noah was "a just man and perfect in his generations, and Noah walked with God" (Genesis 6:9). God warned Noah about the flood and commanded him to build an ark, a massive boat that would save his family and the animals. Noah obeyed, even though it must have seemed incredible to everyone around him. He trusted God's word and did exactly what he was told.

After the flood, the earth had a **new beginning**. It was almost like a second creation. And just as God gave Adam commandments and covenants after the creation, God gave Noah commandments and a covenant after the flood.

God gave Noah specific commandments, recorded in Genesis 9:1-7. He told Noah and his sons to "be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth." He gave them permission to eat meat but commanded them not to eat blood. And He established the sacredness of human life, that no one should shed the blood of another person.

Then came the covenant. God made a remarkable promise: "I establish my covenant with you ... neither shall there any more be a flood to destroy the earth" (Genesis 9:11). God promised that He would **never again destroy the earth by flood**. This wasn't just a promise to Noah. It was a promise to every person who would ever live on this planet.

And God gave a sign of that covenant: the **rainbow**. Every time you see a rainbow in the sky, you are seeing God's reminder of His promise to Noah. It's a visible, beautiful sign that God keeps His covenants.

This covenant with Noah is sometimes called the **covenant of preservation**. It's the broadest covenant in scripture because it applies to every human being. God promised to preserve the earth so that His plan could continue and His children could have the chance to live, learn, and make covenants of their own.

Noah trusted God, obeyed His commandments, and received incredible promises in return, just like Adam before him.
