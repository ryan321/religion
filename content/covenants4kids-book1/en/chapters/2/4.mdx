### Lesson 2.4 -- What These First Covenants Teach

You've now learned about two of the earliest covenants in scripture: the covenant with Adam and the covenant with Noah. Before we move on to Abraham, let's step back and see what these first covenants teach us about how God works.

**Adam's covenant establishes a pattern.** Look at what happened with Adam: God taught him the gospel. God asked for obedience (offering sacrifices, being baptized, keeping commandments). And God promised blessings: the gift of the Holy Ghost, the priesthood, and the promise of a Savior. This pattern (**God teaches, God asks for obedience, God promises blessings**) is the pattern for every covenant that will ever be made. Every single one. Whether it's the covenant with Abraham, the covenant at Sinai, or the covenant you make at baptism, this same pattern is always there.

**Noah's covenant is the broadest covenant in all of scripture.** Most covenants are made with specific people or groups. But the covenant with Noah applies to every human being who has ever lived or ever will live. God promised to preserve the earth so His plan could go forward. That means you are living under Noah's covenant right now. Every time the rain stops and the sun comes out, you are seeing that covenant in action.

**The rainbow is a visible reminder.** One of the wonderful things about the covenant with Noah is that God gave a sign you can actually see. The **rainbow** is not just a pretty thing in the sky. It is a reminder that God keeps His promises. When you see one, you can remember that the God who promised Noah He would preserve the earth is the same God who has made promises to you.

**Covenants have been here from the very beginning.** Sometimes people think covenants are a later invention, something that started with Moses or even with Jesus. But as you've learned, covenants started with the very first people on earth. God's plan has always included covenants. From Adam to Noah, from Abraham to you, God has always worked through the same pattern of teaching, asking, and blessing.

These first covenants laid the foundation. They proved that God is faithful, that His promises are real, and that He wants a relationship with every one of His children. Now, in the next chapter, you'll learn about one of the most important covenants ever made -- the covenant with Abraham -- and you'll discover that it has everything to do with *you*.
