### Lesson 3.1 -- God Calls Abraham

In the last chapter, you learned about God's covenants with Adam and Noah. Now we come to one of the most important covenants in all of history: the **Abrahamic covenant**. This covenant is so important that it still affects your life today. But before we talk about the covenant itself, let's meet the man God chose.

Abraham grew up in a place called **Ur of the Chaldees**, which was in the land we now call Mesopotamia (modern-day Iraq). It was a place full of idol worship. Many of the people there, including some of Abraham's own family, worshipped false gods made of wood and stone. It was a dangerous time to believe in the true God.

But Abraham was different. Even as a young man, he wanted something more. He wrote, "I sought for the blessings of the fathers, and the right whereunto I should be ordained to administer the same ... desiring also to be one who possessed great knowledge, and to be a greater follower of righteousness" (Abraham 1:2). Abraham wasn't content to go along with the crowd. He wanted the **priesthood**, he wanted knowledge, and he wanted to follow God.

This is remarkable when you think about it. Abraham didn't grow up in a faithful Latter-day Saint family with scriptures on the shelf and family home evening every Monday. He grew up surrounded by wickedness and false religion. But he chose to seek God anyway. He chose righteousness even when it was hard and dangerous.

Because of Abraham's faithfulness, God **called** him. God told Abraham to leave his home, his country, and his family's land, and to go to a place that God would show him. This took enormous faith. Abraham didn't get a map or a detailed plan. He simply had to trust God and go.

And Abraham obeyed. He left Ur with his wife Sarah, and he followed God's direction. He didn't know exactly where he was going, but he knew *who* was leading him. That trust and obedience is what made Abraham ready to receive one of the greatest covenants ever given.

God doesn't always call us to leave our homes, but He does call each of us to follow Him, even when we don't know exactly where the path will lead. Abraham's example shows us that the first step in any covenant is simply being willing to say yes.
