### Lesson 3.2 -- The Four Promises

When God made His covenant with Abraham, He didn't give just one promise -- He gave **four great promises**. These promises are found in Genesis 12:1-3, Genesis 17:1-8, and Abraham 2:9-11. Together, they make up the heart of the Abrahamic covenant.

**Promise 1: Land.** God promised Abraham that He would give him and his descendants a land for their inheritance. This land became known as the promised land -- the land of Canaan, which we now call Israel. God said, "Unto thy seed will I give this land" (Genesis 12:7). This wasn't just about having a nice place to live. It was about God providing a home where His covenant people could worship, grow, and build Zion.

**Promise 2: Posterity.** God promised Abraham that his descendants would be as numerous as "the stars of the heaven" and "the sand which is upon the sea shore" (Genesis 22:17). This was an incredible promise because Abraham and his wife Sarah were very old and had no children at the time. It seemed impossible. But God kept His promise, and Abraham became the father of a family that has grown into millions and millions of people across the earth.

**Promise 3: Priesthood.** God promised that the priesthood would continue through Abraham's lineage. Abraham 2:9 says, "I will make of thee a great nation, and I will bless thee above measure, and make thy name great among all nations, and thou shalt be a blessing unto thy seed after thee, that in their hands they shall bear this ministry and **Priesthood** unto all nations." The priesthood -- God's authority to act in His name -- would be passed down through Abraham's family.

**Promise 4: The gospel to all nations.** God promised that through Abraham's seed, all the families of the earth would be blessed (Genesis 12:3). This means the gospel of Jesus Christ would go to every nation, kindred, tongue, and people through Abraham's descendants. Abraham's covenant isn't just for one family -- it's for **everyone**.

These four promises -- land, posterity, priesthood, and the gospel to all nations -- are the foundation of the Abrahamic covenant. They are the promises God made thousands of years ago, and they are still being fulfilled today. In fact, as you'll learn soon, *you* are part of their fulfillment.
