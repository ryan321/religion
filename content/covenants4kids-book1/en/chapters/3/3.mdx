### Lesson 3.3 -- Passed to Isaac, Jacob, and the Twelve Tribes

God's covenant with Abraham was never meant to stop with Abraham. It was meant to be passed down through his family, generation after generation. And that's exactly what happened.

After Abraham, the covenant was **renewed with Isaac**, Abraham's son. God appeared to Isaac and said, "I will perform the oath which I sware unto Abraham thy father; and I will make thy seed to multiply as the stars of heaven ... and in thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed" (Genesis 26:3-4). Isaac received the same promises: the same land, the same posterity, the same priesthood, and the same mission to bless all nations.

Then the covenant was renewed again with **Jacob**, Isaac's son. God appeared to Jacob in a vision at a place called Bethel and said, "The land whereon thou liest, to thee will I give it, and to thy seed; and thy seed shall be as the dust of the earth ... and in thee and in thy seed shall all the families of the earth be blessed" (Genesis 28:13-14). God also gave Jacob a new name, **Israel**, which is why his descendants are called the children of Israel.

Jacob had **twelve sons**, and these twelve sons became the fathers of the **Twelve Tribes of Israel**. Their names were Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Dan, Naphtali, Gad, Asher, Issachar, Zebulun, Joseph, and Benjamin. Each tribe inherited part of the Abrahamic covenant. Together, they became the covenant people of God, the house of Israel.

Why does this matter to you? Because from this point on in scripture, **every covenant God makes is connected to the Abrahamic covenant**. The covenant at Mount Sinai with Moses? That was made with the children of Israel -- Abraham's descendants. The covenant of the Book of Mormon peoples? They were from the tribe of Joseph, one of Jacob's twelve sons. The covenants you make at baptism and in the temple? They are part of the same covenant line that stretches all the way back to Abraham.

The Abrahamic covenant is like a great river. It started with one man, flowed through his son and grandson, split into twelve streams through the twelve tribes, and has continued to flow through all of history, right down to you. Every covenant you make connects you to this ancient, powerful promise.
