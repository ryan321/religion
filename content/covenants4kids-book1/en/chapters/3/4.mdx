### Lesson 3.4 -- You Are Part of This Covenant

Here's the most exciting part of everything you've learned in this chapter: the Abrahamic covenant isn't just an ancient story about people who lived thousands of years ago. It is alive and active right now, and **you are part of it**.

How did you enter into this covenant? Through **baptism**. When you were baptized (or when you will be baptized), you entered into a covenant with God. And that covenant connects you directly to the promises God made to Abraham. You became part of the house of Israel, part of the covenant family that stretches all the way back through the twelve tribes, through Jacob, Isaac, and Abraham, all the way back to Adam.

There's another way you learn about your place in this covenant: your **patriarchal blessing**. When you are old enough (usually as a teenager), you can receive a patriarchal blessing from an ordained patriarch. In that blessing, you will be told which tribe of Israel you belong to. This is called your **lineage declaration**. It tells you which branch of Abraham's family you are connected to.

Some members of the Church are **literal descendants** of Abraham -- meaning their ancestors were part of one of the twelve tribes. Others are **adopted** into the house of Israel through baptism and the covenant. But here's the beautiful thing: it doesn't matter which one you are. The promises are exactly the same. Whether you are a literal descendant or adopted, you receive all the blessings of the Abrahamic covenant. As Paul taught, "If ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise" (Galatians 3:29).

Think about what this means. The promises God made to Abraham (land, posterity, priesthood, and the gospel going to all nations) are *your* promises too. You are part of the family that carries the priesthood to the world. You are part of the people who share the gospel with every nation. You have a role to play in fulfilling the covenant God made thousands of years ago.

You aren't just reading about the Abrahamic covenant in a book. You are **living it right now**. Every time you keep your baptismal covenants, every time you serve others, every time you share what you believe, you are helping to fulfill the promise God made to Abraham that through his seed, all the families of the earth would be blessed.

That includes your family, your friends, and people you haven't even met yet. You are part of something ancient, something powerful, and something very much alive.
