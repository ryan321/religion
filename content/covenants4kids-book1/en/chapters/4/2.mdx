### Lesson 4.2 -- The Ten Commandments

After God descended onto Mount Sinai in fire and thunder, He spoke directly to the people of Israel and gave them the **Ten Commandments**. You can find them in Exodus 20. These weren't suggestions or helpful tips. They were commandments, God's laws for how His covenant people should live.

The Ten Commandments are split into two groups, and understanding the split helps you remember them.

The **first four commandments** are about your relationship with **God**. Have no other gods before me. Don't make idols. Don't take God's name in vain. Keep the Sabbath day holy. These four teach you to put God first, respect Him, and set aside time for Him every week. They answer the question: "How should I treat God?"

The **last six commandments** are about your relationship with **other people**. Honor your father and mother. Don't kill. Don't commit adultery. Don't steal. Don't lie about others. Don't covet what belongs to someone else. These six teach you to treat people with respect, honesty, and fairness. They answer the question: "How should I treat the people around me?"

Here's something important: the Ten Commandments are still binding today. Some people think they were just for the ancient Israelites, but that's not true. When Jesus was asked which commandment was the greatest, He said to love God with all your heart and to love your neighbor as yourself (Matthew 22:37-39). Those two great commandments are basically a summary of the Ten Commandments: love God (commandments 1-4) and love your neighbor (commandments 5-10).

But Jesus didn't just repeat the Ten Commandments and leave them the same. He **deepened** them. For example, the old commandment said, "Thou shalt not kill." Jesus said that even being angry with your brother without cause is a serious sin (Matthew 5:22). The old commandment said, "Thou shalt not commit adultery." Jesus said that even looking at someone with lustful thoughts breaks that law in your heart (Matthew 5:28). Christ raised the bar. He showed that God cares not just about what you do, but about what you think and feel.

So the Ten Commandments are like a foundation. They were never meant to be the ceiling, the highest we could reach. They were the floor, the starting point. Jesus came and showed us how to build something much higher on that foundation. He wants us to be good not just in our actions, but all the way down to our hearts.
