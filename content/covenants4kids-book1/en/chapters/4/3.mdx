### Lesson 4.3 -- Sacrifices, Festivals, and the Sabbath

When God gave Israel the Law of Moses, He didn't just give them commandments about right and wrong. He also gave them a whole system of **sacrifices**, **festivals**, and **Sabbath** worship -- and every single piece of it was designed to teach them about Jesus Christ, even though He wouldn't come for over a thousand years.

Let's start with **sacrifices**. The Israelites were commanded to bring animals -- usually lambs, goats, or bulls -- to the tabernacle as offerings to God. These were called **burnt offerings**, and the animal had to be "without blemish" -- no broken legs, no spots, no sickness. Why? Because that perfect animal represented Jesus Christ, who would one day offer Himself as the perfect sacrifice. He had no sin, no flaw, no blemish. Every time an Israelite brought a spotless lamb to the altar, they were acting out a symbol of what the Savior would do.

Next, the **festivals**. Israel had several important holy days each year, and each one taught a lesson about God's plan.

- **Passover** reminded them of how the destroying angel "passed over" Israelite homes in Egypt when they put lamb's blood on their doorposts. This pointed to Christ, the "Lamb of God," whose blood saves us from spiritual death.
- The **Day of Atonement** was the one day each year when the high priest entered the most sacred part of the tabernacle to make an offering for the sins of the whole nation. This pointed to Christ entering God's presence to atone for the sins of all people.
- The **Feast of Tabernacles** celebrated the harvest and reminded Israel that God had sheltered them in the wilderness. It pointed to the day when God would gather His people and dwell with them forever.

Finally, the **Sabbath**. God commanded Israel to rest on the seventh day of every week. But it wasn't just about taking a break. Exodus 31:13 says the Sabbath was a **sign** of the covenant between God and Israel -- a weekly reminder that they belonged to Him and that He was the one who made them holy. Every Sabbath was like wearing a badge that said, "I am in a covenant with God."

All of these things -- the sacrifices, the festivals, the Sabbath -- were like a giant classroom. They taught Israel to look forward to the coming of Jesus Christ and to remember that they were God's covenant people.
