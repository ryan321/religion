### Lesson 4.4 -- The Law as a Schoolmaster

Here's something most people don't realize: the Law of Moses was never God's first choice for Israel. He originally planned to give them something much higher.

When Moses went up Mount Sinai, God wanted to give the people the **fulness of the gospel** -- the higher priesthood and the higher law. But while Moses was on the mountain receiving this incredible gift, the people down below got impatient. They melted their gold jewelry, shaped it into a golden calf, and started worshipping it like an idol (Exodus 32). They had just promised God they would obey Him, and within weeks they were bowing down to a statue made of earrings.

When Moses came down and saw what they had done, he broke the stone tablets God had given him. God was angry too. Because the people showed they weren't ready for the higher law, God took it away and gave them a **lesser law** instead: the Law of Moses. It was full of rules about what to eat, what to wear, when to wash, how to sacrifice, and dozens of other detailed instructions. It told them exactly what to do in nearly every situation so they could learn obedience step by step.

The Apostle Paul described this perfectly in Galatians 3:24. He said the law was a "**schoolmaster** to bring us unto Christ." A schoolmaster is a teacher or tutor. Think of it like training wheels on a bicycle. Training wheels aren't the goal; riding freely is the goal. But training wheels help you learn balance until you're ready for the real thing. The Law of Moses was Israel's training wheels. It taught them discipline, obedience, and reverence, preparing them for the day when Jesus Christ would come and give them the higher law.

And here's the key: **every element of the Law of Moses pointed to Christ**. The sacrifices pointed to His Atonement. The festivals pointed to His mission. The high priest pointed to Christ as the great High Priest. The tabernacle pointed to God's presence among His people. None of it was random. All of it was a giant arrow pointing forward to the Savior.

The Law of Moses was always meant to be **temporary**. It was given to prepare Israel for the Messiah, and when Jesus finally came, He fulfilled the law and replaced it with something better: the higher law of the gospel. The schoolmaster's job was done.
