### Lesson 5.1 -- The Covenant with David

After Israel entered the promised land and was led by judges for many years, they eventually got kings. The first king, Saul, didn't work out very well. But the second king, **David**, was a man after God's own heart (Acts 13:22). And God made a very special covenant with him.

You can find this covenant in 2 Samuel 7:12-16. God told David through the prophet Nathan: "When thy days be fulfilled... I will set up thy seed after thee... and I will establish the throne of his kingdom for ever." God promised David that his **descendants** would sit on the throne, and that this kingdom would last **forever**. Not for a hundred years, not for a thousand years. Forever.

This is what we call the **Davidic Covenant**, and it's different from the covenant at Sinai in one important way. The covenant at Sinai was **conditional**, meaning Israel had to obey in order to receive the blessings. But the Davidic Covenant was **unconditional**. God didn't say, "If your children are righteous, then maybe one of them will always be king." He said it would happen, period. Individual kings might be punished for wickedness, but the line of David on the throne would never be permanently broken.

Now, you might be thinking: "Wait, there's no king from David's family sitting on a throne in Jerusalem right now. So was God wrong?" Not at all. That's because this covenant was ultimately about someone much greater than any earthly king. It was about **Jesus Christ**.

In Luke 1:32-33, the angel Gabriel appeared to Mary and told her about the baby she would have: "The Lord God shall give unto him the throne of his father David: And he shall reign over the house of Jacob for ever; and of his kingdom there shall be no end." Jesus Christ is the ultimate fulfillment of the Davidic Covenant. He is the descendant of David who reigns forever, not over a small piece of land in the Middle East, but over all of heaven and earth.

Every covenant God made in the Old Testament points toward Christ, and the Davidic Covenant is a perfect example. God promised David an eternal kingdom, and He delivered on that promise through His Son. The throne of David will never end because the King who sits on it is the King of Kings.
