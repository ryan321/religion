### Lesson 5.2 -- Prophets as Covenant Enforcers

After God made covenants with Israel, He didn't just walk away and hope for the best. He sent **prophets**, messengers who spoke for God and reminded the people of their covenant promises. The great prophets of the Old Testament (**Isaiah**, **Jeremiah**, **Ezekiel**, and many others) all had the same basic job.

Here's something important to understand: the prophets did **not** invent a new religion. They didn't show up with brand-new ideas that nobody had heard before. Instead, they reminded Israel of the covenants and commandments God had already given them. They were like referees in a game. They didn't make up the rules, but they made sure everyone knew the rules and called out fouls when people broke them.

The prophets did three main things:

**First, they reminded Israel of existing promises.** When the people forgot their covenants or started drifting toward idol worship, the prophets pointed back to Sinai. They said things like, "Remember what God told you. Remember what you promised Him." Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel all spent large portions of their ministries calling Israel back to the covenant they had already made.

**Second, they warned of consequences.** This is the part people usually remember. The prophets warned that if Israel continued to break their covenants (worshipping idols, mistreating the poor, ignoring the Sabbath) then God would allow terrible things to happen. Enemies would conquer them. They would be scattered from their land. These weren't empty threats. When Israel refused to listen, those consequences came. Jeremiah watched Jerusalem be destroyed by Babylon. Ezekiel prophesied from exile.

**Third, they promised restoration.** Even in the middle of the harshest warnings, the prophets always included a message of hope. God would not abandon His people forever. He would gather them again. He would send a Messiah. He would make a new covenant. No matter how badly Israel failed, God's love and His plan would not fail.

Think of it this way: if the covenants are like a contract between God and Israel, then the prophets were like lawyers who showed up to say, "You signed this. Here's what it says. Here's what happens if you break it. And here's what happens when you come back."

The prophets didn't bring a new message. They brought the **same** message God had always given: return to your covenants, and God will bless you.

#### Questions

1. The prophets did not invent a new religion -- they reminded Israel of the __________ God had already given them.

2. When Israel broke their covenants, the prophets warned of __________ that would follow.

3. Even in the harshest warnings, the prophets always promised __________ -- that God would gather His people and send a Messiah.
