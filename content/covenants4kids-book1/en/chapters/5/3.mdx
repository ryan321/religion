### Lesson 5.3 -- The New Covenant Promised

By the time of the prophet Jeremiah, things were bad in Israel. The people had broken their covenants over and over again. They worshipped idols, ignored the poor, and turned away from God. The nation was about to be conquered by Babylon, and the people would be dragged away from their homeland into exile. It seemed like the covenant relationship between God and Israel was falling apart.

But right in the middle of all that darkness, God gave Jeremiah one of the most beautiful promises in all of scripture. It's found in Jeremiah 31:31-33:

"Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, that I will make a **new covenant** with the house of Israel... I will put my law in their inward parts, and **write it in their hearts**; and will be their God, and they shall be my people."

This is a game-changing promise. Let's break it down.

The old covenant at Sinai was written on **stone tablets**. It was external -- a set of rules carved into rock and kept in a box (the Ark of the Covenant). The people could read it, memorize it, and even carry it around, but that didn't mean it was in their hearts. They could follow the rules on the outside while being rebellious on the inside. And that's exactly what happened, again and again.

The new covenant would be different. God said He would write His law **in their hearts**. This means the covenant would be **internalized** -- not just rules you follow, but principles you believe and love. It would be **personal** -- each person would know God for themselves, not just through priests and prophets. And it would be **transformative** -- it would actually change who you are on the inside, not just what you do on the outside.

Think about the difference between a kid who doesn't steal because a parent is watching, and a kid who doesn't steal because they genuinely believe it's wrong. The first kid is following an external rule. The second kid has the law written in their heart. That's the difference God is describing.

This new covenant would come through the **Messiah** -- Jesus Christ. He would be the one to make it possible for God's law to move from stone tablets to human hearts. When Jesus came, He brought exactly this kind of covenant: one built on love, faith, and the Holy Ghost working inside you to change who you are.

Jeremiah's promise gave Israel hope that even though they had failed, God had not given up. A better covenant was coming.
