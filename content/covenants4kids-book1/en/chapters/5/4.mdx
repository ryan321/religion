### Lesson 5.4 -- How Old Testament Covenants Connect

You've now studied several covenants from the Old Testament, and they might seem like a bunch of separate deals God made with different people at different times. But when you step back and look at the big picture, something amazing appears: they are all **connected**, they all **build on each other**, and they all **point to Jesus Christ**.

Let's walk through them like milestones on a single road.

**Adam.** The covenant with Adam introduced the **gospel** itself. God taught Adam and Eve about the Fall, about sacrifice, about the promise of a Savior. This was the starting point, the first milestone on the road.

**Noah.** The covenant with Noah was about **preservation**. God promised to preserve the earth and never destroy it entirely by flood again. He set the rainbow as a sign. This covenant made sure the road itself would continue, that the world would keep going long enough for God's plan to unfold.

**Abraham.** The covenant with Abraham had three great promises: **posterity** (descendants as numerous as the stars), **priesthood** (the authority to act in God's name), and the **gospel** blessing going to all families of the earth. This covenant broadened the road and established the family through whom the Savior would come.

**Sinai.** The covenant at Sinai gave Israel the Law of Moses, which Paul called a **schoolmaster** to bring them to Christ. It was the training wheels, the detailed rules and rituals that taught obedience and pointed forward to the Messiah. This covenant trained the people to walk the road.

**David.** The covenant with David promised an **eternal throne**. A descendant of David would reign forever. This covenant identified *who* the Savior would be -- a king from David's line.

Do you see the pattern? Each covenant adds a new piece. Adam gets the gospel. Noah preserves the earth. Abraham establishes the family. Sinai trains the people. David identifies the king. And all of them point forward to the same destination: **Jesus Christ**.

These aren't separate stories. They are one story, God's story of bringing His children back to Him. Every covenant is a milestone on the same road, and that road leads to the Savior. When you understand this, the Old Testament stops being a confusing collection of ancient tales and becomes what it really is: a carefully planned journey toward the greatest event in history: the coming of Jesus Christ.
