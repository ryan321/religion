### Lesson 6.1 -- What the New and Everlasting Covenant Is

You've spent several chapters learning about individual covenants: the covenant with Adam, Noah, Abraham, Israel at Sinai, and David. Now it's time to learn about the one covenant that contains **all** of them. It's called the **New and Everlasting Covenant**.

In Doctrine and Covenants 66:2, the Lord says to a man named William McLellin: "Verily I say unto you, blessed are you for receiving mine **everlasting covenant**, even the fulness of my gospel." Notice that the Lord calls the everlasting covenant "the fulness of my gospel." That's the key to understanding what this covenant really is.

The New and Everlasting Covenant is not just one single promise or one single ordinance. It is the **whole package**, the complete collection of all gospel ordinances, covenants, laws, and principles that God has ever given to His children. Baptism is part of it. The sacrament is part of it. Temple covenants are part of it. The law of tithing, the Word of Wisdom, the commandment to love one another. All of it fits under this one big umbrella.

Think of it like this: imagine a giant umbrella. Under that umbrella are dozens of smaller items: baptism, confirmation, priesthood ordinances, temple ordinances, and every covenant you will ever make with God. The umbrella that holds them all together is the New and Everlasting Covenant. It's not one item under the umbrella. It **is** the umbrella.

But why is it called "**new**"? Because it has been **restored** in our day through the Prophet Joseph Smith. After centuries of apostasy, when the fulness of the gospel was lost from the earth, God brought it back. It's new to us because we're receiving it again.

And why is it called "**everlasting**"? Because the principles and truths it contains have existed from the very beginning. God taught Adam the gospel. He made covenants with Noah and Abraham. The truths are eternal; they have always been part of God's plan. They didn't start with Joseph Smith. They started with God, before the world was even created.

So the New and Everlasting Covenant is both brand new (restored in the last days) and incredibly old (the same plan God has always had). It is the fulness of the gospel -- everything God offers to bring His children home.
