### Lesson 6.2 -- Christ Fulfills the Law of Moses

When Jesus came to earth, some people worried that He was trying to throw away everything God had given to Moses. After all, He healed on the Sabbath, ate with sinners, and challenged the religious leaders at every turn. Was He getting rid of God's law?

Jesus answered that question directly in Matthew 5:17: "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to **fulfil**."

To **fulfil** the law means to complete it, to bring it to its intended destination. Think about a seed. When a seed grows into a tree, the seed isn't destroyed. It's fulfilled. It became what it was always meant to become. The Law of Moses was the seed, and the gospel of Jesus Christ is the tree.

So what changed when Christ fulfilled the law? Two things happened:

**First, the ritual laws ended.** Remember all those animal sacrifices, the washings, the detailed rules about clean and unclean foods? Those were symbols. They pointed forward to Christ. Once the real thing arrived, the symbols were no longer needed. You don't need a picture of someone after they walk into the room. The sacrifices of bulls and lambs stopped because the Lamb of God had come and offered the final sacrifice: Himself.

But the sacrificing didn't stop completely. It changed form. In 3 Nephi 9:19-20, the resurrected Christ told the people in the Americas: "Ye shall offer up unto me no more the shedding of blood... and ye shall offer for a sacrifice unto me a **broken heart and a contrite spirit**." Instead of offering an animal on an altar, we now offer our own hearts (our pride, our selfishness, our will) to God. That is the sacrifice He asks for today.

**Second, the moral laws remained and were deepened.** The commandments about honesty, kindness, faithfulness, and respect for God didn't go away. If anything, they got stronger. Jesus didn't say, "You don't have to be honest anymore." He said, "Let your yes be yes and your no be no" -- be so honest that you don't even need to swear an oath (Matthew 5:37).

Christ fulfilled the Law of Moses perfectly. He didn't throw it away. He completed it and gave us something higher and deeper in its place.

#### Questions

1. Jesus said in Matthew 5:17 that He came not to destroy the law but to __________ it.

2. When Christ came, the ritual laws ended but the __________ laws remained and were deepened.

3. What do you think it means to offer God "a broken heart and a contrite spirit" instead of an animal sacrifice? What might that look like in your daily life?
