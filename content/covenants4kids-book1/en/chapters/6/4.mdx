### Lesson 6.4 -- The Atonement: The Heart of Every Covenant

Every covenant you've studied in this book, from Adam to Noah to Abraham to Sinai to David, has one thing in common. None of them could save anyone on their own. Not a single one. The power behind every covenant, the thing that makes all of them work, is the **Atonement of Jesus Christ**.

Think of it like a tree. Each covenant is a **branch**, important, connected, reaching upward. But the **trunk** that holds every branch in place, that gives life and strength to all of them, is the Atonement. Cut down the trunk, and every branch falls. Without the Atonement, no covenant could bring anyone back to God.

What exactly is the Atonement? It's what Jesus Christ did in the Garden of **Gethsemane** and on the **cross** to pay for the sins of every person who has ever lived or ever will live. In Gethsemane, He suffered so intensely that He bled from every pore (Luke 22:44; D&C 19:18). On the cross, He gave His life. And three days later, He rose from the dead, breaking the power of death forever.

But the Atonement covers more than just sin. Alma 7:11-12 teaches that Christ took upon Himself "the pains and the sicknesses of his people... their infirmities... the sins of his people." He experienced every kind of suffering (physical pain, loneliness, grief, sickness, temptation) so that He would know exactly how to help you. Whatever you're going through, He has already been through it. He understands.

Now think about why the Atonement is the heart of every covenant. When you are baptized, you promise to follow Christ, but it's the Atonement that washes your sins away. When you take the sacrament, you renew your covenants, but it's the Atonement that gives you the chance to start fresh. When you make temple covenants, you promise to consecrate your life -- but it's the Atonement that makes eternal life possible. Every covenant you make is an agreement to access the power of the Atonement in your life.

Without the Atonement, baptism would just be getting wet. The sacrament would just be bread and water. Temple covenants would just be words. It's the Atonement that fills every covenant with real, saving power.

This is the most important lesson in this entire book: Jesus Christ is not just part of the covenant story. He **is** the covenant story. Every promise God has ever made to His children leads to Christ, depends on Christ, and is fulfilled through Christ. The Atonement is not one branch among many. It is the trunk that holds everything together.
