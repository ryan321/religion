### Lesson 7.1 -- What Baptism Is

If you have been baptized (or are getting ready to be) you have taken part in something very important. Baptism is the **first saving ordinance** of the gospel. It is the gate that opens the covenant path, and everything else on that path follows from it.

The prophet Nephi described baptism as the "gate by which ye should enter" (2 Nephi 31:17). Think of a gate at the entrance to a beautiful garden. You can see the garden from outside, but you can't walk through it until you go through the gate. Baptism is that gate. It is the way you officially begin your covenant journey with God.

There are several things that make baptism what it is. First, it must be performed by someone who holds the **Aaronic Priesthood** and has the proper authority from God. Not just anyone can baptize; it requires God's permission and power. Second, baptism must be done by **immersion**, which means your whole body goes under the water and comes back up. This isn't just a tradition. It has deep meaning.

When you go under the water, it symbolizes the **death and burial** of Jesus Christ. When you come up out of the water, it symbolizes His **resurrection**, His rising from the grave to new life. So every baptism is a reminder of what Jesus did for you. It also represents your own choice to leave behind your old life of mistakes and start fresh as a follower of Christ.

Jesus Himself was baptized, even though He was perfect and had no sins to wash away. When John the Baptist hesitated, Jesus told him it was necessary "to fulfil all righteousness" (Matthew 3:15). If the Son of God needed to be baptized to follow His Father's plan, then baptism matters for all of us. Jesus showed us the way and expected us to follow.

Baptism is not just getting wet. It is not just a ceremony. It is an ordinance -- a sacred act performed by priesthood authority that creates a real covenant between you and God. When you are baptized, you are telling Heavenly Father that you choose Him, that you want to follow His Son, and that you are ready to walk the covenant path. And He responds with promises of His own, which you will learn about in the lessons ahead.

This is where the journey begins.
