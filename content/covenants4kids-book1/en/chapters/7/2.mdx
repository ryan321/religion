### Lesson 7.2 -- What You Promise

When you are baptized, you make real promises to God. These are not small promises. They are covenants -- and they define how you live every single day.

The best description of these promises comes from the Book of Mormon, when a prophet named Alma taught a group of believers at a place called the Waters of Mormon. In Mosiah 18:8-10, Alma explained what it means to enter the baptismal covenant. Let's look at these promises one by one.

First, you promise to "come into the **fold of God**." This means you are joining God's family, His people. You are saying, "I belong to God. I am one of His." It is like putting on a team jersey. You are declaring which side you are on.

Second, you promise to "**bear one another's burdens**, that they may be light." This means you help carry the heavy things that other people face: their sadness, their struggles, their hard days. When a friend at school is having a terrible week, and you sit with them and listen, you are keeping this promise.

Third, you promise to "**mourn with those that mourn**" and "**comfort those that stand in need of comfort**." When someone is hurting, you don't ignore them or walk away. You feel sad with them. You care. And then you do something about it. You comfort them. This promise means your heart is turned outward, toward other people.

Fourth, you promise to "stand as **witnesses of God** at all times and in all things, and in all places." This is a big one. A witness is someone who tells the truth about what they know. You are promising to stand up for God, not just at church on Sunday, but everywhere. At school, at home, with friends, online. At all times and in all places.

Fifth, you promise to **keep God's commandments**. This one is straightforward. You commit to obeying what God has asked.

And finally, you **take upon yourself the name of Christ**. This means you represent Him. When people see you, they should see someone trying to live the way Jesus lived.

These are serious promises. But here is the beautiful part: God never asks you to give something without offering something even greater in return. When you keep these promises, He pours out blessings that are beyond anything you could earn on your own. That is the subject of the next lesson.
