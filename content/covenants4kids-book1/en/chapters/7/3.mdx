### Lesson 7.3 -- What God Promises

You have learned what you promise when you are baptized. Now let's talk about what God promises in return, because His side of the covenant is extraordinary.

The most important promise God makes at baptism is the gift of the **Holy Ghost**. After you are baptized, you are confirmed a member of the Church and given this gift. The sacrament prayer in Doctrine and Covenants 20:77 says that those who keep their covenants will "always have his Spirit to be with them." Think about that. *Always*. Not just on Sundays. Not just when you pray. Always.

The Holy Ghost is not just a feeling or an influence. He is a **member of the Godhead**, a real, divine being. When God promises you the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost, He is giving you a personal guide who can warn you of danger, comfort you when you are sad, teach you truth, and help you make good decisions. That is an incredible gift. You are never truly alone when you are living your covenant.

But the promises go further. In Mosiah 18:9, Alma taught that those who keep the baptismal covenant will be "numbered among those of the **first resurrection**." The first resurrection is the resurrection of the righteous, those who inherit the highest blessings God has to offer. This is a promise about your eternal future.

God also promises **redemption**. Redemption means being rescued from sin and spiritual death. Because of Jesus Christ's Atonement, you can be forgiven when you make mistakes and repent. Your baptismal covenant connects you directly to that redeeming power. It is the channel through which Christ's grace flows into your life.

And the ultimate promise? **Eternal life**, living with God and your family forever. This is what the scriptures call the "greatest of all the gifts of God" (D&C 14:7). It doesn't come all at once, and it requires a lifetime of faithfulness, but it begins at baptism. The path starts at the gate.

Now here is something worth thinking about: you promise to mourn with those who mourn and keep the commandments. God promises you eternal life and the constant companionship of a member of the Godhead. That is not an even trade. It is overwhelmingly generous. God always gives more than He asks. That is who He is, and that is the nature of every covenant He makes.
