### Lesson 7.4 -- Baptism as the Beginning

Some people think of baptism as the finish line -- the big event that marks the end of preparation. But the scriptures teach the exact opposite. Baptism is not the end. It is the **beginning**.

Nephi made this clear when he called baptism the "gate by which ye should enter" (2 Nephi 31:17). A gate is not a destination. Nobody walks up to a gate and says, "Well, I'm here. I'm done." A gate is an entrance -- you walk through it to get somewhere. Nephi continued: "After ye have gotten into this strait and narrow path, I would ask if all is done? Behold, I say unto you, Nay" (2 Nephi 31:19). He could not have been more direct. After baptism, the work continues.

So what comes after the gate? Immediately after baptism, you receive **confirmation** and the gift of the **Holy Ghost**. This ordinance is performed by Melchizedek Priesthood holders who lay their hands on your head and give you the right to the constant companionship of the Spirit. Baptism gets you through the gate, and the Holy Ghost becomes your guide on the path ahead.

As you grow older, more covenants and ordinances follow. Young men receive the **priesthood** -- first the Aaronic Priesthood, and later the Melchizedek Priesthood. All faithful members look forward to **temple ordinances**, including the endowment and sealing. Each of these builds on the foundation that baptism laid. They are all part of the same covenant path, and each step brings you closer to God.

But here is something beautiful: you do not have to wait for those future ordinances to live your covenant. Every single week, you can **renew your baptismal covenant** through the **sacrament**. When you take the bread and water on Sunday, you are recommitting to the same promises you made at baptism -- to take Christ's name, always remember Him, and keep His commandments. And God renews His promise that you can always have His Spirit with you.

The sacrament means you never have to feel like you have fallen too far behind. You make mistakes during the week -- everyone does. But Sunday comes, and you can start fresh. The covenant is renewed. The Spirit returns in full strength. You are back on the path.

Baptism opened the gate. The sacrament keeps you walking. And the path leads all the way home to your Heavenly Father.
