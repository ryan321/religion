### Lesson 8.1 -- What the Sacrament Is

Every Sunday, something sacred happens in meetinghouses around the world. Trays of bread and water are blessed and passed to the congregation. It might look simple -- just small pieces of bread and tiny cups of water. But the sacrament is the most sacred ordinance you participate in on a weekly basis, and understanding what it really is can change how you experience it.

The sacrament is **bread and water** that are blessed by holders of the **priesthood**. The bread represents the **body** of Jesus Christ, and the water represents His **blood**. Together, they remind you of what Jesus did for you -- He suffered, bled, and died so that you could be forgiven of your sins and live again after death. Every piece of bread and every cup of water points back to that sacrifice.

Jesus Himself started this ordinance. At the **Last Supper**, the night before He was crucified, Jesus gathered His apostles and broke bread with them. He said, "This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me." He then took a cup and said, "This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you" (Luke 22:19-20). He was telling them -- and us -- to keep doing this so we would never forget what He was about to do.

After His resurrection, Jesus visited the Nephites in the Americas and gave them the same ordinance. In 3 Nephi 18:1-12, He broke bread, blessed it, and gave it to the people. Then He did the same with wine. He commanded them to do this regularly as a way to remember Him. The fact that Jesus instituted the sacrament in two different parts of the world shows how important it is to Him.

Today, we use water instead of wine, but the meaning is the same. The sacrament is not just a tradition or a routine. It is an **ordinance** -- a sacred act performed by priesthood authority that carries real spiritual power. When the bread and water are blessed and you take them, something real is happening between you and God.

Every Sunday, you have the chance to sit quietly, think about Jesus Christ, and participate in the very ordinance He created. It is a gift that most of us have so often that we can forget how remarkable it really is.
