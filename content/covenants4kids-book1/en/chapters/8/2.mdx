### Lesson 8.2 -- The Sacrament Prayers

Have you ever noticed that the sacrament prayers sound the same every single week? That is not an accident. The sacrament prayers are the only prayers in the Church that must be said **word for word**, exactly as they are written. If a priest makes a mistake, he has to start over. That is how important every word is.

You can find these prayers in two places: **Doctrine and Covenants 20:77 and 79**, and **Moroni chapters 4 and 5**. The fact that they appear in both the Doctrine and Covenants and the Book of Mormon shows that God wants these words preserved carefully.

So what do the prayers actually say? Let's break them down. When the priest blesses the bread, he asks Heavenly Father to bless and sanctify it for the souls of all who partake. Then he states the three promises that you make:

**First**, you promise to **take upon yourself the name of Jesus Christ**. This means you represent Him. You carry His name. When people see how you act, they are seeing someone who claims to follow Christ.

**Second**, you promise to **always remember Him**. Not just during the sacrament. Not just on Sundays. Always. In every choice, in every moment, you try to keep Jesus in your thoughts and let His example guide you.

**Third**, you promise to **keep His commandments which He has given you**. This is your commitment to obey, to live the way God has asked you to live.

And here is the guarantee that comes with those three promises: God promises that you will "**always have his Spirit to be with them**." That is the blessing of the Holy Ghost, the constant companionship of a member of the Godhead as your guide, comforter, and teacher.

The prayer over the water is similar. It asks that those who drink it do so "in remembrance of the blood of the Son" and reaffirms the same promises and the same guarantee.

These prayers are a covenant, spoken out loud, every single week. Three promises from you. One enormous promise from God. And the words never change because the covenant never changes. God is perfectly consistent, and He wants you to hear the same promise and make the same commitment every Sunday so it sinks deep into your heart.
