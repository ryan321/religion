### Lesson 8.3 -- Renewing Your Covenant

Here is one of the most practical and powerful things about the sacrament: every Sunday, when you take the bread and water, you are **renewing your baptismal covenant**. You are recommitting to the same promises you made when you were baptized, and God is recommitting to the same blessings He promised you.

Think about why that matters. Nobody is perfect. During the week, you might lose your temper, say something unkind, forget to pray, or make a choice that you know was wrong. Mistakes happen. Sin happens. And when it does, it can feel like you have drifted away from God, like the connection between you and Him has gotten a little weaker.

The sacrament is God's way of saying, "Come back. Start again." You do not need to be rebaptized every time you make a mistake. Instead, God gave you the sacrament so you could **repent**, **recommit**, and **receive the Spirit** again, every single week. It is like a reset button on your covenant relationship with God.

But that reset button only works if you actually use it. Renewing your covenant is not automatic. Just sitting in the pew while the tray goes by is not the same as truly renewing your promise. To really renew, you need to do some things in your heart and mind.

First, **think about your week**. What went well? Where did you fall short? What do you want to do better? This is a form of self-examination, honestly looking at your own life.

Second, **repent**. This means you feel genuine sorrow for the things you did wrong, and you make a real decision to change. Repentance is not just feeling bad. It is turning around and heading in a better direction.

Third, **recommit**. As you take the bread and water, silently tell Heavenly Father that you are ready to try again. You will take Christ's name. You will remember Him. You will keep His commandments. You mean it, even though you know you are not perfect yet.

When you do these things, something real happens. The Holy Ghost returns in full strength. Your spiritual slate is clean. Your covenant with God is renewed, and His promises are active in your life again.

The sacrament is not just a nice tradition. It is the weekly lifeline of your covenant relationship with God.
