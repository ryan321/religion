### Lesson 8.4 -- Making the Sacrament Matter

Because the sacrament happens every Sunday, it can be easy to take it for granted. The trays come around, you take a piece of bread, you take a cup of water, and then it is over. If you are not careful, the whole thing can feel like just another part of the meeting: routine, ordinary, forgettable.

But think about what is actually happening during those few minutes. You are **making a covenant with God**. You are **remembering the sacrifice of Jesus Christ**: His suffering in Gethsemane, His death on the cross, and His resurrection. And you are **receiving a promise** that the Holy Ghost will be with you. Those are not ordinary things. Those might be the most important minutes of your entire week.

So how do you make the sacrament matter? Here are some ideas.

**Prepare before the meeting.** Think about your week before you even walk through the chapel doors. What do you need to repent of? What blessings have you received? Coming to sacrament meeting with a ready heart makes a big difference.

**Listen to the prayers carefully.** You have heard them many times, but try listening as if it were the first time. Notice the three promises you are making. Notice the promise God is making to you. Let the words land.

**Think about Jesus.** The whole point of the sacrament is to remember Him. During the quiet moments, think about a scripture story about Jesus. Think about something He taught. Think about how His Atonement has helped you personally. Let your mind focus on Him, not on what you are doing after church.

**Make it personal.** Talk to Heavenly Father in your mind during the sacrament. Tell Him what you are grateful for. Tell Him where you need help. Recommit to the specific things you want to do better this week. Make the covenant personal and specific, not vague and general.

**Be reverent.** The sacrament is a sacred time. Being quiet and still is not just a rule for little kids. It is a way of showing respect for something holy. When you are reverent, you create space for the Spirit to speak to you.

The sacrament is offered to you fifty-two times a year. That is fifty-two chances to reconnect with God, to be forgiven, and to start fresh. Do not waste them. Make each one count.
