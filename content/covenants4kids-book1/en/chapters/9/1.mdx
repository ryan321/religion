### Lesson 9.1 -- What the Priesthood Is

You have probably heard the word "priesthood" many times at church. But what is it, exactly? The priesthood is the **authority to act in God's name**. It is God's own power, given to people on earth so they can perform ordinances, lead His Church, and bless others.

Think about it this way. If a police officer tells you to stop, you stop -- not because the officer is bigger or stronger than you, but because the officer has been given **authority** by the government. The officer represents something larger than herself. The priesthood works in a similar way. When a priesthood holder baptizes someone or blesses the sacrament, he is not acting on his own authority. He is acting on **God's** authority, because God gave him permission to do so.

There are **two priesthoods** in the Church, and each one was restored to the earth in a specific way.

The **Aaronic Priesthood** is sometimes called the "lesser" priesthood, but that does not mean it is unimportant. It holds the keys to baptism, the sacrament, and the ministering of angels. It was restored on May 15, 1829, when **John the Baptist** appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery and laid his hands on their heads. John the Baptist had held this priesthood during his life on earth, and he came as a resurrected being to pass it on.

The **Melchizedek Priesthood** is the "higher" priesthood. It holds the authority for confirmation, priesthood blessings, temple ordinances, and the leadership of the Church. It was restored shortly after the Aaronic Priesthood by the apostles **Peter, James, and John** -- three of Jesus Christ's original twelve apostles. They appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery and conferred this higher authority upon them.

One important thing to understand: the priesthood is **not a personal possession**. Nobody "owns" the priesthood the way you own a bicycle or a backpack. It is a **stewardship** -- something entrusted to you by God for the purpose of serving others. A priesthood holder does not have special power for himself. He has a sacred responsibility to serve, bless, and minister to others using God's authority.

The priesthood exists for one reason: to bring God's children back to Him through ordinances, covenants, and service.
