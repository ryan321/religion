### Lesson 9.2 -- The Oath and Covenant

In Doctrine and Covenants 84:33-44, the Lord reveals something called the **oath and covenant of the priesthood**. This is one of the most powerful passages of scripture in all of the standard works, and it contains the most sweeping promise God has ever made to His children.

Here is how it works. Like every covenant, it has two sides: your part and God's part.

**Your part:** Those who receive the Melchizedek Priesthood make two commitments. First, they covenant to **magnify their callings**. That means they promise to take seriously whatever responsibilities God gives them, to serve faithfully, not halfway or grudgingly. Second, they covenant to be **faithful** and to "live by every word that proceedeth forth from the mouth of God" (D&C 84:44). They commit to a life of obedience and diligence.

**God's part:** And here is where it gets breathtaking. God does not just promise to help or bless. He promises -- by **oath** -- to give the faithful "**all that my Father hath**" (D&C 84:38). Read that again. All that the Father has. Everything. Every power, every glory, every blessing that God Himself possesses. That is the promise.

An oath is the strongest kind of promise that exists. When God gives an oath, He is putting His perfect honor behind the promise. He cannot break it. He will not break it. It is guaranteed by His own nature. That is why this is called the oath *and* covenant -- it is a covenant on your side and an oath on God's side.

No other promise in all of scripture is as vast as this one. God is offering to share everything He has with those who are faithful to the priesthood. Think about what that means. He is not offering you a reward or a gift. He is offering you **everything**: a fulness of His glory and power, eternal life, and a place in His presence forever.

But there is a warning too. The Lord says that breaking this covenant after receiving it is a very serious matter (D&C 84:41). The greater the promise, the greater the responsibility. God takes this covenant seriously, and so should everyone who enters into it.

The oath and covenant of the priesthood is not just for adults or for missionaries. It is something every young person should understand now, so that when the time comes, you are ready to receive it with a full heart.
