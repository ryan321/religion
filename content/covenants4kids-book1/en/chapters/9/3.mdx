### Lesson 9.3 -- What It Means to Magnify a Calling

The oath and covenant of the priesthood says you must "magnify" your calling. But what does that actually mean? The word "magnify" means to make something larger, to enlarge it. When you look through a magnifying glass, small things appear bigger. To magnify a calling means to make it bigger than it might seem: to grow into it, to take it seriously, and to give it your full heart.

Magnifying a calling is **not just showing up**. Think about the difference between a deacon who passes the sacrament while daydreaming about lunch and a deacon who passes the sacrament reverently, understanding that he is helping people renew their covenant with God. Both of them showed up. But only one of them is magnifying his calling.

Here are some things that magnifying a calling looks like in real life.

**Serving with your full heart.** When you have a responsibility at church -- whether it is passing the sacrament, preparing the bread, giving a talk, or helping clean the building -- you do it like it matters. Because it does. You put effort and care into it, not because someone is watching, but because you know it is sacred work.

**Taking it seriously.** A calling is not a chore. It is not like taking out the trash or doing homework. A calling is a **sacred trust**. God, through His leaders, has asked you to do something specific. When you take it seriously, you prepare for it, you think about it, and you try to do your best.

**Growing into the responsibility.** Sometimes a calling feels bigger than you. That is normal. Nobody starts out knowing how to do everything perfectly. To magnify a calling means you are willing to grow, to learn, to improve, and to stretch yourself. God does not call people who are already perfect for the job. He calls people who are willing to become what the job requires.

**Treating it as a sacred trust, not a burden.** There is a big difference between thinking "I have to do this" and thinking "I get to do this." When you see your calling as something God has entrusted to you, as a sign of His confidence in you, it changes how you approach it. You stop seeing it as a burden and start seeing it as an honor.

When you magnify a calling, you are not just doing a task. You are participating in God's work. And God notices. He blesses those who serve faithfully, not because they are perfect, but because they are willing.
