### Lesson 9.4 -- The Priesthood and You

The priesthood is not just something that a few people have and everyone else watches from the sidelines. The priesthood is meant to bless **everyone** -- every member of God's family.

For **young men**, the priesthood becomes personal at age twelve, when they can receive the **Aaronic Priesthood** and be ordained as deacons. This is a real ordination with real authority. A twelve-year-old deacon who passes the sacrament is acting with the same priesthood authority that John the Baptist held. That is a remarkable thing. As young men grow older, they advance to teachers and then priests, receiving greater responsibilities and authority. Eventually, they prepare to receive the **Melchizedek Priesthood**, which carries the oath and covenant you learned about in this chapter.

For **young women**, the priesthood is equally important, even though their relationship with it looks different. Every ordinance that blesses your life (your baptism, your confirmation, the sacrament you take each week, priesthood blessings when you are sick or struggling) comes through priesthood authority. The priesthood is not something that exists apart from you. It exists *for* you. Its entire purpose is to serve, bless, and strengthen God's children, and that includes you completely.

There is another important truth: the **temple** gives priesthood power to all worthy members. When you go to the temple and receive the endowment, you are given access to priesthood power and blessings that go beyond what any one person holds on their own. President Russell M. Nelson has taught that God's power is available to every covenant-keeping member, male or female. The priesthood is not just about who holds an office. It is about God's power flowing into the lives of all His faithful children.

So what does all of this mean for you right now?

If you are a young man, **prepare**. Learn what the priesthood is. Take your ordinations seriously. Practice magnifying your callings now, because the habits you build as a deacon will shape the kind of elder, husband, and father you become.

If you are a young woman, **understand** that the priesthood serves you and that you will access its full power through your own covenants. Your role in God's plan is not secondary; it is essential.

For everyone, the message is the same: the priesthood is God's power on earth, and it is meant for you. Everything on the covenant path (baptism, the sacrament, confirmation, the temple) is made possible by the priesthood. It is the framework that holds all of God's covenants together.
