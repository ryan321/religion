### Lesson 1.1 -- The School of the Prophets

Have you ever been part of a really special class -- maybe a scripture study group or a priesthood quorum meeting where you felt the Spirit extra strong? Back in 1833, the early leaders of the Church had something like that, and it was called the **School of the Prophets**.

The Church was still very young. Joseph Smith had only organized it three years earlier, in 1830. The members were growing in number, and missionaries were being sent out to preach the gospel. But here was the problem -- many of these leaders and missionaries didn't have a lot of formal education. They loved the Lord and wanted to serve, but they needed to understand the gospel more deeply so they could teach it clearly to others.

So the Lord gave a revelation. In **Doctrine and Covenants 88:117-141**, He told the Saints to organize a "school of the prophets." This wasn't a regular school with desks and textbooks. It was a sacred gathering where Church leaders would come together to study the gospel, learn from one another, and prepare themselves spiritually and intellectually to go out and preach.

The school met in **Kirtland, Ohio**, which was the headquarters of the Church at that time. The men who attended included Joseph Smith, Sidney Rigdon, Frederick G. Williams, and other early leaders. They studied scripture, theology, history, and even languages. The Lord wanted His servants to be well-prepared -- not just filled with the Spirit, but also filled with **knowledge**.

Think of it this way: imagine you were asked to give a talk in Primary about faith. You'd want to feel the Spirit, right? But you'd also want to *understand* what faith really means so you could explain it well. That's exactly what the School of the Prophets was about -- combining spiritual power with deep understanding.

This school became the setting where some of the most important theological teachings in Church history were developed. The leaders didn't just read scriptures together -- they asked big questions and worked together to find answers. They wanted to know: What is faith, really? What do we need to know about God? How does faith actually work?

The answers to those questions became the **Lectures on Faith**, which we'll learn about in the next lesson. But it all started here -- in a small room in Kirtland, with humble men who wanted to learn everything they could about God so they could share it with the world.

That desire to learn and grow is something you can have too. Every time you study the scriptures, attend church, or ask a question about the gospel, you're following in the footsteps of those early leaders.
