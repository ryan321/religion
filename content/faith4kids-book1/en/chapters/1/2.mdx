### Lesson 1.2 -- What the Lectures Are

Now that you know about the School of the Prophets, let's talk about what came out of it: the **Lectures on Faith**.

During the winter of 1834-35, **Joseph Smith** and **Sidney Rigdon** worked together to prepare a series of **seven theological lectures**. These weren't just casual talks or simple devotionals. They were carefully organized, **systematic** teachings about faith: what it is, how it works, and what you need to know about God in order to have it.

Think about it like this: if someone asked you, "What is faith?" you might say, "It's believing in something you can't see." That's a good start! But the Lectures on Faith go much deeper. They break faith down step by step, almost like a science textbook breaks down how plants grow. Each lecture builds on the one before it, teaching you more and more about how faith really operates.

The seven lectures cover topics like:
- What faith is and why it matters
- What you need to know about God
- What God's character is like
- How knowing God leads to real faith
- What faith can actually do in your life

When these lectures were finished, the Church considered them so important that they were included in the **1835 edition of the Doctrine and Covenants**. In fact, the book was divided into two parts. The first part was called the **"Doctrine"** portion, and that was the Lectures on Faith! The second part was called the **"Covenants"** portion, and those were the revelations we still have in the Doctrine and Covenants today.

So when early members talked about the "Doctrine and Covenants," the *doctrine* they were referring to was literally the Lectures on Faith. That's how central these teachings were to the early Church.

The lectures were used to teach missionaries and leaders. They gave people a framework, a way of thinking about faith that was clear, logical, and deeply scriptural. They quoted the Bible, the Book of Mormon, and modern revelation to build a complete picture of what it means to truly have faith in God.

Even though these lectures were prepared by Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon, they reflect the collective learning and revelation that came through the School of the Prophets. They represent the best thinking of early Church leaders, guided by the Spirit, about one of the most important topics in the gospel.

In the next lesson, we'll talk about what happened to these lectures and why they're not in your Doctrine and Covenants today.
