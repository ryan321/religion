### Lesson 1.3 -- Why They Were Removed

If the Lectures on Faith were so important that they were included as the "doctrine" part of the Doctrine and Covenants, why aren't they in your copy today? That's a great question, and the answer might surprise you.

In **1921**, the Church published a new edition of the Doctrine and Covenants. When they did, the Lectures on Faith were taken out. But here's the important thing to understand: they were **not removed because they were false**. Nobody said, "These teachings are wrong." Instead, the committee that made the decision explained that the lectures were **reclassified**. They said the lectures were just that (*lectures*) and not *revelations* from God in the same way that the sections of the Doctrine and Covenants are revelations.

Think of it this way: your bishop might give an amazing talk in sacrament meeting that teaches true doctrine and is filled with the Spirit. But that talk wouldn't be added to the scriptures, because it's not a *revelation* in the same category as, say, Doctrine and Covenants Section 76. It doesn't mean the talk is wrong. It just means it's a different kind of teaching.

That's basically what happened with the Lectures on Faith. The Church decided that since they were theological lectures rather than direct revelations, they belonged in a different category. They were never condemned, criticized, or called incorrect by Church leaders.

In fact, many Church leaders have praised the Lectures on Faith over the years. **Elder Bruce R. McConkie**, an apostle, once said that the Lectures on Faith contain **"some of the best doctrine we have"** in the entire Church. That's a powerful statement! He encouraged members to study them carefully.

Other leaders and scholars have also pointed to the lectures as an incredibly valuable resource for understanding faith. They've been reprinted many times and are still available for anyone who wants to read them.

So why does this matter for you? Because sometimes people hear that the Lectures on Faith were "removed" and they assume that means something is wrong with them. Now you know the real story. They were reclassified (moved from one category to another), but their teachings remain just as true and just as powerful as they were when Joseph Smith first delivered them in the School of the Prophets.

You can think of the Lectures on Faith as a hidden treasure in Church history. They're not hidden because anyone is trying to keep them from you. They're just not as well known as they used to be. And that's one reason we're studying them together. These lectures have amazing things to teach you about faith, about God, and about your own potential.
