### Lesson 10.1 -- The Pattern of Sacrifice

Have you ever had to give up something you really wanted in order to do what was right? Maybe you skipped a fun activity to help someone who needed you. That feeling of choosing the harder thing -- that's what sacrifice is all about. And according to the Lectures on Faith, sacrifice has been part of God's plan from the very beginning.

It started with **Adam**. After Adam and Eve left the Garden of Eden, the Lord commanded Adam to offer the **firstlings of his flocks** as sacrifices. In **Moses 5:5**, we learn that Adam obeyed, even though he didn't fully understand why at first. An angel later explained that the sacrifice was "a similitude of the sacrifice of the Only Begotten of the Father." Adam's offerings pointed forward to Jesus Christ.

Then came **Abraham**. In one of the most powerful stories in all of scripture, God asked Abraham to sacrifice his son **Isaac** on Mount Moriah (**Genesis 22**). Imagine how hard that must have been. Isaac was the son Abraham had waited decades to receive. But Abraham trusted God completely. He was willing to give up the thing he loved most -- and because of his willingness, God provided a ram in the thicket and blessed Abraham beyond anything he could have imagined.

After that, the **Law of Moses** established a system of **daily sacrifices** in the tabernacle and later the temple. Animals were offered morning and evening. These sacrifices weren't random rituals -- every one of them was meant to teach the people about the coming Savior and His sacrifice.

And then came the **ultimate sacrifice**. Jesus Christ -- the Lamb of God -- offered Himself as a sacrifice for all of us. He suffered in Gethsemane, bled from every pore, and gave His life on the cross. His was the sacrifice that all the others had been pointing to.

Do you see the pattern? Sacrifice is woven into **every covenant and every dispensation**. From Adam to Abraham to Moses to Christ, God has always asked His people to give something up as a way of showing their trust in Him. Sacrifice isn't a punishment -- it's a **mechanism for developing and proving faith**. When you sacrifice, you show God that you trust Him more than you trust your own comfort. And that is exactly how faith grows.

Every prophet who ever had real faith also made real sacrifices. That pattern hasn't changed. It's the same for you today.
