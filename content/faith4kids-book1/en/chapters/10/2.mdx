### Lesson 10.2 -- What You Give Up You Get Back

Here's something that might surprise you about sacrifice: when you give something to God, He doesn't just take it and walk away. He gives you something **better** in return. Every single time. The scriptures are full of examples, and once you see the pattern, you'll never look at sacrifice the same way again.

Let's start with **Abraham**. God asked him to sacrifice his only covenant son, Isaac. Abraham was willing to do it. He bound Isaac, laid him on the altar, and raised the knife. But God stopped him and provided a ram instead (**Genesis 22:12-13**). Abraham got Isaac back, alive and well. But that wasn't all. Because of Abraham's willingness, God promised him an **eternal posterity** -- descendants as numerous as the stars in the sky and the sand on the seashore. Abraham gave up his son for a few agonizing hours and received him back, plus blessings that would last forever.

Now think about the **early Latter-day Saints**. They were driven from their homes in Kirtland, Missouri, and Nauvoo. They lost houses, farms, and businesses. They gave up everything they had built. But what did they receive? They received **temples**, sacred places where families could be sealed together for eternity. The homes they lost were temporary. The blessings they received were permanent.

And then there's **Joseph Smith**. He sacrificed more than almost anyone. He was mocked, beaten, imprisoned, and eventually **gave his life** at Carthage Jail. But what did he receive in return? An **eternal glory** that cannot be measured! The Lord told Joseph in **Doctrine and Covenants 135:3** that he had done more for the salvation of the world than any other man except Jesus Christ. Joseph's sacrifice wasn't the end of his story. It was the gateway to something far greater.

Do you see the lesson here? God doesn't ask you to sacrifice in order to make you poor or empty. He asks you to sacrifice to **make room for something better**. When your hands are full of the things you're clinging to, there's no room for God to place His blessings in them. But when you open your hands and let go, He fills them with things you never could have earned on your own.

This is one of the most important lessons in the Lectures on Faith. Sacrifice isn't loss; it's **exchange**. And God always gives more than He takes.

The next time you're asked to give something up for the gospel, remember Abraham, the early Saints, and Joseph. What they gave up, they got back, and then some.
