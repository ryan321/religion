### Lesson 10.4 -- Living the Law of Sacrifice Today

You might be thinking, "Okay, sacrifice was important for Adam, Abraham, and the early Saints, but what about me? I'm not being asked to put a lamb on an altar or leave my home and walk across the plains." And you're right. We don't practice animal sacrifice anymore. Christ's Atonement fulfilled that part of the law. But the **law of sacrifice** itself? That's still very much alive.

Today, sacrifice looks different, but it's just as real.

Think about the people in your ward who serve in **callings**. The bishop spends hours every week meeting with members, attending meetings, and ministering to people who are struggling. The Primary teachers prepare lessons and show up every Sunday ready to teach. Your parents might serve in the Young Men or Young Women programs, or as ward missionaries, or in the temple. All of that takes **time**, and time is one of the most valuable things we have.

What about **tithing and fast offerings**? When your family pays ten percent of their income to the Lord, that's money they could have spent on something else. When they fast and donate the money they would have spent on meals, they're giving up something real to help people in need. That's sacrifice.

And then there's the kind of sacrifice that might hit closest to home for you: **standing up for what you believe** even when other people don't agree. Living the **Word of Wisdom** means saying no to things that lots of other kids might be trying. Keeping the **law of chastity** means making choices that the world might think are old-fashioned. Studying your **scriptures** when you'd rather be playing video games or watching shows takes real effort.

None of these sacrifices are dramatic. Nobody is going to write a story in the scriptures about the time you turned off your phone to read the Book of Mormon. But every single one of them is **real**. And every single one of them builds your faith.

That's what the Lectures on Faith are teaching. Sacrifice isn't just a historical concept. It's a daily practice. Every time you choose the Lord's way over the easy way, you're exercising faith. And faith that is exercised through sacrifice becomes faith with **power**.

You don't have to do something huge. You just have to do something honest, something daily, and something for the right reasons. That's how ordinary people develop extraordinary faith.
