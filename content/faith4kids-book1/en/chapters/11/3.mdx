### Lesson 11.3 -- Faith and Knowledge

Here's something about faith that many people don't realize: **faith doesn't stay faith forever**. If you exercise faith the right way, it eventually turns into something even stronger -- **knowledge**.

Think about this. When you pray for the first time and ask God if He's really there, you're exercising faith. You don't know for sure yet. You're choosing to believe and act on that belief. But then something happens -- you feel the Holy Ghost. You get a warm, peaceful feeling. You sense that someone is listening. At that moment, your faith in prayer starts to become **knowledge**. You prayed, you received an answer, and now you **know** that God hears you.

The same thing happens with commandments. Let's say you start paying tithing. At first, it takes faith -- you're giving away money and trusting that God will bless you for it. But then the blessings come. Maybe not in the way you expected, but they come. And now you **know** that God keeps His promises. Your faith in tithing has become knowledge.

The prophet Alma explained this beautifully in **Alma 32:28-43**. He compared faith to planting a **seed**. When you first plant the seed, you don't know what it will become. But if it's a good seed and you nourish it, it starts to grow. It swells and sprouts and pushes upward. As it grows, you can feel that it's a good seed -- and your faith begins to turn into knowledge.

But Alma didn't stop there. He said that if you keep nourishing the seed -- keep praying, keep obeying, keep studying -- it eventually grows into a great **tree** that produces fruit. That fruit is sweet and pure and fills you with joy. At that point, you don't just believe anymore. You **know**.

This is one of the most important ideas in the Lectures on Faith. Faith is the **beginning**, not the end. It's the starting point that leads to knowledge, which leads to power, which leads to a deep, personal relationship with God.

You aren't meant to stay in the "hoping" stage forever. God wants you to exercise your faith so that it becomes knowledge. He wants you to pray and receive answers. He wants you to keep His commandments and see His blessings. He wants you to plant the seed and watch it grow into a tree.

Faith is how you start. Knowledge is where you're headed.
