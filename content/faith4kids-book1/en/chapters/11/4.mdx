### Lesson 11.4 -- Faith and Righteousness

A lot of people think you have to be really righteous before you can have faith. They think, "Once I'm good enough, *then* I'll have strong faith." But the Lectures on Faith teach the exact opposite. **Faith produces righteousness**, not the other way around.

**Lecture 7:9** makes this clear. Faith comes first. You don't wait until you're perfect to start believing and acting. You start with faith, even small, imperfect faith, and that faith begins to **transform you**.

Think of it like learning to play a musical instrument. You don't wait until you're a great musician before you start practicing. You start practicing *because* you believe that practice will make you better. You have faith in the process. And as you practice, you actually become a musician. The skill comes from the effort, not the other way around.

Faith works the same way. When you choose to pray even though you're not sure God is listening, that's faith. When you choose to be honest even though lying would be easier, that's faith. When you choose to repent and try again after you've made a mistake, that's faith. And every time you make one of those choices, you become a little more righteous. Not because you forced yourself to be good, but because faith is quietly changing you from the inside out.

How does this happen? Through the **Holy Ghost**. When you exercise faith, the Holy Ghost comes into your life in greater measure. And the Holy Ghost does something wonderful. He **changes your desires**. Things that used to tempt you start to lose their appeal. Things you used to find boring (like reading scriptures or going to the temple) start to feel meaningful and even exciting. The Holy Ghost **softens your heart** and helps you want what God wants.

This is the process. You don't become righteous and then receive faith as a reward. You exercise faith, and through that exercise, you **become righteous**. It's a gift that comes through the process, not a requirement for starting the process.

So if you've ever felt like you're not good enough to have real faith, take heart. You don't need to be perfect. You just need to be willing to start. Exercise your faith, even a little at a time, and watch what happens. The Holy Ghost will do the rest. Faith will change you, shape you, and make you into the person God already knows you can become.
