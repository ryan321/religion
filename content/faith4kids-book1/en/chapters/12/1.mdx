### Lesson 12.1 -- What "Salvation" Means

Throughout these lessons, we've talked a lot about faith: what it is, how it works, what it requires, and what it produces. But now it's time to talk about where faith is leading you. The Lectures on Faith call it **salvation**, and they don't use that word casually. It means something real, something specific, and something breathtaking.

**Salvation** means **returning to God's presence**. Not just believing in God from far away or hoping you end up somewhere nice after you die. It means actually going back to live with your Heavenly Father, face to face, in His presence, forever.

But it means even more than that. Salvation means **eternal life**, which is the kind of life God lives. It means being part of a family that never ends. It means having all the knowledge, power, and love that God has. It means **becoming like Him**.

In **Doctrine and Covenants 131:1-4**, the Lord explains that in the **celestial kingdom**, there are three degrees, and the highest degree is where you receive the fullness of God's glory. To reach that highest degree, you must enter into the **new and everlasting covenant of marriage**. This isn't just about getting into heaven. It's about receiving **everything** the Father has.

Think about that for a moment. God isn't offering you a participation trophy. He's offering you His entire kingdom. He's saying, "Everything I have is yours, if you will have the faith to receive it."

This is what the word "salvation" means in the Lectures on Faith. It's not a vague idea about being "saved." It's a specific promise: you can **live with God and your family forever** in the highest degree of the celestial kingdom. You can become like your Father in Heaven.

Now, that might sound like something only super-righteous people or prophets could achieve. But the whole point of the Lectures on Faith is to show you that it's available to **everyone**. Every lesson we've studied (about God's character, His attributes, the need for sacrifice, the power of faith) has been building toward this one truth: salvation is real, and it's meant for you.

Everything we've learned has been preparing you to understand what you're reaching for. And now you know. The question is: will you reach?
