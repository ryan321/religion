### Lesson 12.2 -- The Faith of the Ancients

Lecture 7 gives us some of the most powerful examples of faith in all of scripture. These are people who didn't just believe in God -- they **knew** Him, **sacrificed** for Him, and received the **greatest promises** heaven had to offer. Their stories show us what faith can actually accomplish when it's real and complete.

Start with **Enoch**. Enoch was a preacher in a wicked generation. People mocked him and rejected his message. But Enoch's faith didn't waver. He taught, he obeyed, and he built a city of people who lived God's law so faithfully that the entire city was **translated** -- taken off the earth and into God's presence (**Moses 7:21**). Not just Enoch, but everyone in his city. That's what faith did.

Then there's **Elijah**. His faith was so powerful and his life so righteous that he never tasted death. He was **taken up into heaven** in a chariot of fire (**2 Kings 2:11**). He didn't just believe in God from a distance -- he walked with God so closely that God took him home without passing through the grave.

**Abraham** is another towering example. He waited decades for the son God promised him. He was willing to sacrifice that son when God asked. And because of his faith, God promised him an **eternal posterity** -- a family line that would bless every nation on earth forever. Abraham's faith wasn't about a single moment. It was a lifetime of trusting God through uncertainty and sacrifice.

And **Moses** -- the man who led Israel out of Egypt -- spoke with God **face to face** (**Moses 1:2**). He didn't just read about God or hear about Him from someone else. He stood in God's presence and received His word directly. That kind of experience came from a lifetime of faith built through obedience and sacrifice.

Notice the pattern. These people didn't just believe. They **knew God personally**. They **sacrificed** -- sometimes everything they had. And in return, they received the **greatest promises** available: translation, eternal families, face-to-face communion with God.

Here's the most important thing Lecture 7 wants you to understand: this same pattern is available to **you**. The God who translated Enoch, took Elijah, blessed Abraham, and spoke to Moses is the same God who invites you to develop that same kind of faith. The path hasn't changed. It's still open.
