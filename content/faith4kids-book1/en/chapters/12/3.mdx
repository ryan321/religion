### Lesson 12.3 -- Can I Have This Kind of Faith?

After reading about Enoch translating a city, Elijah being taken to heaven, Abraham receiving eternal promises, and Moses speaking with God face to face, you might be thinking: "That's amazing, but those are prophets. I'm just a kid. Can I really have that kind of faith?"

The answer is **yes**. Absolutely, completely, without hesitation: **yes**.

The same faith that moved mountains, parted seas, and translated cities is available to **every person** who is willing to develop it. It is not reserved for prophets. It is not locked behind some special door that only a few people have the key to. The Lectures on Faith make this clear: the principles of faith are the same for everyone.

But how? How do you actually develop faith like that? The Lectures give you a **roadmap**, and it's the same roadmap we've been studying this entire time. Let's review it.

**First, learn about God.** You can't have faith in someone you don't know. That's why we spent time studying who God is (His reality, His existence, and the fact that He has revealed Himself to people throughout history). The more you learn about God, the stronger the foundation of your faith becomes.

**Second, understand His character and attributes.** God is merciful, just, truthful, loving, and unchanging. He doesn't lie. He doesn't break His promises. When you understand *what God is like*, you can trust Him completely, even when life is confusing or hard.

**Third, align your life with His will.** This means keeping the commandments, repenting when you fall short, and seeking the guidance of the Holy Ghost. When your life is in harmony with God's plan, you can approach Him with confidence, knowing that you're on the right path.

**Fourth, be willing to sacrifice.** You've learned that sacrifice is not optional. It's the mechanism that proves your faith is real. Every time you give up something for the gospel, your faith grows stronger.

That's it. That's the roadmap. And it works for a **patriarch** leading an entire family and for a **twelve-year-old** kneeling beside a bed. The principles don't change based on your age, your calling, or your experience. They work for everyone who applies them.

You are not too young. You are not too ordinary. You are exactly the kind of person the Lectures on Faith were written for. The question was never *can* you have this kind of faith. The question is *will* you.
