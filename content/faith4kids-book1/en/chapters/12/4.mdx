### Lesson 12.4 -- Faith Is a Choice

We've come to the final lesson in our study of the Lectures on Faith for Kids. And it ends with the most important truth of all: **faith is a choice**.

Nobody can force you to believe. Nobody can make you pray, read your scriptures, or keep the commandments. And nobody can make you trust God when things are hard. All of that is up to you. Faith is something you **choose**, every single day.

But here's what makes this choice different from a blind guess or a leap in the dark. The Lectures on Faith have given you **knowledge** to make this choice intelligently. You're not being asked to believe in something you know nothing about. Over the course of these lessons, you've learned exactly who God is. You've studied His **character**: that He is merciful, just, and full of love. You've studied His **attributes**: that He has all knowledge, all power, and all truth. And you've studied His **promises**: that He will never lie, never change, and never abandon you.

You know all of this now. And that knowledge changes the nature of your choice.

When you choose faith, you're not choosing blindly. You're choosing based on everything you've learned. You're saying, "I know who God is. I know what He's like. I know what He's promised. And I choose to **act** on that knowledge."

That's what faith really is: **choosing to act** on what you know.

You choose to **believe** when doubt whispers in your ear. You choose to **act** when it would be easier to sit still. You choose to **sacrifice** when holding on feels safer than letting go. You choose to **trust God** even when you can't see the outcome.

The Lectures on Faith haven't just given you information. They've given you a foundation. You now know who God is, what He expects, and what He has promised. The only question that remains is the most personal one of all: **Will you act on what you know?**

That is faith.

It's the choice that Adam made when he offered his firstlings. It's the choice Abraham made on Mount Moriah. It's the choice Joseph Smith made when he knelt in the Sacred Grove. It's the choice every prophet and every faithful person in history has made.

And now it's yours.

You have the knowledge. You have the roadmap. You have the invitation. Faith is not something that happens to you. It's something you **choose**. And that choice, made daily, is the most powerful thing you will ever do.
