### Lesson 13.2 -- Is Faith the Same as Hope?

Faith and hope are two words you hear a lot at church, and sometimes people use them almost interchangeably. But they're not the same thing. They're related (like close cousins), but each one does something different.

**Faith** is trust in God that leads to action right now. It's based on *knowledge*, knowing who God is, what He's like, and what He's promised. When you pray believing God will hear you, that's faith. When you keep the commandments because you trust that God's way is the best way, that's faith. Faith is active. Faith is present tense. It's something you exercise today, in this moment.

**Hope**, on the other hand, is confidence about the *future*. It's the feeling that things will work out, that God's promises will be fulfilled, that there's something wonderful waiting for you. Hope looks forward. When you believe that you'll be resurrected someday, that's hope. When you trust that your family can be together forever, that's hope.

Here's the key: hope is *built on* faith. You can't really hope for something if you don't first believe in the God who promised it. The prophet Moroni taught this clearly: **"If a man have faith he must needs have hope; for without faith there cannot be any hope"** (Moroni 7:42). He also taught that those who are "meek and lowly in heart" can have hope through the Atonement of Jesus Christ for eternal life (Moroni 7:43).

Think of it this way. Imagine you're on a hike and you come to a river. Faith is trusting that the bridge across the river will hold you, based on what you know about who built it and how strong it is. You step onto the bridge. That's faith in action. Hope is the confidence that when you get to the other side, the trail will lead you to somewhere beautiful.

The Lectures on Faith focus mostly on faith, understanding God so that you can trust Him and act. But they set the stage for hope, too. When you learn in Lecture 3 that God is merciful and gracious, and in Lecture 4 that He is truthful and unchangeable, those truths give you a reason to hope. You can look to the future with confidence because you *know* the God who holds the future.

So you need both. Faith gets you moving. Hope keeps you going. Faith is the engine, and hope is the destination on the map. Without faith, you wouldn't start the journey; without hope, you wouldn't know where you're headed.

As you study the gospel, pay attention to when you're exercising faith and when you're feeling hope. They work together, and they both come from knowing and trusting God.
