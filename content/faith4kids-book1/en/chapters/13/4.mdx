### Lesson 13.4 -- How Do I Grow My Faith?

So you've learned what faith is, where it comes from, and how it works. Now the big question: how do you actually *grow* it? The Lectures on Faith give you a clear roadmap, and it's something you can start following right now. Not when you're older, not when you're on a mission, but today.

**Step 1: Learn about God.** This is where it all starts. You can't trust someone you don't know. The Lectures spend most of their time teaching you about God's character, attributes, and nature because *knowledge is the foundation of faith*. So read your scriptures. Study what God is like: His mercy, His power, His truthfulness, His justice. The more you learn about Him, the easier it becomes to trust Him.

**Step 2: Align your life.** Once you know what God is like, you start shaping your life to match His teachings. This means repenting when you mess up, keeping the commandments, and making and keeping covenants. It's not about being perfect. It's about facing the right direction and trying every day. When your life lines up with what God has taught, your confidence in Him grows.

**Step 3: Sacrifice.** This is the hard one. Lecture 6 taught that faith requires sacrifice, giving up things that compete with God for your attention and devotion. Maybe it's giving up time to serve someone. Maybe it's choosing to go to church when you'd rather sleep in. Maybe it's standing up for what's right when your friends might not understand. Sacrifice proves to your own heart that God matters most to you.

**Step 4: Act.** Faith grows through *use*, like a muscle. You have to actually exercise it. Pray, not just routine prayers, but real prayers where you pour out your heart and listen for answers. Serve, not just when it's convenient, but with real effort. Trust God with real problems, not just easy ones. Every time you act in faith and see the results, your faith gets stronger.

Here's the beautiful thing: these four steps create a cycle. You learn, so you act. You act, so you sacrifice. You sacrifice, so you learn even more about God. And round and round it goes, with your faith growing deeper and stronger each time.

Think of faith like a fire. Knowledge is the wood. Obedience is the match. Sacrifice is the fuel that keeps it burning. And action is the oxygen that keeps the flame alive. Take away any one of those things and the fire goes out. But keep feeding all of them, and your faith can become an unquenchable blaze.

You don't have to do everything at once. Start with one thing today. Read a chapter of scripture. Say a more meaningful prayer. Choose to obey when it's hard. That's how faith grows: one step, one choice, one day at a time.
