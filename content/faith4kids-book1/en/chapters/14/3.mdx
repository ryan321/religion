### Lesson 14.3 -- How the Lectures Connect to the Covenant Path

You've probably heard the phrase "covenant path" at church. It describes the journey you take as a member of the Church -- from baptism all the way through the temple and beyond. Every step on that path requires one thing above all else: **faith**.

Think about it. It takes faith to be **baptized** -- to trust that making a covenant with God will bless your life. It takes faith to **keep the commandments** even when they're hard or unpopular. It takes faith to **pay tithing** -- to give 10% of your money to the Lord and trust that He'll take care of you. It takes faith to **serve a mission** -- to leave your home, your friends, and your comfort zone to share the gospel. It takes faith to **enter the temple** -- to make sacred promises to God and dedicate your whole life to Him. And it takes faith to **sacrifice** what the world offers in order to follow Christ.

Every single step on the covenant path requires faith. And the Lectures on Faith describe **exactly** the kind of faith you need for each of those steps.

The lectures teach that faith starts with knowledge -- knowing who God is and what He's promised. That knowledge gives you the confidence to take the first step. Then they teach that faith requires action -- you have to actually *do* something, not just believe in your head. That action is what the covenant path is all about: making promises and keeping them.

The lectures also teach that faith requires sacrifice. And that's true at every stage of the covenant path. Baptism requires giving up your old life. The sacrament requires giving up your pride and repenting each week. The temple requires giving up your own will and consecrating everything to God. The deeper you go on the covenant path, the more sacrifice is required -- and the more faith you need.

Think of the Lectures on Faith as an **operating manual** for the most important principle in the gospel. The covenant path is the road, and faith is the fuel that moves you along it. Without faith, you might stand at the beginning of the path and never take a step. But with the kind of faith the lectures describe -- informed, active, sacrificial, and grounded in knowledge of God -- you can walk that path with confidence, no matter how steep it gets.

Every covenant you make is an act of faith. And every time you keep that covenant, your faith grows stronger. The Lectures on Faith show you exactly how that process works.
