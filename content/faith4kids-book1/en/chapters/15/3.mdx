### Lesson 15.3 -- What the Lectures Taught You

You've come a long way. Over the course of this book, you've studied the seven Lectures on Faith and learned some of the most powerful truths in the gospel. Let's take a moment to look back at what each lecture taught you.

**Lecture 1** taught you that faith is a **principle of action and power**. It's not just a feeling or a wish. Faith is what moves you to act, and it's the power by which God Himself created the worlds. When you exercise faith, you're using the same principle that governs the universe.

**Lecture 2** taught you that **knowledge of God** has been passed down from the very beginning. Starting with Adam, God made sure that every generation had the knowledge they needed to exercise faith. That knowledge came through revelation, through prophets, and through the scriptures. You have access to the same truths today.

**Lecture 3** taught you about God's **character** -- who He is as a person. He is merciful, gracious, slow to anger, abundant in goodness, full of truth, and keeps His promises for a thousand generations. Knowing His character gives you a reason to trust Him.

**Lecture 4** taught you about God's **attributes** -- His qualities and perfections. He has all knowledge, all power, perfect justice, perfect judgment, perfect mercy, and perfect truth. He is no respecter of persons and He never changes. These attributes give you confidence that God can and will do what He promises.

**Lecture 5** taught you about God's **nature and the Godhead**. You learned about the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost -- three separate beings united in purpose, working together for your salvation. Understanding the Godhead helps you know who you're praying to and who is guiding your life.

**Lecture 6** taught you about **sacrifice**. Real faith requires giving up things that compete with God. The early Saints sacrificed comfort, safety, and sometimes their lives. You may never face those exact trials, but you will face your own opportunities to put God first -- and when you do, your faith deepens.

**Lecture 7** taught you about the **effects and power of faith** -- what faith can do in your life. It gives you power to endure trials, overcome weakness, receive revelation, and ultimately be brought into the presence of God. Faith isn't just a starting point. It's the power that carries you all the way to salvation.

These seven lectures form a complete picture. They tell you what faith is, what you need to know, and what it can do. They give you the tools to build a faith that will last your entire life.
