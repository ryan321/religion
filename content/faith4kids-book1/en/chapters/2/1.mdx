### Lesson 2.1 -- Faith Is Not Just Believing

If someone asked you, "What is faith?" what would you say? A lot of people, even adults, would answer something like, "Faith is believing in something you can't see." And that's partly true! But the Lectures on Faith teach us that faith is so much more than just believing. It's actually the **first principle of action** in all intelligent beings.

Let's read what **Lecture 1, verse 9** says. It teaches that faith is "the first principle of action in all intelligent beings." That means faith isn't just something you *think*. It's something that makes you *do*. Faith is **active**, not **passive**.

Here's what that means in your everyday life. Think about planting a seed in a garden. Why would you put a tiny seed in the dirt, water it, and wait? Because you have *faith* that it will grow! You believe that if you do your part, something will happen. That belief, that faith, is what moved you to act. Without it, you'd never pick up the seed in the first place.

The same thing happens when you study for a test at school. Why do you sit down and read your notes? Because you have faith that studying will help you do better. You can't *see* the future grade, but you believe that your effort will lead to a result. That faith moves you to action.

And think about prayer. Why do you kneel down and talk to Heavenly Father? Because you have faith that He is really there and that He really listens. That faith, that trust, is what moves you to open your mouth and speak to Him. Without faith, prayer wouldn't make any sense.

So faith isn't just a warm feeling in your heart, and it's not just hoping for the best. **Faith is the engine that drives everything you do.** Every time you act on something you believe in, you're exercising faith.

The Lectures on Faith teach that this principle applies to *all* intelligent beings, not just humans, not just Church members, but everyone. Even Heavenly Father operates by faith! (We'll learn more about that in the next lesson.) Faith is a universal principle, like gravity. It's always at work, whether you notice it or not.

So the next time someone asks you what faith is, you can tell them: "Faith isn't just believing. It's the first principle of action. It's what moves me to do things: to pray, to serve, to obey, to keep going even when things are hard."

That's a much deeper answer, and it comes straight from the Lectures on Faith.
