### Lesson 2.2 -- Faith as the Principle of Power

In the last lesson, you learned that faith is the first principle of action -- it's what moves you to do things. But the Lectures on Faith take it even further. In **Lecture 1, verses 13-16**, we learn that faith is also the **principle of power** -- not just for you and me, but for God Himself.

Think about the creation of the world. How did God make the earth, the stars, the oceans, and everything in them? The scriptures tell us He spoke, and it happened. **"Let there be light,"** He said, and there was light (Genesis 1:3). But what *power* was behind those words? The Lectures on Faith teach that it was **faith**.

That's an amazing idea. God didn't use some kind of magic wand or push a cosmic button. He used faith. He had perfect knowledge of what He wanted to create, a perfect will to make it happen, and perfect faith to turn that knowledge and will into reality. Faith was the power that made creation possible.

The lectures explain it this way: **faith turns knowledge and will into reality**. God knew what He wanted to create. He willed it to happen. And through faith, it became real. That's the pattern -- knowledge plus will plus faith equals action and results.

Now, you might be thinking, "That's great for God, but what about me?" Here's the exciting part -- the same principle works in your life too, just on a smaller scale. When you know something is true (like the gospel), and you choose to act on it (your will), and you trust God enough to follow through (faith) -- things happen. Prayers get answered. Testimonies grow. Lives change.

Think about Nephi building a ship. He had never built one before. But he had knowledge that God had commanded it, the will to obey, and faith that God would show him how. And it worked! The ship was built, and his family crossed the ocean. That's faith as a principle of power in action.

Or think about the brother of Jared. He brought stones to the Lord and asked Him to touch them so they would glow and light the barges. He had knowledge of the problem, the will to find a solution, and faith that God could do it. And the Lord touched the stones, and they shone (Ether 6:3).

Faith isn't just about believing -- it's about **power**. It's the force that moves mountains, parts seas, and creates worlds. And it's available to you every single day.
