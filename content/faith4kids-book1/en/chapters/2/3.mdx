### Lesson 2.3 -- Faith in Daily Life

So far, we've learned that faith is the first principle of action and the principle of power. Those are big, important ideas. But you might be wondering, does faith really show up in my regular, everyday life? The answer is **yes**, and it shows up more than you probably realize.

Let's start with something simple. When you sit down in a chair, do you check to make sure it's going to hold you? Probably not. You just sit down. Why? Because you have **faith** that the chair will support you. You've sat in chairs before. You trust that this one will work too. That's faith: a quiet, confident trust that leads you to act.

What about eating food? When someone puts a plate of dinner in front of you, do you take it to a laboratory and test every bite to make sure it's safe? Of course not. You eat it because you have faith that the person who made it prepared it properly and that the food is good. That trust, that faith, is what moves you to pick up your fork.

Going to school works the same way. You wake up, get dressed, and head to class because you have faith that your teachers will be there, that the building will be open, and that learning will help you in the future. You can't prove any of those things in advance. You just trust and act.

Now here's where it gets really interesting. The Lectures on Faith teach that this same basic principle (trust leading to action) is the **exact same principle** that operates at the highest levels of the universe. The faith that helps you sit in a chair is the same *type* of faith that God used to create the world. Obviously, God's faith is perfect and infinitely more powerful, but the principle is the same.

It's like water. A tiny drop of water and the Pacific Ocean are made of the exact same stuff, H2O. The ocean is obviously bigger, but the basic nature is identical. Your everyday faith and God's creative faith are the same principle, just at very different scales.

That should be encouraging to you! It means you already use faith every single day. You're not starting from zero. The challenge is to take that natural, everyday faith and **grow it**, to direct it toward God, toward His promises, and toward the things that matter most.

Every time you pray, read your scriptures, serve someone, or choose the right when it's hard, you're taking the same principle you use to sit in a chair and elevating it to something sacred. You're exercising faith in God, and that's the most powerful thing you can do.
