### Lesson 2.4 -- The First Principle of the Gospel

You've probably memorized the **Articles of Faith**, or at least the first few. Do you remember the fourth one? It says: **"We believe that the first principles and ordinances of the Gospel are: first, Faith in the Lord Jesus Christ; second, Repentance; third, Baptism by immersion for the remission of sins; fourth, Laying on of hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost."**

Notice what comes first: **faith**. Not repentance. Not baptism. Not the gift of the Holy Ghost. Faith. And the Lectures on Faith help us understand *why* faith has to come first: **nothing else works without it**.

Think about repentance. Repentance means recognizing you've done something wrong, feeling sorry, and choosing to change. But why would you repent if you didn't believe that God is real, that His commandments matter, and that He has the power to forgive? You wouldn't. You'd just shrug and keep doing whatever you wanted. **Faith** is what motivates you to repent.

What about baptism? Baptism is a covenant, a sacred promise between you and God. But why would you make that promise if you didn't have faith that God would keep His side of it? Why would you step into the water if you didn't trust that something real and powerful was happening? Again, **faith** comes first.

And keeping the commandments, all of them, requires faith too. Why would you pay tithing if you didn't believe God would bless you for it? Why would you keep the Sabbath day holy if you didn't trust that it mattered? Why would you be honest when it might be easier to lie? Because you have **faith** that God's way is the right way, even when it's hard.

The Lectures on Faith describe faith as the **engine** that drives everything else in the gospel. Without it, the whole system stops. It's like trying to drive a car without an engine. You can have a beautiful car with perfect paint and comfortable seats, but without the engine, it's not going anywhere.

Your faith is your engine. It's what powers your prayers, your obedience, your service, and your growth. And the more you understand faith (what it really is and how it really works), the more powerful that engine becomes.

That's why we're studying the Lectures on Faith. Not just to learn interesting facts from Church history, but to build a deeper, stronger, more powerful faith that will carry you through your whole life. The early Saints knew this. The School of the Prophets knew this. And now you know it too.

Faith isn't just the first principle of the gospel. It's the most important one. Everything else depends on it.
