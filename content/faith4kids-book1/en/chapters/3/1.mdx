### Lesson 3.1 -- You Can't Have Faith in a Stranger

Imagine someone you've never met walks up to you and says, "Give me your lunch money. I promise I'll bring it back tomorrow with a candy bar." Would you hand over your money? Probably not! Why? Because you don't know this person. You don't know if they're honest, if they keep their promises, or if they even exist in your neighborhood. You can't trust a total stranger.

**Lecture 2** of the Lectures on Faith teaches that the same thing is true about our faith in God. You can't have real, deep, powerful faith in someone you don't know. And the lecture identifies **three things** that are necessary before you can have faith in God:

1. **You must know that God exists.** This is the foundation. If you're not sure God is even real, how can you trust Him? How can you pray to Him, rely on Him, or obey His commandments? You can't have faith in someone whose existence you doubt.

2. **You must have a correct understanding of His character.** It's not enough to just know God is "out there." You need to know what He's *like*. Is He kind? Is He just? Does He keep His promises? Does He love you? Your understanding of God's character shapes your ability to trust Him.

3. **You must know that the life you are living is pleasing to Him.** This one is really important. Even if you know God exists and you know He's good, if you feel guilty or unsure about how you're living, it's hard to approach Him with confidence. When you're keeping the commandments and doing your best, you can pray with boldness because you know you're on the right path.

These three requirements make so much sense when you think about them. Consider your best friend. You trust them because you *know* them. You know they exist (obviously!), you know their character (they're kind, honest, fun), and you feel good about your friendship (you treat each other well). That knowledge is what makes trust, and faith, possible.

The Lectures on Faith teach that your relationship with God works the same way. Faith isn't blind. It isn't a leap in the dark. It's built on **knowledge**, real, personal knowledge of who God is and what He's like.

Over the next few lessons, we'll explore how this knowledge was first given to mankind and how it has been passed down through the ages, all the way to you.
