### Lesson 3.2 -- How Adam Knew God

In the last lesson, you learned that faith requires knowledge. You have to *know* God exists before you can trust Him. So here's an important question: How did the very first people on earth know that God was real? The answer is incredible. They knew because **they talked to Him face to face**.

The scriptures tell us that **Adam and Eve walked with God in the Garden of Eden**. They didn't learn about God from a book or hear about Him from a missionary. They were right there with Him. They heard His voice. They received His instructions directly. God was as real to them as your parents are to you.

But then came the **Fall**. Adam and Eve were cast out of the Garden. They could no longer walk with God the way they had before. So did God abandon them? Did He leave them to figure everything out on their own?

**Absolutely not.** The book of **Moses, chapters 5 and 6**, tells us that God continued to teach Adam and Eve even after the Fall. He sent angels to instruct them. He gave them commandments. He taught them about the plan of salvation, about the Savior who would come, and about the gospel. Adam even received the **Holy Ghost** and was **baptized** (Moses 6:64-66).

This is so important for understanding faith. Adam's knowledge of God wasn't based on guessing or speculation. It wasn't a vague feeling that "something" might be out there. Adam had **firsthand, personal knowledge** of God, the kind of knowledge that makes powerful faith possible.

Think about how different that is from just *hoping* God is real. Adam didn't hope; he *knew*. He had heard God's voice. He had walked with Him. And even after the Fall, God kept communicating with him. Adam's faith was built on a rock-solid foundation of personal experience.

The Lectures on Faith point to Adam as the starting point of all faith on earth. Every person who has ever believed in God can trace that belief back, link by link, to Adam's firsthand experience. Adam knew God, and he shared that knowledge with his children, and they shared it with theirs.

This teaches us something powerful: God doesn't hide. He *wants* us to know Him. From the very beginning, He has made Himself known to His children. He spoke to Adam, and He'll speak to you too, through the scriptures, through the Spirit, through prayer, and through His prophets.

Your faith, just like Adam's, can be built on real, personal knowledge of God.
