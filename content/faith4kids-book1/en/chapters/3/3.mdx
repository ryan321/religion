### Lesson 3.3 -- Passing the Knowledge Down

You've learned that Adam knew God personally. He walked with Him, talked with Him, and received direct instruction from Him. But Adam didn't keep that knowledge to himself. He shared it. And **Lecture 2** of the Lectures on Faith carefully traces how that knowledge was passed from generation to generation.

Here's something amazing about the timeline. The scriptures tell us that **Adam lived 930 years** (Genesis 5:5). That's a very, very long life! Because he lived so long, Adam was able to personally teach many generations of his descendants about God. His children, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, and many more generations could hear the truth directly from the man who had walked with God in the Garden.

Now let's follow the chain. **Methuselah**, who lived to be 969 years old, was born while Adam was still alive. That means Methuselah could have personally known Adam and heard his testimony of God firsthand. Methuselah didn't have to take anyone else's word for it. He could go directly to Adam and ask, "What was it like to walk with God?"

Then comes **Noah**. Noah was born while Methuselah was still alive. So Noah could have heard the testimony from Methuselah, who had heard it from Adam himself. Think about how short that chain is. From Adam to Methuselah to Noah, the knowledge of God was passed down through just **two links** of testimony covering thousands of years.

The Lectures on Faith trace this chain very carefully because it makes an important point: the knowledge of God was never lost during this time. It wasn't a game of telephone where the message got confused and changed. It was a **short, direct chain** of testimony from people who personally knew each other and who took the responsibility of passing on sacred truth.

This is how faith worked in the ancient world. People didn't just believe in God randomly. They believed because trustworthy witnesses, starting with Adam, testified of what they knew. And because these patriarchs lived so long, the chain stayed remarkably short and strong.

Think about your own life. You have people who have passed down spiritual knowledge to you: parents, grandparents, Primary teachers, bishops. Each of them is a link in a chain that goes all the way back through the prophets, through the ancient patriarchs, and ultimately back to Adam and to God Himself.

You're part of a chain of faith that stretches across all of human history. That's an incredible thing to be part of.
