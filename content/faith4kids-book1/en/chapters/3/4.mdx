### Lesson 3.4 -- What This Means for Your Faith

We've covered a lot of ground in this chapter. You've learned that faith requires knowledge of God, that Adam had firsthand knowledge from walking with God, and that this knowledge was carefully passed down through a short chain of patriarchs. But now comes the question that matters most: **What does all of this mean for you?**

The Lectures on Faith are making a powerful argument: **faith is not blind**. It never has been. From the very beginning, faith has been based on real knowledge, real testimony, and real experience. Adam didn't blindly hope God was real. Methuselah didn't just guess. Noah didn't make it up. They *knew*, and they shared what they knew.

And you have even more than they did.

Think about the resources available to you. You have the **scriptures**: the Bible, the Book of Mormon, the Doctrine and Covenants, and the Pearl of Great Price. These books contain the testimonies of prophets across thousands of years, all testifying of God and His plan. You have the **Book of Mormon**, which is a second witness of Jesus Christ, delivered to the world through the Prophet Joseph Smith. You have **living prophets**, a prophet, apostles, and other leaders who continue to receive revelation from God today.

That's an incredible amount of testimony and knowledge available to you. The ancient patriarchs had a chain of witnesses going back to Adam. Your chain is even longer and richer. It stretches from Adam through Noah, through Abraham, through Moses, through the prophets of the Book of Mormon, through Joseph Smith, and all the way to the living prophet today.

But here's the most exciting part: **you can add your own link to the chain**.

You don't have to rely only on what other people have told you about God. You can seek your own experience with Him. You can pray and receive answers. You can read the scriptures and feel the Spirit confirming truth to your heart. You can keep the commandments and see the blessings that come. You can develop your own personal, firsthand knowledge of God, just like Adam had, just like the prophets have had throughout history.

**Moroni 10:4-5** promises that if you pray with real intent and faith in Christ, the Holy Ghost will manifest the truth to you. That's God's invitation to *you* to add your own link to this ancient chain of testimony.

Your faith doesn't have to be borrowed forever. It can become your own. And when it does, when you know for yourself that God is real, that He loves you, and that His gospel is true, your faith will have the same kind of power that moved the ancient patriarchs, the same power that the Lectures on Faith describe.

Start today. Pray. Ask. Seek. And add your link to the chain.
