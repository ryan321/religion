### Lesson 4.1 -- From Adam to Noah

Have you ever played the telephone game, where someone whispers a message and it gets passed along from person to person? By the end, the message is usually completely mixed up. Now imagine playing that game with something really important, like the truth about God. That's basically what the Lectures on Faith talk about when they trace how knowledge of God was passed down from Adam all the way to Noah.

Here's the amazing part: it wasn't like a long game of telephone at all. The chain of people who carried this knowledge was surprisingly short, and many of them actually *overlapped* in their lifetimes. Let's look at how.

**Adam** was the first man on earth, and he walked and talked with God. He knew God personally. Adam lived to be 930 years old. That's an incredibly long life by our standards, but it was normal in those early days. Because he lived so long, Adam was actually alive at the same time as many of his descendants, not just his children, but his great-great-great-grandchildren and beyond.

Adam's son **Seth** was born when Adam was 130. Then came **Enos**, **Cainan**, **Mahalaleel**, and **Jared**, each one born while Adam was still living. That means Adam could have taught every single one of them directly. They didn't have to wonder what God was like. They could ask a man who had actually spoken with Him face to face.

Then came **Enoch**, who was so righteous that he and his entire city were **translated**, taken up to heaven without dying. After Enoch came **Methuselah**, who lived 969 years, the longest lifespan recorded in the Bible. Methuselah overlapped with Adam for hundreds of years.

And here's the key connection: **Lamech**, Methuselah's son and **Noah's father**, was also alive during Adam's lifetime. That means the knowledge of God passed through a direct, unbroken chain from Adam to Lamech, and Lamech passed it straight to Noah.

This whole chain covered more than **1,600 years**, yet the message didn't have to travel through hundreds of strangers. It moved through a small group of faithful men who knew each other personally and lived during the same time periods. The truth about God wasn't a distant rumor. It was a living, personal testimony passed from father to son.

That's what Lecture 2 wants us to understand. Faith in God wasn't invented or guessed at. It was handed down carefully through real people who really knew Him.
