### Lesson 4.2 -- From Noah to Abraham

In our last lesson, we followed the chain of faith from Adam all the way to Noah. That chain was strong and personal -- men who actually knew each other passing along the truth about God. But what happened after the Flood? Did the chain break? Not at all. Let's pick up where we left off.

After the great Flood, **Noah lived another 350 years** (Genesis 9:28). That's an incredibly important detail. Noah wasn't just a survivor of the Flood who quickly passed away -- he continued living and teaching for *centuries* afterward. He had plenty of time to share everything he knew about God with his children, grandchildren, and many generations beyond.

Noah's son **Shem** is the next critical link in the chain. Shem was on the ark during the Flood, so he personally witnessed God's power to save the righteous. Shem also lived an extraordinarily long life. And here's the remarkable part -- Shem's lifespan actually **overlapped with Abraham's**. That means Abraham, one of the greatest prophets in all of scripture, could have learned about God from someone who personally survived the Flood and who had been taught by Noah himself.

Think about what this means for the quality of the knowledge being passed along. When Abraham learned about God, he wasn't hearing some ancient legend that had been retold hundreds of times over thousands of years. He was hearing **recent, personal, credible testimony** from someone who had been there -- or who had been taught by someone who had been there.

This is very different from how we usually think about old stories. Normally, the older a story gets, the less reliable it becomes. Details get changed or forgotten. People add things that didn't happen or leave out things that did. But the timeline in the scriptures shows us that the story of God didn't have to survive centuries of retelling. It traveled through just a few faithful people whose lives overlapped.

From Adam to Noah was essentially two or three links in the chain. From Noah to Abraham was basically one or two more. That means the entire history of faith from Adam to Abraham -- covering roughly **2,000 years** -- passed through fewer than a half-dozen key people.

The Lectures on Faith emphasize this because it matters for *our* faith too. The gospel we believe in isn't built on fairy tales or guesses. It's built on real experiences passed down by real people who really knew God. The chain from the beginning was short, strong, and trustworthy.
