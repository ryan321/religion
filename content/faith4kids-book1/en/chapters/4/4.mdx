### Lesson 4.4 -- Your Role in the Chain

We've spent this chapter tracing the chain of faith from Adam to Noah, from Noah to Abraham, and watching what happens when the chain breaks. Now it's time for the most important question of all: **Where do you fit in this chain?**

Think about it. Someone taught *you* about God. Maybe it was your parents who read you scripture stories at bedtime. Maybe it was a Primary teacher who told you about Jesus. Maybe it was a grandparent, an older sibling, or a friend. However it happened, someone took the time to share the truth with you.

And that person received it from someone else: a parent, a teacher, a missionary. And *that* person received it from someone before them. If you could trace it all the way back, link by link, you'd eventually arrive at the very beginning, at Adam, who received his knowledge directly from God.

That's an incredible thought. The gospel you're learning right now has been handed down through an unbroken chain of faithful people stretching across thousands of years. Prophets protected it. Parents taught it. Missionaries carried it to new lands. And eventually, it reached you.

But the chain doesn't end with you. **One day, you will teach your own children.** You'll share with them what you know about Heavenly Father, about Jesus Christ, about the plan of salvation. You'll tell them why you pray, why you go to church, why the scriptures matter. And when you do, you'll be adding your own link to the chain.

Here's the thing about chains, though. A chain is only as strong as its individual links. It doesn't matter how strong the links before you were if your link is weak. One broken link can stop the whole chain. One generation that stops teaching, stops caring, or stops living the gospel can cause the knowledge of God to be lost for their children and grandchildren.

So ask yourself: **Are you going to be a strong link or a broken one?**

Being a strong link doesn't mean you have to be perfect. None of the people in the chain were perfect, not even Adam. Being a strong link means you **keep learning**, **keep believing**, and **keep sharing** what you know. It means you take the gospel seriously enough to study it, live it, and eventually pass it on.

Right now, you're preparing. Every scripture you read, every prayer you offer, every lesson you pay attention to is making your link in the chain a little bit stronger. And that matters, not just for you, but for everyone who will come after you.

The chain of faith has survived for thousands of years. Your job is to make sure it keeps going.
