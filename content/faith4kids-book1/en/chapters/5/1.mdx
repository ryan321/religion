### Lesson 5.1 -- Three Things Necessary for Faith

Imagine you're about to jump off the high dive at the swimming pool for the very first time. Your stomach is in knots. What would you need to know before you'd actually jump?

First, you'd need to know that the **pool is really there**, that there's water below you, not just empty concrete. Second, you'd need to know something about the **nature of the pool**, that it's deep enough to catch you safely. And third, you'd need to feel confident that **you're jumping correctly**, that you're not going to belly flop in a way that hurts.

Lecture 3 of the Lectures on Faith teaches that exercising faith in God works the same way. There are **three things** a person must have in order to have real, living faith. If any one of them is missing, your faith will be weak or won't work at all.

**First, you must know that God actually exists.** This might seem obvious, but it's the foundation of everything. You can't have faith in someone you don't believe is real. This is why the scriptures teach that "he that cometh to God must believe that he is" (Hebrews 11:6). Before anything else, you need to believe that Heavenly Father is there.

**Second, you must have a correct understanding of God's character.** It's not enough just to know God exists. You need to know what He's *like*. Is He kind or cruel? Is He patient or angry? Does He keep His promises or break them? If you have a wrong idea about God's character, your faith will be pointed in the wrong direction. You might be afraid of God when you should trust Him, or you might trust Him for the wrong reasons.

**Third, you must know that your life is aligned with God's will.** This means you need to feel confident that you're trying to live the way God wants you to live. If you know you're doing something wrong and you haven't repented, it's very hard to have faith because deep down you don't feel right about approaching God. But when you're doing your best to keep His commandments, you can come to Him with confidence.

This chapter is going to focus mostly on that second requirement: understanding God's character. Because what you believe about *who God is* shapes everything about how you relate to Him. If you think God is mean, you'll hide from Him. If you know He's loving, you'll run to Him.

Let's find out what God is really like.
