### Lesson 5.3 -- God Does Not Change

Imagine you had a friend who was really nice to you on Monday, then mean to you on Tuesday, then nice again on Wednesday. Could you ever really trust that friend? Probably not, because you'd never know which version of them you were going to get. You'd always be wondering, "Is today a nice day or a mean day?"

Now imagine the opposite: a friend who is always kind, always honest, always fair, no matter what day it is or what mood they're in. *That* is someone you can trust completely. And that's exactly what God is like.

The scriptures teach this clearly. **Hebrews 13:8** says, "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever." **Mormon 9:9** echoes this: "God is the same yesterday, today, and forever, and in him there is no variableness neither shadow of changing."

This is one of the most important truths about God's character, and the Lectures on Faith emphasize it for a very specific reason: **consistency is what makes faith possible.**

Think about it. Faith means trusting God to keep His promises. But if God could change, if He might be merciful today and harsh tomorrow, if He might keep one promise but break the next, then how could you ever rely on Him? You couldn't. You'd never be sure if the promise He made to you was one He would actually keep.

But because God does **not** change, every promise He has ever made is still in force. When He told ancient Israel that He would bless those who keep His commandments, that promise applies to you today. When Jesus promised that "whatsoever ye shall ask the Father in my name, which is right, believing that ye shall receive, behold it shall be given unto you" (3 Nephi 18:20), that promise hasn't expired. It's still good because the God who made it is still the same.

His character is like a **rock**: solid, unmovable, and dependable. You can build your whole life on it and never worry that it will shift beneath you. The same God who parted the Red Sea for Moses is the same God who hears your prayers tonight. The same God who forgave Peter for denying Him three times is the same God who will forgive you when you sincerely repent.

This means the scriptures aren't just old stories about a God who used to do great things. They're evidence of what God **will** do, because He hasn't changed. What He was, He is. What He is, He will always be.

And that's the kind of God you can have faith in.
