### Lesson 5.4 -- God Is No Respecter of Persons

Have you ever felt like someone was playing favorites? Maybe a teacher always called on the same student, or a coach always put the same kid in the game no matter what. It feels unfair, doesn't it? You start to wonder, "Does it even matter how hard I try?"

The good news is that God doesn't work that way. Not even a little bit.

In **Acts 10:34**, the apostle Peter declared, "Of a truth I perceive that God is no respecter of persons." This means God doesn't play favorites. He doesn't bless one person more than another because of their family, their country, their wealth, or their appearance. His promises are available to **everyone** who exercises faith and keeps His commandments.

This was actually a big deal when Peter said it. At the time, many Jewish believers thought the gospel was only for them, that God had chosen their nation and everyone else was left out. But through a vision and the experience of teaching Cornelius (a non-Jewish Roman soldier), Peter came to understand that God's blessings are for all people. The door is open to everyone.

The Lectures on Faith highlight this aspect of God's character because it has a direct impact on *your* faith. Here's why: if you believed that God only answered certain people's prayers, or only helped people who were older, smarter, or more "spiritual" than you, would you bother praying? Would you bother trying? Probably not. You'd feel like the outcome was already decided and there was nothing you could do about it.

But because God is no respecter of persons, **His promises apply to you personally**. The same God who answered the prayers of Nephi, of Alma, of Joseph Smith will answer your prayers. Not because you're special in the way the world defines special, but because God treats everyone the same. He responds to faith wherever He finds it.

This also means you can't make excuses. You can't say, "Well, God helps other people but not me." If you exercise faith, keep His commandments, and sincerely seek Him, He *will* respond. He has to, because His character demands it. He is the same God with the same character and the same promises for every single one of His children.

Think about what this means for you right now. You're young. You might feel like you're not important enough for God to notice. But God doesn't measure importance the way people do. He sees your heart. He hears your prayers. And He responds to your faith just as readily as He responds to anyone else's.

You are not too young, too small, or too ordinary to receive blessings from God. His door is open to you. All you have to do is knock.
