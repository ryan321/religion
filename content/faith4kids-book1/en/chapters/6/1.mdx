### Lesson 6.1 -- Why God's Attributes Matter

In the last chapter, we learned about God's **character** -- what He's like as a person. He is merciful, gracious, unchanging, and fair. Those are wonderful things to know, and they're essential for faith. But Lecture 4 of the Lectures on Faith takes us one step further and asks a new question: What can God actually **do**?

There's an important difference between character and **attributes**. Character is about who someone is on the inside -- their personality, their values, their heart. Attributes are about their abilities -- what they're capable of accomplishing. And the Lectures on Faith teach that both are necessary for faith.

Here's why. Imagine someone who is incredibly kind, patient, and loving -- the best character you can think of. But imagine that person has no power to actually help you. They want to save you, but they can't. They want to answer your prayers, but they don't have the ability. Would you put your faith in that person? You might love them, but you wouldn't *rely* on them -- because being good isn't enough if you're also weak.

Now flip it around. Imagine someone who is all-powerful -- they can do anything. But they don't really know what's going on in your life. They're powerful but **ignorant**. Would you trust them to help you? Not really, because they might use their power in the wrong way since they don't understand your situation.

Or imagine someone with power and knowledge, but no sense of right and wrong. They could help you, but they might just as easily hurt you. Power without justice is terrifying.

Lecture 4 teaches that **faith requires knowing God is both good AND capable**. It's not enough to know He loves you -- you need to know He can actually do something about it. It's not enough to know He's powerful -- you need to know He'll use that power wisely, fairly, and lovingly.

This is why the attributes of God matter so much. When you understand that God is not only perfectly kind but also perfectly powerful, perfectly knowing, and perfectly just, your faith has a solid foundation. You're not trusting in someone who *wishes* He could help you. You're trusting in someone who *can* help you and *will* help you because of who He is.

Over the next few lessons, we'll explore these attributes one by one. As you learn about each one, pay attention to how they work together. It's the combination of all of them that makes God the kind of being you can place your complete faith in.
