### Lesson 6.2 -- Knowledge, Power, and Justice

Lecture 4 identifies several specific attributes of God that are necessary for faith. In this lesson, we're going to look at three of the most important ones: God's **knowledge**, His **power**, and His **justice**.

**God is omniscient -- He knows all things.** The word "omniscient" comes from Latin and means "all-knowing." God doesn't have to guess about anything. He knows the past, the present, and the future. He knows every thought you've ever had, every choice you'll ever make, and every consequence that will follow. He understands science, history, languages, and the hearts of every person who has ever lived.

Why does this matter for faith? Because when you ask God for help, you can trust that He understands your situation *completely*. You don't have to worry that He'll give you bad advice because He missed something important. He sees the whole picture -- beginning, middle, and end. When His answer to your prayer isn't what you expected, you can trust that He knows something you don't.

**God is omnipotent -- He has all power.** "Omnipotent" means "all-powerful." There is no problem too big for God to solve, no enemy too strong for Him to defeat, no situation too hopeless for Him to rescue. When He makes a promise, He has the power to keep it. When He says He will protect you, nothing in the universe can stop Him.

Lecture 4 teaches that if God were lacking in power, we couldn't exercise faith in Him because we'd always wonder, "Can He actually deliver on what He promised?" But because His power is unlimited, we know that anything He says He will do, He *can* do.

**God is just -- He always does what is right.** Justice means fairness and righteousness. God doesn't make mistakes in judgment. He doesn't punish the innocent or reward the guilty. Every decision He makes is perfectly fair and perfectly right.

This attribute matters because it means you can trust God's system. When He says that blessings come from obedience and consequences come from sin, you can count on it. The universe under God's rule is not random or chaotic. It's orderly and fair.

These three attributes -- knowledge, power, and justice -- work together beautifully. God knows what's right (knowledge), He can make it happen (power), and He always chooses to do it (justice). A God who has all three is a God who is fully capable and fully fair -- and that is a God in whom you can place your complete trust.
