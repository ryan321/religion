### Lesson 6.3 -- Mercy and Truth

In our last lesson, we learned that God is all-knowing, all-powerful, and perfectly just. Those are incredible attributes. But if justice were God's *only* attribute, we'd all be in serious trouble. Do you know why?

Because justice means you get exactly what you deserve. And the honest truth is that every single one of us has sinned. Every one of us has made choices we knew were wrong. If God were only just, if all He ever did was give people what they deserved, then none of us would make it back to live with Him. We would all fall short.

That's why **mercy** is so essential. Lecture 4 teaches that God is merciful, meaning He **doesn't give us the full punishment we deserve when we repent**. Mercy is what makes the Atonement of Jesus Christ possible. Because God is merciful, He provided a way for us to be forgiven. Jesus Christ suffered for our sins so that when we repent, the demands of justice are satisfied *and* we can still be made clean.

Think of it this way: justice says, "You broke the rule, so you have to pay the price." Mercy says, "Someone else has paid the price for you, if you'll accept the gift and change your ways." Without mercy, the gospel would be a system of impossible demands. But because God is merciful, it becomes a system of hope and second chances.

The other attribute we need to understand is **truth**. Lecture 4 teaches that God **cannot lie**. The Book of Mormon makes this very clear. In **Ether 3:12**, the Lord told the brother of Jared, "I am a God of truth, and cannot lie." This isn't just a choice God makes. It's part of His very nature. Lying is simply not in Him.

Why does truth matter for faith? Because faith is built on promises. God has promised that if you repent, you'll be forgiven. He has promised that if you pray, He'll hear you. He has promised that if you keep His commandments, you'll be blessed. But those promises only matter if the person making them is **absolutely reliable**. If God could lie, you'd never know which promises were real. But because He cannot lie, every single promise He makes is completely trustworthy.

**Mercy** makes the Atonement possible. **Truth** makes God's promises reliable. Together, they give you the confidence to exercise faith, knowing that God is kind enough to forgive you and honest enough to keep every word He's spoken.
