### Lesson 6.4 -- Why All Attributes Are Necessary

We've now studied several of God's attributes: knowledge, power, justice, mercy, and truth. Each one is amazing on its own. But Lecture 4 makes a bold and important claim: **if God were missing even a single one of these attributes, faith in Him would be impossible.**

That's a big statement. Let's test it and see if it's true.

**What if God lacked knowledge?** If God didn't know everything, you could never fully trust His guidance. Maybe He'd tell you to do something, but He'd be wrong because He didn't have all the information. You'd always have to second-guess Him. "Does God really know what's best for me? What if He's missing something?" That uncertainty would destroy faith.

**What if God lacked power?** If God weren't all-powerful, His promises wouldn't mean much. He might *want* to bless you, but He might not be able to. He might *try* to protect you, but fail. Faith requires believing that God can actually deliver, and without power, He can't.

**What if God lacked justice?** If God weren't fair, the universe would feel random and unpredictable. Good people might suffer while wicked people prosper, and there would be no guarantee that doing the right thing mattered. Why exercise faith in a God who doesn't care about right and wrong?

**What if God lacked mercy?** Without mercy, there would be no forgiveness and no Atonement. Every sin would be held against you forever, with no way to make things right. You'd live in constant fear, knowing that one mistake could ruin everything. Faith would turn into dread.

**What if God lacked truth?** If God could lie, nothing He said would be reliable. His promises might be empty. His scriptures might be misleading. You'd have no foundation to build your faith on, because you could never be sure if what He told you was real.

Do you see the pattern? Remove any single attribute, and the entire structure of faith **collapses**. It's like a building that needs every support beam to stand. Pull out one beam, and the whole thing comes crashing down.

But here's the glorious truth: **God has every attribute in perfection**. He doesn't just have some knowledge; He knows *everything*. He doesn't just have some power; He has *all* power. He isn't just somewhat merciful; He is *perfectly* merciful. He isn't mostly truthful; He *cannot* lie.

Because every attribute is present and perfect, faith works. You can trust God completely, lean on Him fully, and rely on His promises absolutely. The foundation is solid from every angle.

Lecture 4 wants you to understand this so your faith isn't built on a vague feeling. It's built on solid knowledge of who God is and what He can do. And when you know that, nothing can shake you.
