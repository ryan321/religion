### Lesson 7.2 -- The Father

When you hear the word "father," you probably think of someone specific, a real person who knows your name, who cares about your life, and who you can talk to. That's exactly how the Lectures on Faith want you to think about **God the Father**. He is not a mysterious force floating through the universe. He is not an abstract idea that no one can really understand. He is a **person**, the supreme being of the universe, and He knows you by name.

The Father has a **glorified, perfected body of flesh and bones**. That means He has a real, physical form, not like our mortal bodies that get tired and sick, but a perfected, resurrected body filled with light and glory. When Doctrine and Covenants 130:22 says His body is "as tangible as man's," it means that if you could stand in His presence, He would be *real* in every sense of the word.

He is the **literal Father of our spirits**. Before you were born on this earth, you lived with Him as a spirit child. Every person who has ever lived, or ever will live, is His child. That's not just a nice way of talking. It's the truth. You are His son or His daughter, and He has known you since before the world was created.

Do you remember the story of Jesus' baptism? When Jesus came up out of the water, a voice came from heaven saying, "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased" (Matthew 3:17). That was the Father's voice. He spoke from heaven as a separate being, watching His Son perform an act of obedience. He wasn't talking to Himself. He was a proud Father witnessing a sacred moment.

This is one of the reasons the Restoration is so important. Over the centuries, many people came to think of God as unknowable and distant, a being so far beyond understanding that you couldn't really picture Him at all. The Lectures on Faith and the scriptures of the Restoration brought back the truth: the Father is a real, personal being. He has a face, a voice, and a heart full of love for His children.

Knowing this changes everything about prayer. When you kneel down and say "Heavenly Father," you are talking to someone who is actually there, someone with a glorified body, who hears your words and cares deeply about you. That's the kind of God you can build real faith in.
