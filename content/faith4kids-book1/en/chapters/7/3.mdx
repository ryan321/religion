### Lesson 7.3 -- The Son

If you could meet someone who was exactly like your dad in every way -- same kindness, same honesty, same strength of character -- you'd feel like you already knew that person, right? That's a little bit like the relationship between **God the Father** and **Jesus Christ, the Son**. The Lectures on Faith teach that the Son is "in all things like unto the Father" in character, attributes, and perfections. Everything we've learned about the Father's character -- His knowledge, power, justice, mercy, and truth -- is also perfectly true of the Son.

But here's the key point: Jesus is **not** the Father. They are **two separate beings**. The Son has His own glorified, **resurrected body of flesh and bones**. After Jesus rose from the tomb on Easter morning, He appeared to His disciples and said, "Handle me, and see; for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have" (Luke 24:39). He invited them to touch His hands and feet. His resurrection was real, and His body is real, forever.

Jesus Christ is the **Only Begotten Son** of the Father in the flesh. That means He is the only person who was born on this earth with God the Father as His literal, physical father. That gave Him power that no other mortal had: the power to live a sinless life and to overcome death for all of us.

The Lectures on Faith describe Jesus in several important roles. He is the **Creator**. Under the Father's direction, He organized the earth and the heavens. He is the **Redeemer**, and through His suffering in Gethsemane and His death on the cross, He paid the price for our sins so we can repent and be forgiven. And He is our **Mediator**. He stands between us and the Father, speaking on our behalf, helping us return to God's presence.

So when Lecture 5 says the Father and the Son are both God, it doesn't mean they are the same person. It means they are both divine, both glorified, and both perfectly united in purpose. If you want to know what the Father is like, look at the Son. Jesus said it Himself: "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father" (John 14:9). Not because they are the same being, but because the Son reflects the Father's character so perfectly that knowing one is like knowing the other.

Understanding this helps your faith grow. You're trusting in a Savior who has a real body, a real character, and a real mission: to bring you home.
