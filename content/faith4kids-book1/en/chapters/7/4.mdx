### Lesson 7.4 -- The Holy Ghost

Imagine having a friend who could be with you all the time (in school, at home, on the soccer field) whispering helpful advice, warning you about danger, and making you feel peaceful when you're scared. That's a little bit like what the **Holy Ghost** does, and He is the **third member of the Godhead**.

The Holy Ghost is different from the Father and the Son in one very important way: He does **not** have a physical body. Doctrine and Covenants 130:22 calls Him a **"personage of Spirit."** That means He is a real person with a mind, a will, and a purpose, but He exists in spirit form rather than in a body of flesh and bones.

Why doesn't the Holy Ghost have a body? Because this is part of His mission. Since He is a spirit, He can **dwell in you**. A person with a physical body can only be in one place at one time. But the Holy Ghost can touch your heart and mind in a deeply personal way.

So what does the Holy Ghost actually do? His roles are many and important:

**He testifies.** When you feel a burning in your heart while reading the scriptures or listening to a testimony, that's the Holy Ghost telling you something is true.

**He comforts.** When you're sad or going through something hard, the Holy Ghost can fill you with peace, a peace that doesn't always make sense given your circumstances, but is real.

**He guides.** Have you ever had a thought come into your mind that helped you make a good decision? The Holy Ghost can speak to your mind and heart, directing you toward the right path.

**He warns.** Sometimes you might feel uneasy about a situation, like something just doesn't feel right. That can be the Holy Ghost helping you avoid danger or bad choices.

**He sanctifies.** This is a big word that means "to make clean or holy." As you repent and try to follow Christ, the Holy Ghost works in you to gradually change your heart and help you become more like the Savior.

The **gift of the Holy Ghost** is received after baptism, when someone holding the priesthood places their hands on your head and says, "Receive the Holy Ghost." This gift means you can have His constant companionship, a steady, ongoing relationship with this member of the Godhead. He is a real person, and His work in your life is one of the greatest blessings of the gospel.
