### Lesson 8.1 -- Three Beings, One Purpose

Have you ever been on a team where everyone worked together so well that it felt like you were all thinking the same thing? Maybe a basketball team where every pass was perfect, or a group project where nobody argued and the work just flowed. Now imagine a team like that, but absolutely perfect, with zero disagreements, ever. That's what the Godhead is like.

Lecture 5, section 2, teaches that the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost are called **"one God"**, but not because they are literally one being. They are called one God because they are perfectly **united in purpose, will, and character**. What the Father wants, the Son wants. What the Son teaches, the Holy Ghost confirms. They work together in complete, flawless harmony.

This explains how there can be three separate beings and yet the scriptures still say "one God." It's not about their number; it's about their **unity**. They are one in the same way a perfectly united family might say, "We're all on the same page." Except with the Godhead, that unity is absolute and eternal.

Here's how it works in practice. When you see what **Jesus Christ** did during His life on earth (His kindness, His patience, His willingness to forgive, His power to heal), you are seeing exactly what the **Father** is like. Jesus said, "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father" (John 14:9). Not because they are the same person, but because the Son's character is a perfect mirror of the Father's character.

And when you **feel the Holy Ghost**, that warm, peaceful feeling during prayer or scripture study, that gentle nudge to do the right thing, you are feeling the influence of both the Father and the Son reaching you through the Spirit. The Holy Ghost doesn't have His own separate message. He delivers their message, their love, and their guidance directly to your heart.

Think of it like this: the Father is the architect who designed the plan of salvation. The Son is the builder who carried it out through the Atonement. The Holy Ghost is the messenger who brings the blueprints to you personally and helps you understand them. Three different roles, three different persons, but one perfectly coordinated work.

This perfect unity is what makes faith possible. When you pray to the Father in the name of the Son and feel the witness of the Holy Ghost, you're experiencing the full power of a perfectly united Godhead working on your behalf.
