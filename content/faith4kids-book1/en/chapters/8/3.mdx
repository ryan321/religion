### Lesson 8.3 -- Knowing God Produces Faith

Think about the people you trust most in your life -- maybe a parent, a grandparent, or a really good friend. Why do you trust them? It's probably not because someone just told you to. It's because you **know** them. You've seen how they act. You've watched them keep their promises. You've experienced their love. The more you know someone, the more naturally you trust them.

The Lectures on Faith have been building to exactly this point across five lectures, and now it all comes together. Let's trace the path:

**Lecture 1** taught that faith is a principle of power and action -- not just a nice feeling, but something that drives you to act. **Lecture 2** showed that you first need to know that God actually **exists**. You can't trust someone you don't believe is real. **Lecture 3** explained that you need to understand God's **character** -- that He is merciful, just, loving, and truthful. If you thought God was cruel or dishonest, you'd never trust Him. **Lecture 4** taught that you need to know God's **attributes** -- His knowledge, power, and unchanging nature. A God who changes His mind all the time or who doesn't know what's going to happen wouldn't be very trustworthy. And **Lecture 5** revealed the **nature** of the Godhead -- three distinct beings working in perfect unity.

Now step back and look at the whole picture. Each lecture added another piece of knowledge about God, and each piece made faith more natural and logical. Faith isn't a blind leap into the dark. It's not closing your eyes and hoping for the best. Real faith -- the kind the Lectures describe -- is **trust based on knowledge**.

Imagine you're about to jump off a diving board for the first time. If you've never seen a pool before and have no idea if there's water below, jumping would be terrifying and foolish. But if you've seen the pool, checked the depth, and watched other people jump safely -- jumping still takes courage, but it's a **reasonable** step. You have **knowledge** that supports your action.

That's how faith in God works. The more you learn about Him, the more naturally you trust Him. Your faith isn't built on ignorance. It's built on a foundation of understanding.

This is why scripture study, prayer, and learning the gospel matter so much. Every time you learn something true about God, you're adding another brick to the foundation of your faith. And the stronger that foundation, the more confidently you can act on what He asks you to do.
