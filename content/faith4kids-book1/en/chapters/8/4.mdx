### Lesson 8.4 -- Your Personal Knowledge of God

Imagine you're doing a report on George Washington. You read books about him, watch a documentary, and listen to your teacher explain his life. By the end, you know a lot *about* him. But do you really *know* him? Of course not. You've only learned about him secondhand. Now imagine if you could actually meet George Washington, talk with him, and spend time with him. That would be completely different, wouldn't it?

The Lectures on Faith make a similar point about knowing God. Everything we've studied so far has come through **secondhand sources**. We read the scriptures. We listen to teachers. We hear testimonies from parents and prophets. All of that is good and important.

But the ultimate goal is bigger than secondhand knowledge. The goal is to **know God for yourself**.

Jesus taught this in one of the most powerful verses in all of scripture. In **John 17:3**, He prayed to the Father and said, *"And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent."* Notice what He said. Eternal life isn't just about living forever. It's about **knowing God**. That's how central and important this idea is. Knowing God isn't just a nice bonus. It IS eternal life.

How do you move from secondhand to firsthand knowledge? It starts with what you're already learning to do. When you **pray**, you're not just talking into the air. You're reaching out to a real person and inviting Him to communicate back. When you **read the scriptures**, you're not just studying an old book. You're learning the words of someone who wants you to know Him. When you **keep the commandments** and feel the peace that comes from living right, you're experiencing God's influence in your life firsthand.

Over time, these experiences build on each other. You begin to recognize the Holy Ghost's voice. You start to see God's hand in your daily life. You feel His love in quiet, personal moments. And slowly, your knowledge of God shifts from what other people told you to what you've experienced for yourself.

This is the whole point of the first five lectures. They don't just want you to pass a test about God. They want you to actually **know** Him, personally and deeply. Knowing God is not just the prerequisite for faith. It is the very definition of eternal life.

You're not too young to start this journey. Every prayer you offer, every scripture you read, and every time you choose the right, you're one step closer to knowing God for yourself.
