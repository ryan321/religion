### Lesson 9.1 -- The Third Requirement

Imagine you have a really kind teacher who you trust completely. You know she's fair, you know she cares about you, and you know she keeps her promises. But then imagine you haven't done your homework in three weeks. You've been goofing off in class and ignoring the rules. Would you feel confident walking up to her desk and asking for a letter of recommendation? Probably not. Even though she's a great teacher, *you* would feel uneasy because you know you haven't been doing your part.

That's the idea behind **Lecture 6** of the Lectures on Faith, and it introduces what we'll call the **third requirement** for faith. Up to this point, we've learned two major requirements. First, you need to **know that God exists**. Second, you need to **understand His character, attributes, and nature**: that He is merciful, just, powerful, unchanging, and truthful. Those two requirements are about *God*, what He's like and who He is.

But the third requirement is about **you**. Lecture 6 teaches that in order to have real, powerful faith, you must also **know that your life is aligned with God's will**. In other words, you need to be living in a way that pleases Him.

This makes sense when you think about it. Faith is about approaching God with confidence, trusting that He will hear your prayers, guide your life, and fulfill His promises to you. But if you're living in **rebellion**, deliberately breaking commandments, being dishonest, or refusing to repent, it's very hard to approach God with that kind of confidence. Not because God has stopped loving you, but because something inside you knows that things aren't right.

Think of it like tuning a radio. God is always broadcasting His love and guidance. That signal never stops. But when your life is out of alignment with His will, it's like your radio is full of static. You can't receive the signal clearly. When you repent and bring your life back into harmony with His commandments, the static clears and you can hear Him again.

This is the most **personal** of the three requirements. The first two are things you learn. The third is something you **live**. It's not enough to know about God. You have to be willing to walk the path He's laid out. And when you do, your faith grows in ways that nothing else can produce.
