### Lesson 9.2 -- The Sacrifice of All Earthly Things

There's a line in **Lecture 6, section 7** that might be one of the boldest statements in all of the Lectures on Faith. It says: *"A religion that does not require the sacrifice of all things never has power sufficient to produce the faith necessary unto life and salvation."*

Read that again slowly. A religion that doesn't ask you to be willing to give up **everything** will never produce the kind of faith that leads to eternal life. That's a big claim, and it deserves some careful thinking.

What does "the sacrifice of all things" actually mean? It doesn't mean that God is going to take away everything you love just to test you. It means that real faith requires a **willingness** to put God first, above everything else. Your hobbies, your comfort, your popularity, your money, your time, all of it. If God asked you to give up any of those things, would you do it?

Think about some examples from the scriptures. **Abraham** was asked to sacrifice his son Isaac, the person he loved most in the world. He was willing to do it because he trusted God completely. **The early Saints** left their homes, their farms, and sometimes their families to follow the prophet and build Zion. They gave up comfort and safety because they believed God's promises were worth more.

Now, does God usually ask people to give up everything? No. Abraham got Isaac back. Many sacrifices are temporary. But the **willingness** has to be real. It can't be fake. You can't say, "I'll follow God as long as it's easy" and expect to have the same kind of faith as someone who says, "I'll follow God no matter what it costs."

Why is this so important? Because faith (real, deep, powerful faith) requires that God is the most important thing in your life. Not second. Not tied for first. **First**. When you've made that decision in your heart, something shifts. Your doubts shrink. Your confidence grows. You stop worrying about what you might lose, because you know that what God offers is worth infinitely more.

This can sound scary, especially when you're young. But remember, God is not trying to make you miserable. He's trying to make you free. When you're willing to sacrifice anything for Him, you discover that He gives back far more than He ever takes away.
