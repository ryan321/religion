### Lesson 9.3 -- Why Sacrifice Produces Faith

Have you ever been nervous about something because you hadn't fully committed to it? Maybe you signed up for a race but kept wondering if you should drop out. Or maybe you agreed to give a talk in church but kept thinking about backing out. That back-and-forth feeling is **doubt**, and it eats away at your confidence. But the moment you fully commit, "I'm doing this, no matter what," something changes. The doubt fades, and you feel a new kind of strength.

Lecture 6 teaches that the same principle applies to your relationship with God. As long as you're **holding something back**, as long as there's something in your life that you're not willing to give up for God, you'll always have a little bit of doubt. It might be small, like a quiet whisper in the back of your mind: "Would I really follow God if it cost me *that*?" And as long as that question is unsettled, your faith can't reach its full power.

But when you've **settled the question**, when you've decided deep in your heart that you would choose God over anything else, the doubt disappears. Not because your life suddenly becomes easy, but because you're no longer divided. You're all in. And that kind of total commitment produces what the lectures call **unshakeable confidence** before God.

Think about Peter, one of Jesus' apostles. Early on, Peter was bold sometimes and fearful other times. He walked on water but then sank when he doubted. He declared his loyalty to Jesus but then denied knowing Him three times on the night before the crucifixion. Peter was holding something back. He was still afraid of what following Jesus might cost him.

But after the Resurrection, Peter was a completely different man. He preached fearlessly, healed the sick, and eventually gave his own life for the gospel. What changed? Peter had settled the question. He was no longer holding anything back. And because of that, his faith became unshakeable.

The lesson here isn't that you need to be perfect right now. It's that the **direction** matters. Every time you choose God over something else, every time you let go of something you're clinging to and trust Him instead, you're settling the question a little more. And as the question gets more settled, your doubt shrinks and your faith grows.

Sacrifice doesn't weaken you. It strengthens you. It clears away the things that create doubt and replaces them with confidence in God.
