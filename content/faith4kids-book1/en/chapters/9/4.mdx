### Lesson 9.4 -- What This Means for You

If you're reading this and thinking, "I'm just a kid. I'm probably not going to be asked to sacrifice everything for God right now," you're probably right. Most people your age aren't facing the kind of dramatic tests that Abraham or the early pioneers faced. But here's the thing: the **principle** of sacrifice is already at work in your life, and it shows up in ways you might not expect.

Think about **keeping the Sabbath day holy**. Your friends might be playing video games or going to a movie on Sunday, and they want you to come along. Choosing to go to church and keep the Sabbath instead of doing what everyone else is doing is a sacrifice. It's small compared to crossing the plains, but it's real. And every time you make that choice, you're telling God, "You matter more to me than fitting in."

Or think about **paying tithing**. Maybe you earned twenty dollars mowing a lawn, and ten percent of that is two dollars. Giving that two dollars to the Lord when you could have spent it on yourself is an act of sacrifice. It says, "I trust that God's promises about tithing are true."

What about the **Word of Wisdom**? Maybe someone at school offers you something you know you shouldn't have. Saying no when everyone around you is saying yes takes courage, and it's a form of sacrifice, giving up the approval of your peers to keep God's commandments.

Each of these choices might seem small on its own. But they add up. Every small sacrifice is like a deposit in a bank account of faith. Over time, those deposits build into something powerful. You develop a pattern of choosing God, and that pattern becomes part of who you are.

Remember what we learned from Lecture 6: sacrifice produces faith because it settles the question of where your heart really is. You don't have to settle that question all at once. You settle it piece by piece, choice by choice, day by day.

So the next time you're faced with a small sacrifice, remember that you're not just following a rule. You're building faith. You're proving to yourself and to God that He comes first. The Lectures on Faith started with a simple idea: faith is a principle of power. Now you know how that power is built: through knowledge of God and through a life that reflects that knowledge.
