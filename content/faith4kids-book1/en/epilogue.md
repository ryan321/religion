# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing Lectures on Faith for Kids. You've just studied some of the deepest, most powerful doctrinal teaching in the entire Restoration -- and you understood it.

Let's review what you learned.

Faith is not just believing. It is the first principle of action and power -- the force by which God created the world and by which every miracle in the scriptures was performed (Lecture 1). The knowledge of God has been passed down from Adam through the patriarchs in an unbroken chain of testimony -- and you are part of that chain (Lecture 2).

To exercise faith, you need to know three things: that God exists, that His character is worthy of trust, and that your life is in line with His will (Lectures 3-5). You learned that God is merciful, gracious, unchangeable, and no respecter of persons. You learned that He is all-knowing, all-powerful, just, merciful, and truthful -- and that if He lacked any single attribute, faith in Him would be impossible (Lecture 4). You learned that the Father and the Son are two separate beings with glorified bodies, and the Holy Ghost is a personage of Spirit -- three beings perfectly united in purpose (Lecture 5).

You learned that real faith requires the willingness to sacrifice everything -- because as long as you're holding something back, you'll always have doubt (Lecture 6). And you learned that faith produces miracles, knowledge, righteousness, and ultimately salvation -- eternal life in the presence of God (Lecture 7).

These are not abstract ideas. They are the foundation for your prayers, your covenants, your temple worship, and your daily walk with God. Every time you pray, you are exercising faith. Every time you sacrifice, you are building faith. Every time you choose God over the world, your faith grows stronger.

The Lectures on Faith were written for the School of the Prophets -- adults preparing to lead a church. But the principles are for everyone, including you. You don't have to wait to exercise faith. You can start right now -- and you already have.

Thank you for learning with us.
