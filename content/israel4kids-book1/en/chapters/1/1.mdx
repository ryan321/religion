### Lesson 1.1 -- The Land God Promised

Have you ever made a really important promise to someone? Maybe you promised to take care of a pet or to always be there for a friend. Now imagine God making a promise -- not just to one person, but to an entire family that would grow into a whole nation. That is exactly what happened with Abraham.

Thousands of years ago, God made a **covenant** with a man named **Abraham**. A covenant is not just a regular promise. It is a two-way agreement -- God promised to do certain things, and Abraham promised to do certain things in return. One of the biggest parts of that covenant was land. God told Abraham that his descendants would inherit a specific piece of land -- the land we now call **Israel**.

But Abraham did not just walk in and claim it right away. The promise passed down through his family. It went to his son **Isaac**, then to Isaac's son **Jacob**. God changed Jacob's name to **Israel**, and Jacob had twelve sons. Those twelve sons became the fathers of the **twelve tribes of Israel**. So when we talk about "the children of Israel," we are talking about Jacob's descendants -- a huge family that became a nation.

Here is the thing that makes this covenant so important: almost everything that happens in the Old Testament connects back to it. Wars, kings, prophets, exile, return -- all of it ties to God's promise to Abraham about this land and this people. In the Pearl of Great Price, you can read Abraham's own account of receiving this covenant (Abraham 2:6-11). It was not just about land, either. God promised Abraham that through his descendants, all the nations of the earth would be blessed. Latter-day Saints understand that this blessing includes the gospel and the priesthood being shared with the whole world.

The covenant also shows up in the Book of Mormon. Nephi and other prophets talk about the promises God made to the house of Israel and how those promises will be fulfilled in the last days. So when you read about Israel in the Old Testament, you are reading your own family history -- because through baptism and the gospel, you are part of the house of Israel too.

The land God promised was not random. It sat right in the middle of the ancient world, connecting great empires. And that location would shape everything that happened to Israel for centuries to come.
