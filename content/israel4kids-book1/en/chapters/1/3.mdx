### Lesson 1.3 -- Joshua and the Conquest

Moses had led the children of Israel for forty years -- through the Red Sea, through the wilderness, and right up to the edge of the promised land. But Moses would not be the one to take them in. That job fell to his assistant, **Joshua**.

Think about how Joshua must have felt. He had been Moses' right-hand man for decades. Now Moses was gone, and the entire nation was looking at Joshua, waiting for him to tell them what to do. God spoke to Joshua directly and told him to "be strong and of a good courage" (Joshua 1:6). That phrase comes up several times -- Joshua needed to hear it more than once.

The first obstacle was the **Jordan River**. It was flood season, and the river was running high. But God performed a miracle similar to the Red Sea crossing. When the priests carrying the **Ark of the Covenant** stepped into the water, the river stopped flowing and the people walked across on dry ground.

Next came **Jericho** -- a walled city blocking Israel's path. God gave Joshua unusual battle instructions. No battering rams. No siege towers. Instead, the Israelites marched around the city once a day for six days. On the seventh day, they marched around it seven times, the priests blew their trumpets, the people shouted, and the walls collapsed. It was a clear message: this victory came from the Lord, not from military power.

After Jericho, Joshua led campaigns throughout Canaan over several years. There were battles in the south and battles in the north. Some cities were conquered. Others were not. The **conquest was not instant or total**. Many **Canaanite** groups remained in the land, and that would cause problems for Israel later on.

Why did God command the conquest? This is a hard question, and it is okay to sit with it. The scriptures tell us the Canaanite nations had become deeply wicked -- practicing things like child sacrifice. God had been patient with them for generations (see Genesis 15:16), but the time for judgment had come. Israel was meant to establish a holy nation in this land, a people who would keep God's covenant and prepare the way for the Savior.

Joshua's leadership reminds us that God keeps His promises, even when the path is not easy. The land God promised to Abraham was finally becoming Israel's home.
