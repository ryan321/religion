### Lesson 11.1 -- The Revolt Begins

Sometimes the bravest thing a person can do is say "no." That is exactly how the Maccabean revolt started -- with one old man who refused to obey an evil command.

In the small village of **Modiin**, about halfway between Jerusalem and the coast, Seleucid soldiers arrived to enforce Antiochus's orders. They set up a pagan altar and told the villagers to sacrifice to Greek gods. They turned to the local priest, an elderly man named **Mattathias**, and told him to go first. If the priest obeyed, the rest of the village would follow.

Mattathias refused. Flat out said no. But then something else happened -- another Jewish man stepped forward and agreed to make the pagan sacrifice. Mattathias was so furious that he killed the man right there, then turned and killed the Seleucid officer too. There was no going back after that.

Mattathias grabbed his **five sons** and fled into the hill country of Judea. Word spread fast. Other faithful Jews joined them -- families who had been hiding in caves, men who had been waiting for someone to take a stand. They formed a guerrilla fighting force, launching surprise raids on Seleucid patrols and tearing down pagan altars in Jewish towns.

This was an impossible fight by any human measure. The Seleucid Empire had professional soldiers, war elephants, and vast resources. Mattathias's group had farming tools, courage, and their covenants. They were outnumbered and outgunned in every way.

The family became known as the **Maccabees**, which likely means **"the hammer."** It was a nickname that stuck, probably because of the way they struck hard and fast against the enemy. Mattathias was old, and he did not live long after the revolt began. But before he died, he passed leadership to his son **Judah** -- who turned out to be one of the greatest military leaders in Jewish history.

The Maccabean revolt is a reminder that God does not always wait for perfect conditions or overwhelming odds to be in your favor. Sometimes faithfulness means standing up when everyone else sits down. Mattathias did not know he would win. He just knew he could not bow to a false god. That was enough to start.
