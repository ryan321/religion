### Lesson 11.3 -- The Hasmonean Dynasty

After Judah Maccabee's victories, his family did not just fade into the background. They built something that had not existed for over four hundred years: an **independent Jewish kingdom**. The Maccabees' family name was **Hasmonean**, so this kingdom is called the **Hasmonean dynasty**.

From about **140 to 63 BC** (roughly eighty years) the Jews governed themselves. Think about that. Since the Babylonians destroyed Jerusalem in 586 BC, the Jews had been ruled by one empire after another: Babylon, Persia, the Ptolemies, the Seleucids. Now, for the first time in over four centuries, they had their own king, their own army, and their own government. It must have felt amazing.

But there was a problem. Actually, several problems.

First, the Hasmonean kings were not from the **line of David**. God had promised that the Messiah would come through David's family, and that the rightful king of Israel would be a descendant of David. The Hasmoneans were from the tribe of Levi, the priestly tribe. They were priests, not kings. Many faithful Jews were uncomfortable with this from the start.

Second, the Hasmonean rulers made themselves **both king and high priest** at the same time. Under the Law of Moses, these were supposed to be separate roles. Combining them meant one person had all the political and religious power, with no one to hold them accountable. That is a recipe for trouble, and trouble is exactly what they got.

Third, the dynasty became **corrupt**. Later Hasmonean rulers acted more like Greek kings than Jewish leaders. They fought bloody wars of expansion, forcing conquered peoples to convert to Judaism at sword-point. Family members murdered each other for the throne. Sons killed mothers. Brothers killed brothers. It was ugly.

This is a hard but important lesson. The Maccabees started out as heroes of faith, people who risked everything for God. But within a few generations, their descendants were no better than the pagan rulers they had overthrown. Having political power is not the same as being righteous. Freedom from outside oppression does not automatically make you faithful. What matters is what you do with your freedom.
