### Lesson 11.4 -- The Problems with Independence

You might think that once the Jews had their own kingdom again, everything would be great. They had their temple, their land, and their freedom. But political independence does not automatically make a nation righteous. The Hasmonean period proved that painfully.

As the Hasmonean kings became more worldly and corrupt, faithful Jews started splitting into groups with very different ideas about how to live and worship. Three of the most important groups were the **Pharisees**, the **Sadducees**, and the **Essenes**. You will hear a lot about these groups when you study the New Testament, so it helps to understand where they came from.

The **Pharisees** believed in strict obedience to the Torah, plus a huge set of extra rules and traditions that they said Moses had passed down orally. They believed in resurrection, angels, and a coming Messiah. Many ordinary Jews respected them. They were the group that would later clash most with Jesus, not because they did not care about God's law, but because they added so much to it that they sometimes missed the point.

The **Sadducees** were the wealthy, priestly elite. They controlled the temple and cooperated with whoever was in power. They only accepted the five books of Moses as scripture and did not believe in resurrection or angels. They were practical, political, and focused on keeping things stable, even if that meant compromising.

The **Essenes** thought both groups had it wrong. They saw the temple leadership as corrupt and the whole system as broken. So they withdrew from society. Some lived in small communities in the desert, like the group at **Qumran** that produced the **Dead Sea Scrolls**. They lived strict, disciplined lives, waiting for God to intervene and set things right.

Here is the biggest mistake of all. When two Hasmonean brothers (**Hyrcanus II** and **Aristobulus II**) got into a civil war over the throne, they could not settle it themselves. So both of them sent messengers to the Roman general **Pompey**, asking him to come and pick a side. They literally invited the most powerful military force on earth to march into their country. It was like two kids fighting over a toy and asking the neighborhood bully to decide who gets it. Rome came in and never left.

The hard-won independence of the Maccabees ended not because a foreign army invaded, but because Jewish leaders handed it away.
