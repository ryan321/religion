### Lesson 12.4 -- The World Jesus Was Born Into

By the time Jesus was born, the Jewish people had been living under foreign rule for centuries. Think about that timeline. The Babylonians conquered them in 586 BC. Then came the Persians, then the Greeks, then a brief period of independence under the Hasmoneans, and then Rome. By the time of Jesus' birth, most Jews had never known what it felt like to be truly free.

But they had not given up hope. The prophets (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Daniel, and many others) had promised that a **Messiah** would come. This anointed one would be from the **line of David**, and he would establish a kingdom that would never end. After centuries of suffering, the longing for the Messiah was intense.

The problem was that most people had a very specific picture of what the Messiah would look like. They expected a **military king**, a warrior like David who would raise an army, crush Rome, and restore Israel to political glory. They wanted a Maccabee, but bigger. A champion with a sword. When the Messiah turned out to be a carpenter's son born in a stable, it was not what they had in mind.

The religious landscape was fractured. The **Pharisees** had built an elaborate system of rules on top of the Torah. They were sincere in many ways, but they sometimes cared more about the rules than about the people the rules were supposed to help. The **Sadducees** ran the temple and cooperated with Rome. They did not believe in resurrection or angels, and they were more interested in politics than prophecy. The **Essenes** had withdrawn to places like Qumran, copying scriptures and waiting for a great final battle between good and evil.

Into this divided, occupied, expectant world, **Jesus Christ** was born in **Bethlehem**, the city of David, just as the prophet Micah had foretold (Micah 5:2). He was laid in a **manger** because there was no room in the inn. Shepherds came to see him because angels told them to. Wise men followed a star. And an old man named **Simeon** held the baby in the temple and said he could now die in peace because he had seen the Lord's salvation (Luke 2:29-30).

The entire Old Testament, every covenant, every prophecy, every exile, every return, had been pointing to this moment. Every lesson you have studied in this book has been part of the road that led here. God had been preparing a people, a land, and a world for the coming of his Son. And now he was here.
