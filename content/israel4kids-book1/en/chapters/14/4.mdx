### Lesson 14.4 -- Civil War Inside the Walls

Here is one of the saddest parts of this whole story. While the Roman army was closing in on Jerusalem from the outside, the Jewish defenders were tearing each other apart on the inside.

By the time Titus and his legions arrived at Jerusalem's walls, **three rival factions** controlled different sections of the city. Each faction had its own leader, its own fighters, and its own idea about how to run things. Instead of uniting against the common enemy, they fought each other, sometimes in brutal street battles that killed their own people.

One faction was led by **Simon bar Giora**, who controlled much of the upper city. Another was led by **John of Gischala**, who held the temple mount. A third, smaller group also occupied part of the temple area. These men did not trust each other. They ambushed each other's forces. They blocked each other's supplies. It was a civil war inside a besieged city.

The worst moment came when one of the factions **deliberately burned the city's food stores**. Think about that. With a Roman army camped outside, ready to starve them out, someone inside destroyed the food on purpose. The idea was to force everyone to fight instead of waiting behind the walls. But it was a catastrophic decision. It guaranteed that starvation would ravage the city long before the Romans ever broke through.

This kind of division is a theme you see throughout Israel's history. When the people were united, they accomplished incredible things. When they turned on each other, disaster followed. The same pattern shows up in the Book of Mormon. Think of how often the Nephites were weakened more by internal pride and division than by Lamanite armies.

The defenders of Jerusalem were brave. Many fought with extraordinary courage. But bravery alone is not enough when your side is at war with itself. The internal fighting wasted lives, destroyed supplies, and made it impossible to mount a coordinated defense. By the time the Romans broke through the outer walls, Jerusalem was already bleeding from the inside.
