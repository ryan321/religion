### Lesson 15.2 -- The Temple Burns

In August of 70 AD, the Roman soldiers broke through the walls of the **temple complex**. What happened next would change the Jewish world forever.

According to **Josephus**, General Titus actually wanted to **preserve the temple**. It was one of the most magnificent buildings in the world, and keeping it intact would have been a trophy for Rome. Titus reportedly gave orders not to destroy it. But in the chaos of battle, a Roman soldier grabbed a burning piece of wood and threw it through a window into one of the temple chambers.

The fire caught. And it spread.

The temple was built with massive stones, but it also had enormous amounts of wood: cedar beams, wooden doors, wooden scaffolding. Gold decorations melted in the heat and ran between the stones. Soldiers, seeing the gold, later pried the stones apart to get it, which is why almost nothing remains of the temple structure today. The flames consumed room after room. Defenders fought and died in the burning corridors. By the time the fire burned out, **Herod's Temple**, one of the greatest buildings of the ancient world, was gone.

The date of the destruction was the **9th of Av** on the Jewish calendar. Jewish tradition holds that this was the very same date the **Babylonians had destroyed Solomon's Temple** back in 586 BC. Whether or not the dates line up exactly, the connection is powerful. Both temples fell, both times marking a devastating turning point for the people of Israel.

The Romans did not stop with the temple. They plundered the city, killed thousands, and enslaved many of the survivors. The **Arch of Titus** still stands in Rome today, carved with images of Roman soldiers carrying away the golden **menorah** and other sacred objects from the temple.

For Latter-day Saints, the destruction of the temple is especially significant because Jesus himself had prophesied it. Standing on the Mount of Olives, looking at the temple, he told his disciples: "There shall not be left here one stone upon another, that shall not be thrown down" (Matthew 24:2). That prophecy was fulfilled exactly as he said.
