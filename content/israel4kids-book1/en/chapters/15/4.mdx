### Lesson 15.4 -- The Story Continues

This book ends at 70 AD, but Israel's story is far from over.

After the destruction of Jerusalem, the Jewish people entered a long period of **exile and dispersion**. They scattered across the Roman Empire and beyond: to Babylon, North Africa, Spain, Eastern Europe, and eventually every corner of the world. This scattering is called the **Diaspora**, a Greek word meaning "dispersion."

For nearly **2,000 years**, Jewish communities survived without a homeland, without a temple, and often without basic rights. They faced persecution, forced conversions, expulsions, and worse. And yet they survived. They kept their identity, their faith, their traditions, and their connection to the land of Israel alive through centuries of hardship. That survival is one of the most remarkable stories in all of human history.

How did they do it? Through the very things that Rabbinic Judaism preserved: the Torah, the synagogue, the Sabbath, family traditions, and an unshakable belief that God's covenant with Abraham was still in force. Every Passover, Jewish families said, "Next year in Jerusalem." They never forgot.

For Latter-day Saints, this long history connects directly to what is happening right now. The **gathering of Israel** is one of the central themes of the Restoration. The Lord told Abraham that through his seed, all the nations of the earth would be blessed (Abraham 2:11). The Book of Mormon prophets (Nephi, Jacob, and many others) spoke of a day when Israel would be gathered again, both physically and spiritually.

President Russell M. Nelson has called the gathering of Israel "the most important thing taking place on earth today." Every person who learns about Jesus Christ, makes covenants through baptism and in the temple, and strives to follow him is part of that gathering. That includes you.

The covenant God made with Abraham thousands of years ago is the same covenant you participate in when you are baptized and when you take the sacrament. The history you have studied in this book, from Abraham to Moses, from David to the exile, from the Maccabees to the destruction of the temple, is **your history**. You are part of the house of Israel, and the story is still being written.

So what comes next? That depends, in part, on you.
