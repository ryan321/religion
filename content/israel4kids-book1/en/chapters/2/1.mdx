### Lesson 2.1 -- The Pattern

Have you ever noticed a pattern in your own life? Maybe every time you stay up too late, the next day is miserable. You know it will happen, but you keep doing it anyway. The book of Judges describes a pattern just like that -- except it played out over an entire nation for about 300 years.

Here is how the **cycle of the judges** worked:

**Step 1: Faithfulness.** The Israelites worship the Lord, keep His commandments, and life goes well. The land is at peace. Crops grow. Enemies leave them alone.

**Step 2: Apostasy.** Over time, the people drift. They start worshiping the **false gods** of their neighbors -- idols like **Baal** and **Ashtoreth**. They forget the covenant. They stop teaching their children about the Lord.

**Step 3: Oppression.** Because Israel has broken the covenant, God allows enemy nations to conquer and oppress them. Midianites raid their farms. Philistines take their land. Moabites rule over them. Life becomes very hard.

**Step 4: Crying out.** After years of suffering, the people finally remember the Lord. They cry out for help. They repent.

**Step 5: Deliverance.** God raises up a **judge** -- a leader chosen to deliver Israel from their enemies. This is not a courtroom judge with a robe and gavel. These judges were more like military leaders and spiritual guides rolled into one. They rallied the tribes, fought battles, and reminded the people to follow God.

**Step 6: Peace.** With the enemy defeated, the land rests in peace -- usually for the lifetime of that judge. But after the judge dies, the whole cycle starts over again.

This cycle repeated over and over. Each time, you might expect the Israelites to have learned their lesson. But they kept falling back into the same mistakes. The book of Judges is honest about human nature -- people forget. Even people who have seen miracles forget.

Latter-day Saints can recognize this pattern. In the Book of Mormon, the Nephites go through a similar cycle that we call the **pride cycle**. Prosperity leads to pride, pride leads to sin, sin leads to suffering, suffering leads to humility, and humility brings blessings -- which leads right back to prosperity. It is the same pattern, different continent.

Understanding this cycle is the key to understanding everything else in the book of Judges.
