### Lesson 2.2 -- Deborah, Gideon, and Samson

The book of Judges names about a dozen judges, but three stand out because their stories teach very different lessons about faith, obedience, and choices.

**Deborah** was a **prophetess**, a woman who received revelation from God. She held court under a palm tree, and people came from all over Israel for her wisdom and judgment. When the Canaanite general **Sisera** oppressed Israel with 900 iron chariots, Deborah called on a man named **Barak** to lead the army. Barak said he would only go if Deborah came with him. She agreed but told him the glory of the victory would go to a woman, not to him. The battle was won, and Sisera was killed, just as Deborah said. Her story shows that God calls whoever He needs, regardless of what the surrounding culture expects.

**Gideon** was threshing wheat in a winepress, hiding from the **Midianites**, who had been raiding Israel's farms for seven years. An angel appeared and called him a "mighty man of valor," which must have seemed like a joke at the time. God told Gideon to lead an army against Midian. Gideon gathered 32,000 men, but God said that was too many. If Israel won with a huge army, they would take the credit themselves. So God shrank the army, first to 10,000, then to just **300 men**. With torches, trumpets, and clay jars, those 300 threw the Midianite camp into confusion, and the enemy fled. The lesson was unmistakable: the victory belonged to the Lord, not to human strength.

**Samson** is the most complicated judge. God gave him extraordinary **physical strength**. He tore a lion apart with his bare hands, broke ropes like thread, and knocked down a building with his arms. But Samson constantly made terrible choices. He ignored his parents' counsel, broke his **Nazirite vows**, and trusted people he should not have trusted. His relationship with **Delilah** led to his capture, blindness, and imprisonment by the Philistines. In the end, he brought down the Philistine temple, defeating more enemies in his death than in his life, but at a terrible cost.

Samson's story is as much a **warning** as a triumph. Having gifts from God does not matter if you throw them away through bad decisions. Deborah and Gideon trusted in the Lord and used what they had been given. Samson trusted in himself and lost everything.
