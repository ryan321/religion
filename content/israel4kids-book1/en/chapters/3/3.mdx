### Lesson 3.3 -- David's Rise

David was not the obvious choice for anything. He was the **youngest son of Jesse**, from the tribe of **Judah**, and his family lived in **Bethlehem**, a small town that nobody thought much about. While his older brothers looked like warriors, David was out in the fields watching sheep.

But God sees differently than people do. When the Lord sent Samuel to anoint a new king from Jesse's family, Samuel looked at the older brothers and assumed one of them must be the one. God told him no: **"The Lord looketh on the heart"** (1 Samuel 16:7). One by one, Jesse's sons were passed over until someone remembered the youngest boy out with the flock. David was brought in, and Samuel anointed him right there.

David's first claim to fame came on the battlefield. The Philistines had a champion named **Goliath**, a giant over nine feet tall, wearing heavy armor, carrying a massive spear. For forty days, Goliath challenged Israel to send someone to fight him in single combat. Nobody volunteered. The entire Israelite army was terrified.

David showed up bringing food to his brothers and heard Goliath's taunts. He was offended, not scared. He told King Saul he would fight the giant. Saul tried to give David his own armor, but it did not fit, and David was not used to it. Instead, David took five smooth stones and a **sling**. He told Goliath, "Thou comest to me with a sword, and with a spear, and with a shield: but I come to thee in the name of the Lord of hosts" (1 Samuel 17:45). One stone. One hit. Goliath went down.

After the victory, Saul brought David to court. David became best friends with Saul's son **Jonathan**, one of the great friendships in all of scripture. David was a gifted **musician** who played the harp to calm Saul's troubled mind. He was also a successful military commander. The people loved him. They sang songs about him. And that is exactly when things got dangerous.

Saul's jealousy turned violent. He threw a spear at David twice. He sent David on missions designed to get him killed. Eventually, David had to flee. He spent years as a **fugitive**, hiding in caves, gathering a band of loyal followers, and constantly staying one step ahead of Saul's army.

During this time, David had at least two chances to kill Saul. Once, he crept close enough to cut a piece of Saul's robe. Another time, he stood over Saul while he slept. Both times, David **refused to harm him**. His reason was simple and firm: Saul was "the Lord's anointed." Even though Saul was trying to murder him, David would not take matters into his own hands. He trusted that God would work things out in God's own time.

Saul eventually died in battle against the Philistines on Mount Gilboa. David mourned him genuinely. And then, finally, David became king, first over Judah, and later over all twelve tribes.
