### Lesson 4.1 -- Solomon's Wisdom

When King David grew old, his son **Solomon** became the next king of Israel. Solomon was young, and ruling an entire nation is a huge responsibility. Imagine being put in charge of your whole school, not just your class, but every student, every teacher, every rule. That's a tiny taste of what Solomon faced.

One night, God appeared to Solomon in a dream and made an incredible offer: "Ask for whatever you want." Think about that for a second. If God told you that you could have anything, what would you pick? A mansion? Superpowers? Solomon could have asked for riches, long life, or victory over his enemies. Instead, he asked for **wisdom**: the ability to know right from wrong so he could lead God's people well.

God was pleased with that answer. Because Solomon did not ask for selfish things, God gave him wisdom *and* everything else: wealth, honor, and fame beyond any king before or after him. Solomon became the wisest person in the ancient world. He composed thousands of proverbs and songs. Kings and queens from distant lands heard about him and wanted to meet him.

One of the most famous visitors was the **Queen of Sheba**. She traveled a long way (probably from modern-day Yemen or Ethiopia) to test Solomon with hard questions. When she saw his wisdom, his palace, and how well his kingdom ran, she was completely amazed. She said that the reports she had heard did not even tell half the truth.

Solomon's wisdom was not just about being smart. In the scriptures, wisdom means understanding God's ways and applying them to real life. It is practical, not just bookish. Solomon judged difficult court cases, managed trade routes, and kept peace with surrounding nations, all because he relied on the understanding God gave him.

There is a lesson here for you, too. In Doctrine and Covenants 42:61, the Lord promises that if you ask, you will receive knowledge and wisdom. You probably will not be running a kingdom anytime soon, but you make decisions every day: how to treat a friend, how to handle a tough situation, whether to do the right thing when nobody is watching. Asking Heavenly Father for wisdom is something you can do right now, just like Solomon did.
