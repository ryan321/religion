### Lesson 4.2 -- Building the Temple

Solomon's father, David, had wanted to build a permanent house for the Lord. For years, the Israelites had worshipped in the **tabernacle**, a portable tent they carried through the wilderness. David thought God deserved something grander. But the Lord told David that because he had been a man of war, his son would be the one to build it. So David gathered materials, drew up plans, and left everything ready for Solomon.

When Solomon became king, he got to work on what would become his greatest achievement: the **First Temple** in Jerusalem. This was not a small project. Think of the biggest building you have ever seen, maybe a stadium or a skyscraper. Now imagine building something that impressive without trucks, cranes, or power tools. Everything had to be cut, shaped, and moved by hand.

Solomon brought in the finest craftsmen. He made a deal with King Hiram of Tyre for massive **cedar logs** from Lebanon, trees famous for their beauty and strength. Thousands of workers quarried enormous stones for the foundation. The stones were cut so precisely at the quarry that no hammer or chisel was heard at the temple site during construction. That detail tells you something about how sacred this project was.

The temple took **seven years** to build. Inside, the walls were lined with cedar and overlaid with pure gold. Carved angels, palm trees, and flowers decorated the walls. The **Ark of the Covenant** (the sacred chest containing the tablets of the Ten Commandments) was placed in the innermost room, called the **Holy of Holies**.

When the temple was finally finished, Solomon held a grand dedication ceremony. He gathered all of Israel. Priests carried the Ark into the Holy of Holies. And then something incredible happened: the **glory of the Lord** filled the temple like a thick cloud. It was so powerful that the priests could not even stand up to do their work. God's presence was physically, unmistakably there.

Solomon offered a long, beautiful prayer dedicating the temple to the Lord. He asked God to hear the prayers of anyone who turned toward this holy place, whether they were Israelites or foreigners. It was a moment the entire nation would remember for generations.
