### Lesson 4.4 -- Solomon's Decline

Solomon started out so well. He asked for wisdom, built the temple, and led Israel into its most prosperous era. But the later years of his reign tell a very different story. Even the wisest person can make terrible choices -- and Solomon did.

The trouble started with **marriages**. In the ancient world, kings often married princesses from other nations to seal political alliances. Solomon took this to an extreme. The Bible says he had **700 wives and 300 concubines**, many of them from nations God had specifically warned Israel to avoid -- Moabites, Ammonites, Edomites, Sidonians, and Hittites. The problem was not just the number. These women brought their own religions with them.

As Solomon grew older, his foreign wives **turned his heart after other gods**. He started building altars and shrines to gods like Ashtoreth, Chemosh, and Molech -- right near Jerusalem. The man who had built the Lord's temple was now building places of worship for false gods. It is hard to overstate how serious this was. Solomon was not just breaking a rule. He was breaking the covenant -- the very first commandment: "Thou shalt have no other gods before me."

God was angry. He told Solomon directly that because of this, the kingdom would be **torn apart**. Not during Solomon's lifetime -- God would wait, for David's sake -- but under Solomon's son. And even then, one tribe would remain with David's descendants so the promises God made to David would not be completely lost.

On top of the idol worship, Solomon had placed **heavy taxes** and forced labor on the people to fund his massive building projects. The northern tribes especially resented this. They felt like they were doing all the work while Solomon and his court in Jerusalem lived in luxury. That resentment simmered for years and would soon boil over.

Solomon's story is a warning. Having great gifts does not protect you from bad choices. Wisdom only works if you keep using it. Solomon stopped listening to the very God who gave him everything. In the end, his personal choices cost his family the kingdom and set Israel on a path toward division and disaster.
