### Lesson 5.1 -- The Split

After Solomon died, his son **Rehoboam** became king. Right away, he faced a crisis. The northern tribes came to him and basically said, "Your father worked us to death with heavy taxes and forced labor. Lighten the load, and we will serve you loyally."

Rehoboam had a choice. He asked two groups for advice. First, the **old advisors**, men who had served Solomon for years. They told him to listen to the people. "Be kind to them," they said. "Lighten their burden, and they will follow you forever." That was wise counsel.

Then Rehoboam asked his **young friends**, guys he had grown up with. Their advice was the opposite. They said, "Tell the people that your little finger is thicker than your father's waist. If Solomon was tough, you will be tougher." In other words, do not give an inch. Show them who is boss.

Rehoboam listened to his young friends. It was one of the worst decisions in the entire Bible.

When the northern tribes heard Rehoboam's harsh answer, they revolted. A man named **Jeroboam**, a former official under Solomon who had already clashed with the king, became their leader. **Ten tribes** broke away and formed their own nation, which they called **Israel**. Only the tribes of **Judah** and **Benjamin** stayed loyal to Rehoboam. They became the kingdom of **Judah**.

Just like that, the **united kingdom** that Saul, David, and Solomon had built over roughly 120 years was finished. Gone. One bad decision by an arrogant young king, and the nation split in two.

This was not a surprise to God. He had already told Solomon this would happen because of Solomon's unfaithfulness. A prophet named Ahijah had even torn a cloak into twelve pieces and given ten to Jeroboam as a sign. But just because God knew it was coming does not mean it was not a disaster. The split would bring centuries of conflict, weakness, and heartbreak for both kingdoms.

Think about what you can learn from Rehoboam. When people you trust give you good advice, listen. Especially when it lines up with what is kind and fair.
