### Lesson 5.4 -- Two Kingdoms, Two Paths

After the split, the Bible tracks **two parallel stories**: the northern kingdom of Israel and the southern kingdom of Judah. Sometimes they were allies. Sometimes they fought each other. But their paths were very different, and so were their endings.

The **northern kingdom** never had a righteous dynasty. Not one. Its kings came and went through assassinations, coups, and rebellions. One family would seize the throne, rule for a while, and then be overthrown by the next. In about 200 years, Israel went through **nineteen kings** from multiple families. The golden calves Jeroboam set up were never removed. Baal worship spread. God sent prophets (Elijah, Elisha, Amos, Hosea) but the nation as a whole refused to listen.

The **southern kingdom** had its share of wicked kings, but it also had something the north never did: **David's covenant**. God had promised that David's line would endure. That promise acted like a safety net. When a terrible king came along, God did not destroy Judah completely. He preserved it for David's sake. And when a good king like Hezekiah or Josiah rose up, there was real reform and renewal.

The two kingdoms sometimes joined forces against common enemies, like the Assyrians or the Arameans. Other times they went to war against each other, which must have been heartbreaking. These were cousins and brothers fighting on opposite sides.

Both kingdoms would eventually fall, but in different ways and at different times. The northern kingdom would be conquered first, by **Assyria** in about 722 BC. The southern kingdom would last about 136 years longer before falling to **Babylon** around 586 BC. The consequences were also different. The northern tribes were scattered and largely lost to history. Judah was taken into exile but eventually returned.

Understanding these two paths helps you make sense of the rest of the Old Testament. When you read about prophets, wars, and miracles in the coming chapters, you will need to know which kingdom the story is happening in. The split changed everything.
