### Lesson 6.1 -- What Prophets Did

When you hear the word **prophet**, you might think of someone who predicts the future. And prophets did sometimes foretell things that had not happened yet. But that was not their main job. A prophet's primary role was to be **God's messenger**, calling people and kings back to the covenant when they strayed.

Think of a prophet like a referee in a basketball game. The referee does not play the game. The referee watches and blows the whistle when someone breaks the rules. Prophets did something similar. When Israel or Judah broke their covenant with God (worshipping idols, mistreating the poor, ignoring the commandments) prophets stepped in and said, "Stop. You are breaking the rules. Here is what will happen if you do not change."

Prophets **warned of consequences**. If the people did not repent, destruction, famine, or exile would come. But prophets also **promised blessings**. If the people turned back to God, He would heal their land, protect them, and keep His promises. The message was always the same at its core: repent and return to the covenant.

This was not an easy job. Prophets had to speak **uncomfortable truths to powerful people**. Imagine walking into a king's throne room and telling him that God was angry with him. That took serious courage. Kings did not always take it well. Prophets were mocked, beaten, thrown in prison, and sometimes killed. Being a prophet was often a lonely, dangerous calling.

As Latter-day Saints, you understand that prophets are part of a **continuing pattern**. God did not just speak through prophets in ancient Israel and then stop. He speaks through **living prophets** in every dispensation, including right now. The prophet and apostles you sustain today serve the same basic role that Isaiah, Elijah, and Jeremiah served thousands of years ago. They call people to repentance, testify of Christ, and help the covenant people stay on track.

The ancient prophets of Israel and Judah were some of the bravest people who ever lived. Their stories show what it looks like to stand for God even when everyone else is going the other direction.
