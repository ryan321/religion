### Lesson 6.3 -- Isaiah: The Prophet of the Messiah

While Elijah and Elisha served in the northern kingdom, the prophet **Isaiah** served in the southern kingdom of **Judah**. He lived during a dangerous time -- the mighty **Assyrian Empire** was expanding and threatening to swallow small nations whole. Isaiah had to guide kings and people through real, immediate crises. But his writings go far beyond his own time.

Isaiah is sometimes called the **"Fifth Gospel"** because his prophecies about Jesus Christ are so detailed and powerful that they read almost like the New Testament. Hundreds of years before Jesus was born, Isaiah described events that would unfold with stunning precision.

He prophesied that a **virgin would conceive and bear a son** called Immanuel, meaning "God with us" (Isaiah 7:14). He described a child who would be called **"Wonderful, Counselor, The Mighty God, The Everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace"** (Isaiah 9:6). And in chapter 53, Isaiah painted a picture of the **suffering servant** -- someone who would be "despised and rejected," who would bear our griefs, be wounded for our transgressions, and be led like a lamb to the slaughter. When you read those verses, it is impossible not to see Jesus Christ.

Isaiah also spoke about events in his own day. He counseled King Hezekiah when the Assyrians surrounded Jerusalem. He warned against trusting in alliances with Egypt instead of trusting God. He called out corruption, greed, and empty religious rituals. He was not just a fortune-teller. He was a covenant enforcer.

Latter-day Saints have a special connection to Isaiah. His words appear extensively in the **Book of Mormon**. When Nephi taught his people, he quoted Isaiah at length. Nephi said, **"My soul delighteth in the words of Isaiah"** (2 Nephi 11:2). The Savior Himself, when He visited the Nephites, quoted Isaiah and told the people to search his words carefully.

Isaiah can be hard to read -- his language is poetic and full of symbols. But the more you study him, the more you see how everything points to Christ. Isaiah saw the Savior's mission clearly, and his testimony has strengthened believers for nearly 3,000 years.
