### Lesson 6.4 -- Jeremiah: The Prophet Nobody Listened To

Imagine being given a message that you know is true, but every time you share it, people get angry, call you a liar, and try to hurt you. That was **Jeremiah's** life.

Jeremiah served as a prophet in **Judah** during its final decades -- the years right before the **Babylonian destruction** of Jerusalem. God called Jeremiah when he was still young. Jeremiah protested, saying he was just a child and did not know how to speak. But God told him, "Do not say you are a child. I ordained you a prophet before you were born" (Jeremiah 1:5). That verse is meaningful to Latter-day Saints -- it tells us that God knows us and has plans for us before we even arrive on earth.

Jeremiah's message was simple and deeply unpopular: **Jerusalem would fall**. The Babylonians were coming, and unless the people repented, the city and the temple would be destroyed. He told the people to surrender to Babylon rather than fight -- because fighting would only make the destruction worse.

Nobody wanted to hear it. The kings ignored him. The priests accused him of treason. The people hated him. He was **thrown in prison**. At one point, officials lowered him into a **muddy cistern** -- basically a pit used to collect rainwater -- and left him to sink in the mud and die. A kind official named Ebed-Melech rescued him, but Jeremiah's suffering did not stop.

The heartbreaking thing is that **Jeremiah was right about everything**. Jerusalem did fall. The temple was destroyed. The people were taken captive to Babylon. Everything he warned about came true exactly as he said.

Jeremiah's story connects directly to the **Book of Mormon**. Lehi's family lived in Jerusalem during Jeremiah's time. The Book of Mormon tells us that "many prophets" were warning the people, and Lehi was one of them (1 Nephi 1:4). Lehi's family left before the destruction came. If they had stayed and ignored the warnings -- like most people did -- there would be no Book of Mormon.

Jeremiah teaches us something important about prophets: being right is not the same as being popular. Sometimes the truth is hard to hear. But ignoring a prophet's warning does not make the warning go away. It just means you are not prepared when it comes true.
