### Lesson 7.2 -- Israel Falls (722 BC)

Have you ever watched a sandcastle slowly wash away as the waves creep in? You can see it happening, but you cannot stop it. That is what the end of the Northern Kingdom of Israel looked like: a slow collapse that everyone could see coming.

For about 200 years after the kingdom split, the **Northern Kingdom of Israel** had king after king who turned away from God. A few were okay, but most were terrible. They set up idol worship, ignored the prophets, and made alliances with foreign nations instead of trusting in the Lord. God sent prophet after prophet to warn them, but they refused to change.

In **722 BC**, the end finally came. The Assyrian king **Shalmaneser V** began a siege of **Samaria**, the capital of Israel. His successor **Sargon II** finished the job. The city fell, and the Northern Kingdom was destroyed for good.

What happened next was devastating. The Assyrians used their favorite strategy: **deportation**. They took large numbers of Israelites (people from the ten northern tribes) and scattered them across the Assyrian Empire. These people were sent to places in Mesopotamia and Media, far from their homeland.

But Assyria did not leave the land empty. They brought in people from other conquered nations (from Babylon, Cuthah, and other places) and settled them in Israel's territory. Over time, these foreign settlers mixed with the Israelites who had been left behind. Their descendants became the people known as the **Samaritans**.

This mixing is important to understand. By the time Jesus lived, hundreds of years later, the Jews looked down on the Samaritans. They considered them half-breeds who did not follow the law properly. That is why the story of the Good Samaritan was so shocking to the people listening. Jesus made the despised Samaritan the hero. Now you know the backstory behind that tension.

The **ten tribes** of the Northern Kingdom were scattered and largely lost to history. They did not disappear overnight. They were absorbed into the populations around them, generation after generation, until they could no longer be identified as Israelites. At least, not by the world. But the Lord always knew where they were.

The Northern Kingdom was gone. Only **Judah** in the south remained. And Assyria was already looking in that direction.
