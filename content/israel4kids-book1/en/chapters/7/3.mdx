### Lesson 7.3 -- The Lost Ten Tribes

Here is one of the biggest mysteries in the scriptures: what happened to the ten tribes of Israel after Assyria carried them away?

The Bible does not really tell us. After the Assyrians deported the people of the Northern Kingdom, the ten tribes mostly vanish from the biblical record. We know they were scattered across the Assyrian Empire. Over centuries, many of their descendants were absorbed into the surrounding populations. They intermarried, adopted new languages and customs, and lost track of their Israelite identity. To the rest of the world, they simply disappeared.

People have wondered about the **lost ten tribes** for thousands of years. Some have claimed to find them in places as far away as Afghanistan, Ethiopia, Japan, and even the Americas. Most of these claims do not hold up, but they show how much people have wanted to solve this mystery.

So are the ten tribes really gone forever? As Latter-day Saints, we know the answer is no.

The **Tenth Article of Faith** says we believe in "the literal gathering of Israel and in the restoration of the Ten Tribes." That is pretty clear. The lost tribes are not lost to God. He knows exactly where their descendants are, even if the world does not. The gathering of Israel, which is happening right now through missionary work, includes bringing these descendants back to the covenant.

The Book of Mormon talks about this too. In **3 Nephi 21**, the Savior himself speaks about the gathering of the house of Israel in the last days. He promises that the scattered remnants of Israel will be brought back. The Doctrine and Covenants also mentions the return of the ten tribes and says that their prophets and scriptures will come forth (D&C 133:26-34).

Think about it this way: if you lose something valuable, you do not forget about it. You keep looking. God made covenants with all twelve tribes, not just Judah. He has not forgotten a single one of them. The gathering might be spread across centuries and continents, but it is happening.

The story of the lost ten tribes is not a story with a sad ending. It is a story that has not ended yet. Their scattering was part of a larger plan, one that would eventually spread the blood and blessings of Israel across the whole earth, so that when the gospel was restored, covenant descendants would be everywhere, ready to hear the message.
