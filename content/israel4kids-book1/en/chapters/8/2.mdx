### Lesson 8.2 -- Lehi Leaves Jerusalem

Around **600 BC**, during the reign of King **Zedekiah**, the city of Jerusalem was a mess. The people were wicked, the king was weak, and Babylon was breathing down their necks. But God had not stopped trying to reach the people. In fact, the Bible tells us that many prophets were warning Jerusalem at this time.

One of those prophets was **Jeremiah**, who you can read about in the Old Testament. He begged the people to repent, and they threw him in prison for it.

But there was another prophet in Jerusalem at the same time, one whose story is not in the Bible. His name was **Lehi**.

This is where the **Book of Mormon** begins. Lehi was a righteous man living in Jerusalem. God gave him a **vision** showing him that Jerusalem would be destroyed. The Lord told Lehi to take his family and leave. So Lehi gathered his wife **Sariah** and his sons (**Laman, Lemuel, Sam, and Nephi**) and they departed into the wilderness.

Think about what that must have been like. Leaving your home, your friends, everything you know, because God told you to. Nephi tells us that his father left behind gold, silver, and precious things. They took almost nothing. It was an incredible act of **faith**.

The Book of Mormon confirms something important about this period: there were **many prophets** warning the people of Jerusalem (1 Nephi 1:4). This matches what we see in the Bible. God was not silent. He gave the people every chance to repent. They just would not listen.

Lehi's family traveled through the Arabian desert, built a ship, and eventually crossed the ocean to the **Americas**. Their story becomes the rest of the Book of Mormon, a record of God's dealings with a branch of Israel in a new land.

Here is something to appreciate: the Book of Mormon and the Bible are telling the same story from different angles. While Jeremiah was prophesying in Jerusalem, Lehi was receiving his own revelations from the same God. While Judah was about to fall to Babylon, the Lord was preserving a faithful family to carry the gospel to another continent. The two records support and confirm each other.

Lehi leaving Jerusalem is not just the beginning of the Book of Mormon. It is part of the larger story of Israel's scattering, and eventually, its gathering.
