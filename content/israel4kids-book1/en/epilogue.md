# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing History of Israel for Kids. Think about everything you've learned. You started with Abraham's covenant and Joshua crossing the Jordan River. You met the judges, watched Israel rise under David and Solomon, saw the kingdom split in two, and followed both halves to their falls. You walked with the prophets -- Elijah, Isaiah, Jeremiah -- and saw what happened when people listened and when they didn't.

You watched the Jews go into exile in Babylon and come back to rebuild. You saw the Greeks arrive, the Maccabees fight back, and Rome take over. You learned what the world looked like when Jesus was born -- and what happened to Jerusalem and the temple after He left.

That's roughly 1,400 years of history. Not many kids -- or adults -- have a timeline like that in their heads. You do now.

Here's the best part: this history is not just ancient facts. It's the backdrop to the scriptures you read every week. The next time you open the Old Testament, the Book of Mormon, or the New Testament, you'll see the stories differently. You'll know who the Pharisees were and why they acted the way they did. You'll know why Lehi had to leave Jerusalem. You'll know what the temple meant and why its destruction mattered.

And the story isn't over. Latter-day Saints believe that the gathering of Israel is happening right now -- in our time. Every missionary who teaches the gospel, every person who makes covenants with God, every family sealed in the temple is part of that gathering. The covenant God made with Abraham is the same covenant you participate in today.

You are part of Israel's story.

Thank you for learning with us. Keep studying, keep reading your scriptures, and keep building on what you've learned here.
