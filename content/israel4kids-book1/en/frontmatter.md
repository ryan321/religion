## Introduction

Have you ever read a scripture story and wondered -- where exactly is this happening? Who are these people? What was going on in the world at the time?

The scriptures are full of incredible stories. But those stories didn't happen in a vacuum. They happened in a real place -- a small strip of land along the Mediterranean Sea -- during real historical periods, surrounded by powerful empires that were constantly fighting for control.

This book is a history of that land and those people. It starts when Joshua led the twelve tribes of Israel into the promised land and ends with the destruction of the temple in Jerusalem by the Romans in 70 AD. In between, you'll meet judges, kings, prophets, conquerors, and rebels. You'll learn how a small group of tribes became a kingdom, how that kingdom split in two, how both halves fell to foreign empires, and how the Jewish people rebuilt, fought for independence, and eventually came under Roman rule -- the world the Savior was born into.

Along the way, you'll see how the history connects to scriptures you already know. When Lehi left Jerusalem, what was happening in the kingdom of Judah? When Nephi quoted Isaiah, what was Isaiah's world like? When the Savior walked through the temple courts, what did that temple look like and who controlled it? History answers those questions.

You don't need to know anything about history or the scriptures to start this book. But by the time you finish, you'll have a timeline in your head that will make every scripture story richer.

## How to use this book

This book is designed as a workbook for self-study or guided by a parent or teacher. No expertise required -- the lessons explain everything you need to know.

The book is designed to be used over the course of a semester. Each chapter corresponds to a week, with a total of 15 weeks. Each chapter has 4 lessons which are meant to be done once a day, leaving a 5th day for review and the chapter test.

For example, if you started on Monday each week, you could do:

Monday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.1

Tuesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.2

Wednesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.3

Thursday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.4

Friday  Chapter 1, Review and test

Then the next Monday you would start Chapter 2, Lesson 2.1, and so on.

Each lesson has questions at the end, and each chapter has a test. Your AI tutor will help you check your answers and talk through anything you're unsure about.

After each lesson, there is a checkbox to mark when the lesson is completed to keep track of progress.

After 15 weeks (and 15 chapters) you'll have a solid understanding of the history of Israel -- and the scriptures will never look quite the same.

## Note to parents

You don't need to be a history expert or a scripture scholar to help your child with this book. The lessons are written directly to the child in plain language. Everything is explained as it comes up.

For the best experience, we recommend going through each lesson with your child. The lessons are short -- about 15-20 minutes each. You may want to discuss the content together, especially when the lessons connect to scriptures your family has studied.

This book is written from a Latter-day Saint perspective. It treats the scriptural record as historical and makes natural connections to the Book of Mormon, Doctrine and Covenants, and Pearl of Great Price. It's designed for families who are active members of the Church and want their children to understand the historical world of the scriptures.

The goal is not to replace scripture study but to give your child the historical context that makes scripture study richer. When your child reads about Nephi, Elijah, or the Savior, they'll understand the world those people lived in.
