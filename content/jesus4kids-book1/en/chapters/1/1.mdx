### Lesson 1.1 -- The World Was Waiting

Have you ever waited a really long time for something important? Maybe a birthday or a big trip? Now imagine waiting hundreds of years. That is how long God's people waited for the **Messiah**, the Savior who would come to rescue Israel and bless the whole world.

Long before Jesus was born, God sent **prophets** to tell people He was coming. These prophets didn't just say, "Someone special is on the way." They gave specific details so people would recognize the Messiah when He arrived.

The prophet **Isaiah** said that a virgin would have a son, and His name would be called **Immanuel**, which means "God with us" (Isaiah 7:14). Think about that name for a second. The Messiah wouldn't just be sent by God. He would actually be God among His people.

Another prophet, **Micah**, told everyone exactly where the Messiah would be born: **Bethlehem**, a small town in Judea (Micah 5:2). Not Jerusalem, the big capital city. Not some powerful nation far away. A tiny, quiet village. God often works through small and simple things.

But it wasn't only Old Testament prophets who knew about Jesus. Prophets in the Book of Mormon taught about Him too. **King Benjamin** told his people that the Messiah would be called **Jesus Christ** and that He would come down among the children of men (Mosiah 3:8). King Benjamin knew the Savior's actual name centuries before He was born.

**Nephi**, the son of Lehi, saw the birth of Jesus in a vision more than 500 years before it happened (1 Nephi 11:18-21). He saw the virgin Mary. He saw the baby. And an angel helped him understand that this child was the Son of God, the Redeemer of the world.

When you look at the scriptures as a whole (both the Bible and the Book of Mormon) you can see that the **entire record** points toward one event: the birth of Jesus Christ. Every prophet, every promise, every sign was building toward that moment. The Old Testament prophets spoke to Israel. The Book of Mormon prophets spoke to people on the American continent. But the message was the same: He is coming.

The world was waiting. And God made sure people on both sides of the ocean knew exactly who to look for.
