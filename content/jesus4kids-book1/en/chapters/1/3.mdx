### Lesson 1.3 -- Born in Bethlehem

The Messiah had been promised for centuries. Prophets said He would be born in **Bethlehem** (Micah 5:2). But Mary and Joseph lived in Nazareth, a town about 80 miles to the north. So how would Jesus end up being born in the right place? God had a plan, and it involved a Roman emperor.

**Caesar Augustus** issued a decree that everyone in the Roman Empire had to return to their family's ancestral town to be counted in a **census** (Luke 2:1-3). Joseph was a descendant of King David, and David's hometown was Bethlehem. So Joseph and Mary made the long journey south, even though Mary was close to having her baby.

When they arrived in Bethlehem, the town was packed. There was **no room at the inn** (Luke 2:7). So they ended up in a place where animals were kept -- likely a stable or a cave. And that is where Jesus was born. Mary wrapped Him in **swaddling clothes** and laid Him in a **manger**, which is a feeding trough for animals. The King of Kings came into the world in the humblest way possible.

That same night, out in the fields nearby, **shepherds** were watching their flocks. An angel appeared to them, and they were terrified. But the angel said, "Fear not: for, behold, I bring you good tidings of great joy, which shall be to all people" (Luke 2:10). Then a whole multitude of angels appeared, praising God and saying, "Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace, good will toward men" (Luke 2:14). The shepherds hurried to Bethlehem and found the baby, just as the angel described.

Think about who got the first announcement that night. Not kings. Not religious leaders. **Shepherds**, ordinary working men out in the fields. God chose them. It tells you something about what matters to the Lord.

On the other side of the world, the signs **Samuel the Lamanite** had prophesied came true. There was a night with no darkness (3 Nephi 1:15, 19). A new star appeared in the sky (3 Nephi 1:21). The believers who had been waiting and even risking their lives for their faith were filled with joy. The prophecies were fulfilled in every detail.

Jesus Christ, the Son of God, had come to earth. The long wait was over.
