### Lesson 1.4 -- The Wise Men and the Flight to Egypt

After Jesus was born, visitors came from far away to find Him. The scriptures call them **wise men** -- sometimes called Magi. They had seen a special **star** in the east, and they knew it meant a new king had been born (Matthew 2:1-2). They traveled a long distance to find Him.

But the wise men made a mistake on the way. They stopped in **Jerusalem** and asked King **Herod** where the new king was. This was a problem. Herod was the current king, and he did not want competition. When he heard that a child had been born who was supposed to be "King of the Jews," he was furious -- and afraid.

Herod called together the chief priests and scribes and asked where the Messiah was supposed to be born. They told him: Bethlehem, just as the prophet Micah had written (Matthew 2:4-6). Herod then told the wise men to go find the child and come back to tell him where He was. He claimed he wanted to worship the child too. He was lying.

The wise men followed the star to the place where Jesus was. When they found Him, they knelt down and **worshipped** Him. They opened their treasures and gave Him three famous gifts: **gold**, **frankincense**, and **myrrh** (Matthew 2:11). Gold was a gift for a king. Frankincense was used in temple worship. Myrrh was a spice used for burial. Each gift pointed to something about who Jesus was and what His life would mean.

After visiting Jesus, the wise men were **warned in a dream** not to go back to Herod (Matthew 2:12). So they went home a different way.

Then an angel appeared to **Joseph** in a dream and told him to take Mary and Jesus and **flee to Egypt** immediately. Herod was going to search for the child and try to kill Him (Matthew 2:13). Joseph got up that very night and left.

When Herod realized the wise men were not coming back, he was enraged. He ordered that all boys in Bethlehem **two years old and younger** be killed (Matthew 2:16). It was a horrible, evil act. But Jesus was safe in Egypt.

The family stayed in Egypt until Herod died. Then they returned and settled in **Nazareth**, where Jesus would grow up (Matthew 2:23). Even when powerful people tried to stop God's plan, they could not.
