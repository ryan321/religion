### Lesson 10.1 -- Teaching Along the Way

Jesus knew He was running out of time. As He traveled toward Jerusalem for the last time, He taught with urgency. Every parable mattered. Every lesson was meant to stick. He wanted His followers to be ready for what was coming -- not just His death, but the day He would return again.

One of the most powerful parables He told was the **Parable of the Ten Virgins** (Matthew 25:1-13). In this story, ten young women were waiting for a bridegroom to arrive for a wedding feast. Five of them were wise -- they brought extra oil for their lamps. Five were foolish -- they brought their lamps but no extra oil. When the bridegroom finally came late at night, the foolish ones realized their lamps were going out. They asked the wise ones to share, but there wasn't enough for everyone. While they ran off to buy more oil, the door to the feast was shut. They missed it.

The lesson is simple but serious: **be prepared**. You can't borrow someone else's testimony or spiritual strength. You have to build your own, a little at a time, before the moment arrives when you need it most.

Then Jesus told the **Parable of the Talents** (Matthew 25:14-30). A man going on a journey gave three servants different amounts of money -- called **talents**. One received five, another two, and another one. The first two servants went to work. They invested and doubled what they had been given. The third servant was afraid of losing his talent, so he dug a hole and buried it in the ground.

When the master returned, he praised the first two servants. "Well done, thou good and faithful servant," he said. But to the one who buried his talent, the master was not pleased at all. He called him wicked and slothful and took the talent away from him.

The message here is about using what God gives you. It doesn't matter whether you have a lot or a little. What matters is that you **do something with it**. God gives everyone gifts -- abilities, opportunities, time. He expects you to develop them, not sit on them. Being afraid is not an excuse for doing nothing.

Both parables carry the same big idea: **don't waste time**. Be spiritually ready. Use your gifts. Jesus was heading to the cross, and He wanted every listener to understand that life is not a waiting game. It is a chance to grow, prepare, and serve.
