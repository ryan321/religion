### Lesson 10.2 -- The Rich Young Ruler

One day a wealthy young man came running up to Jesus with a question that seemed sincere: "Good Master, what good thing shall I do, that I may have eternal life?" (Matthew 19:16). It sounds like exactly the right question. He wanted to know what it took to live forever with God. That's a good thing to wonder about.

Jesus started with the basics. He told the young man to **keep the commandments**: don't kill, don't steal, don't lie, honor your father and mother, love your neighbor as yourself. The young man's answer was confident: "All these things have I kept from my youth up: what lack I yet?" (Matthew 19:20). He had been obedient his whole life. He followed the rules. But he felt like something was still missing.

Then came the hard part. Jesus looked at him and said, "If thou wilt be perfect, go and sell that thou hast, and give to the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven: and come and follow me" (Matthew 19:21). That was the test. Not the commandments; he already had those down. The test was whether he was willing to give up the thing he valued most.

The young man **walked away sad**, because he had great possessions (Matthew 19:22). He couldn't do it. He wanted eternal life, but not enough to let go of his wealth.

After the young man left, Jesus turned to His disciples and said, "It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle, than for a rich man to enter into the kingdom of God" (Matthew 19:24). The disciples were stunned. If a good, wealthy, commandment-keeping person can't make it, who can? Jesus answered, "With God all things are possible" (Matthew 19:26).

Now, this story is not saying that being rich is automatically sinful. Plenty of righteous people in the scriptures had wealth (Abraham, Job, and others). The point is about **priorities**. Nothing can come before God. Not money, not comfort, not popularity, not anything. Whatever sits on the throne of your heart has to be the Lord. If something else is there, He will ask you to move it.

The rich young ruler was so close. He had done so much right. But when the Savior asked for his whole heart, he held back. That is the real lesson. **Following Jesus means being willing to give Him everything**, even the things that are hardest to let go of.
