### Lesson 10.3 -- The Triumphal Entry

For three years Jesus had traveled, taught, and healed, mostly in quiet towns and villages. He often told people not to spread the word about His miracles. But now, everything changed. As He approached Jerusalem for the last time, He made a bold, public entrance that announced exactly who He was.

Jesus sent two disciples ahead to find a donkey and her colt. He rode into the city on the young donkey, and this was not random. The prophet **Zechariah** had written about this moment hundreds of years earlier: "Rejoice greatly, O daughter of Zion... thy King cometh unto thee... lowly, and riding upon an ass" (Zechariah 9:9). Jesus was fulfilling prophecy on purpose, in plain sight, so that anyone who knew the scriptures could recognize what was happening.

The crowds went wild. People spread their **cloaks** and **palm branches** on the road in front of Him, a sign of honor and celebration. They shouted, "**Hosanna** to the Son of David: Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the Lord; Hosanna in the highest" (Matthew 21:9). The word "Hosanna" means "save us now." They were calling Him the **Messiah** openly. The whole city was stirred up. People were asking, "Who is this?" (Matthew 21:10).

Not everyone was happy about it. The **Pharisees** in the crowd were furious. They told Jesus to make His followers be quiet. His response was unforgettable: "I tell you that, if these should hold their peace, the stones would immediately cry out" (Luke 19:40). In other words, this moment was so important that creation itself would not stay silent. If the people stopped praising God, the rocks on the ground would do it instead.

This event is called the **Triumphal Entry**, and it marks the beginning of the **final week** of Jesus' mortal life. Up until now, Jesus had been careful about when and how He revealed Himself. But this was the time. He entered the capital city as a King, not on a warhorse, not with an army, but humbly, on a donkey. He was the King of kings, but He came in peace.

The Triumphal Entry also teaches something about courage. Jesus knew exactly what was waiting for Him in Jerusalem. He knew about the arrest, the trial, and the cross. He rode in anyway. He didn't hide. He didn't run. He walked straight into the hardest week of His life because it was His Father's will.
