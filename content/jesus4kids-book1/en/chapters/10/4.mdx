### Lesson 10.4 -- The Olivet Discourse

A few days after the Triumphal Entry, Jesus sat on the **Mount of Olives** with His disciples, looking out over the city of Jerusalem. The disciples had just admired the massive, beautiful temple. Jesus told them something shocking: the time would come when **not one stone would be left upon another**. The whole thing would be torn down (Matthew 24:2).

That got their attention. They asked Him three big questions: When would this happen? What would be the signs of His coming? And what about the end of the world? Jesus answered all three in what we call the **Olivet Discourse**, one of the longest prophecies He ever gave (Matthew 24-25).

He warned them about **false prophets** and **false Christs**. People would come claiming to be the Messiah or claiming to speak for God, and many would be fooled. Jesus said not to follow them. He described terrible events: **wars and rumors of wars**, **famines**, **earthquakes**, and **pestilences**. Nations would fight nations. His followers would be hated and persecuted. He said these things would be like "the beginning of sorrows" (Matthew 24:8), meaning it would only be the start.

But Jesus also gave hope. The **gospel would be preached in all the world** before the end (Matthew 24:14). And He promised that for the sake of His chosen people, those terrible days would be shortened. God would not let it go on forever.

One of the most important parts of the Olivet Discourse is this: **"Of that day and hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels of heaven, but my Father only"** (Matthew 24:36). Nobody knows the exact time of the Second Coming. Not the angels, not even the Son at that moment. Only the Father knows. So anyone who claims to know the date is wrong.

Some of these prophecies were fulfilled when the Romans destroyed the temple in **70 AD**, just about forty years after Jesus spoke these words. The temple was torn apart, and the Jewish people were scattered. But the full prophecy looks further ahead, to the last days before the **Second Coming of Jesus Christ**. Many of the signs He described (wars, earthquakes, false prophets, the gospel going to all nations) apply to our time too.

The big takeaway from the Olivet Discourse is simple: **watch and be ready**. You don't know when the Lord will come, so live every day as if it could be today. Stay faithful, stay prepared, and don't be caught off guard.
