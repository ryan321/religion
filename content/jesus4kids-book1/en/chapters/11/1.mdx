### Lesson 11.1 -- The Last Supper

It was Thursday evening, the night before Jesus would die. He gathered His twelve apostles in an upper room in Jerusalem to share the **Passover** meal (Luke 22:7-20). The Passover was an ancient Jewish celebration that remembered the night God freed Israel from slavery in Egypt. Every year, families ate a special meal together and remembered how God had saved them.

But this Passover would be different from every one that had come before. Jesus was about to give it an entirely new meaning.

During the meal, Jesus took **bread**, blessed it, and broke it into pieces. He gave it to His disciples and said, "This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me" (Luke 22:19). Then He took a **cup** of wine, gave thanks, and passed it around, saying, "This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you" (Luke 22:20).

This was the first **sacrament**. The bread represented His body, which would be broken on the cross. The cup represented His blood, which would be shed for the sins of the world. Jesus was asking His followers to remember His sacrifice, not just once, but regularly, over and over again.

If you are a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, you participate in this same ordinance every Sunday. When the bread and water are passed during **sacrament meeting**, you are doing exactly what Jesus asked His disciples to do that night. You are remembering His body and His blood. You are renewing the covenants you made at baptism. And you are promising to always remember Him and keep His commandments.

Jesus also told His disciples during the meal that one of them would **betray** Him (Matthew 26:21). The room went quiet. One by one, they asked, "Lord, is it I?" **Judas Iscariot**, who had already made a deal with the chief priests to hand Jesus over for **thirty pieces of silver**, asked the same question. Jesus confirmed it was him. Judas left the room and went out into the night (John 13:30).

The Last Supper was a night of deep meaning. Jesus gave His closest friends the ordinance that would help them, and all of us, remember Him forever. Every time you take the sacrament, you are connected to that upper room and that final meal.
