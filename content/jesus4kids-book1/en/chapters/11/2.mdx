### Lesson 11.2 -- Washing the Disciples' Feet

During that same evening in the upper room, Jesus did something that stunned everyone. He stood up from the table, took off His outer robe, wrapped a **towel** around His waist, and poured water into a **basin**. Then the Son of God, the Creator of the world, the King of kings, knelt down and began washing His disciples' feet (John 13:1-17).

In Jesus' time, foot washing was one of the lowliest jobs there was. Roads were dusty and dirty. People wore sandals. Feet got filthy. Washing someone's feet was usually the job of the lowest servant in the household, not the master, and certainly not the Messiah.

When Jesus came to **Peter**, Peter pulled his feet back. He couldn't accept it. "Lord, dost thou wash my feet?" he asked (John 13:6). Peter loved Jesus. He respected Him too much to let Him do a servant's job. Jesus answered him, "What I do thou knowest not now; but thou shalt know hereafter" (John 13:7). In other words, you don't understand this yet, but you will.

Peter still resisted: "Thou shalt never wash my feet." Jesus' response was firm: "If I wash thee not, thou hast no part with me" (John 13:8). That changed Peter's mind fast. He said, "Lord, not my feet only, but also my hands and my head!" Peter went from refusing to wanting everything washed. That's Peter for you. All or nothing.

After Jesus finished and sat back down, He explained what He had done. "Ye call me Master and Lord: and ye say well; for so I am. If I then, your Lord and Master, have washed your feet; ye also ought to wash one another's feet. For I have given you an example, that ye should do as I have done to you" (John 13:13-15).

The lesson was crystal clear: **true leadership is service**. If the Son of God is willing to kneel down and do the dirtiest, humblest job, then no act of service is beneath any of His followers. Being great in God's kingdom doesn't mean being in charge or being important. It means being willing to serve the people around you, especially when it's not glamorous or noticed.

Jesus didn't just teach this idea in a parable. He demonstrated it with a towel and a basin of water. He showed His disciples, and you, exactly what a leader looks like in the kingdom of God.
