### Lesson 11.3 -- Gethsemane

After the Last Supper, Jesus and His disciples walked to a garden called **Gethsemane**, on the **Mount of Olives** just outside Jerusalem (Matthew 26:36-46). It was late at night. Jesus had been to this garden before; it was a place He went to pray. But this night would be different from any other night in the history of the world.

Jesus asked most of the disciples to sit and wait. He took **Peter**, **James**, and **John** a little farther with Him. Then He told them something they had never heard Him say before: "My soul is exceeding sorrowful, even unto death" (Matthew 26:38). The Savior of the world was in deep anguish. He asked His three closest friends to stay awake and watch with Him.

Then Jesus went a little farther by Himself, fell on His face, and prayed. His words were raw and honest: "O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt" (Matthew 26:39). He was asking if there was any other way. But in the same breath, He submitted to His Father's plan. That phrase, **"not as I will, but as thou wilt"**, is one of the most important sentences ever spoken. It shows perfect obedience, even in the middle of unimaginable pain.

What happened next was the beginning of the **Atonement**. In that garden, Jesus took upon Himself the **sins**, **pains**, **sicknesses**, and **sorrows** of every person who has ever lived or ever will live (Alma 7:11-12). Every sin you have committed or will commit, every heartbreak, every moment of grief. He felt all of it. The weight was so great that He **bled from every pore** (Doctrine and Covenants 19:18). An angel came from heaven to strengthen Him (Luke 22:43), but He bore it alone in a way no one else could.

He went back to Peter, James, and John and found them **asleep**. He woke them and asked, "Could ye not watch with me one hour?" (Matthew 26:40). He went away and prayed again, and again they fell asleep. Three times this happened. In His darkest hour, His friends could not stay awake.

The Atonement did not end in Gethsemane. It would continue through the trial and the cross. But it began here, in a quiet garden, in the dark, with the Savior on His knees. Because of what He suffered that night, every person who has ever lived can be forgiven, healed, and made whole.
