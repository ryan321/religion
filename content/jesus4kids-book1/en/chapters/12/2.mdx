### Lesson 12.2 -- Peter's Denial and Pilate's Decision

While Jesus stood before the Sanhedrin inside, **Peter** was outside in the courtyard. He had followed at a distance after the arrest, the only disciple brave enough to come that close. But bravery and faithfulness are not always the same thing.

A servant girl looked at Peter by the firelight and said, "Thou also wast with Jesus of Galilee" (Matthew 26:69). Peter denied it in front of everyone: "I know not what thou sayest." He moved to the porch, and another girl recognized him: "This fellow was also with Jesus of Nazareth." Again he denied it, this time with an oath: "I do not know the man." A little later, bystanders came up and said his Galilean accent gave him away. Peter denied Jesus a third time, cursing and swearing (Luke 22:54-62).

Immediately, a **rooster crowed**. Peter remembered what Jesus had told him just hours earlier: "Before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice" (Matthew 26:34). The realization hit him like a wall. Peter **"went out, and wept bitterly"** (Luke 22:62). He was shattered. The man who had once said he would die for Jesus had just pretended not to know Him. Three times.

Peter's denial is one of the most human moments in the scriptures. He wasn't a bad person. He was afraid. Fear can make people do things they never thought they would. But Peter's story doesn't end here. He would repent, be forgiven, and become one of the most powerful leaders in the early Church. His failure was real, but so was his recovery.

Meanwhile, Jesus was brought before **Pontius Pilate**, the Roman governor of Judea (Matthew 27:1-2). Pilate questioned Jesus and came to a conclusion quickly: **he found no fault in Him** (Luke 23:4). Jesus was innocent, and Pilate knew it.

Pilate tried to release Jesus. He offered the crowd a choice. He would free one prisoner for the Passover. He gave them two options: Jesus or **Barabbas**, a convicted criminal. Pilate expected the crowd to choose Jesus. They didn't. The chief priests stirred up the people, and the crowd shouted, "Release Barabbas!" When Pilate asked what to do with Jesus, they screamed, **"Crucify him!"** (Matthew 27:22-23).

Pilate gave in to the pressure. He **washed his hands** in front of the crowd and said, "I am innocent of the blood of this just person" (Matthew 27:24). Then he handed Jesus over to be crucified. Washing his hands didn't remove his responsibility. He had the power to stop it and chose not to.
