### Lesson 12.3 -- The Crucifixion

After Pilate handed Jesus over, the Roman soldiers took Him and **scourged** Him -- a brutal whipping with a leather whip embedded with metal and bone. They stripped Him, put a scarlet robe on Him, pressed a crown of sharp **thorns** onto His head, and shoved a reed into His hand like a fake scepter. They knelt before Him in mockery, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews!" Then they spat on Him and hit Him in the head (Matthew 27:27-31).

After the mocking, they forced Jesus to carry His own cross through the streets of Jerusalem toward a hill called **Golgotha**, which means "the place of a skull" (Matthew 27:33). Along the way, Jesus stumbled under the weight. The soldiers pulled a man from the crowd -- **Simon of Cyrene** -- and made him carry the cross the rest of the way (Matthew 27:32).

At Golgotha, they nailed Jesus to the cross. They crucified Him between **two thieves**, one on each side. Above His head they posted a sign: **"This is Jesus the King of the Jews"** (Matthew 27:37). Crucifixion was one of the most painful forms of execution ever invented. It was designed to be slow, agonizing, and public.

Even on the cross, even in unbearable pain, Jesus showed who He truly was. His first words were a prayer: **"Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do"** (Luke 23:34). The men who had beaten Him, mocked Him, and nailed Him to the wood -- He asked God to forgive them. That is mercy beyond anything we can fully understand.

One of the thieves hanging next to Him mocked Him. But the other thief defended Jesus and asked, "Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom." Jesus answered him with a promise: **"To day shalt thou be with me in paradise"** (Luke 23:43). Even dying, Jesus offered hope and salvation to a repentant sinner.

He also looked down from the cross and saw His mother, **Mary**, standing near His beloved disciple **John**. He said to Mary, "Woman, behold thy son!" and to John, "Behold thy mother!" (John 19:26-27). From that moment, John took Mary into his own home and cared for her. In His final hours, Jesus made sure His mother would be looked after.

The Crucifixion was the most important event in human history. It was not just a death. It was a sacrifice -- willing, deliberate, and filled with love from the first moment to the last.
