### Lesson 12.4 -- "It Is Finished"

As Jesus hung on the cross, something strange happened in the sky. Beginning at noon, **darkness** covered the entire land for **three hours** (Matthew 27:45). This was not a normal eclipse or a passing storm. It was a sign. Creation itself seemed to mourn as the Creator suffered.

Near the end of those three dark hours, Jesus cried out with a loud voice: **"My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"** (Matthew 27:46). This is one of the most heartbreaking moments in all of scripture. For the first time in His entire existence, Jesus was completely separated from His Father's presence. God the Father **withdrew** so that Jesus could experience the full weight of mortality and suffering entirely on His own.

Why did the Father pull away? Because that was part of the sacrifice. Jesus needed to descend below all things (Doctrine and Covenants 88:6). He needed to know what it felt like to be utterly alone -- without any divine comfort at all. If He had not experienced that complete abandonment, there would be some human suffering He had never felt. And the Atonement had to cover everything.

After that terrible cry, Jesus said, **"It is finished"** (John 19:30). The work was done. Every sin, every pain, every sorrow -- He had taken it all upon Himself. The debt was paid. Then He spoke His final words: **"Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit"** (Luke 23:46). With that, He bowed His head and gave up His life.

The moment Jesus died, powerful signs appeared. The **veil of the temple** -- a massive, thick curtain that separated the Holy of Holies from the rest of the temple -- was **torn in two from top to bottom** (Matthew 27:51). This was symbolic. The veil had represented the separation between God and His people. Now, through Christ's sacrifice, the way to God was open. The earth shook. Rocks split apart. Graves opened.

On the other side of the world, the signs were even more dramatic. The Book of Mormon records that in the Americas there was **terrible destruction** -- cities burned, mountains collapsed, and the land was reshaped (3 Nephi 8). Then came **three days of total darkness** so thick it could be felt. The people heard a voice out of the darkness: the voice of Jesus Christ, telling them that He had suffered for them and inviting them to come unto Him (3 Nephi 9:13-14).

Jesus Christ, the Son of God, was dead. But death would not hold Him. The story was not over. It was only beginning.
