### Lesson 13.1 -- Where Did Jesus Go?

When Jesus died on the cross Friday afternoon, His body was taken down and placed in a borrowed tomb. A large stone was rolled in front of the entrance. From the outside, it looked like nothing was happening. But Jesus was not simply lying still for three days. Something incredible was going on.

The Apostle Peter taught that Jesus "went and preached unto the spirits in prison" (1 Peter 3:19). That means His spirit left His body and traveled to the **spirit world**, the place where all people go after they die to wait for the resurrection. While His body rested in the tomb, His spirit was very much alive and active.

On the cross, Jesus had spoken to the thief hanging next to Him: "To day shalt thou be with me in paradise" (Luke 23:43). **Paradise** is the part of the spirit world where righteous people dwell after death. It is not the same as heaven or the celestial kingdom. It is a temporary place of peace and rest. Jesus went there, and He had work to do.

Think about that for a moment. Even between His death and His resurrection, Jesus did not take a break. He had a mission to fulfill. The plan of salvation did not pause. It kept moving forward, even on the other side of the veil.

So what is the **spirit world** exactly? It is not some faraway planet. Latter-day prophets have taught that it is right here on this earth, just beyond what our mortal eyes can see. Every person who has ever lived and died is there, waiting for the day when their spirit and body will be reunited in the resurrection.

The spirit world has two main parts. **Paradise** is for those who accepted the gospel and lived faithfully. **Spirit prison** is for those who either rejected the gospel or never had a chance to hear it. But as you will learn in the next lessons, the separation between those two groups is not permanent. Jesus made sure of that.

The three days between the Crucifixion and the Resurrection were not empty days. They were some of the most important days in the history of the world. Jesus carried His ministry right through death and into the spirit world, because saving God's children was never something He would stop doing.
