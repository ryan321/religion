### Lesson 13.2 -- President Joseph F. Smith's Vision

For centuries, Christians knew that Jesus had visited the spirit world. Peter said so. But the details were limited. What exactly did Jesus do there? Who did He meet? How was it organized? Those questions went mostly unanswered until October 1918.

**President Joseph F. Smith** was the sixth president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He was eighty years old and in the final weeks of his life. On October 3, 1918, he sat in his room pondering the scriptures, especially Peter's words about Jesus preaching to the spirits. As he meditated, the heavens opened. He received a **vision** of what had happened in the spirit world when Jesus arrived there after His death on the cross.

What President Smith saw was breathtaking. He described "an innumerable company of the spirits of the just," a vast congregation of righteous people who had died and were now in paradise (Doctrine and Covenants 138:12). These were not strangers. He recognized them. He saw **Adam and Eve**, the first parents of the human family. He saw **Abel**, **Seth**, **Noah**, **Abraham**, **Isaac**, **Jacob**, and **Moses**. He saw prophets and faithful Saints from every age of the world.

All of them had been "faithful in the testimony of Jesus while they lived in mortality" (D&C 138:12). They had obeyed God, kept their covenants, and endured hard things during their lives on earth. Now, in the spirit world, they were gathered together in one enormous assembly, filled with joy and anticipation.

Why were they so happy? Because they were waiting for the **Savior** to arrive. They knew He was coming. They had looked forward to His visit the way you might look forward to a family reunion with someone you love more than anyone else. And when Jesus finally came to them, their joy was complete. He declared "liberty to the captives who had been faithful" (D&C 138:18).

This vision was so important that it was added to the scriptures. You can read the whole thing in **Doctrine and Covenants, Section 138**. It is one of the most recent sections of scripture in the Doctrine and Covenants, and it answers questions that people had wondered about for nearly two thousand years.

President Smith's vision shows us that God reveals truth when His children need it. The spirit world is not a mystery. It is part of the plan, and the Lord has made sure we understand it.
