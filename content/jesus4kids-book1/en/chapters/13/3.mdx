### Lesson 13.3 -- How the Gospel Reaches Everyone

Here is a question that has troubled people for centuries: What about the billions of people who lived and died without ever hearing about Jesus Christ? What about people born in places where no missionary ever went? Are they just out of luck?

The answer is no, and Jesus made sure of it.

When President Joseph F. Smith saw his vision of the spirit world, he noticed something important. Jesus did **not** go personally to the wicked or disobedient spirits. Instead, He visited the righteous in paradise and organized them. He "appointed messengers, clothed with power and authority, and commissioned them to go forth and carry the light of the gospel to them that were in darkness" (Doctrine and Covenants 138:30).

In other words, Jesus set up **missionary work in the spirit world**. He gave faithful spirits the authority to teach the gospel to those who had never heard it. Just like missionaries on earth knock on doors and share the message of the restored gospel, spirits in the spirit world do the same thing, teaching, testifying, and inviting others to accept Jesus Christ.

This is a huge deal. It means the gospel is not limited to this life. If someone dies without a fair chance to learn about the Savior, they will get that chance on the other side. God does not give up on anyone.

But there is still a problem. Even if a spirit in the spirit world hears the gospel and believes it, they no longer have a physical body. They cannot be baptized. They cannot receive the laying on of hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost. Ordinances require a body.

That is exactly why members of the Church perform **ordinances for the dead** in temples. When you are baptized for someone who has died, you are lending your body to make that ordinance available to them. They still get to choose whether to accept it. No one is forced. But the ordinance is done and waiting for them if they want it.

This is why **family history** and **temple work** matter so much. Every name you find and every ordinance you perform in the temple is connected to a real person in the spirit world who may be waiting for exactly that.

The combination is beautiful: teaching on that side, ordinances on this side. Together, they make it possible for every person who ever lived to receive the full blessings of the gospel.
