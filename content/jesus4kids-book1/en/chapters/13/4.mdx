### Lesson 13.4 -- Why This Matters

Imagine you are standing in a giant stadium. It holds a hundred thousand people -- every seat is full. Now imagine a thousand stadiums like that, all packed. That is a hundred million people. Now multiply that by seventy or more. That is roughly how many people have lived on this earth throughout history.

Most of those billions of people never heard the name of Jesus Christ during their lifetimes. They were born in the wrong century, in the wrong country, or in a culture where the gospel never arrived. Some of them were deeply good people. Some were parents who loved their children. Some were honest and hardworking and kind. But they never had a missionary knock on their door.

Does God just forget about them? Absolutely not.

Everything you have learned in this chapter points to one truth: **God's plan is fair**. The Savior's Atonement is not only for the lucky few who happen to hear the gospel during mortality. It is for everyone. And God has built a system to make sure no one is left out.

On the other side of the veil, **spirit world missionary work** continues right now. Righteous spirits who have the gospel are teaching those who do not. Imagine the conversations happening at this very moment -- ancestors learning about Jesus Christ, hearing about the plan of salvation, and deciding whether to accept it.

On this side of the veil, **temple work** provides the ordinances those spirits need. Baptisms, confirmations, endowments, and sealings are performed every day in temples around the world. Members of the Church do this work on behalf of people who have died, so that the ordinances are ready and waiting if those spirits choose to accept the gospel.

This is why the Church spends so much effort on **family history**. Finding the names and dates of ancestors is not just a hobby. It is rescue work. Every name represents a real person -- someone's grandmother, someone's little brother, someone's friend. When you take that name to the temple, you are reaching across the veil and offering them something they cannot get on their own.

The Savior's visit to the spirit world was not a small side trip. It was one of the most important events in the plan of salvation. It proved that Jesus Christ is the Lord of the living and the dead -- and that His love does not stop at the grave. No one is forgotten. No one is left behind.
