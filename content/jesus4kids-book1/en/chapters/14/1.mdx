### Lesson 14.1 -- The Empty Tomb

It was early Sunday morning. The sun was barely up. A small group of women made their way to the tomb where Jesus had been buried two days before. They carried spices and ointments to anoint His body, a common practice at the time to show love and respect for someone who had died (Mark 16:1-6).

But when they arrived, something was wrong. The massive stone that had sealed the entrance had been rolled away. The tomb was open. And inside, nothing. The body of Jesus was gone.

Two **angels** appeared, dressed in shining white. The women were terrified. But the angels had the most important announcement in the history of the world: "He is not here: for he is risen, as he said" (Matthew 28:6). Those words changed everything. Jesus was not dead anymore. He had conquered death itself.

Most of the women hurried back to tell the apostles. But **Mary Magdalene** stayed behind. She stood outside the tomb, weeping. She thought someone had stolen the body. Through her tears, she noticed a man standing nearby. She assumed He was the gardener and begged Him to tell her where the body had been taken.

Then the man spoke a single word: "Mary" (John 20:16).

She knew that voice. It was Jesus. He was alive. He was standing right in front of her. She must have reached out to embrace Him, because He gently said, "Touch me not; for I am not yet ascended to my Father" (John 20:17). There was still work for Him to do. He needed to present Himself to His Father in Heaven first.

Mary Magdalene became the **first witness** of the resurrected Christ. She ran to tell the disciples, and her words carried the weight of the greatest news ever spoken: "I have seen the Lord" (John 20:18).

Think about how that morning must have felt. Friday had been the worst day imaginable. Saturday was silent and heavy with grief. And then Sunday morning, everything flipped upside down. Death was not the end. The tomb was empty. The Savior was alive.

That is why Christians celebrate Easter. Not because of eggs and candy, but because on that Sunday morning, Jesus Christ rose from the dead and made it possible for every person who has ever lived to rise too.
