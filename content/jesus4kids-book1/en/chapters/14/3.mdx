### Lesson 14.3 -- The Forty Days

The Resurrection was not the end of Jesus' time on earth. He did not rise from the dead and immediately go back to heaven. He stayed for **forty days** (Acts 1:3). During that time, He appeared to His followers again and again, teaching them, strengthening them, and preparing them for what lay ahead.

These were not quick visits. Jesus spent real time with His disciples. He taught them about **the kingdom of God** -- how His Church should be organized, what they should teach, and how the gospel would spread across the world. He was getting them ready to lead without Him physically present.

One of the most memorable appearances happened at the **Sea of Galilee** (John 21). Several of the apostles had gone fishing. They worked all night and caught nothing. At dawn, a man on the shore called out and told them to cast their net on the other side of the boat. They did, and the net was suddenly so full of fish they could barely drag it in. John recognized the man first: "It is the Lord!"

Peter did not wait. He jumped into the water and swam to shore. When they all arrived, they found that Jesus had already built a fire and was cooking breakfast -- bread and fish. He sat down and ate with them. The resurrected Lord of all creation made breakfast for His friends. That tells you something about who Jesus is.

After the meal, Jesus turned to **Peter** and asked him a pointed question: "Simon, son of Jonas, lovest thou me?" (John 21:15). Peter said yes. Jesus said, "Feed my lambs." He asked the same question a second time. Peter said yes again. "Feed my sheep." A third time. Peter was grieved -- but he answered, "Lord, thou knowest all things; thou knowest that I love thee" (John 21:17).

Three questions. Three answers. One for each time Peter had denied knowing Jesus on the night of the arrest. Jesus was not punishing Peter. He was giving him a chance to **reaffirm his love** and his commitment. And He was giving Peter a mission: take care of my people.

The Apostle Paul later wrote that during those forty days, Jesus appeared to more than **five hundred people** at one time (1 Corinthians 15:6). The Resurrection was not a secret and it was not a rumor. Hundreds of people saw the risen Christ with their own eyes.
