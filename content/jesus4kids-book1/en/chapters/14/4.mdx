### Lesson 14.4 -- The Ascension

The forty days were almost over. Jesus had taught His apostles everything they needed to carry the work forward. Now it was time for Him to return to His Father in Heaven.

He led the apostles out to the **Mount of Olives**, just outside Jerusalem (Acts 1:9-11). This was a place they knew well. Jesus had taught there many times. He had prayed there in Gethsemane, on the lower slopes of this very mountain. Now it would be the place where He said goodbye -- at least for a while.

Before He left, Jesus gave final instructions. He told the apostles, "Ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you" (Acts 1:8). The **Holy Ghost** would be their guide after Jesus was gone. He would comfort them, teach them, and give them the power they needed to do the work ahead.

Then came the commission: "Ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth" (Acts 1:8). Start where you are, then go everywhere. The gospel was not meant to stay in one city or one country. It was for the whole world.

With those words still hanging in the air, Jesus was **taken up** into heaven. The apostles watched as He rose from the ground, higher and higher, until a cloud received Him out of their sight (Acts 1:9). He was gone.

The apostles stood there, staring up at the sky. It must have been overwhelming. But they were not left alone for long. **Two angels** appeared beside them, dressed in white, and said something that still echoes today: "Ye men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up into heaven? This same Jesus, which is taken up from you into heaven, shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven" (Acts 1:11).

That is a promise. Jesus **will come back**. He will return to the earth the same way He left -- in glory, from the sky. Latter-day Saints call this the **Second Coming**, and it is one of the most important events still ahead in God's plan. When He returns, He will reign as King of kings and Lord of lords.

The Ascension was not an ending. It was a transition. Jesus moved from doing the work Himself on earth to directing it from heaven -- through His Spirit, through His prophets, and through His Church. The work never stopped. It just entered a new chapter.

The apostles went back to Jerusalem, and ten days later, on the day of **Pentecost**, the Holy Ghost came upon them exactly as Jesus had promised. But that is a story for another time.
