### Lesson 15.1 -- Destruction in the Americas

While the events of the Crucifixion and Resurrection were happening in Jerusalem, something dramatic and terrifying was taking place on the other side of the world. The Book of Mormon tells us that when Jesus died on the cross, the land of the Nephites experienced destruction unlike anything its people had ever seen.

It started suddenly. **Great storms** rolled in. **Earthquakes** shook the ground so violently that entire cities collapsed. The city of **Zarahemla** caught fire and burned. The city of **Moroni** sank into the sea. The city of **Moronihah** was buried under a mountain of earth. Roads broke apart. Buildings crumbled. The surface of the whole land was changed (3 Nephi 8).

The destruction lasted about **three hours**. Three hours of storms, fires, earthquakes, and winds that tore apart everything in their path. Many people died. Whole cities were wiped off the map.

Then something even more frightening happened. A **thick darkness** settled over the entire land. It was so dense that the people could not see anything at all. Not a candle, not a torch, not even a hand held in front of their face. This darkness lasted for **three days** (3 Nephi 8:23). The people sat in blackness, mourning, crying out in anguish over the destruction and the loss of their loved ones.

In the middle of that darkness, a **voice** spoke. It was not the voice of an angel or a prophet. It was the voice of Jesus Christ Himself, speaking from heaven to the survivors. He told them which cities had been destroyed and why. The wicked had been swept away. Then His tone shifted, and He pleaded with those who remained: "Will ye not now return unto me, and repent of your sins, and be converted, that I may heal you?" (3 Nephi 9:13).

He also announced a major change. The **law of Moses**, with its animal sacrifices and burnt offerings, was now fulfilled. Jesus had become the final sacrifice. From now on, He did not want the blood of animals on an altar. He asked instead for "a broken heart and a contrite spirit" (3 Nephi 9:20). That meant humility, honesty about your mistakes, and a real willingness to change.

The destruction was devastating, but the voice in the darkness carried a message of hope. The Savior was reaching out to His people, even in their darkest hour.
