### Lesson 15.2 -- Jesus Appears at the Temple in Bountiful

After three days, the darkness lifted. The storms stopped. The land was scarred and broken, but it was quiet at last. The survivors began to gather, and a large group of about **2,500 people** came together near the temple in the land of **Bountiful** (3 Nephi 11).

As they stood there talking about everything that had happened, they heard a **voice from heaven**. It was small and piercing, not loud, but it went right through them. The first time they heard it, they could not understand what it said. The second time, they still could not make out the words. But the third time, they "did open their ears to hear it; and their eyes were towards the sound thereof; and they did look steadfastly towards heaven, from whence the sound came" (3 Nephi 11:5).

The voice was the voice of **God the Father**. And the words were simple and powerful: "Behold my Beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased, in whom I have glorified my name; hear ye him" (3 Nephi 11:7).

Then they saw Him. A man descended from heaven, dressed in a **white robe**. He came down and stood among them. The people did not know what to think at first. They thought it might be an angel. But the man stretched out His hand and spoke: "Behold, I am Jesus Christ, whom the prophets testified shall come into the world" (3 Nephi 11:10).

Twenty-five hundred people fell to the earth. They remembered their prophets had foretold this moment, that the Lord would appear to them after His resurrection.

Then Jesus did something remarkable. He did not just wave to the crowd or speak from a distance. He invited every single person -- all 2,500 of them -- to come forward, **one by one**, and feel the prints of the nails in His hands and in His feet, and the wound in His side (3 Nephi 11:14-15). Each person touched the marks and knew for themselves that this was the risen Lord.

That took time. Think about 2,500 people, each one coming up individually. Jesus was in no hurry. He gave every person a personal, physical witness. No one was too unimportant. No one was skipped.

When they had all come forward, they cried out together: "Hosanna! Blessed be the name of the Most High God!" They fell at His feet and worshipped Him (3 Nephi 11:17).

This was not a distant God shouting instructions from the sky. This was the Savior standing among His children, letting them see and touch and know for themselves.
