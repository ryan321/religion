### Lesson 15.3 -- His Ministry Among the Nephites

After the people felt the wounds in His hands and feet, Jesus got to work. He had come not just to show Himself, but to teach, heal, and establish His Church among the Nephites. What followed was the most sacred experience in all of Nephite history.

First, Jesus called **twelve disciples** and gave them authority to baptize and to lead His Church in the Americas (3 Nephi 11:21-22). He taught them exactly how to perform the ordinance of baptism and made it clear there should be no arguments about the right way to do it. He wanted unity, not contention.

Then He taught the people. Much of what He said will sound familiar if you have read the New Testament, because He gave them the same great sermon He had delivered on a mountainside in Galilee, the **Sermon on the Mount** (3 Nephi 12-14). He taught about being meek, being a peacemaker, loving your enemies, and praying sincerely. He taught the Lord's Prayer. He taught them not to worry about tomorrow but to trust in God.

He also instituted the **sacrament** among the Nephites (3 Nephi 18). He blessed bread and wine and gave it to the people, just as He had done with His apostles in Jerusalem at the Last Supper. He explained that this ordinance would help them always remember His body and His blood, His sacrifice for them.

But the most tender moments came when Jesus healed the sick and blessed the children. He commanded the people to bring anyone who was ill, injured, blind, or suffering, "and I will heal them" (3 Nephi 17:7). They brought them, and He healed **every single one**.

Then He asked for the little children to be brought to Him. He prayed over them with words so powerful that no one could write them down. Then He blessed them **one by one**. As He did, the heavens opened and **angels descended**, encircling the children in what appeared to be **fire** (3 Nephi 17:24). Everyone there saw it. Everyone wept.

Jesus also gave the Nephites **scriptures** they were missing, things that had been prophesied but never written in their records. He commanded them to write everything down so future generations would have it.

His ministry among the Nephites shows the same Jesus who walked the roads of Galilee: patient, compassionate, powerful, and deeply personal. He cared about doctrine and He cared about people. He taught truth and He held children. That is who He is.
