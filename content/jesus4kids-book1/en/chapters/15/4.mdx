### Lesson 15.4 -- Other Sheep

While Jesus was with the Nephites, He said something that unlocks a bigger picture of His mission. He reminded them of words He had spoken to His apostles in Jerusalem: "Other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring, and they shall hear my voice" (John 10:16).

The people in Jerusalem had assumed He was talking about the Gentiles -- non-Jewish people who would eventually join the Church. But Jesus told the Nephites the truth: "Ye are they of whom I said: Other sheep I have which are not of this fold" (3 Nephi 15:21). The Nephites were the "other sheep." They were the ones He had been talking about all along.

But it did not stop there. Jesus told the Nephites that He had **still other groups** to visit. "I have other sheep, which are not of this land, neither of the land of Jerusalem, neither in any parts of that land round about whither I have been to minister" (3 Nephi 16:1). There were more people -- scattered groups that the Father had led away to other places -- and Jesus would go to them too.

We do not have the full record of where He went or who those people were. The scriptures mention the **lost tribes of Israel** as one possibility (3 Nephi 17:4). Throughout history, groups of Israelites were scattered to unknown lands. Jesus indicated He would visit them and that they would eventually write their own records of His visit.

This is a powerful idea. Jesus' ministry was not limited to one small region in the Middle East. It was not limited to the Nephites in the Americas. The Savior is the **Lord of the whole earth**, and His love extends to every nation, kindred, tongue, and people. Wherever the Father led His children, the Son followed.

Think about what that means. Right now, there may be records -- scriptures, histories, testimonies -- written by people in other parts of the world who saw the resurrected Christ. We do not have those records yet. But Jesus said they exist, and one day they will come forth (2 Nephi 29:12-13).

This should change the way you think about God's relationship with the world. He does not have favorites. He does not focus on one group and ignore the rest. He is the Good Shepherd, and He knows every flock. He goes to them. He speaks to them. He calls them by name.

The message of "other sheep" is ultimately a message about **God's love**. It is huge. It has no borders. It reaches people you have never heard of, in places you have never been. And it is the same love He offers you.
