### Lesson 2.1 -- Presented at the Temple

When Jesus was about **forty days old**, Mary and Joseph brought Him to the temple in Jerusalem. This wasn't unusual. The **Law of Moses** required that every firstborn son be presented to the Lord, and that the mother offer a sacrifice of purification after giving birth (Luke 2:22-24). Mary and Joseph were faithful, and they followed the law carefully.

The sacrifice they brought was **two young pigeons**, the offering allowed for families who could not afford a lamb. This tells you something. The Son of God was born into a humble, working family. Not wealthy. Not powerful by the world's standards.

At the temple, something remarkable happened. An old man named **Simeon** was there. The Holy Ghost had promised Simeon that he would not die before he had seen the **Lord's Christ**, the Messiah (Luke 2:26). When Mary and Joseph walked in carrying baby Jesus, Simeon knew immediately. He took the baby in his arms and said a prayer of thanks.

Simeon's words are beautiful and important. He said, "Lord, now lettest thou thy servant depart in peace, according to thy word: for mine eyes have seen thy salvation" (Luke 2:29-30). He had been waiting his entire life for this moment. Now he had held the Savior in his own arms, and he was ready to die in peace. He also said that Jesus would be "a light to lighten the Gentiles, and the glory of thy people Israel" (Luke 2:32). Jesus wasn't just for one group of people. He was for everyone.

There was also a woman at the temple that day, a prophetess named **Anna**. She was very old, about eighty-four years of age, and she spent her days fasting and praying at the temple (Luke 2:36-37). When she saw the baby Jesus, she gave thanks to God and began telling everyone who was looking for the redemption of Israel that the Messiah had come (Luke 2:38).

Two witnesses. Both recognized Jesus through the power of the Holy Ghost. Both had spent their lives preparing spiritually. Simeon and Anna remind us that when we live close to the Spirit, we can recognize the hand of God, even when it shows up in unexpected ways, like a tiny baby in a poor family's arms.
