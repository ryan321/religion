### Lesson 2.3 -- The Boy at the Temple

Every year, faithful Jewish families traveled to **Jerusalem** for the feast of **Passover**. It was a big deal, a religious holiday that remembered how God delivered Israel from slavery in Egypt. Families walked for days to get there, usually traveling in large groups with relatives and neighbors.

When Jesus was **twelve years old**, His family made the trip as usual (Luke 2:41-42). They celebrated the feast, and when it was over, the group started the long walk back to Nazareth. Mary and Joseph assumed Jesus was somewhere in the group, probably walking with friends or cousins. But after a full day of travel, they realized He was not with them at all (Luke 2:43-44).

Imagine how they felt. They turned around and went back to Jerusalem. They searched for **three days**. Three days of worry, looking through streets and crowds. Finally, they found Him in the **temple** (Luke 2:46).

And what was twelve-year-old Jesus doing? He was sitting with the **teachers** of the law, the educated religious scholars of Israel. He was listening to them, asking them questions, and giving answers. Everyone who heard Him was **amazed** at His understanding (Luke 2:46-47). This wasn't a kid reciting memorized lines. He genuinely understood the scriptures at a level that stunned the adults around Him.

When Mary found Him, she said what any worried parent would say: "Son, why hast thou thus dealt with us? Behold, thy father and I have sought thee sorrowing" (Luke 2:48). In other words, "We have been so worried about you!"

Jesus' answer is one of the most important things He said as a child. He replied, "How is it that ye sought me? Wist ye not that I must be about my **Father's business**?" (Luke 2:49). Notice He didn't say "Joseph's business." He said "my Father's business." Even at twelve, Jesus knew exactly who He was. He knew He was the Son of God, and He understood that His life had a divine purpose.

After this, Jesus went home with His parents and was obedient to them (Luke 2:51). He didn't rebel or act superior. He respected His earthly family while also understanding His heavenly mission. That balance is worth paying attention to. You can be dedicated to God and still honor your parents and do what they ask.
