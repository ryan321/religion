### Lesson 2.4 -- John the Baptist Prepares the Way

Before a king arrives, someone usually goes ahead to prepare the way. Maybe they announce the king is coming, clear the road, or get things ready. That is exactly what **John the Baptist** did for Jesus.

John was born to **Zacharias** and **Elisabeth**. Zacharias was a priest, and Elisabeth was a cousin of Mary, the mother of Jesus. Before John was even born, an angel told Zacharias that his son would be special. He would "make ready a people prepared for the Lord" (Luke 1:17).

When John grew up, he didn't live a comfortable life in the city. He went out into the **wilderness** near the Jordan River. He wore rough clothing made of **camel's hair** and ate **locusts and wild honey** (Matthew 3:4). He was not interested in fitting in. He had a job to do.

John's message was simple and direct: **"Repent ye: for the kingdom of heaven is at hand"** (Matthew 3:2). He told people to stop sinning, change their lives, and get ready because the Messiah was coming soon. People came from all over -- from Jerusalem, from Judea, from the whole region around the Jordan. They confessed their sins and were **baptized** by John in the river (Matthew 3:5-6).

John was bold. When religious leaders called **Pharisees** and **Sadducees** came to be baptized, John called them out. He said, "Bring forth fruits meet for repentance" (Matthew 3:8). In other words, don't just show up and go through the motions. Actually change your behavior. Prove it with your actions.

But John was also very clear about his role. He knew he was not the Messiah. He said, "I indeed baptize you with water unto repentance: but he that cometh after me is mightier than I, whose shoes I am not worthy to bear: he shall baptize you with the **Holy Ghost**, and with fire" (Matthew 3:11). John understood that his job was to prepare, not to be the star. He was the opening act, and Jesus was the main event.

The prophet **Isaiah** had foretold this mission hundreds of years earlier: "The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord" (Isaiah 40:3). John fulfilled that prophecy perfectly.
