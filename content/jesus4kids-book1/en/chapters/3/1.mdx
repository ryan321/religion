### Lesson 3.1 -- The Baptism of Jesus

John the Baptist had been preaching and baptizing at the **Jordan River** for some time. People came from all over to repent and be baptized. Then one day, Jesus Himself walked up to be baptized (Matthew 3:13).

John recognized Him. And John's reaction is really interesting. He tried to stop it. He said, "I have need to be baptized of thee, and comest thou to me?" (Matthew 3:14). John knew that Jesus was the **Son of God**, the sinless, perfect Messiah. Baptism was for repentance, for washing away sins. Jesus had no sins. So why would He need to be baptized?

Jesus gave a clear answer: "Suffer it to be so now: for thus it becometh us to **fulfil all righteousness**" (Matthew 3:15). Jesus was baptized not because He had sinned, but because it was the right thing to do. He was setting an **example**. He was showing everyone that baptism is essential, not optional, not just for some people, but for everyone. If even the Son of God needed to be baptized, then so does everyone else.

Notice how the baptism happened. Jesus went down **into the water**, and John baptized Him by **immersion**, putting Him completely under the water and bringing Him back up (Matthew 3:16). This is the same way baptisms are performed in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints today. It matters because it follows the pattern Jesus set.

After Jesus came up out of the water, three amazing things happened at once. The **heavens were opened**. The **Holy Ghost** descended on Jesus in the form of a **dove**. And the voice of **Heavenly Father** spoke from heaven, saying, "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased" (Matthew 3:16-17).

All three members of the **Godhead** were present at that moment. Jesus the Son was in the water. The Holy Ghost descended as a dove. And God the Father spoke from heaven. This is powerful evidence that the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost are **three separate beings** who work together in perfect unity. That is a key teaching of the restored gospel.

The baptism of Jesus marks the real beginning of His public ministry. From this point forward, He would teach, heal, call disciples, and change the world.
