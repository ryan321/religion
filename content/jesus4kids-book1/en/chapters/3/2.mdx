### Lesson 3.2 -- The Temptations in the Wilderness

Right after His baptism, Jesus didn't jump straight into teaching and performing miracles. Instead, the Spirit led Him into the **wilderness** to be alone (Matthew 4:1). He went there to prepare -- to fast, to pray, and to get ready for the enormous mission ahead of Him.

Jesus **fasted for forty days and forty nights** (Matthew 4:2). That means He ate nothing for nearly six weeks. By the end, He was physically weak and extremely hungry. And that is exactly when **Satan** showed up. The devil is strategic. He attacks when you are at your weakest.

Satan tempted Jesus **three times**, and each temptation was designed to get Jesus to misuse His power or abandon His mission.

**Temptation one:** Satan said, "If thou be the Son of God, command that these stones be made bread" (Matthew 4:3). Jesus was starving. He absolutely had the power to turn stones into bread. But this was about more than food. Satan was trying to get Jesus to use His divine power for selfish reasons. Jesus answered with scripture: "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" (Matthew 4:4, quoting Deuteronomy 8:3).

**Temptation two:** Satan took Jesus to the top of the temple in Jerusalem and said, "If thou be the Son of God, cast thyself down" -- and then he quoted a scripture about angels protecting Him (Matthew 4:5-6). Satan actually used the scriptures to try to trick Jesus. But Jesus saw through it. He replied, "Thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God" (Matthew 4:7, quoting Deuteronomy 6:16).

**Temptation three:** Satan showed Jesus all the kingdoms of the world and said he would give them all to Jesus if Jesus would just bow down and **worship** him (Matthew 4:8-9). This was the biggest temptation -- power over everything, right now, no suffering required. Jesus answered firmly: "Get thee hence, Satan: for it is written, Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve" (Matthew 4:10, quoting Deuteronomy 6:13).

After that, Satan left, and angels came and ministered to Jesus (Matthew 4:11).

Here is what matters: even the **Son of God** was tempted. Temptation is not a sin. Giving in is. And Jesus showed the best way to fight back -- **know the scriptures** and use them.
