### Lesson 3.3 -- The First Disciples

After Jesus returned from the wilderness, He began doing something that might seem surprising. Before performing big miracles or preaching to huge crowds, He started gathering a small group of followers. He called them **disciples**, people who would learn from Him, travel with Him, and eventually carry on His work.

The story of the first disciples starts with John the Baptist. One day, John was standing with two of his own followers when Jesus walked by. John looked at Jesus and said, "Behold the **Lamb of God**" (John 1:36). That was all it took. The two men left John and started following Jesus.

One of those two men was **Andrew**. When Jesus noticed them following, He asked, "What seek ye?" They asked where He was staying, and He simply said, "Come and see" (John 1:38-39). They spent the day with Him. Whatever happened during that time changed everything for Andrew.

The first thing Andrew did was go find his brother **Simon** and tell him, "We have found the Messias," the Messiah (John 1:41). Andrew brought Simon to Jesus. When Jesus saw Simon, He said, "Thou shalt be called **Cephas**," which means Peter, or "a rock" (John 1:42). Jesus saw in Simon something that Simon probably didn't even see in himself yet. He gave him a new name and a new identity.

The next day, Jesus found **Philip** and simply said, "Follow me" (John 1:43). Philip was from the same town as Andrew and Peter. Then Philip went and found **Nathanael** and told him about Jesus. Nathanael was skeptical at first. He said, "Can there any good thing come out of Nazareth?" (John 1:46). Philip just said, "Come and see." When Nathanael met Jesus, Jesus knew things about Nathanael that no stranger should have known. Nathanael was convinced: "Rabbi, thou art the Son of God; thou art the King of Israel" (John 1:49).

Look at who these men were. **Fishermen. Working people.** Not priests or scholars or politicians. Not people with money or influence. They were ordinary, and that was exactly the point. Jesus didn't need people who already had all the answers. He needed people who were willing to **follow, learn, and serve**.

That is still how He works today. He doesn't wait until you are perfect or powerful. He calls you where you are and helps you become who you need to be.
