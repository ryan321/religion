### Lesson 3.4 -- The Wedding at Cana

Jesus had been baptized, He had overcome Satan's temptations, and He had begun calling His first disciples. Now something happened that changed everything -- He performed His **first recorded miracle**.

Jesus, His mother Mary, and His disciples were invited to a **wedding** in a town called **Cana** in Galilee (John 2:1-2). Weddings in ancient Israel were big celebrations that could last for several days. The whole community would come together to eat, drink, and celebrate with the new couple. Running out of food or drink at a wedding was a serious embarrassment for the host family.

And that is exactly what happened. The wine ran out (John 2:3). Mary noticed and went to Jesus. She simply told Him, "They have no wine." She didn't ask Him for a miracle. She didn't give Him instructions. She just brought the problem to Him and trusted He would handle it.

Jesus' response sounds a little unusual at first. He said, "Woman, what have I to do with thee? mine hour is not yet come" (John 2:4). He wasn't being rude -- "woman" was actually a respectful way to address someone in that culture. He was telling Mary that the time for Him to fully reveal Himself had not yet arrived. But Mary clearly believed He would act. She turned to the servants and said, "Whatsoever he saith unto you, do it" (John 2:5). That is great advice for all of us.

Jesus told the servants to fill **six large stone pots** with water. These pots were big -- each one held twenty to thirty gallons. The servants filled them to the brim. Then Jesus told them to draw some out and take it to the **governor of the feast** -- the person in charge (John 2:7-8).

When the governor tasted it, the water had become **wine**. And not just ordinary wine -- it was the best wine at the whole celebration. The governor was confused. He told the bridegroom, "Thou hast kept the good wine until now" (John 2:10). He had no idea where it came from, but the servants knew.

The scripture says that this miracle "manifested forth his glory; and his **disciples believed on him**" (John 2:11). That is the key. The miracle wasn't about the wine itself. It was about showing His disciples who He was. He had power over the physical world. He could transform ordinary things into something extraordinary.

And He still does. That is what the gospel is about -- Jesus taking ordinary people and ordinary lives and making them into something more than we could ever be on our own.
