### Lesson 4.2 -- Teaching in the Synagogues

After calling the Twelve, Jesus traveled all through **Galilee** teaching in the **synagogues** (Matthew 4:23). Synagogues were the local places of worship -- kind of like our chapels today. Every town had one, and people gathered there each Sabbath to read the scriptures and learn. It was a natural place for Jesus to share His message, because the people were already there to listen.

One visit stands out above the rest. Jesus went back to **Nazareth** -- His hometown, the place where He had grown up. He walked into the synagogue on the Sabbath, stood up to read, and was handed the scroll of Isaiah. He found a specific passage and read it out loud: "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he hath sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives" (Luke 4:18).

Then He rolled up the scroll, sat down, and said something that stunned everyone in the room: **"This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears"** (Luke 4:21). He was saying, plainly, that He was the one Isaiah had been writing about. He was the promised Messiah.

At first, the people were amazed. They whispered to each other, "Isn't this Joseph's son?" They had watched Him grow up. They knew His family. How could this carpenter's kid be the Anointed One?

Their amazement quickly turned to anger. Jesus pointed out that prophets in the past -- like Elijah and Elisha -- had been sent to help people outside of Israel because their own people wouldn't listen. That made the crowd furious. They grabbed Him, dragged Him to the edge of a cliff outside town, and tried to throw Him off (Luke 4:28-29). But Jesus simply **walked right through the crowd** and went on His way. It wasn't His time.

This event teaches something that comes up again and again in the Gospels: **a prophet is not accepted in his own country** (Luke 4:24). The people who thought they knew Jesus best had the hardest time believing who He really was. Sometimes being too familiar with someone makes it harder to see them clearly.

Jesus didn't stop teaching because of one rough reception. He kept moving, kept preaching, and kept sharing truth -- even when people pushed back.
