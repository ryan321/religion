### Lesson 4.3 -- Healing the Sick

One of the most powerful things Jesus did during His ministry was heal people. Not just a few people here and there -- He healed enormous crowds, one person at a time, with real compassion and real power. These weren't magic tricks or illusions. Every healing was an **act of love** that also testified of God's power.

One of the most striking stories involves a man with **leprosy**. Leprosy was a terrible skin disease, and in those days, lepers had to live apart from everyone else. Nobody would touch them. But this man came to Jesus, knelt down, and said, "If thou wilt, thou canst make me clean." Jesus reached out and **touched him** -- something no one else would do -- and said, "I will; be thou clean." Immediately, the leprosy was gone (Mark 1:40-42). Jesus didn't just heal the man's body. He treated him like a person again.

Another time, Jesus was teaching inside a house that was so packed with people that no one else could get in. Four friends had a **paralyzed man** they wanted Jesus to heal, but they couldn't get through the door. So they climbed up on the roof, dug a hole in it, and **lowered their friend down** right in front of Jesus (Mark 2:1-5). Jesus saw their faith and said to the man, "Thy sins be forgiven thee." Then, to prove He had that authority, He told the man to pick up his bed and walk -- and the man did (Mark 2:10-12).

Jesus also healed **Peter's mother-in-law**, who was sick with a fever. He simply took her by the hand, and the fever left her. She got up and immediately started serving dinner (Matthew 8:14-15). No recovery time needed.

Word spread fast. Soon, crowds brought everyone who was sick or suffering, and Jesus **healed them all** (Matthew 4:24). People with diseases, people in pain, people possessed by evil spirits -- He turned no one away.

These healings showed something important: Jesus had **power over sickness, sin, and even nature itself**. He wasn't just a good teacher. He was the Son of God, and His miracles proved it. Every time He healed someone, it was like a sign pointing to who He really was.
