### Lesson 5.2 -- The Higher Law

Before Jesus came, the people of Israel had the **Law of Moses**. It was a set of rules that told them what to do and what not to do. Don't kill. Don't steal. Don't lie. It covered a lot of ground, and it had been guiding Israel for centuries. But Jesus didn't come to throw those rules away. He came to take them higher.

He said it clearly: "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil" (Matthew 5:17). He wasn't erasing the old rules. He was showing what they were always pointing toward.

Here's how He did it. The law said, **"Thou shalt not kill."** Fair enough; don't murder anyone. But Jesus said that wasn't enough. He taught, "Whosoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment" (Matthew 5:21-22). In other words, it's not just about what your hands do. It's about what's going on in your heart.

The law said, **"Thou shalt not commit adultery."** Jesus raised the bar even further: "Whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart" (Matthew 5:27-28). Again, the heart matters, not just the outward action.

The old law said, **"An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth"**, meaning punishment should match the crime. Jesus said to skip the revenge altogether. "Whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also" (Matthew 5:38-39). That's not weakness. That's strength under control.

Jesus was raising the standard from **outward behavior to inner character**. It's not enough to just look good on the outside. God cares about who you are on the inside: your thoughts, your intentions, your feelings toward others.

And then He dropped the biggest challenge of all: **"Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect"** (Matthew 5:48). That sounds impossible, and on our own, it is. But with the Savior's help, we can keep growing closer to that standard every day. Perfection is the direction, not a pass/fail test.

The **higher law** is about becoming better, not just behaving better.
