### Lesson 5.3 -- How to Pray

In the middle of the Sermon on the Mount, Jesus taught His followers how to pray. This might seem surprising. Hadn't they been praying their whole lives? Yes, but Jesus wanted to show them what real prayer looks like, because a lot of people had gotten it wrong.

The problem was that some people prayed **to be seen**. They would stand on street corners or in the synagogues and pray loudly so everyone would notice how righteous they were. Jesus said that kind of prayer already has its reward: the attention of other people. But it doesn't reach God the way it should (Matthew 6:5).

Instead, Jesus said to go into your room, shut the door, and pray to your Father "which is in secret" (Matthew 6:6). Prayer isn't a performance. It's a **real conversation with Heavenly Father**. No audience needed.

Then He gave them a model, what we now call the **Lord's Prayer** (Matthew 6:9-13). It goes like this:

**"Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name."** Start by honoring God and recognizing who He is. **"Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven."** Ask for God's plans to move forward, not just your own. **"Give us this day our daily bread."** Ask for what you need, not a giant wish list, just what you need today. **"And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors."** Ask for forgiveness, and remember to forgive others too. **"And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil."** Ask for help staying on the right path.

Notice the pattern. It starts with God, not with you. It asks for His will first. It keeps things simple and honest. And it connects your own forgiveness to how you forgive the people around you. That last part is a big deal. Jesus even repeated it after the prayer, saying that if you don't forgive others, your Father won't forgive you (Matthew 6:14-15).

Prayer doesn't have to be fancy or long. It has to be **sincere**. God already knows what you need. He's not waiting for the perfect words. He's waiting for a real, humble heart.

You can pray anytime: morning, night, before meals, before a hard test, when you're sad, when you're grateful. There's no wrong time to talk to your Father in Heaven.
