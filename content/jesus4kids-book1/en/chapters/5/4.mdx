### Lesson 5.4 -- Wise and Foolish Builders

Jesus packed a lot into the Sermon on the Mount, and as He wrapped it up, He left His listeners with some of the most memorable images in all of scripture. These are the kind of word pictures that stick with you.

First, He told His followers, **"Ye are the light of the world"** (Matthew 5:14). Think about that. He didn't say, "I am the light and you should watch." He said *you* are the light. A city on a hill can't be hidden. You don't light a candle and then cover it with a basket. You put it where everyone can see it. In the same way, your good works should shine so that people can see them and give glory to God (Matthew 5:16). You're supposed to be visible, not to show off, but to light the way for others.

Next, Jesus warned about **judging other people**. He used a funny image to make the point: "Why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother's eye, but considerest not the beam that is in thine own eye?" (Matthew 7:3). A **mote** is a tiny speck. A **beam** is a huge piece of lumber. Picture someone walking around with a plank sticking out of their face, pointing at someone else's splinter. It's ridiculous, and that's the point. Fix your own problems before you start picking apart someone else's.

Finally, Jesus ended the whole sermon with the parable of the **wise and foolish builders** (Matthew 7:24-27). The wise man built his house on a **rock**. When the rain poured down, the floods came, and the winds blew, his house stood firm. The foolish man built his house on **sand**. Same storm hit, and his house fell apart completely.

Jesus made the meaning clear: "Whosoever heareth these sayings of mine, and **doeth them**, I will liken him unto a wise man" (Matthew 7:24). It's not enough to hear the teachings. You have to **live them**. That's the rock. If you just listen and do nothing, you're building on sand, and when life gets hard, everything crumbles.

The Sermon on the Mount isn't just beautiful teaching. It's a blueprint for life. Jesus is the rock, and everything He taught in this sermon is meant to be built on, day by day, choice by choice.
