### Lesson 6.1 -- Why Jesus Taught in Parables

As Jesus continued His ministry, He started teaching in a very specific way -- through **parables**. A parable is a short story that uses everyday situations to teach a spiritual truth. Jesus told stories about farmers, fishermen, shepherds, merchants, and families. He talked about seeds, coins, sheep, bread, and weddings. He used things people already understood to help them see things they didn't.

Why stories? Because **stories stick**. You might forget a list of rules, but you'll remember a good story. A parable meets people right where they are. A farmer hears a story about planting seeds and immediately connects. A shepherd hears about a lost sheep and gets it. Jesus was brilliant at finding the right image for the right crowd.

But there's another reason Jesus taught in parables. Not everyone who listened to Him was there for the right reasons. Some people came out of curiosity. Some came to argue. Some came hoping to catch Him saying something wrong. Parables worked like a kind of filter. If you were truly seeking the truth, the meaning of the story would open up to you. If you weren't, it might just sound like a nice tale about farming.

Jesus's own disciples actually asked Him about this. "Why speakest thou unto them in parables?" (Matthew 13:10). He answered by quoting Isaiah: "By hearing ye shall hear, and shall not understand; and seeing ye shall see, and shall not perceive" (Matthew 13:14). Some people hear but don't really listen. Some people look but don't really see. The parables rewarded the ones who truly wanted to understand.

Parables also have **layers**. You can hear a parable as a child and understand it one way, then hear it again as a teenager and catch something new. They grow with you. That's part of what makes them so powerful -- they're simple on the surface but deep underneath.

Over the next few lessons, you'll study some of the most famous parables Jesus ever told. Each one has something specific to teach you. Pay attention, because these stories have been changing lives for two thousand years.
