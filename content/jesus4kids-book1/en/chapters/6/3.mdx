### Lesson 6.3 -- The Good Samaritan and the Prodigal Son

Two of Jesus's most beloved parables appear in the Gospel of Luke, and both of them answer huge questions about what it means to follow God.

The first is the **Parable of the Good Samaritan** (Luke 10:25-37). A lawyer asked Jesus, "Who is my neighbour?" -- basically trying to figure out the minimum requirement. Jesus answered with a story. A man was traveling from Jerusalem to Jericho when robbers attacked him, beat him, took his money, and left him half dead on the road.

A **priest** came along and saw the man lying there -- but he crossed to the other side and kept walking. Then a **Levite** (another religious leader) did the exact same thing. Two men who should have known better just walked on by.

Then a **Samaritan** came along. This is important because Jews and Samaritans did not get along at all. They avoided each other. But the Samaritan stopped, bandaged the man's wounds, put him on his own animal, took him to an inn, and paid for his care. Jesus asked, "Which now of these three was neighbour unto him that fell among the thieves?" The answer was obvious: the one who helped. Jesus said, **"Go, and do thou likewise"** (Luke 10:37).

The lesson is clear: your **neighbor is anyone who needs you** -- regardless of background, nationality, or whether you even like each other.

The second parable is the **Prodigal Son** (Luke 15:11-32). A young man asked his father for his inheritance early, then left home and wasted every penny on wild living. When the money ran out and a famine hit, he ended up feeding pigs and wishing he could eat what they ate. He hit absolute bottom.

Finally, he came to his senses. He decided to go home and beg his father to take him back, even as a servant. But when his father saw him coming down the road, he didn't wait. He **ran** to his son, threw his arms around him, and celebrated his return.

That father represents **Heavenly Father**. No matter how far you've wandered, no matter what you've done, His love doesn't give up. The door is always open. You are always welcome home.
