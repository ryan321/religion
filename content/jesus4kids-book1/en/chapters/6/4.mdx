### Lesson 6.4 -- The Lost Sheep

In Luke chapter 15, Jesus told three parables in a row -- all about things that were lost and then found. We've already talked about the Prodigal Son. Now let's look at the one that came first: the **Parable of the Lost Sheep** (Luke 15:3-7).

The setup is simple. A shepherd has **100 sheep**. One of them wanders off. What does the shepherd do? He leaves the 99 in a safe place and goes after the **one** that's missing. He searches until he finds it. And when he does, he doesn't scold it or drag it back. He picks it up, puts it on his shoulders, and **carries it home**. Then he calls his friends and neighbors together and says, "Rejoice with me; for I have found my sheep which was lost" (Luke 15:6).

Jesus's point was clear: **"I say unto you, that likewise joy shall be in heaven over one sinner that repenteth, more than over ninety and nine just persons, which need no repentance"** (Luke 15:7).

Let that sink in. Heaven -- all of heaven -- celebrates when one person turns around and comes back. Not a polite nod. Not a quiet sigh of relief. **Joy.** A real celebration.

This parable teaches something that matters more than almost anything else you'll learn: **every single person matters to the Savior**. Not just the group. Not just the majority. The one. If you were the only person on earth, Jesus would still have come. He would still have searched for you. He would still have carried you home.

Sometimes you might feel lost. Maybe you've made mistakes you feel embarrassed about. Maybe you've drifted away from things you know are right. Maybe you feel invisible, like nobody notices you. This parable is for you. The Shepherd knows you're missing, and He's already looking for you.

And here's something else: Jesus doesn't just want you to be found. He wants you to be a **finder** too. Part of following Him means looking out for the people around you who might be struggling, lonely, or lost. You can be someone who goes after the one.

You are never too far gone to be found. You are never too small to matter. The Good Shepherd knows His sheep by name -- and that includes you.
