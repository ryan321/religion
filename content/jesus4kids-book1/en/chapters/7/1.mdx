### Lesson 7.1 -- Why Miracles?

When you hear the word "miracle," you might picture something flashy -- like a magician pulling a rabbit out of a hat or a superhero saving the day. But the miracles of Jesus were nothing like that. They weren't performances to impress a crowd. Every single miracle He did was an act of love.

Think about it. When He healed a blind man, it was because He cared about that person. When He calmed a raging storm, it was because His friends were terrified. When He fed thousands of hungry people, it was because they had nothing to eat and He had compassion on them. Jesus never did miracles to show off. He did them because people needed help.

His miracles also served another purpose -- they showed everyone who He really was. He wasn't just a good teacher or a wise rabbi. He was the **Son of God**, with actual power over sickness, nature, and even death. Every healing, every calmed sea, every person brought back to life pointed to the same truth: this man had the power of God in Him.

**Faith** played a big role too. Over and over, Jesus connected miracles to the faith of the people involved. When people believed in Him, amazing things happened. When they doubted, not much did. He once told a woman who had been healed, "Thy faith hath made thee whole" (Mark 5:34). The miracle came because she believed it could.

The prophet Nephi in the Book of Mormon summed it up well. He taught that Jesus "doeth not anything save it be for the benefit of the world" (2 Nephi 26:24). Everything Jesus did -- every miracle, every sermon, every quiet moment -- was meant to help, to teach, or to save. There was never a selfish reason behind any of it.

So when you read about the miracles in the next few lessons, don't just think, "Wow, that's cool." Ask yourself what each miracle tells you about the Savior. What does it say about His power? What does it say about His heart? That's the real point.
