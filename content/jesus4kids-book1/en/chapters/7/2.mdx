### Lesson 7.2 -- Healing the Blind, the Lame, and the Lepers

Jesus healed a lot of people during His ministry. But the way He did it tells you just as much as the fact that He did it. He didn't stand far away and wave His hand. He got close. He touched people. He spoke to them personally. That matters more than you might think.

Take the **man born blind** (John 9:1-7). This man had never seen anything -- not once in his entire life. Jesus made clay with dirt and spit, put it on the man's eyes, and told him to go wash in the pool of Siloam. The man did it, and for the first time ever, he could see. The religious leaders got upset about it because it happened on the Sabbath, but the man's response was simple and honest: "One thing I know, that, whereas I was blind, now I see" (John 9:25).

Then there were the **ten lepers** (Luke 17:12-19). Leprosy was a terrible skin disease, and people with it had to live apart from everyone else. They couldn't hug their families. They couldn't go to the market. They were completely cut off. Jesus healed all ten of them. But only **one came back to say thank you**. Just one. Jesus noticed. He asked, "Were there not ten cleansed? but where are the nine?" That's a lesson about **gratitude** that's hard to miss.

One of the most touching stories is about a **woman who had been sick for twelve years** (Mark 5:25-34). She had spent all her money on doctors and nothing worked. She believed that if she could just touch the edge of Jesus' clothing, she would be healed. So she pushed through the crowd and touched His garment. Immediately, she was well. Jesus felt the power go out of Him, turned around, and found her. He didn't scold her. He called her "daughter" and told her that her **faith** had made her whole.

What stands out in all of these stories is the **tenderness** of Jesus. He didn't just fix the problem and walk away. He saw each person as an individual. He cared about their suffering, and He cared about them after the healing too. His power was real, but so was His love.
