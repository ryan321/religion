### Lesson 7.3 -- Feeding the Five Thousand and Walking on Water

Picture this: Jesus has been teaching all day in a remote area, far from any town. Thousands of people have come to listen. Now it's getting late, and everyone is hungry. The disciples come to Jesus with a practical suggestion: send the people away so they can go buy food. But Jesus has a different idea. He says, "They need not depart; give ye them to eat" (Matthew 14:16).

The disciples are confused. They don't have nearly enough food for this many people. But a boy in the crowd has **five loaves of bread and two fish**. That's it. A small lunch for one kid. Jesus takes it, looks up to heaven, blesses it, and starts breaking it apart. The disciples begin handing it out. And it just keeps coming. Over **five thousand people** eat until they are full -- and when they pick up the leftovers, there are **twelve baskets** of food remaining (Matthew 14:13-21). More was left over than they started with.

This miracle showed that Jesus has power to provide. He can take something small and make it more than enough. That's true with food, and it's true with everything else in your life. When you give what you have to the Lord, even if it doesn't seem like much, He can multiply it.

That same night, the disciples got in a boat to cross the Sea of Galilee. A storm hit, and the waves were tossing the boat around. Then, in the middle of the night, they saw someone **walking on the water** toward them (Matthew 14:25). They were terrified. They thought it was a ghost. But Jesus called out, "Be of good cheer; it is I; be not afraid."

Peter, always the bold one, said, "Lord, if it be thou, bid me come unto thee on the water." Jesus said, "Come." And Peter actually did it -- he climbed out of the boat and started **walking on the water** toward Jesus. But then he noticed the wind and the waves, got scared, and began to sink. He cried out, "Lord, save me." Jesus immediately reached out and caught him. Then He said something worth remembering: "O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt?" (Matthew 14:31).

The lesson is clear. When Peter kept his eyes on Jesus, he could do the impossible. The moment he focused on the storm around him, he sank. That's how faith works for all of us.
