### Lesson 7.4 -- Raising the Dead

Of all the miracles Jesus performed, none showed His power more clearly than raising people from the dead. Healing sickness is remarkable. Calming a storm is incredible. But bringing someone back to life? That's power over death itself. And Jesus did it three times that we know of.

The first was the **daughter of Jairus** (Mark 5:35-42). Jairus was a ruler of the synagogue, and his twelve-year-old daughter was dying. He begged Jesus to come. On the way, word arrived that the girl had already died. People told Jairus not to bother Jesus anymore. But Jesus said, "Be not afraid, only believe." When He arrived at the house, everyone was weeping. Jesus told them the girl was only sleeping. They laughed at Him. He went into the room, took the girl by the hand, and said, "Talitha cumi," which means, "Little girl, arise." She stood up immediately and walked around.

The second was the **widow's son at Nain** (Luke 7:11-15). Jesus was approaching the town and saw a funeral procession coming out. A mother was burying her only son. She was a widow, which meant she had no one left to take care of her. When Jesus saw her, He had **compassion** on her and said, "Weep not." Then He touched the coffin and said, "Young man, I say unto thee, arise." The dead man sat up and began to speak. Jesus gave him back to his mother.

The third and most dramatic was **Lazarus** (John 11:1-44). Lazarus was a close friend of Jesus. When Lazarus got sick, his sisters Mary and Martha sent word to Jesus, but He waited two more days before going. By the time He arrived, Lazarus had been dead and buried for four days. Martha met Jesus and said, "Lord, if thou hadst been here, my brother had not died." Jesus told her, "I am the resurrection, and the life" (John 11:25).

Then something powerful happened. Standing at the tomb, **Jesus wept** (John 11:35). Even though He knew He was about to raise Lazarus, He still felt the sorrow of those around Him. His love was real and deep. Then He commanded the stone to be removed and called out with a loud voice: "Lazarus, come forth." And Lazarus walked out, still wrapped in burial cloths.

Each of these miracles pointed to something even bigger: the **Resurrection** of Jesus Himself. He wasn't just showing that He could bring others back. He was showing that He held the keys to life and death, and that one day He would conquer death for all of us.
