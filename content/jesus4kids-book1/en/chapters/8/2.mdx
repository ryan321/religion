### Lesson 8.2 -- Cleansing the Temple

The temple in Jerusalem was the most sacred building in all of Israel. It was supposed to be **the house of God**, a place where people came to pray, to worship, and to feel close to their Heavenly Father. But when Jesus arrived, that's not what He found.

The outer area of the temple was called the **Court of the Gentiles**. It was the one place where non-Jewish people could come to worship. But instead of being a quiet, reverent space, it had been turned into a noisy marketplace. Merchants were selling animals for sacrifices at inflated prices. **Money changers** sat at tables, exchanging foreign coins for temple currency and charging unfair fees. The whole area was loud, crowded, and more like a shopping mall than a holy place.

Jesus was not okay with it.

He made a whip out of cords, went into the court, and started driving out the merchants and their animals (John 2:13-16). He flipped over the tables of the money changers. Coins scattered across the stone floor. He told the people selling doves, "Take these things hence; make not my Father's house an house of merchandise."

This wasn't Jesus losing His temper. This was **righteous anger**, the kind of anger that comes from seeing something sacred being treated like trash. The temple belonged to God. It was meant to be "a house of prayer" (Matthew 21:13), and these people had turned it into a place to make money. Jesus had every right to be upset. It was, after all, His Father's house.

The merchants and money changers weren't just being greedy in the wrong location. They were taking advantage of people who had come to worship. Poor families who could barely afford a sacrifice were being charged outrageous prices. The very place that should have welcomed everyone, including Gentiles, was being used to cheat them.

This event shows something important about the Savior. He is loving and gentle, yes. But He is also perfectly **just**. He will not stand by while sacred things are treated with disrespect. The temple matters to Him. Worship matters to Him. How we treat each other in holy places matters to Him.

Today, Latter-day Saints treat their temples with deep reverence. They are the house of the Lord, and we keep them clean, quiet, and focused on the things of God, just the way Jesus said they should be.
