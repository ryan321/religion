### Lesson 8.4 -- The Plot Begins

There's a moment in the Gospels where the opposition to Jesus shifts from grumbling and arguing to something much darker. The leaders stop just watching Him. They start **planning to kill Him**.

The turning point came after Jesus raised **Lazarus** from the dead (John 11:1-44). You read about that miracle in Chapter 7. When people in Jerusalem heard that a man who had been dead for four days was now walking around alive, many of them believed in Jesus. That scared the leaders more than anything else had.

The **chief priests and Pharisees** called an emergency meeting of the **Sanhedrin**, the ruling council (John 11:47-53). Their concern wasn't about truth or right and wrong. It was about politics. They said, "What do we? for this man doeth many miracles. If we let him thus alone, all men will believe on him: and the Romans shall come and take away both our place and nation."

Notice what they were worried about. Not whether Jesus might actually be the Messiah. Not whether God was at work. They were afraid of losing their **positions** and their **nation**. Their power mattered more to them than the truth standing right in front of them.

Then the high priest **Caiaphas** spoke up. He said something that was more prophetic than he realized: "It is expedient for us, that one man should die for the people, and that the whole nation perish not" (John 11:50). He meant it as a cold political calculation. Better to kill one troublemaker than risk Rome destroying everything. But without knowing it, he was describing exactly what was going to happen. Jesus would die for the people. Not because the leaders forced it, but because it was the Father's plan all along.

John recorded the outcome plainly: "Then from that day forth they took counsel together for to put him to death" (John 11:53). The decision was made. They weren't debating anymore. They were scheming.

Jesus knew what was coming. He didn't run. He didn't fight back. He continued teaching, continued healing, and continued moving toward Jerusalem. The plot was underway, but it was unfolding exactly as God had planned since before the world began. The leaders thought they were in control. They weren't.
