### Lesson 9.1 -- "Whom Say Ye That I Am?"

Of all the questions Jesus ever asked, this one might be the most important. Not because He didn't know the answer, but because He wanted His disciples to know it -- and say it -- for themselves.

Jesus and His disciples were traveling near **Caesarea Philippi**, a town in the north of Israel (Matthew 16:13-19). He started with an easy question: "Whom do men say that I the Son of man am?" The disciples listed what they had heard from the crowds. Some people thought Jesus was **John the Baptist** come back from the dead. Others said He was **Elijah**, the great Old Testament prophet. Still others said He was **Jeremiah** or one of the other prophets.

Those were all compliments, in a way. The people were comparing Jesus to some of the greatest figures in Israel's history. But they were all wrong. Jesus wasn't a prophet who had returned. He was something far greater.

Then Jesus asked the real question -- the one that mattered: "But whom say ye that I am?"

**Peter** answered. And his answer changed everything. He said, "Thou art the **Christ**, the Son of the living God" (Matthew 16:16).

That word **Christ** comes from the Greek word for "anointed one." It's the same as the Hebrew word **Messiah**. Peter wasn't just saying Jesus was a good man or a great teacher. He was declaring that Jesus was the promised Savior -- the one Israel had been waiting for -- and that He was literally the Son of God.

Jesus told Peter that this knowledge didn't come from other people. It came straight from **Heavenly Father**. "Flesh and blood hath not revealed it unto thee, but my Father which is in heaven" (Matthew 16:17). A testimony of Jesus Christ comes from God through the **Holy Ghost**, not from studying alone.

This is still the most important question anyone can answer. People today have lots of opinions about Jesus. Some say He was just a teacher. Some say He was only a good example. But the true answer is the same one Peter gave: He is the Christ, the Son of the living God. And you can know it the same way Peter did -- through personal revelation from your Heavenly Father.
