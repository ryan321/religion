### Lesson 9.2 -- The Transfiguration

Shortly after Peter declared that Jesus was the Christ, something extraordinary happened. Jesus took three of His closest apostles -- **Peter**, **James**, and **John** -- and led them up a high mountain to pray (Matthew 17:1-9). What they saw there would stay with them for the rest of their lives.

While they were on the mountain, Jesus was **transfigured** before them. That means His appearance changed. His face began to shine like the sun. His clothes became brilliant white -- "white as the light," Matthew says. The three apostles were seeing Jesus in His glory, not just as the carpenter from Nazareth, but as the divine Son of God He truly was.

Then two figures appeared beside Him: **Moses** and **Elijah**. These were two of the most important prophets in the entire Old Testament. Moses had led Israel out of Egypt and received the law on Mount Sinai. Elijah had sealed the heavens and called down fire from God. Both had been dead for centuries. Now here they were, talking with Jesus.

But the most powerful moment was yet to come. A bright cloud covered them, and a voice spoke from the cloud. It was the voice of **God the Father**. He said, "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased; hear ye him" (Matthew 17:5). The disciples fell on their faces in fear. Jesus came and touched them and told them to get up and not be afraid.

This was a direct witness from Heavenly Father Himself. Not a feeling. Not a rumor. A voice from heaven declaring that Jesus was His Son. The message was clear: listen to Him. Follow Him. He speaks for God.

Latter-day Saints understand something extra about this event. Moses and Elijah didn't just show up for a visit. They came to give **priesthood keys** to Jesus and His apostles. This same pattern repeated itself in the latter days. In 1836, Moses and Elijah appeared at the **Kirtland Temple** and restored those same keys to the Prophet Joseph Smith (D&C 110). God's work follows the same pattern in every dispensation.

The Transfiguration confirmed everything. Peter's testimony was right. The Father's voice sealed it. Jesus is the Christ, and He has the authority of heaven behind Him.
