### Lesson 9.3 -- "I Am"

Throughout His ministry, Jesus made a series of bold declarations about Himself. These weren't humble suggestions or careful hints. They were direct, powerful statements that left no room for confusion. They are called the **"I Am" statements**, and they tell you exactly who Jesus claimed to be.

"I am the **bread of life**" (John 6:35). Just as bread keeps your body alive, Jesus gives life to your soul. He is the source of spiritual nourishment. Without Him, we starve spiritually.

"I am the **light of the world**" (John 8:12). In a world full of darkness, confusion, and sin, Jesus is the light that shows you where to go. Follow Him, and you won't stumble around in the dark.

"I am the **door**" (John 10:9). He is the way into God's presence. There's no sneaking in through a side window. You come to the Father through Jesus Christ, or you don't come at all.

"I am the **good shepherd**" (John 10:11). He watches over His people the way a shepherd watches over sheep, with constant care and a willingness to lay down His life to protect them.

"I am the **resurrection, and the life**" (John 11:25). Death is not the end. Because of Jesus, everyone who has ever lived will be resurrected. And those who believe in Him will have eternal life.

"I am the **way, the truth, and the life**: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" (John 14:6). He isn't pointing to someone else. He is the path, the reality, and the source of life all in one.

But the most stunning statement came in John 8:58. When the Jewish leaders challenged Him, Jesus said, "Before Abraham was, **I am**." They understood exactly what He meant. **"I AM"** was the name God gave to Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:14). By using that name, Jesus was declaring that He was the God of the Old Testament, the same God who spoke to Moses, who led Israel, who created the heavens and the earth. He wasn't just claiming to be a prophet or a teacher. He was claiming to be **God**.

The leaders picked up stones to kill Him for blasphemy. But it wasn't blasphemy. It was the truth. And He was.
