### Lesson 9.4 -- The Good Shepherd

In the time of Jesus, nearly everyone in Israel understood sheep and shepherding. It was one of the most common jobs around. So when Jesus compared Himself to a **Good Shepherd**, people immediately understood what He was talking about, and what made a good shepherd different from a bad one.

Jesus laid out the comparison clearly in John 10:1-18. A **hired worker** watches the sheep only because he's getting paid. He doesn't actually care about the animals. When a wolf comes, the hired worker runs away. He saves himself and lets the sheep get attacked. The sheep are just a job to him.

But the **Good Shepherd** is different. He owns the sheep. He raised them. He knows every single one of them by name. When danger comes, he doesn't run. He stays and fights. He puts himself between the wolf and the flock. And if it comes to it, he "giveth his life for the sheep" (John 10:11).

Jesus was talking about Himself. He was the Good Shepherd. He knew each person individually. He wasn't protecting people because it was His job. He did it because He loved them. And He would literally lay down His life for them. That's exactly what happened on the cross and in Gethsemane.

Then Jesus said something that must have puzzled the people listening: "Other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring, and they shall hear my voice; and there shall be one fold, and one shepherd" (John 10:16). The people in Jerusalem didn't know what He meant. Who were these "other sheep"?

Latter-day Saints know the answer. After His resurrection, Jesus visited the **Nephites** in the Americas (3 Nephi 15:21). He told them directly, "Ye are they of whom I said: Other sheep I have which are not of this fold." The Book of Mormon records that He taught them, healed their sick, blessed their children, and organized His church among them. The Good Shepherd didn't just care about one group of people. He cared about all of His sheep, wherever they were.

That includes you. No matter where you are or what you're going through, the Good Shepherd knows your name. He hasn't forgotten you. He never will.
