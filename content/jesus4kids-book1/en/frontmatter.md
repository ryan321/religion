## Introduction

This book is about the most important person who has ever lived.

Jesus Christ is the Son of God, the Creator of the world, and the Savior of every person who has ever been born. His life, His teachings, His suffering, His death, and His resurrection are the center of everything we believe. And His story is worth knowing well.

You've probably heard many of these stories before -- the baby in the manger, the boy at the temple, the loaves and fishes, the Good Samaritan, the Crucifixion, the empty tomb. But this book puts them all together in order, from beginning to end, so you can see the whole picture. You'll understand not just what happened, but when it happened, why it happened, and how it all fits together.

This book uses the New Testament, the Book of Mormon, the Doctrine and Covenants, and the Pearl of Great Price together. That's because they all testify of the same Savior. When the New Testament tells us about the Crucifixion, the Book of Mormon shows us what happened on the other side of the world at the same moment. When the Savior speaks of "other sheep," the Book of Mormon tells us who they were. These scriptures are meant to be read together, and this book treats them that way.

Scripture references are included throughout. When you see something like (John 3:16) or (3 Nephi 11:10), that's an invitation to open your scriptures and read the original account. The more you do that, the richer this experience will be.

## How to use this book

This book is designed as a workbook for self-study or guided by a parent or teacher. No expertise required -- the lessons explain everything you need to know.

The book is designed to be used over the course of a semester. Each chapter corresponds to a week, with a total of 15 weeks. Each chapter has 4 lessons which are meant to be done once a day, leaving a 5th day for review and the chapter test.

For example, if you started on Monday each week, you could do:

Monday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.1

Tuesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.2

Wednesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.3

Thursday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.4

Friday  Chapter 1, Review and test

Then the next Monday you would start Chapter 2, Lesson 2.1, and so on.

Each lesson has questions at the end, and each chapter has a test. Your AI tutor will help you check your answers and talk through anything you're unsure about.

After each lesson, there is a checkbox to mark when the lesson is completed to keep track of progress.

After 15 weeks (and 15 chapters) you'll know the story of Jesus' life and ministry -- not as isolated stories, but as one continuous narrative that stretches from Bethlehem to Bountiful.

## Note to parents

You don't need to be a scripture scholar to help your child with this book. The lessons are written directly to the child in plain language. Everything is explained as it comes up.

For the best experience, we recommend going through each lesson with your child. The lessons are short -- about 15-20 minutes each. You may find that some of the most meaningful family discussions happen naturally after a lesson, especially as the book moves into the Atonement, the Crucifixion, and the Resurrection.

This book is written from a Latter-day Saint perspective. It treats the scriptural accounts as historical and uses the Book of Mormon, Doctrine and Covenants, and Pearl of Great Price alongside the New Testament. It's designed for families who are active members of the Church and want their children to know the Savior's story deeply.

The goal is not to replace scripture study -- it's to give your child a narrative framework that makes scripture study richer. When they encounter these stories in Come Follow Me or seminary, they'll already know where they fit in the bigger picture.
