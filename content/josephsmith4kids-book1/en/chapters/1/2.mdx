### Lesson 1.2 -- Isaiah and the Marvelous Work

The prophet **Isaiah** lived about 700 years before Jesus Christ was born. He was one of the greatest prophets in the Old Testament, and he saw far into the future. One of the things Isaiah saw was the coming forth of the **Book of Mormon** and the work that Joseph Smith would do.

In **Isaiah 29:14**, the Lord says, "I will proceed to do a marvellous work among this people, even a marvellous work and a wonder." That phrase, **"a marvellous work and a wonder,"** is one of the most famous descriptions of the Restoration. God was telling the world, hundreds of years in advance, that He would do something so amazing that people would marvel at it.

But Isaiah got even more specific. In **Isaiah 29:11-12**, he describes a **sealed book**. The words of this book would be delivered to "one that is learned" (someone with education and status), and that person would say, "I cannot read it, for it is sealed." Then the book would be given to someone who is "not learned," and that person would bring it forth by the power of God.

Does that story sound familiar? It should. When Joseph Smith began translating the **golden plates**, his scribe **Martin Harris** took a sample of the translated characters to **Professor Charles Anthon** in New York City. Anthon was a well-known scholar. At first, he seemed to confirm the characters were genuine. But when Harris told him the book had been delivered by an angel, Anthon reportedly said he could not read a sealed book. Harris went back to Joseph, the unlearned young man, and the translation continued by the gift and power of God.

This is remarkable. Isaiah described what would happen with the Book of Mormon about 2,500 years before it actually took place. He knew a book would come forward. He knew the learned would reject it. And he knew someone without formal education would be the one to bring it to the world.

Joseph Smith had very little schooling. He was not a scholar, a minister, or a professor. He was a farm boy. But that was exactly the point. God chose someone **unlearned** so that everyone would know the work was done by God's power, not by human cleverness. Isaiah saw this, and it happened just as he said it would.
