### Lesson 1.3 -- Other Prophecies

Joseph of Egypt and Isaiah were not the only prophets who saw the Restoration coming. Other Old Testament prophets also gave prophecies that Latter-day Saints connect directly to the work of Joseph Smith.

The prophet **Ezekiel** wrote about two sticks. In **Ezekiel 37:16-17**, God told Ezekiel to take one stick and write on it "For Judah" and to take another stick and write on it "For Joseph." Then God said to join them together so they become **one stick** in his hand. Latter-day Saints understand the **stick of Judah** to be the **Bible** -- the record of the Jewish people. The **stick of Joseph** is the **Book of Mormon** -- the record of the descendants of Joseph of Egypt who traveled to the Americas. When both books are used together, they become one witness of Jesus Christ. Ezekiel saw this joining of records about 2,600 years before the Book of Mormon was published in 1830.

The prophet **Malachi** gave another important prophecy. In **Malachi 4:5-6**, God promised, "Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord: And he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers." This prophecy was fulfilled on **April 3, 1836**, when the prophet **Elijah** appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery in the **Kirtland Temple**. Elijah restored the **sealing keys** -- the priesthood authority that makes it possible to perform ordinances like baptism and temple sealings for our ancestors. This is why Latter-day Saints do **family history** and **temple work**. It all goes back to Malachi's prophecy and Elijah's visit.

When you look at all of these prophecies together, a clear picture forms. The Restoration was not an afterthought. It was not something God came up with at the last minute. Prophets across thousands of years -- Joseph of Egypt, Isaiah, Ezekiel, Malachi, and others -- all saw pieces of what would happen. They saw the Book of Mormon. They saw the return of Elijah. They saw the "choice seer." God **planned the Restoration from the beginning** of the world's history.

Joseph Smith walked into a story that had been written long before he was born. Every major part of his mission had been seen and recorded by ancient prophets. That is not a coincidence. That is evidence of God's hand in history.
