### Lesson 1.4 -- Why Prophecy Matters

You have spent the last three lessons learning about prophecies from Joseph of Egypt, Isaiah, Ezekiel, and Malachi. But you might be wondering: why does it matter that these prophets saw the Restoration coming? Why should you care about prophecies made thousands of years ago?

Here is the first reason: **prophecy shows that God plans history.** God does not sit back and watch things happen. He is actively involved in the world. He knows the end from the beginning (Isaiah 46:10). When God told Joseph of Egypt about a future "choice seer," He was showing that He already had a plan for the last days. When He told Isaiah about a sealed book, He already knew that the Book of Mormon would come forth through an uneducated young man. God is not surprised by anything. He is in charge.

Here is the second reason: **Joseph Smith was prophesied by name.** That is extraordinary. Think about how many billions of people have lived on this earth. Out of all of them, Joseph Smith was specifically named in prophecy thousands of years before his birth (2 Nephi 3:15). God did not just know that someone would do this work. He knew exactly who it would be. He knew the boy's name. He knew his father's name. He knew where and when he would be born. That tells you something important about how well God knows His children -- and that includes you.

Here is the third reason: **prophecy helps us trust.** When you see that God's promises from thousands of years ago came true, it gives you confidence that His promises to you will come true as well. If God could arrange for a specific boy named Joseph to be born to a specific father named Joseph at a specific time in a specific place, then God can certainly keep His promises in your own life.

When fourteen-year-old Joseph Smith knelt in the **Sacred Grove** in the spring of 1820, he was walking into something that God had been preparing for **millennia**. He did not know it yet. He was just a boy with a question. But God had been getting ready for that moment for a very, very long time. Every prophecy, every ancient record, every promised event pointed to that grove of trees and that young man on his knees.

The life of Joseph Smith did not begin in 1805. In a very real sense, it began thousands of years earlier, in the words of prophets who saw him coming.
