### Lesson 12.1 -- The Plan of Salvation

Joseph Smith taught more about where we came from, why we are here, and where we are going than any prophet before him. The things he revealed are sometimes called the **Plan of Salvation** -- God's plan for His children from the very beginning to the very end and beyond.

One of Joseph's most important teachings was about **premortal life**. Before you were born on this earth, you existed as a spirit child of God. You lived with Heavenly Father. You knew Him. You were there when He presented His plan, and you chose to come to earth. That might seem like a simple idea, but think about what it means -- you are not an accident. You existed before this life, and God has known you for a very long time.

Joseph taught that the reason we are here on earth is to **gain a body**, to be **tested**, and to learn to **choose between right and wrong**. Earth life is like a school. We cannot grow and become like God without going through hard experiences, making choices, and learning from our mistakes. That is why God does not remove every trial from our lives -- the trials are part of the plan.

But what about after this life? Before Joseph's revelations, most Christians taught that people would go to either **heaven or hell** -- and that was it. Joseph revealed something very different. In **D&C 76**, Joseph and Sidney Rigdon received a vision of the **degrees of glory** -- the **celestial**, **terrestrial**, and **telestial** kingdoms. Instead of a simple pass-or-fail system, God had prepared a place for nearly everyone. Each kingdom was glorious, though they differed in the degree of glory, like the sun, moon, and stars differ in brightness.

This was a revolutionary idea. It meant that God was not waiting to punish most of humanity. He was preparing places of glory for His children based on how they lived and what they accepted. It was a plan of **mercy** as well as justice.

Joseph did not come up with these ideas through study or philosophy. He received them through **revelation** -- directly from God. He saw visions, received the words of the Lord, and then shared what he had learned. That is what made his teachings different from the ideas of theologians or scholars. Joseph spoke as someone who had been shown the truth, not someone who was guessing at it.

The Plan of Salvation gave Latter-day Saints a way to understand life, death, and eternity that was more detailed and more hopeful than anything taught in the Christian world at that time.
