### Lesson 12.2 -- Temples and Eternal Families

If you had to choose one teaching that makes The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints different from every other church in the world, it might be this: **families can be together forever**. That idea -- so familiar to Latter-day Saints that children sing songs about it -- came through Joseph Smith, and it changed everything.

Most Christian churches teach that marriage ends at death. "Till death do us part" is how the traditional wedding vow goes. Joseph taught something radically different. Through the power of the **priesthood**, and through sacred ordinances performed in **temples**, a husband and wife can be **sealed** to each other for eternity -- not just for this life but for all of time and beyond. Children can be sealed to their parents. Families are not temporary arrangements that dissolve when someone dies. They are meant to be **eternal**.

This teaching came into full focus during the **Nauvoo period**. Joseph introduced the **endowment** -- a sacred temple ceremony in which members make covenants with God and receive knowledge about the plan of salvation. He also introduced the **sealing ordinance**, which binds families together with priesthood authority that reaches beyond the grave.

Joseph taught the Saints a beautiful principle recorded in **D&C 130:2**: "That same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with eternal glory." In other words, the relationships you build here -- with your family, with your friends -- are not temporary. They matter eternally. The love you feel for your parents, your brothers and sisters, your grandparents -- that love does not have to end.

This teaching is **unique to the Restoration**. No other Christian church teaches that families are sealed by priesthood power for eternity. And it changes everything about how Latter-day Saints think about **death**, **family**, and **temples**. Death is not the end of a family. It is a separation, yes, and it is painful. But the sealing power means that separation is temporary for those who are faithful.

This is also why temples are so important to Latter-day Saints. Temples are not just beautiful buildings. They are the places where these eternal ordinances are performed -- where families are bound together by the authority of God. Joseph spent the last years of his life urgently working to build the **Nauvoo Temple** and to make sure the Saints received these ordinances. He knew how important they were. He had seen eternity, and he wanted every family to have what God was offering.
