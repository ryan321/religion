### Lesson 14.4 -- The Prophet Is Dead

The news traveled to Nauvoo like a wound. **Joseph Smith** was dead at age thirty-eight. His brother **Hyrum Smith** was dead at forty-four. The two men who had led the Church through its most remarkable years were gone in a single afternoon.

The scripture that captures this moment best comes from **Doctrine and Covenants 135:3**: **"In life they were not divided, and in death they were not separated."** Joseph and Hyrum had walked side by side through every trial -- Missouri, Liberty Jail, the building of Nauvoo, the work of the temple. They entered Carthage together, and they left this life together. Their bond as brothers and as servants of God was unbroken.

When the bodies of Joseph and Hyrum were brought back to Nauvoo, the grief was **indescribable**. Thousands of Saints lined the streets and came to view the bodies of their beloved Prophet and Patriarch. Women wept. Men stood in stunned silence. Children who had known Joseph as a kind neighbor and friend could not understand why he was gone. **Emma Smith**, who had endured so much, now faced the greatest sorrow of her life.

And then came the question that hung over everything: **Is it over?**

Joseph's enemies fully expected the Church to collapse. They believed that without Joseph, the Saints would scatter and the Restoration would fade into history like so many other movements that died with their founders. They had killed the shepherd, and they expected the sheep to run.

But they **underestimated** what Joseph Smith had built. More importantly, they underestimated what **God** had planned. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was not built on one man alone. It was built on the priesthood, on sacred ordinances, on the Book of Mormon, on revelation, and on covenants made between God and His people. Those things could not be destroyed by a mob with guns.

Joseph had spent his final months making sure of that. He had prepared others to carry the work forward. The seeds he planted were already growing, and no amount of violence could uproot them.

The Prophet was dead. But the Restoration was alive. And it would continue.
