### Lesson 15.1 -- What He Left Behind

Joseph Smith died at thirty-eight years old. That is young, younger than most people's parents. And yet what he accomplished in that short life is staggering. If you tried to list everything Joseph did, you would run out of space long before you ran out of things to write.

Let's try anyway.

Joseph translated the **Book of Mormon**, over 500 pages of scripture, in roughly sixty working days. He received and recorded the revelations that became the **Doctrine and Covenants**. He translated portions of the Bible and produced the **Pearl of Great Price**. He gave the world more scripture than any single person since the apostles of Jesus Christ.

He restored the **priesthood**, both the **Aaronic** and **Melchizedek**, with authority received directly from heavenly messengers. He organized **The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints** and led it from six members to tens of thousands. He built two major cities: **Kirtland**, Ohio, and **Nauvoo**, Illinois. He oversaw the construction of two **temples** and laid the groundwork for temple worship that continues today.

He introduced sacred **temple ordinances**, including **baptism for the dead** and **eternal marriage**, the sealing of families for eternity. He taught the **plan of salvation** with a clarity and completeness that had not been known on the earth for centuries. He organized the **Relief Society**, giving women a formal role in the work of the Church. He sent **missionaries** across the United States, to England, and beyond, spreading the restored gospel to thousands who were searching for truth.

He trained the **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles** and prepared them to lead after he was gone. He established patterns of Church government, welfare, education, and community building that the Church still follows today.

All of this, every bit of it, was accomplished by a man who started as an uneducated farm boy in upstate New York. Joseph had no wealth, no political connections, and no formal training. What he had was a **calling from God** and the willingness to answer it, no matter the cost.

Most people could not accomplish what Joseph did in ten lifetimes. He did it in one, and he did it while being persecuted nearly every step of the way.
