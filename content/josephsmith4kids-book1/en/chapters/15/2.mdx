### Lesson 15.2 -- The Keys Were Safe

Have you ever watched a relay race? One runner carries the baton as fast as they can, and then, at just the right moment, they hand it off to the next runner. If the handoff fails, the whole race is lost. Joseph Smith understood this principle, and he made sure the handoff of the Restoration would not fail.

In the months before his death, Joseph did something of enormous importance. He **conferred every key, power, and authority** of the priesthood upon the **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles**. This was not an accident or a last-minute decision. Joseph had been preparing the Twelve for years, teaching them, training them, and entrusting them with sacred knowledge. But in those final months, he made it official and complete.

Joseph told the Twelve: **"I have lived until I have seen this burden, which has rested on my shoulders, rolled on to the shoulders of other men."** He expressed relief and even joy that the work no longer depended on him alone. The **keys of the kingdom**, the authority to direct God's work on the earth, were now held by a quorum, not just one man.

This was essential. If Joseph had been the only one holding priesthood keys when he died, the Restoration could have died with him. But because he transferred those keys, the Church could continue with full authority. The ordinances would still be valid. The temples would still function. The missionary work would go on. Everything Joseph had built would endure.

After Joseph's death, **Brigham Young** -- the president of the Quorum of the Twelve -- stepped forward to lead the Church. In a remarkable meeting in August 1844, many Saints reported that as Brigham spoke, he looked and sounded like Joseph. They took this as a sign that God had confirmed the transition of leadership. Brigham would lead the Saints westward to the Salt Lake Valley and continue the work Joseph had begun.

The pattern Joseph established continues today. When a prophet dies, the keys pass to the next authorized leader. There is no gap, no confusion, no question of authority. The Restoration does not depend on any one man staying alive. It depends on the **priesthood keys** that Jesus Christ has placed in His Church, keys that Joseph Smith faithfully received, exercised, and passed on.
