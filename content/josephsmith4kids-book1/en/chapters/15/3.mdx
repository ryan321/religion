### Lesson 15.3 -- The Testimony That Endures

Nearly 200 years have passed since Joseph Smith knelt in the Sacred Grove and offered his first humble prayer. In all that time, critics have never stopped trying to disprove his claims. They have attacked the Book of Mormon, questioned his character, challenged the history, and predicted the downfall of the Church. And yet, here we are.

The **Book of Mormon** is still here. It has been printed in over 100 languages and distributed to millions of people around the world. Scholars have examined it from every possible angle. Critics have offered theory after theory to explain it away: that Joseph copied it, that someone else wrote it, that he made it up. None of those theories have held up. The book remains, and it continues to change lives the way it always has: by bringing people closer to **Jesus Christ**.

The **Church** is still here. From six members in a log cabin in 1830, it has grown to millions of members in virtually every country on earth. It did not fade away after Joseph's death, as his enemies predicted. It did not crumble under persecution, as so many movements have. It grew. It continues to grow.

**Temples** are still here, not just the ones Joseph started, but hundreds more, standing in cities and towns across the globe. The ordinances Joseph restored are performed every single day, blessing the living and the dead.

But the most important evidence is not found in books or buildings. It is found in the hearts of people who have **tested the message for themselves**. Millions of Latter-day Saints have received their own personal witness from the Holy Ghost that the Book of Mormon is true and that Joseph Smith was a prophet of God. That witness does not come from reading a history book or winning an argument. It comes the same way it came to Joseph, by asking God in faith, just as **James 1:5** invites us to do.

Joseph's testimony does not depend on anyone defending it. It depends on people **testing** it. Every person who reads the Book of Mormon with a sincere heart and asks God if it is true is following the same path Joseph followed. And the same God who answered Joseph's prayer is ready to answer theirs.

The testimony of Joseph Smith has endured for nearly two centuries. It will continue to endure because it is true.
