### Lesson 15.4 -- A Prophet for Our Time

It can be easy to think of Joseph Smith as someone from long ago, a figure in an old painting, someone who lived in a world of horses and candles and log cabins. But here is something important to understand: Joseph Smith is not just a historical figure. He is **your prophet**, the prophet of the **last dispensation**, and everything he restored touches your life right now.

The **priesthood** Joseph received from Peter, James, and John is the same priesthood that is used in your ward today. When your bishop leads your congregation, when someone is baptized or confirmed, when a baby receives a name and a blessing, that authority traces directly back to what Joseph Smith received through heavenly messengers. The line is unbroken.

The **Book of Mormon** that Joseph translated is the book you study in seminary, in Sunday School, and at home. When you read its pages, you are holding the same record that Joseph brought forth from the ground by the gift and power of God. The truths in that book (about the Atonement of Jesus Christ, about faith, repentance, and the plan of salvation) are the same truths Joseph taught nearly 200 years ago.

The **temple ordinances** Joseph introduced are performed in temples all over the world right now, today. Families are being sealed for eternity. Baptisms for the dead are being performed by young people your age. The work Joseph started in the Kirtland and Nauvoo Temples has expanded into hundreds of temples across the globe.

Joseph Smith was called to be the prophet of the **dispensation of the fulness of times**, the final era before the Savior's return. That dispensation is not over. You are living in it. Every time you take the sacrament, read the scriptures Joseph brought forth, or enter a temple, you are participating in the Restoration that Joseph gave his life to establish.

Understanding Joseph's life helps you understand your own. You are part of the same work he began. The same God who called a fourteen-year-old boy in a grove of trees is watching over you. The same gospel that changed Joseph's life can change yours. And the same invitation that started it all, to ask God in faith, is open to you today, just as it was to Joseph in the spring of 1820.

His story is not just his. It is yours too.
