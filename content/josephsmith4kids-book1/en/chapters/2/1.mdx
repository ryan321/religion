### Lesson 2.1 -- Joseph Smith Sr.

Before we talk about the Prophet Joseph Smith, we need to talk about his father. **Joseph Smith Sr.** was a tall, strong, hardworking man. He spent most of his life as a farmer, doing the kind of backbreaking labor that early American life demanded. He cleared land, planted crops, and did everything he could to provide for a large family.

Joseph Sr. did not have much formal education. Schools were scarce in rural New England, and farm families needed their children working, not sitting in classrooms. But a lack of schooling did not mean a lack of intelligence. Joseph Sr. was a **thoughtful, reflective man**, especially when it came to religion. He spent years thinking deeply about God, the Bible, and which church was right. He was not satisfied with the churches he saw around him. He felt that something was missing.

One of the most remarkable things about Joseph Sr. is his **dreams and visions**. His wife, Lucy Mack Smith, carefully recorded several of these in her history of the family. In one dream, Joseph Sr. saw a barren field and was told that the world lacked the true religion of God. In another, he saw a beautiful tree with fruit that filled his soul with joy -- a dream that is strikingly similar to Lehi's vision in the Book of Mormon (1 Nephi 8). These dreams came years before his son brought forth the Book of Mormon. Joseph Sr. was being spiritually prepared without even knowing it.

Joseph Sr. was not a member of any church for most of his life, but he **believed in God**. He prayed. He read the Bible. He was honest in his dealings and kind to those around him. Neighbors later described him as a good man, though some looked down on the family because they were poor.

The Smith family **struggled financially** for much of their lives. Joseph Sr. tried different ventures -- farming, teaching school, selling goods -- but bad luck and dishonest business partners often set them back. Despite these struggles, he kept his family together and kept his faith.

When his son Joseph Jr. eventually told him about the angel Moroni and the golden plates, Joseph Sr. believed him. He supported his son completely. He became one of the **Eight Witnesses** of the Book of Mormon and later served as the first **Patriarch** of the restored Church. Joseph Smith Jr. had a father who was prepared by God -- a humble, faithful man who was ready to believe.
