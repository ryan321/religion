### Lesson 2.2 -- Lucy Mack Smith

If Joseph Smith Sr. was the quiet, steady foundation of the Smith family, then **Lucy Mack Smith** was the fire. She was a woman of tremendous **faith and energy**, and she played a major role in shaping the family that would produce a prophet.

Lucy came from the **Mack family** of New England. The Macks were respected, hardworking people. Several of Lucy's relatives had served in the American Revolution. Her family valued independence, courage, and faith in God. Lucy carried those qualities throughout her entire life.

From a young age, Lucy was **deeply religious**. She searched earnestly for the true church of Jesus Christ. She visited different congregations, listened to different ministers, and read the Bible carefully. But like her husband, she never felt fully satisfied with any church she found. Something was missing. She knew God was real, and she prayed often, but she sensed that the fullness of the gospel was not available on the earth. That searching spirit prepared her heart for what her son would eventually bring forth.

Lucy was the **emotional and spiritual anchor** of the Smith family. When times were hard -- and they were hard often -- Lucy kept the family together through prayer, faith, and sheer determination. She organized family worship. She taught her children the Bible. She encouraged them to seek God for themselves. The Smith home was a place where spiritual things were taken seriously, and that was largely because of Lucy.

One of Lucy's greatest contributions to history is the **detailed family record** she left behind. After Joseph Smith's death, Lucy dictated a history of the Smith family that is one of the most important **primary sources** we have about Joseph's early life. Without Lucy's record, we would know far less about Joseph's childhood, the family's struggles, and the events leading up to the Restoration. Historians -- both inside and outside the Church -- rely on her account heavily.

Lucy was not someone who sat quietly in the background. She spoke up. She defended her family. She bore her testimony boldly. When enemies attacked her son and the Church, Lucy stood firm. She endured the deaths of several of her children, including her sons Joseph and Hyrum, who were murdered in 1844. Through all of that heartbreak, her faith never broke.

God did not just prepare a prophet. He prepared a **family** for that prophet. And at the center of that family was Lucy Mack Smith -- a mother whose faith, record-keeping, and fierce loyalty helped make the Restoration possible.
