### Lesson 2.3 -- Brothers and Sisters

Joseph Smith Jr. grew up in a **large family**. He was the fifth of eleven children. That meant the Smith home was always busy, always noisy, and always full of people who depended on each other. In early 1800s America, big families were common, and brothers and sisters were not just playmates. They were coworkers, teammates, and lifelong companions.

The brother who stood out most in Joseph's early life was **Alvin Smith**, the eldest son. Alvin was a **natural leader**. He was strong, responsible, and deeply devoted to his family. When the Smiths were working to buy their farm near Palmyra, New York, Alvin drove the effort. He worked tirelessly to make sure the family would have a home of their own.

Alvin also took a special interest in Joseph's spiritual mission. After Joseph told his family about the angel Moroni's visit in 1823, Alvin encouraged him and urged him to be faithful. Alvin believed his younger brother. Tragically, **Alvin died on November 19, 1823**, just months after Moroni's first visit. He was only 25 years old. His death was **devastating** to the entire family, especially Joseph. Years later, Joseph received a vision showing Alvin in the **celestial kingdom** (Doctrine and Covenants 137:5-6), which brought the family great comfort.

After Alvin's death, **Hyrum Smith** became Joseph's closest companion. Hyrum was about five years older than Joseph, and the two brothers shared a bond that would last their entire lives. Hyrum supported Joseph through every trial: the translation of the Book of Mormon, the organization of the Church, persecutions, imprisonments, and more. Hyrum was one of the **Eight Witnesses** of the golden plates. He served as **Assistant President of the Church** and later as Church **Patriarch**. Joseph and Hyrum were so close that they even died together at **Carthage Jail** on June 27, 1844.

The other Smith siblings also played important roles. **Samuel Harrison Smith** became one of the first missionaries of the restored Church. **William Smith** served as an apostle. **Don Carlos Smith** worked in Church publishing. The Smith sisters -- Sophronia, Catherine, and Lucy -- supported the family and the Church in their own ways.

The Smith family was **tight-knit**. They worked together, prayed together, and supported each other through poverty, grief, and persecution. Joseph did not do his work alone. He had a family behind him every step of the way.
