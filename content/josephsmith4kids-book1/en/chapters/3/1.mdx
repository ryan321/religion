### Lesson 3.1 -- Born in Sharon, Vermont

On **December 23, 1805**, a baby boy was born in a small farmhouse in **Sharon, Vermont**. His parents named him Joseph, after his father. He was the **fifth of eleven children** in the Smith family. Nobody in that tiny Vermont town could have guessed that this baby would grow up to change the world.

The Smiths' home in Sharon was modest. Vermont in the early 1800s was rugged country -- **rocky soil, thick forests, and long, harsh winters**. Farming there was difficult. The growing season was short, and even in good years, families struggled to produce enough food. The Smiths were no exception. They worked hard just to survive.

Joseph's birth came during a period of constant movement for the family. The Smiths did not stay in one place for long during Joseph's early years. From Sharon, they moved to **Tunbridge**, then to **Royalton**, then across the Connecticut River to **Lebanon, New Hampshire**. Each move was driven by the search for better opportunities -- cheaper land, more fertile soil, or a chance to earn a living through teaching, farming, or trade.

Despite all the moving, the Smith home was a place of **love and faith**. Joseph Sr. and Lucy cared deeply about their children. Family prayer was a regular practice. The Bible was read aloud. The children were taught to be honest, to work hard, and to seek God. Even though the family had little in the way of material wealth, they were rich in the things that mattered most.

Vermont itself shaped young Joseph in important ways. Life there taught him about **hard work and perseverance**. Nothing came easy. The soil was stubborn, the winters were cold, and comfort was scarce. People in early Vermont had to be tough, resourceful, and willing to keep going when things got hard. Joseph learned those lessons early.

The family eventually left Vermont for good around **1816**, when Joseph was about ten years old. A series of **crop failures** -- including the devastating "Year Without a Summer" -- made it impossible to keep farming there. The Smiths packed up and headed west to New York, where they hoped for a fresh start. Joseph would never return to Vermont to live, but the state where he was born gave him his first lessons in endurance, poverty, and faith.
