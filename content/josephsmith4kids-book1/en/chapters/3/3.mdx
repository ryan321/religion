### Lesson 3.3 -- Joseph's Personality

When you think of a prophet, you might picture someone quiet and serious, maybe an old man with a long beard sitting in a room full of books. Joseph Smith was nothing like that. He was **cheerful, energetic, and fun**. People who knew him described a young man who was full of life and always ready for a good time.

Joseph was **physically strong**. He grew to be about six feet tall, which was above average for his time. He was broad-shouldered and muscular from years of farm work. He loved **wrestling** and was known as one of the best wrestlers in his area. He would challenge friends, neighbors, and even strangers to friendly matches. He enjoyed **games and physical contests** of all kinds. He was competitive but good-natured about it.

Joseph also had a great sense of **humor**. He loved to laugh and tell stories. He enjoyed jokes and was known for being witty. People who spent time around him often commented on how enjoyable his company was. He was not stiff or formal. He was warm, approachable, and genuinely interested in the people he met.

But there was another side to Joseph too. He was **deeply feeling**. When the Spirit moved him, he wept openly. When he saw people suffering, he was moved to tears. He felt things intensely, both joy and sorrow. He could go from wrestling with friends in the yard to weeping in prayer within the same afternoon. This combination of strength and tenderness is one of the most striking things about him.

Joseph was not **quiet or bookish**. He did not spend his youth buried in libraries. He was an **active, sociable, curious** person who wanted to be out in the world, talking to people, learning from experience, and engaging with life. He had a natural ability to draw people to him. Friends, family members, and even strangers often felt an immediate connection with him. He had what people today might call **charisma**, a magnetic quality that made others want to listen to him and follow him.

This matters because it shows that God does not only call one type of person to be a prophet. Joseph was not chosen because he was the most scholarly or the most solemn. He was chosen because he was faithful, brave, and willing. And he happened to be a lot of fun along the way.
