### Lesson 4.2 -- James 1:5

Have you ever read something that hit you so hard it felt like it was written just for you? That's exactly what happened to Joseph Smith one day while reading the **Bible**.

Joseph was still trying to figure out which church to join. He had listened to preacher after preacher, and none of them could settle the question for him. So he turned to the scriptures, hoping to find guidance there.

One day, he came across a verse in the **Epistle of James, chapter 1, verse 5**. It said: **"If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him."**

Read that again slowly. It says that if you don't know something, you can ask God, and He won't scold you for asking. He'll give you an answer generously. What an amazing promise!

Joseph later wrote that **"never did any passage of scripture come with more power to the heart of man than this did at this time to mine"** (Joseph Smith--History 1:12). It was like a light turned on inside him. The answer to his problem was simple. He didn't need to keep arguing with preachers or reading pamphlets. He could go straight to **God** and ask.

Now, this might sound obvious to you. Of course you can pray to God! But remember, Joseph was a **14-year-old farm boy** with very little formal education. The idea that God would actually answer a personal prayer from someone like him was bold. Most preachers taught that God had stopped speaking to people long ago. The heavens were closed, they said.

But Joseph believed the Bible. If James 1:5 was true, then God would answer. It was that simple. Joseph made up his mind: he would find a quiet place and **pray out loud** for the very first time in his life. He would ask God directly which church was right.

Think about the courage that took. Joseph wasn't just going to whisper a prayer at bedtime. He was going to go somewhere alone and talk to God, trusting that a promise written thousands of years ago still applied to a teenage boy in New York. That kind of **faith** is what set everything in motion.
