### Lesson 4.3 -- Into the Grove

It was a beautiful morning in the **spring of 1820**. Joseph Smith, just **14 years old**, walked into a grove of trees near his family's farm. He had made his decision. He was going to pray out loud and ask God which church he should join.

Joseph found a quiet spot among the trees. He looked around to make sure he was alone. Then he knelt down and opened his mouth to pray. It was the **first time he had ever prayed vocally**, out loud, with real words spoken into the air. Can you imagine how that must have felt? Your heart pounding, your voice shaking, talking to God all by yourself in the woods?

But something terrible happened almost immediately. As soon as Joseph began to pray, a **dark force** seized him. He described it as a thick darkness that gathered around him. It felt like some actual being from an unseen world was trying to destroy him. His tongue was bound. He couldn't speak. He felt as if he were about to be swallowed up completely (Joseph Smith--History 1:15).

Joseph was terrified, but he didn't run. He didn't give up. Instead, at the very moment he was ready to sink into despair, he **called upon God with everything he had**. He used every ounce of energy and faith inside him to cry out for help.

And then it happened.

A **pillar of light** appeared above his head. It descended slowly, coming down through the trees until it rested upon him. The instant that light touched him, the darkness fled. The evil power that had been crushing him vanished completely (Joseph Smith--History 1:16-17).

Joseph described the light as brighter than the sun, yet it didn't hurt his eyes. It was warm and glorious and filled him with a feeling he had never experienced before. Someone was in that light.

This moment teaches us something powerful. Joseph's prayer wasn't easy. He faced real opposition before he ever got an answer. But he didn't quit. He pushed through the darkness, and the light came. Sometimes the hardest prayers are the ones that matter most.
