### Lesson 4.4 -- "This Is My Beloved Son"

When the pillar of light rested upon Joseph Smith in that grove of trees, he looked up and saw something no person had seen in centuries. Standing above him in the air were **two personages**, two glorious beings whose brightness and glory were beyond anything Joseph could describe.

One of them spoke. He pointed to the other and said: **"This is My Beloved Son. Hear Him!"** (Joseph Smith--History 1:17).

Do you understand what that means? **God the Father** was pointing to **Jesus Christ** and telling Joseph to listen to His Son. The Father and the Son, two separate, real beings, appeared to a 14-year-old farm boy kneeling in the woods of New York.

Joseph remembered why he was there. He asked the Savior his question: **Which church should I join?** The answer was clear and direct. He was told to **join none of them**. None of the churches on the earth at that time had the fulness of the gospel. They had some truth, but they had also changed important things over the centuries.

But there was hope in the message too. Joseph was told that the **fulness of the gospel** would be made known to him at a future time. Something big was coming, and Joseph had a role to play in it.

When the vision ended, Joseph found himself lying on his back, looking up at the sky through the trees. He was weak and drained, the way you might feel after something overwhelmingly powerful. He slowly gathered his strength and made his way home.

Think about what had just happened. A teenage boy with no money, no education, and no influence had just spoken face to face with **God the Father and Jesus Christ**. The heavens were not closed. God had not stopped speaking. And He chose to speak to someone the world would have considered nobody special at all.

This event, the **First Vision**, is one of the most important moments in the history of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Everything that followed grew from this single, sacred experience in a grove of trees. Joseph now knew for himself that God was real, that Jesus Christ lived, and that he had a work to do.
