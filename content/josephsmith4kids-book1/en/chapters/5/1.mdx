### Lesson 5.1 -- Nobody Believed Him

After the most incredible experience of his life, Joseph Smith did what most of us would do -- he told someone about it. But what happened next was something Joseph never expected.

A few days after the **First Vision**, Joseph happened to be talking with a **Methodist minister** -- one of the local preachers he had been listening to. Joseph told him about the vision, expecting the man to be excited or at least interested. Instead, the minister **dismissed him completely**. He told Joseph that such things were of the devil, that there were no more visions from God, and that those kinds of experiences had ended with the ancient apostles.

Joseph was stunned. Here was a man who preached about God every Sunday, and he didn't believe that God could actually speak to people anymore. It made no sense to Joseph.

But it got worse. Word spread quickly in a small town like Palmyra. Soon **the whole community** seemed to turn against Joseph. People who had been friendly neighbors now mocked him and treated him with contempt. Grown men -- important men in the community -- spent time and energy attacking a **teenage boy** simply because he said he had seen a vision.

Joseph couldn't understand it. He was just telling the truth. He hadn't hurt anyone. He hadn't stolen anything. He had simply prayed and received an answer. Why did that make people so angry?

Years later, Joseph compared himself to the **Apostle Paul**, who also told people about a vision and was ridiculed for it. Joseph wrote: "I had actually seen a light, and in the midst of that light I saw two Personages, and they did in reality speak to me; and though I was hated and persecuted for saying that I had seen a vision, yet it was true" (Joseph Smith--History 1:24-25).

This was a pattern that would follow Joseph for the rest of his life -- **tell the truth, get attacked**. Over and over again, Joseph would share what God had revealed to him, and over and over again, people would try to silence him. But he never denied what he saw. Not once. Not ever.

That kind of courage in a young person is remarkable. It's one thing to have a sacred experience. It's another thing entirely to stand by it when the whole world tells you you're wrong.
