### Lesson 5.2 -- Three Years Between

After the First Vision in the spring of 1820, you might expect that amazing things happened to Joseph Smith right away. But that's not how it worked. For the next **three years**, from 1820 to 1823, Joseph received **no major revelations**. No angels appeared. No new scriptures came. It was just... life.

And honestly? Joseph's life during those three years was pretty normal for a teenage boy in rural New York. He worked on the family farm. He helped his father and brothers clear land, plant crops, and do the hard physical labor that kept the family fed. He hired out to neighbors for extra money. He grew taller and stronger.

But Joseph was honest about this period of his life. He admitted that he **made mistakes** during those years. He wasn't claiming to be perfect. He was a teenager, after all, full of energy and sometimes getting into the kinds of trouble that young people get into. He later said he was guilty of **"levity"** and mixing with **"jovial company"** -- basically, goofing off and hanging around with fun but not always the best crowd (Joseph Smith--History 1:28).

Does that surprise you? It shouldn't. Joseph never claimed to be perfect before or after the First Vision. He was a real person with real flaws. What made him different wasn't perfection. It was his willingness to keep trying and keep turning back to God.

And here's the important thing: Joseph **never forgot what happened in the grove**. Even during those quiet years, even when he made mistakes, he knew what he had seen. That knowledge stayed with him like a fire that wouldn't go out.

By September of **1823**, Joseph felt a deep need to reconnect with God. He wanted to know where he stood, whether God was still pleased with him despite his weaknesses. So on the night of **September 21, 1823**, Joseph knelt by his bed and prayed earnestly. He poured out his heart, asking to know his **standing before God** and what the Lord wanted him to do next.

He had no idea that this prayer was about to open the next chapter of his extraordinary life.
