### Lesson 5.4 -- Four Years of Waiting

Imagine finding a buried treasure and being told you can't touch it. That's essentially what happened to Joseph Smith at **Hill Cumorah**. He saw the gold plates. He knew exactly where they were. But Moroni told him he could not take them yet. Joseph had to **return to the hill every September** for four years and meet with Moroni each time.

Why the wait? Because Joseph wasn't ready yet. Translating an ancient sacred record and restoring God's church to the earth was an enormous responsibility. Joseph needed to grow in maturity, in faith, and in his ability to handle what was coming. Each year when he visited the hill, Moroni gave him **instructions and teachings** to prepare him for the work ahead.

During those four years of waiting, life didn't stand still. In **1823**, tragedy struck the Smith family. Joseph's oldest brother, **Alvin**, became seriously ill and died on November 19. Alvin had been the strongest, hardest-working member of the family, and he had believed Joseph's story about the angel and the plates. He had encouraged Joseph to be faithful. Losing Alvin was heartbreaking for the entire family, and especially for Joseph, who loved and admired his older brother deeply.

Joseph continued to work hard during those years. He **hired out as a laborer** to help support his family. It was during one of these jobs, in **1825**, that he traveled to **Harmony, Pennsylvania**, and met a young woman who would change his life: **Emma Hale**. But we'll talk more about Emma in the next chapter.

Joseph and Emma were married in **January 1827**, and later that year, the waiting finally ended. On **September 22, 1827**, Joseph went to Hill Cumorah once more. This time, Moroni entrusted the gold plates to him, along with special instruments to help with the translation: the **Urim and Thummim**.

Those four years of waiting might seem like wasted time, but they weren't. Every month of labor, every visit with Moroni, every heartbreak and hardship shaped Joseph into the person he needed to be. God's timing isn't always our timing, but it's always right.
