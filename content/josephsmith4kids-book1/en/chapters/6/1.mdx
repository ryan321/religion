### Lesson 6.1 -- Meeting Emma

In **1825**, Joseph Smith traveled to **Harmony, Pennsylvania**, to work for a man named Josiah Stowell. It was there, while boarding at the home of **Isaac Hale**, that Joseph met Isaac's daughter, **Emma Hale**.

Emma was not an ordinary young woman for her time. She was **tall, dark-haired, and strikingly intelligent**. She had received more formal education than most women, and certainly more than Joseph had. She was well-read, thoughtful, and carried herself with a quiet dignity that caught Joseph's attention right away.

Joseph was smitten. And honestly, who could blame him? Emma was remarkable. But there was a problem, a big one. Her father, **Isaac Hale**, did not approve of Joseph Smith one bit.

Isaac saw Joseph as a **poor young man** with no real prospects. Joseph didn't own land. He didn't have a trade that promised a comfortable income. And then there were the **strange stories**: talk of visions, angels, and gold plates buried in a hill. To a practical man like Isaac Hale, this all sounded suspicious at best and crazy at worst. He wanted better for his daughter.

But Emma had a mind of her own. She saw something in Joseph that her father couldn't see, or wouldn't see. She recognized his honesty, his sincerity, and his goodness. Joseph wasn't putting on an act. He genuinely believed what he said, and Emma could tell.

Joseph and Emma began to court each other, which means they spent time together getting to know one another. In those days, courting was more formal than dating is today, but the feelings were the same: two young people falling in love and imagining a future together.

Isaac Hale made it clear that he would **not give his blessing** for a marriage. This put Joseph and Emma in a difficult position. They cared about Emma's family, but they also cared about each other. The tension between Joseph's calling and the disapproval of Emma's father would continue for years and cause real pain on all sides.

But for now, Joseph had met the woman who would stand beside him through everything that was coming. And there was a lot coming.
