### Lesson 6.3 -- Emma as Scribe and Partner

When Joseph Smith began translating the **Book of Mormon** from the gold plates, he needed someone to write down the words as he spoke them. The very first person to serve as his **scribe** was his wife, **Emma**.

Picture the scene: Joseph sat on one side of a **curtain** or partition, looking at the plates and using the instruments God had provided to translate the ancient writing. On the other side sat Emma with a pen, ink, and paper, carefully writing down every word Joseph dictated. Hour after hour, day after day, the words of an ancient record flowed from Joseph's mouth and onto the page through Emma's hand.

Emma was more than just a secretary, though. She was a **witness** to the translation process. She saw things that no one else saw. She watched Joseph produce page after page of complex history, doctrine, geography, and poetry -- all without notes, without a manuscript, and without ever going back to ask, "What did I say before?"

Years later, Emma gave powerful **testimony** about what she observed. She said that Joseph could not have written the Book of Mormon on his own because **he could barely compose a well-worded letter**. She had seen his writing. She knew his education level. And she knew that what came through him during the translation was far beyond his natural abilities.

"He couldn't have written it himself," she essentially said, "because he couldn't even write a good letter." Coming from his own wife -- the person who knew him best -- that testimony carries enormous weight.

Emma also described how Joseph would stop translating, attend to other duties, and then come back and **pick up exactly where he left off** without asking what the last words had been. She found this remarkable and saw it as evidence that the translation was genuinely inspired.

Emma's role as scribe didn't last the entire translation -- other scribes, especially **Oliver Cowdery**, took over most of the work later. But Emma was there at the beginning. She was the first to hear those sacred words spoken aloud. She wasn't just the prophet's wife. She was a **partner in the Restoration** and a firsthand witness to a miracle.
