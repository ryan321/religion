### Lesson 6.4 -- The Cost of Being a Prophet's Wife

Being married to a prophet sounds exciting, and in many ways it was. But for **Emma Smith**, it also meant a life of enormous **sacrifice**. If you only remember one thing about Emma, remember this: she gave up almost everything a woman of her time would have wanted -- comfort, stability, safety -- and she did it because she believed in Joseph and in the work God had called him to do.

One of the most heartbreaking parts of Emma's story is the loss of her **children**. Emma gave birth to eleven children during her lifetime, but tragically, **several died in infancy** or were stillborn. Her first three babies all died. Can you imagine the grief of losing not one, but multiple children? And she bore this sorrow while also dealing with everything else that came with being Joseph's wife.

Emma also lived with constant **danger**. Angry mobs came for Joseph again and again. There were nights when Joseph had to flee in the darkness, leaving Emma behind not knowing if she would ever see him again. There were times when mobs dragged Joseph from their home, tarred and feathered him, and left him for dead. Emma cleaned the tar from his skin and nursed him back to health.

Then there was the **poverty**. Joseph wasn't earning a regular income -- he was doing the Lord's work, which didn't come with a paycheck. Emma had to make do with very little. She cooked, cleaned, sewed, and managed a household on almost nothing, often while also hosting visitors, missionaries, and church members who needed a place to stay.

And the **moving** -- oh, the moving. Emma packed up her household and relocated again and again. From New York to Pennsylvania, back to New York, then to Ohio, to Missouri, and finally to Illinois. Each time, she left behind whatever small sense of home she had built and started over.

Not every moment between Joseph and Emma was easy. They had disagreements. They faced pressures that would have broken most marriages. But through it all, Emma's sacrifices were **immense and real**. She deserves to be remembered not just as the prophet's wife, but as a woman of extraordinary strength who paid a very personal price for the Restoration.
