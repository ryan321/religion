### Lesson 7.1 -- The 116 Pages

Do you remember how Joseph finally received the golden plates on September 22, 1827? It was the moment he had been waiting for since the angel Moroni first visited him four years earlier. Now the real work began -- translating the ancient record into English.

Joseph needed help. He couldn't write quickly enough while also translating, so he needed someone to write down the words as he spoke them. A farmer named **Martin Harris** became one of his first scribes. Martin was older than Joseph and well-known in the Palmyra area. He believed in Joseph's work and had even given him money to help with expenses.

Martin's wife, **Lucy Harris**, was not happy about any of this. She thought Joseph was tricking her husband, and she pressured Martin to bring home proof that the plates were real. Martin felt torn between his wife's demands and his work with Joseph.

Eventually, Martin begged Joseph to let him take the **116 pages** they had already translated so he could show them to his wife and family. Joseph asked the Lord, and the answer was **no**. Martin pleaded again. Again the Lord said **no**. But Martin kept asking, and finally, on the third request, the Lord **permitted** it -- with strict instructions about who could see the pages.

Martin took the manuscript home. And then something terrible happened -- the pages **disappeared**. They were lost, likely stolen, and never recovered.

When Joseph learned what had happened, he was absolutely **devastated**. He knew he had made a serious mistake by giving in to Martin's pressure instead of following the Lord's clear answer. The Lord gave Joseph a powerful rebuke recorded in **Doctrine and Covenants Section 3**: "How oft you have transgressed... and have gone on in the persuasions of men."

As a consequence, the **plates and the gift to translate were taken away** from Joseph for a time. It was one of the most painful periods of his young life. He had to wait and repent before he could begin again.

This experience taught Joseph a hard but important lesson. Even when people we care about pressure us to do something, we need to **follow God's answer** -- especially when He has already said no. Joseph was still learning and growing, and this mistake helped shape him into the leader he would need to become.

Have you ever been pressured to do something you knew wasn't right? Joseph learned that standing firm, even when it's hard, is always better than giving in.
