### Lesson 7.2 -- Oliver Cowdery

After the painful loss of the 116 pages, Joseph eventually received the plates back and was ready to begin translating again. But he still needed a scribe -- someone to write down the words as he dictated them. The Lord had the perfect person in mind.

In **April 1829**, a young schoolteacher named **Oliver Cowdery** arrived at Joseph's home. Oliver had been boarding with the Smith family while teaching school nearby, and he had heard about Joseph and the golden plates. He felt strongly that he should go meet Joseph and offer to help.

From the very first day they worked together, something special happened. Oliver later described those weeks of translation as **"days never to be forgotten."** He and Joseph worked hour after hour, day after day, with Oliver writing as Joseph translated. They became close friends and trusted companions in this sacred work.

As they translated, Joseph and Oliver came across passages about **baptism**. They wanted to know more, so they went into the woods near the Susquehanna River to pray. While they were praying, a heavenly messenger appeared to them -- **John the Baptist**, sent by the apostles Peter, James, and John. John the Baptist laid his hands on their heads and conferred the **Aaronic Priesthood** upon them, giving them authority to baptize.

Joseph and Oliver then **baptized each other** in the river. It was a joyful, sacred experience. Oliver described feeling the Holy Ghost fill him immediately after his baptism, and he began to understand scriptures in ways he never had before.

Oliver Cowdery would go on to play many important roles. He became one of the **Three Witnesses** who were shown the golden plates by an angel and who heard the voice of God declaring that the translation was correct. His testimony of that experience never wavered, even during years when he was not a member of the Church.

Oliver was more than just a scribe -- he was Joseph's companion during some of the most important events of the Restoration. The **restoration of the Aaronic Priesthood** was a turning point, because it meant that God was not just giving Joseph knowledge -- He was giving him **authority** to act in His name.

Think about what it would be like to have a friend who shared your most important experiences. That's what Oliver was to Joseph during those early, exciting days.
