### Lesson 7.4 -- Witnesses and Publication

Joseph Smith knew that people would have a hard time believing his story. A young man claiming to have translated an ancient record from golden plates? Most people would be skeptical. But God had a plan to provide additional witnesses so the world wouldn't have to rely on Joseph's word alone.

First came the **Three Witnesses**: **Oliver Cowdery**, **David Whitmer**, and **Martin Harris**. These three men were taken to a quiet spot in the woods near the Whitmer farm in Fayette, New York. There, an **angel appeared** and showed them the golden plates. They saw the engravings on the plates with their own eyes. Then they heard the **voice of God** declaring that the book had been translated correctly and commanding them to testify of what they had seen.

This was a powerful experience that none of the three ever denied, even during times when some of them left the Church and had disagreements with Joseph. Their written testimony is printed in every copy of the Book of Mormon to this day.

Next came the **Eight Witnesses**, a group that included members of the Smith and Whitmer families. Their experience was different from the Three Witnesses. No angel appeared to them. Instead, Joseph simply showed them the plates, and they **handled them physically**. They turned the pages, felt the weight of the metal, and examined the engravings. Their testimony confirmed that the plates were a real, tangible object, not something imagined or made up.

With the translation complete and the witnesses prepared, the next challenge was **publishing** the book. Joseph found a printer named **E.B. Grandin** in Palmyra, New York, who agreed to print the book, but it would cost **$3,000**, an enormous sum at the time. Martin Harris stepped forward and **mortgaged his farm** to guarantee payment. It was a tremendous sacrifice that showed how deeply Martin believed in the work.

The printing process took several months, and in **March 1830**, the first copies of the **Book of Mormon** were available for sale. The initial print run was **5,000 copies**. After years of preparation, persecution, lost pages, and hard work, the book that Joseph had been called to bring forth was finally in the hands of the world.

Joseph was just 24 years old. The farm boy from Vermont had done what God asked of him, and now anyone who wanted to could read the record for themselves.
