### Lesson 8.1 -- April 6, 1830

The Book of Mormon was published. Witnesses had testified. The priesthood had been restored. Now it was time for the next step: organizing a church.

On **April 6, 1830**, Joseph Smith and a small group of believers gathered in a log cabin in **Fayette, New York**, at the home of Peter Whitmer Sr. The day had come to formally organize the **Church of Christ** (later known as The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints).

How many people do you think were there? Hundreds? Thousands? No. There were only about **six official members** required by New York law to form a religious organization, along with a small number of family members and friends. It was a very **humble beginning** for what would grow into a worldwide church with millions of members.

At the meeting, Joseph was sustained as the **first elder** of the Church and recognized as a **prophet, seer, and revelator**. He was just **24 years old**. Think about that. Most 24-year-olds today are just finishing college or starting their first jobs. Joseph was being called to lead a church that God intended to spread across the entire earth.

The meeting was simple but filled with the Spirit. The sacrament was administered for the first time in the new church. Several people were baptized and confirmed. Joseph received a revelation (now **Doctrine and Covenants Section 21**) in which the Lord told the members to listen to Joseph's words "as if from mine own mouth, in all patience and faith."

It's important to understand how **small and fragile** this beginning was. Joseph had no money, no formal training, no powerful friends, and no organization behind him. He had already faced years of persecution, and much more was coming. But he trusted that God was directing the work, and he moved forward.

The Church grew quickly from that tiny gathering. Within weeks, missionary work began. Within months, there were branches in several states. Within a few years, thousands had joined.

Every massive movement starts somewhere small. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints started in a log cabin with a handful of believers and a young prophet who was willing to follow wherever God led him. Sometimes the greatest things begin with the most humble steps.
