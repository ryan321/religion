### Lesson 8.2 -- How Joseph Led

When you think of a prophet, what do you picture? Maybe someone standing at a pulpit giving a serious talk? Joseph Smith was definitely a prophet, but he led in ways that might surprise you.

Joseph was **not distant or formal**. He didn't act like he was better than everyone else or separate himself from ordinary people. Instead, he was right in the middle of everything. He **worked alongside the Saints**, helping them build their homes, clear their land, and settle into new communities. He rolled up his sleeves and got his hands dirty right next to everyone else.

One of the things people loved most about Joseph was that he genuinely **enjoyed being around people**. He played with children, wrestled with young men, and joked with friends. He was known for his **warmth and affection**. Many who met him said they felt like they had known him their whole lives, even after just a few minutes of conversation.

But Joseph's leadership wasn't just about being friendly. He led by **example**. When he asked people to sacrifice, he sacrificed first. When he asked people to work, he worked hardest. When he asked people to trust God, he showed his own trust through his actions, even when things were incredibly difficult.

Here's something really important about Joseph: he was **willing to be corrected**. Several revelations in the Doctrine and Covenants contain direct rebukes from the Lord to Joseph himself. He didn't hide these or pretend they didn't happen. He included them in the scriptures for everyone to read. He was honest about his mistakes.

Joseph never claimed to be **perfect**, and he didn't pretend to be. He once said, "I never told you I was perfect; but there is no error in the revelations which I have taught." He understood the difference between being a flawed human being and being called by God to do sacred work. Both things were true at the same time.

This kind of leadership (humble, honest, hardworking, and loving) drew people to Joseph. They followed him not because he demanded it, but because they could see that he truly cared about them and about doing what God asked. Even people who disagreed with his religious beliefs often admitted that Joseph Smith was one of the most **magnetic and genuine** people they had ever met.

What kind of leader do you want to be? Joseph showed that the best leaders serve others and aren't afraid to admit when they're wrong.
