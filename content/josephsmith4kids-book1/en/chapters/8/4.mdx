### Lesson 8.4 -- Daily Life

Sometimes we think of prophets as people who spend all their time praying, receiving visions, and doing dramatic spiritual things. But Joseph Smith's daily life looked a lot more ordinary than you might expect, and that's actually an important part of his story.

Joseph **chopped wood**. He **farmed**. He **ran a small store**. He did the same kind of hard physical work that most people on the American frontier did in the 1830s and 1840s. Being called as a prophet didn't mean he got to skip the everyday labor of keeping a household running and food on the table.

But Joseph also did things that went far beyond what most frontier farmers attempted. He **studied languages**, working hard to learn Hebrew with a Jewish professor named **Joshua Seixas**. He studied history, law, and other subjects. He served as **mayor** of Nauvoo, Illinois. He held the rank of **lieutenant general** in the Nauvoo Legion militia. He practiced law and was involved in civic government. Joseph was a man of enormous energy who threw himself into everything he did.

Through all of this, Joseph faced constant **financial problems**. He was rarely paid for his work as a church leader, and the expenses of leading a growing church often fell on his own shoulders. He borrowed money, struggled to pay debts, and worried about providing for his family. He was not a wealthy man.

Joseph also experienced deep **personal sorrow**. He and his wife Emma had eleven children, but six of them **died** in infancy or childhood. Think about how heartbreaking that would be. Joseph knew what it felt like to hold a sick child and pray desperately for healing that didn't come. He understood grief in a very personal way.

These experiences (the hard work, the financial stress, the illness, the loss of children) did not lift Joseph **above mortality**. He experienced it fully, with all its pain and difficulty. And that's part of what made him such a compassionate leader. When Saints came to him with their struggles, he understood. He had been through similar things himself.

Joseph's life reminds us that God doesn't call perfect people living easy lives. He calls real people living real lives, and He works through them, struggles, heartbreaks, and all.
