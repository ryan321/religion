### Lesson 9.2 -- The School of the Prophets

Joseph Smith only had a few years of formal schooling as a boy. He once described himself as an "obscure" and "unlearned" young man. But here's something important about Joseph -- he **never stopped learning**. He was determined to grow, and he wanted the Saints to grow with him.

In **1833**, Joseph established the **School of the Prophets** in Kirtland. This wasn't a regular school with desks and report cards. It was a gathering of Church leaders -- mostly men at that time -- who came together to study the gospel, learn from each other, and prepare themselves for the work God was calling them to do.

The school met in a small room above a store, and the sessions could be intense. They studied the **scriptures deeply**, discussed doctrine, practiced preaching, and learned about Church organization. But they didn't limit themselves to religious subjects. Joseph believed that **all knowledge** was valuable because it came from God.

One of the most remarkable things Joseph did was hire a **Jewish professor named Joshua Seixas** to teach **Hebrew** to the Church leaders. Joseph threw himself into the study with passion. He wanted to read the Old Testament in its original language so he could understand it better. He studied for hours every day and became one of the best students in the class.

Joseph also studied **history, geography, and law**. He read widely and encouraged others to do the same. He took seriously the revelation he had received: **"The glory of God is intelligence"** (Doctrine and Covenants 93:36). For Joseph, seeking knowledge wasn't separate from seeking God -- it was part of the same journey.

This is one of the most inspiring things about Joseph's character. He could have been content to remain an uneducated farm boy. After all, God had called him just as he was. But Joseph wasn't content with staying where he started. He was **constantly growing** -- intellectually, spiritually, and as a leader.

The School of the Prophets set a pattern that continues in the Church today. Latter-day Saints are encouraged to study, learn, and seek truth from every good source. Joseph showed by his own example that a love of learning and a love of God go hand in hand.

What are you learning right now that helps you grow? Joseph would say that every bit of knowledge you gain is worth the effort.
