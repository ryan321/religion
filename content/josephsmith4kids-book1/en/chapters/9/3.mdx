### Lesson 9.3 -- The Kirtland Temple

In 1833, the Lord gave Joseph Smith a commandment that must have seemed almost impossible: **build a temple**. The revelation is recorded in **Doctrine and Covenants Section 95**, and it came with a rebuke. The Lord chastised the Saints for not beginning the work sooner.

There was just one problem. The Saints in Kirtland were **desperately poor**. They were farmers, laborers, and tradespeople with very little money. Building a grand temple seemed completely beyond their means. But they trusted the Lord's command and decided to move forward in faith.

What followed was one of the most remarkable stories of **sacrifice** in Church history. The men quarried stone from a nearby hillside, cutting and hauling massive blocks by hand. They worked on the temple while also trying to farm and support their families. Many worked through the night by torchlight.

The women made extraordinary sacrifices too. They **crushed their fine china and glassware** so the fragments could be mixed into the temple's exterior plaster, making it sparkle in the sunlight. These were some of their most prized possessions, beautiful dishes that many had brought from their former homes, and they gave them up willingly for the house of the Lord.

The construction took about **three years** of backbreaking labor. Finally, on **March 27, 1836**, the **Kirtland Temple** was dedicated. Joseph offered the dedicatory prayer (now recorded as Doctrine and Covenants Section 109), and the **Spirit was poured out** in remarkable ways. Members saw visions, heard heavenly music, and felt the presence of angels. Some said it reminded them of the Day of Pentecost described in the New Testament.

One week later, on **April 3, 1836**, something even more extraordinary happened. While Joseph and Oliver Cowdery were praying in the temple, the **Savior Jesus Christ appeared** to them (Doctrine and Covenants Section 110). Then three more heavenly messengers came: **Moses**, **Elias**, and **Elijah**. Each one restored essential **priesthood keys**: authority needed for the gathering of Israel, the gospel of Abraham, and the sealing power that binds families for eternity.

The Kirtland Temple was the fulfillment of years of sacrifice and faith. It showed what ordinary people could accomplish when they trusted God and were willing to give everything they had.
