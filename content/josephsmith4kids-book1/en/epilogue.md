# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing The Life of Joseph Smith for Kids.

You've walked with Joseph from before he was born -- from the prophecies of ancient Joseph in Egypt and the visions of Isaiah -- all the way to Carthage Jail and beyond. You know his family now. You know his mother's faith and his father's kindness and his brother Alvin's encouragement and Hyrum's lifelong loyalty. You know about the leg surgery that showed his courage at seven years old, and the limited schooling that makes the Book of Mormon all the more remarkable.

You were there in the Sacred Grove when a fourteen-year-old farm boy saw the Father and the Son. You watched him grow through four years of preparation with Moroni. You met Emma and saw what it cost to be a prophet's wife. You sat with Oliver Cowdery while Joseph dictated the Book of Mormon in sixty-five days. You were at the Susquehanna River when John the Baptist restored the priesthood.

You followed Joseph through Kirtland's triumphs and failures, Missouri's suffering, and Nauvoo's beauty. You heard him teach about the plan of salvation, eternal families, and the nature of God. You stood outside Liberty Jail when the Lord told him, "Peace be unto thy soul." And you were there at Carthage when he and Hyrum fell.

Now you know Joseph -- not just as a name in a lesson manual, but as a person. A person who laughed and cried and wrestled and prayed and made mistakes and kept going. A person who saw God and never denied it, no matter what it cost him.

Joseph's work is not over. The Book of Mormon he translated is the same book you read today. The priesthood he restored is the same priesthood used in your ward. The temples he built are the pattern for the temples where your family makes covenants. His life made your life in the gospel possible.

He once said, "No man knows my history." That's less true now. You know it. And because you know it, the Restoration means more to you than it did before you started this book.

Thank you for learning with us.
