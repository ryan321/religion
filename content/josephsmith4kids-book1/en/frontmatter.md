## Introduction

This book is about a person. Not just a prophet -- a person.

Joseph Smith was a farm boy from Vermont who loved to wrestle, play games, and make people laugh. He had rough hands from hard work and rough spelling from little schooling. He was tall, strong, and full of energy. He cried easily when something touched his heart. He played with children. He helped build houses. He dealt with money problems, family grief, and people who betrayed him.

He was also the prophet through whom God restored the gospel of Jesus Christ to the earth. He saw the Father and the Son in a grove of trees. He translated the Book of Mormon. He received the priesthood from angels. He built temples. He received more published revelation than any other prophet in history. And he sealed his testimony with his life at the age of thirty-eight.

Both of those descriptions are true at the same time. Joseph was a normal person with an extraordinary calling. Understanding both sides -- the human and the divine -- is the point of this book.

You'll learn about his family, his childhood, his leg surgery, his marriage, his friendships, and his daily life. You'll also learn about the First Vision, the gold plates, the restoration of the priesthood, and the building of the Church. You'll see triumphs and failures, revelations and heartbreaks, courage and grief -- all in the life of one remarkable man.

The more you know Joseph, the more you'll understand what it cost him to do what God asked. And the more you'll appreciate what he gave so that you could have the gospel.

## How to use this book

This book is designed as a workbook for self-study or guided by a parent or teacher. No expertise required -- the lessons explain everything you need to know.

The book is designed to be used over the course of a semester. Each chapter corresponds to a week, with a total of 15 weeks. Each chapter has 4 lessons which are meant to be done once a day, leaving a 5th day for review and the chapter test.

For example, if you started on Monday each week, you could do:

Monday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.1

Tuesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.2

Wednesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.3

Thursday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.4

Friday  Chapter 1, Review and test

Each lesson has questions at the end, and each chapter has a test. Your AI tutor will help you check your answers and talk through anything you're unsure about.

After each lesson, there is a checkbox to mark when the lesson is completed to keep track of progress.

## Note to parents

You don't need to be a Church history expert to help your child with this book. The lessons are written directly to the child in plain language.

This book is a biography. It focuses on Joseph Smith as a person -- his family, his personality, his struggles, and his growth -- alongside the sacred events of the Restoration. It is honest about Joseph's humanity without being critical. He made mistakes, he learned from them, and he grew into the prophet God needed him to be.

Some lessons cover difficult topics (the leg surgery, persecution, Liberty Jail, the martyrdom) honestly but in an age-appropriate way. You may want to discuss those lessons together.

This book is written from a faithful Latter-day Saint perspective. Joseph Smith was a prophet of God. That is the starting point. The goal is to help your child know the prophet -- not just know about him.
