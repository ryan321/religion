### Lesson 1.1 -- Jerusalem in Trouble

Imagine living in a city where everyone around you is ignoring warnings that something terrible is coming. That was Jerusalem around 600 BC, and the story of the Book of Mormon starts right there.

At this time, the kingdom of **Judah** was one of the last pieces of the once-great nation of Israel. The northern kingdom had already been conquered and scattered over a hundred years earlier. Now Judah -- with its capital city of **Jerusalem** -- was in serious danger too.

The biggest threat was **Babylon**, the most powerful empire in the world at that time. Babylon had already defeated Egypt and was expanding in every direction. Judah was a small kingdom caught right in the middle, and its kings kept making bad decisions. Instead of submitting to Babylon, they rebelled. Instead of trusting God, they made secret deals with Egypt, hoping Egypt would protect them. Egypt couldn't even protect itself.

God sent **prophets** to warn the people. **Jeremiah** was one of the most important prophets preaching in Jerusalem at this time. He told the people plainly that if they didn't repent, the city would be destroyed and the people carried away as captives. But almost nobody listened. The people didn't want to hear bad news. They wanted prophets who told them everything was fine.

This is the world that **Lehi** and his family lived in. Jerusalem was full of wickedness. The people had broken their covenants with God. The leaders were corrupt. The prophets were being ignored or even threatened. And a massive enemy army was getting closer every year.

It's important to understand this background because it explains why God was about to do something drastic. He wasn't just going to warn the people one more time -- he was going to call a new prophet, deliver a message of destruction, and then command that prophet to take his family and leave before it was too late.

Within just a few years of Lehi's departure, Babylon's king **Nebuchadnezzar** would lay siege to Jerusalem, break through the walls, burn the temple, and carry the surviving people away as prisoners. Everything the prophets warned about came true.
