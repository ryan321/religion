### Lesson 1.2 -- Lehi Is Called

With Jerusalem heading toward disaster, God chose a new prophet to deliver his message. That prophet was **Lehi**.

Lehi lived in Jerusalem and was likely a wealthy man. He had land, possessions, and a comfortable life. His wife was **Sariah**, and together they had at least six children: **Laman**, **Lemuel**, **Sam**, **Nephi**, and later **Jacob** and **Joseph**, who were born in the wilderness. Lehi was a faithful man who cared deeply about his family and about doing what God asked.

One day, Lehi prayed to the Lord on behalf of his people. In response, God gave him a powerful **vision**. Lehi saw God sitting on his throne, surrounded by angels singing and praising him (1 Nephi 1:8-13). Then he saw what was coming for Jerusalem: **destruction**. The city would be conquered, and the people would be carried away or killed. It was a stunning and terrifying experience.

But the vision didn't just show destruction. It also showed God's mercy. Even though judgment was coming, God still loved his people enough to warn them and give them a chance to repent.

After the vision, Lehi was filled with the **Spirit of the Lord**. He was given a clear calling: go and **warn the people** of Jerusalem. Tell them what was coming. Tell them to repent and turn back to God, or face the consequences.

This was not an easy assignment. Lehi wasn't a professional preacher or a priest in the temple. He was being asked to stand up in front of his neighbors, his community, and his city's leaders, and tell them things they absolutely did not want to hear. He had to tell them that their beloved city, the city of David, the city where Solomon's temple stood, was going to be destroyed.

But Lehi obeyed. When God calls someone as a prophet, the message matters more than comfort or safety. Lehi accepted the calling and began to preach. What happened next would test him and his family in ways they never expected.
