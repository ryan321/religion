### Lesson 1.3 -- The People Reject Him

Lehi did exactly what God asked. He went out among the people of Jerusalem and began to **preach repentance**. He told them plainly that they needed to change their ways, turn back to God, and keep the commandments, or their city would be destroyed.

How do you think the people reacted? They did not listen. In fact, the reaction was fierce and violent.

The people **mocked** Lehi. They laughed at him and made fun of his message. Who was this man to tell them that Jerusalem, the holy city, the city God himself had chosen, would be destroyed? They didn't believe it was possible. They thought God would never let that happen, no matter what they did.

But it didn't stop at mocking. The people became **angry**. Lehi's message offended them. He was telling them that they were wicked, that their leaders were corrupt, and that judgment was coming. Nobody likes hearing that, especially when it's true. The anger grew so intense that some people actually **wanted to kill him** (1 Nephi 1:19-20).

Lehi was not the only prophet facing this kind of treatment. **Jeremiah** was preaching the exact same message at the exact same time, and the people treated him just as badly. Jeremiah was thrown into prison, lowered into a muddy pit, and constantly threatened. Other prophets in Jerusalem were even killed for speaking God's words. This was a pattern. The people of Jerusalem had a long history of rejecting and harming the prophets God sent to them.

Nephi, who recorded his father's story, pointed out something important: even though the people rejected Lehi, God's **mercy** was still at work (1 Nephi 1:20). God didn't abandon Lehi just because the people refused to listen. Instead, he continued to guide and protect Lehi and his family.

This part of the story teaches a hard lesson. Doing what God asks doesn't mean everyone will like you for it. Sometimes doing the right thing means standing alone while people mock you or get angry. Lehi kept going anyway. He trusted God more than he feared the crowd. And because he did, his family was eventually led to safety while Jerusalem fell exactly as the prophets said it would.
