### Lesson 1.4 -- The Command to Leave

After Lehi preached and the people rejected him, things became dangerous. People in Jerusalem were not just ignoring Lehi. They were actively trying to kill him. That's when the Lord stepped in with a new command.

God spoke to Lehi **in a dream** and told him to take his family and **leave Jerusalem** (1 Nephi 2:1-2). This wasn't a short trip or a temporary escape. The Lord was telling Lehi to abandon his home permanently and travel into the **wilderness**.

Think about what that meant. Lehi had to leave behind his **house**, his **land**, his **gold and silver**, and everything his family had built over the years. He couldn't load up a moving truck. He couldn't sell his property and take the money. The Lord told him to go, and he went, taking only his family, provisions, and tents. That was it.

Lehi obeyed without argument. He gathered **Sariah** and their sons (**Laman, Lemuel, Sam, and Nephi**) and they departed into the wilderness near the **Red Sea** (1 Nephi 2:5-6). They traveled for three days before making their first camp in a valley with a river running through it. Lehi named the valley **Lemuel** and the river **Laman**, hoping his two oldest sons would be as steady and faithful as those landmarks.

This departure was permanent. Lehi and his family would never see Jerusalem again. And it turned out to be exactly the right decision. Just a few years later, in 586 BC, **Nebuchadnezzar's Babylonian army** conquered Jerusalem. The city walls were broken down, Solomon's temple was burned, and thousands of people were killed or carried away as captives to Babylon. Everything the prophets had warned about came true.

Lehi's obedience saved his family. He gave up everything comfortable and familiar because God told him to. He didn't wait to see if the warnings were real. He didn't negotiate or delay. He trusted the Lord and acted immediately. That willingness to obey, even when it was painful and costly, is one of the most important lessons from the very beginning of the Book of Mormon.
