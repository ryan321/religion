### Lesson 10.1 -- Zeniff's Expedition

Up to this point, you've been following the story of the Nephites as they settled in Zarahemla. But now the Book of Mormon takes you back in time to tell a different story -- the story of a man named **Zeniff** who wanted to go back to the land the Nephites had originally left behind.

Remember, when **Mosiah I** led the Nephites out of the **land of Nephi**, they fled because the Lamanites were becoming too dangerous. They eventually found the people of Zarahemla and built a new life there. But not everyone was happy about leaving. Some Nephites wanted to reclaim their old homeland -- the land of their inheritance.

Zeniff was one of those people. The Book of Mormon says he was "**over-zealous**" to inherit the land of his fathers (Mosiah 9:3). That's an interesting word. It doesn't mean his desire was evil -- he genuinely loved that land. But his eagerness made him overlook some serious dangers.

Zeniff had actually been part of an earlier military expedition to spy on the Lamanites. During that mission, he saw good things among the Lamanites and didn't want to destroy them. This caused a conflict within the group that turned into a terrible fight among the Nephites themselves. Most of the expedition was killed, and the survivors returned to Zarahemla (Mosiah 9:1-2).

But Zeniff didn't give up. He gathered a group of people and led them back toward the land of Nephi. When they arrived, he went directly to the **Lamanite king** and asked permission to settle in the land. The king agreed -- but not because he was being generous. The king had a plan. He would let the Nephites move in, build up farms and cities, and grow comfortable. Then, once they had created something worth taking, the Lamanites would swoop in, conquer them, and take everything they'd built (Mosiah 9:10-12).

Zeniff didn't see the trap. He was so focused on what he wanted that he walked right into it. His story is a powerful reminder that good desires can still lead to trouble if you don't pay attention to warnings and think carefully about the consequences.

Sometimes wanting something badly can make you blind to the risks. Zeniff's expedition was about to put his entire people in a very dangerous position.
