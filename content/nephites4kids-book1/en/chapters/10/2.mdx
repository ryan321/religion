### Lesson 10.2 -- Life in the Land of Nephi

Once Zeniff and his people settled in the land of Nephi, they got to work. They planted crops, built buildings, and repaired the walls of the cities. For a while, things seemed to be going well. The people were industrious and faithful, and the Lord blessed their efforts.

But they were surrounded by **Lamanites** -- and the Lamanites hadn't forgotten the king's plan. It wasn't long before the attacks started. The Lamanites came against Zeniff's people, raiding their flocks and killing those who were out working in the fields (Mosiah 9:14).

Zeniff was a good leader in a crisis. He armed his people and led them into battle, calling on the Lord for help. The Nephites fought back and won, driving the Lamanites away. The Book of Mormon records that they fought "**in the strength of the Lord**" (Mosiah 9:17). As long as they trusted God, they were able to defend themselves even though they were outnumbered and surrounded.

This pattern repeated itself. The Lamanites would attack, the Nephites would pray and fight, and the Lord would deliver them. Zeniff's people enjoyed periods of peace between the wars, and they continued to build up their land and prosper. Zeniff led them for many years, and during his leadership the people generally stayed faithful.

But here's where the story takes a sharp turn. When Zeniff grew old, he had to pass the throne to someone else. The new king was his son, **Noah**. And Noah was nothing like his father.

This is one of the most important lessons in the entire Book of Mormon: **leadership matters**. One generation can be faithful, hardworking, and humble. The next generation, under bad leadership, can fall apart almost overnight. Zeniff trusted God. Noah trusted himself. Zeniff sacrificed for his people. Noah would take from them.

You'll see this pattern over and over in the scriptures. When leaders are righteous, the people prosper. When leaders are wicked, the people suffer. It's a reminder that who you follow -- and who you choose to become -- makes an enormous difference.

Zeniff's colony had survived wars and hardship through faith. But the biggest danger wasn't coming from the Lamanites. It was coming from their own throne.
