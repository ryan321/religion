### Lesson 10.3 -- King Noah

If you made a list of the worst kings in the Book of Mormon, **King Noah** would be right at the top. He took a faithful community that his father Zeniff had built and turned it into something completely different.

The first thing Noah did was place **heavy taxes** on the people -- one-fifth of everything they owned (Mosiah 11:3). That's a huge amount. And what did he do with all that money? He didn't use it to help the poor or defend the people. He spent it on himself. He built **elegant and spacious buildings**, decorated them with fine wood and precious metals, and built himself a lavish throne. He constructed a grand palace and even built a special tower near the temple so he could look out over the land (Mosiah 11:8-12).

Noah also loved **wine** and spent his days drinking with his court. He surrounded himself with people who told him what he wanted to hear, not what he needed to hear.

One of his worst decisions was getting rid of the **righteous priests** his father had appointed. He replaced them with proud, lazy men who were just like him. These new priests didn't teach the people God's word. They taught whatever made them popular and kept the money flowing. They lived off the taxes of the people while doing nothing to help them spiritually (Mosiah 11:5-7).

And the people followed along. That's the scary part. When a leader sets a bad example, it's easy for everyone else to go along with it. The people under King Noah became proud, boastful, and wicked. They stopped following God's commandments and started living for pleasure and wealth.

When **prophets** came to warn the people, Noah tried to have them killed. He didn't want anyone disrupting his comfortable life with talk of repentance and consequences. This is what happens when people love their sins more than the truth -- they try to silence anyone who tells them they're wrong.

King Noah's story is a warning. Wealth, power, and luxury aren't bad by themselves. But when they become the most important things in your life -- when you'll do anything to protect them, including ignoring God -- you're headed for disaster.
