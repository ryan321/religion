### Lesson 10.4 -- Abinadi Arrives

Into the middle of King Noah's corrupt kingdom, God sent a **prophet**. His name was **Abinadi**, and his message was not one that Noah or his people wanted to hear.

Abinadi came among the people and began to prophesy. He told them plainly that if they did not **repent**, terrible things would come upon them. They would be brought into **bondage** -- enslaved by their enemies. They would be afflicted and driven and scattered. And all of it would happen because they had turned away from God and followed a wicked king (Mosiah 11:20-21).

You can imagine how popular that message was. King Noah was furious. He demanded to know who this man was and why he was stirring up trouble among the people. He ordered his men to **arrest Abinadi** and bring him in so he could be put to death. Noah didn't want to examine whether the prophet's words were true. He just wanted the prophet gone (Mosiah 11:26-28).

But Abinadi escaped. For **two years**, he stayed away. We don't know exactly where he went or what he did during that time. But when two years had passed, he came back -- this time in **disguise** so no one would recognize him right away. He began prophesying again, and his message was even more urgent than before (Mosiah 12:1).

This time, the people caught him. They bound him and brought him before King Noah and his priests. He was a prisoner, standing alone against the most powerful men in the land. He had no army, no political power, and no one to defend him. All he had was the word of God.

What happened next is one of the most **dramatic scenes** in the entire Book of Mormon. Abinadi's trial before King Noah and his priests would become a turning point -- not because the king would listen, but because one person in the room would. One listener in a crowd of enemies would change the course of history for an entire people.

You're about to see what it looks like when one person stands for truth no matter the cost.
