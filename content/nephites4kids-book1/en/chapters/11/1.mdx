### Lesson 11.1 -- Abinadi Before the King

Picture this scene: a lone prophet, bound and under guard, standing before the most powerful men in the land. King Noah sat on his throne. His wicked priests sat around him. And **Abinadi** stood in front of them all -- on trial for his life (Mosiah 12-16).

The priests thought they were clever. They started asking Abinadi questions about the scriptures, quoting passages from **Isaiah** about how beautiful it is when messengers bring good news of peace and salvation. They were trying to trap him. If Abinadi said the words were true, they could argue that their own teachings of comfort and prosperity were exactly what Isaiah meant. If he said they were wrong, they could accuse him of rejecting scripture.

But Abinadi saw right through it. He turned the question back on them with one of the sharpest responses in the Book of Mormon: "**Are you priests, and pretend to teach this people, and to understand the spirit of prophesying, and yet desire to know of me what these things mean?**" (Mosiah 12:25). In other words -- you claim to be religious teachers, and you don't even understand your own scriptures?

It was a bold challenge. These men had power over his life, and he called them out directly. He told them they had not applied their hearts to understanding. They had not been teaching the people what they should have been teaching. They had twisted the word of God to serve their own comfort.

Then Abinadi began to teach. And what he taught was powerful. He didn't give a short answer -- he delivered one of the greatest sermons recorded in the Book of Mormon. He would walk these priests through the **Ten Commandments**, the **Law of Moses**, and the coming of **Jesus Christ**. Every word pointed to the truth they had been ignoring.

Abinadi knew exactly what was going to happen to him. He wasn't trying to save himself. He was trying to deliver the message God had given him -- clearly and completely -- no matter the cost.
