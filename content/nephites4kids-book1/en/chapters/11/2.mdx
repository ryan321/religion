### Lesson 11.2 -- The Ten Commandments and the Law

Standing before King Noah and his priests, Abinadi did something remarkable. He asked them a simple question: **Do you keep the commandments of God?** Then he quoted the **Ten Commandments** one by one (Mosiah 13).

You can imagine how uncomfortable that must have been. "Thou shalt not steal." "Thou shalt not commit adultery." "Thou shalt have no other gods before me." Every commandment was a mirror held up to their lives, and none of them could honestly say they were keeping them. These priests had stolen from the people through unjust taxes. They had lived in wickedness. They had put wealth and pleasure above God.

When they tried to grab Abinadi, the power of God came upon him and his face **shone with a brightness** that frightened them. He warned them not to touch him until he had finished delivering his message, or God would strike them down (Mosiah 13:3-5). They backed off, and Abinadi continued.

Then he taught them something deeper. He explained that the **Law of Moses**, all the rules about sacrifices, burnt offerings, and rituals that the Israelites had been following for centuries, was not the final destination. It was a **teacher**. Every sacrifice of a lamb on the altar was a **type and shadow** of something greater that was coming. Every part of the law pointed forward to one person: **Jesus Christ** (Mosiah 13:29-33).

This was what the priests should have been teaching the people all along. The law wasn't just a set of rules to follow mechanically. It was designed to help people understand that a Savior was coming who would be the ultimate sacrifice. Every time an Israelite offered a lamb, they were supposed to think about the Lamb of God who would come to save them.

But Noah's priests didn't teach any of that. They taught whatever was easy, whatever was popular, whatever kept them in power. Abinadi was showing them, and showing us, that if you don't understand the purpose behind God's commandments, you've missed the whole point.
