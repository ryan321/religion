### Lesson 11.3 -- Abinadi Testifies of Christ

After teaching about the Law of Moses, Abinadi reached the heart of his message. He bore one of the most powerful testimonies of **Jesus Christ** found anywhere in the Book of Mormon (Mosiah 15).

He quoted the prophet **Isaiah**, who had described a "suffering servant" -- someone who would be despised and rejected, who would carry the sorrows and griefs of the whole world. Abinadi explained that this was talking about Christ. The Savior would come to earth, live among the people, and be rejected by the very people He came to save.

Then Abinadi spoke words that would echo across the centuries: "**He shall be led, crucified, and slain, the flesh becoming subject even unto death, the will of the Son being swallowed up in the will of the Father**" (Mosiah 15:7). Think about what he was saying. Hundreds of years before Jesus was born, Abinadi was describing exactly what would happen -- the crucifixion, the suffering, the perfect obedience of the Son to the Father.

Abinadi taught that Christ's sacrifice would make **redemption** possible. Because of what Christ would do, people could be forgiven of their sins and brought back into God's presence. Without Christ, the law alone couldn't save anyone. The law could teach and prepare, but **salvation comes through Christ alone**.

He also taught about the **resurrection** -- that Christ would break the bands of death so that everyone who ever lived would rise again. This was a gift given to all people, not just the righteous. Death would not have the final word.

King Noah had heard enough. He commanded Abinadi to **take back his words** -- to deny everything he had just said. If Abinadi would recant, perhaps his life would be spared. But Abinadi refused. He had not come to say what was convenient. He had come to say what was true. He would not take back a single word, even if it meant his death.

That kind of courage doesn't come from nowhere. It comes from knowing -- really knowing -- that what you're teaching is true.
