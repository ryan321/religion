### Lesson 11.4 -- Death by Fire

Abinadi would not take back his words. He had delivered the message God sent him to deliver, and he would stand by every word of it. King Noah's response was to order his death.

Before they carried out the sentence, Abinadi gave one final warning. He told Noah and his priests that what they were about to do to him would come back upon them. "**What you shall do unto me shall be a type of things which are to come**," he said (Mosiah 17:15). In other words, the way they treated him would foreshadow what would happen to them. They would be afflicted with diseases, they would be scattered and slain, and they would be hunted from every direction.

Then Abinadi prophesied something specific about King Noah: he would suffer **death by fire**, just as Abinadi was about to (Mosiah 17:18). This prophecy was fulfilled almost exactly. Later, when Noah's own people turned against him, he was burned to death while trying to flee (Mosiah 19:20). The very fate Noah imposed on the prophet came upon himself.

Abinadi was put to death by **fire**. He sealed his testimony with his life. The Book of Mormon records that he "suffered death by fire; yea, having been put to death because he would not deny the commandments of God" (Mosiah 17:20). He died as a **martyr** -- someone who gives their life rather than deny the truth.

But here is the part of the story that changes everything. While Abinadi was teaching and testifying, one man in the room was listening -- really listening. His name was **Alma**. He was one of Noah's priests, one of those wicked men who had been living off the people's taxes and ignoring God's word. But something about Abinadi's testimony reached him.

Alma believed Abinadi's words. He stood up and pleaded with King Noah to let the prophet go. But Noah expelled Alma and sent servants to kill him. Alma fled for his life (Mosiah 17:2-4).

One prophet. One testimony. One person who listened. It seemed like such a small thing. But that one listener was about to change everything.
