### Lesson 12.1 -- Alma Believes

Think about who Alma was before he heard Abinadi. He was one of King Noah's **wicked priests**. He had lived off the heavy taxes squeezed from the people. He had been part of a corrupt system that ignored God and silenced prophets. By every measure, Alma was one of the bad guys.

But something happened when Abinadi spoke. The prophet's words cut through all the pride, all the comfort, all the excuses. Alma **believed** (Mosiah 17:2). That might sound simple, but it was one of the most important moments in the Book of Mormon. One man in a room full of enemies chose to listen, and it changed the course of history for an entire people.

When Alma stood up and pleaded with King Noah to let Abinadi go, it cost him everything. Noah was furious. He **expelled** Alma and sent servants after him to kill him. Alma had to run for his life, leaving behind his position, his wealth, and his status. In a single moment, he went from being a priest in the king's court to a fugitive in the wilderness (Mosiah 17:2-4).

But Alma did something else that was critical. While he was in hiding, he **wrote down** everything Abinadi had taught. Every word about the Ten Commandments, the Law of Moses pointing to Christ, and the powerful testimony of the Savior -- Alma recorded it all. If he hadn't, those teachings might have been lost forever. Instead, they were preserved and would be passed on to generations.

Alma's story teaches you something powerful about **change**. It doesn't matter who you were yesterday. It matters what you choose today. Alma had been wicked, but when the truth reached him, he accepted it. He didn't make excuses. He didn't wait for a more convenient time. He acted immediately, even though it meant giving up everything he had.

One testimony from Abinadi. One listener who took it seriously. That was enough to start something extraordinary.
