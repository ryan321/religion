### Lesson 12.2 -- Teaching in Secret

After fleeing from King Noah's soldiers, Alma went into hiding. But he didn't hide just to save himself. He had work to do. He began **teaching** the words of Abinadi to anyone who would listen (Mosiah 18:1).

This wasn't easy. King Noah was watching for exactly this kind of thing. He had **spies** among the people, looking for anyone who might be spreading ideas that went against his authority. If Alma was caught, he would be killed, just like Abinadi. So Alma had to be careful. He taught **privately**, meeting with small groups of people in secret locations away from the cities.

Think about how different Alma was now compared to who he had been. As one of Noah's priests, he had lived in luxury, teaching whatever the king wanted him to teach. Now he was a **fugitive**, hiding in the wilderness, teaching the truth at the risk of his life. He had gone from a corrupt priest to a repentant man carrying the words of a martyr.

And what did he teach? He taught the things Abinadi had taught him. He taught about **repentance**, turning away from sin and coming back to God. He taught about **faith in Jesus Christ**, trusting that the Savior would come and make it possible for people to be forgiven. And he taught about the **covenant of baptism**, making a promise to God to follow Him and serve His children (Mosiah 18:1-7).

People started to listen. Quietly, in small groups, they came to hear Alma teach. The message spread from person to person, household to household. Something was growing in the shadows of Noah's kingdom: a community of believers who wanted something better than what their wicked king was offering.

It all started with one man who heard the truth, believed it, and then risked everything to share it with others. That's how the gospel has always spread. Not usually through grand public events, but through one person telling another, one heart opening at a time.
