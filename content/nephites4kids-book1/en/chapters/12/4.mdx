### Lesson 12.4 -- Fleeing from King Noah

It was only a matter of time before King Noah found out what Alma was doing. When the king discovered that Alma had gathered a community of believers and was baptizing people at the Waters of Mormon, he was furious. He sent his **army** to find them and destroy them (Mosiah 18:33).

But the Lord warned Alma, and he and his people moved fast. About **450 people**, men, women, and children, gathered their flocks and their belongings and fled into the wilderness before Noah's soldiers could reach them (Mosiah 18:34-35). They traveled for days, eventually settling in a beautiful valley they called the land of **Helam**.

In Helam, the people of Alma built a thriving community. They planted crops, constructed buildings, and lived according to the covenants they had made at the Waters of Mormon. Alma was their leader, but he didn't rule like King Noah had. He served the people and taught them to serve each other.

But their peace didn't last forever. A Lamanite army discovered Helam and brought Alma's people into **bondage** (Mosiah 23:25-29). To make things worse, the Lamanites put one of Noah's former wicked priests, a man named **Amulon**, in charge of them. Amulon was cruel. He loaded the people with heavy burdens and threatened to kill anyone caught praying (Mosiah 24:8-11).

The people couldn't pray out loud, so they **prayed in their hearts**. And the Lord heard them. He told them to be comforted and promised to deliver them. Then He did something remarkable. He "**did strengthen them that they could bear up their burdens with ease**" (Mosiah 24:15). The loads didn't disappear, but God gave them the strength to carry them.

Eventually, the Lord put the Lamanite guards into a deep sleep, and Alma's people escaped. They traveled through the wilderness and were finally led to the land of **Zarahemla**, where they were welcomed by King Mosiah and the Nephites (Mosiah 24:20-25).

Alma's journey, from wicked priest, to believer, to fugitive, to leader, to bondage, to freedom, shows that following God doesn't mean life will be easy. But it does mean you will never be alone, and eventually, the Lord will deliver you.
