### Lesson 13.1 -- King Noah Falls

Do you remember wicked King Noah? He was the king who had the prophet Abinadi burned to death for preaching repentance. But Abinadi made a prophecy before he died. He said that what happened to him would happen to Noah. Fire for fire. And that prophecy was about to come true.

After **Alma** fled into the wilderness with his followers, things in the land of Nephi fell apart fast. The **Lamanites** saw their chance and invaded. Suddenly, Noah's kingdom, the one he had spent so much time decorating with fancy buildings and filling with lazy priests, was under attack.

When the Lamanite army came, Noah panicked. Instead of standing to defend his people, he ordered all the men to abandon their wives and children and run. Can you imagine? A king telling fathers to leave their families behind to save themselves. Some men actually did it and followed Noah into the wilderness. But many others refused. They would rather stay and die with their families than abandon them (Mosiah 19:11-12).

The men who stayed behind had an idea. They sent their daughters out to plead with the Lamanites, and the Lamanites had compassion and spared them. But now they were captives, forced to pay **tribute** to the Lamanite king.

Meanwhile, the men who had fled with Noah became furious with him. They realized what a coward and a fool he was. They caught Noah and **burned him to death**, exactly the same way he had killed Abinadi (Mosiah 19:20). Abinadi's prophecy was fulfilled perfectly. It's a powerful reminder that God's words always come true, even when wicked people think they've gotten away with something.

Noah's priests escaped into the wilderness, afraid to return. We'll hear more about the trouble they cause later. But for now, the people needed a new king. Noah's son **Limhi** stepped up. Limhi was not like his father. He was a decent man who genuinely cared about his people. But he inherited a terrible situation. His people were now under Lamanite control, paying a heavy price for his father's wickedness.

Sometimes we have to deal with problems that other people created. Limhi didn't choose this mess, but he had to lead his people through it.
