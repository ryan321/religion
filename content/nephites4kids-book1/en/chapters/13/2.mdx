### Lesson 13.2 -- Limhi's People in Bondage

Have you ever had to deal with consequences from something that wasn't really your fault? Maybe a sibling broke something and the whole family lost a privilege. That's a little bit like what happened to Limhi's people. They were paying the price for King Noah's terrible choices.

After the Lamanites took control, Limhi's people were forced to pay **half of everything** they had as tribute. Half their grain, half their gold and silver, half their flocks. Half of everything went to the Lamanite king (Mosiah 19:22). Imagine working hard all day and then giving away half of your food. It was a heavy, grinding burden.

**Limhi** was a good man, and he tried to make the best of a terrible situation. But his people grew desperate. Three separate times, they gathered their armies and tried to fight their way free from the Lamanites. And three times, they were badly defeated (Mosiah 21:6-12). Each battle brought more suffering, more widows, and more fatherless children. Trying to fix the problem with swords just made things worse.

Here's something really important to notice. The scriptures tell us that Limhi's people cried out to the Lord for help, but the Lord was **slow to hear their cry** (Mosiah 21:15). Why? Because they had been slow to turn to Him. For years under King Noah, the people had ignored prophets, supported wickedness, and turned away from God. Now that they were in trouble, they called out for help, but the Lord let them struggle for a while before answering.

Does that mean God didn't love them? Not at all. But sometimes we need to truly feel the weight of our choices before we're ready to change. The Lord did eventually begin to soften the hearts of the Lamanites so the burdens got a little lighter (Mosiah 21:15). He didn't abandon them. He just waited until they were truly humble.

Limhi's people also discovered something interesting during this time. A group of explorers they sent out to find Zarahemla got lost and instead found the ruins of a destroyed civilization. They brought back **twenty-four gold plates** covered in engravings. They didn't know it yet, but these were the records of the **Jaredites**, a people who had been destroyed long before. Those plates would later be translated by a seer and would reveal an incredible history.

Even in their hardest times, God was preparing important things for the future.
