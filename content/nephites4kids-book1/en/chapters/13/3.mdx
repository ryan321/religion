### Lesson 13.3 -- Ammon Finds Them

Have you ever been lost and then finally found by someone looking for you? The relief you feel in that moment is hard to describe. That's something like what happened when a man named Ammon arrived in the land of Nephi.

Back in **Zarahemla**, King **Mosiah** was worried. A large group of Nephites had left years earlier to go back to the land of Nephi, and nobody had heard from them since. Were they alive? Were they in trouble? Mosiah wanted answers. So he chose sixteen strong men, led by a man named **Ammon**, and sent them to find out what had happened (Mosiah 7:1-2).

The journey was not easy. Ammon and his group wandered in the wilderness for **forty days**, getting lost and struggling to find their way. But they kept going, and eventually they reached the land of Nephi -- where they were immediately captured and thrown into prison by the guards. Limhi's people had learned to be suspicious of strangers.

After two days, Ammon was brought before **King Limhi**. When Limhi realized these men were from Zarahemla -- fellow Nephites who had come looking for them -- he was overjoyed. The scriptures say he was **"exceedingly glad"** (Mosiah 7:14). Finally, there was hope. They weren't forgotten. Their brothers in Zarahemla cared about them.

Ammon and Limhi had a lot to share with each other. Ammon told Limhi about **King Benjamin's** great sermon and how the people of Zarahemla were living in peace and righteousness. Can you imagine how that must have sounded to people who had been suffering under Lamanite bondage?

Limhi told Ammon everything that had happened -- the story of wicked King Noah, the courage of the prophet **Abinadi**, how **Alma** had believed Abinadi and fled with his followers, and how Noah had been killed. He also showed Ammon the **twenty-four gold plates** that his explorers had found. Limhi wanted desperately to know what was written on them. Ammon told him that King Mosiah had the gift of a **seer** and could translate them.

This meeting was a turning point. Limhi's people had been stuck, defeated, and hopeless. Now they had a connection to Zarahemla, a friend with knowledge of the wilderness, and for the first time in years -- a real plan to escape.

Sometimes help comes after a long wait, but it comes right when you need it most.
