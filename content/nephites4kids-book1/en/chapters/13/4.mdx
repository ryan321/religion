### Lesson 13.4 -- Escape to Zarahemla

Have you ever had to sneak around really quietly so you wouldn't get caught? Now imagine doing that with your entire community -- hundreds of people, plus all their animals and belongings. That's exactly what Limhi's people had to pull off.

Limhi's people had tried fighting the Lamanites three times and lost every time. Force wasn't going to work. They needed a different plan. When **Ammon** arrived from Zarahemla, he brought not just hope but also knowledge of the wilderness routes. Together, Ammon and Limhi came up with a clever escape plan.

Here's what they did. They gathered extra wine and sent it as a tribute gift to the **Lamanite guards** who watched over them. The guards drank the wine and got drunk. While the guards were passed out, Limhi's people gathered their families, their flocks, their herds, and everything they could carry, and they **slipped away into the wilderness under cover of night** (Mosiah 22:6-11).

It was a bold move. If the guards had woken up, the consequences would have been terrible. But God was with them. They traveled through the wilderness with Ammon guiding them, and they made it safely to **Zarahemla**.

King **Mosiah** welcomed them with open arms. Imagine the reunion -- people who had been separated for years, some who probably thought they'd never see each other again. Limhi's people were finally free from bondage and back among their fellow Nephites.

But that wasn't the only reunion. Around the same time, **Alma** and his people also arrived in Zarahemla. Remember, Alma had fled from King Noah after believing Abinadi's words. His group had their own incredible story of bondage and deliverance, which we learned about in an earlier chapter. Now both groups -- Limhi's people and Alma's people -- were gathered together in Zarahemla.

Think about what this means. God had been working with multiple groups of people at the same time, guiding each one through their own challenges, and bringing them all together in the end. Limhi's people learned that trying to fight their way out didn't work, but trusting in the Lord's timing and using wisdom did.

Mosiah received the **twenty-four gold plates** that Limhi's explorers had found and later translated them using the interpreters God had given him. Those plates told the story of the **Jaredites** -- an even older civilization that had been completely destroyed. It was a powerful warning about what happens when an entire nation turns away from God.

The Nephites were now all together in one place, stronger and wiser. But new challenges were coming.
