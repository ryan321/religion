### Lesson 14.1 -- The Sons Who Went Wrong

Have you ever seen someone who knows better make really bad choices anyway? That's one of the saddest parts of the Book of Mormon story, and it happened right in the families of the most faithful leaders.

When all the Nephites were gathered in Zarahemla, **Alma the Elder** became the **high priest** over the whole Church. Remember, this was the same Alma who had believed Abinadi, risked his life, and led hundreds of people to Christ. He had given up everything to follow God. He knew what it meant to sacrifice for the truth.

But Alma had a son, also named **Alma**, known as **Alma the Younger**, and this young man wanted nothing to do with his father's faith. Even worse, he actively worked against it. Alma the Younger became one of the most dangerous enemies of the Church from the inside.

He wasn't alone, either. The four **sons of King Mosiah** (Aaron, Ammon, Omner, and Himni) joined him. Together, these five young men went around secretly trying to destroy the Church of God (Mosiah 27:8-10). They were smooth talkers who used flattering words to draw people away from the faith. Because they were the sons of the king and the high priest, people listened to them. They caused serious damage.

Stop and think about how painful this must have been for Alma the Elder. This was a man who had personally witnessed Abinadi's courage. He had built the Church from scratch in the wilderness. He had baptized people at the Waters of Mormon. And now his own son, the boy he had raised and loved, was tearing it all apart.

And King Mosiah felt the same heartbreak. His four sons were supposed to be future leaders of the Nephites. Instead, they were leading people away from God.

The scriptures tell us that Alma the Younger and the sons of Mosiah caused many people to commit serious sins. They were "a great hinderment to the prosperity of the church of God" (Mosiah 27:9). Whole groups of people stopped believing because of their influence.

But here's what makes this story incredible. It doesn't end here. God wasn't done with these young men. Sometimes the people who fall the hardest become the ones who rise the highest. And what was about to happen would change everything.
