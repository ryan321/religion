### Lesson 14.2 -- The Angel Appears

What would it take to stop you in your tracks, completely and totally? For Alma the Younger and the sons of Mosiah, it took something extraordinary. It took an angel.

One day, while Alma the Younger and his friends were out doing their usual work of fighting against the Church, something happened that none of them could have expected. An **angel of the Lord** appeared to them. He didn't come quietly, either. He descended in a cloud, and when he spoke, his voice was like **thunder**. The ground itself shook beneath their feet (Mosiah 27:11-18).

The angel said something that Alma would never forget: **"Alma, arise and stand forth, for why persecutest thou the church of God?"** The angel told him that God had heard the prayers of His people, and especially the prayers of Alma's own father. Alma the Elder had been praying desperately for his son, and God answered those prayers in the most dramatic way possible.

The power of the angel's visit was overwhelming. Alma the Younger **fell to the earth**. He was so struck by the experience that he couldn't move his arms or legs. He couldn't speak a word. His companions, terrified, picked him up and carried him to his father. For **three days and three nights**, Alma lay there, completely helpless.

But what was happening on the outside wasn't nearly as intense as what was happening on the inside. Later, Alma described this experience in vivid detail. He said he was **"racked with eternal torment"** (Mosiah 27:29). He felt the full weight of every sin he had ever committed and every person he had led astray. He said he felt the **pains of the damned**, a suffering so terrible that he wished he could stop existing entirely.

He remembered every person he had pulled away from God. Every lie he had told. Every testimony he had destroyed. And he couldn't escape any of it. For three agonizing days, he experienced what it would feel like to stand before God with no excuse and no way out.

This might sound harsh. But sometimes the Lord lets us feel the real consequences of our choices so that we're finally willing to change completely. Alma wasn't being punished forever. He was being given a chance to truly understand what he had done so he could truly repent.

And that's exactly what happened next.
