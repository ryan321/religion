### Lesson 14.3 -- Born Again

Have you ever been in so much pain -- maybe a bad stomach ache or a broken bone -- that you would have done anything to make it stop? Now imagine that kind of pain, but in your heart and your spirit, lasting for three whole days. That's where Alma the Younger was.

For three days and nights, Alma lay helpless, tormented by the memory of every wrong thing he had ever done. He felt what it would be like to face God as an enemy. He later said he was "racked with all my sins" and that the very thought of coming into God's presence "did rack my soul with inexpressible horror" (Alma 36:14).

But then, in the very deepest moment of his agony, something broke through. Alma remembered his father's teachings about Jesus Christ. He remembered hearing about a Savior who would come to **atone** for the sins of the world. And in that moment of absolute desperation, Alma did the one thing that changes everything -- he called out to Christ.

**"O Jesus, thou Son of God, have mercy on me, who am in the gall of bitterness"** (Alma 36:18).

That was it. One sincere, desperate prayer. And what happened next was one of the most powerful moments in all of scripture.

The pain vanished. In its place came **joy** so overwhelming that Alma said it was **"as exceeding as was my pain"** (Alma 36:20). The same intensity of suffering was now matched by an equal intensity of happiness and peace. He said he could see God sitting on His throne, surrounded by angels, and his soul longed to be there.

Alma was **born again**. The old Alma -- the one who had fought against God -- was gone. A completely new person took his place. This wasn't a slow, gradual change. It was dramatic, sudden, and complete. The worst enemy of the Church became one of its greatest champions in a single moment.

When Alma finally stood up after those three days, the first thing he said was: **"I have repented of my sins, and have been redeemed of the Lord; behold I am born of the Spirit"** (Mosiah 27:24). The people watching couldn't believe it. The young man who had been destroying the Church was now testifying of Jesus Christ.

This story teaches us something incredibly important -- no one is too far gone for the Savior to reach. No matter what you've done, if you sincerely turn to Christ and ask for mercy, He can change your heart completely.
