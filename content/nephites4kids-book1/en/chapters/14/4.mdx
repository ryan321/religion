### Lesson 14.4 -- A New Life

When someone truly changes, you can see it in what they do with the rest of their life. And nobody showed a bigger change than Alma the Younger.

After his miraculous conversion, Alma didn't just go back to living a normal life. He dedicated everything he had to making up for the damage he had caused. He and the **sons of Mosiah** traveled throughout the land, confessing their sins and telling everyone what had happened to them. They went to the very same people they had led astray and tried to bring them back (Mosiah 27:32-35).

Alma became a powerful preacher. He eventually became the **first chief judge** of the Nephites and also served as the **high priest** over the Church. He held the two most important positions in the entire nation. But as time went on, Alma realized something -- the problems facing the Church couldn't be solved by laws and government. They needed something deeper.

So Alma did something remarkable. He **gave up the judgment seat** -- one of the most powerful positions in the land -- so he could spend all his time preaching the gospel (Alma 4:18-19). He walked away from political power because he believed that teaching people the word of God was more important.

Why? Because Alma had learned from personal experience that **"the preaching of the word had a great tendency to lead the people to do that which was just -- yea, it had had more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword, or anything else"** (Alma 31:5). Swords could force people to obey, but the word of God could change their hearts. And Alma knew all about changed hearts.

For the rest of his life, Alma traveled to city after city, preaching repentance, testifying of Christ, and calling people to come back to God. He faced rejection, anger, imprisonment, and danger. But he never stopped. The man who had once tried to destroy the Church became the man who saved it.

Alma's story is one of the greatest messages in the Book of Mormon -- it doesn't matter where you've been or what you've done. What matters is what you choose to do from this point forward. Through the power of Jesus Christ, anyone can become a completely new person and do incredible good in the world.

That's the power of the Atonement. That's what it means to be truly converted.
