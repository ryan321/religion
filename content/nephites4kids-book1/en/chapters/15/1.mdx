### Lesson 15.1 -- The Sons of Mosiah Choose Missions

After being visited by an angel and going through a complete change of heart, the **sons of Mosiah** -- Ammon, Aaron, Omner, and Himni -- had a serious question to answer: What now? They had spent years tearing down the Church. How could they possibly make up for that?

Their answer was bold, unexpected, and maybe a little bit crazy. They wanted to go preach the gospel to the **Lamanites** (Mosiah 28:1-7).

Think about that for a moment. The Lamanites were the Nephites' greatest enemies. They had attacked, enslaved, and killed Nephites for hundreds of years. Going to preach to them wasn't like going to a friendly neighbor's house -- it was walking straight into enemy territory. Most Nephites thought the idea was insane.

But the sons of Mosiah couldn't shake the feeling. The scriptures say they **"could not bear that any human soul should perish"** (Mosiah 28:3). They had felt what it was like to be lost in sin and then rescued by Christ. That experience gave them compassion for everyone -- even the Lamanites. They wanted others to feel the same joy they had found.

Their father, King **Mosiah**, was terrified. These were his sons. Sending them into Lamanite territory could mean sending them to their deaths. But Mosiah prayed about it, and the **Lord promised** that He would deliver them out of the hands of the Lamanites (Mosiah 28:7). With that promise, Mosiah let them go.

So the sons of Mosiah left behind their royal privileges -- their right to the throne, their comfortable lives, everything -- and headed into the wilderness toward the land of Nephi. They would spend **fourteen years** among the Lamanites, preaching, suffering, and working miracles.

Fourteen years. That's not a weekend service project. That's more than your entire life if you're seven years old. These young men gave up the best years of their lives to serve people who had been their enemies.

Their decision also created a big problem back home. Since none of Mosiah's sons wanted to be king, who would rule the Nephites? That question was about to change the entire government forever. But for now, the sons of Mosiah were focused on one thing -- sharing the gospel with people who had never heard it.
