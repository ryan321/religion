### Lesson 15.2 -- Ammon and King Lamoni

Of all the missionary stories in the Book of Mormon, the story of **Ammon** and **King Lamoni** might be the most exciting. It has action, danger, and the power of God on full display.

When the sons of Mosiah split up to cover more ground, Ammon headed to the **land of Ishmael**. He was immediately captured by Lamanite guards and brought before the king, a man named **Lamoni** (Alma 17:20-21). Lamoni could have killed him or thrown him in prison. Instead, he asked Ammon what he wanted. Ammon said something surprising. He wanted to be Lamoni's **servant**.

A prince of the Nephites, offering to be a servant to a Lamanite king. That takes real humility.

Lamoni agreed, and Ammon was sent to watch over the king's flocks. This is where the famous story happens. While guarding the flocks at the waters of Sebus, a group of **robbers** scattered the animals. The other servants panicked because Lamoni had executed servants for losing flocks before. But Ammon saw an opportunity.

He told the other servants to gather the flocks while he dealt with the robbers. When the robbers came at him with clubs and stones, Ammon fought back with incredible strength. He **cut off the arms** of those who raised weapons against him and drove the rest away (Alma 17:36-38). The other servants gathered up the severed arms and brought them to Lamoni as evidence.

When Lamoni heard what happened, he was astonished. He asked, "Where is this man?" and his servants told him Ammon was out faithfully caring for the horses and chariots, doing his job as if nothing extraordinary had happened (Alma 18:8-9). Lamoni began to wonder if Ammon was the **Great Spirit** his people believed in.

Ammon came before the king and taught him the gospel: about God, the creation, the fall of Adam, and the plan of redemption. The **Spirit of the Lord** fell upon Lamoni so powerfully that the king collapsed. His wife, his servants, and many others also fell under the power of the Spirit (Alma 19). When they arose, they were **converted** to the Lord.

Ammon didn't convert Lamoni by arguing or debating. He did it by serving, by showing courage, and then by teaching the truth when the king's heart was ready. His example opened the door, and the Spirit did the rest.
