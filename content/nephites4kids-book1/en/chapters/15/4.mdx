### Lesson 15.4 -- The Reign of the Judges Begins

Every big story has turning points -- moments where everything shifts and nothing is ever the same again. For the Nephites, this was one of those moments.

The people voted, and they chose **Alma the Younger** as the **first chief judge** of the Nephites. He also continued serving as the **high priest** over the Church (Mosiah 29:42). Think about how incredible that is. The same young man who had once tried to destroy the Church was now leading both the government and the Church. His story of repentance and conversion had come full circle.

Shortly after this, King **Mosiah** died. He was a righteous king who had served his people well, translated ancient records, and had the wisdom to end the monarchy before it could produce another Noah. With his death, the era of Nephite kings was officially over.

The scriptures mark this as such an important moment that they started counting time differently. Instead of dating events by the reign of kings, they now measured years by the **reign of the judges**. Year one of the reign of the judges was year one of a new era for the Nephites.

But this new system wasn't going to be easy. Almost immediately, challenges arose. Not everyone was happy about the change. Some people wanted power and tried to overthrow the judges. Others wanted to go back to having a king. The freedom that came with choosing their own leaders also came with the responsibility to defend that freedom.

Looking back, this moment marks the end of the first great period of Nephite history. Think about everything we've covered in this book. It started with **Lehi** being called to leave Jerusalem. His family crossed the ocean, built a new civilization, and split into Nephites and Lamanites. We watched prophets preach, saw wicked kings rise and fall, witnessed Abinadi's courage, Alma's conversion, and Limhi's deliverance. We saw the sons of Mosiah leave to preach to their enemies, and we watched Alma the Younger go from the Church's worst enemy to its greatest defender.

Now the Nephites stand at the beginning of something new -- the reign of the judges. The stories ahead in the books of Alma and Helaman will bring wars, missionaries, miracles, and prophecies of Christ's coming. But all of that grows from the foundation laid in these first five hundred years.

The first chapter of Nephite history is complete. And what a story it has been.
