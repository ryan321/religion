### Lesson 2.2 -- Laman and Lemuel's Attempts

When Lehi told his sons they needed to go back to Jerusalem to get the brass plates, not everyone was happy about it. **Laman and Lemuel** complained. They called it a **"hard thing"** their father was asking of them (1 Nephi 3:5). Going back meant returning to a city where people had tried to kill their father, and now they had to convince a powerful man to hand over his most valuable records.

**Nephi** responded differently. He said the famous words: **"I will go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded"** (1 Nephi 3:7). He believed that if God gave a commandment, God would also make a way for it to be accomplished.

The brothers made their first attempt. They cast lots, and **Laman** was chosen to go to Laban's house alone. He went in and asked Laban for the plates. Laban refused. Not only did he refuse, he called Laman a robber and **threatened to kill him** (1 Nephi 3:13). Laman barely escaped with his life.

The brothers almost gave up, but Nephi insisted they try again. This time they came up with a plan. They went back to their old family home and gathered up all their **gold, silver, and precious things**, the wealth their father had left behind. They brought it to Laban and offered to **buy** the plates.

Laban looked at the treasure and wanted it, but he had no intention of giving up the plates. Instead, he **kept the gold and silver** and sent his servants to **kill the brothers** (1 Nephi 3:25-26). The brothers ran for their lives, hiding in a cave to escape.

At this point Laman and Lemuel were furious. They had now nearly been killed twice, and they had lost all of their family's wealth. They took out their anger on **Nephi and Sam**, beating them with a rod. An **angel** appeared and told them to stop, promising that the Lord would deliver Laban into their hands (1 Nephi 3:29). But even after seeing an angel, Laman and Lemuel kept complaining.

Two attempts had failed. The treasure was gone. But Nephi still wasn't ready to give up.
