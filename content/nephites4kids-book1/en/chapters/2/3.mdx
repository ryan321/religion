### Lesson 2.3 -- Nephi's Faith

After two failed attempts and an angelic visit, Nephi went back into Jerusalem **alone at night**. His brothers stayed outside the city walls, still afraid and doubtful. Nephi didn't have a detailed plan. He later wrote that he was **"led by the Spirit, not knowing beforehand the things which I should do"** (1 Nephi 4:6). He trusted that God would guide him step by step.

As Nephi made his way through the dark streets toward Laban's house, he came across a man lying on the ground. It was **Laban**, and he was **drunk** (1 Nephi 4:7-8). He had passed out after a night of drinking with the elders of the city. His sword was lying right there beside him.

Then the **Spirit of the Lord** told Nephi to **kill Laban** (1 Nephi 4:10-12).

Nephi was shocked. He had never killed anyone before, and the idea horrified him. He pulled back and resisted. But the Spirit spoke to him again and explained why it was necessary. Laban had already tried to murder Nephi and his brothers. He had stolen their property. He refused to obey the Lord's command to give up the plates. And most importantly, the Spirit told Nephi that it was **"better that one man should perish than that a nation should dwindle and perish in unbelief"** (1 Nephi 4:13).

Without the brass plates, Lehi's entire future people, thousands and thousands of descendants, would lose the word of God. The scriptures were that important.

Nephi also remembered that the Lord had promised to bless his family and their descendants if they kept the commandments. The plates were essential to keeping those commandments. After struggling deeply with the command, Nephi **obeyed** (1 Nephi 4:18). He took Laban's life with Laban's own sword.

This is one of the most difficult events in the Book of Mormon. It was not easy for Nephi. He didn't want to do it. But he trusted God's wisdom over his own feelings. He believed that the Lord could see what he could not: the long-term consequences for an entire civilization. Nephi chose obedience, even when it was the hardest thing he had ever done.
