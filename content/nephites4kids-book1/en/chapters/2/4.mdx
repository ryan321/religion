### Lesson 2.4 -- The Plates Secured

After slaying Laban, Nephi knew he still had to actually get the brass plates out of Laban's treasury. He came up with a plan on the spot. He put on **Laban's clothes** and **armor**, disguising himself to look like Laban in the dark (1 Nephi 4:19-21). Then he went to Laban's house and spoke to Laban's **servant Zoram** in Laban's voice.

Nephi, still disguised, commanded Zoram to bring the **brass plates** from the treasury and follow him outside the city walls. Zoram didn't suspect anything. He assumed he was obeying his master. He gathered the plates and walked with Nephi, and as they went, Nephi talked with him about the elders of the Jews to keep up the disguise (1 Nephi 4:22-27).

When they reached Nephi's brothers waiting outside the walls, the truth came out. Laman, Lemuel, and Sam saw Nephi coming and at first thought he was Laban -- they were terrified and started to run. When Nephi called out to them in his own voice, they realized it was him. But now **Zoram** understood the deception and tried to flee.

Nephi was strong and grabbed Zoram before he could escape. But instead of threatening him, Nephi made Zoram a **promise**: if Zoram would come with them into the wilderness, he would be a **free man** among their people. He would no longer be a servant. Zoram agreed and **made an oath** to stay with them (1 Nephi 4:33-37). In that culture, an oath was completely binding -- once Zoram swore, Nephi's brothers knew he would keep his word, and their fears about him ended.

The brothers returned to their father's camp with the brass plates. **Sariah** had been deeply worried. She had started to fear that her sons were dead and had even complained that Lehi was a visionary man who had led them to disaster. But when she saw her sons return safely with the plates, she **rejoiced** and her faith was strengthened. She knew the Lord had protected them (1 Nephi 5:1-8).

Lehi examined the plates and found they contained everything they needed -- the scriptures, their genealogy, and the prophecies. The family now had the word of God preserved in writing, ready to carry with them wherever the Lord would lead.
