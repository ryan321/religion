### Lesson 3.1 -- Ishmael's Family Joins

Lehi's family had the brass plates, but the Lord had another important errand. He commanded Lehi to send his sons back to Jerusalem **one more time** to bring the family of a man named **Ishmael** into the wilderness (1 Nephi 7:1-2).

Why? Because Lehi's sons needed **wives**, and Ishmael's family had daughters. If the Lord's plan was to build a new nation in a promised land, the group needed more than one family. Marriages between Lehi's sons and Ishmael's daughters would provide the foundation for future generations.

Nephi and his brothers went back to Jerusalem and spoke with Ishmael. Remarkably, **Ishmael agreed** to come. The Lord softened his heart, and he gathered his household (his wife, his sons, his daughters, and their families) and they set out toward Lehi's camp in the wilderness.

But the journey didn't go smoothly. Along the way, some of Ishmael's children and **Laman and Lemuel** turned against Nephi. They wanted to go back to Jerusalem. They didn't want the hard life of the wilderness. The group divided, and those who sided against Nephi decided to **tie him up** with cords, planning to leave him in the desert to die (1 Nephi 7:16).

Nephi was in real danger, but he didn't panic. He **prayed** to the Lord for strength, and the Lord answered. The cords that bound Nephi **came loose**, and he was freed (1 Nephi 7:17-18). Some members of Ishmael's family then stood up for Nephi and pleaded with Laman and Lemuel to stop.

Laman and Lemuel eventually felt sorry for what they had done, at least for the moment, and asked Nephi to forgive them. Nephi forgave them immediately and sincerely (1 Nephi 7:21). The group continued together and reached Lehi's camp safely.

With Ishmael's family now part of the group, Lehi's camp had grown significantly. There were now enough people to form marriages, raise families, and eventually build a new community. The Lord's plan was moving forward, even though every step seemed to come with conflict and struggle.
