### Lesson 3.2 -- The Liahona

One morning, Lehi stepped outside his tent and found something remarkable on the ground. It was a round ball made of **fine brass**, and it had **two spindles** inside it (1 Nephi 16:10). This object would later be called the **Liahona**.

The Liahona was not something Lehi built or bought. It was a **gift from God**, a tool designed to guide the family through the wilderness. One of the spindles **pointed the direction** they should travel, showing them which way to go through unfamiliar and dangerous terrain. But the Liahona did more than just point. At times, **words appeared** on the ball, giving the family specific instructions and spiritual messages (1 Nephi 16:26-29).

Here is the most important thing about the Liahona: it **only worked according to their faith and diligence**. When the family was faithful, obedient, and paying attention to God, the spindles pointed clearly and the words were helpful. When they were disobedient, complaining, or careless, the Liahona **stopped working** (1 Nephi 16:28-29). It went still. The directions disappeared. They were left on their own.

This made the Liahona more than just a compass. It was a **physical reminder** that God's guidance depends on faithfulness. You can't ignore God all week and then expect clear direction when you suddenly need it. The connection between obedience and revelation is constant. The Liahona made that truth visible and unmistakable every single day.

Later in the Book of Mormon, **Alma** compared the Liahona to the **words of Christ** (Alma 37:38-46). He told his son Helaman that just as the Liahona guided Lehi's family through the physical wilderness, the scriptures and words of the prophets guide us through life. And just like the Liahona, those words only help us if we pay attention to them and follow them with faith.

The Liahona is one of the most memorable objects in the Book of Mormon because it teaches such a clear lesson. God is willing to guide you. He will show you the way. But you have to be willing to listen, believe, and follow. The guidance is always available; the question is whether you are ready to receive it.
