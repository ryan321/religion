### Lesson 3.3 -- Hardship in the Wilderness

Traveling through the wilderness was not a short adventure. Lehi's group spent roughly **eight years** journeying through the Arabian desert before they reached the coast where they would build a ship (1 Nephi 17:4). Eight years of walking, camping, packing, unpacking, and moving again. It was a grueling test of endurance and faith.

The conditions were harsh. The group traveled through hot, dry, and desolate terrain. They often ate **raw meat** because the Lord told them not to make fires, likely to avoid attracting dangerous attention from other groups in the area (1 Nephi 17:2). Imagine years of eating raw food in the desert heat. It was not comfortable.

Tragedy struck when **Ishmael died** and was buried at a place called Nahom (1 Nephi 16:34). His daughters were devastated with grief. The death deepened the divisions in the group. **Laman and Lemuel** and some of Ishmael's children used the moment to complain bitterly against Lehi and Nephi. They blamed them for dragging everyone into the wilderness. They talked about how much better life had been in Jerusalem. They even discussed **killing** Lehi and Nephi so they could turn back (1 Nephi 16:37-38).

During these years, **Sariah** and other women in the group gave birth to children in wilderness conditions: no homes, no midwives, no beds. The fact that mothers and babies survived this journey is itself remarkable. Nephi noted that the women became **strong** through their hardships and were able to bear the journey (1 Nephi 17:1-2).

The constant pattern during these wilderness years was this: hardship came, Laman and Lemuel complained, and they often dragged others into rebellion with them. Meanwhile, Nephi, Sam, and others kept trusting God and moving forward. Every trial seemed to deepen the divide between those who had faith and those who didn't.

These eight years were not wasted time. They were a refining process. The wilderness was teaching this group what it would take to survive, build a new society, and keep their covenants in a land far from everything they had known.
