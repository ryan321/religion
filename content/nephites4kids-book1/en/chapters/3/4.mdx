### Lesson 3.4 -- Nephi's Broken Bow

One of the most well-known stories from the wilderness journey involves Nephi's bow. Nephi had a **steel bow** that he used to hunt food for the group. One day, while hunting, the bow **broke** (1 Nephi 16:18). At the same time, his brothers' bows had **lost their spring**, meaning they could no longer shoot arrows effectively either. The group suddenly had no way to hunt.

This was a crisis. Without hunting, the families faced **starvation**. And the reaction was immediate and ugly. Laman and Lemuel murmured -- that was expected. But this time, even **Lehi** began to **murmur against the Lord** (1 Nephi 16:20). The prophet himself, the man who had led them into the wilderness, was complaining. The hunger and exhaustion had gotten to everyone.

Here is where Nephi stood out. Instead of joining the complaining, he **acted**. He made a **new bow** out of wood and crafted an arrow from a straight stick (1 Nephi 16:23). Then -- and this is the key part -- he went to his father and asked, **"Where shall I go to find food?"**

Nephi could have just gone hunting on his own. He was capable. But he went to his father, the **prophet**, and asked for direction. This humbled Lehi. The prophet realized he had been wrong to complain, and he went to the Lord in prayer. The Lord spoke to Lehi and directed him to look at the **Liahona**. When he did, the ball showed Nephi exactly where to go. Nephi followed the directions and brought back food for the group (1 Nephi 16:30-32).

This story follows a clear pattern that comes up again and again in the scriptures. First: **don't complain** -- it doesn't fix anything. Second: **act** -- do what you can with what you have. Third: **exercise faith** -- trust that God will help. Fourth: **follow the prophet** -- go to the Lord's appointed leader for guidance.

Nephi didn't sit around waiting for someone else to solve the problem. He didn't blame God for the broken bow. He took responsibility, showed respect for priesthood leadership, and trusted the Lord. The result was that the whole group was fed and the journey continued.
