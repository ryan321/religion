### Lesson 4.1 -- Lehi's Dream

Have you ever had a dream that felt so real you couldn't stop thinking about it? Lehi had one of those -- except his wasn't just a dream. It was a **vision from God**, and it contained one of the most important messages in all of scripture.

In **1 Nephi 8**, Lehi tells his family about this vision. He saw a dark and dreary wilderness. He walked for hours through thick darkness, feeling lost and afraid. Then he prayed, and suddenly everything changed. He saw a **tree** with fruit that was white, pure, and beautiful. When he tasted the fruit, it was "**desirable to make one happy**" and "**most sweet, above all**" that he had ever tasted (1 Nephi 8:10-11). It filled his soul with joy, and the first thing he wanted to do was share it with his family.

Lehi looked around and saw more of the vision. There was a **rod of iron** that ran alongside a **strait and narrow path** leading to the tree. People were trying to walk the path to reach the fruit. But thick **mists of darkness** kept rolling in, making it impossible to see. Some people grabbed hold of the rod of iron and held on tight, pressing forward through the darkness until they reached the tree. They tasted the fruit and were happy.

But then Lehi noticed something else. Across a river, there was a **great and spacious building** floating in the air, filled with people dressed in fine clothes. These people were pointing at the ones eating the fruit and **laughing at them**. They mocked them and made fun of them. And here's the heartbreaking part -- some of the people who had already reached the tree looked up, felt ashamed, and **walked away** from the fruit. The mockers got to them.

Others never even made it. Some wandered off the path when the mists came. Some made it partway but let go of the rod. Some drowned in the river. And some went straight to the great and spacious building and joined the mockers.

Lehi was worried about his own family. He saw Sariah, Sam, and Nephi come to the tree. But **Laman and Lemuel refused to come**. No matter what he said, they wouldn't eat the fruit. This broke Lehi's heart.

This dream wasn't just a random vision. It was a roadmap for life -- and Nephi was about to find out exactly what every symbol meant.
