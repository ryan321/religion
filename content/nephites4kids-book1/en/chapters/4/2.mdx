### Lesson 4.2 -- What the Symbols Mean

Lehi's dream was powerful, but what did it all mean? Nephi wanted to know. He didn't just wonder about it. He sat down and **prayed**, asking God to show him the same things his father had seen. And God answered.

In **1 Nephi 11-14**, the Spirit of the Lord and an angel guided Nephi through the meaning of every part of the dream. What Nephi learned turned a beautiful vision into one of the most important teachings in scripture.

The **tree** represented the **love of God**, the greatest of all gifts. When Nephi asked what the tree meant, the Spirit showed him the birth of Jesus Christ, the Son of God, coming down to earth. God's love was so great that He sent His own Son to save us. That's what the tree stands for.

The **fruit** represented the blessings that come from God's love, especially the **Atonement of Jesus Christ** and **eternal life**. It was sweet and desirable because nothing in the whole world is better than what God offers us through His Son.

The **rod of iron** was the **word of God**: the scriptures, the teachings of prophets, and personal revelation. If you hold on to the word of God, you can make it through anything, even when you can't see the way forward. That's why the people in the dream who **held the rod** made it to the tree.

The **mists of darkness** were the **temptations of the devil**. Satan doesn't always attack with obvious things. Sometimes he just makes everything confusing and hard to see, so you let go of what you know is true.

The **great and spacious building** represented the **pride of the world**, people who think they're better than everyone else and make fun of anyone who tries to follow God. Nephi saw that the building had no foundation. It eventually **fell**, and the fall of it was "exceedingly great" (1 Nephi 11:36).

The **strait and narrow path** was the **covenant path**, the way you live when you follow Jesus Christ, get baptized, keep commandments, and stay faithful.

When you put it all together, the message is clear: hold on to the scriptures, stay on the path, don't let temptation blind you, ignore the mockers, and you'll reach the love of God. Let go of the rod, and you'll get lost. It's as simple, and as hard, as that.
