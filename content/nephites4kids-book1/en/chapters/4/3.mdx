### Lesson 4.3 -- Nephi Sees the Future

Nephi's vision didn't stop with the symbols from his father's dream. The angel kept showing him more, much more. What started as an explanation of a dream became a sweeping **vision of the entire future of the world**.

First, Nephi saw the **birth of Jesus Christ**. He saw a beautiful virgin in the city of Nazareth. He saw the Son of God born into the world, walk among the people, and teach with power. He saw Christ heal the sick, raise the dead, and bless the lives of everyone around Him. Then he saw something painful: the **crucifixion**. The Lamb of God was taken by the people and lifted upon the cross (1 Nephi 11:32-33). It was the most important event in all of history, and Nephi witnessed it hundreds of years before it happened.

He also saw Christ call **Twelve Apostles** to lead His Church. These men would carry the gospel to the world after Christ's resurrection. But Nephi saw something sad too. Over time, the world would reject the apostles and change the teachings. This was the **Great Apostasy**, when the true Church was lost from the earth for many centuries.

Then the vision jumped forward. Nephi saw a man inspired by God who crossed the ocean to the Americas, **Christopher Columbus** (1 Nephi 13:12). He saw groups of people leaving Europe to find religious freedom, the **Pilgrims** and other settlers. He saw a great nation rise up and fight for its independence, the **American Revolution**. God was preparing the way for something.

What was He preparing for? Nephi saw the **Bible** go forth among the nations, carrying many parts of the gospel to the world. But he also saw that some "plain and precious" truths had been taken out or lost over time (1 Nephi 13:28). That's where the **Book of Mormon** came in. Nephi saw that God would bring forth another book, a record from his own people, that would restore the lost truths and work alongside the Bible to bring people to Christ (1 Nephi 13:39-40).

Think about that. Nephi saw Columbus, the founding of America, and the coming forth of the very book you're studying right now, all in one vision. God showed him the entire sweep of history to teach him that everything has a purpose and that God's plan stretches across thousands of years.
