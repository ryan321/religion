### Lesson 4.4 -- Laman and Lemuel Don't Understand

After receiving his incredible vision, Nephi returned to his father's tent and found his brothers **arguing**. Laman and Lemuel were confused about their father's dream. They couldn't figure out what it meant, and they were getting frustrated with each other about it.

Nephi asked them a simple but powerful question: "**Have ye inquired of the Lord?**" (1 Nephi 15:8). It's the kind of question that should have been obvious. If you don't understand something God has shown your father, why not ask God to help you understand it too? That's exactly what Nephi had done, and he received one of the greatest visions in scripture because of it.

But Laman and Lemuel's answer revealed the real problem. They said, "**The Lord maketh no such thing known unto us**" (1 Nephi 15:9). They had completely given up before they even tried. They didn't ask because they didn't believe God would answer them.

This is one of the most important lessons in the entire Book of Mormon. **Revelation requires action.** You have to do something -- pray, study, ponder, ask -- before God will give you answers. He doesn't usually force knowledge into your mind when you haven't bothered to seek it. Laman and Lemuel wanted to understand, but they weren't willing to do the one thing that would actually help: humble themselves and pray.

Nephi was frustrated but still tried to help. He explained the symbols of the dream to his brothers -- the tree, the rod, the river, the building. He taught them that the **river of filthy water** represented the depths of hell, and that the **justice of God** separated the wicked from the righteous like the river divided the tree from the building (1 Nephi 15:26-30). He laid it all out plainly.

Did it change them? For a little while, maybe. The record says they humbled themselves temporarily (1 Nephi 16:5). But Laman and Lemuel's pattern was always the same -- they'd soften for a moment, then harden again. The core problem never went away. They didn't want to do the work of seeking answers from God themselves.

This pattern is a warning for all of us. You can sit in church, listen to lessons, and hear the scriptures read out loud -- but if you never **personally ask God** to teach you, you'll always be stuck on the outside, confused and frustrated, just like Laman and Lemuel.
