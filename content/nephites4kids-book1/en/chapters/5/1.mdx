### Lesson 5.1 -- The Command to Build a Ship

After eight long years of traveling through the wilderness, Lehi's family finally reached a land they called **Bountiful**. And it really was bountiful. There was fruit, wild honey, and everything they needed to survive. After years of raw meat and desert hardship, they must have felt like they'd arrived in paradise. The ocean stretched out in front of them, vast and beautiful.

But God wasn't done with them yet. He had one more enormous task for Nephi.

The Lord told Nephi, "**Thou shalt construct a ship**" (1 Nephi 17:8). Not find a ship. Not buy a ship. Build one. From scratch. In the wilderness. With no shipyard, no training, and no experience.

You can probably guess how **Laman and Lemuel** reacted. They didn't just doubt Nephi. They **laughed** at him. They thought it was the most ridiculous thing they'd ever heard. Nephi, a young man who had spent his life in the desert, was going to build an ocean-going vessel? They called him a fool. They refused to help. They said he couldn't do it and he was wasting everyone's time.

But Nephi had learned something powerful over the years of watching God work miracles for his family. He had seen the Liahona guide them. He had seen God provide food in the desert. He had received visions. And he had broken a steel bow and still managed to feed his family by trusting in the Lord. Every single challenge had taught him the same lesson: **God helps those who obey**.

So Nephi asked God where to find ore to make tools, and God showed him. He built a bellows to make fire hot enough to melt the metal. He made his own tools. And then, step by step, he started building the ship, "**not after the manner of men**" but after the manner God showed him (1 Nephi 18:2).

Nephi later declared one of the most powerful statements in all of scripture: "**If God had commanded me to do all things I could do them**" (1 Nephi 17:50). He didn't say it would be easy. He said that with God's help, it was possible. That's faith.
