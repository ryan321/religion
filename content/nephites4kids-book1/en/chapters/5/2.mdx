### Lesson 5.2 -- Nephi and His Brothers

Building a ship was already an enormous challenge, but Nephi had a bigger problem -- his own brothers. Laman and Lemuel didn't just refuse to help. They actively tried to **stop** him.

They called Nephi a **fool**. They said he was just like their father -- someone with ridiculous visions and impossible ideas. In their minds, they never should have left Jerusalem. They'd been dragged through the desert for eight years, and now this younger brother was telling them to build a boat? They had no intention of lifting a finger.

But Nephi didn't back down. He stood before his brothers and gave them one of the longest, boldest speeches in the Book of Mormon. He reminded them of everything **God had done for Israel** -- how He delivered Moses and the children of Israel from slavery in Egypt, how He parted the Red Sea, how He fed them manna in the wilderness, and how He brought water from a rock. God had done impossible things over and over again. Why would building a ship be any different?

Nephi also reminded them of what God had done for **their own family**. He had guided them through the wilderness. He had provided the Liahona. He had fed them and protected them every single day. Laman and Lemuel had seen all of these miracles with their own eyes, yet they still couldn't bring themselves to trust God.

Then things got dangerous. Laman and Lemuel were so angry that they decided to **throw Nephi into the sea** and drown him. They came toward him, ready to do it. But the Lord intervened. He **filled Nephi with power**, and when Nephi said, "**Touch me not**," his brothers felt the power of God so strongly that they were **afraid** (1 Nephi 17:48-55). They didn't dare lay a hand on him. In fact, the experience shook them so badly that they were ready to **worship** Nephi, but he stopped them and told them to worship God instead.

After this, Laman and Lemuel finally began to help build the ship. Not because their hearts had truly changed, but because they were afraid. They worked alongside Nephi, and together the ship was completed -- built not by human skill alone, but by God's direction.

This story teaches something important: **doing the right thing doesn't mean everyone will support you.** Sometimes the people closest to you will push back the hardest. But if God is with you, no one can stop His work.
