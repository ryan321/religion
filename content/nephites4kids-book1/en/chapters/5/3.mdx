### Lesson 5.3 -- Crossing the Ocean

The ship was finished, and it was time for the most daring part of the journey -- **crossing the ocean**. Lehi's entire family, along with Ishmael's family, loaded their provisions, seeds, and everything they needed and set sail for the **promised land**.

At first, things went well. The wind blew in the right direction. The Liahona helped guide them. They were actually doing it -- a group of former city-dwellers from Jerusalem were sailing across an ocean in a ship they had built with their own hands, guided by God.

But then the trouble started.

After several days at sea, Laman, Lemuel, and some of the **sons of Ishmael** began to party. They were singing, dancing wildly, and speaking with "**much rudeness**" (1 Nephi 18:9). Nephi was worried that their behavior would offend God, so he spoke to them about it. He asked them to be reverent and remember who was actually guiding their ship.

They didn't take it well. In fact, they were so angry that they **tied Nephi up** with cords and refused to let him go (1 Nephi 18:11). The moment they did this, the **Liahona stopped working**. Without its guidance, they had no idea where to go.

Then the ocean turned against them. A **terrible storm** hit -- fierce winds, massive waves, and the ship was driven backward for **four days**. It got so bad that everyone on board, except Laman and Lemuel's group, thought they were going to drown. Nephi's wrists and ankles swelled from the cords. Even his aging parents, Lehi and Sariah, became so sick from grief and the rough seas that they were close to death.

Laman and Lemuel still wouldn't let Nephi go. The storm raged on. It wasn't until they realized they were genuinely about to **perish** that they finally **untied him** (1 Nephi 18:15). The moment Nephi was free, he picked up the Liahona, prayed, and the **storm stopped**. The winds returned to normal, and they continued sailing toward the promised land.

What a powerful lesson. The family's safety depended on following God, and the moment they chose rebellion over obedience, everything fell apart. The Liahona -- which only worked through **faith and diligence** -- went dead. And only repentance and humility brought it back to life.
