### Lesson 5.4 -- The Promised Land

After weeks of sailing across the ocean, Lehi's family finally spotted land. They had made it. They stepped off the ship and set foot on the **promised land**, somewhere in the Americas.

Imagine what that moment must have felt like. They had left everything behind in Jerusalem. They had spent eight years crossing a brutal wilderness. They had built a ship with their own hands. They had survived a storm that nearly killed them all. And now, finally, they were home, in a land God had prepared just for them.

The first thing they did was practical. They **planted seeds** they had brought from Jerusalem and waited to see if they would grow. They did, and grew abundantly (1 Nephi 18:24). The land was rich and fertile, perfect for farming.

As they explored, they found incredible things. There were **animals** in the forests: "the cow and the ox, and the ass and the horse, and the goat and the wild goat" (1 Nephi 18:25). They discovered **gold, silver, and copper** in abundance. The land had everything they could possibly need to build a new civilization. God had truly led them to a land of **promise and plenty**.

But even with all this blessing, the story wasn't going to be simple. The same problems that had plagued the family for years (Laman and Lemuel's resentment, their anger toward Nephi, their refusal to fully trust God) hadn't gone away just because they arrived in a new place. A change of location doesn't fix a change of heart that never happened.

The **seeds of division** had been growing since the very first day Lehi told his family to leave Jerusalem. Every time Laman and Lemuel murmured, every time they tried to hurt Nephi, every time they refused to pray, the divide got deeper. They were all living in the same land now, but they were not truly one family anymore.

Lehi could see it. As an aging father, he knew that his time was running short, and he knew what was coming. His family was going to **split apart**. The only question was when. Before that happened, he had final words to share: blessings, warnings, and testimony. Those last words would become some of the most powerful chapters in the Book of Mormon.

The promised land was beautiful and abundant, but it was about to become the stage for one of the saddest family breakups in all of scripture.
