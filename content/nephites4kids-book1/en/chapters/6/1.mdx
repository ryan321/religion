### Lesson 6.1 -- Lehi's Final Words

Lehi was old. He had led his family across a wilderness, over an ocean, and into a new land. He had served as prophet, father, and leader through some of the most difficult years any family could face. Now he knew his time was running out, and he had one last thing to do -- gather his family and speak to them one final time.

In **2 Nephi 1-4**, Lehi delivered his final blessings and counsel. These chapters contain some of the most tender and powerful words in the Book of Mormon.

First, Lehi **testified about the promised land**. He told his family that this land was set apart by God -- a land of liberty for those who serve Him. But he also gave a serious warning: if the people who lived in this land ever turned to wickedness, they would be "**cut off from the presence of the Lord**" (2 Nephi 1:20). The land was a blessing, but it came with conditions. Righteousness was required.

Then Lehi turned to **Laman and Lemuel**. Even after everything they had done -- the murmuring, the rebellion, the violence toward Nephi -- Lehi still loved them. He **pled** with them to change. He begged them to "awake" from a deep sleep, to shake off the chains that bound them, and to rise up and be the men God wanted them to be (2 Nephi 1:13-14, 23). You can hear the heartbreak in his words. He was a father who desperately wanted his sons to choose the right.

Lehi also gave **blessings to his grandchildren** -- the children of Laman and Lemuel. He promised that even if their fathers went astray, God would not forget them. Those blessings would echo through generations.

He blessed **Jacob and Joseph**, his younger sons born in the wilderness. He taught Jacob about the Atonement of Christ and the importance of opposition in life (2 Nephi 2). He told Joseph about the ancient Joseph of Egypt and the prophecies about the last days (2 Nephi 3).

After all his words were spoken, **Lehi died**. The man who had received the original call to leave Jerusalem, who had carried the weight of his family's journey on his shoulders, was gone. He had been faithful to the end.
