### Lesson 6.3 -- The Nephites

Once Nephi and his followers settled in the land of Nephi, they didn't sit around feeling sorry for themselves. They got to work building a **civilization**.

The Nephites were industrious people. They **constructed buildings** and worked with wood, iron, copper, brass, steel, gold, and silver (2 Nephi 5:15). They cleared land and **farmed**. They raised crops and animals. In a remarkably short time, they went from a group of refugees to a functioning society.

One of the most impressive things Nephi did was build a **temple**. Not just any building -- a temple patterned after the **temple of Solomon** in Jerusalem. It wasn't as elaborate as Solomon's, because they didn't have access to the same precious materials in the same abundance. But the structure and the purpose were the same (2 Nephi 5:16). Nephi understood that if his people were going to stay faithful to God, they needed a place of worship at the center of their community.

Nephi also made sure his people had the **scriptures**. He taught from the **brass plates** -- the record of the Jews that his family had risked their lives to obtain back in Jerusalem. Those plates contained the law of Moses, the words of the prophets, and the history of God's dealings with His people. Without them, the Nephites would have lost their connection to their heritage and their covenants.

For defense, Nephi made **swords** -- patterned after the sword of Laban -- because he knew the Lamanites would eventually come after them (2 Nephi 5:14). It was a sad reality, but a necessary one.

Nephi also set up proper **religious leadership**. He consecrated his brothers **Jacob and Joseph** as **priests and teachers** over the people (2 Nephi 5:26). He didn't try to do everything himself. He established a structure so the people could be taught, led, and spiritually cared for even after he was gone.

The Nephite civilization was built on a foundation of **covenants, scriptures, temple worship, and hard work**. They weren't perfect -- Nephi himself acknowledged that his people had struggles. But they were trying. They were a covenant people doing their best to follow the Lord in a new land, knowing that their faithfulness was the only thing that would keep them safe.
