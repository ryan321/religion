### Lesson 7.1 -- Nephi Teaches About Christ

Have you ever had something so important to share that you just couldn't keep it inside? That's how Nephi felt about Jesus Christ. Out of everything Nephi experienced (leaving Jerusalem, crossing the ocean, building a civilization), his greatest contribution was his **testimony of the Savior**.

Remember those incredible visions Nephi received? He didn't just see a few glimpses of the future. He saw the **birth of Jesus Christ**, His **ministry** among the people, and His **crucifixion**. Nephi knew, hundreds of years before it happened, exactly who Jesus was and what He would do for the world. And he wanted everyone else to know it too.

Nephi spent the last part of his life writing and teaching about Christ. He wasn't just sharing facts or history. He was pouring out his heart. He wanted his children, his grandchildren, and every generation after them to understand where to turn when they needed help.

That's why he wrote one of the most powerful verses in all of scripture:

> "We talk of Christ, we rejoice in Christ, we preach of Christ, we prophesy of Christ...that our children may know to what source they may look for a remission of their sins" (2 Nephi 25:26).

Read that again carefully. Nephi said **"our children may know."** He wasn't just thinking about himself. He was thinking about you! He wanted future generations, including kids reading his words thousands of years later, to know that **Jesus Christ** is the one who can forgive sins and bring peace.

Think about what that means. Every time Nephi carved words into those metal plates, it was hard work. He didn't have a computer or even paper. He was carefully engraving each word into metal because he believed it mattered that much. And what did he choose to write about more than anything else? **Christ**.

Nephi's example teaches us something important. When we really believe something is true, we want to share it. We talk about it, we live it, and we help others understand it. That's what it means to be a **witness of Christ**: not just believing in Him quietly, but letting that belief shape everything you do and say.

Today, we have Nephi's words in the **Book of Mormon** because he made that effort. His testimony of Christ has now reached millions of people around the world, just like he hoped it would.
