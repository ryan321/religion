### Lesson 7.2 -- Isaiah in the Book of Mormon

If you've ever tried reading the Book of Mormon straight through, you might have hit a section that felt really hard to understand. Chances are, you ran into the **Isaiah chapters**. Don't worry, you're not alone! But these chapters are there for a very good reason.

Nephi included large portions of the prophet **Isaiah's** writings in his record, especially in **2 Nephi chapters 12 through 24**. That's a lot of Isaiah! Some people wonder why Nephi copied so much of another prophet's words into his own book. Was it just filler to take up space on the plates?

Not at all. Nephi had a very specific purpose. He explained it himself when he said, **"I did liken all scriptures unto us, that it might be for our profit and learning"** (1 Nephi 19:23). To **"liken"** means to apply something to your own life. Nephi read Isaiah's words and saw that they applied directly to his people and to us.

So what did Isaiah write about that was so important? His prophecies cover some huge topics:

- The **scattering of Israel**, how God's covenant people would be spread across the earth
- The **gathering of Israel**, how they would eventually be brought back together
- The coming of the **Messiah**, Jesus Christ
- Events of the **last days** before Christ's Second Coming

These aren't small topics. They're some of the biggest themes in all of scripture. Isaiah saw the grand sweep of God's plan, and Nephi recognized that his own family's story, being scattered from Jerusalem, was part of that bigger picture.

Here's something cool to think about. Jesus Himself told the Nephites later in the Book of Mormon to study Isaiah's words carefully (3 Nephi 23:1). If the Savior says Isaiah is important, then it's worth the effort to understand, even when the language feels tricky.

The next time you come across Isaiah's words in the Book of Mormon, try doing what Nephi did. Ask yourself, **"How does this apply to me?"** You might be surprised at what you discover. Those ancient prophecies about gathering, about the Messiah, and about the last days are unfolding right now, in your lifetime.

Nephi didn't include Isaiah to make the Book of Mormon harder. He included Isaiah because those prophecies are some of the most important words ever written.
