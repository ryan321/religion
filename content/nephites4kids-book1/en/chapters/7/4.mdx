### Lesson 7.4 -- Passing the Record

Imagine spending your entire adult life leading a group of people through the wilderness, across an ocean, and into a new land. You've been their prophet, their king, their teacher, and their record keeper. Now you're growing old, and it's time to hand everything off to the next generation. That's exactly where Nephi found himself near the end of his life.

Nephi had carried an incredible load of responsibility for **decades**. He had kept the commandments of God to make the **small plates** -- a record focused on spiritual teachings and prophecies. He also had the **brass plates**, the ancient scriptures his family had carried all the way from Jerusalem. These records were priceless, and someone trustworthy needed to take care of them next.

Nephi chose his younger brother **Jacob**. We read about this transfer in **Jacob 1:1-4**, where Jacob explains that Nephi gave him charge of the plates and asked him to write down the most important spiritual experiences and teachings. Nephi didn't ask Jacob to record every detail of daily life -- other records kept track of the history of their kings and wars. The small plates were specifically for **sacred things**.

Think about how much trust this took. Nephi was handing over the work of his lifetime. Everything he had written, every testimony he had carved into metal, was now in Jacob's hands. And Jacob took that responsibility seriously.

This moment also marks the beginning of a chain that would stretch across a **thousand years**. The small plates would pass from Jacob to his son Enos, then to Jarom, and then through a series of record keepers all the way down through Nephite history. Each person who received the plates understood that they were guarding something precious -- a record that would one day come forth to bless the entire world.

Nephi couldn't have known every detail of how his record would eventually reach us. But he had faith that God had a plan for those plates. And he was right. The words Nephi engraved were preserved, translated by the Prophet **Joseph Smith**, and published as part of the **Book of Mormon**.

Every time you open the Book of Mormon and read Nephi's words, you're holding the result of that long chain of faithful record keepers. It started with Nephi passing the plates to Jacob and trusting that God would take care of the rest.
