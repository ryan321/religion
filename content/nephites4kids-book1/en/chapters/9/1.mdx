### Lesson 9.1 -- A Righteous King

Not all kings in the Book of Mormon were good. Some were selfish, and some were downright wicked. But **King Benjamin** was different. He's one of the finest examples of leadership in all of scripture.

Benjamin ruled in **Zarahemla** during a difficult time. The **Lamanites** kept attacking, and Benjamin had to defend his people in battle. He was a real **warrior** who actually fought alongside his armies to protect the Nephites. He didn't sit safely in a palace while others did the hard work.

But what really set Benjamin apart was his **humility**. Even though he was the king, he didn't live a life of luxury or demand that the people serve him. Instead, he **worked with his own hands** to support himself (Mosiah 2:14). Can you imagine that? A king who earned his own living instead of taxing the people to pay for a fancy lifestyle! Benjamin wanted the people to know that he wasn't above them. He was their servant, not their master.

Benjamin also understood that **spiritual leadership** mattered just as much as political leadership. He didn't just run the government. He taught alongside the **prophets and priests**, making sure the people understood the gospel. He fought against false teachings and worked hard to establish **peace** throughout the land.

As Benjamin grew old, he knew it was time to pass the kingdom to his son **Mosiah**. But before he did, he wanted to give his people one final message, a speech so important that it would be remembered for generations. He felt the weight of everything he had learned from God over his lifetime, and he wanted to share it all before his time was through.

So Benjamin made a plan. He would gather the entire nation together for a great assembly. He had something to teach them that would change their lives, a message given to him by an **angel**. This wasn't going to be an ordinary farewell speech. It would become one of the most important addresses in the entire Book of Mormon.

And that's exactly where our next lessons pick up.
