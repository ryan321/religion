### Lesson 9.2 -- The Great Address

Picture this: an entire nation -- thousands and thousands of families -- gathering together in one place. They set up their **tents** with the doors facing the temple so they could listen. And at the center of it all, King Benjamin had a **tower** built so that his voice could reach as many people as possible (Mosiah 2:1-8).

Even with the tower, the crowd was so large that not everyone could hear him. So Benjamin had his words **written down** and passed among the people. This might be one of the earliest examples of publishing a talk so everyone could read it!

So what did Benjamin say that was so important?

He started by reminding the people that he had served them faithfully his whole life. He hadn't sought for riches or power. He had worked alongside them and done his best to keep peace. But then he made a crucial point -- he wasn't telling them this to brag. He was making a point about **service**.

Benjamin taught one of the most quoted verses in all of Latter-day Saint scripture:

> "When ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God" (Mosiah 2:17).

Think about that for a moment. Every time you help someone -- a friend, a neighbor, a stranger -- you're actually serving **God**. It's not just a nice thing to do. It's one of the main ways we show our love for Heavenly Father.

Benjamin also taught that every person is a **beggar** before God. Nobody earns their blessings on their own. Everything we have -- our breath, our strength, our very lives -- comes from God. So we should never **judge the poor** or look down on someone who has less than we do. If we're all beggars depending on God's mercy, how can we refuse to help someone else who's in need?

This message was revolutionary. Benjamin wasn't just teaching nice ideas. He was reshaping how the Nephites thought about themselves, each other, and God. **True greatness**, he taught, comes not from power or wealth, but from humble, selfless **service**.
