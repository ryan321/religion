### Lesson 9.3 -- The Natural Man

King Benjamin's speech got even more powerful as he continued. He revealed something astonishing: an **angel** had appeared to him and given him a message directly from God. And that message was about **Jesus Christ**, by name, centuries before Christ would be born.

The angel told Benjamin about the Savior's coming. Christ would perform miracles, suffer incredible pain, and be crucified. He would take upon Himself the sins of the world so that anyone who repented and believed could be forgiven. Benjamin shared all of this with the people so they could prepare their hearts.

Then came one of the most important teachings in all of scripture. Benjamin, repeating the angel's words, declared:

> "For the natural man is an enemy to God, and has been from the fall of Adam, and will be, forever and ever, unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord" (Mosiah 3:19).

So what is the **"natural man"**? It's the part of us that is selfish, lazy, proud, angry, or unkind, the part that wants to do whatever feels good right now without thinking about what's right. Everyone has a natural man inside them. It's part of being human.

But Benjamin taught that we don't have to stay that way. We can **"put off"** the natural man, meaning we choose to set aside those selfish tendencies. How? By **yielding to the Holy Spirit**. That means listening to those quiet promptings that tell you to be kind, to be honest, to try again when you've messed up.

And the power to actually change? That comes through the **Atonement of Christ**. We can't overcome the natural man by willpower alone. We need the Savior's help.

Benjamin also said we need to become like a **child**: submissive, meek, humble, patient, and willing to follow God. That doesn't mean being weak. It means being strong enough to let God shape you into something better than you could become on your own.

This teaching is one of the core doctrines of the gospel. The natural man pulls us down, but through Christ, we can rise above it and become **saints**, not perfect people, but people who are trying, with God's help, to become more like Him every day.
