### Lesson 9.4 -- Taking Christ's Name

King Benjamin's words hit the people like a wave. As they listened to him teach about the Atonement and the natural man, they were so overcome with emotion that they **fell to the earth** (Mosiah 4:1-2). They weren't just sitting quietly and nodding along. They felt the truth of his words so deeply that they cried out with one voice, asking God for **mercy** and forgiveness through the blood of Christ.

And then something extraordinary happened. The **Spirit of the Lord** came upon them. They were **filled with joy** and received a remission of their sins. They felt peace, real, deep, lasting peace. It was exactly what Benjamin had been pointing them toward the entire time.

But Benjamin wasn't finished. He knew that feeling the Spirit wasn't the end of the journey. It was the beginning. So he invited the people to do something that would change their lives forever. He asked them to **take upon themselves the name of Christ**.

What does that mean? It means making a **covenant**, a sacred promise, with God. The people promised to follow Christ, keep His commandments, and be called by His name. In return, God promised to bless them, guide them, and ultimately save them.

This is exactly what members of **The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints** do today. When you are **baptized**, you take upon yourself the name of Christ. You promise to follow Him. And every week when you take the **sacrament**, you renew that promise. The bread and water are reminders of Christ's sacrifice, and the sacrament prayers use language very similar to what Benjamin taught his people.

King Benjamin made this covenant real for an **entire nation**. Every person there, men, women, and children, entered into this promise together. They committed to be Christ's people, not just in words, but in how they lived their daily lives.

Benjamin's address shows us that the gospel isn't just about learning facts or hearing good talks. It's about being **changed** by the Spirit, making **covenants** with God, and then living those covenants every single day. The people who heard Benjamin that day were different when they went home than when they arrived. And that's the whole point.
