# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing History of the Nephites, Book 1. You've covered roughly 500 years of Nephite history -- from Lehi leaving Jerusalem around 600 BC to the beginning of the reign of the judges around 92 BC.

Think about everything you've learned. You followed Lehi's family out of Jerusalem and into the wilderness. You watched Nephi obtain the brass plates, build a ship, and cross the ocean. You saw the vision of the tree of life and learned what the symbols mean. You were there when the Nephites and Lamanites split into two civilizations.

You met Jacob, Enos, and the keepers of the small books. You heard King Benjamin's great address and watched an entire nation take upon themselves the name of Christ. You followed Zeniff's colony to the land of Nephi and watched King Noah's wickedness destroy what his father had built. You stood with Abinadi as he testified of Christ before the wicked king -- and gave his life for that testimony.

You watched Alma believe, repent, and baptize his followers at the Waters of Mormon with one of the most beautiful covenants in all of scripture. You saw Alma the Younger go from the Church's worst enemy to its greatest champion. And you watched the sons of Mosiah leave for the most dangerous missionary work in the Book of Mormon.

Now you understand how the pieces fit together. You know where Zarahemla is, where the land of Nephi is, and how Zeniff's people, Alma's people, and Limhi's people all ended up back in the same place. You have a timeline in your head that will make the Book of Mormon come alive every time you read it.

But the story is just getting started. In Book 2, you'll follow Alma the Younger as chief judge, meet Captain Moroni, march with the stripling warriors, and watch the Gadianton robbers threaten everything the Nephites have built. The reign of the judges is full of war, faith, and some of the most powerful chapters in all of scripture.

Thank you for learning with us. Keep reading the Book of Mormon -- and keep building on what you've learned here.
