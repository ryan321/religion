### Lesson 1.1 -- The New Government

Have you ever thought about what happens when a country changes the way it's run? That's exactly what happened to the Nephites at the beginning of the book of Alma. King Mosiah had just finished translating the records of the Jaredites, and he saw how dangerous it could be when wicked kings ruled over people. So he decided to make a big change.

Instead of having kings, Mosiah set up a **system of judges**. In this new government, the people got to **choose their own leaders** by voting. The highest leader was called the **chief judge**, and he was also the leader over the Church. This was a big deal because the Nephites had been ruled by kings ever since Nephi's time!

Mosiah taught the people that it was better to be judged by judges chosen by the people than to have a king. He explained that if the people chose good judges, things would go well. But if the people started choosing wickedness, then serious consequences would follow. He wanted the people to take **responsibility** for their own choices (see Mosiah 29:25-27).

So who was the very first chief judge? It was **Alma the Younger**, the same young man who had once tried to destroy the Church! Remember how an angel appeared to him and he was struck down? After his mighty change of heart, Alma became one of the most faithful leaders the Nephites ever had.

Alma was chosen by **the voice of the people**, meaning the Nephites voted for him. He became both the chief judge over the land and the **high priest** over the Church (see Alma 2:16). This meant he had two very important jobs at the same time: leading the government and leading the Church.

This was the beginning of a new era for the Nephites. The **reign of the judges** would last for over a hundred years. Some of those years would be peaceful, and some would be filled with wars and challenges. But it all started here, with a people who chose to govern themselves and a leader who had experienced the power of repentance firsthand.

Alma's story teaches us that no matter what mistakes we've made in the past, Heavenly Father can help us change and even use us to do great things. Alma went from being an enemy of the Church to being its most important leader. Pretty amazing, right?
