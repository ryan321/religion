### Lesson 1.3 -- Amlici's Rebellion

Just a few years into the new government, the Nephites faced their first major crisis. A man named **Amlici** decided he wanted to throw out the system of judges and make himself **king**. He was cunning, clever, and a follower of the same false ideas that Nehor had taught.

Amlici was a smooth talker, and he managed to convince a large number of people to support him. The disagreement got so heated that the Nephites decided to settle it with a **vote**. The whole nation gathered to cast their voices for or against Amlici becoming king. When the votes were counted, the people chose **against** Amlici -- they wanted to keep the system of judges (see Alma 2:5-7).

But Amlici didn't accept the result. Instead of respecting what the people had chosen, he gathered his followers, and they **crowned him king** anyway. Then he commanded his people -- now called the **Amlicites** -- to take up weapons and go to war against the rest of the Nephites (see Alma 2:9-10).

This was a civil war -- Nephites fighting Nephites. The battles were fierce and bloody. The scriptures tell us that thousands upon thousands were killed on both sides. It was one of the worst things that had happened to the Nephite nation up to that point (see Alma 2:19).

To make things even worse, Amlici made an **alliance with the Lamanites**. He sent messengers to the Lamanite armies and convinced them to join the fight against Alma and the Nephites. Now Alma's people had enemies coming at them from multiple directions.

But Alma was not just a political leader -- he was also a man of faith. He cried out to the Lord for help, and the Lord **strengthened** the Nephite soldiers. In the final battle, **Alma fought Amlici face to face** and killed him. The Nephites drove back both the Amlicites and the Lamanites, winning a hard-fought victory (see Alma 2:29-31).

This story teaches us an important lesson about **agency**. The people voted and made their choice, but Amlici refused to accept it. When people try to force their will on others -- especially through violence -- it never leads to anything good. Alma trusted in the Lord and defended his people, and the Lord delivered them.

After the war, there was a time of peace, but the Nephites had learned a difficult lesson about how fragile freedom can be.
