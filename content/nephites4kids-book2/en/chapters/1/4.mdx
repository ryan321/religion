### Lesson 1.4 -- Alma Gives Up the Judgment Seat

Imagine you had the most powerful job in the whole country. Would you give it up? That's exactly what Alma did, and his reasons might surprise you.

After the wars with the Amlicites and Lamanites, the Nephite people entered a time of peace. But peace brought a new problem: **pride**. As the people became wealthier and more comfortable, many members of the Church began to turn their hearts away from God. They started wearing expensive clothing, looking down on people who were poor, and treating others badly. The Church was in serious trouble (see Alma 4:6-8).

Alma could see that the real problem wasn't political; it was **spiritual**. The people didn't need more laws or a stronger government. They needed to have their hearts changed. They needed to come back to God.

So Alma made an incredible decision. He **gave up the judgment seat**, the most powerful position in the Nephite government, and kept only his calling as the **high priest** over the Church. He chose a faithful man named **Nephihah** to take over as chief judge (see Alma 4:18-19).

Why would he do that? Because Alma understood something really important: **the word of God was more powerful than anything else**, even more powerful than the government or the sword. He believed that preaching the gospel was the best way to change people's hearts and fix the problems in the nation (see Alma 31:5).

Think about that for a moment. Alma had been a chief judge, a military leader, and a high priest all at the same time. He had led armies into battle and governed the entire nation. But he decided that the **most important thing** he could do was teach people about Jesus Christ.

This is a pattern we see throughout the scriptures. When things go wrong, the answer isn't always more rules or more punishment. Often, the real answer is helping people feel the Spirit and choose to follow God because they **want** to, not because they're forced to.

Alma spent the rest of his life traveling from city to city, preaching repentance, and calling people back to the covenant. He left behind power, comfort, and prestige because he knew that saving souls was more important than ruling a nation.

Alma's choice reminds us that **testimony and teaching** can do more to change the world than any government or army ever could. When we share the gospel with others, we're doing some of the most important work there is.
