### Lesson 10.3 -- The War Ends

After years of blood and sacrifice, the long Nephite-Lamanite war was finally about to end. But first, there was one more battle to fight -- and it wasn't against the Lamanites.

When Captain Moroni received Pahoran's letter explaining that rebels had overthrown the government, he didn't waste any time. He gathered a portion of his army and **marched toward Zarahemla** -- not as a threat this time, but as an ally. Along the way, he raised a flag of liberty and called for volunteers. Thousands of men joined him, rallying to the cause of freedom.

Moroni and Pahoran **joined forces** and attacked the rebels who had taken over the capital. The king-men were defeated. Some were killed in battle, and those who were captured and refused to defend their country were **put to death** for treason. Pahoran was restored to the judgment seat, and the government was back in the hands of the people.

Now the Nephites could finally focus all their strength on ending the war with the Lamanites. With the government reunited and supplies flowing again, the Nephite armies pushed back against the Lamanite forces across the land. City by city, the Nephites reclaimed their territory. The Lamanites were driven out, and **peace was restored** to the land. The war that had lasted for many long, painful years was over at last.

With peace came the hard work of **rebuilding**. The war had left deep wounds -- destroyed cities, broken families, and weakened faith. **Helaman**, the son of Alma, took on the task of rebuilding the Church. He went among the people, re-establishing order, calling priests and teachers, and helping the Nephites return to living the gospel. Many had drifted away from God during the long years of fighting, and Helaman worked tirelessly to bring them back.

As for Captain Moroni, his work was finally done. After spending most of his adult life on the battlefield defending his people, Moroni **retired from military service**. He had given everything he had to the cause of freedom and faith. He could finally rest.

The end of this war teaches us something important. When good people are willing to **unite** and work together -- like Moroni and Pahoran did -- they can overcome even the most difficult challenges. But winning a war is only part of the battle. The harder work often comes afterward, when you have to rebuild what was broken and help people heal. That's why leaders like Helaman were just as important as warriors like Moroni.
