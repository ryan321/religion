### Lesson 11.1 -- A New Kind of Enemy

For most of the Book of Mormon so far, the Nephites' biggest threat came from the **outside**: Lamanite armies marching against their cities. But now a new and very different kind of enemy appeared, and this one came from **within**.

It started with a man named **Kishkumen**. After the long war ended and the Nephites were trying to rebuild, a power struggle broke out over who would lead the government. Kishkumen crept into the judgment seat area and **assassinated the chief judge**, stabbing him so secretly that no one even saw it happen. Kishkumen then escaped back to his supporters, and they all swore an oath to keep his crime a secret (see Helaman 1).

But the real danger wasn't just one murderer. It was what came next. A man named **Gadianton** stepped in and organized Kishkumen's followers into a formal **criminal band**. Gadianton was cunning and persuasive. He promised his followers that if they supported him and kept their secrets, he would make them powerful. He would place them in positions of authority in the government. Gadianton became the leader, and the group became known as the **Gadianton robbers** (see Helaman 2).

What made the Gadianton robbers different from the Lamanites? The Lamanites were an outside army you could see coming. The Gadianton robbers worked in **secret**. They used **hidden oaths and conspiracies** to protect each other. They murdered political opponents and then covered it up. They wormed their way into the government from the inside, pretending to be normal citizens while secretly working to gain power and wealth for themselves.

Think of it this way. Fighting the Lamanites was like dealing with a storm you can see on the horizon. You can prepare for it, build walls, and gather your army. But the Gadianton robbers were like a disease inside the body. You can't see it on the outside, but it's quietly destroying everything from within. That's what made them so dangerous.

This was the beginning of a threat that would haunt the Nephite nation for **hundreds of years**. The Gadianton robbers would grow, shrink, and grow again, and eventually, they would play a major role in the total destruction of the Nephite people. Mormon wanted us to pay very close attention to this part of the story, because he believed it carried a warning for people in every age.
