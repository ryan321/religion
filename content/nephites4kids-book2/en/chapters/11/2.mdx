### Lesson 11.2 -- How Secret Combinations Work

The Book of Mormon uses a specific term for groups like the Gadianton robbers -- it calls them **"secret combinations."** Mormon spent a lot of time explaining exactly how these groups worked, because he wanted future readers to recognize the pattern.

So how did secret combinations operate? First, members were bound together by **secret oaths**. When someone joined, they swore a solemn promise -- not to God, but to each other -- that they would never reveal the group's secrets. They also swore to protect any member who got caught committing a crime. If a member murdered someone, the others would lie for him, hide him, and make sure he escaped justice. The oath came before everything -- before honesty, before the law, before even what was right.

Second, they used **secret signs and passwords** to recognize each other. These signals let members identify fellow conspirators without outsiders knowing. A Gadianton robber could walk into a courtroom, give a secret sign to the judge, and receive a favorable ruling -- even if he was guilty. This is how they **infiltrated the government**. Over time, they placed their own people in positions as judges, governors, and leaders. From those positions of power, they could pass laws that benefited themselves and punish anyone who opposed them.

Third, they used **murder** as a political tool. If a leader stood in their way, they simply had him killed. If someone threatened to expose them, that person disappeared. Violence wasn't a last resort for them -- it was a regular method of doing business.

Mormon, the prophet who compiled the Book of Mormon, made it very clear that he was including this information **on purpose**. He wasn't just telling a story. He was issuing a **warning**. In the book of Ether, Mormon (through Moroni, his son) wrote one of the most direct warnings in all of scripture: **"Whatsoever nation shall uphold such secret combinations, to get power and gain, until they shall spread over the nation, behold, they shall be destroyed"** (Ether 8:22).

He also wrote that secret combinations are **"built up by the devil"** and that they have caused the destruction of entire civilizations -- not just the Nephites, but also the Jaredites before them (see Ether 8:25). Mormon wanted us to understand that secret combinations aren't just an ancient problem. They are a pattern the devil uses over and over, in every time period and every nation.

The lesson is clear -- when people combine in secret to gain power through wickedness, and when good people allow it to continue, the results are always catastrophic.
