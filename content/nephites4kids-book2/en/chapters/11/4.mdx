### Lesson 11.4 -- Why Mormon Included This Warning

Have you ever wondered why the Book of Mormon spends so much time talking about the Gadianton robbers? There are lots of stories Mormon could have included in his record, but he chose to give **a lot of attention** to secret combinations. Why?

The answer is that Mormon **wrote for us**. He saw our day. Through the power of God, Mormon was shown the future (our future) and he carefully chose which stories and warnings to include based on what **we** would need to hear. He wasn't just writing a history book. He was writing a **survival guide** for people living in the last days.

Mormon made this very clear. Through his son Moroni, he wrote one of the most urgent warnings in all of scripture: **"Awake to a sense of your awful situation"** (Ether 8:24). He was speaking directly to people who would read the Book of Mormon in the future. He warned that secret combinations (groups that use secrecy, murder, and conspiracy to gain power) would exist in **every generation**, and that we must be on guard against them.

Mormon told us that secret combinations were the thing that **destroyed the Nephite nation**. Not the Lamanites. Not natural disasters. Not outside enemies. It was the corruption from within (the secret oaths, the murder for gain, the conspiracies in government) that ultimately tore the Nephites apart. And the same thing had happened to the **Jaredites** before them. Two entire civilizations, wiped out by the same pattern.

So what does this warning mean for us? Mormon wasn't saying we should be suspicious of everyone or see conspiracies everywhere. He was teaching us to **recognize the pattern**. Whenever people combine in secret to get power and money through wickedness, whenever they protect each other from justice, silence opponents, and put themselves above the law, that is a secret combination, and it leads to destruction.

Mormon also taught us the **solution**. The antidote to secret combinations is righteousness. When people choose honesty over secrecy, service over selfishness, and God over personal gain, secret combinations cannot thrive. The power of good people living good lives is stronger than any conspiracy.

The fact that Mormon dedicated so much space to this warning tells us how important he thought it was. He sacrificed other stories (other teachings, other history) to make room for this message. He wanted us to understand, beyond any doubt, that this is one of the most critical lessons the Book of Mormon has to offer. We would be wise to listen.
