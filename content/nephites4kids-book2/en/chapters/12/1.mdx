### Lesson 12.1 -- Nephi Gives Up the Judgment Seat

Does this story sound familiar? A leader of the Nephites realizes that the government has become so corrupt that the only real solution is to go out and **preach the gospel**. That's exactly what happened with Nephi, the son of Helaman, and it's the same choice his great-grandfather **Alma the Younger** had made years earlier.

Nephi had been serving as the **chief judge** over the Nephites, but things had gotten bad. Really bad. The Gadianton robbers had infiltrated the government so deeply that the judgment seats were filled with corrupt men. Righteous judges were being outvoted and pushed aside. The laws were being twisted to protect the wicked and punish the good. The whole system was broken from the inside.

Nephi could see that no amount of political effort was going to fix this problem. He could pass new laws, but the Gadianton judges would just ignore them. He could try to remove corrupt leaders, but they would be replaced with more of the same. The disease had gone too deep. The government was beyond saving through government alone (see Helaman 5:1-4).

So Nephi made a dramatic decision. He **gave up the judgment seat** and chose to devote the rest of his life to preaching the word of God. He understood what Alma had understood before him: that the **only real solution** to the Nephites' problems was a spiritual one. If the people's hearts could be changed, everything else would follow. But if their hearts stayed wicked, no law or leader could save them.

This is a powerful principle. Think about it. Could any rule at school completely stop bullying if students didn't care about being kind? Could any law stop stealing if people didn't believe it was wrong? Laws and rules are important, but they only work when people **choose in their hearts** to do what's right. That's why preaching the gospel was more important than holding political power.

Nephi took his brother **Lehi** with him (named after their great ancestor) and together they set out to teach repentance and faith in Jesus Christ. They understood that the Nephite nation didn't just need better leaders. It needed better **people**. And the only way to make better people was to bring them to Christ.
