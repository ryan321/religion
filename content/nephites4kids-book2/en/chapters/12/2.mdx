### Lesson 12.2 -- Preaching with Power

When Nephi and Lehi left the judgment seat behind and set out to preach, they didn't start small. They went straight to the people who needed it most, and the results were nothing short of **miraculous**.

Their first stop was among the Nephites in the land of **Zarahemla**, where they preached with such power and conviction that many people repented and were baptized. But what happened next was truly astonishing. Nephi and Lehi traveled into **Lamanite territory** -- the land of their enemies -- to preach the gospel there. This took incredible courage. The Lamanites had been at war with the Nephites for generations. Walking into their cities to preach was extremely dangerous.

And the Lamanites proved that danger was real. Nephi and Lehi were **captured** and thrown into **prison**. They were held for many days without food. Their captors planned to kill them. By every human measure, their mission seemed like it had ended in failure and death (see Helaman 5:14-21).

But Nephi and Lehi had something more powerful than armies or weapons -- they had the **power of God**. Their father Helaman had taught them to build their lives on the foundation of Jesus Christ, and they had taken that counsel to heart. Even in prison, facing death, they did not waver in their faith.

What happened to Nephi and Lehi in that prison is one of the most dramatic events in the entire Book of Mormon -- and we'll cover it in the next lesson. But for now, think about the faith it took just to get to this point. These two brothers gave up power, comfort, and safety. They walked into enemy territory knowing they might not come back. They sat in prison for days without food, waiting to be executed.

Why would anyone do that? Because they believed -- truly and completely -- that the gospel of Jesus Christ was more important than their own lives. They had seen what wickedness was doing to their people, and they knew that only the power of God could change hearts.

Their example teaches us that **real faith requires real sacrifice**. It's easy to believe in Jesus when everything is going well. But Nephi and Lehi believed when everything was going terribly. That kind of faith -- the kind that holds firm even in a dark prison cell -- is the kind that changes the world.

Before they were even delivered from prison, Nephi and Lehi had already baptized **eight thousand Lamanites** in and around Zarahemla. Their preaching was that powerful. And the greatest miracle was still to come.
