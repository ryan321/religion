### Lesson 12.3 -- Fire in Prison

What happened to Nephi and Lehi in that Lamanite prison is one of the most spectacular miracles in all of scripture. It's the kind of event that reminds us just how real and how powerful God truly is.

When the Lamanites came to take Nephi and Lehi out of prison to kill them, something incredible happened. Nephi and Lehi were suddenly **encircled by fire**. Not the kind of fire that burns and destroys. This fire surrounded their bodies like a protective wall, and it **did not hurt them**. The Lamanites who had come to kill them were terrified and stepped back, afraid to touch them (see Helaman 5:23-24).

Then the **earth began to shake**. The walls of the prison trembled as if the whole building might come apart. But the walls did not fall. Instead, a **thick darkness** covered the Lamanites. It was not ordinary darkness. It was a heavy, suffocating darkness that they could feel, and it filled them with a terrible fear. They could not move. They could not run. They were frozen in place.

Then, out of the darkness, came a **voice**. It was not a loud, thundering voice. It was a quiet, gentle voice, but it pierced right to the center of their souls. The voice said: **"Repent ye, repent ye, and seek no more to destroy my servants"** (Helaman 5:29). The voice came three times, and each time the earth shook again. The Lamanites were completely overcome with awe and fear.

One of the Lamanite captors, a man named **Aminadab** who was a Nephite dissenter and had once been a member of the Church. He looked through the darkness and saw that Nephi and Lehi's **faces were shining**. They were looking upward, as if talking with angels. The other Lamanites asked Aminadab what they should do. He told them to **repent and cry out to God** until the darkness lifted.

They did. They cried out to God with all their hearts, and the darkness lifted. When it did, they discovered that they too were **encircled by fire** (the same fire that had surrounded Nephi and Lehi) and it did not burn them. They were filled with a joy that cannot be described. They saw **angels coming down from heaven** to minister to them. About **three hundred people** witnessed this miracle, and every single one of them was converted to the Lord (see Helaman 5:29-52).

This event teaches us that God's power is real, and that even people who seem like enemies can have their hearts completely changed when they experience the love of God.

2. The voice from the darkness told the Lamanites to "__________ ye" and seek no more to destroy God's servants.
