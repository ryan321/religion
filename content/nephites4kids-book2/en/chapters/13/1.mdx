### Lesson 13.1 -- Preaching from the Garden Tower

Imagine praying in your backyard and suddenly a huge crowd gathers to listen. That's close to what happened to the prophet **Nephi**, the son of Helaman and grandson of Helaman the son of Alma. By this point in the Book of Mormon, Nephi had given up the judgment seat to focus entirely on preaching. The Nephites were becoming deeply wicked, and Nephi knew that only the word of God could change their hearts.

Nephi had been traveling in the land northward, preaching to people who rejected him completely. Feeling sorrowful and discouraged, he returned home to Zarahemla. When he got there, he went into his **garden** and climbed onto a **tower** near the highway that led to the chief market. There he poured out his soul in prayer to God (see Helaman 7:6-11).

Nephi wasn't trying to attract attention. He was genuinely heartbroken over how wicked his people had become. But because the tower was near a busy road, people walking by noticed him praying. Soon a large crowd gathered, curious about what was happening. When Nephi saw them, he began to speak.

And he didn't hold back. Nephi boldly **rebuked the people** for their wickedness. He told them that the Gadianton robbers had taken over their government and that the people had allowed it to happen by choosing evil. He reminded them of the prophets of old and warned that if they did not repent, they would be destroyed.

Then Nephi did something astonishing. He **prophesied** that the chief judge had been murdered **at that very moment**, while Nephi was standing on the tower speaking. He said the chief judge had been struck down by his own brother, and both of them belonged to the secret Gadianton band (see Helaman 8:27-28).

This was an incredibly bold declaration. If Nephi was wrong, he would lose all credibility. But Nephi wasn't guessing. He was speaking by the power of God. This moment shows the courage it takes to be a true prophet. Nephi didn't say things that were popular or easy. He spoke the truth even when it was dangerous, and he trusted the Lord to back up every word.

Sometimes standing up for what's right feels lonely and even scary, just like it did for Nephi on that tower. But when we speak truth with the Lord's help, we are never truly alone.
