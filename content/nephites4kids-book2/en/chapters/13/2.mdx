### Lesson 13.2 -- The Murder Revealed

When Nephi announced from his garden tower that the chief judge had been murdered, the crowd must have been stunned. Some of them probably thought he was making it up. Five men in the crowd decided to run to the judgment seat and see for themselves. They said that if Nephi was right, they would believe he was a prophet (see Helaman 9:1-2).

When these five men arrived at the judgment seat, they found the chief judge **lying dead in his own blood** -- exactly as Nephi had said. The shock was so great that all five men fell to the ground. They had doubted Nephi, but now they could see that his words were completely true (see Helaman 9:3-5).

But the story didn't end there. When other people came and found these five men near the dead judge, the judges of the land **accused them** of the murder! The five men were thrown into prison. And then -- even more unfairly -- some people turned around and accused **Nephi** of being involved. They thought maybe Nephi had paid someone to kill the judge and had "prophesied" about it to make himself look like a prophet.

Nephi was brought before the people and questioned. But instead of backing down, Nephi gave them a way to **prove** his innocence and find the real murderer. He told them to go to **Seantum**, the brother of the dead judge. He said they should ask Seantum specific questions, and they would find blood on his cloak. When confronted, Seantum would confess to the murder (see Helaman 9:26-36).

Everything happened **exactly** as Nephi described. Seantum was found, the blood was on his garment, and he confessed. He had murdered his own brother, and they were both part of the **Gadianton band**. Nephi was completely cleared.

Even after all of this, the people were divided. Some said Nephi was a prophet. Others said he must be a god. But many still hardened their hearts and refused to believe. This is one of those moments in the Book of Mormon that makes you wonder -- how much evidence do some people need?

Nephi's experience teaches us that the truth will always come out eventually. God knows all things, and He reveals them to His prophets. We can trust the words of true prophets because their words come from God, who cannot lie.
