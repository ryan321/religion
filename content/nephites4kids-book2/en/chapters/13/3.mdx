### Lesson 13.3 -- The Sealing Power

After Nephi proved his prophetic calling by revealing the murder of the chief judge, the Lord gave him something extraordinary. As Nephi walked home, feeling heavy with sorrow for the wickedness of his people, **the voice of the Lord** came to him. The Lord told Nephi that because he had been so faithful and tireless, because he had declared the word "with unwearyingness" and had not feared the people or sought his own life, he would be blessed with great power (see Helaman 10:4-5).

The Lord gave Nephi the **sealing power**. This meant that whatever Nephi **bound on earth** would be **bound in heaven**, and whatever he loosed on earth would be loosed in heaven. If Nephi said a temple would be destroyed, it would be destroyed. If he said a people would be cursed, they would be cursed. The Lord trusted Nephi completely because Nephi would never ask for anything contrary to God's will (see Helaman 10:5-7).

The Book of Mormon describes Nephi as being **"mighty in word and in deed."** He went right back to preaching after receiving this power, even though the people had just tried to accuse him of murder. That takes serious courage and faith.

But the people still would not listen. Wickedness spread so badly that there were wars and Gadianton robbers everywhere. So Nephi used his sealing power in a surprising way. He asked the Lord to send a **famine** instead of war. He figured a famine might humble the people without as much bloodshed. And the Lord granted his request. The rain stopped, the crops failed, and thousands of people began to die of hunger (see Helaman 11:4-6).

The famine worked. After several years of suffering, the people finally cried out for mercy. They **repented**, they drove out the Gadianton robbers, and they begged Nephi to ask God to end the famine. Nephi prayed, the rains came, and the land produced food again (see Helaman 11:9-17).

The sealing power shows how much the Lord trusts His most faithful servants. It also shows that God sometimes uses difficult experiences, like famine, to get people to turn back to Him. Heavenly Father doesn't enjoy watching His children suffer, but He knows that sometimes hard times are the only way people will humble themselves enough to listen.
