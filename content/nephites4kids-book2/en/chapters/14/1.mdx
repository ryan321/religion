### Lesson 14.1 -- A Lamanite Prophet

Throughout the Book of Mormon, most of the prophets who preached to the Nephites were Nephites themselves. But around 6 BC, just a few years before the birth of Jesus Christ, God did something unexpected. He sent a **Lamanite** to call the Nephites to repentance. His name was **Samuel**.

Think about how shocking this was. The Nephites had always considered themselves the "good guys," the people who kept the records, built the temples, and followed the gospel. The Lamanites were the ones who usually needed to be taught. But by this point, the Nephites had become so wicked that God sent one of their traditional enemies to preach to them. That alone tells you how far the Nephites had fallen (see Helaman 13:1-2).

Samuel came to the city of **Zarahemla** and began preaching repentance. But the people didn't want to hear it. They got angry and **cast him out** of the city. They literally wouldn't let him back in.

But Samuel didn't give up and go home. Instead, he did something bold and dramatic. He climbed up on top of the **city wall** and began preaching from there. Standing high above the people where everyone could see and hear him, Samuel delivered one of the most powerful sermons in the entire Book of Mormon (see Helaman 13:2-4).

From the wall, Samuel warned the Nephites that if they did not repent, they would be **destroyed**. He told them that their riches would become "slippery," that they would hide their treasures in the ground and then not be able to find them again. He said that the Lord's anger was kindled against them because they had cast out the prophets and rejected the truth.

Samuel's story is a powerful reminder that **God's messengers come in unexpected forms.** Sometimes the person delivering the message isn't who we would expect. The question isn't who the messenger is. It's whether the message is true. God can call anyone from anywhere to do His work. What matters is whether we are humble enough to listen, no matter who is speaking.
