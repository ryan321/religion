### Lesson 14.2 -- Signs of Christ's Birth

Standing on the wall of Zarahemla, Samuel the Lamanite didn't just warn the people about destruction. He also delivered some of the most exciting and specific **prophecies** in the entire Book of Mormon: signs that would announce the **birth of Jesus Christ**.

Samuel told the people that in **five years**, Christ would come into the world. And when He did, there would be unmistakable signs in the heavens so everyone would know it had happened (see Helaman 14:2).

The first sign would be incredible: there would be **a day and a night and a day** with no darkness at all. When the sun went down, it would not get dark. The night would be as bright as daytime. People would know that Jesus Christ had been born because there would be two days that felt like one long, continuous day (see Helaman 14:3-4).

The second sign would be a **new star** appearing in the sky, a star unlike anything they had ever seen before. This matches what we read in the New Testament about the star of Bethlehem that guided the Wise Men to the baby Jesus (see Helaman 14:5-6).

Samuel also said there would be many other signs and wonders in the heavens. These signs weren't just interesting things to look at. They had a purpose. They were meant to help people **believe** in Christ. Samuel said that these things were given "to the intent that there should be no cause for unbelief among the children of men" (Helaman 14:28).

And here's the amazing part: every single one of these prophecies was **fulfilled exactly** as Samuel described. Years later, in 3 Nephi chapter 1, the night without darkness happened just as Samuel said. The sun went down, and the sky stayed bright. The new star appeared. Every sign came to pass perfectly.

Samuel's prophecies about Christ's birth teach us something important about how God works. He doesn't leave us guessing. He sends prophets to tell us what will happen **before** it happens, so that when it does happen, we can know that God is real and that His plan is true. Prophecy is one of the ways God builds our faith, if we're willing to pay attention.

The signs of Christ's birth weren't just for the Nephites. They remind all of us that Jesus really did come to earth, and that His coming was announced and planned from the very beginning.
