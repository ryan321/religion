### Lesson 14.3 -- Signs of Christ's Death

Samuel the Lamanite didn't only prophesy about the joyful signs of Christ's birth. He also gave a solemn warning about what would happen when Jesus Christ **died** on the cross. These signs would be terrifying, a dramatic contrast to the beautiful night without darkness.

Samuel said that at the time of Christ's death, there would be **three days of darkness** across the land. No sun, no moon, no stars. Complete blackness. There would be no fire or candle that could give light. The darkness would be so thick and complete that people would know it was no ordinary event (see Helaman 14:20, 27).

But the darkness was just the beginning. Samuel also prophesied that there would be terrible **earthquakes** that would shake the whole land. Mountains would crumble, roads would be broken up, and the very surface of the earth would change. Flat ground would become rough, and smooth places would be torn apart (see Helaman 14:21-22).

Even more devastating, Samuel warned that entire **cities** would be destroyed. Some would be **sunk** into the earth. Others would be **burned** with fire. Still others would be **buried** under fallen mountains. The destruction would be so widespread and so terrible that nothing like it had ever happened before in all the land (see Helaman 14:23-24).

Why would all of this happen? Samuel explained that these signs were given to testify of Christ's death and resurrection. The destruction would come because of the wickedness of the people who rejected the prophets and killed the saints. It was a consequence of turning away from God.

Just like the signs of Christ's birth, every single one of these prophecies was later **fulfilled exactly** as described. In 3 Nephi chapter 8, we read about the terrible destruction that struck the land. Cities were burned, buried, and sunk. The earthquake was unlike anything in history. And the darkness lasted for three full days, just as Samuel had said.

These prophecies remind us that Christ's life, death, and resurrection are the most important events in all of history. They were prophesied hundreds of years in advance on the American continent, just as they were prophesied by prophets in the Old World. Everything in God's plan points to Jesus Christ.
