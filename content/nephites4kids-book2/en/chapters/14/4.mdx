### Lesson 14.4 -- Rejected by the Nephites

Samuel the Lamanite had just delivered some of the most powerful prophecies in scripture. He had warned of destruction, foretold specific signs of Christ's birth and death, and called the people to repentance. Standing on the city wall of Zarahemla, he was completely exposed and vulnerable. And the Nephites' response? They tried to **kill him**.

The people who didn't want to hear Samuel's message picked up **arrows and stones** and hurled them at him. Samuel was standing right there on the wall, an easy target. But something miraculous happened. No matter how many arrows or stones they threw, **they could not hit him**. The hand of the Lord was protecting Samuel, and nothing the people did could touch him (see Helaman 16:2).

This miracle had a real effect on some of the people. When they saw that Samuel could not be struck, **some believed**. They recognized that only God's power could protect someone like that. These believers went out and found **Nephi** and were **baptized** by him. They confessed their sins and showed real faith through their actions (see Helaman 16:3-5).

But most of the people responded very differently. When they saw they couldn't hit Samuel with stones and arrows, they didn't fall to their knees in awe. Instead, they got even angrier. They said it must be the **devil** protecting him. They shouted for others to seize Samuel and tie him up. They wanted to silence him by force (see Helaman 16:6-7).

Seeing that the people were beyond persuasion, Samuel **jumped down from the wall** and fled. He left the land of the Nephites and was **never heard from again** among them. We don't know what happened to Samuel after that. He simply disappears from the record, having faithfully delivered the message God gave him.

Samuel's story carries a powerful lesson for all of us. **God's messengers come in unexpected forms.** The Nephites expected prophets to look and sound a certain way, and a Lamanite preacher on a wall didn't fit their expectations. They rejected him not because his message was wrong, but because they didn't like who was delivering it, or what it required of them.

We should ask ourselves: are we willing to listen to truth no matter where it comes from? Or do we only accept messages from people who look and sound like us? The Lord works through all kinds of people. Our job is to have hearts soft enough to recognize truth when we hear it.
