### Lesson 15.1 -- Believers and Doubters

Five years had passed since Samuel the Lamanite stood on the wall of Zarahemla and prophesied that Christ would be born. He had given a specific timeline (five years) and now that deadline was approaching. The tension among the Nephites was incredible.

The people who **believed** Samuel's prophecy were watching the skies anxiously, waiting for the signs. They had staked their faith on his words. Every year that passed without the signs appearing tested their patience, but they held on. They remembered the promise: a day, a night, and a day without darkness, and a new star in the heavens.

But the **unbelievers** were watching too, and they were mocking. As the fifth year came and no signs had appeared yet, they began to taunt the believers. They said things like, "The time is past, and the words of Samuel are not fulfilled; therefore, your joy and your faith concerning this thing hath been vain" (3 Nephi 1:6). Basically, they were saying: "See? Nothing happened. You believed for nothing."

Then things took a dark and terrifying turn. The unbelievers didn't just mock. They **set a specific day** to kill all the believers. They said that if the signs hadn't appeared by that day, every person who believed in Samuel's prophecy would be **put to death**. This wasn't an empty threat. The unbelievers had the power and the numbers to carry it out (see 3 Nephi 1:9).

Imagine being one of the believers during this time. You've been waiting years for the signs. People around you are laughing at you and calling you foolish. And now someone has set a date to execute you if the signs don't come in time. Would you keep believing? Would you hold on to your faith even when it might cost you your life?

This moment in the Book of Mormon is one of the most dramatic tests of faith in all of scripture. The believers had to decide whether they trusted God's promise more than they feared the threats of men. It's a question each of us faces in our own way: will we hold on to what we know is true, even when the world pressures us to give up?
