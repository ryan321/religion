### Lesson 15.2 -- Nephi's Prayer

With the day of execution approaching and the lives of all the believers at stake, there was one man who refused to give up hope. **Nephi** -- the son of Nephi, who was the son of Helaman -- knew what he had to do. He went out and **bowed himself upon the earth** and cried mightily to God all day long on behalf of his people, the believers who were about to be destroyed (see 3 Nephi 1:11-12).

Think about what Nephi was facing. He was the spiritual leader of the believers. These people trusted him. They had put their faith in the prophecies that he and Samuel and other prophets had taught. And now they were about to be slaughtered unless God intervened. The weight of that responsibility must have been almost unbearable.

Nephi prayed with everything he had. He didn't pray for an hour and then go home. He prayed **all day** -- pouring out his whole soul, pleading for the lives of the faithful, begging God to send the promised signs.

And then the answer came. **The voice of the Lord** spoke to Nephi directly. The words are simple and powerful: "Lift up your head and be of good cheer; for behold, the time is at hand, and on this night shall the sign be given, and on the morrow come I into the world, to show unto the world that I will fulfil all that which I have caused to be spoken by the mouth of my holy prophets" (3 Nephi 1:13).

What an incredible moment. The Creator of the universe, on the very eve of His own birth into mortality, took the time to speak to a prophet on the other side of the world. He told Nephi not to fear -- the signs were coming **that very night**. And then He declared His purpose: He was coming into the world to fulfill everything the prophets had ever spoken.

Nephi's experience teaches us something deeply important about prayer. When things seem most hopeless -- when every reason to doubt is staring us in the face -- that is exactly when we should pray the hardest. God hears desperate prayers. He heard Nephi's prayer on the most important eve in all of history, and He answered at exactly the right moment.

Sometimes God's answers don't come when we want them to. But they always come when we need them to.
