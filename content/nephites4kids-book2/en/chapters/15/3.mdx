### Lesson 15.3 -- The Night Without Darkness

That evening, the sun set over the land just as it always did. But this time, something was different. **It didn't get dark.** The sky stayed bright. Night had come, but it looked like day. The sign that Samuel the Lamanite had prophesied five years earlier was finally happening.

The people looked up in amazement. There was no darkness at all. The night was as light as if it were the middle of the afternoon. Exactly as Samuel had foretold, the people experienced **a day and a night and a day** that felt like one continuous day. You could tell the sun had gone down, and you could tell when it came back up again, but the darkness never came (see 3 Nephi 1:15, 19).

And that wasn't all. A **new star** appeared in the sky, just as Samuel had prophesied. Along with it came many other signs and wonders in the heavens. Everything Samuel had described, every detail, was fulfilled exactly as he had said (see 3 Nephi 1:21).

The reaction among the people was dramatic. The **believers** were filled with joy. They had held on through years of waiting, months of mockery, and the terrifying threat of death. Now they could see with their own eyes that God had kept His promise. Their faith had not been in vain.

The **unbelievers**, on the other hand, were **terrified**. Many of them fell to the earth in fear. Just hours earlier, they had been planning to kill the believers. Now they realized they had been fighting against God. The signs were so clear and so undeniable that most of the doubters, at least in that moment, believed. The deadly plot was abandoned. No one was killed. The day that was supposed to be a day of destruction for the believers became a day of **salvation** (see 3 Nephi 1:15-18).

The night without darkness is one of the most beautiful moments in the Book of Mormon. It reminds us that Jesus Christ's coming into the world brought **light**, both literally and spiritually. The darkness could not overcome that light, just as the darkness in our own lives cannot overcome the light of Christ if we hold on to our faith.

The believers who waited and trusted were rewarded. The signs came not a moment too soon, but right on time. That is how God works. He is never late, even when it feels like He might be.
