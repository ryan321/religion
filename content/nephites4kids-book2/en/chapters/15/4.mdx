### Lesson 15.4 -- Looking Ahead

The signs of Christ's birth had been given. The night without darkness, the new star, and the many wonders in the heavens all proved that Samuel the Lamanite was a true prophet. His words had been fulfilled down to the last detail. For a time, the people were humbled and they believed.

But sadly, this belief didn't last. Within just a few years, people started coming up with explanations for the signs. Some said it was all just a coincidence. Others said it was some kind of trick. **Satan** sent forth rumors and lies to harden people's hearts, and many Nephites began to doubt what they had seen with their own eyes (see 3 Nephi 1:22, 2:1-3).

Young people who had been children at the time of the signs grew up without the same deep impression. A new generation arose that didn't remember the fear and the wonder of that night. They became hardened and wicked, and within about **thirty years**, the Nephite nation would face the greatest destruction in its entire history -- the catastrophic events at Christ's death that Samuel had also prophesied.

But here is what makes this moment in the Book of Mormon so remarkable. The greatest destruction in Nephite history would be followed by the **greatest blessing** in Nephite history. After the earthquakes, the fires, the buried cities, and the three days of darkness, the survivors would hear a voice from heaven. And then **Jesus Christ Himself** would descend from the sky and visit the people at the temple in Bountiful.

Everything in the Book of Mormon has been building toward this moment. All the prophecies from Nephi, King Benjamin, Abinadi, Alma, Samuel, and dozens of others pointed to one thing: **Christ would come**. The stage is now set for the climax of the entire Book of Mormon -- the personal visit of the resurrected Savior to the people in the Americas.

The story of the signs being fulfilled teaches us that God keeps every promise. Not some of them -- all of them. If He said the signs would come, they came. If He said Christ would visit His people, He would visit them. And the promises He has made to us -- about forgiveness, about eternal life, about His love for each of us -- He will keep those too.

As we look ahead to the coming of Christ in 3 Nephi, we can feel the same excitement the faithful Nephites must have felt. The best part of the story is just ahead.
