### Lesson 2.1 -- The Wicked City

After giving up the judgment seat, Alma went from city to city preaching the gospel. Some cities received him with open arms. But one city did not -- and that city was **Ammonihah**.

The people of Ammonihah had turned completely away from God. Many of them followed the false teachings of **Nehor** -- the same man who had taught that priests should be popular and paid and that everyone would be saved no matter what. These people didn't want to hear from Alma, and they definitely didn't want to repent.

When Alma arrived in Ammonihah, the people **rejected** him. They insulted him, spit on him, and threw him out of the city. Can you imagine how discouraging that must have been? Here was a man who had given up the most powerful position in the land so he could preach the gospel, and the people literally kicked him out (see Alma 8:13).

Alma left the city feeling weighed down with sorrow. He was so heartbroken over the wickedness of the people that he could hardly bear it. But as he was walking away, something amazing happened -- an **angel** appeared to him. And it wasn't just any angel. It was the same angel who had appeared to Alma years before when he was young and rebellious (see Alma 8:14-15).

The angel told Alma to **go back** to Ammonihah. That must have been hard to hear! The people had just rejected and humiliated him, and now he was being told to return. But Alma obeyed immediately. He turned around and headed straight back to the city (see Alma 8:18).

When Alma arrived, he was hungry from his journey. He asked a man if he would give him something to eat. That man was **Amulek**, and he told Alma something remarkable -- an angel had visited him too and told him that a **prophet of God** would come who he should receive into his home. Amulek fed Alma and took care of him for many days (see Alma 8:19-22).

This story shows us that the Lord doesn't always remove our challenges -- sometimes He sends us right back into them. But He also prepares help along the way. Alma didn't have to face Ammonihah alone. God had already prepared Amulek to stand beside him. When you face hard things, remember that God knows what you're going through and has a plan to help you.
