### Lesson 2.3 -- The Believers Are Burned

This is one of the hardest stories in the entire Book of Mormon. It's difficult to read, but it teaches some of the most important lessons about faith, agency, and God's justice.

After Alma and Amulek began preaching in Ammonihah, some of the people believed their message. They repented and turned their hearts to God. But the wicked leaders of the city were furious. They didn't want anyone believing in the gospel, and they decided to make an example of those who did.

The wicked people gathered up the **believers** (men, women, and children who had accepted the gospel). They also gathered their **scriptures** and threw them all into a great fire, **burning them alive** (see Alma 14:8). This is one of the most heartbreaking events in all of scripture. Innocent people were killed simply because they believed in God.

Amulek was forced to watch this happen. His heart was breaking. He turned to Alma and said they should use the **power of God** to stop it and stretch out their hands and save those innocent people. Amulek knew they had the priesthood power to do it. So why didn't they? (see Alma 14:10).

Alma's answer is one of the most important teachings in the Book of Mormon. He told Amulek that the **Spirit** constrained him from stopping it. He said, **"The Lord receiveth them up unto himself, in glory"** (Alma 14:11). In other words, those faithful people who died were received by God into heaven. Their suffering was real, but it was temporary. God would reward them for their faithfulness.

Alma also taught that the Lord allowed it to happen so that the **judgments of God** would come upon the wicked people **justly**. The wicked had to be given the chance to show what was in their hearts. By choosing to do such terrible things, they brought God's judgment upon themselves (see Alma 14:11).

This story is hard because it shows us that God doesn't always stop bad things from happening to good people. But it also teaches us that He **sees everything** and that perfect justice will come. Those faithful believers were not forgotten. They were received into glory.

For us, this story is a reminder that living the gospel sometimes comes with a cost. But God honors every sacrifice we make for Him, and nothing we give up for the truth is ever truly lost.
