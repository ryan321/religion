### Lesson 2.4 -- Delivered from Prison

After the terrible burning of the believers, Alma and Amulek were **thrown into prison** by the wicked leaders of Ammonihah. But this was not an ordinary prison stay. The leaders wanted to break them -- physically and spiritually.

For many days, Alma and Amulek were **beaten, starved, and mocked**. Lawyers, judges, priests, and teachers came to taunt them. They spit on them, hit them, and asked cruel questions like, "Why don't you save yourselves if you have so much power?" They were stripped of their clothing and bound with strong cords. Day after day, the abuse continued (see Alma 14:17-22).

Can you imagine how hard that must have been? They were hungry, in pain, and surrounded by people who hated them. But Alma and Amulek held on to their faith. They didn't give up, and they didn't stop trusting in God.

Then came the moment of deliverance. After many days, Alma stood up and cried out to the Lord, saying, **"How long shall we suffer these great afflictions, O Lord? Give us strength according to our faith"** (see Alma 14:26). As soon as he said these words, something incredible happened.

The **power of God** came upon Alma and Amulek. They broke the cords that bound them and stood up. The people in the prison saw this and were terrified. They tried to run, but the earth began to shake. The **walls of the prison fell to the ground**, crushing the wicked men who had been tormenting them. Alma and Amulek walked out of the rubble completely unharmed (see Alma 14:26-28).

When the people of the city came to see what had happened, they found Alma and Amulek standing free among the ruins. The people were so afraid that they **fled from Alma and Amulek** as fast as they could. Alma and Amulek walked out of Ammonihah and went to the land of Sidom, where they healed and recovered (see Alma 14:29, 15:1).

But the story of Ammonihah doesn't end there. Some time later, a Lamanite army attacked the city and **destroyed it in a single day**. The great city that had rejected God's prophets and burned innocent believers was wiped out completely (see Alma 16:2-3). God's justice, though sometimes slow, is always sure.

This story teaches us that God delivers His faithful servants in His own time and way. Alma and Amulek didn't know when or how they would be saved, but they trusted the Lord -- and He came through for them in a way no one could have imagined.
