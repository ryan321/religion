### Lesson 3.1 -- Sons of Mosiah's Success

Do you remember the sons of Mosiah? They were the young men who, along with Alma the Younger, had once gone around trying to destroy the Church. But after they were visited by an angel and experienced mighty changes of heart, they dedicated their lives to the gospel. And they made an extraordinary choice: they gave up their right to the throne and went on a **mission to the Lamanites** (see Alma 17:6-9).

This wasn't a short trip. The sons of Mosiah (**Ammon, Aaron, Omner, and Himni**) spent **fourteen years** living among the Lamanites and teaching them the gospel. Fourteen years! Think about that. These young men gave up their comfortable lives as princes to live among a people who hated the Nephites and might kill them at any moment.

Their mission was incredibly difficult. They were thrown into prison, mocked, beaten, and suffered hunger and thirst. But they never gave up. They prayed constantly and fasted often, and the Lord blessed them with the **power of the Spirit** to teach with authority (see Alma 17:9-11).

And the results were astonishing. **Thousands of Lamanites** were converted to the gospel. One of the most amazing conversion stories was that of **King Lamoni**. Ammon went to serve Lamoni and ended up defending the king's flocks against robbers. When Lamoni saw Ammon's incredible courage and faithfulness, his heart was opened. Ammon taught him the gospel, and Lamoni was so overcome by the Spirit that he fell to the earth. When he arose, he was a changed man and declared his belief in God (see Alma 17-19).

But it didn't stop there. Aaron taught Lamoni's father, who was the **king over all the Lamanites**. This great king was also converted and declared to his people that they should listen to the missionaries. When the most powerful man in the land accepted the gospel, it opened the door for thousands more (see Alma 22).

The sons of Mosiah's mission is one of the greatest missionary stories in all of scripture. They proved that even the hardest hearts can be softened by the power of God and that **no one is beyond the reach of the Atonement** of Jesus Christ. Their fourteen years of sacrifice changed the course of history for both the Nephites and the Lamanites.
