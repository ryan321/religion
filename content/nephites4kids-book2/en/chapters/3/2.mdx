### Lesson 3.2 -- Burying Their Weapons

When the Lamanite converts received the gospel, their change of heart was so deep and so real that they wanted to make sure they could **never go back** to their old, violent ways. What they did next is one of the most powerful acts of faith in the entire Book of Mormon.

These converted Lamanites had spent their lives fighting, shedding blood, and committing terrible sins. Now that they understood the Atonement of Jesus Christ and had been forgiven, they were terrified of ever falling back into sin. They didn't just want to stop fighting. They wanted to make it **impossible** to go back.

So they made a **covenant** with God. They gathered all their weapons of war (their swords, their spears, every instrument of battle) and **buried them deep in the earth**. They promised the Lord that they would **never shed blood again**, no matter what happened. This wasn't just putting their weapons in a closet. They buried them as a testimony to God that they were done with violence forever (see Alma 24:15-18).

It didn't take long for their commitment to be tested. When the other Lamanites (the ones who had not been converted) heard about this, they were angry. They gathered a massive army and came to **attack** the believers.

What did the converted Lamanites do? They didn't dig up their weapons. They didn't run. Instead, they went out to meet their attackers and **lay down on the ground**. They prostrated themselves before their enemies and began praying to God. They chose to die rather than break their covenant (see Alma 24:21-22).

The attacking Lamanites began killing them. **Over one thousand** of the believers were slain without any resistance. But then something unexpected happened. Many of the attacking Lamanites were so moved by the courage and faith of these people that their own hearts were changed. They **threw down their weapons** and refused to keep fighting. In fact, **more Lamanites were converted** that day than the number who had been killed (see Alma 24:23-27).

This story shows us what **true conversion** looks like. These people were so changed by the gospel that they would rather die than sin again. Their faith was not just words; it was something they were willing to give their lives for. That kind of commitment is rare and powerful, and it teaches us to take our own covenants seriously.
