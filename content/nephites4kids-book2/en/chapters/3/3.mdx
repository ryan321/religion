### Lesson 3.3 -- The People of Ammon

The converted Lamanites who had buried their weapons were in a difficult position. They had made a covenant never to fight again, but they were living in Lamanite territory surrounded by enemies who wanted to destroy them. They needed a safe place to live, but where could they go?

The answer came through the sons of Mosiah and Alma. They brought the converted Lamanites north into **Nephite territory**. But this created a new question: would the Nephites welcome a group of Lamanites into their land? After all, the Nephites and Lamanites had been enemies for hundreds of years.

The Nephites did something truly remarkable. They met together and decided to give the converted Lamanites the **land of Jershon** as their new home. This was real, valuable land, not some empty wilderness. The Nephites gave it freely and generously (see Alma 27:22-24).

But there was more. The Nephites knew that the converted Lamanites couldn't defend themselves because of their covenant. So the Nephites **promised to protect them**. Nephite armies would stand guard around Jershon and fight any Lamanite army that came to attack. The only thing the people of Jershon were asked to do was help support the Nephite armies with supplies and provisions (see Alma 27:23-24).

From that point on, these converted Lamanites became known as the **People of Ammon**, named after Ammon, the son of Mosiah who had first come to teach them the gospel. They also came to be called the **Ammonites** (see Alma 27:26).

The People of Ammon settled in Jershon and became some of the most faithful people in the entire Book of Mormon. They **never broke their covenant**. Even when terrible wars came and the Nephite nation was in danger, the People of Ammon kept their promise to God. They did not pick up their weapons. They supported the war effort with food and supplies, but they did not fight (see Alma 27:28-29).

This story teaches us several beautiful lessons. First, it shows us the power of **keeping covenants**. The People of Ammon made a promise to God, and they kept it no matter what. Second, it shows us the importance of **helping others**. The Nephites could have turned the Ammonites away, but instead they welcomed them and promised to defend them. And third, it shows us that there is always a place in God's kingdom for people who are willing to change, no matter where they came from.
