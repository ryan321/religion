### Lesson 3.4 -- Why This Story Matters

We've spent this whole chapter learning about the Anti-Nephi-Lehies, the Lamanite converts who became the People of Ammon. Now let's talk about why this story is so important for you and me today.

The People of Ammon show us what **true conversion** really means. True conversion isn't just saying you believe in Jesus Christ. It isn't just going to church on Sunday or knowing the right answers in Primary. True conversion means a **complete change of heart**, becoming a totally different person from the inside out.

Think about how dramatic the change was for these people. Before their conversion, they were warriors. Fighting and violence were a way of life for them. After their conversion, they would rather **die** than pick up a weapon and sin again. That is an incredible transformation. It didn't happen because someone forced them to change. It happened because the Atonement of Jesus Christ worked a mighty miracle in their hearts (see Alma 24:10-13).

Their story also teaches us about the power of **covenants**. A covenant is a sacred promise between you and God. When you make a covenant (like when you're baptized) you are promising God that you will live a certain way. The People of Ammon took their covenant so seriously that they were willing to give their lives rather than break it. That kind of commitment is what Heavenly Father hopes for from each of us.

Now, God probably won't ask you to bury a sword in the ground or lay down in front of an army. But He does ask you to make and keep promises. Every time you take the **sacrament**, you are renewing your baptismal covenant. You are promising to always remember Jesus Christ and keep His commandments. The People of Ammon remind us that those promises should mean something deep and real to us.

There's one more lesson from this story. The People of Ammon show us that **no one is too far gone** for the gospel. These were people who had lived in darkness and violence for generations. If the Atonement could change them so completely, it can change anyone, including you and me. No matter what mistakes we've made, the Savior's power is strong enough to make us new.

The story of the People of Ammon is really the story of what the gospel of Jesus Christ can do in a person's life. It can take someone who is lost and make them found, someone who is broken and make them whole. That's the message of this chapter, and it's a message for all of us.
