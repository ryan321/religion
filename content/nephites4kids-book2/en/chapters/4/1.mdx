### Lesson 4.1 -- Korihor the Anti-Christ

Have you ever met someone who is really good at arguing? Someone who sounds smart and confident, even when they're wrong? That's what the Nephites were dealing with when a man named **Korihor** showed up.

Korihor traveled around preaching against the Church. He taught that there was **no God**, no Christ would ever come, there was no such thing as sin, and there was no life after death. He said the Church leaders were foolish and that religious people were just following silly traditions. He told people they could do whatever they wanted because nothing they did really mattered.

What made Korihor dangerous wasn't just what he said; it was **how** he said it. He was smooth and persuasive. He used clever-sounding arguments that made people feel smart for agreeing with him. He didn't just share his own opinions quietly. He actively tried to pull people away from God. The Book of Mormon calls him an **anti-Christ** because he specifically fought against the teachings of Jesus Christ.

Korihor traveled through several Nephite cities, and in some places he had real success. People listened. People left the Church. Eventually he came to the land of Zarahemla, and then to the land of Gideon, where he was arrested and brought before the chief judge and the high priest.

Finally, Korihor was brought before **Alma**, who was both the chief judge and the high priest over the whole land. Alma bore his testimony and reminded Korihor of all the evidence of God: the scriptures, the prophets, the earth, the stars, the motion of the planets. Everything testified that there was a God.

But Korihor kept demanding a **sign**. He said he wouldn't believe unless God gave him proof. So God gave him a sign, but not the kind he wanted. Korihor was **struck dumb**. He could not speak. He finally admitted in writing that he had always known there was a God, but that the devil had appeared to him as an angel and taught him what to say. He had been deceived, and then he had deceived others.

Korihor begged for his voice back, but Alma said that if it were restored, Korihor would just go back to leading people astray. The curse stayed. Korihor went from place to place begging for food, and eventually he was **trampled to death** among a group of people called the Zoramites (Alma 30).

The lesson the Book of Mormon draws from this is simple and direct: "Thus we see that the devil will not support his children at the last day, but doth speedily drag them down to hell."
