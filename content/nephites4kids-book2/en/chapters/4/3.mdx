### Lesson 4.3 -- Alma's Mission to the Zoramites

When Alma and his companions began preaching among the Zoramites, they quickly discovered something interesting. The **wealthy Zoramites** wanted nothing to do with them. These were the people who climbed the Rameumptom every week, wore fine clothing, and looked down on everyone else. They were comfortable and proud, and they had no interest in changing.

But there was another group: the **poor Zoramites**. These people had been cast out of the synagogues because they weren't rich enough. They couldn't even worship in the same buildings as the wealthy. They were looked down on, rejected, and forgotten. And because of their suffering, their hearts were humble. They were ready to listen.

One of the poor Zoramites approached Alma and asked an important question: if they weren't allowed in the synagogues, how could they worship God? Alma told them something powerful: you don't need a fancy building to worship. You can pray anywhere, anytime. God cares about your heart, not your building.

Then Alma taught one of the most famous lessons in all of scripture: **faith is like a seed** (Alma 32:28-43).

He told the people to imagine planting a seed in their hearts. The seed represents the word of God. You don't have to know everything at first. You just have to be willing to try. Plant the word. Give it a chance. If it's a good seed, it will begin to grow. You'll feel it swelling inside you. Your mind will start to expand. It will feel good and right.

But Alma warned that planting the seed wasn't enough. You have to **nourish** it. You have to keep feeding it with faith, patience, and diligence. If you neglect it, it will wither and die, not because the seed was bad, but because you didn't take care of it.

This is one of the most hopeful teachings in the Book of Mormon. You don't need to have perfect knowledge before you start. You don't need to understand everything. You just need to be willing to plant the seed and see what grows. Let the experiment work. Pay attention to how you feel. Watch the fruit it produces in your life.

The poor Zoramites understood this because they had already learned that wealth and pride didn't bring happiness. They were ready to try something real. Many of them believed and were baptized.
