### Lesson 4.4 -- Amulek on the Atonement

After Alma taught the Zoramites about faith being like a seed, his missionary companion **Amulek** stood up and delivered one of the most important sermons in the Book of Mormon. He taught about the **Atonement of Jesus Christ**, the sacrifice that would make everything else possible.

Amulek explained that throughout history, people had offered animal sacrifices. These sacrifices pointed forward to something greater: a final, complete sacrifice that would be made by the Son of God himself. Amulek called this an **"infinite and eternal sacrifice"** (Alma 34:10). It had to be infinite because no ordinary offering could pay for the sins of the whole world. Only someone with infinite power, the Son of God, could do that.

This teaching mattered because the Zoramites had rejected Christ. They had declared on the Rameumptom that no Christ would come. Amulek was telling them directly: Christ will come, and his sacrifice is the most important event in all of history. Without it, everyone would be lost forever.

But Amulek didn't stop with explaining the Atonement. He also delivered an urgent warning: **"Now is the time and the day of your salvation"** (Alma 34:31). He told the people not to put off repenting. Don't wait. Don't say, "I'll change later." Don't think you have forever to get around to it. This life is the time to prepare to meet God (Alma 34:32).

Amulek taught that after this life, it becomes much harder to change. The same spirit that possesses your body when you leave this life will have power over you in the next world. If you've spent your whole life ignoring God, you can't just flip a switch after you die. Now is the time to act.

The reaction to this preaching was split right down the middle. The **poor Zoramites** who had already shown faith through Alma's teachings believed Amulek too. They felt the truth of what he said about Christ's sacrifice. But the **wealthy Zoramites** refused to listen. Their pride was too strong. They didn't want a Savior because they already thought they were good enough on their own.

This division had real consequences. The believing Zoramites were cast out of the land by the unbelievers. They lost their homes and had to start over among the people of Ammon. But they had gained something the wealthy Zoramites never had: faith in a Savior who could actually save them.
