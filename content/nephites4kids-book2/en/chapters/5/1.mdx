### Lesson 5.1 -- Alma to Helaman: His Conversion Story

As Alma grew older, he knew it was time to pass on the sacred records and share the most important lessons of his life with his sons. He started with his oldest son, **Helaman**, and he began with something deeply personal -- the full story of his own conversion.

Alma told Helaman about his younger days when he had been wicked. He had traveled around trying to destroy the Church of God. He had led many people away from the truth. Then came the moment that changed everything: **an angel appeared** and spoke with a voice of thunder. Alma fell to the ground and for three days and three nights, he could not move or speak. He was racked with **torment** (Alma 36).

During those three days, Alma experienced something terrible. He remembered every sin. He saw how much damage he had done. He felt the weight of his rebellion against God, and it was agonizing. He said the pain was so intense that he wished he could "become extinct" -- just disappear so he wouldn't have to face God.

But then, in the middle of all that suffering, Alma remembered something his father had taught about **Jesus Christ** -- that Christ would come to atone for the sins of the world. In his darkest moment, Alma cried out in his heart: "O Jesus, thou Son of God, have mercy on me."

And everything changed.

The moment Alma called on Christ, the pain vanished. Instead of torment, he felt **joy**. Instead of darkness, he saw light. He said his joy was as intense as his pain had been. He saw a vision of God sitting on his throne, and he wanted nothing more than to be there with him.

Alma shared this story with Helaman not to brag or to shock him, but to teach him the most important truth he knew: **Jesus Christ can save anyone**. If someone as rebellious as Alma had been could be forgiven and transformed, then the power of the Atonement has no limits.

Alma wanted Helaman to understand that the records he was about to receive were sacred because they testified of this very thing -- that God is real, Christ is the Savior, and repentance is always possible.
