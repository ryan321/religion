### Lesson 5.3 -- Alma to Shiblon

After his long, detailed counsel to Helaman, Alma turned to his second son, **Shiblon**. And something interesting happened -- his message was much shorter. Not because he loved Shiblon less, but because Shiblon didn't need a long lecture. He was already doing things right (Alma 38).

Alma told Shiblon that he had great joy in him. Shiblon had gone on a mission to the Zoramites alongside his father and brothers. During that mission, he had suffered real hardship -- he had been **bound, stoned, and thrown into prison**. Through all of that, Shiblon had remained **patient and faithful**. He didn't complain. He didn't give up. He just kept going.

This is what made Shiblon different from his brothers. He wasn't flashy. He didn't have a dramatic conversion story like his father. He wasn't causing serious problems like his younger brother Corianton. Shiblon was simply **steady**. And Alma wanted him to know that being steady matters.

Alma counseled Shiblon to be **diligent** and **temperate** -- meaning balanced, not going to extremes. He told him to be humble, not to boast about his own wisdom. He said to be bold but not overbearing, and to control his passions so that he could be filled with love. It was practical, straightforward advice for someone who was already on the right track.

One of the most important things about Alma's message to Shiblon is what it teaches us about parenting and about life in general: **not every child needs a dramatic intervention**. Some kids are struggling and need urgent help. Others are quietly doing their best and just need encouragement. Shiblon was the second type, and Alma was wise enough to recognize that.

It can be easy to overlook the Shiblons of the world. They don't demand attention. They don't cause crises. They just show up and do what's right, day after day. But that kind of faithful consistency is exactly what God asks of us. You don't have to have a spectacular story to be a good person. Sometimes the most powerful thing you can do is simply be reliable.

Alma's brief but loving counsel to Shiblon reminds us that quiet faithfulness is one of the greatest strengths a person can have.
