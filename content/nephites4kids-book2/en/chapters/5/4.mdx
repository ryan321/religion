### Lesson 5.4 -- Alma to Corianton

Alma's conversation with his youngest son, **Corianton**, was very different from his talks with Helaman and Shiblon. Corianton had made serious mistakes, and Alma did not sugarcoat them. But he also didn't give up on his son. He spoke with honesty and love, showing Corianton the way back (Alma 39-42).

Corianton had been called to serve a mission among the Zoramites, but during his mission he had abandoned his work and committed **serious sexual sin** with a woman named Isabel. Alma told Corianton directly that this was a grievous sin -- one of the most serious in God's eyes. He explained that sexual sin is so serious because it involves sacred, creative powers that God has given us. Misusing those powers causes deep harm to ourselves and others.

Alma didn't just condemn Corianton's actions. He also explained **why** the consequences mattered. When a missionary goes astray, the people they were supposed to teach see it and lose faith. Corianton's bad example had caused real damage to the work he was supposed to be doing.

But then Alma turned to hope. He spent several chapters teaching Corianton about the **resurrection**, the **spirit world**, and the principles of **justice and mercy**. He explained that after death, the spirits of all people go to either a state of happiness or a state of misery, depending on how they lived. At the resurrection, body and spirit are reunited, and everyone stands before God.

Corianton seemed worried that God's justice was unfair -- that punishing sinners was too harsh. Alma explained that justice and mercy work together. Justice requires a punishment for every sin. But because of Jesus Christ's Atonement, **mercy** can step in for those who repent. Christ paid the price that justice demanded, so those who turn to him can be forgiven. Without the Atonement, mercy couldn't exist. Without justice, mercy would have no meaning.

Alma's message to Corianton is powerful because it's completely honest. He didn't pretend the sin wasn't serious. He didn't lower the standard. But he also didn't slam the door shut. He showed his son exactly what had gone wrong, exactly why it mattered, and exactly how to come back through faith in Christ, repentance, and the merciful plan of God.

Corianton listened. He repented and eventually returned to his missionary work.
