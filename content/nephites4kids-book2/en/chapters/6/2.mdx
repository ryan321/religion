### Lesson 6.2 -- The Title of Liberty

One of the most dramatic moments in Captain Moroni's story happened not on a battlefield but in a city square. A man named **Amalickiah** was causing serious trouble among the Nephites. He was cunning, ambitious, and had one goal: **he wanted to be king**.

Amalickiah gathered followers by flattering them with promises of power. He told certain leaders that if they supported him, he would make them rulers over the people. Many Nephites, especially those who were proud and hungry for authority, started following him. It was a genuine threat to the Nephite government and to the people's freedom.

When Captain Moroni heard what was happening, he was furious, not because someone disagreed with him, but because Amalickiah was trying to destroy the liberty that so many people had fought and died to protect. Moroni knew he had to act fast before Amalickiah gained too much support.

So Moroni did something bold. He **tore a piece from his own coat** and wrote on it with powerful words: **"In memory of our God, our religion, and freedom, and our peace, our wives, and our children"** (Alma 46:12). He fastened this cloth to a pole and called it the **Title of Liberty**.

Then Moroni put on his armor, knelt down, and prayed. He asked God to bless the land and protect the cause of freedom. After praying, he went out among the people carrying the Title of Liberty and calling on everyone who believed in God and wanted to keep their freedom to come forward and make a covenant to defend it.

The response was enormous. People came **running** from every direction, tearing their own garments as a symbol of their covenant with God. They promised they would not forsake the Lord. Amalickiah saw that he was vastly outnumbered and fled with a small group of followers to join the Lamanites, where he would eventually scheme his way into becoming their king instead.

The Title of Liberty is one of the most powerful symbols in the Book of Mormon. It wasn't a fancy banner made by professionals. It was a torn piece of clothing written on by a man who loved his people. Moroni's message was simple: some things are worth fighting for: God, family, freedom, and peace. When those are threatened, good people need to stand up.
