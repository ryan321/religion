### Lesson 6.3 -- Fortifying the Cities

After Amalickiah fled to the Lamanites, Captain Moroni knew that the threat wasn't over. It was just beginning. Amalickiah would return with a Lamanite army, and the Nephites needed to be ready. So instead of waiting for the next attack, Moroni did something smart: he started **preparing** (Alma 49-50).

Moroni commanded his people to build **fortifications** around their cities. They dug deep **ditches** around the outside. They piled up the dirt from those ditches to form tall **walls of earth**. On top of the walls, they placed **timbers and pickets** (sharpened logs that made it nearly impossible for anyone to climb over). They built **towers** above the walls so defenders could see the enemy coming and throw stones and arrows down on them.

When the Lamanite armies finally came, they were stunned. Cities that had once been easy targets were now **impossible to penetrate**. The Lamanites would march up to a city expecting a quick victory, only to find walls, ditches, towers, and well-armed Nephites waiting for them. In several battles, the Lamanites gave up without even trying because the fortifications were so strong.

This frustrated the Lamanite commanders enormously. They would retreat from one fortified city and head to another, only to find that it was fortified too. Moroni had prepared **every major city**, not just one or two. He thought ahead, planned carefully, and worked hard to make sure his people were protected.

The Book of Mormon makes an important observation during this time of preparation. It says that the Nephites were in a time of peace and happiness, not because there were no enemies, but because they were **prepared** and living righteously. Their readiness gave them confidence and security.

The lesson here goes far beyond military strategy. Captain Moroni teaches us that **preparation is just as important as courage**. Being brave in the moment matters, but doing the hard work before the challenge arrives is what makes the difference between victory and defeat.

Think about your own life. Studying before a test, practicing before a game, praying before a hard day. These are all ways of building your own walls and towers. When the challenge comes, you'll be ready, just like the Nephites were.

Moroni didn't just hope his people would be safe. He made sure of it through careful, dedicated preparation.
