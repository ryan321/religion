### Lesson 7.2 -- How He Became Lamanite King

When Amalickiah arrived in the land of the Lamanites, he did not come as a humble refugee. He came with a plan. Step by step, through lies, poison, and murder, he worked his way to the very top of the Lamanite nation. It was one of the most calculated acts of treachery in the entire Book of Mormon.

First, Amalickiah needed to get close to the Lamanite army. The Lamanite king had ordered his people to go to war against the Nephites, but many Lamanites were afraid and did not want to fight. The king put a commander named Lehonti in charge of the reluctant soldiers, and Lehonti gathered his forces on top of a mountain, refusing to come down.

Amalickiah saw his chance. He marched to the base of that mountain and sent messages to Lehonti, asking him to come down and meet. Lehonti refused twice. On the third try, Amalickiah asked Lehonti to come just partway down the mountain, promising that he would bring his own soldiers. Lehonti finally agreed.

When they met, Amalickiah offered a deal. He said he would hand over his entire army to Lehonti if Lehonti would just make him second in command. It seemed like an amazing offer. Lehonti would get a bigger army and total control. Lehonti accepted.

But Amalickiah had no intention of staying second. He arranged for one of his servants to slowly poison Lehonti. Day after day, Lehonti grew sicker and sicker until he died. And just like that, Amalickiah became the commander of the entire Lamanite army. Nobody suspected a thing.

Now Amalickiah marched his army to the Lamanite capital. When the Lamanite king came out to greet him, Amalickiah's servants bowed before the king as if to show respect, and then they stabbed him. The king's own servants fled in terror, and Amalickiah blamed them for the murder. He pretended to be outraged and sent his armies to chase down the innocent servants.

With the king dead and everyone believing his lies, Amalickiah married the Lamanite queen and took the throne for himself. In just a short time, a Nephite traitor had become king of the entire Lamanite nation. Every step was planned. Every move was deliberate. Alma chapter 47 records this chilling rise to power as a reminder of what evil can accomplish when good people are not watchful.
