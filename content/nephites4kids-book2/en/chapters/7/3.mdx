### Lesson 7.3 -- War Against the Nephites

Now that Amalickiah sat on the Lamanite throne, he turned his attention to the goal he had wanted all along -- destroying the Nephites. He had tried once to conquer them through politics, and he had failed. This time he would use the full power of the Lamanite armies.

Amalickiah began by stirring up the Lamanites with anger and hatred. He sent messengers throughout the land spreading propaganda about how terrible the Nephites were. He twisted the truth and told lies until the Lamanite people burned with a desire for war. It was not hard to convince them. The Lamanites already had old grudges against the Nephites, and Amalickiah knew exactly how to fan those flames.

What followed were some of the bloodiest wars in the entire Book of Mormon. The battles described in Alma chapters 48 through 51 are fierce, long, and devastating. Cities were attacked. Fortifications were tested. Soldiers on both sides suffered terribly. These were not quick skirmishes -- they were grinding, relentless campaigns that dragged on and took a heavy toll.

One of the saddest parts of these wars is that the worst fighters on the Lamanite side were often Nephite dissenters -- people who had once been part of the Nephite nation and had left to join the enemy. These dissenters were more vicious and more determined than ordinary Lamanite soldiers. They knew how the Nephites defended themselves. They knew the layout of Nephite cities. And they fought with a hatred that came from betrayal rather than simple rivalry.

Mormon, who compiled these records centuries later, made sure to point that out. He wanted future readers to understand that when people turn away from the truth they once knew, they can become more dangerous than those who never knew it at all.

Meanwhile, the Nephites were not only fighting enemies outside their borders. They had problems at home too. A group called the king-men refused to help defend the nation because they wanted to change the government. Captain Moroni had to deal with traitors inside his own country while armies attacked from outside. It was one of the most difficult periods in Nephite history.

Through it all, Moroni and his faithful captains held on. They trusted God, prepared their people, and fought with everything they had. But the cost was enormous, and the wars Amalickiah started would continue for many years.
