### Lesson 9.1 -- The People of Ammon's Dilemma

The people of Ammon had a problem, and it was breaking their hearts. Years earlier, they had been Lamanites who converted to the gospel through the missionary work of Ammon, Aaron, and their brothers. Before their conversion, many of them had been warriors who shed a great deal of blood. When they accepted the gospel, they were so horrified by their past violence that they made a sacred covenant: they buried their weapons in the ground and promised God they would never fight again, even if it meant their own deaths.

The Nephites honored that covenant. They gave the people of Ammon land in Jershon and promised to protect them with Nephite armies. For years, this arrangement worked. The people of Ammon lived peacefully, supported the Nephite military with food and supplies, and kept their promise to God.

But now the wars with the Lamanites had grown worse than ever. Amalickiah's armies were attacking everywhere. Nephite soldiers were dying in battle after battle, dying to protect the very people who had sworn not to fight. The people of Ammon watched this happening, and they felt terrible. How could they sit safely at home while others gave their lives for them?

Alma 53:10-15 describes the painful moment when the people of Ammon seriously considered breaking their covenant. They wanted to pick up their weapons and join the fight. They felt it was not fair for the Nephites to bear all the burden. Their gratitude and their guilt pushed them toward the battlefield.

But Helaman, the son of Alma and a great spiritual leader, begged them not to do it. He knew how important their covenant was. They had promised God they would never shed blood again, and breaking that promise could destroy their souls. Helaman reminded them that God takes covenants seriously. A promise made to God is not something you set aside because circumstances get hard.

The people of Ammon listened. They agreed not to break their covenant. But they could not just do nothing while others died for them. There had to be another way to help. And as it turned out, there was, one that nobody expected. Their own sons would provide the answer.
