### Lesson 9.2 -- The 2,000 Stripling Warriors

The people of Ammon had made a covenant never to fight again, and Helaman had convinced them to keep it. But their sons, young men who had grown up in the land of Jershon, had not made that same covenant. They had been children or had not yet been born when their parents buried their weapons. They were free to fight if they chose to.

And they chose to fight.

About 2,000 of these young men volunteered to join the Nephite army. They were young, and they had never been in battle before. They had no military experience. They had never held a sword in combat or faced an enemy army charging toward them. By every normal measure, they were not ready for war.

But they had something that most seasoned soldiers did not have: absolute, unshakable faith. Their mothers had taught them from childhood that God would protect them if they did not doubt. That teaching had sunk deep into their hearts. They believed it completely.

When it came time to choose a leader, these young men did not pick a military veteran. They chose Helaman. He was a spiritual leader, a priest, a man of God. They wanted a leader who would keep them close to the Lord. Helaman was deeply moved by their faith and their courage. He loved them so much that he called them "my sons," and they called him father.

Alma 53:16-22 records the moment these young warriors entered the war. They covenanted to fight for the liberty of the Nephites and to protect the land even at the cost of their own lives. They were not fighting for revenge or glory. They were fighting because they saw their adopted nation suffering, and they wanted to do their part.

It must have been a remarkable sight: 2,000 young men with no battle experience, led by a prophet rather than a general, marching off to join one of the bloodiest wars in Nephite history. Most commanders would have looked at them and seen inexperienced boys. But Helaman saw something different. He saw young men whose faith made them stronger than their inexperience made them weak.

The Nephite army welcomed them. And very soon, the stripling warriors would prove that faith planted by righteous mothers was more powerful than years of military training.
