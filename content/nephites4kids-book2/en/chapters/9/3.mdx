### Lesson 9.3 -- "We Do Not Doubt Our Mothers Knew It"

The stripling warriors were young and untested, but when the fighting began, they showed a kind of courage that stunned everyone around them. These were not reckless boys trying to prove themselves. They were young men fighting with a calm, deep faith that came from something they had been taught their entire lives.

When Helaman asked them if they were afraid, they gave one of the most powerful answers in all of scripture. They told him that their mothers had taught them that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them. And then they said words that have echoed through centuries: "We do not doubt our mothers knew it" (Alma 56:48).

Think about what that means. These young men were standing on a battlefield, facing experienced enemy soldiers who wanted to kill them. They had never fought before. And their confidence did not come from military training or armor or weapons. It came from what their mothers had told them about God. They believed their mothers, and they believed God.

The battles they fought in were not small skirmishes. They were some of the fiercest engagements of the entire war. Helaman's letters to Captain Moroni describe desperate fighting where the stripling warriors were in the thick of the action. They fought alongside seasoned Nephite soldiers and held their ground when others faltered.

After one particularly brutal battle, Helaman feared the worst. He gathered his young soldiers and counted them. Every single one was alive. Not one of the 2,000 had been killed. Many were wounded, some of them badly, but every one of them survived. Alma 56:56 records this miraculous result.

It happened again. After another fierce battle described in Alma 57:25, Helaman counted his stripling warriors once more. Again, not a single one had died. They had received many wounds, but every one of them lived. Helaman credited their survival to their "exceeding faith in that which they had been taught," faith that came from their mothers.

This was not luck. This was not coincidence. This was a living demonstration that God keeps His promises to those who trust Him. The mothers of these young men could not go to war themselves. But the faith they planted in their children's hearts protected those children more effectively than any shield or fortress ever could.
