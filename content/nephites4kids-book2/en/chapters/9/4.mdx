### Lesson 9.4 -- What Their Story Teaches

The story of the stripling warriors is one of the most beloved in the Book of Mormon, and for good reason. It is a story about faith, family, and the extraordinary power of what parents teach their children. But there is even more going on beneath the surface when you look at the full picture.

Start with the mothers. These women could not pick up swords and join the army. Their covenant -- and their husbands' covenant -- kept them out of the fight. Some people might think that made them powerless. But they were anything but powerless. The faith they taught their sons turned out to be the most potent weapon on the battlefield. Every lesson about God, every testimony shared at home, every example of keeping promises even when it was hard -- all of it went to war with those 2,000 young men.

What the mothers planted, the sons harvested. This is one of the great truths of the stripling warrior story. Faith is something that can be passed from one generation to the next. It is not automatic -- each person has to choose to believe for themselves -- but the foundation matters enormously. These young men chose to believe, and the foundation their mothers gave them made that choice feel natural and strong.

Now look at the bigger picture. The people of Ammon had made a covenant to never fight. That covenant looked like a weakness during wartime. People could have said it was foolish or impractical. But keeping that covenant -- generation after generation -- is exactly what produced the stripling warriors. If the parents had broken their promise and picked up their swords, the story would have been completely different. Their children might never have learned what it means to trust God no matter what.

Covenant keeping saved a nation. Not in the way anyone expected, but it did. The parents kept their covenant. The children, raised in that atmosphere of faithfulness, developed a faith so powerful it carried them through battles that should have killed them. God honored both the parents' covenant and the children's faith.

This story asks each of us a question -- what are you planting in the hearts of the people around you? The stripling warriors' mothers could not see the future. They did not know their sons would go to war. They simply taught the truth, day after day, and trusted God with the results. That is all any of us can do. And as this story proves, it is enough.
