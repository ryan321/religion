# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing History of the Nephites, Book 2. You've covered the entire reign of the judges -- from Alma giving up the judgment seat to the night without darkness that announced the birth of Jesus Christ.

You walked with Alma and Amulek through the horrors of Ammonihah and the miracle of the prison walls falling. You watched the Anti-Nephi-Lehies bury their weapons and choose death over breaking a covenant with God. You planted a seed of faith with the Zoramite poor and learned that small and simple things bring about great things.

You marched with Captain Moroni under the Title of Liberty and fortified cities against impossible odds. You fought alongside the 2,000 stripling warriors whose mothers' faith was stronger than any sword. You read Moroni's angry letter and Pahoran's gracious response -- and learned something about how righteous people handle conflict.

You watched the Gadianton robbers grow from a small band of criminals into a force that nearly destroyed the Nephite nation. You stood with Nephi and Lehi in a prison surrounded by fire. You heard Samuel the Lamanite prophesy from the city wall. And you saw the sun set and the darkness never come -- the sign that the Savior of the world had been born.

In Book 3, the story reaches its climax. Destruction will come. Darkness will cover the land. And then Jesus Christ Himself will descend from heaven and stand among the Nephites at the temple in Bountiful. You'll follow the civilization through 200 years of peace, the final decline, the last battle at Cumorah, and Moroni's promise -- the invitation that is still open to every person who reads the Book of Mormon.

The best is yet to come.
