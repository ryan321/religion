## Introduction

The reign of the judges was the most dramatic period in Nephite history. In about 90 years, the Nephites faced everything: war, betrayal, secret combinations, missionary triumphs, and one of the most remarkable acts of faith ever recorded -- 2,000 young men marching into battle because their mothers taught them not to doubt.

This book picks up where Book 1 left off. Alma the Younger is the first chief judge. The Nephites are experimenting with self-government for the first time. And almost immediately, everything is tested -- by internal rebellion, external war, and the constant pull of pride.

You'll meet some of the greatest heroes in the Book of Mormon: Captain Moroni, who tore his coat to make the Title of Liberty. Helaman, who led the stripling warriors. The Anti-Nephi-Lehies, who buried their weapons and chose death over breaking a covenant. You'll also meet some of the greatest villains: Amalickiah, who murdered his way to the Lamanite throne. Korihor, who taught there was no God. The Gadianton robbers, who nearly destroyed the Nephite nation from within.

And you'll end with Samuel the Lamanite standing on a wall, prophesying that in five years a night without darkness would signal the birth of Jesus Christ. The stage is set for the climax of the entire Book of Mormon.

## How to use this book

Same format as Book 1: 15 chapters, 4 lessons per chapter, one chapter per week. Each lesson is about 15-20 minutes. Scripture references throughout -- open your Book of Mormon alongside this book for the best experience.

## Note to parents

This book covers Alma through 3 Nephi 1 -- the war chapters, the missionary stories, and the Gadianton robbers. Some content is intense (the believers burned in Ammonihah, the battles, Haun's Mill parallels). The lessons handle these events honestly but at an age-appropriate level. The war chapters in particular teach important principles about faith, leadership, and the cost of freedom.
