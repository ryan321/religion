### Lesson 1.1 -- Signs Forgotten

Have you ever seen something so amazing that you were sure you'd never forget it? The Nephites had that kind of experience. Just a few years earlier, they had witnessed the incredible sign of Samuel the Lamanite's prophecy: a night with no darkness at all! A new star appeared, and the Christ child was born. You'd think that would change everyone forever, right?

Sadly, it didn't last. Within just a few years, many people started coming up with **explanations** for the signs. Some said it had been a trick. Others called it "some lying wonder" and refused to believe that the signs pointed to Jesus Christ (see 3 Nephi 1:22). They talked themselves right out of their own experience.

This is one of the important lessons from this time period: **dramatic signs can produce strong feelings, but those feelings don't always turn into lasting faith**. The people had seen real miracles with their own eyes, and yet many of them chose to explain them away. Real faith has to be built on something deeper than just seeing signs.

The **children** who were growing up during this time were especially affected. They hadn't been old enough to fully understand the sign of the night without darkness. As they heard adults around them doubting and arguing, many of these young people grew up not believing at all. Satan was working hard to lead hearts away from the truth (see 3 Nephi 1:29).

To make matters worse, the **Gadianton robbers** were growing strong again during this time. While the people argued about signs and prophecies, this dangerous group was spreading its influence across the land. When people lose their faith, it becomes much easier for wickedness to take hold.

This is a pattern we see over and over in the scriptures. The Lord gives a sign or a miracle. People believe for a while. Then they slowly start to forget or doubt. Before long, they're right back where they started, or even worse off than before.

The good news is that we don't have to follow that same pattern. We can choose to **remember** the blessings and spiritual experiences Heavenly Father gives us. Writing in journals, sharing testimonies, and praying regularly can help us keep our faith strong, even when other people around us are doubting.

The Nephites' experience teaches us that seeing isn't always believing. True faith comes from choosing to trust in God day after day, not just when something spectacular happens.
