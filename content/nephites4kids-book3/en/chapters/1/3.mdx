### Lesson 1.3 -- The Gathering Strategy

When you're facing a really big problem, sometimes the best answer isn't what you'd expect. Governor Lachoneus came up with a plan that was unusual but incredibly smart. Instead of sending armies out to chase the Gadianton robbers through the mountains, he decided to **gather the entire Nephite population into one place**.

Think about that for a moment. Lachoneus asked every family, every city, and every village to pack up and move to a central location. They brought all their animals, all their grain, and **seven years' worth of supplies** (see 3 Nephi 3:22-26). That's a lot of food! It was like one enormous camping trip -- except it was deadly serious.

Why would Lachoneus do something so dramatic? Because he understood how the Gadianton robbers worked. The robbers **lived by plunder** -- they didn't grow their own food or raise their own animals. They survived by **stealing** from others. If there was nobody left in the scattered cities and villages to steal from, the robbers would have nothing to eat.

So the Nephites left the land empty. They gathered together with all their supplies and built fortifications to protect themselves. They also **repented of their sins** and prayed for God's help. Lachoneus appointed a great military leader named **Gidgiddoni** to command their armies. Gidgiddoni was not only a skilled warrior but also a prophet who sought the Lord's guidance.

When some people suggested going on the offensive and attacking the robbers in their mountain hideouts, Gidgiddoni said no. He told them the Lord would **not** bless them if they went up to attack. Instead, they would **wait and prepare**, and God would deliver the robbers into their hands (see 3 Nephi 3:21).

The strategy worked beautifully. When the Gadianton robbers came down from the mountains to attack, they found the land empty. There was nothing to steal, no food to take, and no scattered villages to raid. They had no choice but to try to attack the gathered Nephites directly -- and the Nephites were ready.

This story teaches us that sometimes the wisest thing to do is to **gather together and prepare** rather than rush into danger. Lachoneus combined practical planning with spiritual preparation. He didn't just stockpile food -- he also called his people to repent. That's a pattern we can follow too: do our best to prepare, and trust the Lord to help with the rest.
