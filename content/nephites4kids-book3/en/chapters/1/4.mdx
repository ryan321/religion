### Lesson 1.4 -- Victory and Collapse

The Nephites' gathering strategy worked. When the Gadianton robbers finally came down to fight, the Nephites were prepared, united, and strengthened by their repentance and faith. The great battle described in 3 Nephi 4 was one of the most important victories in Nephite history. The robbers, unable to find food in the empty land and unable to break through the Nephite defenses, were **defeated**.

The Nephite armies surrounded the robbers, cut off their supplies, and forced them to either surrender or be destroyed. Their leader **Zemnarihah** tried to retreat, but the Nephites chased him down and captured him. The Gadianton robbers who surrendered were given a choice: repent and make a covenant to stop their crimes, or face punishment. Many of them chose to repent and were allowed to join the Nephites peacefully.

After this great victory, the Nephites gave **thanks to God**. They knew it wasn't just their military strength that had saved them -- it was the Lord's help. For a time, there was real **peace** in the land. The people kept the commandments and prospered.

But here is where the story takes a sad and familiar turn. The peace didn't last. Within just a few years, the people **returned to wickedness**. Pride crept back in. People began to divide themselves by wealth and class. The Church was mocked. Prophets were rejected and even killed.

Things got so bad that the **government itself collapsed**. The chief judge was murdered by a secret combination, and the people couldn't agree on a new leader. By about **30 AD**, there was **no central government** at all. The entire nation broke apart into separate **tribes**, each with its own leader (see 3 Nephi 7). Nephite, Lamanite -- those old names still existed, but the real division was tribe by tribe.

It's hard to believe, isn't it? The same people who had united to defeat the Gadianton robbers fell apart in just a few short years. They went from praying together and thanking God to murdering their own judges and rejecting the prophets.

This pattern -- victory, peace, pride, collapse -- repeats throughout the Book of Mormon. It's a powerful warning for all of us. Staying faithful isn't just about winning one big battle. It's about **choosing to follow God every single day**, even when things are going well and it seems like we don't need His help.
