### Lesson 10.2 -- Mormon Is Killed

After the terrible battle at Cumorah, Mormon was among the wounded. He had somehow survived the slaughter that killed hundreds of thousands of his people, but his injuries were severe. The Lamanites were not finished, though. They swept across the land, hunting down every Nephite survivor they could find. There was no mercy, no peace treaty, no surrender accepted. The Nephite nation was being wiped from the earth.

Mormon was killed around **385 AD**. The exact details of his death are not recorded in the Book of Mormon, but we know from his son Moroni's writings that Mormon fell along with the rest of the Nephite survivors (see Mormon 8:3). After decades of leading, writing, and pleading with his people to repent, Mormon's life came to an end in the final destruction of his civilization.

When you think about Mormon's life, it's almost overwhelming. Here was a man who was chosen at age ten to one day take charge of the sacred records. By fifteen, he was visited by the Lord. By sixteen, he was leading Nephite armies. And for the rest of his life, he carried two enormous burdens: leading a doomed army and writing a sacred record.

The people Mormon led had **rejected God**. They cursed and swore and refused to repent, no matter what Mormon said. Can you imagine how lonely and frustrating that must have been? He was a prophet among people who didn't want a prophet. He was a righteous man surrounded by wickedness. Yet he never abandoned them. He kept leading them into battle, kept trying to protect them, even when he knew it was hopeless.

At the same time, Mormon spent years carefully reading, selecting, and abridging centuries of Nephite records onto the golden plates. He poured his heart into this work, knowing he would **never see anyone read it**. He would never know the names of the people his words would touch. He simply trusted God's promise that the record would come forth someday.

Mormon's faith is one of the greatest examples in all of scripture. He didn't have faith because things were going well. He had faith because he knew God was real and God's promises were true, no matter what was happening around him. His faith wasn't based on **visible outcomes**. It was based on an unshakable trust in the Lord.

We may never face anything as difficult as what Mormon went through. But we will all have times when doing the right thing seems pointless or when nobody around us seems to care about God. Mormon's life teaches us to keep going anyway. Faithfulness matters, even when we can't see the results.
