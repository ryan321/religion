### Lesson 10.4 -- Mormon and Moroni: Father and Son

The story of Mormon and Moroni is one of the most remarkable father-and-son stories in all of scripture. Both were prophets. Both lived through impossible circumstances. And both exercised extraordinary faith, not because things worked out in their lifetimes, but because they trusted God's promises for the future.

Think about what **Mormon** did. He led an army that was doomed to fail. He knew the Nephites would not repent. He knew they would be destroyed. And yet he kept leading them, kept loving them, and kept trying. At the same time, he spent decades writing and abridging the sacred records, creating a book he would never see published, for an audience he would never meet. Mormon worked his entire life on something whose results he would never witness.

Now think about **Moroni**. After his father's death, he took the plates and wandered alone for decades. He kept writing, kept adding to the record, and eventually sealed it up in a stone box in the hill Cumorah. That record would sit in the ground for approximately **1,400 years** before a young man named Joseph Smith would be led to find it. Moroni spent his entire life protecting and completing a record that wouldn't be read until long after he was gone.

What kind of faith does that take? It takes the kind of faith that isn't based on seeing results. Both Mormon and Moroni had to trust that God meant what He said: that the record would come forth, that it would change lives, that their work mattered. They had no proof that any of it would happen. They had only God's word.

This is what makes their faith so **inspiring**. Most of us want to see results right away. We want to know that our efforts are paying off. But Mormon and Moroni teach us that sometimes God asks us to plant seeds we will never see grow. Sometimes He asks us to do hard things without showing us why. That's real faith: trusting God even when we can't see the outcome.

And here's the beautiful part: their faith **was** rewarded. The Book of Mormon did come forth. It has been read by millions of people around the world. It has changed countless lives and brought people to Christ. Mormon and Moroni's work mattered more than they could have imagined. They just didn't get to see it in their lifetimes.

We hold the proof of their faith in our hands every time we open the Book of Mormon. Their sacrifice and obedience made it possible for us to have these sacred writings today. What a gift. And what a reminder that our own faithful efforts, even the ones nobody notices, can matter far more than we realize.
