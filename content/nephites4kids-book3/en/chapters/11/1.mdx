### Lesson 11.1 -- Tower of Babel

Long before Lehi's family ever left Jerusalem, long before the Nephites and Lamanites existed, another group of people was led by God to the promised land. Their story begins at one of the most famous events in the Bible: the **Tower of Babel**.

After the great flood in Noah's time, the people of the earth all spoke the same language. They settled in a land called Shinar and decided to build a massive tower that would reach up to heaven. Their goal wasn't to get closer to God in a righteous way; it was about **pride**. They wanted to make a name for themselves and show their own greatness.

God was not pleased with their pride. To stop the project, He **confounded their language**, meaning He caused the people to suddenly speak different languages. When they couldn't understand each other anymore, they couldn't work together. The people scattered across the earth, forming different nations and cultures.

In the middle of this confusion, there was a man named **Jared**. Jared had a brother who was a man of extraordinary faith (we'll learn more about him in the next lesson). When Jared saw what was happening (people suddenly unable to understand each other, families being torn apart, communities breaking up), he went to his brother and asked him to pray. He wanted the Lord to have mercy on them and not confound their language.

Jared's brother cried unto the Lord, and the Lord heard his prayer. Their language was **not** confounded. They could still understand each other. But the Lord did more than just preserve their language. He gave them specific instructions: gather your families, your friends, your flocks, and your seeds of every kind, and prepare to travel to a land "which is choice above all the lands of the earth" (Ether 1:33-43).

This was an incredible promise. God was offering them a **promised land**, a place of safety and blessing. But it came with a responsibility. They had to follow the Lord's guidance, trust His direction, and be willing to leave everything familiar behind.

The Jaredites' journey began with a simple act of faith: asking God for help when everything was falling apart. Jared didn't know what God would say. His brother didn't know where they would end up. But they asked, and God answered. That's how many of the greatest adventures in the scriptures begin not with a detailed plan, but with a prayer.
