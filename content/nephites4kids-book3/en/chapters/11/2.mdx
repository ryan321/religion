### Lesson 11.2 -- The Brother of Jared

The brother of Jared is one of the most remarkable people in all of scripture. His name was **Mahonri Moriancumer** -- quite a mouthful! But it's his faith, not his name, that makes his story unforgettable.

When the Lord commanded the Jaredites to travel to the promised land, they would need to cross the great ocean. The Lord instructed the brother of Jared to build special barges -- vessels that were small, light, and sealed tight "like unto a dish" so water couldn't get in. But there was a problem: the barges had no windows and would be completely dark inside. How would the people have light during their long journey across the sea?

The Lord asked the brother of Jared a powerful question -- essentially, "What would you have me do?" Instead of just telling him the answer, God invited him to come up with a solution and bring it to the Lord. This teaches us something important: God wants us to **think and work**, not just wait for answers to be handed to us.

The brother of Jared came up with an amazing idea. He went to a mountain and **molten sixteen small stones** out of rock -- clear, transparent stones, two for each of the eight barges. Then he carried them to the top of the mountain and humbly asked the Lord to touch the stones with His finger so they would shine and give light in the darkness (see Ether 3:1-6).

What happened next is one of the most astonishing moments in scripture. The Lord reached out and **touched the stones one by one** with His finger. As He did, the brother of Jared saw the finger of the Lord -- and it looked like the finger of a man! He was so startled that he fell down in shock.

But then something even more extraordinary happened. Because the brother of Jared's faith was so incredibly great, the Lord revealed His **entire spirit body** to him. The brother of Jared saw Jesus Christ -- not in the flesh (that wouldn't happen for thousands of years), but in His spirit form. The Lord explained that He looked like this because He would one day take on a body of flesh and blood.

The Lord told him that "never has man come before me with such exceeding faith" (Ether 3:9). The brother of Jared's faith was so powerful that the **veil** -- the barrier between the mortal world and God's presence -- could not keep him out. He saw through it completely.

This story teaches us that faith is not just believing -- it's acting on that belief. The brother of Jared didn't just pray and hope. He worked, prepared, and brought his best effort to the Lord. And because of his faith and effort, he received one of the greatest revelations ever given to a mortal person.
