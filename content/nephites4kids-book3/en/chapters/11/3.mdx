### Lesson 11.3 -- Crossing the Ocean

With the glowing stones now providing light, the Jaredites were ready for their great journey across the ocean. They loaded their **eight barges** with food, water, flocks, and everything they would need. Then they committed themselves to God and set out onto the vast sea.

These barges were unlike any ship you've ever seen. They were sealed "tight like unto a dish," both on top and on the bottom (see Ether 6:7). This meant they were basically **submersible**. When huge waves crashed over them, the barges would go underwater and then pop back up to the surface. They had plugs they could open for air when above water and seal when waves came. It must have been a wild ride!

The barges had no sails, no oars, no rudder, and no way for the people to steer. They were completely dependent on **God** to get them where they needed to go. The Lord caused a powerful wind to blow across the ocean, pushing the barges toward the promised land. The people inside couldn't control their direction. They could only trust that God knew the way.

The journey lasted **344 days**, nearly an entire year on the open ocean (see Ether 6:11). Think about that. Almost a full year in a small, sealed barge being tossed by waves, with no idea exactly where you were or how much longer the trip would take. That required an incredible amount of patience and trust.

But here's the most beautiful part of the story: the Jaredites didn't spend that year complaining or worrying. The record says they "did sing praises unto the Lord" and "did thank and praise the Lord all the day long; and when the night came, they did not cease to praise the Lord" (Ether 6:9). Even while being tossed around on a dark ocean, they **praised God** constantly. They sang, they prayed, they thanked the Lord, day and night, for almost a year.

When they finally reached the promised land, the Jaredites "bowed themselves down upon the face of the land, and did shed tears of joy before the Lord" (Ether 6:12). After 344 days of trusting God through storms and waves and darkness, they had arrived safely. Every single person made it. Not one barge was lost.

The Jaredite crossing teaches us that God can guide us even when we can't see where we're going. Sometimes life feels like being in a sealed barge on a dark ocean. We don't know what's ahead, and we can't control the direction. But if we trust God and keep praising Him through the storms, He will bring us safely to where we need to be. The Jaredites' faithful, humble beginning in the promised land would sadly not last, but their journey across the sea remains one of the most inspiring stories of faith in all of scripture.
