### Lesson 12.1 -- The Jaredite Cycle

If you've been studying the Book of Mormon, you've probably noticed a pattern by now. The Nephites went through a cycle that repeated over and over: they were righteous, so God blessed them. They became prosperous. Prosperity led to pride. Pride led to wickedness. Wickedness led to suffering and destruction. Suffering brought humility. And humility brought them back to righteousness, starting the whole cycle again.

The Jaredites went through this **exact same cycle**. In fact, their history may be the clearest example of this cycle in all of scripture because it played out over an incredibly long period, roughly **1,500 years**. That's far longer than the Nephite civilization lasted. And during those fifteen centuries, the Jaredites repeated the cycle many, many times.

When the Jaredites had righteous kings and followed God, they were blessed with peace, prosperity, and growth. Their civilization became large and powerful. They built cities, raised crops, and had large families. Everything was going well. But then, almost like clockwork, **pride** would creep in. The people would start to think that their success was because of their own greatness rather than God's blessings.

Once pride took hold, the people would turn to **wickedness**. They would reject the prophets God sent to warn them. They would form secret combinations, groups of people who made evil covenants to get power and wealth. Murders, plots, and corruption would spread through the land.

Eventually, the wickedness would bring **consequences**: wars, famines, plagues, or other suffering. The people would be humbled, sometimes almost to the point of destruction. In their suffering, they would remember God and turn back to Him. The Lord, in His mercy, would forgive them and bless them again. And then the whole thing would start over.

What makes this cycle so important for us to understand? Because **we are not immune to it**. The same pattern of righteousness, prosperity, pride, wickedness, and suffering can happen in our own lives, families, and communities. When things are going well, it's easy to forget who blessed us. When we're comfortable, it's tempting to think we don't need God as much.

The Jaredite cycle is included in the Book of Mormon as a **warning**. Mormon and Moroni wanted us to see this pattern clearly so we could recognize it in our own time and break out of it. The key to avoiding the cycle is to stay humble and grateful even when things are going well, to remember God in prosperity, not just in hardship.
