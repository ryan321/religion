### Lesson 12.3 -- The Final Destruction

The end of the Jaredite nation is one of the most disturbing and heartbreaking stories in all of scripture. What had once been a great civilization -- blessed by God, guided by prophets, and established in a promised land -- was consumed by a **civil war** so terrible that it wiped out the entire nation.

The final conflict came down to two leaders: **Shiz** and **Coriantumr**. Each man gathered every person in the land to one side or the other. There was no sitting this out -- everyone was forced to choose a side. The hatred between the two groups was so intense and so complete that neither side would stop fighting until the other was totally destroyed.

The battles went on and on. The record describes scenes that are almost impossible to imagine. **Millions** of people died. Men, women, and even children were caught up in the destruction. The land was covered with the bodies of the dead. The people were so consumed by anger and bloodlust that they couldn't stop fighting, even when it was clear that everyone was going to die.

Finally, after all the battles, all the death, and all the destruction, only one man was left standing -- **Coriantumr**. He was the last Jaredite, and he was mortally wounded. The record tells us that after Shiz was finally killed, Coriantumr "fell to the earth, and became as if he had no life" (Ether 15:29-32). He was alone, surrounded by the bodies of his entire nation. Everyone he had ever known was dead.

Coriantumr eventually recovered enough to wander the land, but he was a broken man -- the sole survivor of a civilization that had destroyed itself. He was later found by the **people of Zarahemla** (the Mulekites), who took him in. He lived among them for about nine months before he died. He never learned their language, and they never fully learned his story -- at least not until the Jaredite record was discovered and translated centuries later.

The destruction of the Jaredites is devastating to read about. But it's supposed to be. Mormon and Moroni included this story specifically to show what happens when an entire people **rejects God**, rejects His prophets, and gives in to pride, hatred, and secret combinations. The Jaredites had every advantage -- a promised land, prophets, and God's direct guidance. And they threw it all away.

This story is a solemn warning. No civilization, no matter how great, is guaranteed to survive if its people turn away from God. The Jaredites proved that. The Nephites proved that. And the warning is meant for us too.
