### Lesson 12.4 -- Why the Jaredite Story Is in the Book of Mormon

Have you ever wondered why the Book of Mormon includes the story of the Jaredites at all? The Book of Mormon is mainly about the Nephites and Lamanites. So why did Mormon and Moroni take the time to include an entirely different civilization's history, one that began at the Tower of Babel and ended centuries before Christ's visit to the Americas?

The answer is simple but powerful: the Jaredite story is a **warning**.

Moroni was the one who abridged the Jaredite record into the book of Ether. He had just finished recording the complete destruction of his own people, the Nephites. As he read through the Jaredite history, he must have been struck by how eerily familiar it all was. The **parallels** between the two civilizations were impossible to miss, and they were not accidental.

Both the Jaredites and the Nephites were led by God to a **promised land**. Both were given prophets, scriptures, and direct guidance from the Lord. Both prospered when they were righteous. Both fell into cycles of pride and wickedness. Both developed **secret combinations**, groups of people who made dark covenants to gain power and wealth. And both were ultimately destroyed when they rejected God's prophets and refused to repent.

These parallels are **deliberate**. Mormon and Moroni wanted future readers to see the pattern clearly. One civilization destroyed by pride and secret combinations might seem like a unique historical event. But two civilizations destroyed by the same sins, in the same promised land: that's a pattern. And patterns are meant to teach us something.

So what is the lesson for us? The Book of Mormon makes it clear that the **same dangers** exist in our time. Pride, secret combinations, rejecting prophets, and forgetting God: these aren't just ancient problems. They are human problems that show up in every generation. The Jaredite story was included specifically so we would recognize these dangers in our own world.

Moroni wrote directly to our generation. He said he had **seen our day** and knew the conditions we would face (see Mormon 8:35). He included the Jaredite record because he wanted us to have two complete examples of what happens when a blessed people turns away from God. He wanted us to have no excuse for making the same mistakes.

The Jaredite story is a mirror. It's meant to make us look at ourselves and ask hard questions. Are we being humble or proud? Are we listening to prophets or ignoring them? Are we grateful for our blessings or taking them for granted? The civilizations that ignored these questions are gone. The warning in the Book of Mormon exists so that we don't have to share their fate.
