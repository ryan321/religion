### Lesson 13.3 -- Moroni's Teachings

Moroni was alone, but he wasn't idle. During his decades of wandering, he added something truly remarkable to the record: **practical teachings and instructions** that would be needed by a future church. It's as if Moroni was writing a handbook for people who wouldn't exist for another 1,400 years.

One of the most important things Moroni recorded was the **sacrament prayers** (see Moroni 4-5). These are the exact prayers that are spoken every Sunday when the bread and water are blessed. If you've ever listened carefully during the sacrament, you've heard the very words that Moroni wrote down. He preserved them so that the ordinance could be performed correctly when the Church of Jesus Christ was restored.

Moroni also wrote down instructions for **conferring the Holy Ghost** and for **ordaining priests and teachers** (see Moroni 2-3). These might sound like small details, but they were essential. When the Church was restored through Joseph Smith, these instructions provided a guide for how priesthood ordinances should be performed. Moroni was thinking ahead, making sure that nothing important would be lost.

Then, in Moroni chapter 7, he recorded a sermon from his father Mormon about **faith, hope, and charity**. This is one of the most beautiful chapters in all of scripture. Mormon taught that faith in Christ leads to hope, and hope leads to charity, which is the **pure love of Christ**. He said that charity is the greatest of all and that we should "pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart" to be filled with this love (Moroni 7:48).

Think about what Moroni was doing. He was a man with no congregation, no church building, no members to teach. He was hiding in the wilderness, completely alone. And yet he was carefully writing down sacrament prayers, ordination procedures, and sermons about charity. He was **building a handbook for a church that wouldn't exist for over a thousand years**.

That takes extraordinary faith. Moroni couldn't see the future. He didn't know exactly when or how the record would come forth. But he trusted that God had a plan, and he did everything he could to make sure the people of that future day would have what they needed.

Moroni's example teaches us that **our work matters even when we can't see the results**. Sometimes we plant seeds that won't grow until long after we're gone. But if we do our part faithfully, God will make sure our efforts count.
