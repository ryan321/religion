### Lesson 13.4 -- Preparing to Seal the Record

After decades of wandering alone, adding teachings and letters to the record, Moroni knew his work was nearly finished. The time had come to **seal up the plates** and bury them in the earth. This was the moment everything had been leading to.

Moroni understood that he would never see the people who would eventually read his words. He was writing for a **future generation** -- people who would live in a world completely different from his own. He didn't know their names. He didn't know what their lives would look like. But he knew, through the power of the Spirit, that his record would someday come forth and reach them.

That's an incredible act of faith when you think about it. Moroni had spent more than 36 years protecting these plates with his life. He had wandered through wilderness, hidden from enemies, and endured loneliness that most of us can't even imagine. And now he was going to **bury the record in the ground** and trust that God would bring it to light at the right time.

Moroni didn't just hope this would happen -- he **knew** it would. He wrote with confidence that the record would come forth **in the last days**, when the world would desperately need it. He had seen visions of the future and knew what kind of world his readers would live in. He wrote directly to them, warning them about the challenges they would face and encouraging them to trust in Christ.

Think about the courage that took. Moroni had given his entire life to this record. He had sacrificed everything -- his safety, his comfort, his companionship -- to preserve it. And now he was placing it in the ground, knowing he would never find out (in this life) whether his work had made a difference.

But Moroni **was right**. About 1,400 years later, a young man named Joseph Smith was led to the Hill Cumorah by the angel Moroni -- the very same Moroni who had buried the plates. The record came forth exactly as Moroni had been promised. It was translated, published, and sent across the world. It changed millions of lives and continues to change them today.

Moroni's faith was not wasted. Every step he took, every word he wrote, every year he spent in hiding -- it all mattered. The Book of Mormon exists because one faithful man trusted God's promise and **never gave up**.

His example reminds us that when we act in faith, even when we can't see the outcome, **God keeps His promises**. Our efforts are never lost. What we do for the Lord will always bear fruit -- sometimes in ways and times we never expected.
