### Lesson 14.4 -- "Come Unto Christ"

Every great book needs a great ending. And the Book of Mormon has one of the greatest endings in all of scripture. Moroni's final words are not a quiet goodbye. They are a **bold invitation** that echoes across the centuries.

In Moroni 10:32, Moroni wrote his last message to every person who would ever read his record: **"Come unto Christ, and be perfected in him, and deny yourselves of all ungodliness."** These are not just nice words. They are the entire point of the Book of Mormon wrapped up in a single sentence.

Everything in the record (every story, every prophecy, every war, every miracle) leads to this one invitation. Lehi left Jerusalem so that this message could be preserved. Nephi kept records so that it would survive. Mormon spent his life compiling it. Moroni spent his life protecting it. And it all comes down to this: **come unto Christ**.

What does it mean to "come unto Christ"? It means to put your trust in Him. It means to follow His teachings, keep His commandments, and rely on His Atonement to become better than you are on your own. It means recognizing that you can't be perfected by yourself. You need a Savior, and Jesus Christ is that Savior.

Moroni also said to "deny yourselves of all ungodliness." That means choosing to turn away from things that pull you away from God. It doesn't mean being perfect right now; it means **trying**, day by day, to choose the right and let Christ help you along the way.

After writing these words, Moroni **sealed up the record**. He gathered the gold plates, the work of a thousand years of prophets, and buried them in the **Hill Cumorah**. Then he was gone. The record sat hidden in the earth, waiting.

It waited for **1,400 years**.

Then, in 1827, a young man named **Joseph Smith** was led to that same hill by an angel: Moroni himself, now a resurrected being. Joseph received the plates, translated them by the gift and power of God, and the **Book of Mormon came forth** to the world. It was first published in 1830 and has since been translated into over 100 languages.

Moroni's invitation still stands. His words are still alive. And the question he asks every reader is the same one he asked when he first wrote it: **Will you come unto Christ?**
