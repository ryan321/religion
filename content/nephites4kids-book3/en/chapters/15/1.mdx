### Lesson 15.1 -- A Thousand Years in One Record

Step back for a moment and think about what you've just read. Over the course of three books, you've traveled through an entire **thousand years of history**, from Lehi leaving Jerusalem around 600 BC all the way to Moroni burying the plates around 421 AD. That's an incredible journey.

You've met dozens of prophets, kings, warriors, and everyday people. You've watched families grow into nations. You've seen cities built and destroyed. You've witnessed moments of tremendous faith and heartbreaking wickedness. And through it all, one message has stayed constant: **Jesus Christ is the Savior of the world**.

That's not an accident. The **entire purpose** of the Book of Mormon is to testify of Christ. Every story, every sermon, every prophecy points toward Him. When Nephi wrote, he wrote about Christ. When King Benjamin spoke, he spoke about Christ. When Alma taught, he taught about Christ. When Mormon compiled the record, he chose the stories and teachings that would best help people believe in Christ. The whole book is built around one central truth.

The record was preserved by **faithful record keepers** who passed the plates from one generation to the next. Think about how many hands touched those plates over a thousand years. Nephi started the record. It passed to Jacob, then Enos, then through many other writers. Some added a lot; others added only a few lines. But each one understood that they were part of something bigger than themselves.

Then came **Mormon**, the great compiler. Out of all the records, histories, and writings that had been created over a thousand years, Mormon carefully selected and abridged the most important parts. He didn't just copy everything; he chose the stories and teachings that would matter most to future readers. He was guided by the Spirit in deciding what to include and what to leave out.

Mormon's son **Moroni** then finished the work, adding his own writings and his father's letters before sealing up the record. The result is the Book of Mormon, a thousand years of history, compiled by a prophet, completed by his son, and preserved by God for our day.

It's one of the most remarkable records in human history. And its message is as clear today as it was when the first words were written: Jesus is the Christ, and He invites everyone to come unto Him.
