### Lesson 15.2 -- The Record's Journey

The Book of Mormon didn't just appear out of nowhere. It has a **journey** unlike any other book in history -- a journey that spans centuries, continents, and the line between heaven and earth.

It began with **Nephi**, who was commanded by God to create a record of his people. Around 600 BC, Nephi started writing on metal plates, recording his family's journey from Jerusalem to the promised land. He wrote about visions, prophecies, and the teachings of the gospel. From the very beginning, Nephi understood that these records would have a purpose far beyond his own lifetime.

After Nephi, the plates were **passed from one keeper to the next**. Over the course of a thousand years, dozens of hands held those sacred records. Some record keepers were great prophets who added extensively to the plates. Others were faithful men who simply received the records and passed them on to the next generation. But each one protected them, because each one understood their importance.

Around 385 AD, the plates came to **Mormon**, a prophet and military leader who had watched his entire civilization crumble. Mormon spent years reading through all the records that had been accumulated and **abridging** them -- condensing a thousand years of history into a single, focused record. He was guided by the Spirit in choosing what to include.

When Mormon was killed, his son **Moroni** received the plates. Moroni added his own teachings, included his father's letters, and abridged the Jaredite record (the book of Ether). Then, around 421 AD, he **buried the plates in the Hill Cumorah** and they disappeared from the world.

For **1,400 years**, the record lay hidden in the earth. Then, in 1823, the angel **Moroni** -- the same Moroni who had buried the plates -- appeared to a young man named Joseph Smith in New York. After several years of preparation, Joseph received the plates in **1827** and translated them by the **gift and power of God**.

The Book of Mormon was first **published in 1830**. Since then, it has been translated into over 100 languages and distributed to millions of people around the world. Think about that journey: created in ancient America, passed through dozens of hands over a millennium, buried for over a thousand years, delivered by an angel, translated by a prophet, and now available to anyone who wants to read it.

The record traveled across centuries and continents to reach you. That's not a coincidence -- it's a miracle.
