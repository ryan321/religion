### Lesson 15.3 -- What the Book of Mormon Teaches

You've now traveled through the entire Book of Mormon story. But what does it all add up to? What are the big lessons that this thousand-year record is trying to teach us? Let's look at the most important ones.

First and most importantly: **Jesus is the Christ**. This is the central message of the entire book. From Lehi's visions to Nephi's prophecies, from King Benjamin's sermon to Alma's teachings, from Christ's actual visit to the Nephites to Moroni's final testimony -- every part of the record points to Jesus Christ as the Son of God, the Savior and Redeemer of the world. The Book of Mormon is "Another Testament of Jesus Christ," and that title says it all.

Second: **God keeps His covenants**. Throughout the Book of Mormon, we see God making promises to His people and keeping them -- even when the people don't always keep their end of the deal. He promised Lehi a land of inheritance. He promised to preserve the Nephite record. He promised to bring the gospel to the Lamanites. Every promise has been or will be fulfilled.

Third: **wickedness leads to destruction, and righteousness leads to peace**. This pattern repeats over and over in the Book of Mormon. When the people follow God, they prosper. When they turn away, they suffer. It happened with the Nephites again and again, and it happened on a massive scale with their final destruction. It's a warning and a lesson for every generation.

Fourth: **prophets are real, and rejecting them has consequences**. From Abinadi to Samuel the Lamanite, the Book of Mormon is full of prophets who were ignored, mocked, or killed. Every time a people rejected God's prophets, trouble followed. This teaches us to listen to and follow the prophets God sends.

Fifth: **the Atonement of Jesus Christ can change anyone**. Alma the Younger went from trying to destroy the Church to becoming one of its greatest missionaries. The Anti-Nephi-Lehies went from being a warlike people to burying their weapons and choosing peace. No one is too far gone for the Savior's grace.

Finally: the Book of Mormon **invites every reader to find out for themselves**. It doesn't ask for blind faith. It says: read it, think about it, pray about it, and God will tell you the truth. That invitation is open to everyone.
