### Lesson 15.4 -- Your Invitation

You've reached the end of the story. You've traveled with Lehi across the ocean. You've watched Nephi build a civilization. You've listened to King Benjamin and Abinadi and Alma. You've felt the darkness and the light when Jesus Christ visited the Nephites. You've walked with Mormon through the final battles and with Moroni through his years of solitude. You know these people now. You know their stories, their struggles, and their teachings.

And now Moroni has one last thing to say to you.

His promise -- the one found in Moroni 10:4 -- is **still open**. It wasn't just written for people in 1830. It wasn't just written for missionaries or scholars or adults. It was written for **every person** who would ever hold the Book of Mormon in their hands. That includes you, right now, wherever you are.

The question is the same one Moroni asked nearly 1,600 years ago: **Will you take this to God?** Will you read the Book of Mormon -- really read it, with an open heart? Will you pray and ask Heavenly Father if it's true? Will you ask with sincerity, with real intent, and with faith in Christ?

If you do, Moroni promises that the answer will come. God will manifest the truth to you **by the power of the Holy Ghost**. That's not a vague hope -- it's a specific promise from a prophet who gave everything he had to make sure you could receive it.

The witness of the Holy Ghost is available **right now**. You don't have to wait until you're older. You don't have to be a scriptures expert. You don't have to be perfect. You just have to be sincere. God answers honest questions from honest hearts, no matter how young or old you are.

Think about everything it took to get this book to you. Nephi started the record 2,600 years ago. Dozens of prophets added to it. Mormon spent years compiling it. Moroni spent decades protecting it. It was buried, preserved, delivered by an angel, translated by a prophet, and published for the world. All of that -- all of those sacrifices across all of those centuries -- happened so that **you** could have the chance to read it and know for yourself.

The book is in your hands. The promise is real. The invitation is yours.

What will you do with it?
