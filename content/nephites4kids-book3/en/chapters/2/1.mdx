### Lesson 2.1 -- The Destruction Begins

In the thirty-fourth year after the sign of Christ's birth, something happened that the Nephites would never forget. It began with a **terrible storm**, but this was no ordinary storm. This was unlike anything anyone had ever experienced.

The destruction described in 3 Nephi 8 is one of the most dramatic events in all of scripture. There were **earthquakes** so violent that entire cities were shaken to pieces. There were **fires** that burned cities to the ground. **Whirlwinds** carried buildings and people into the air. **Floods** swept away entire communities. It all happened in about **three hours**, but those three hours changed everything.

The city of **Zarahemla** caught fire and burned. The city of **Moroni** sank into the sea. The city of **Moronihah** was buried under a mountain of earth. Other cities were swallowed up by the ground or swept away by water. The **highways** that connected the cities were broken up. The very **face of the land** was changed so completely that people wouldn't have recognized it.

Can you imagine what that must have been like? In just three hours, the world the Nephites knew was torn apart. Cities they had lived in their whole lives were simply gone. Roads they had walked on no longer existed. Mountains appeared where there had been flat land, and flat land appeared where mountains had stood.

This destruction happened at the same time Jesus Christ was being **crucified** in Jerusalem, on the other side of the world. The earth itself seemed to mourn and break apart at the death of its Creator. The prophets had warned that these things would happen, but experiencing them was far more terrible than anyone had imagined.

The people who survived must have been terrified. They had no idea what was happening or why. Many of them had ignored the prophets' warnings and had not repented. Now they were facing the consequences. Those three hours of destruction killed many thousands of people and completely reshaped the land.

This part of the story reminds us that the prophets' warnings are real. God is patient, but He always keeps His promises, both the blessings and the consequences. The Nephites learned this in the most dramatic way possible.
