### Lesson 2.2 -- The Darkness

After the three hours of terrible destruction, something even more frightening happened. A **thick darkness** settled over the entire land. This wasn't ordinary nighttime darkness. It was so thick and heavy that the people could actually **feel** it (see 3 Nephi 8:20-23).

The people tried to light fires, but they **could not**. No torch, no candle, no fire of any kind would burn. The darkness was so complete that the survivors couldn't see anything at all. Not their own hands in front of their faces, not their families, not anything. It was total, absolute blackness.

This darkness lasted for **three days**.

Think about how frightening that would be. You've just survived the worst natural disaster in history. Cities are destroyed. People you know are gone. And now you can't see anything. You can't light a fire to cook food or stay warm. You don't know where you are. You can only stumble around, calling out, hoping to find someone.

The scriptures describe the people **mourning and weeping**. They cried out in anguish. They could hear others around them, but they couldn't reach them. They said things like, "Oh, that we had repented before this great and terrible day!" (see 3 Nephi 8:24-25). Now that the destruction had come, they wished they had listened to the prophets.

Nobody knew who was alive and who was dead. Families were separated. The darkness made it impossible to search for loved ones or even understand how bad the damage truly was. All they could do was wait and mourn.

These three days of darkness were a **symbol** of what was happening on the other side of the world. Jesus Christ, the **Light of the World**, had died. While His body lay in the tomb for three days, the land of the Nephites was covered in darkness. The connection is powerful: when the Light of the World was taken away, literal darkness covered the land.

This experience must have felt hopeless. But even in the deepest darkness, God had not abandoned His people. Something was about to happen that would change everything. A voice was about to speak through that heavy blackness, a voice the survivors would never forget.
