### Lesson 2.3 -- The Voice in the Darkness

While the survivors huddled in total darkness, unable to see or light any fire, they suddenly heard something that made them go completely still. A **voice** pierced through the blackness. It wasn't the voice of another person nearby. It was the voice of **Jesus Christ** Himself, speaking to the entire land (see 3 Nephi 9).

The voice told them which cities had been **destroyed** and **why**. City by city, the Savior named them: Zarahemla, burned with fire. Moroni, sunk into the sea. Moronihah, buried by earth. Gilgal, sunk into the ground. Each city was named, and each time the reason was the same -- the **wickedness** of the people who lived there. They had cast out the prophets, stoned the righteous, and shed innocent blood.

Then the voice said something that changed the rules of worship forever. For hundreds of years, the Nephites had followed the **law of Moses**, which included offering animal sacrifices. Now the Savior declared that those sacrifices were **ended**. The law of Moses was fulfilled. Christ's own sacrifice -- His death on the cross -- had completed the purpose of all those earlier offerings.

So what did God want from His people now? Instead of sacrificing animals, the Lord asked for something much more personal. He said, "Ye shall offer for a sacrifice unto me a **broken heart and a contrite spirit**" (3 Nephi 9:19-20). That means God wanted their humble, sincere hearts. He wanted them to feel truly sorry for their sins and to come to Him with real intent to change.

This is still what God asks of us today. He doesn't want us to just go through the motions of being good. He wants our hearts. He wants us to **honestly** turn to Him, admit when we're wrong, and be willing to change.

Even in the middle of terrible destruction and frightening darkness, the Savior's message included an invitation. He called the survivors to **repent and come unto Him**. He wasn't just punishing them -- He was reaching out, offering a way back. The destruction was devastating, but His love was still there, even in the darkest moment.

The voice of Christ in the darkness is a reminder that even when everything seems lost, the Savior is still speaking to us. We just have to be willing to listen.
