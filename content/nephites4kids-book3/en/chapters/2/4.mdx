### Lesson 2.4 -- Silence Then Light

After the voice of Christ finished speaking in the darkness, something unexpected happened. There was **silence**. For many hours, no voice spoke. No sound broke through the heavy blackness. The people waited, still unable to see, still unable to light a fire. Just silence and darkness together (see 3 Nephi 10:1-2).

Can you imagine what those long, quiet hours felt like? The people had just heard the voice of Jesus Christ telling them about the destruction and calling them to repent. Now there was nothing. The silence must have given them time to think about their choices, about the prophets they had ignored, and about what kind of people they wanted to be going forward.

Then the **voice came again**. This time, the Savior's words carried deep sadness and love. He spoke to His people the way a parent speaks to children who have wandered away. He used a tender image that you might recognize: "How oft have I gathered you as a **hen gathereth her chickens** under her wings" (3 Nephi 10:4-6).

Think about a mother hen gathering her baby chicks under her wings to protect them from danger. That's how the Savior described His feelings for the Nephites. He had **wanted** to gather and protect them, over and over again. But too many times, they had refused. They had run away from His protection and chosen their own path.

Even so, His invitation was still open. He told them He would **still** gather them if they would repent and come to Him. His love had not run out. His arms were still stretched open.

After three full days, the **darkness finally lifted**. Light returned to the land. And when the people could finally see again, they saw the full **devastation** around them. The landscape was changed. Cities were gone. But they were alive, and the light had come back.

The people who survived began to feel something stirring in their hearts. They had heard the voice of Christ. They had sat in darkness and silence and thought about their lives. They had heard His tender invitation to come back to Him. And now, with the light returning, they sensed that something truly extraordinary was about to happen -- something they could never have expected.
