### Lesson 3.1 -- Voice from Heaven

After the darkness lifted and light returned to the land, the survivors began to pick up the pieces of their shattered world. Some time later, about **2,500 people** gathered at the **temple in the land of Bountiful**. They were talking with each other about the incredible things that had happened -- the destruction, the darkness, and the voice they had heard.

While they were gathered there, they heard a **voice**. It came from heaven, but at first they **could not understand** what it said. It wasn't loud or harsh. The scriptures describe it as a small voice -- not a shout, but something that pierced them to their very center (see 3 Nephi 11:3).

They heard the voice a **second time**, and still they could not understand it. It was as if the words were just beyond their ability to grasp. Imagine hearing something that you feel deeply in your heart but can't quite make sense of. The people looked at each other in wonder. They looked toward heaven, trying to understand.

Then the voice spoke a **third time**. This time, something was different. The people **opened their ears** to hear it. They looked steadfastly toward heaven, from where the sound came. And this time, they understood every word.

The voice said: "**Behold my Beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased, in whom I have glorified my name -- hear ye him**" (3 Nephi 11:7).

It was the voice of **God the Father**, introducing His Son, Jesus Christ. These are almost the same words spoken at the Savior's baptism in the New Testament. The Father was telling these 2,500 people to listen to His Son, because His Son was about to appear to them.

There is something important in the fact that the people had to hear the voice three times before they understood. Heavenly Father's voice was gentle, not overpowering. The people had to **choose** to listen carefully, to open their hearts and their ears. God doesn't force anyone to hear Him. He speaks quietly, and we have to be willing to pay attention.

This teaches us about how God often communicates with us today -- through the still, small voice of the Holy Ghost. If we want to hear, we need to be still, be reverent, and open our hearts.
