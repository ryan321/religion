### Lesson 3.2 -- Jesus Descends

After the voice of the Father spoke, the 2,500 people at the temple looked up into heaven. What they saw next was the most sacred and wonderful event in the entire Book of Mormon. A **Man descended from heaven**, clothed in a **white robe**. He came down and stood among them.

The people didn't know at first who He was. They had never seen anything like this before. They thought perhaps it was an angel. Then He stretched forth His hand and spoke. His words were clear and unmistakable:

"**Behold, I am Jesus Christ, whom the prophets testified shall come into the world**" (3 Nephi 11:10).

The Savior of the world was standing before them. The very person that prophets like Nephi, Abinadi, Samuel the Lamanite, and so many others had testified about for hundreds of years. He was here. He had been crucified, He had risen from the dead, and now He had come to visit His "other sheep," just as He had promised in Jerusalem.

When the people realized who was standing among them, they **fell to the earth**. They remembered that it had been prophesied He would appear to them, and now it was happening, right before their eyes.

Then the Savior did something remarkable. He invited the people to come forward and **feel the prints of the nails** in His hands and in His feet, and to feel the wound in His side. He wanted them to **know** for certain that He was the God of Israel who had been crucified for the sins of the world.

And they came, every single one of them. All **2,500 people** went forward, **one by one**, and felt the marks in His hands, feet, and side. This was not a quick moment. It took time for each person to come forward individually. The Savior was patient with every one of them. Each person mattered to Him.

When they had all come forward, they cried out together: "**Hosanna! Blessed be the name of the Most High God!**" And they fell at the feet of Jesus and worshipped Him (see 3 Nephi 11:17).

This sacred event teaches us that the Savior knows each of us personally. He didn't just appear to a crowd. He let every person come to Him, one at a time. He does the same for us today.
