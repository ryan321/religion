### Lesson 3.3 -- "Arise and Come Forth"

After the 2,500 people at the temple had each felt the wounds of the Savior and knew for themselves that He truly was Jesus Christ, the Savior began His ministry among them. One of the first things He did was call a man named **Nephi** to come forward.

The Savior told Nephi to "**arise and come forth**." Nephi came and bowed before the Lord, and he kissed the Savior's feet. Then Jesus gave Nephi **authority to baptize** the people. He also called others and gave them the same power. This was important because the Savior wanted to establish His Church among the Nephites with proper priesthood authority (see 3 Nephi 11:18-22).

Then the Savior taught something that might surprise you. He explained exactly **how** baptism should be done. He described that a person should go down into the water and be **immersed** (buried completely under the water) and then brought back up. He was very specific about this because He wanted there to be **no confusion or contention** about the proper way to perform this sacred ordinance.

In fact, Jesus addressed the problem of **contention** directly. He said, "There shall be no disputations among you" about points of doctrine (see 3 Nephi 11:28). He taught that contention and arguing are not from God. They are from the devil. The spirit of contention drives away the Holy Ghost and divides people. The Savior wanted His people to be **united**.

Then Jesus taught the core of His **doctrine**. It is beautifully simple: believe in Christ, repent, be baptized, and receive the Holy Ghost. He declared, "The **Father, and the Son, and the Holy Ghost are one**," united in purpose and love for us (3 Nephi 11:27). He invited **all people** to come unto Him and be saved.

The Savior's message was an open invitation: "**Whoso believeth in me, and is baptized, shall be saved**" (3 Nephi 11:33). He didn't make it complicated. He wanted everyone to understand: this is the path, and it is available to all who choose to walk it.

He closed this teaching with both a promise and a warning. Those who build their lives on His gospel will stand firm. But those who build on anything else will fall when the storms come (see 3 Nephi 11:39-40). His doctrine is the sure foundation.
