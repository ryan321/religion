### Lesson 3.4 -- The Savior Prays

Of all the sacred things that happened when Jesus visited the Nephites, what happened next may be the most tender and personal of all. The Savior asked the people to **kneel down**, and then He knelt with them. And He began to **pray** to Heavenly Father for them.

What did He say in that prayer? We don't fully know. The prayer was so **sacred** that the people who heard it said it **could not be written** (see 3 Nephi 17:15-17). Mormon, who compiled these records, explained that no tongue can speak, no one can write, and no heart can imagine the things they heard Jesus say to the Father. It was beyond anything they had ever experienced.

Think about that. These people had already seen the Savior descend from heaven. They had touched the prints of the nails. They had heard Him teach with power. And yet this prayer was so far beyond anything they had known that **words could not capture it**.

What we do know is what happened to the people while Jesus prayed. They were **overwhelmed with joy**. The experience was so powerful, so full of the Spirit, that it filled them completely. They were in the very **presence of God**, hearing the Son of God speak directly to the Father on their behalf.

When Jesus finished His prayer, He stood and looked at the multitude. And He **wept**. The scriptures say He wept, and then He spoke words that still touch hearts today: "**Behold, my joy is full**" (3 Nephi 17:20). The Savior of all creation was weeping, not from sadness, but from the overwhelming fullness of His love for these people.

He then took their little **children**, one by one, and blessed them. He prayed to the Father for them. And then something remarkable happened: **angels descended** out of heaven, as if surrounded by fire, and ministered to the children (see 3 Nephi 17:24). Every person there saw it with their own eyes.

This moment reveals the heart of the Savior more clearly than almost any other in scripture. He is not distant. He is not harsh. He is a God who kneels beside His people, prays for them with words too sacred to record, weeps with joy over them, and tenderly blesses their children. This is who Jesus Christ is.

When we pray, we can remember that this same Savior cares about us, too. He knows us. He weeps with joy over us. And His arms are always open.
