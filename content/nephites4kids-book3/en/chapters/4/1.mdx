### Lesson 4.1 -- Sermon at the Temple

When Jesus appeared to the Nephites at the temple in Bountiful, one of the first things He did was **teach**. And what He taught will sound very familiar if you've ever read the New Testament, because He gave a sermon very similar to the Sermon on the Mount.

In the Bible, Jesus taught the Sermon on the Mount to His followers in Galilee. Now, on the other side of the world, He taught many of the same truths to the Nephites. This sermon is recorded in **3 Nephi 12-14**, and it includes the Beatitudes, the higher law, the Lord's Prayer, and the parable of the wise and foolish builders.

The **Beatitudes** are a series of blessings. "Blessed are the poor in spirit," Jesus taught, "for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." He blessed those who mourn, those who are meek, those who hunger and thirst after righteousness, the merciful, the pure in heart, the peacemakers, and those who are persecuted for His name's sake. These weren't rewards for being powerful or popular. They were promises for people who were humble, kind, and trying to do right.

Jesus then taught a **higher law**. The old law said "thou shalt not kill." Jesus said don't even be angry with your brother without a cause. The old law said "thou shalt not commit adultery." Jesus said don't even look at someone with lust. Over and over, He raised the standard. It wasn't enough to just follow rules on the outside. What mattered was the **condition of your heart**.

He taught them the **Lord's Prayer** ("Our Father who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name"), showing them how to talk to God with reverence, trust, and simplicity. He told the parable of the **wise man** who built his house on a rock and the **foolish man** who built on sand, teaching that hearing His words is not enough; you have to actually live them.

There are some small differences between the sermon in Matthew and the one in 3 Nephi. Some of these differences reflect the Nephite situation. For example, in 3 Nephi, Jesus speaks to people who have already been baptized, so He adjusts some of His words to fit what they've already experienced. These differences remind us that Jesus teaches each group of people what they specifically need to hear.

The core message of this sermon is the same in both places: Jesus Christ's standard isn't just about what you do; it's about **who you are becoming** on the inside.
