### Lesson 4.2 -- The Twelve Disciples

When Jesus organized His Church in Jerusalem, one of the first things He did was call **twelve apostles** -- men who would lead, teach, and carry His authority after He left. When He came to the Nephites, He did the same thing. He chose **twelve disciples** to lead His people on this side of the world.

The names of these twelve are recorded in **3 Nephi 19:4**. They included Nephi, Timothy, Jonas, Mathoni, Mathonihah, Kumen, Kumenonhi, Jeremiah, Shemnon, Jonas (a different one), Zedekiah, and Isaiah. These weren't just good men who volunteered -- Jesus specifically **chose** them and gave them His authority.

In **3 Nephi 12:1**, Jesus told the multitude to listen to these twelve and to believe in them, because He would give them power to **baptize** with water, and then the Holy Ghost would come. This was important. Baptism and the gift of the Holy Ghost require proper authority. You can't just decide on your own to perform those ordinances -- someone with the right power has to do it. Jesus made sure the Nephites had that power.

These twelve disciples were the Nephite equivalent of the apostles in Jerusalem. They administered the **sacrament**, they baptized new members, and they taught the people what Jesus had commanded. They were the foundation of the Church of Jesus Christ among the Nephites, just as Peter, James, John, and the other apostles were the foundation in the Old World.

Think about what this means. Jesus didn't just appear, give a nice talk, and leave. He **organized** His Church. He set up leaders. He gave them authority. He established ordinances. He created a structure that could continue to guide and bless people after He ascended back to heaven. This is the same pattern He followed in Jerusalem, and it's the same pattern we see in His Church today.

The fact that Jesus established His Church on **both sides of the world** tells us something important: His gospel isn't just for one group of people. It's for everyone. And wherever it's established, it follows the same pattern -- apostles or disciples called by God, authorized to act in His name, and responsible for keeping the Church on the right path.

These twelve Nephite disciples would go on to lead one of the most righteous periods in all of scripture -- a time of peace, unity, and faithfulness that lasted for generations.
