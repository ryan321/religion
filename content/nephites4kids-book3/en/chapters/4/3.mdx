### Lesson 4.3 -- The Sacrament

After teaching the Nephites at the temple, Jesus did something deeply sacred. He introduced the **sacrament** -- the same ordinance He had given His apostles at the Last Supper in Jerusalem just days before.

Jesus commanded that **bread and wine** be brought. He broke the bread, blessed it, and gave it to His disciples, who then gave it to the multitude. Then He did the same with the wine. He told them that the bread was to help them **remember His body**, and the wine was to help them **remember His blood**, which He had shed for them. This is recorded in **3 Nephi 18:1-12**.

Jesus gave the Nephites a clear commandment: they should do this **always** -- meet together and take the bread and wine in remembrance of Him. And He gave them a promise. If they would always remember Him, they would always have His Spirit to be with them. That's a remarkable promise. Not "sometimes." Not "when you're lucky." **Always** -- if you always remember Him.

This was the exact same commandment Jesus had given at the **Last Supper** with His apostles in the upper room in Jerusalem. The words are almost identical. This tells us that the sacrament isn't something that was invented later or that only applies to one group of people. It was established by Jesus Himself, on both continents, as a permanent part of His gospel.

Every Sunday when you take the sacrament at church, you are participating in the **same ordinance** that Jesus established among the Nephites at the temple in Bountiful. The same ordinance He gave to Peter, James, John, and the others in Jerusalem. The bread and water you take each week connect you directly to those sacred moments.

Jesus also taught something important about **worthiness**. He said that people should not take the sacrament unworthily. This doesn't mean you have to be perfect -- no one is. But it means you should come with a sincere heart, truly wanting to remember Him and keep His commandments. The sacrament is a time to think about your week, recognize where you've fallen short, and commit to do better.

The sacrament is one of the simplest ordinances in the Church -- just bread and water, a few minutes of quiet. But it's one of the most powerful, because it renews your connection with Jesus Christ every single week.
