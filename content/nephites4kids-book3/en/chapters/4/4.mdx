### Lesson 4.4 -- "Come Unto Me"

Throughout His ministry among the Nephites, Jesus repeated one invitation over and over again: **"Come unto me."** He didn't give an abstract lecture. He didn't deliver a distant philosophy. He spoke directly, personally, and urgently to the people standing in front of Him.

In **3 Nephi 12:20**, He said, "Come unto me and be saved." Not "think about it." Not "consider your options." Come. Now. Be saved. This was a direct invitation from the Son of God to every person listening -- and it still applies to you today.

Jesus also taught them about **prayer**. In **3 Nephi 18:15-21**, He commanded them to pray always -- to watch and pray so they wouldn't be tempted. He told them to pray in their families. He told them to pray in their hearts. He made it clear that prayer wasn't optional or occasional. It was supposed to be constant -- a running conversation with your Heavenly Father.

Then He gave them a powerful image. In **3 Nephi 18:24**, He said, "Hold up your light that it may shine unto the world." He told them that they were the light -- that by keeping His commandments and living His gospel, they would become examples that would draw other people to God. You don't have to be a missionary or a church leader to hold up your light. Every time you choose to be honest, kind, brave, or faithful, you're doing exactly what Jesus asked.

What makes these teachings so remarkable is how **personal** they are. Jesus wasn't speaking to millions through a screen. He wasn't writing a letter to be read centuries later (though we're reading it now). He was standing in front of real people -- people who had just survived terrible destruction, who had heard His voice in the darkness, who had come to the temple hoping to find Him. And He looked at them and said, "Come unto me."

This is the heart of Jesus Christ's message to the Nephites and to us. He doesn't want distant followers who admire Him from far away. He wants a real, close, personal relationship with each of us. He wants us to pray, to live His teachings, to shine our light, and to **come to Him** -- not someday, but today.

His invitation is not complicated. It's not hidden behind big words or difficult requirements. It's simple: come, pray, follow, shine. That's what He asked the Nephites to do, and it's what He asks you to do.
