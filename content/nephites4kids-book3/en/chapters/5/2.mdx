### Lesson 5.2 -- Blessing the Children

After healing every sick person in the multitude, Jesus turned His attention to the people who mattered most to Him -- the **children**.

Jesus commanded the people to bring their **little children** and set them on the ground around Him. The parents gathered their children and placed them near the Savior, and then Jesus asked the entire multitude to **kneel down**. What happened next is one of the most sacred moments in all of scripture.

Jesus Himself knelt and began to **pray**. And what He prayed was so powerful, so full of feeling, that the record says no tongue can speak it and no one who was there could write it down. His words were beyond anything they had ever heard. The people were overcome. And the record tells us that Jesus **groaned within himself** as He prayed -- not because He was in physical pain, but because His feelings for these people and their children were so deep and so intense that they came out in groans (3 Nephi 17:14-15).

After praying, Jesus stood and wept. He **wept**. The Son of God, the Creator of the world, the one who had power over death itself -- He cried. He cried because of what He felt for those children and their families.

Then He did something no one expected. He took the children **one by one**, blessed each of them individually, and prayed to the Father for each one. Not as a group. Not with a general wave of His hand. One by one. Every single child received personal attention from the Son of God (3 Nephi 17:21).

Think about how long that must have taken. There were about 2,500 people in the multitude, and many of them had children. Jesus took the time to hold each child, bless each child, and pray for each child individually. He wasn't in a hurry. He wasn't checking a clock. These children mattered to Him -- each one.

This tells us something profound about what Jesus values. In a world that often ignores children, that tells kids to be quiet and stay out of the way, Jesus did the opposite. He put the children at the **center**. He gave them His full attention. He wept over them. He prayed for them by name.

If you're a kid reading this, know that this story is about **you**. Jesus loves children. He cares about you personally. And if He were here right now, He would take time for you, too.
