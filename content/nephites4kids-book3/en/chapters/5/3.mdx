### Lesson 5.3 -- Angels Descend

What happened next is one of the most extraordinary events recorded in any scripture, anywhere, ever.

After Jesus had blessed the children one by one, the heavens opened. **Angels descended** out of heaven, surrounded by light, and came down among the people. But they didn't come to the adults. They didn't come to the leaders or the twelve disciples. They came to the **children**.

The angels **encircled the little ones**, and they were surrounded in **fire**. Not fire that burned, but fire that glowed. The children were wrapped in heavenly light while angels ministered to them. And the entire multitude, all **2,500 people**, watched it happen (3 Nephi 17:24).

Stop and picture that. Thousands of people standing together, watching as angels from heaven come down and surround their children with fire and light. Every parent, every brother and sister, every grandparent could see it. This wasn't a private vision. It wasn't something only one prophet saw. It was public, visible, and witnessed by every single person there.

Mormon, who later wrote down this account, called this one of the most **sacred** scenes in all of scripture. And he's right. In the entire Book of Mormon (a book full of wars, prophecies, visions, and miracles), this moment stands apart. This is the moment where heaven literally came to earth, and it came for the children.

Think about what this tells you about what Jesus **cares about most**. He had limited time with the Nephites. He could have spent it doing anything: organizing governments, teaching complicated doctrines, performing great signs for the leaders. Instead, He spent it healing the sick, blessing children, and calling angels down from heaven to minister to little ones.

This isn't a small detail in the story. This is the **point** of the story. Jesus showed the Nephites, and He's showing us, what matters most to God. It's not power. It's not politics. It's not impressing important people. It's **children**. It's the smallest, the most vulnerable, the ones the world often overlooks.

If you ever wonder where you stand with God, remember this scene. He sent angels for the children. Not for the kings. Not for the generals. For the kids.
