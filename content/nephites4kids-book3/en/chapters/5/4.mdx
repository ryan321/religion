### Lesson 5.4 -- Why This Matters

When Jesus Christ visited the Nephites, He could have done **anything** with His time. He was the resurrected Son of God. He had all power. He could have rebuilt cities with a word. He could have called down fire on the Nephites' enemies. He could have organized a massive army or set up a government that would last forever. He had every option available to Him.

And what did He choose to do? He **healed the sick**. He **blessed the children**. He **wept** over families. He **prayed** so deeply that His words couldn't even be written down. He served, one person at a time.

This is one of the most important lessons in all of scripture, because it shows us the Savior's **priorities**. When Jesus had total freedom to spend His time however He wanted, He chose individuals. He chose families. He chose the most **vulnerable**: the sick, the suffering, the children.

He didn't give a quick speech and move on. He took children **one by one** and blessed each of them. He healed **every** sick person who was brought to Him. He didn't rush. He didn't delegate everything to His disciples and step back. He got on His knees. He wept. He served with His own hands.

This matters because it tells you what kind of God you worship. You worship a God who **weeps** for people. You worship a God who gets down on His knees. You worship a God who isn't too busy or too important to spend time with one child, one sick person, one struggling family.

It also matters because it sets the **example** for how we should live. If the most powerful being in the universe chooses to spend His time serving the sick and blessing children, then that tells us something about what we should value too. We should look for people who are hurting. We should pay attention to the people who are often ignored. We should serve, not in big, showy ways, but in personal, quiet, one-by-one ways.

The Nephite ministry of Jesus Christ isn't just a nice story from a long time ago. It's a **blueprint**. It shows us who Jesus is, what He cares about, and how He wants us to treat each other. Heal the sick. Bless the children. Weep with those who weep. Pray for each other. Serve one by one.

That's the gospel. That's what Jesus did when He had all the time and power in the world. And that's what He's asking us to do too.
