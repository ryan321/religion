### Lesson 6.1 -- Other Sheep

During His ministry among the Nephites, Jesus said something that connected His visit directly to a teaching He had given in Jerusalem. He told the Nephites, "Ye are they of whom I said: **Other sheep I have which are not of this fold**" (3 Nephi 15:21).

This was a reference to a passage in the Gospel of John (**John 10:16**) where Jesus told His followers in Jerusalem that He had other sheep who were not of that fold, and that He needed to bring them too, and they would hear His voice. The people in Jerusalem didn't understand what He meant. They assumed He was talking about the Gentiles. But Jesus revealed to the Nephites that **they** were the other sheep.

The people in Jerusalem didn't know about the Nephites. And the Nephites, living across the ocean, didn't know about the specific conversations Jesus had in Jerusalem. But Jesus knew both groups. He loved both groups. And He personally visited both groups to teach them His gospel.

But Jesus didn't stop there. He told the Nephites that the Father had led **still other groups** away to different parts of the world, and that He would visit them too. "I have other sheep," He said, "which are not of this land, neither of the land of Jerusalem... and they shall hear my voice" (3 Nephi 16:1-3). We don't know who these other groups were or where they lived. The Book of Mormon doesn't give us those details. But the message is clear: Jesus' ministry was **not limited to one land** or one people.

This is a powerful idea. It means that the gospel of Jesus Christ has been carried to people and places we may never learn about in this life. God has been working with His children across the entire world, not just in the lands the Bible talks about.

It also means that Jesus keeps His promises. He said He had other sheep, and He went to them. He promised to gather all of God's children, and He did. No group was too small, too distant, or too forgotten.

The visit to the Nephites is proof that Jesus Christ is the Savior of the **whole world**, not just one country, one continent, or one group of people. His love and His gospel reach everywhere.
