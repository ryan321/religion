### Lesson 6.2 -- Correcting the Record

One of the things Jesus did during His visit to the Nephites might surprise you. He didn't just teach new things. He checked the Nephites' **scriptures** to make sure they were accurate.

Jesus asked to see the records that the Nephites had been keeping. He reviewed them, and He found that some things were **missing**. Specifically, He noticed that the fulfillment of certain prophecies had not been written down. The most important example was the prophecies of **Samuel the Lamanite**.

Samuel had stood on the walls of Zarahemla years before and prophesied that there would be signs when Jesus was born and when He died. He predicted a night with no darkness, a new star, and at Christ's death, three days of darkness and destruction. All of these things had come true. The people had seen them happen with their own eyes. But no one had bothered to write down that the prophecies had been **fulfilled**.

Jesus asked Nephi (the keeper of the records) directly: "How is it that ye have not written this thing, that many saints did arise and appear unto many and did minister unto them?" (3 Nephi 23:11). When Nephi realized the omission, the missing events were immediately recorded, just as Jesus commanded (3 Nephi 23:7-13).

This is a fascinating moment because it shows that Jesus **cared about accuracy**. He didn't just want the Nephites to have nice feelings about their faith. He wanted their records to be **complete and correct**. If a prophecy was given and it came true, that needed to be documented. If something was left out, it needed to be added.

Jesus also gave the Nephites **additional scripture** during His visit, teachings and prophecies that they hadn't had before. He quoted prophecies from the prophet Malachi, which the Nephites didn't have because Malachi lived after Lehi's family left Jerusalem. Jesus made sure they had access to these important teachings.

This tells us something important about how God views scripture. It's not just a collection of old stories. It's a **living record** that should be maintained, updated, and kept accurate. God cares about what's in the record because future generations will depend on it to learn about Him.

The scriptures you read today exist because people like Nephi took Jesus' command seriously and made sure the record was right.
