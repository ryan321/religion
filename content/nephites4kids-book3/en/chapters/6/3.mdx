### Lesson 6.3 -- The Three Nephites

Before Jesus departed from the Nephites, He did something personal and tender with His twelve disciples. He asked each of them a simple question: **"What is it that ye desire of me?"** (3 Nephi 28:1).

Nine of the twelve gave the same answer. They wanted to live long lives, continue serving the Lord in their ministry, and then when their work was done, come to Him in His kingdom. Jesus told them that after they reached the age of seventy-two, they would come to Him in His kingdom, and their joy would be full. It was a beautiful promise: a faithful life followed by rest with the Savior.

But **three of the disciples** wanted something different. They were too embarrassed to say it out loud, but Jesus already knew what was in their hearts. He said to them, "Ye have desired the thing which John my beloved... desired of me," the same thing the apostle John had asked for in Jerusalem. These three wanted to **remain on the earth** and continue preaching the gospel until Jesus came again at His **Second Coming** (3 Nephi 28:4-9).

Jesus granted their request. He told them they would never taste death. They would live to see all of God's purposes fulfilled, and then when He came again, they would be changed in the twinkling of an eye from mortality to immortality. They would never experience the pain of death.

The three were immediately **changed**. Their bodies were transformed so they could not feel pain and could not die. They were caught up into heaven, saw and heard unspeakable things, and were then returned to earth to begin their ministry.

According to the record, the Three Nephites went on to preach, to be cast into prisons, into dens of wild beasts, and into furnaces, and nothing could harm them. They **ministered for centuries** among the Nephites, and Mormon, who wrote the record hundreds of years later, said they had ministered to him personally.

The story of the Three Nephites teaches us that people can desire different righteous things, and God honors those differences. The nine who wanted to come to Christ were not less faithful than the three who wanted to stay. Both desires were good. Jesus honored both.

It also tells us that the work of spreading the gospel is so important that three men chose to give up death itself rather than stop doing it.
