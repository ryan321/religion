### Lesson 6.4 -- Jesus Departs

After teaching, healing, blessing the children, establishing His Church, correcting the records, and commissioning His twelve disciples, the time came for Jesus to **leave**. He had done everything He came to do. And then, as recorded in **3 Nephi 18:38-39**, He ascended into heaven.

Try to imagine what that moment felt like for the people watching. For days, they had been in the **presence of the resurrected Lord**. They had heard His voice. They had felt the wounds in His hands, His feet, and His side. They had watched Him heal the sick -- every single one of them. They had seen Him take their children one by one and bless them while He wept. They had watched angels descend from heaven and encircle their little ones in fire. They had eaten the bread and wine He blessed. They had heard Him pray with groans so deep that the words couldn't be written down.

And now He was leaving.

This was the most **sacred event** in Nephite history. Nothing before it or after it would compare. Lehi's departure from Jerusalem was important. The Brother of Jared seeing the Lord's finger was remarkable. Alma's conversion was powerful. But this -- the visit of the resurrected Christ to an entire people -- was the centerpiece of the entire Book of Mormon.

Everything before 3 Nephi points **forward** to this event. The prophecies, the signs, the wars, the preaching -- all of it was leading to the moment when Christ would come. And everything after 3 Nephi points **backward** to it. The people measured their lives and their faithfulness by how well they remembered and kept what Jesus taught them during this visit.

When Jesus ascended, the Nephites were left with a responsibility. They had **seen** the risen Lord. They had **heard** His teachings. They had **felt** His love. Now they had to **live** what He taught. No more waiting for the Messiah to come -- He had come. Now the question was whether they would keep His commandments, remember His words, and follow the example He had set.

For a time, they did. The years after Christ's visit were the most peaceful and righteous period in Nephite history. The people lived in unity, shared everything, and there were no divisions among them. Jesus' visit changed them completely -- at least for a while.

The departure of Jesus reminds us that having a testimony is not the end of the journey. It's the **beginning**. Knowing the truth means you now have to live it, every day, for the rest of your life.
