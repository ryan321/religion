### Lesson 7.1 -- A Perfect Society

What would it be like if everyone in your town loved each other so much that nobody ever fought, stole, or said mean things? It sounds impossible, right? But after Jesus Christ visited the Nephites and Lamanites, that is exactly what happened -- and it lasted for about **two hundred years**.

The book of **4 Nephi** tells us about this incredible time. After Jesus taught the people, organized His church, and chose twelve disciples, the people actually listened. They didn't just agree with His words on Sunday and forget them on Monday. They changed their whole lives. Every single person was baptized, received the Holy Ghost, and began living the way Jesus asked them to live.

The results were amazing. The scriptures say there were "no contentions and disputations among them" (4 Nephi 1:2). No arguments. No jealousy. No fights over who had more stuff. Everyone shared what they had, so there were no rich people and no poor people. They were completely equal.

Crime disappeared. The record says there were **"no robbers, nor murderers, neither were there Lamanites, nor any manner of -ites; but they were in one, the children of Christ"** (4 Nephi 1:17). Think about that. The old labels -- Nephite, Lamanite, Jacobite, all of them -- simply vanished. People stopped dividing themselves into groups. They saw themselves as one family, all belonging to Christ.

This wasn't just a short burst of good behavior. It went on for generation after generation. They built new cities. They rebuilt cities that had been destroyed during the terrible storms and earthquakes before Jesus came. The population grew. They spread across the land and prospered in every way.

This period stands out as the **only sustained time of true peace** in the entire Book of Mormon. Before this, even the best years had wars lurking around the corner. But for roughly two centuries, the people lived in harmony because they followed Christ completely.

It proves something important: peace is possible. When people truly love God and love each other, society works the way it was always meant to. The question is not whether a perfect society can exist -- 4 Nephi shows us it can. The question is whether people will choose to keep it that way.

As we will see in the next lesson, what made it work wasn't a perfect government or a perfect set of rules. It was something deeper than that.
