### Lesson 7.2 -- What Made It Work

Imagine two classrooms. In the first one, the teacher has a hundred strict rules posted on the wall, and kids follow them only because they are afraid of getting in trouble. In the second classroom, the kids are kind to each other because they genuinely care. Which classroom would be a better place to learn? The second one, and that is the secret behind the two hundred years of peace among the Nephites.

The people didn't live in harmony because they had a perfect government or a perfect set of laws. The scripture tells us the reason plainly: **"because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people"** (4 Nephi 1:15). That is it. The source was love: love for God and love for each other. Everything else flowed from that.

Because they loved God, they kept His commandments willingly, not grudgingly. Because they loved each other, they shared what they had. The scriptures say they had **"all things common among them"** (4 Nephi 1:3). That means no one hoarded wealth while a neighbor went hungry. If someone needed something, others helped. There were no rich people looking down on poor people because those categories didn't exist.

They also **lived the gospel completely**. They didn't pick and choose the parts they liked. They were baptized, they prayed, they worshipped together, and they followed the teachings Jesus had given them when He visited. The twelve disciples that Jesus had chosen led the church, and the people followed their counsel.

This total commitment produced real results. They built beautiful cities. Families grew and multiplied across the land. They prospered not because they were chasing money but because God blessed them when they lived His way.

Here is the important lesson for us: you cannot force people to be good. Laws and rules are necessary, but they are not enough by themselves. Real, lasting peace comes from the inside out. It starts with a change of heart, wanting to do right because you love God, not just because you are told to.

Think about your own life. When you help a friend because you genuinely care, it feels different from helping because a parent told you to. Both actions are good, but the one that comes from love is stronger and longer-lasting. That is what powered this extraordinary society for two centuries.

The Nephites showed us that when the love of God fills people's hearts, everything changes. Sadly, as we will learn next, that love didn't last forever.
