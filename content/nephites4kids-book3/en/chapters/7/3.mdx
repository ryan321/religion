### Lesson 7.3 -- The Decline Begins

Have you ever built something amazing out of blocks or sand, only to watch it slowly fall apart? That is what happened to the most peaceful society in the Book of Mormon. After nearly two hundred years of living in harmony, the Nephite people began to change -- and not for the better.

It started with **pride**. Some people began to think they were better than others. They stopped sharing their possessions and started caring more about fancy clothes, expensive things, and looking important. The scripture says they "began to be divided into classes" (4 Nephi 1:26). Remember, for two centuries there had been no rich or poor. Now people were separating themselves again -- the wealthy over here, the less fortunate over there.

Next came **false churches**. People started building churches not to worship God but to **make money** (4 Nephi 1:26). They told people what they wanted to hear instead of what was true. Some of these churches taught that Jesus Christ had not really come, or that His gospel didn't matter anymore. They **denied the true church of Christ** and persecuted those who still believed.

The old tribal divisions came roaring back. For two hundred years there had been no "-ites" -- no Nephites, no Lamanites, no Jacobites. Now people started calling themselves by those old names again (4 Nephi 1:36-38). It was as if they had forgotten everything Jesus taught about being one people, one family.

And then came the worst part: the **Gadianton robbers** returned (4 Nephi 1:42). These secret combinations -- groups that used murder, lies, and secret oaths to get power and money -- had been completely destroyed during the time of peace. But wickedness gave them room to grow again. Like weeds in a neglected garden, they spread quickly once people stopped caring for what was right.

By the end of 4 Nephi, the picture is heartbreaking. The people who once had everything -- peace, love, equality, the blessings of God -- threw it all away. They traded a perfect society for pride, selfishness, and division.

The saddest part is how unnecessary it was. Nobody forced them to abandon their way of life. They chose it, one bad decision at a time. Pride crept in quietly, and by the time people noticed how far they had fallen, the damage was enormous. The golden age was over, and dark times were coming.
