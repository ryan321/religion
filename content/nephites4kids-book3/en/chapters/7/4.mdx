### Lesson 7.4 -- How the Best Was Lost

Here is one of the most important questions in the entire Book of Mormon: How do you go from the most perfect society ever recorded to complete wickedness in just a few generations?

Think about it this way. The people who were alive when Jesus visited had an incredible experience. They **saw** Him. They **heard** His voice. They **felt the marks in His hands and feet**. When He taught them to love one another, they didn't need to take someone else's word for it. They had been there. Their faith was built on personal experience, and it was rock solid.

Their children grew up hearing those stories directly from people who had been there. Mom and Dad could say, "I stood in that crowd. I watched Him heal the sick. I heard Him pray." That was powerful. The second generation had strong faith because they were close to the source.

But what about the **third generation**? And the fourth? And the fifth? By then, the visit of Jesus was a story from long ago. Great-great-grandparents had passed away. The living memory faded. People had only **stories and records**, with no personal witnesses left to talk to. And for some, stories were not enough.

This is where the trouble started. Without **personal conversion**, without each person finding Christ for themselves through prayer, scripture study, and living the gospel, even the most perfect society will crumble. You cannot inherit a testimony the way you inherit a house. Every single person has to build their own.

Some people in these later generations decided the old stories were just legends. Others thought the gospel was outdated and didn't apply to them. They stopped praying. They stopped sharing. They started wanting power and wealth instead of love and unity. And piece by piece, the perfect society fell apart.

This teaches us something that matters right now, today. It does not matter how faithful your parents or grandparents are. Their testimony cannot save you; only your own can. Every generation has to find Christ for itself. You have to read the scriptures, pray, and discover for yourself that Jesus is real and that His gospel is true.

The Nephites had every advantage. They had seen the Lord Himself. Yet within a few generations, their descendants threw it all away because they never made it personal. The lesson is clear: faith is not inherited. It must be chosen, built, and renewed by each individual in every generation.

Don't let the best be lost in your life. Make your faith your own.
