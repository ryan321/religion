### Lesson 8.1 -- Who Mormon Was

The Book of Mormon is named after one man: **Mormon**. But who was he? Where did he come from, and why does his name sit on the cover of one of the most important books in the world?

Mormon was born around **311 AD**, near the end of Nephite civilization. His people were already in deep trouble. Wickedness was everywhere, wars were constant, and the days of peace after Christ's visit were a distant memory. It was a hard time to grow up.

His name came from a special place. He was named after the **Waters of Mormon**, the lake where Alma the Elder had secretly baptized believers hundreds of years earlier (Mosiah 18). That baptism site was a symbol of faith, courage, and the covenant people make with God. Giving a child that name was like saying, "We remember. We still believe."

When Mormon was just **ten years old**, something remarkable happened. A man named **Ammaron** came to him. Ammaron was the keeper of the sacred Nephite records, the massive collection of plates, histories, and scriptures that prophets had been writing on for nearly a thousand years. Ammaron could see that Mormon was no ordinary boy. He told young Mormon that when he turned twenty-four, he should go to a hill called **Shim** and retrieve the plates of Nephi. Ammaron described exactly where the records were buried and gave Mormon specific instructions about what to do with them (Mormon 1:1-5).

Think about that for a moment. A ten-year-old boy was being trusted with the most important records in the history of his entire civilization. Ammaron didn't choose a general, a king, or a high priest for this job. He chose a child, because God had prepared that child for something enormous.

The scriptures describe young Mormon as "a sober child" who was "quick to observe" (Mormon 1:2). He was serious, thoughtful, and paid attention to things around him. Even as a boy, he had the qualities that would make him one of the greatest prophets and record keepers in all of scripture.

Mormon didn't know yet everything that lay ahead: the wars, the heartbreak, the long nights spent writing by lamplight. But God knew. And God had been preparing Mormon from the very beginning, placing him in the right time, with the right name, and with the right heart for a work that would bless millions of people across the centuries.
