### Lesson 8.2 -- A Boy Prophet

Most fifteen-year-olds worry about school, friends, and figuring out who they are. Mormon was no different in some ways; he was still a teenager. But at age fifteen, something happened that set him apart from almost everyone around him.

Mormon tells us himself: **"I, being fifteen years of age and being somewhat of a sober mind, therefore I was visited of the Lord, and tasted and knew of the goodness of Jesus"** (Mormon 1:15). At fifteen, Mormon had a personal experience with the Savior. He didn't just believe in Jesus because his parents told him to. He **knew**, because the Lord Himself had visited him.

Filled with this experience, Mormon wanted to share what he had learned. He tried to **preach** to his people. He wanted to tell them about Jesus, about repentance, about the hope that was still available even in their darkened world. But the Lord told him to stop. He was forbidden to preach because the people had become so wicked that they had "willfully rebelled against their God" (Mormon 1:16). They didn't want to hear it. Their hearts were too hard.

Imagine how painful that must have been. You have tasted the goodness of Jesus, you know the truth, and you want desperately to help the people around you, but they refuse to listen. Mormon had to carry that knowledge in his heart and wait.

Then, at just **sixteen years old**, Mormon was appointed to lead the Nephite armies (Mormon 2:1). A teenager. Leading thousands of soldiers in a real war, fighting for the survival of his entire civilization. He wasn't playing a video game or reading about battles in a book. He was standing on actual battlefields, making life-and-death decisions, watching people he cared about get hurt and killed.

Think about how unusual this was. At sixteen, Mormon was both a **prophet** and a **military commander**. He had the spiritual sensitivity to be visited by the Lord and the physical courage and leadership ability to command an army. He combined qualities that rarely exist in one person, let alone in a teenager.

Mormon's youth teaches us that God does not wait until people are old to use them. He calls the young, the unlikely, and the underestimated. If you feel like you are too young to make a difference, remember Mormon. He was visited by the Lord at fifteen and leading armies at sixteen. Age is no barrier when God has a mission for you.
