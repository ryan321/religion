### Lesson 9.3 -- Gathering at Cumorah

Mormon could see the end coming. The Nephites were losing the war, their armies were shrinking, and there was no hope of turning things around. So Mormon made a decision that would shape the final chapter of Nephite history. He decided to **gather his people** for one last stand.

Mormon wrote a letter to the **king of the Lamanites**, asking for time. He requested that the Lamanites allow the Nephites to gather together at a place called the **land of Cumorah**, near a hill by the same name (Mormon 6:2). Remarkably, the Lamanite king agreed. He gave the Nephites the time they needed to assemble.

Why Cumorah? This hill was significant. It was where Ammaron had originally hidden the sacred records years earlier. Mormon knew this land, and he knew it would be the place where the Nephite story would end and where the records would be preserved for the future.

While the people gathered, Mormon took care of the most important task of all. He went to the Hill Cumorah and **hid all the sacred records** -- the vast collection of plates that contained the entire history of the Nephite people (Mormon 6:6). A thousand years of prophecy, history, sermons, and revelation, sealed away in the earth.

But Mormon did not bury everything. He kept out the **abridged plates** -- the condensed record he had spent years creating -- and gave them to his son **Moroni**. These plates would become the Book of Mormon. Mormon knew he likely would not survive the coming battle, and he needed someone to carry the record forward. Moroni was that person.

Then the Nephites gathered. Mormon organized them into groups, each led by a commander. The numbers were staggering: **230,000 warriors** assembled at Cumorah, along with their wives and children. This was not just an army -- it was an entire civilization making its last stand.

Picture the scene. Thousands upon thousands of families camping around a hill, knowing what was coming. Parents holding children. Soldiers sharpening swords. Mormon walking among them, the prophet-general who had led them for decades, now preparing them for a battle he knew they could not win.

Everything was in place. The records were hidden. The abridged plates were in Moroni's hands. The people were gathered. All that remained was the battle itself -- the last battle of the Nephite nation.
