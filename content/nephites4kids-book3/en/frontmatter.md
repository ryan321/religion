## Introduction

This is the final book. And it contains both the most sacred and the most heartbreaking events in the entire Book of Mormon.

You'll start with a civilization tearing itself apart -- the Gadianton robbers, the collapse of government, and a nation rushing toward destruction. Then the destruction comes. Cities burn, sink, and are buried. Three days of absolute darkness cover the land. And in that darkness, the voice of Jesus Christ speaks.

What happens next is the climax of the entire Book of Mormon. Jesus Christ descends from heaven, stands among 2,500 Nephites at the temple in Bountiful, and invites every one of them to come forward and feel the wounds in His hands and feet. He teaches them. He heals their sick. He blesses their children one by one, weeping as He does. Angels descend and encircle the children in fire.

Then comes 200 years of perfect peace -- the only time in the record when the people fully lived the gospel. And then the slow, terrible decline back into wickedness, war, and destruction.

You'll meet Mormon, the prophet-warrior who compiled the entire Book of Mormon while watching his civilization die. You'll walk with Moroni, the last Nephite, wandering alone for decades with a record he would bury for 1,400 years. You'll learn the story of the Jaredites -- an even older civilization that made the same mistakes. And you'll read Moroni's final promise: ask God if this book is true, and He will tell you.

This is where the story ends. And where your story with the Book of Mormon begins.

## How to use this book

Same format as Books 1 and 2: 15 chapters, 4 lessons per chapter, one chapter per week. Each lesson is about 15-20 minutes. Scripture references throughout.

## Note to parents

This book covers some of the most sacred events in scripture (Christ's appearance, the blessing of the children) and some of the most intense (the destruction, the final wars, Moroni alone). The lessons handle both with appropriate reverence and honesty. The final chapters lead to Moroni's promise -- a personal invitation that your child can act on. This may be a good time to read Moroni 10 together as a family.
