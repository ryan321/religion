### Lesson 1.1 -- What Is the Plan of Salvation?

Have you ever looked up at the stars and wondered, "Why am I here? Where did I come from? What happens after I die?" These are the biggest questions anyone can ask, and Heavenly Father didn't leave us to guess the answers. He gave us a plan, and the scriptures call it by several names: the **plan of salvation**, the **plan of happiness**, and the **plan of mercy** (Alma 42:5, 8, 15).

The plan of salvation is Heavenly Father's plan for us to come to earth, learn, grow, and eventually return to live with Him again. It covers everything: where we were before we were born, why we're here now, and what happens after we die. It's like a giant map that shows the whole journey of your life, and not just the part you can see right now.

Here's something important: **every single teaching, commandment, and covenant in the gospel exists because of this plan.** Why do we get baptized? Because it's part of the plan. Why do we take the sacrament? Because it helps us stay on the path the plan describes. Why do we have prophets, scriptures, and temples? Because they all point us back to the plan and help us follow it.

Think of it like building a house. The plan of salvation is the blueprint. Every nail, every board, and every window has a purpose because the blueprint says where it goes. Without the blueprint, the pieces wouldn't make sense. In the same way, when you understand the plan, every part of the gospel starts to make sense. You can see how everything fits together.

The plan is also perfectly **just** and perfectly **merciful** at the same time. Justice means everyone is treated fairly, with real consequences for our choices. Mercy means we're given help we don't deserve: a way to fix our mistakes and keep going. Most plans in the world have to choose one or the other. Heavenly Father's plan has both, and that's what makes it so remarkable.

As we study each part of the plan in the lessons ahead, you'll see how Heavenly Father thought of everything. Nothing was left out. Nothing was an accident. This plan was designed by a perfect, loving God, and it was designed for you.
