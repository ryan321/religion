### Lesson 1.2 -- Intelligences

Here's something amazing that most people never think about: you didn't begin when you were born. You didn't even begin when you became a spirit child of Heavenly Father. Something about you has existed forever.

The scriptures teach that before we became spirit children of God, we existed as **intelligences**. Doctrine and Covenants 93:29 says, "Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created, neither indeed can be." That means there is a part of you -- the core of who you are -- that was never created. It has always existed. Nobody made it. It just is.

We don't know everything about what intelligences were like before becoming spirit children. The scriptures don't give us a lot of details about that stage. But we do know that intelligences were real, and that they were different from each other. Abraham 3:22-23 says that God looked among the intelligences and saw "that they were good," and He said, "These I will make my rulers." Some intelligences stood out. Some were more noble and great than others. That tells us that even before we had spirit bodies, there were differences -- each intelligence was unique.

Why does this matter? Because it means **something about you is eternal**. You are not just a random accident. You are not a temporary thing that popped into existence and will someday disappear. The deepest part of who you are has always been real, and it will always be real. That gives your life incredible meaning.

Think about it this way. Imagine someone hands you a rock and says, "This rock appeared five minutes ago and will vanish in five more minutes." You probably wouldn't care much about it. But what if they said, "This rock has existed forever and will exist forever"? You'd look at it very differently. You'd treat it with a lot more respect. That's how you should think about yourself -- and about every person you meet. Each one carries something eternal inside.

Heavenly Father saw these intelligences and loved them. He wanted to help them grow and progress. And so He organized them into spirit children -- giving them spirit bodies, making them part of His family, and opening the door to something greater. That's where the plan of salvation really begins to take shape.

Understanding intelligences helps us understand the plan. The plan isn't about creating something from nothing. It's about helping something eternal become something even greater.
