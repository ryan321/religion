### Lesson 1.3 -- It's All About Progression

If you had to describe the plan of salvation in one word, that word might be **progression**. The whole plan is about helping you become more than you are right now and ultimately becoming more like God.

The Prophet Joseph Smith taught this clearly. In what is known as the **King Follett discourse**, he said that we must "learn how to be gods yourselves... the same as all gods have done before you." That's a breathtaking idea. God isn't trying to keep you small. He isn't trying to keep a distance between you and Him. He wants you to become like Him, and the plan of salvation is the roadmap to get there.

Let's trace the path of progression:

**Intelligence** -- You began as an intelligence, something eternal that was never created (D&C 93:29). This was the starting point, real but not yet everything you could become.

**Spirit** -- Heavenly Father organized your intelligence into a spirit body, making you His child. Now you had a spirit form, a personality, and the ability to learn, choose, and grow in His presence.

**Mortal body** -- You came to earth and received a physical body. This body lets you experience things a spirit alone cannot: pain, hunger, tiredness, but also the taste of food, the warmth of the sun, and the feeling of a hug. Mortality is hard, but it's a necessary step.

**Resurrected body** -- After death, your spirit and body will be reunited permanently through the Resurrection. Your body will be perfected, with no more sickness and no more death. This is a gift made possible by Jesus Christ (1 Corinthians 15:22).

**Exaltation** -- For those who follow the plan fully, the final step is exaltation: living with God, becoming like Him, and receiving all that He has.

Do you see the pattern? At every stage, you gain something new. You grow. You become more. Nothing is taken away; things are added. That's what progression means.

This is why the commandments, ordinances, and covenants of the gospel matter so much. They aren't random rules. They are the steps along the path of progression. Baptism, receiving the Holy Ghost, partaking of the sacrament, temple covenants. Each one moves you forward on the road to becoming like your Heavenly Father.

The plan of salvation isn't just a nice story about where you came from and where you're going. It's the roadmap for the most important journey you'll ever take: the journey of becoming who you were meant to be.
