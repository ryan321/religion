### Lesson 1.4 -- God's Work and Glory

What does God spend His time doing? What matters most to Him? We don't have to guess, because He told us directly. In Moses 1:39, He declared: "For behold, this is my work and my glory -- to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man."

Read that again carefully. God -- the most powerful being in the universe, the Creator of worlds without number -- says that **His work** and **His glory** are focused on one thing: **your progression**. Not His own comfort. Not building things for Himself. You. Helping you gain immortality (living forever with a resurrected body) and eternal life (living the kind of life God lives).

That's an incredible thing to realize. The God of the universe is personally invested in your success. Every scripture, every prophet, every commandment, every ordinance, every covenant, every prayer that is answered -- all of it exists to serve one purpose: helping you progress and return to Him.

Think about the best teacher you've ever had. That teacher probably spent hours preparing lessons, grading your work, and finding ways to help you understand difficult things. Now imagine a teacher who has been doing that for you since before you were born -- a teacher who knows you perfectly, loves you completely, and has infinite patience. That's what Heavenly Father is doing. His entire focus is on helping His children grow.

This also means that nothing in the gospel is pointless. Sometimes commandments can feel like a list of rules that don't matter much. But when you understand Moses 1:39, you realize that every commandment has a purpose -- it's there to help you progress. The Word of Wisdom helps your body (D&C 89:18-21). The law of tithing teaches you to trust God and manage what you have. The commandment to forgive helps you become more like Christ. Nothing is busywork. Everything serves the purpose of your eternal growth.

Here's one more thing to think about: if God's greatest glory comes from your progression, then what brings Him sorrow? When His children stop progressing. When they choose paths that lead away from growth. When they give up on themselves. That's not what He wants for anyone -- and that's why He gave us a Savior, so that even when we stumble, we can get back on the path.

The plan of salvation exists because God loves you and wants the very best for you. His work is your future. His glory is your success.
