### Lesson 10.1 -- A Change of Heart

The plan of salvation doesn't just ask you to do good things. It asks you to **become a different person**. That might sound surprising. You might think that being a good member of the Church means following the rules, being kind, and going to church every Sunday. Those things matter, but they're not the deepest part of the plan. The plan is about transformation, about your very nature changing from the inside out.

The Book of Mormon teaches this clearly. In Mosiah 5:2, King Benjamin's people described what happened to them after hearing his powerful sermon: "The Spirit of the Lord Omnipotent... has wrought a **mighty change in us, or in our hearts**, that we have no more disposition to do evil, but to do good continually." Notice what changed. It wasn't just their behavior. It was their hearts. Their desires shifted. They didn't just stop wanting to do wrong because they were afraid of punishment. They stopped wanting to do wrong because they had become different people.

Later, the prophet Alma asked his own people a piercing question: "Have ye spiritually been **born of God**? Have ye received his image in your countenances? Have ye experienced this **mighty change** in your hearts?" (Alma 5:14). Alma wasn't asking if they had checked off a list of commandments. He was asking if something real had happened inside them, if they had been changed by the power of God.

This is what it means to be "born again." It's not just a phrase people use. It describes a real spiritual transformation. Think of a caterpillar becoming a butterfly. The caterpillar doesn't just put on wings; it becomes an entirely new creature. That's what Heavenly Father wants for each of us. He doesn't just want us to act like His children. He wants us to truly **become** like Him, to have His kind of love, His patience, His desire to bless others.

This change doesn't usually happen all at once. For most people, it's gradual. You might notice that things you used to enjoy (like being mean or dishonest) start to bother you. You might find yourself wanting to help others more than you used to. These are signs that the mighty change is happening in your heart.

The beautiful part is that you don't have to make this change alone. The Savior's Atonement and the power of the Holy Ghost work together to change you from the inside. Your job is to keep choosing to follow Christ, and He will do the transforming.
