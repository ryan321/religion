### Lesson 10.2 -- Born of Water and Spirit

Jesus taught one of the most important truths in all of scripture when He told Nicodemus: "Except a man be **born of water and of the Spirit**, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God" (John 3:5). This verse tells us exactly what spiritual rebirth requires: two things working together.

Being "born of water" means **baptism**. When you go down into the water and come back up, it represents dying to your old life and rising to a new life in Christ. The water washes away your sins and marks the beginning of your covenant with God. It's the doorway into His kingdom.

Being "born of the Spirit" means receiving the **Holy Ghost**. After baptism, someone holding the Melchizedek Priesthood places their hands on your head and confirms you a member of the Church, giving you the gift of the Holy Ghost. This is the power that actually changes you from the inside. The Holy Ghost works in your heart, helping you feel what is right, giving you strength to choose good, and gradually making you more like your Heavenly Father.

Water and Spirit work together. Baptism alone isn't enough. You also need the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost. And the Holy Ghost's influence is connected to the covenants you make at baptism. They are partners in the process of being born again.

But here's something critical to understand: being born again doesn't end the moment you're baptized and confirmed. It's a **lifelong process**. Every day, you choose whether to follow Christ or turn away. Every time you repent, you renew that process. Every time you take the sacrament, you recommit to your baptismal covenants and invite the Spirit back into your life. The Savior taught that we must "take up [our] cross daily" (Luke 9:23), meaning this isn't a one-time event.

Think about being born physically. A baby doesn't stop growing after birth. That's when growth really begins. Spiritual rebirth works the same way. Baptism and confirmation are the beginning, not the finish line. The rest of your life is spent growing, learning, repenting, and relying on the Holy Ghost to keep changing your heart.

Daily repentance, daily reliance on the Spirit, and daily choices to follow Christ. This is what it means to be continually born again. As the Apostle Paul wrote, "Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh" (Galatians 5:16). Every day is a chance to choose the new life Christ offers.
