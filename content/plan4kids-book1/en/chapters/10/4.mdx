### Lesson 10.4 -- Joint Heirs with Christ

The Apostle Paul taught one of the most breathtaking truths in all of scripture: "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God: and if children, then **heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ**" (Romans 8:16-17). Read that carefully. You are not just saved. You are not just rescued from punishment. You are an **heir** -- and not just any heir, but a joint-heir with Jesus Christ Himself.

What does "joint-heir" mean? In the law, a joint heir inherits **everything** the other heir inherits. If two people are joint heirs of a fortune, they both receive the full fortune -- not half each, but all of it. So being a joint-heir with Christ means you can inherit everything that Christ inherits from the Father. And what does Christ inherit? **Everything.**

The Lord confirmed this in modern revelation: "And he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore **all that my Father hath shall be given unto him**" (D&C 84:38). All that the Father has -- His power, His glory, His knowledge, His eternal life, His capacity to create and to love. This isn't a small promise. This is the ultimate promise of the entire plan of salvation.

This is what the scriptures call **exaltation**. It goes far beyond just being "saved" or going to heaven. Exaltation means becoming like God -- receiving His nature, His attributes, and His kind of life. It means living forever with your family in His presence. It means continuing to grow and progress and love for all eternity.

And here is the remarkable part: this promise is available to **every single person** who walks the covenant path. It's not reserved for prophets or apostles or people who seem perfect. Heavenly Father offers it to every one of His children -- every person who will exercise faith in Jesus Christ, repent, be baptized, receive the Holy Ghost, and endure faithfully to the end. The prophet Nephi taught, "The Lord God worketh not in darkness... He doeth not anything save it be for the benefit of the world; for he loveth the world" (2 Nephi 26:23-24).

This is the purpose of the whole plan. Everything -- the premortal life, the creation, the fall, the Atonement, the ordinances, the covenants, the commandments -- all of it leads here. It all exists so that you, a child of God, can become like your Father and inherit everything He has. That is the plan of salvation, and it was designed for you.
