### Lesson 11.1 -- What Happens When You Die

One of the biggest questions people ask is, "What happens after I die?" The plan of salvation gives us a clear answer. When you die, your spirit separates from your physical body. Your body is buried in the ground, but your spirit -- the real you, the part that thinks and feels and loves -- continues to live. It goes to a place the scriptures call the **spirit world**.

The prophet Alma taught about this in detail. He said that "the spirits of all men, as soon as they are departed from this mortal body... are taken home to that God who gave them life" (Alma 40:11). This doesn't mean everyone immediately stands before God for judgment. It means God is aware of every spirit, and each one goes to a place He has prepared.

Alma explained that in the spirit world, there are two general conditions: **paradise** and **prison**. "The spirits of those who are righteous are received into a state of happiness, which is called paradise, a state of rest, a state of peace, where they shall rest from all their troubles and from all care, and sorrow" (Alma 40:12). Those who lived righteously and accepted the gospel find peace and rest after they die.

But what about those who didn't know the gospel, or who knew it and rejected it? Alma taught that these spirits go to a different condition -- sometimes called spirit prison. He described it as a state of "darkness" and "awful, fearful looking for the fiery indignation of the wrath of God" (Alma 40:13-14). This isn't a permanent punishment -- it's a temporary state where spirits who haven't yet accepted the gospel can still learn and change.

Here's the key: both paradise and prison are **temporary**. Nobody stays in the spirit world forever. Every person -- every single one -- will eventually be resurrected and brought before God for judgment. The spirit world is like a waiting room between death and resurrection. It's a place where spirits continue to learn, grow, and make choices.

Understanding the spirit world helps us see that death is not the end. It's a transition -- another step in the plan. And because of Jesus Christ, it's a step that leads to resurrection and new life, not to permanent darkness. The plan doesn't stop when your body stops.
