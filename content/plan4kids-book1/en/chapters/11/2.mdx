### Lesson 11.2 -- Spirit Paradise and Prison

In the last lesson, we learned that the spirit world has two conditions -- paradise and prison. Now let's look more closely at what happens in each one and how they're connected.

**Spirit paradise** is a place of rest and happiness for righteous spirits. But "rest" doesn't mean sitting around doing nothing. The righteous in paradise continue to learn, grow, and serve. They are free from the pains and troubles of mortal life, but they are busy with important work. In fact, one of the most important things they do is **teach the gospel to spirits in prison**.

**Spirit prison** is the condition of spirits who either never heard the gospel during their mortal lives or who heard it but didn't accept it. Many of these spirits aren't terrible people -- they simply never had the chance to learn about Jesus Christ, baptism, or the plan of salvation. Others made mistakes but still have the opportunity to learn and change.

The Apostle Peter taught that "the gospel [was] preached also to them that are dead, that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit" (1 Peter 4:6). This is a stunning truth -- the gospel is preached to people even after they die. Death doesn't cut anyone off from hearing the good news of Jesus Christ.

But who does the teaching? In 1918, President Joseph F. Smith received a remarkable vision, now recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 138. He saw that when Jesus died and His spirit went to the spirit world, He didn't go to spirit prison Himself. Instead, "from among the righteous, he organized his forces and appointed messengers, clothed with power and authority, and **commissioned them to go forth and carry the light of the gospel to them that were in darkness**" (D&C 138:29-30). Jesus organized the righteous spirits in paradise to be missionaries to those in prison.

This vision also tells us who these missionary spirits were: "the faithful elders of this dispensation, when they depart from mortal life, continue their labors in the preaching of the gospel" (D&C 138:57). People who served faithfully in this life keep serving after they die. The work doesn't stop.

This means the spirit world is a place of continued agency and opportunity. Spirits in prison can hear the gospel, feel the Spirit, and choose to accept or reject the message -- just as people do on earth. The plan of salvation doesn't give up on anyone.
