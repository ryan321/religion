### Lesson 11.3 -- Everyone Gets a Chance

Here is one of the most beautiful parts of the entire plan of salvation: **every single person who has ever lived will receive a fair chance to hear and accept the gospel of Jesus Christ.** No one is left out. No one is forgotten.

Think about this: billions of people have lived on the earth without ever hearing the name of Jesus Christ. They were born in times and places where the gospel simply wasn't available. A child born in ancient China, a mother living in medieval Africa, a family in a remote village centuries before missionaries arrived. None of them had the opportunity to be baptized or make covenants. Are they lost forever? Absolutely not.

The plan accounts for every one of them. As we learned, the gospel is taught to spirits in the spirit world (1 Peter 4:6). But hearing the gospel isn't enough. People also need ordinances like baptism. And since baptism requires a physical body and water, spirits can't be baptized for themselves. So how does God solve this?

Through **temple work**. Living members of the Church perform baptisms and other ordinances on behalf of those who have died. Paul mentioned this practice in the New Testament: "Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if the dead rise not at all? Why are they then baptized for the dead?" (1 Corinthians 15:29). Baptism for the dead was practiced in the early Church because it's part of God's plan.

The Lord confirmed this in modern revelation: "These are principles in relation to the dead and the living that cannot be lightly passed over... For their salvation is necessary and essential to our salvation" (D&C 128:15, 18). Our salvation is connected to theirs. We need them, and they need us.

This is what makes the plan **perfectly fair**. No one is denied an ordinance because of when or where they lived. A person born 3,000 years ago in a place with no knowledge of Christ has the same opportunity for exaltation as someone born into a Latter-day Saint family today. The circumstances are different, but the opportunity is equal.

This fairness reveals the character of God. He is not a God who saves some and forgets others. He is a God who designed a plan where **every single child** can come home. The plan of salvation is not just for a few. It is for everyone, and it works for everyone.
