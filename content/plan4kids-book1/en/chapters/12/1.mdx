### Lesson 12.1 -- The Resurrection

One of the greatest gifts in all of Heavenly Father's plan is the **resurrection**, the reuniting of your spirit with your physical body after death. And the best part? It's a **free gift** given to every single person who has ever lived, made possible by the Atonement of Jesus Christ.

Jesus Christ was the first person to be resurrected. The Apostle Paul taught: "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the **firstfruits** of them that slept. For since by man came death, by man came also the resurrection of the dead. For **as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive**" (1 Corinthians 15:20-22). Because Adam fell, every person dies. Because Christ rose, every person will rise again. This is not something you have to earn; it comes to everyone, the righteous and the wicked alike.

What will the resurrection be like? The prophet Alma described it clearly: "The soul shall be **restored to the body, and the body to the soul**; yea, and every limb and joint shall be restored to its body; yea, even a hair of the head shall not be lost; but all things shall be restored to their proper and **perfect frame**" (Alma 11:43). Your resurrected body will be perfect. No sickness, no pain, no disability, no weakness. Every imperfection of mortality will be gone. A child who was born blind will see. A grandparent who struggled to walk will run. A body ravaged by disease will be whole and beautiful.

The scriptures teach that not everyone is resurrected at the same time. The righteous are resurrected first. This is called the "first resurrection" or the "resurrection of the just." The wicked are resurrected later. But eventually, every person who has ever lived will be resurrected. No one is left behind (see Alma 11:44).

The resurrection also means that death is **not permanent**. This is why Paul could write with triumph: "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?" (1 Corinthians 15:55). Because of Jesus Christ, the grave cannot hold anyone forever. Death is temporary. Life is eternal.

This free gift of resurrection is one of the clearest signs of God's love for all His children. He didn't offer resurrection only to the faithful. He gave it to everyone. It shows that the plan of salvation is generous beyond anything we could design on our own. Every person will live again, and every person will stand before God in a perfect, immortal body.
