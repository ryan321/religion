### Lesson 12.2 -- The Celestial Kingdom

After the resurrection, every person will be judged and assigned to a kingdom of glory. The highest of these is the **celestial kingdom** -- the place where Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ dwell. This is the destination the entire plan of salvation points toward.

The Lord described those who will inherit the celestial kingdom in Doctrine and Covenants 76. They are those "who received the testimony of Jesus, and believed on his name and were baptized... that by keeping the commandments they might be **washed and cleansed from all their sins**, and receive the Holy Spirit" (D&C 76:51-52). They are people who made and kept covenants -- who accepted the gospel, followed the Savior, and endured faithfully to the end.

But the celestial kingdom isn't just about where you go -- it's about what you **receive**. Those who reach the highest degree of the celestial kingdom receive the greatest of all God's blessings. The Lord promised: "All that my Father hath shall be given unto him" (D&C 84:38). Everything the Father has -- His glory, His power, His knowledge, His eternal life -- becomes theirs. This is **exaltation**, the fullness of salvation.

What does life in the celestial kingdom look like? The scriptures describe it in breathtaking terms. Those who are exalted "shall dwell in the **presence of God** and his Christ forever and ever" (D&C 76:62). They will live with their families eternally, sealed together by priesthood power. They will continue to grow, learn, and progress forever. The glory of the celestial kingdom is compared to the glory of the sun -- the brightest light in the sky (see D&C 76:70, 96).

This is what Heavenly Father wants for every one of His children. He didn't create the plan hoping most people would fail. He created it hoping you would succeed. Every commandment, every ordinance, every covenant, and every blessing of the gospel exists to help you reach this destination.

It's important to understand that reaching the celestial kingdom doesn't require being perfect in this life. It requires being **faithful** -- consistently repenting, renewing covenants, and relying on the Savior's grace. The Atonement of Jesus Christ makes up the difference between your best efforts and perfection. As the prophet Moroni wrote, "Come unto Christ, and be perfected in him... then is his grace sufficient for you" (Moroni 10:32).

The celestial kingdom is real. Exaltation is real. And the path that leads there -- the covenant path -- is open to you right now.
