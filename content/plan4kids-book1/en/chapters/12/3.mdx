### Lesson 12.3 -- The Terrestrial and Telestial Kingdoms

The celestial kingdom is the highest glory, but the plan of salvation provides a place for everyone. God, in His mercy, has prepared other kingdoms of glory as well: the **terrestrial** and **telestial** kingdoms. Understanding these helps us see both God's justice and His generosity.

The **terrestrial kingdom** is for honorable people who lived good lives but didn't fully accept the gospel of Jesus Christ. The Lord described them as those "who are honorable men of the earth, who were blinded by the craftiness of men" (D&C 76:75). They may have believed in Christ but weren't fully valiant in their testimony. They receive the presence of the Son (Jesus Christ visits and ministers to them) but they do not dwell with the Father (see D&C 76:77). The glory of the terrestrial kingdom is compared to the moon, which gives great light but is less than the sun (D&C 76:71, 78, 97).

The **telestial kingdom** is for those who were wicked in this life and "received not the gospel, neither the testimony of Jesus, neither the prophets" (D&C 76:101). These are people who refused to repent during mortality and had to suffer for their sins before receiving a kingdom of glory. They do not receive the presence of the Father or the Son but are ministered to by the Holy Ghost through angels (see D&C 76:86, 88).

Here's something remarkable: even the telestial kingdom, the lowest degree of glory, "**surpasses all understanding**" (D&C 76:89). It's still a kingdom of glory, still beautiful beyond what mortal minds can imagine. God doesn't throw anyone into permanent misery (except for a very rare group we'll discuss in the next lesson). Even those who made terrible choices receive a place that is more glorious than anything on earth.

But (and this is important) the highest blessings require the highest faithfulness. Those in the terrestrial kingdom don't receive exaltation. Those in the telestial kingdom don't receive the fullness of God's glory. The opportunity to become like God, to live with Him as families forever, to inherit all that He has -- those blessings belong to the celestial kingdom.

This teaches us something about God's character. He is perfectly just; people receive according to their choices and faithfulness. But He is also deeply merciful; He provides a place of glory for virtually everyone. As the Apostle Paul wrote, "In my Father's house are many mansions" (John 14:2). God has room for all His children.
