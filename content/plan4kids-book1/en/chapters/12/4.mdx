### Lesson 12.4 -- Perdition

We've learned that God provides a kingdom of glory for nearly everyone. Even the wicked eventually receive the telestial kingdom, which surpasses all understanding. But there is one terrible exception: **sons of perdition**.

The Lord described these individuals in Doctrine and Covenants 76: "Having denied the Holy Spirit after having received it, and having denied the Only Begotten Son of the Father, having **crucified him unto themselves and put him to an open shame**" (D&C 76:35). These are people who received a perfect, undeniable knowledge of God's truth (not just faith or belief, but absolute knowledge through the Holy Spirit) and then **deliberately turned against God and fought Him**.

This is not ordinary sin. This is not someone who struggles with temptation, makes mistakes, or even falls away from the Church for a time. The level of knowledge and rebellion required to become a son of perdition is something very, very few people will ever reach. The Prophet Joseph Smith taught that a person would have to receive the fullness of the gospel, have the heavens opened to them, know God, and then choose to defy Him completely. As the revelation says, "It had been better for them never to have been born" (D&C 76:32).

Sons of perdition do not receive any kingdom of glory. They are cast into **outer darkness** with Satan and his angels (see D&C 76:33, 36-38). They chose to follow Lucifer even after knowing God perfectly, and they share Lucifer's fate. This is the only group in the entire plan of salvation for whom no place of glory is prepared.

Why does this matter for you? Mostly, it should bring you comfort. The plan of salvation is designed so that virtually everyone receives a kingdom of glory. Perdition is the rare, terrible exception, not the rule. God is not waiting to condemn you. He is waiting to bless you. The plan isn't a trap designed for your failure. It's a path designed for your success.

For nearly every person who has ever lived, the plan provides a place of light, beauty, and glory. And for those who faithfully follow the covenant path, it provides **exaltation**, the opportunity to live with God forever and inherit all that He has. As the Apostle Paul taught, "Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which **God hath prepared for them that love him**" (1 Corinthians 2:9).

The plan of salvation is good news. It was designed by a loving Father, made possible by a perfect Savior, and it is meant for you.
