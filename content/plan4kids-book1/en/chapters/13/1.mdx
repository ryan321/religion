### Lesson 13.1 -- The Second Coming

One of the most glorious promises in all the scriptures is that Jesus Christ will come again. When He ascended into heaven after His resurrection, two angels appeared and told His apostles: "This same Jesus, which is taken up from you into heaven, shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven" (Acts 1:11). That promise has never been canceled. Jesus is coming back.

But His Second Coming won't be quiet or hidden. The first time Jesus came to earth, He was born as a tiny baby in a humble stable. The second time will be completely different. He will come in power and glory, with all the hosts of heaven. The Lord Himself declared: "The Son of Man shall come in his glory, and all the holy angels with him" (D&C 29:11). Every eye will see Him. There will be no doubt about who He is.

The book of Revelation gives Him a title that tells you everything you need to know about that day: He will come as "King of Kings, and Lord of Lords" (Revelation 19:16). Not a carpenter from Nazareth. Not a wandering teacher. He comes as the ruler of all creation -- the one who has all power and all authority over every kingdom that has ever existed.

When He comes, the world will be divided. The wicked -- those who have rejected God and refused to repent -- will be destroyed. They cannot survive the glory of His presence. But the righteous will be caught up to meet Him, and they will rejoice. The scriptures describe this as a day of both terror and triumph -- terrible for those who have chosen darkness, and triumphant for those who have followed the light.

And here is the most amazing part: after He comes, Jesus Christ will reign personally upon the earth for a thousand years. This is not a symbol or a metaphor. He will literally be here, governing the earth in righteousness, and everyone will know Him. The long history of war, corruption, and suffering will finally come to an end. The King will be home.

This event is the hinge of all history. Everything before it points toward it, and everything after it flows from it. The Second Coming is the moment when the plan of salvation moves into its final, glorious stage -- and it is perfectly fair, because every soul will have had the chance to hear the gospel and choose.
