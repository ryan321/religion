### Lesson 13.3 -- The Loosing of Satan and the Final Battle

After a thousand years of peace under the Savior's reign, something surprising happens. Satan, who has been bound during the entire Millennium, will be set free "for a little season" (D&C 88:111). The apostle John saw this same event in vision: "When the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be loosed out of his prison, and shall go out to deceive the nations" (Revelation 20:7-8).

Why would God allow this? Because the plan of salvation is built on agency, the ability to choose for yourself. Even after a thousand years of living in a world of peace, truth, and righteousness, every person must still have the chance to choose. Agency doesn't take a vacation. It is the core of the plan, and it must be honored all the way to the very end.

When Satan is loosed, he will go out and try to gather followers once more. Some people, even after living in a world ruled by Christ Himself, will choose to follow Satan. This is hard to understand, but it shows just how real agency is. No amount of evidence, no amount of blessings, and no amount of peace can force someone to choose the right. Each person must decide for themselves.

But this final rebellion will not last long. The scriptures make it clear that Satan's forces will be defeated swiftly and completely. John writes that "fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them. And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone" (Revelation 20:9-10). Satan will be cast out forever. His power, his influence, and his ability to tempt will be gone for all eternity. He will never return.

This moment matters because it is the final test, the last time anyone will face the choice between God and the adversary. And once it is over, it is over for good. The plan of salvation accounts for every possibility, including this one. No one can say they were never given the chance to choose.

The defeat of Satan is the final proof that the plan is perfectly just. Every person had agency. Every person faced a real choice. And in the end, God's love and truth won -- not by force, but by the willing hearts of His children.
