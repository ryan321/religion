### Lesson 13.4 -- The Final Judgment

After Satan is defeated forever and the Millennium ends, every person who has ever lived will stand before God for the Final Judgment. The scriptures are clear about this: "The sea gave up the dead which were in it; and death and hell delivered up the dead which were in them: and they were judged every man according to their works" (Revelation 20:12-13). No one is skipped. No one is forgotten. Every single soul will be there.

The prophet Nephi confirms this: "All men must come before the judgment-seat of the Holy One of Israel; and then must they be judged according to the holy judgment of God" (2 Nephi 9:15). This is one of the most important moments in the entire plan of salvation, because it is the moment when perfect justice and perfect mercy meet face to face.

What will God judge us by? The scriptures teach that we will be judged by our works, our desires, and the intents of our hearts. God doesn't just look at what you did -- He looks at why you did it and what you wanted to become. He knows every detail of your life: your struggles, your circumstances, your heartbreaks, and your triumphs. Nothing is hidden from Him, and nothing is misunderstood. That is why His judgment is perfectly fair. No earthly judge could ever know you this well, but God does.

Based on this judgment, each person will be assigned to the place they have prepared themselves for. Most people will receive one of the three degrees of glory -- the celestial, terrestrial, or telestial kingdoms. A very small number who knowingly chose to fight against God in full knowledge of His truth will go to outer darkness, sometimes called perdition. But the vast majority of God's children will receive a kingdom of glory, because the plan is merciful and generous beyond anything we can fully imagine.

And here is something truly wonderful: the earth itself will be transformed. The Lord has revealed that "the earth abideth the law of a celestial kingdom" and will be "sanctified" and "celestialized" (D&C 88:17-20). It will become the celestial kingdom for those who inherit celestial glory. The earth you are standing on right now has a glorious future -- and so do you. As Joseph Smith taught, this earth "in its sanctified and immortal state, will be made like unto crystal" (D&C 130:9).

The Final Judgment is the moment when the plan of salvation reaches its fulfillment. Every promise is kept, every wrong is righted, and every child of God receives exactly what is fair. It is perfectly just, perfectly merciful, and free and open to everyone.
