### Lesson 14.1 -- Commanded to Be Perfect

During His earthly ministry, Jesus gave a commandment that might seem impossible: "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect" (Matthew 5:48). Perfect? Like Heavenly Father? That sounds overwhelming. How could anyone ever reach that?

But here is something remarkable. After Jesus was resurrected and visited the Nephites in the Americas, He gave the same commandment -- with one important change. This time He said: "I would that ye should be perfect even as I, or your Father who is in heaven is perfect" (3 Nephi 12:48). Did you catch the difference? He added Himself. Before His resurrection, He pointed only to the Father as the standard of perfection. After His resurrection, He included Himself because He had now completed His mortal journey. He had suffered, died, and risen again. He had finished His mission perfectly.

This teaches us something crucial: perfection is not something you are expected to achieve right now, today, in your mortal life. Jesus Himself did not use the word about Himself until after He had finished His mortal experience. Perfection is the destination -- the end of the journey, not the beginning. You are not expected to be flawless this afternoon. You are expected to be on the path.

So why give the commandment at all if we can't reach it yet? Because it shows us where we're headed. A commandment to be perfect is really an invitation to keep growing, keep repenting, and keep trying. It's a promise that perfection is possible -- not through your own strength alone, but through the Savior. He is how you get there.

This is one of the most hopeful truths in the entire plan of salvation. God would never command something that was truly impossible. If He commands you to become perfect, then He has also provided the way. And that way is Jesus Christ. Through His Atonement, His grace, and His power, you can eventually become what Heavenly Father wants you to become. Not today. Not all at once. But step by step, grace upon grace, the Savior will help you get there.

The plan is perfectly just because it requires real effort and real growth. But it is perfectly merciful because the Savior walks beside you every step of the way.
