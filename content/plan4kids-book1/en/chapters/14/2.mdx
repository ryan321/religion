### Lesson 14.2 -- He Makes Up the Difference

If perfection is the destination, you might be wondering: how do I get there when I make so many mistakes? The answer is one of the most beautiful truths in the gospel -- grace.

The prophet Nephi taught: "It is by grace that we are saved, after all we can do" (2 Nephi 25:23). This scripture has been misunderstood by many people. Some think it means you have to do absolutely everything perfectly, and then -- only after you've exhausted every last ounce of effort -- grace kicks in to handle the tiny bit left over. But that's not what it means at all.

Grace is not a reward for perfect performance. Grace is the power that transforms imperfect people into something more. You do your best -- you try, you repent, you keep going -- and the Savior makes up the difference. Not just a little difference. A huge, infinite, eternal difference. You bring what you have, even when it feels small and weak, and He provides what you lack.

Think of it like a child trying to buy a present for a parent. The child reaches into a piggy bank and pulls out a handful of coins -- nowhere near enough. But a loving older brother steps in and says, "I'll cover the rest." The child's coins still mattered. The effort still mattered. But it was the brother's generosity that made it possible. That is what Jesus does for you.

You cannot make yourself perfect. No amount of willpower, discipline, or effort can erase your sins or transform your nature by itself. But the Savior's Atonement can. When you repent and turn to Him, He doesn't just forgive you -- He changes you. He gives you a new heart. He strengthens your weaknesses. He lifts you beyond what you could ever reach alone.

This is why the plan is free and open to everyone. You don't have to be the smartest, strongest, or most talented person. You don't have to have a perfect life or a perfect family. You just have to come to Christ with a willing heart and let Him do what only He can do. His grace is sufficient for every single person who has ever lived -- no exceptions.

The plan is perfectly merciful because the Savior's grace reaches everyone, everywhere, always. And it is perfectly just because He asks for something real in return -- not perfection, but your heart.
