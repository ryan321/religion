### Lesson 14.4 -- Sealed as Families

Everything we have learned about the plan of salvation leads to one crowning truth: families are meant to be together forever. Not just during this life, but for all eternity. This is not a nice sentiment or a wish -- it is a promise backed by the power of God.

In the temple, husbands and wives can be sealed together, and children can be sealed to their parents, by the authority of the holy priesthood. The Lord revealed: "If a man marry a wife by my word, which is my law, and by the new and everlasting covenant, and it is sealed unto them by the Holy Spirit of promise... it shall be done unto them in all things whatsoever my servant hath put upon them, in time, and through all eternity" (D&C 132:19-20). This sealing power binds families not just until death, but forever.

Where did this power come from? In 1836, the prophet Elijah appeared in the Kirtland Temple and restored the sealing keys to Joseph Smith (D&C 110:13-16). These are the same keys the prophet Malachi spoke of when he said that the Lord would "turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers" (Malachi 4:6). Every time someone does family history work or enters the temple to perform ordinances for an ancestor, this prophecy is being fulfilled.

The plan of salvation is not just about individual salvation. It is about bringing entire families home to Heavenly Father. Think about that. God's goal is not just to save you as one person standing alone -- it's to weave you into an eternal family that stretches back through generations and forward without end. Parents, grandparents, great-grandparents -- all connected, all sealed, all together.

This is why family history and temple work matter so much. When you search for the names of your ancestors, you are reaching across time to offer them the same blessings you enjoy. You are making sure no one in your family is left behind. This work is free and open to everyone, because every family deserves the chance to be together forever.

The sealing power is the crowning element of the plan. It turns the plan of salvation from a plan for individuals into a plan for families. And a perfect, loving Father would have it no other way.
