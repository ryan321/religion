### Lesson 15.3 -- Where You're Going

The plan of salvation does not end with death. In many ways, the most glorious part of the plan is what comes after this life -- and the scriptures paint a breathtaking picture of what awaits those who follow the covenant path.

The covenant path is the road that leads you home. It begins with faith in Jesus Christ and repentance, continues through baptism and receiving the gift of the Holy Ghost, and leads through the temple and enduring faithfully to the end. Each step brings you closer to Heavenly Father, and each covenant you make and keep connects you more deeply to the Savior.

If you follow this path, the promises are staggering. You will be resurrected with a glorified, perfected body that will never again feel pain, sickness, or death. You will live with your family forever, sealed by the power of God. You will dwell in the presence of Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ, not as a stranger or a guest, but as a member of Their family.

The Lord has promised that the faithful will receive "all that my Father hath" (D&C 84:38). Think about what that means. Everything that God has -- His glory, His power, His knowledge, His joy -- will be shared with you. You will be a "joint-heir with Christ" (Romans 8:17), inheriting the same eternal blessings that the Savior Himself receives.

This is what the scriptures call exaltation -- the highest degree of the celestial kingdom. It is not just being saved from sin and death, as wonderful as that is. It is being saved for something: eternal life, eternal families, eternal progression, and eventually becoming like God Himself. The plan does not just rescue you from a bad outcome -- it lifts you to the greatest possible outcome.

And this path is free and open to everyone. It does not matter where you started, how many mistakes you have made, or how far off course you may have wandered. The covenant path is always there. The Savior is always ready to help you back onto it. Repentance is always available. And the destination -- living with God, with your family, forever -- is always within reach for anyone willing to walk the path.

The question is not whether God wants you to make it home. He does, with all His heart. The question is whether you will choose to walk the path He has prepared for you.
