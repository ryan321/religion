### Lesson 2.2 -- Spirit Children of God

When the scriptures say that you are a child of God, they mean it **literally**. This isn't a metaphor or a nice way of saying that God cares about you. You are actually, truly His child -- born to Him as a spirit before you came to earth.

The Apostle Paul taught this clearly. Speaking to people in Athens, he said, "We are the offspring of God" (Acts 17:29). He also wrote to the Romans, "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God" (Romans 8:16). Notice that Paul didn't say we are God's creations, His projects, or His pets. He said we are His **children** and His **offspring** -- family words that mean something very specific.

Why does this matter so much? Because children inherit the nature of their parents. A kitten grows up to be a cat, not a fish. A puppy grows up to be a dog, not a bird. And a child of God has inherited the potential to become like God. That isn't arrogance -- it's what the scriptures teach. It's the whole reason the plan of salvation exists.

Think about what this means for how you see yourself. On hard days, when you feel like you're not good enough or like you don't matter, remember this: you are not some random thing that the universe accidentally produced. You were born to God. He is your Father. You carry His nature inside you. You have divine potential -- the potential to grow and become like Him.

This also changes how you should see other people. Every person you meet -- your friends, your classmates, the kid who annoys you, the stranger at the store -- is also a child of God. They have the same divine origin and the same divine potential that you do. When you understand this, it becomes harder to be unkind and easier to be patient.

Being a literal child of God is the foundation of the entire plan. If we were just creations -- like a painting or a sculpture -- there would be no reason to give us a plan of progression. Paintings don't grow up to become painters. But children grow up to become like their parents. That's the whole point. Heavenly Father didn't just make you. He is raising you. And His plan is how He's doing it (D&C 93:20).
