### Lesson 2.4 -- Why We Couldn't Stay

Life in the premortal world was wonderful in many ways. You lived in the presence of your Heavenly Father. You were surrounded by your spirit brothers and sisters. You were learning, growing, and making choices. So why did you have to leave? Why couldn't you just stay there forever?

The answer is simple but important: **you couldn't progress any further without a physical body and the experience of mortality**. You had gone as far as a spirit alone could go. To become more like Heavenly Father (who has a glorified, perfected, physical body) you needed to gain a body of your own (D&C 93:33-34).

Think of it like a child who has learned everything they can at home. Their parents have taught them to read, to count, to be kind, and to work hard. But eventually, the child needs to leave home and go to school. Not because home is bad, but because the child needs experiences that home alone can't provide. They need to meet new people, face challenges on their own, solve problems without a parent standing right beside them, and discover what they're really made of.

That's what mortality is. It's the school we needed to attend. Here on earth, we face things that simply didn't exist in the premortal life:

**A physical body.** We can now feel, touch, taste, and experience the physical world. Our bodies are a gift, and learning to use them wisely is part of the test (1 Corinthians 6:19-20).

**Walking by faith.** In the premortal life, we lived in God's presence. Here, a veil of forgetfulness was placed over our minds so we wouldn't remember that life. We have to choose to believe, to trust, and to follow God without seeing Him. That's what faith means, and it's a crucial part of our growth (2 Corinthians 5:7).

**Pain and joy.** Without experiencing sorrow, we could never fully understand happiness. Without opposition, we could never truly choose good over evil (2 Nephi 2:11). Mortality gives us the full range of human experience, and that experience changes us in ways nothing else could.

**Being tested.** Heavenly Father needed to know (and we needed to know) whether we would keep His commandments when it wasn't easy. Abraham 3:25 says, "And we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever the Lord their God shall command them."

Leaving the premortal life wasn't a punishment. It was a promotion. You were ready for the next step, and Heavenly Father loved you enough to send you forward.
