### Lesson 3.1 -- The Grand Council

Before the earth was created, before any of us were born into mortal life, Heavenly Father gathered His spirit children together for one of the most important meetings that has ever taken place. This meeting is often called the **Grand Council in Heaven**, and the book of Abraham gives us the clearest picture of what happened there.

Abraham 3:22-28 describes how God stood among the intelligences and spirits, and He presented His plan. The plan went something like this: His children would leave His presence and go to an earth that would be created for them. There they would receive **physical bodies**. They would be **tested** to see if they would choose to obey God even when it was difficult. And if they proved faithful, they would **return to live with Him** and receive everything He has.

It was a magnificent plan -- but it had a serious problem built right into it.

The problem was **sin**. Heavenly Father knew that once His children were on earth, with physical bodies and real agency, every single one of them (except One) would make mistakes. They would sin. And the scriptures are very clear about what sin does: "No unclean thing can dwell with God" (1 Nephi 10:21). Justice -- one of God's most fundamental qualities -- demands that anyone who sins cannot return to His presence. The debt has to be paid.

So here was the dilemma. If God's children went to earth, they would all sin, and justice would keep them from returning. But if they didn't go to earth, they could never progress. Either way, they seemed stuck.

The answer? A **Savior**. Someone would need to step forward and offer to pay the price for everyone else's sins. Someone who would live a perfect life, never sinning even once, so that He could satisfy the demands of justice on behalf of everyone else. Without a Savior, the plan couldn't work. Mercy would have no way to reach God's children, and they would be lost forever (Alma 34:9).

This was the moment everything hinged on. The plan was beautiful, but it needed someone willing to make the ultimate sacrifice to make it work. And as we'll learn in the next lessons, two very different volunteers stepped forward.
