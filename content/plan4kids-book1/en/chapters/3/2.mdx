### Lesson 3.2 -- The Need for a Savior

In the last lesson, we learned that sin would keep every one of Heavenly Father's children from returning to His presence. Now let's look more closely at why a Savior was the only solution.

God operates by eternal laws. One of the most important is the law of **justice**. Justice means that every choice has a consequence. When you obey God's commandments, blessings follow. When you disobey, a penalty follows. Justice cannot be ignored, set aside, or wished away. Alma 42:13 says, "The plan of redemption could not be brought about, only on conditions of repentance... for justice could not be destroyed; for if so, God would cease to be God."

That's a striking verse. It says that if God simply ignored sin and let everyone return without paying the penalty, He would stop being God. Justice is not just a rule He follows -- it's part of who He is. He cannot violate it any more than He can stop being loving or stop being truthful.

So imagine you're standing before God after mortality, and you've sinned -- which everyone will, because that's part of being human. Justice says you owe a debt. You broke the law, and the penalty must be paid. But you can't pay it yourself. No amount of good works can undo the sins you've committed, because justice doesn't work on a points system. The debt is real, and it stands.

This is where the plan would fall apart without a Savior. Without someone to step in, every single person who ever lived would be permanently separated from God. Mercy would want to save you, but justice would block the way. And both justice and mercy are essential -- you can't have a fair universe without justice, and you can't have a loving God without mercy.

The Savior would need to be someone **sinless** -- someone who owed no debt of His own. Only a perfect being could offer to pay for someone else's sins, because He would have nothing to pay for Himself. He would also need to be someone with power over death itself, so that He could break the chains of physical death for everyone (2 Nephi 9:7-9).

The Savior wasn't optional. He was the only way the plan could work -- the only bridge between justice and mercy, between God's perfection and our imperfection. Without Him, the plan of salvation would have been the plan of condemnation. With Him, it becomes the plan of happiness.
