### Lesson 3.3 -- Two Volunteers

When Heavenly Father presented His plan and explained that a Savior was needed, two of His spirit children stepped forward. What they offered could not have been more different, and understanding the difference is one of the most important things you can learn about the plan of salvation.

The first to speak was **Lucifer**, also known as Satan. Moses 4:1 records his words: "Behold, here am I, send me, I will be the redeemer of all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor." Listen carefully to what Lucifer promised. He said he would save everyone -- not a single soul would be lost. That sounds wonderful at first. Who wouldn't want a guarantee that everyone makes it back? But look at what he demanded in return: "Give me thine honor." He wanted God's glory for himself.

And there was a much deeper problem with Lucifer's offer. How was he going to make sure that not one soul was lost? The only way to guarantee that outcome is to **take away agency** -- to force people to obey. If nobody can choose wrong, then nobody can sin, and nobody is lost. But nobody grows, either. Lucifer's plan would have turned God's children into puppets -- creatures that behave perfectly but never truly choose, never truly love, and never truly become anything.

Then **Jesus Christ** stepped forward. Moses 4:2 records His words: "Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever." Notice the difference. Jesus didn't seek His own glory. He offered it all to the Father. He didn't promise to force anyone. He volunteered to suffer for everyone's sins -- to pay the price Himself -- so that those who chose to repent could be saved. His plan preserved **agency**, the very thing that makes progression possible.

Jesus offered to take upon Himself the weight of every sin, every sorrow, and every pain that any human being would ever experience (Alma 7:11-12). He would suffer beyond what any mortal could endure. He would die. And He would rise again, conquering death for everyone. It would cost Him everything.

Two volunteers. One wanted glory and control. The other offered sacrifice and freedom. One would have destroyed the very purpose of the plan. The other made the plan possible.
