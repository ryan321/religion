### Lesson 3.4 -- The Father's Choice

Heavenly Father heard both offers. He considered the two plans laid before Him. And He chose Jesus Christ. Abraham 3:27 records the Father's words: "I will send the first." Jesus -- the Firstborn -- was chosen to be the Savior of the world.

Why did the Father choose Jesus over Lucifer? It wasn't a close call. The two plans were not equally good options with small differences. They were fundamentally opposed to each other, and only one of them was actually the Father's plan at all.

The Father's plan had always been built on **agency**, the freedom to choose. Agency isn't just a nice feature of the plan. It is the **foundation** of everything. Without agency, there is no faith, because faith requires choosing to believe. Without agency, there is no repentance, because repentance requires choosing to change. Without agency, there is no love, because love requires choosing to care. Without agency, there is no growth, because growth requires choosing the harder right over the easier wrong (2 Nephi 2:27).

Lucifer's plan would have eliminated all of that. He promised safety ("not one soul shall be lost") but the cost was everything that makes salvation meaningful. What good is it to return to God if you never became anything along the way? What good is perfection if it was forced on you rather than chosen by you? Lucifer's plan was a **counterfeit**. It looked like salvation on the outside, but on the inside it was hollow. It promised the destination but destroyed the journey.

Jesus's plan was honest about the cost. People would struggle. People would sin. Some would choose not to return. That's the unavoidable reality of agency. When people are truly free, some will choose poorly. But those who do choose to follow the plan, who exercise faith, who repent and rely on the Savior's sacrifice, will return to God having actually **become** something. They won't just be in God's presence; they will be like Him (1 John 3:2).

When the Father chose Jesus, Lucifer rebelled. He refused to accept the decision. He turned against the Father, against the plan, and against his own brothers and sisters. Moses 4:3 tells us he "sought to destroy the agency of man" and was cast out. A third of the hosts of heaven followed him (D&C 29:36). They lost everything: their chance for bodies, for mortality, for progression. They chose the counterfeit, and they lost the real thing.

The Father's choice was the only choice that preserved what mattered most: your freedom to choose, your ability to grow, and your potential to become like Him. The plan moved forward, and you are living proof that it did.
