### Lesson 4.2 -- What Lucifer's Plan Really Involved

Lucifer's proposal in the Council in Heaven might have sounded appealing at first. He basically said, "I will save every single person. No one will be lost." That sounds great, right? But when you look closer, you realize his plan was a fundamental rejection of everything Heavenly Father wanted for His children.

The scriptures explain exactly what Lucifer wanted. In Moses 4:3, we learn that Satan "sought to destroy the agency of man." Agency -- the power to choose for yourself -- is one of the greatest gifts God has given us. Without agency, we couldn't learn, grow, or become like our Heavenly Father. Lucifer wanted to take that away completely. He wanted to force everyone to obey, like puppets on a string.

But that wasn't all. Lucifer also "sought that I should give unto him mine own power," meaning he wanted God's glory for himself (Moses 4:3). He didn't really care about saving people. He wanted to overthrow God and take His place. His plan was rebellion disguised as compassion.

Think about it this way: "No one will be lost" sounds nice until you realize that no one would be free, either. Imagine if someone said, "I'll make sure you never fail any test at school -- but you can never choose what to study, who to be friends with, or what to do with your life." Would that really be a good deal? You'd be "safe," but you wouldn't really be living.

The prophet Lehi taught this principle clearly: "They are free to choose liberty and eternal life, through the great Mediator of all men, or to choose captivity and death, according to the captivity and power of the devil" (2 Nephi 2:27). Real salvation requires real choice. We have to be free to choose the right -- and also free to make mistakes and learn from them.

Lucifer's plan would have turned God's children into something less than what they were meant to be. There would have been no growth, no learning, no becoming. Just forced obedience with no meaning behind it. Heavenly Father rejected this plan because He loves us too much to take away our freedom -- even when that freedom means some of His children will struggle or even choose the wrong path.
