### Lesson 4.4 -- You Chose Right

Here is one of the most amazing truths in all of the gospel: if you are alive on this earth right now, it means you chose the right side in the war in heaven. You were there. You heard both plans. You saw Lucifer's rebellion. And you chose to follow Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ.

That's not a small thing. You didn't just go along with the crowd. You made a real, personal decision to support the Father's plan. You chose Jesus as your Savior. You chose agency, even knowing that agency comes with risks. You could have chosen the path that sounded "safe," where no one would fail but no one would be free. Instead, you chose growth over safety. You chose freedom over force.

The scriptures remind us that being on earth is not an accident. Abraham learned that God looked at His children and said, "We will make an earth whereon these may dwell" (Abraham 3:24). Earth was made for those who chose the Father's plan. That includes you. You are here on purpose, not by chance, because of a courageous choice you made before you were born.

Think about what that means for your life right now. On hard days, when school is tough, when friends are unkind, when things feel unfair, you can remember that you already proved you have courage. You already showed that you can make hard choices. You stood with God once, and you can stand with Him again, every single day.

It also means you are not alone. Every person you see (your family, your friends, your neighbors, even people you've never met) all of them made that same brave choice. Every human being who has ever lived on this earth chose to follow the plan of salvation. We are all on the same team, even if we don't always remember it.

You are a child of God who chose the right before you ever took your first breath. You chose Jesus. You chose agency. You chose to come to earth and be tested. And now you're here, living out the plan you already said yes to. The question isn't whether you were brave enough to start this journey; you already proved that. The question is whether you'll keep choosing the right, one day at a time, until you return home to the Father who loves you.
