### Lesson 5.1 -- Why Earth Was Created

After the war in heaven, the faithful spirits who chose to follow the Father's plan were ready for the next step. But they needed somewhere to go. They needed a place where they could receive physical bodies, be tested, and have the chance to grow toward eternal life. That's why earth was created.

The creation of the earth wasn't random. It wasn't an accident or a cosmic coincidence. It was a purposeful, planned part of the plan of salvation. God told Abraham, "We will make an earth whereon these may dwell; and we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever the Lord their God shall command them" (Abraham 3:24-25). The earth was designed from the very beginning to serve a specific purpose: to be the place where God's children would live, learn, and be tested.

Think of it like building a school. Before you can teach students, you need a building with classrooms, desks, books, and supplies. The school doesn't exist for its own sake; it exists so students can learn. In the same way, the earth doesn't exist just to be a beautiful planet floating in space. It exists so that we, God's children, can come here and progress.

Everything about the earth was designed to support the plan. We needed a place with physical matter so we could have physical bodies. We needed a place with challenges and opposition so we could grow. We needed a place where we would be separated from God's presence so we could learn to walk by faith. The earth provides all of that.

The creation also shows us something important about God's character. He didn't just announce a plan and leave it at that. He actually built a world for us. He prepared a home. That takes incredible love and care. Every mountain, ocean, forest, and sunset is part of a world that God made specifically for His children.

When you look at the world around you (the trees, the sky, the animals, the seasons) you're not just looking at nature. You're looking at a gift from your Heavenly Father, carefully created so that you could come here and fulfill the purpose of the plan of salvation.
