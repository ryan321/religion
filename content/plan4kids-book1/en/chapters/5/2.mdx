### Lesson 5.2 -- The Need for Physical Bodies

One of the main reasons we came to earth was to receive a physical body. In the premortal life, we were spirits -- we could think, feel, learn, and choose, but we didn't have bodies of flesh and bone. Coming to earth gave us something we couldn't get anywhere else.

The scriptures teach how important this is. In Doctrine and Covenants 93:33-34, the Lord says, "Spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy" and "when separated, man cannot receive a fulness of joy." In other words, having a spirit alone isn't enough. To experience the complete happiness that God wants for us, we need both a spirit and a physical body joined together permanently -- which is what happens through the resurrection.

Some people think of the body as a burden -- something that gets tired, sick, hungry, or hurt. But in the gospel, the body is a gift. It's something wonderful and sacred. God Himself has a glorified, perfected body of flesh and bones. Doctrine and Covenants 130:22 teaches, "The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's." Having a body is part of what makes God who He is, and receiving our own bodies is a step toward becoming more like Him.

Remember what happened to Satan and the one-third who followed him? They were denied physical bodies forever. That's one of the greatest punishments they received. They will never know what it feels like to run through grass, taste food, hug someone they love, or feel the warmth of the sun. They are so jealous of our bodies that they try to tempt us to misuse and disrespect them.

Your body lets you do amazing things. You can serve others with your hands, speak kind words with your mouth, and go places where people need help with your feet. You can feel the beauty of music, see the colors of a sunset, and experience the love of a hug from someone you care about. These experiences are only possible because you have a body.

Taking care of your body matters because it is part of God's plan for you. It's not just a temporary shell -- it is an essential part of who you are and who you are becoming. Treating your body with respect honors the gift that Heavenly Father gave you and the plan that made it possible.
