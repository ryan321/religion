### Lesson 5.3 -- The Creators

Who created the earth? The scriptures make it clear that Jesus Christ was the Creator, working under the direction of Heavenly Father. The apostle John wrote, "All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made" (John 1:3). Jesus -- who in the premortal life was known as Jehovah -- is the one who actually performed the work of creation.

But Jesus didn't work alone. He was assisted by Michael, the archangel who would later come to earth as Adam. Together, under the Father's direction, they organized and shaped the earth into the world we know today.

Notice that word -- "organized." Latter-day Saint doctrine teaches that the earth was not created out of nothing. It was organized from materials that already existed. The Creators took existing matter and shaped it into a beautiful, functional world designed to support God's plan.

The creation happened over six creative periods. The scriptures don't tell us exactly how long each period was -- they may not have been ordinary 24-hour days. But they do tell us what happened during each one. We have three scriptural accounts of the creation: Genesis chapters 1-2 in the Bible, Moses chapters 2-3 in the Pearl of Great Price, and Abraham chapters 4-5, also in the Pearl of Great Price. Each account adds important details and perspective.

During the creative periods, the work moved from simple to complex, step by step. First came light, separating it from darkness. Then the waters were divided and dry land appeared. Next, plants and vegetation covered the earth. The sun, moon, and stars were set in place to mark times and seasons. Then animals filled the seas, skies, and land. Finally, in the crowning act of creation, man was created in the image of God.

The order of creation wasn't random. Each step built on the one before it. Plants needed light. Animals needed plants. Humans needed all of it. God organized the earth in a careful, logical sequence so that everything would be ready when His children arrived.

The creation is a testament to God's power, wisdom, and love. Every detail was planned so that we could come to earth and fulfill the purpose of the plan of salvation.
