### Lesson 5.4 -- Adam and Eve

The creation of Adam and Eve was the crowning moment of all creation. After preparing the earth with light, land, plants, and animals, God was ready for the final and most important step: placing His children on the earth.

The scriptures describe Adam's creation in beautiful detail. "And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul" (Genesis 2:7; see also Moses 3:7). Adam was the first man, created in the image of God, with a physical body made from the elements of the earth and a spirit that had existed since the premortal life. Remember, Adam was Michael, the same great spirit who had led the faithful in the war in heaven.

Eve was created next. "And the Lord God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, and he slept: and he took one of his ribs, and closed up the flesh instead thereof; and the rib, which the Lord God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man" (Genesis 2:21-22). Eve was not an afterthought. She was an essential part of the plan, Adam's equal partner, without whom the plan of salvation could not move forward.

God placed Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden, a beautiful paradise where everything was provided for them. There was no death, no pain, no sickness. They lived in God's presence and walked and talked with Him. It sounds perfect, and in many ways it was. But it was not meant to last forever because growth requires change.

In the garden, God gave Adam and Eve two important commandments. First, He told them to "be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth" (Moses 2:28). Second, He commanded them not to eat the fruit of the tree of knowledge of good and evil, saying, "Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it, nevertheless, thou mayest choose for thyself" (Moses 3:16-17).

These two commandments set the stage for one of the most important events in all of history: the Fall. How Adam and Eve handled this situation would change everything for all of God's children.
