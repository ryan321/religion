### Lesson 6.2 -- Eve's Choice

Eve was the first to partake of the fruit of the tree of knowledge of good and evil. In many other Christian traditions, this is seen as a terrible mistake, the worst thing that ever happened. But Latter-day Saints see it very differently. We honor Eve for her wisdom and courage.

Eve understood something crucial. She recognized that the Fall was necessary for the plan of salvation to move forward. Staying in the Garden forever would have meant no children, no growth, no progression, and no way to fulfill God's purposes. Someone had to make the hard choice, and Eve stepped forward.

After the Fall, Eve herself explained why she was grateful for what had happened. In one of the most powerful verses in all scripture, she said: "Were it not for our transgression we never should have had seed, and never should have known good and evil, and the joy of our redemption, and the eternal life which God giveth unto all the obedient" (Moses 5:11).

Read that verse carefully. Eve wasn't saying "Oops, I made a mistake but at least some good came from it." She was saying that without the Fall, none of the most important blessings would exist: children, knowledge, joy, redemption, eternal life. She understood that the Fall opened the door to everything that matters most.

This was a courageous choice. Eve knew there would be consequences. She knew that eating the fruit would bring death, pain, sorrow, and separation from God's presence. But she also understood that those difficult things were necessary for growth. You can't know joy without knowing sorrow. You can't appreciate the light without experiencing darkness. You can't grow stronger without facing challenges.

In the Latter-day Saint understanding, Eve is not a villain. She is a hero. She is one of the greatest and most noble women who ever lived. Her willingness to make a hard, necessary choice shows incredible faith and understanding. She trusted that God had a plan, that the Savior would come, and that the temporary hardships of mortality would be worth it for the eternal blessings on the other side.

Adam also partook of the fruit, choosing to stay with Eve and move the plan forward together. Together, they stepped out of paradise and into the world, and because of that choice, all of us can be here today.
