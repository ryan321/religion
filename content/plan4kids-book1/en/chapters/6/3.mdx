### Lesson 6.3 -- What the Fall Changed

When Adam and Eve partook of the fruit, everything changed. The peaceful, painless, deathless world of the Garden of Eden was replaced by something very different -- mortality. Understanding what the Fall changed helps us see why it was so important and why it was necessary for the plan.

The first major change was physical. Adam and Eve became mortal, meaning they could now experience death, pain, sickness, hunger, and fatigue. Their bodies, which had been in a state where they could live forever, now began to age and would eventually die. This is called physical death -- the separation of the spirit from the body.

The second major change was spiritual. Adam and Eve were cast out of God's presence. They could no longer walk and talk with Him face to face the way they had in the Garden. This separation from God is called spiritual death. It affects every person born into this world -- we are all born away from God's presence and must find our way back to Him.

But the Fall also brought changes that were absolutely essential for progress. For the first time, Adam and Eve could have children. Every person who has ever been born -- including you -- exists because of the Fall. Without it, none of us would be here.

The Fall also introduced opposition into the world. The prophet Lehi taught, "It must needs be, that there is an opposition in all things" (2 Nephi 2:11). Before the Fall, Adam and Eve didn't really experience good and evil, happiness and sadness, pleasure and pain. They existed in a state where "all things must have remained in the same state in which they were after they were created; and they must have remained forever, and had no end" (2 Nephi 2:22-23). Nothing changed. Nothing grew. Nothing progressed.

The Fall was the doorway, not a disaster. It was the event that moved us from a state of innocence where nothing could happen into a state of mortality where everything that matters -- learning, growing, choosing, loving, struggling, overcoming -- became possible.

Yes, mortality is hard. We get sick. We feel pain. We face temptation. We lose people we love. But every one of those experiences is part of the test that helps us grow and become more like our Heavenly Father. The Fall made the test possible.
