### Lesson 6.4 -- The Fall and the Plan

One of the most important things to understand about the Fall is that God didn't react to it -- He planned for it. The Fall wasn't a surprise that caught Heavenly Father off guard. It was anticipated from the very beginning, built into the plan of salvation from before the world was even created.

The prophet Lehi taught this truth with one of the most famous verses in the Book of Mormon: "Adam fell that men might be; and men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25). That verse contains the whole purpose of mortality in a single sentence. Adam fell so that we could exist. And we exist so that we can experience joy. The Fall was not a tragedy -- it was the necessary step that made joy possible.

Think about it this way: God knew the Fall would happen. He knew it needed to happen. And because He knew, He prepared a way to overcome every negative effect of the Fall before it even took place. The Atonement of Jesus Christ was prepared before the foundation of the world. God didn't wait for the Fall to happen and then scramble to find a solution. The solution was already in place.

The Fall brought two main problems: physical death and spiritual death. The plan provides an answer for both.

Physical death -- the separation of the spirit from the body -- is overcome through the resurrection. Because of Jesus Christ, every person who has ever lived will be resurrected. Their spirit and body will be reunited permanently, never to be separated again. This is a free gift given to everyone, regardless of whether they were good or bad.

Spiritual death -- the separation from God's presence -- is overcome through repentance and the Atonement of Jesus Christ. When we sin, we move further from God. But through the Savior's sacrifice, we can repent, be forgiven, and be brought back into God's presence. This requires our choice and effort, but it is made possible entirely by what Jesus did for us.

The Fall and the Atonement work together like two halves of one plan. The Fall created the conditions we needed for growth, and the Atonement provides the way back home. Without the Fall, we wouldn't need a Savior. Without the Savior, the Fall would leave us trapped forever. Together, they make eternal life and eternal joy possible for every child of God.
