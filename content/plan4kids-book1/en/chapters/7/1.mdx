### Lesson 7.1 -- The Problem

Because of the Fall of Adam and Eve, two big problems entered the world for every single person who would ever live. Understanding these problems is the first step to understanding why Heavenly Father's plan is so beautiful.

The first problem is **physical death**. When Adam and Eve ate the fruit, their bodies became mortal, meaning they would eventually die. Every person born on earth inherits a mortal body. No matter how healthy you are, no matter how many good choices you make, your body will someday stop working. You cannot un-die. You cannot fix physical death on your own. No amount of exercise, medicine, or willpower can make your body live forever.

The second problem is **spiritual death**, being separated from God. Because of the Fall, we all came to earth separated from Heavenly Father's presence. On top of that, every person who grows old enough to make choices will sin. The scriptures are very clear about this: "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23). That includes you, your parents, your teachers, and every person you have ever met.

Why does sin matter so much? Because God is perfectly clean and perfectly just. The prophet Nephi taught that "no unclean thing can dwell with God" (1 Nephi 10:21). Even one sin makes you unclean. Even one lie, one unkind word, one selfish moment. And once you have sinned, you cannot undo it by yourself. You cannot go back in time and un-say the mean thing. You cannot erase the selfishness from your record. You cannot make yourself perfectly clean again through your own effort.

So here is the problem in plain terms: you are going to die, and you are going to sin. You cannot fix either one by yourself. You cannot give yourself a glorified, immortal body. You cannot scrub your own soul perfectly clean. You need someone to do for you what you absolutely cannot do for yourself.

This is not a flaw in Heavenly Father's plan. This is exactly why the plan requires a Savior. Heavenly Father knew all of this before the world was created. He knew we would need help. Not just a little help, but a complete rescue. The plan is perfectly just because it does not pretend sin does not matter. And the plan is perfectly merciful because it provides a way to be saved from sin and death. That way is through Jesus Christ.
