### Lesson 7.2 -- Justice Demands a Price

Heavenly Father is perfectly just. That means He always does what is right, fair, and true. He never bends the rules. He never looks the other way. He never says, "Well, it is not that big of a deal." Every law He gives has a consequence attached to it: a blessing for obedience and a penalty for disobedience. That is what justice means.

Think of it this way. Imagine a judge in a courtroom. A person has broken the law, and the evidence is clear. What would you think if the judge said, "I know you broke the law, but I like you, so I am going to let you go free with no consequence"? That would not be justice. That would be corruption. A good judge cannot ignore the law, no matter how much he cares about the person standing in front of him.

God is the perfect judge. Alma taught this principle powerfully: "Do ye suppose that mercy can rob justice? I say unto you, Nay; not one whit. If so, God would cease to be God" (Alma 42:25). That verse is remarkable. It tells us that if God simply erased penalties and ignored sin, He would stop being God. Justice is not optional. It is part of who He is.

Every commandment God gives has a real consequence for breaking it. Sin creates a debt, a price that must be paid. You cannot wave it away. You cannot pretend it does not exist. You cannot bargain your way out of it. The penalty for sin must be satisfied. Justice demands it.

This might sound scary, and in a way it should. If justice were the only principle in the plan, none of us could return to God because we have all sinned. We would all owe a debt we could never pay. We would all be permanently separated from our Heavenly Father.

But here is what makes Heavenly Father's plan so remarkable. Justice is not the only principle. Mercy is also part of the plan. The plan does not ignore justice or weaken it. The plan satisfies justice completely while also making mercy available. The plan is perfectly just because every debt gets paid. Not one sin goes unaccounted for. Not one law gets thrown out. Justice is fully honored.

Understanding this helps you see why the Savior's sacrifice matters so much. Without it, justice would leave every person without hope. The price has to be paid. The question is: who pays it?
