### Lesson 7.3 -- Mercy Cannot Rob Justice

Here is the most beautiful part of Heavenly Father's plan: mercy does not rob justice. It satisfies it. Both justice and mercy are fully honored. Neither one is weakened, ignored, or pushed aside. That is what makes the plan perfectly just and perfectly merciful at the same time.

How does that work? Through Jesus Christ. Jesus volunteered to pay the penalty for every sin ever committed by every person who has ever lived or ever will live. He suffered the full weight of justice so that mercy could be extended to you. Alma taught: "God himself atoneth for the sins of the world, to bring about the plan of mercy, to appease the demands of justice, that God might be a perfect, just God, and a merciful God also" (Alma 42:15).

Read that verse carefully. God Himself, through His Son Jesus Christ, atoned for sins. Why? To bring about mercy. But notice: He did it to "appease the demands of justice." Justice still gets everything it requires. Every single sin is paid for. Every penalty is satisfied. Not one debt is erased or forgotten. Justice gets its full payment.

And at the same time, mercy gets to offer pardon. Because someone already paid the price, you do not have to pay it yourself, if you will accept the conditions of mercy. That means having faith in Jesus Christ, repenting of your sins, being baptized, receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end.

Think of it like this. Imagine you broke a neighbor's window. The cost to replace it is one hundred dollars. You do not have one hundred dollars. Justice says the window must be paid for. Then your older brother steps in and says, "I will pay for the window." He hands the neighbor one hundred dollars. Is justice satisfied? Yes, the window is paid for. Is mercy shown to you? Yes, you did not have to pay. Did mercy rob justice? No, the full price was paid. Everyone is made whole.

That is what Jesus did, but on an infinite scale. Justice gets payment. Mercy gets pardon. You get to come home to Heavenly Father. The plan is perfectly just because no sin is overlooked. The plan is perfectly merciful because a way is provided for every person to be forgiven and made clean. This is the heart of the gospel, and it is the reason the plan of salvation is the most glorious truth you will ever learn.
