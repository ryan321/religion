### Lesson 7.4 -- Why Only Jesus Could Do It

Out of every person who has ever lived or ever will live, only one being in all of creation could perform the Atonement. Only Jesus Christ. No one else could do it. Not an angel, not a prophet, not any other person no matter how good. Understanding why helps you appreciate just how essential He is to the plan.

First, the Savior had to be **sinless**. If the person paying the debt of justice had a sin debt of his own, he could not pay for anyone else. It would be like trying to pay someone's bill when you are already broke. A sinner paying for sin just adds to the total debt. Jesus never sinned. Not once, not ever. He lived a completely perfect life so that He owed nothing to justice. That made Him the only one qualified to pay for the sins of others.

Second, the Savior had to have **power over death**. Physical death came into the world because of the Fall, and overcoming it required someone with the power to lay down His life and take it up again. An ordinary person cannot choose to come back from the dead. But Jesus was the Only Begotten Son of God. From His Father, He inherited divine power, including power over death itself. From His mother Mary, He inherited a mortal body that could experience pain, suffering, and death. He was divine enough to have the power and mortal enough to experience the sacrifice. No other being in all of creation had that combination.

Third, Jesus **volunteered**. Before the world was even created, in the great council in heaven, Heavenly Father asked who would serve as the Savior. Jesus stepped forward and said, "Here am I, send me" (Moses 4:2). He did not volunteer for glory or power. He said, "Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever." He did it out of pure love for His Father and for you.

No angel could qualify. No prophet could qualify. No other child of God could qualify. Only the Only Begotten Son of God, sinless, divine, mortal, and willing, could perform the infinite Atonement. He is the center of the plan. Without Him, the plan could not work, justice could not be satisfied, mercy could not be offered, and no one could ever return home. The plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful because of Jesus Christ.
