### Lesson 8.1 -- Why God Gives Laws

Some people think God gives commandments just to test whether we will obey. They imagine a long list of rules with no real purpose behind them, just hoops to jump through. But that is not how Heavenly Father works at all. Every single commandment He gives is a lesson in how to become like Him. His laws are not arbitrary restrictions. They are a curriculum.

Think about school. Your teacher does not give you math problems to annoy you. She gives them to you because solving them builds your brain and teaches you skills you will need later. In the same way, God gives commandments because keeping them builds your character and teaches you skills you will need to become like Him. The commandment to be honest teaches you integrity. The commandment to forgive teaches you to have a heart like the Savior's. The commandment to keep the Sabbath day holy teaches you to put spiritual things first.

The scriptures teach a powerful principle about this: "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated, and when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law upon which it is predicated" (D&C 130:20-21). That means blessings are not random. They are connected to specific laws. When you obey a law, the blessing connected to that law is unlocked. Obedience is the key that opens the door.

This is one of the reasons the plan is perfectly just. God does not give blessings based on favoritism. He does not withhold blessings to be mean. Every person who obeys the same law receives the same blessing. The system is completely fair.

And it is also perfectly merciful because God does not leave you to figure out the laws on your own. He sends prophets to teach you. He gives you scriptures to study. He provides the Holy Ghost to guide you. He wants you to succeed. He wants you to receive every blessing He has. That is why He reveals His laws. Not to trap you, but to show you exactly how to progress and eventually become like Him.

Every commandment is an invitation to grow. Every law is a step on the path home.
