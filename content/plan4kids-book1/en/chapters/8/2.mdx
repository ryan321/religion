### Lesson 8.2 -- Covenants Help You Progress

A covenant is a sacred agreement between you and God. It is not like an ordinary promise between friends. In a covenant, you promise to do certain things, and God promises to bless you in return. God always keeps His promises. He binds Himself to bless you when you keep your part of the covenant. That is the power of a covenant -- it connects your obedience directly to God's blessings.

The scriptures are full of covenants. When the children of Israel stood at Mount Sinai, God made a covenant with the entire nation: if they would obey His commandments, He would make them His special people and bless them above all nations (Exodus 19:5-6). That was a national covenant. When God spoke to Abraham, He made a covenant that Abraham's posterity would be as numerous as the stars and that through his family all nations of the earth would be blessed (Abraham 2:9-11). That was a dispensational covenant reaching across generations.

You make personal covenants too. When you are baptized, you covenant to take upon yourself the name of Jesus Christ, to always remember Him, and to keep His commandments. In return, God promises you the companionship of the Holy Ghost (Mosiah 18:8-10). That is a personal covenant between you and God.

Each covenant you make moves you one step closer to Heavenly Father. That is why Church leaders talk about the "covenant path." It is not just a nice phrase -- it describes real progression. Baptism, confirmation, the sacrament, priesthood ordination, the temple endowment, and temple sealing are all steps along the covenant path. Each one adds more promises, more blessings, and more power to your life.

The covenant path is the progression path. It is how you move from where you are now toward becoming like Heavenly Father. Every covenant is an invitation to rise higher, and God promises to help you every step of the way.

This is why the plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful. It is just because God requires real commitments from you -- covenants are serious. And it is merciful because God binds Himself to bless you and help you succeed. He does not ask you to walk the path alone. He walks it with you.
