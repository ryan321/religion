### Lesson 8.3 -- Ordinances Connect Heaven and Earth

An ordinance is a sacred physical act performed by the authority of the priesthood. Ordinances are not just symbols or traditions. They are the way God applies His power directly to your life. They are visible, tangible actions that connect heaven and earth -- where something you can see and feel on earth carries real spiritual power from heaven.

**Baptism** is the ordinance that washes you clean from sin. You go down into the water and come up a new person, symbolizing the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ (Romans 6:3-4). Your sins are washed away, and you begin your covenant journey.

**Confirmation** is the ordinance that gives you the gift of the Holy Ghost. Hands are placed on your head, and by priesthood authority you receive the right to have the Holy Ghost as your constant companion. The Holy Ghost guides you, comforts you, and testifies of truth.

**The sacrament** is the ordinance that lets you renew your baptismal covenant each week. You take bread and water in remembrance of the Savior's body and blood (3 Nephi 18:7, 11). It is a chance to recommit and be made clean again.

**The temple endowment** is an ordinance where you receive sacred knowledge and make covenants that give you greater power to progress toward eternal life. The endowment teaches you about the plan of salvation and prepares you for God's presence.

**Temple sealings** bind families together forever -- husband and wife, parents and children. These ordinances ensure that the family relationships you treasure most do not end at death but continue through eternity (D&C 132:19).

Each ordinance is a step in the plan of salvation. Each one is a specific moment where God reaches down and applies His power to your personal progression. Without ordinances, the Atonement's blessings could not be fully applied to your life. They are not optional extras -- they are essential.

This is part of why the plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful. It is just because ordinances require you to act -- you must show up, participate, and commit. It is merciful because through these sacred acts, God gives you access to power far beyond your own. He meets you where you are and lifts you to where He is.
