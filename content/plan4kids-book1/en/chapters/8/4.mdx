### Lesson 8.4 -- Dispensations and the Plan

Heavenly Father's plan of salvation has been taught on earth from the very beginning. It did not start with Jesus's birth in Bethlehem, and it did not start with Joseph Smith. It started with Adam. But because people have agency and can choose to turn away from the truth, the gospel has been lost and restored many times throughout history. Each time God calls a new prophet and restores the truth, it begins a new **dispensation**.

A dispensation is a period of time when God has authorized servants on the earth who hold the priesthood and teach the gospel. The first dispensation began with **Adam**, who was taught the plan, received priesthood authority, and offered sacrifices pointing toward the Savior (Moses 5:6-8). After many generations, people fell away from the truth.

God called **Noah** and gave him the gospel again. Noah preached repentance, but most people refused to listen. Later, God raised up **Abraham** and made sacred covenants with him that would bless every nation on earth. Then came the dispensation of **Moses**, who received the law and led Israel out of Egypt. Each time, the same plan was taught. Each time, people eventually fell away.

The greatest dispensation before our own was when Jesus Christ Himself came to earth, the Meridian of Time. He taught the fulness of the gospel, organized His Church, and performed the Atonement. But after His Apostles died, the fulness of truth was lost again in what we call the Great Apostasy.

Then came the Restoration. In 1820, God the Father and Jesus Christ appeared to a young boy named Joseph Smith. Through Joseph, God restored the gospel one final time. We live in what the scriptures call "the dispensation of the fulness of times" (D&C 112:30). This dispensation will not end in apostasy. It will continue until the Savior comes again.

The beautiful thing is that the plan has been the same in every dispensation. Adam was taught the same truths you are learning right now. The principles of faith, repentance, baptism, the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end have never changed. Only the time period changes. The plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful in every age, for every person, in every dispensation.
