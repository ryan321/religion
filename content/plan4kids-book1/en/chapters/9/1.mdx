### Lesson 9.1 -- Why the "Meridian"

The scriptures describe the time when Jesus Christ came to earth as "the meridian of time" (D&C 20:26). The word meridian means the middle or the highest point. Jesus did not come at the beginning of the world's history, and He did not come at the end. He came at the central point, the hinge upon which all of history turns.

Everything that happened before Jesus pointed forward to Him. When Adam offered animal sacrifices, those sacrifices were "a similitude of the sacrifice of the Only Begotten of the Father" (Moses 5:7). When Abraham was asked to offer his son Isaac, it was a type and shadow of the Father offering His Only Begotten Son. When Moses lifted the brass serpent in the wilderness, it pointed to the day when Christ would be lifted up on the cross (Helaman 8:14-15). Every prophet, every ordinance, every sacrifice, and every covenant before the meridian pointed forward to what Jesus would do.

Everything that has happened since Jesus points back to Him. When you take the sacrament, you look back and remember His body and His blood. When you are baptized, you look back to His death, burial, and resurrection. When prophets speak today, they testify of what He already accomplished. The entire gospel, in every direction, points to Jesus Christ.

He is the center of the plan. He is the center of history. He is the center of every covenant ever made. The most important events that have ever happened or ever will happen occurred during the meridian of time: His birth, His ministry, His Atonement in Gethsemane and on the cross, and His Resurrection.

No other moment in history comes close. Wars have been fought and kingdoms have risen and fallen, but nothing else changed the eternal destiny of every person who has ever lived. Because of what happened in the meridian of time, death is overcome, sin can be forgiven, and every person can return to God. The plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful because of what Jesus Christ did at the center of it all.
