### Lesson 9.2 -- The Atonement

The Atonement of Jesus Christ is the most important event that has ever happened in all of creation. It took place in two parts -- in the Garden of Gethsemane and on the cross at Calvary -- and it changed everything for every person who has ever lived or ever will live.

In Gethsemane, Jesus knelt and began to take upon Himself the sins, pains, and sorrows of every person. Alma described what He suffered: "He shall go forth, suffering pains and afflictions and temptations of every kind; and this that the word might be fulfilled which saith he will take upon him the pains and the sicknesses of his people... and he will take upon him their infirmities... and he will take upon him the sins of his people" (Alma 7:11-12). Every pain you have ever felt, every sorrow, every temptation, every heartbreak -- He experienced it all. He knows exactly what you are going through because He has already been through it Himself.

The suffering was so intense that He "bled at every pore" (D&C 19:18). He trembled because of pain. He suffered both body and spirit. And He did it for you. He did it for every person who has ever lived.

After Gethsemane, Jesus was arrested, mocked, beaten, and nailed to a cross. He suffered for hours and then gave up His life. He died. The Creator of the world allowed Himself to be killed -- not because He had to, but because He chose to. He had the power to stop it at any moment, but He did not, because He loved you and His Father more than He loved His own comfort.

Then on the third day, He rose from the dead. He broke the bands of death. His spirit reunited with His perfected, glorified body, never to be separated again. Through His Resurrection, He made it possible for every person to be resurrected too.

The Atonement accomplishes three things: it makes repentance possible so you can be forgiven and made clean, it makes resurrection universal so every person will live again, and it makes exaltation available so you can return to live with Heavenly Father forever. The plan is perfectly just because the full penalty of sin was paid. The plan is perfectly merciful because Jesus paid it for you.
