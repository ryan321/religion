### Lesson 9.3 -- Universal Resurrection

One of the most glorious truths in all of the gospel is this: because of the Atonement of Jesus Christ, every single person who has ever lived will be resurrected. Every one. No exceptions. The Apostle Paul taught it plainly: "For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" (1 Corinthians 15:22).

Think about what that means. Because of Adam's Fall, every person dies. Physical death comes to everyone -- the righteous and the wicked, the young and the old, the faithful and the faithless. No one escapes it. But because of Jesus Christ, every person will also be made alive again. Death will be completely and permanently overcome.

Resurrection means your spirit and your body will be reunited -- permanently. Your body will be perfected. It will never get sick, never feel pain, never grow old, and never die again. It will be a glorified, immortal body. Every person who has ever been born on this earth will receive this gift.

And it is a gift. You do not have to earn resurrection. You do not have to be baptized to receive it. You do not have to be a member of any church. Resurrection comes to every person freely because of what Jesus Christ did. The righteous will be resurrected, and the wicked will be resurrected. It is universal and unconditional.

This is the first great blessing of the Atonement. Physical death -- the first great problem caused by the Fall -- is completely overcome. Remember the problem from Lesson 7.1? You are going to die, and you cannot un-die by yourself. Jesus solved that problem. Through His Resurrection, He gained power over death for all mankind. Because He rose, you will rise.

The prophet Amulek testified: "The spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its perfect form; both limb and joint shall be restored to its proper frame... and we shall be brought to stand before God, knowing even as we know now, and have a bright recollection of all our guilt" (Alma 11:43). Every person will stand before God in a resurrected body -- whole, complete, and fully alive.

This is part of why the plan is perfectly just and perfectly merciful. It is merciful because resurrection is given freely to all. It is just because every person -- righteous or wicked -- will stand before God to be judged. No one escapes accountability, and no one is denied life.
