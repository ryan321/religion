### Lesson 9.4 -- What the Atonement Makes Possible

The Atonement's first great blessing, resurrection, is free and universal. But the Atonement's second great blessing is different. It is the gift of repentance, and it comes with conditions. Understanding the difference between these two blessings helps you see the perfect balance of justice and mercy in Heavenly Father's plan.

Because Jesus suffered for every sin ever committed, it is now possible for you to repent and be made clean. Without the Atonement, repentance would be meaningless. Even if you felt sorry for your sins, feeling sorry does not pay the debt of justice. But because Jesus already paid that debt, your sincere repentance activates His payment on your behalf. When you repent, His Atonement washes you clean. Your sins are removed. You can stand before God as if you had never sinned.

But this blessing is conditional. It does not happen automatically. The Atonement opens the door, but you must walk through it. Nephi taught the way clearly: "Wherefore, ye must press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God and of all men. Wherefore, if ye shall press forward, feasting upon the word of Christ, and endure to the end, behold, thus saith the Father: Ye shall have eternal life" (2 Nephi 31:20). The path requires faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, baptism by water, receiving the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end (2 Nephi 31:17-18).

Some people wonder why God does not just save everyone automatically. The answer goes back to justice. The plan is perfectly just. It does not force salvation on anyone. You must choose it. You must act. You must exercise your agency to accept the Savior's gift. And you must keep choosing Him throughout your life.

But you never walk alone. The Savior does not just open the door and then leave you to struggle through on your own. He walks with you. He strengthens you. He lifts you when you fall. He knows every pain and every weakness you face because He already experienced them in Gethsemane (Alma 7:12). He is not a distant Savior. He is right beside you.

The Atonement makes it possible for every person to be resurrected, for every person to repent, and for every person who follows the covenant path to return to live with Heavenly Father forever. The plan is perfectly just because it requires real effort, real commitment, and real change. The plan is perfectly merciful because Jesus Christ provides the power to make all of it possible.
