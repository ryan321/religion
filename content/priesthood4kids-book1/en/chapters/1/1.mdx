### Lesson 1.1 -- The Power of God

Have you ever wondered what the **priesthood** really is? Some people think it's just a title or a club, but it's so much more than that. The priesthood is the **power and authority of God**, the very same power He uses to create worlds, govern the heavens, and bless His children.

Think of it this way. Imagine your parents own a car. They hand you the keys and say, "You can drive this car to help our family." You're driving, but the car still belongs to them. The priesthood works in a similar way. God owns the power (it's His), but He lets worthy men use it to serve and bless others. Pretty amazing, right?

The scriptures teach us that the priesthood has always been part of God's plan. In Doctrine and Covenants 84:17, we learn that the priesthood "continueth in the church of God in all generations, and is without beginning of days or end of years." That means the priesthood wasn't invented last week or even a few thousand years ago. It has existed forever because God has existed forever.

So why does God share this power? Because He loves us and wants us to have access to His blessings right here on earth. Without the priesthood, there would be no one authorized to baptize, confirm, bless the sacrament, or perform any of the **ordinances** that help us return to Heavenly Father. The priesthood is the way God's power reaches into our everyday lives.

Here's something important to understand: the priesthood is not about the person holding it. A twelve-year-old deacon and an apostle hold the same God-given authority, just in different offices with different responsibilities. The power doesn't come from the person. It comes from God. The Apostle Paul reminded us that "no man taketh this honour unto himself, but he that is called of God, as was Aaron" (Hebrews 5:4). Nobody just decides on their own to have priesthood authority. It must come from God through proper channels.

When you see someone bless the sacrament, baptize a new member, or give a priesthood blessing to a sick person, you are watching God's own power in action, carried out by ordinary people who have been given an extraordinary trust.

The priesthood is real. It is God's power. And understanding what it truly is will change the way you see everything in the gospel.
