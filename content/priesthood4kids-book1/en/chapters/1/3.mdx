### Lesson 1.3 -- Who Can Hold the Priesthood?

One of the most common questions young members of the Church ask is: "Who gets to hold the priesthood?" It's a fair question, and the answer is both simple and important.

All **worthy male members** of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints can hold the priesthood. It doesn't matter how much money your family has, what country you come from, or how smart you are in school. The priesthood isn't earned by intelligence, wealth, or popularity. It is given by God to those who are worthy and willing to serve.

There are two levels of the priesthood, and young men typically receive them at different ages. The **Aaronic Priesthood** -- sometimes called the "preparatory priesthood" -- is usually conferred at age **12**. This is when a young man is ordained as a **deacon** and begins performing priesthood duties like passing the sacrament. The Aaronic Priesthood is described in Doctrine and Covenants 13, where John the Baptist conferred it upon Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery, saying it "holds the keys of the ministering of angels, and of the preparatory gospel."

The **Melchizedek Priesthood** -- the higher priesthood -- is typically received at age **18**, when a young man is ordained an **elder**. This priesthood carries greater authority and responsibility, including the power to confer the Holy Ghost, give blessings, and officiate in temple ordinances.

Now, what about women? Women in the Church do not hold priesthood **offices** like deacon, priest, or elder. But that doesn't mean they are left out of priesthood blessings and power. Women participate in the power of the priesthood in important ways -- especially through **temple ordinances and covenants**. President Russell M. Nelson taught that "the blessings of the priesthood are available to every worthy Latter-day Saint, male and female." Women who serve in the temple officiate in sacred ordinances by priesthood authority. Every covenant a woman makes connects her to priesthood power.

The Apostle Peter described all members of the Church -- men and women -- as "a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people" (1 Peter 2:9). The priesthood blesses everyone, even if not everyone holds a specific office.

So if you're a young man preparing to receive the priesthood, know that this is not about being better than anyone else. It's about being ready to serve. And if you're a young woman, know that priesthood power and blessings are very much a part of your life, too. We're all in this together.
