### Lesson 1.4 -- Why the Priesthood Matters

Imagine this: someone fills a pool with water, invites you to jump in, and says, "Congratulations, you've been baptized!" Would that count? Not at all. Why? Because the person had no **authority** from God to perform that ordinance. Without the priesthood, ordinances have no power -- they're just actions without heavenly approval.

This is exactly why the priesthood matters so much. It is the link between God's authority in heaven and the ordinances we perform on earth. Jesus Himself established this pattern when He told His apostles, "Whatsoever ye shall bind on earth shall be bound in heaven" (Matthew 18:18). That binding power comes through the priesthood and its **keys**.

Think about all the ordinances that shape our lives as members of the Church. **Baptism** brings us into the fold. **Confirmation** gives us the gift of the Holy Ghost. The **sacrament** renews our covenants each week. **Temple sealings** bind families for eternity. Every single one of these ordinances requires priesthood authority to be valid. Without it, they are just empty gestures.

The next time you watch a deacon pass the sacrament, think about what's really happening. That young man -- maybe he's your age, maybe he's your friend -- is exercising **God's own authority**. The bread and water he passes have been blessed by a priest using the exact words God commanded (D&C 20:77, 79). That's not just a Sunday routine. That's a miracle happening right in your meetinghouse every single week.

The priesthood also connects our Church to God in a direct, unbroken line. The authority we have today wasn't made up by Joseph Smith. It was **restored** by heavenly messengers -- John the Baptist for the Aaronic Priesthood (D&C 13), and Peter, James, and John for the Melchizedek Priesthood (D&C 27:12-13). Those messengers held the same authority that Jesus Christ gave His original apostles. So when someone exercises the priesthood today, the authority traces all the way back to the Savior Himself.

That is an **enormous trust**. When a young man is ordained, he isn't just getting a new title. He is being handed a portion of God's own power and being told, "I trust you with this." That kind of trust should make us want to live our very best -- not out of fear, but out of gratitude and love for a Heavenly Father who believes in us enough to share His authority.

The priesthood isn't just important. It's everything that makes the gospel work on earth.
