### Lesson 10.2 -- Your Part: Magnify Your Calling

In the oath and covenant of the priesthood, your main responsibility is to **magnify your calling**. But what does that actually mean? The word "magnify" means to make something larger, more visible, and more important. When you magnify your priesthood calling, you don't just show up and go through the motions. You pour your heart into it and make your service bigger through real effort.

Think about a magnifying glass. It takes something small and makes it look bigger and clearer. That is what you do with your calling. A deacon who passes the sacrament carelessly, slouching around and whispering to his friends, is not magnifying his calling. But a deacon who dresses appropriately, walks reverently, and thinks about the Savior as he serves? That young man is magnifying his calling. He is taking something and making it greater through the way he approaches it.

The prophet Jacob in the Book of Mormon understood this well. He said, "We did magnify our office unto the Lord, taking upon us the responsibility" (Jacob 1:19). Notice that magnifying your calling means accepting responsibility, not avoiding it. It means looking for ways to serve better, not doing the bare minimum.

This principle applies at every level of the priesthood. An elder who gives a priesthood blessing with real faith and preparation is magnifying his calling. A teacher who earnestly watches over the members he is assigned to visit is magnifying his calling. A priest who carefully and prayerfully blesses the sacrament, thinking about every word he says, is magnifying his calling. In every case, the person is taking his duty seriously and growing into it.

Doctrine and Covenants 84:33 says that those who "magnify their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit." Did you catch that? When you pour yourself into your priesthood service, the Holy Ghost changes you. You become more like the Savior, not just because you are doing things, but because faithful service transforms who you are on the inside.

So here is the question: What calling or duty do you have right now? Whatever it is, how can you make it bigger, better, and more faithful? That is what it means to magnify your calling, and it is your part of the most important covenant you will ever make.
