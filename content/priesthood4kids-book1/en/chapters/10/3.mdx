### Lesson 10.3 -- God's Part: All That the Father Hath

You learned that your part of the oath and covenant is to magnify your calling and live by God's word. Now let's talk about **God's part**, and it is breathtaking.

In Doctrine and Covenants 84:38, the Lord declares: "And he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore all that my Father hath shall be given unto him." Stop and think about that for a moment. God is not promising you a nice neighborhood in heaven. He is not promising a gold star or a pat on the back. He is promising you **everything He has**. All of it.

What does "all that my Father hath" actually include? It means **exaltation**: becoming like Heavenly Father. It means receiving His power, His glory, and His eternal life. It means living forever with your family in His presence. This is not a metaphor or a poetic way of saying "you'll be happy." It is a **literal promise**. God is telling you that faithful Melchizedek Priesthood holders can one day become like Him.

The Apostle Paul understood this when he wrote to the Romans: "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God: and if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" (Romans 8:16-17). An heir is someone who inherits everything from a parent. God is our Father, and He plans to share His entire inheritance with those who are faithful.

Here is what makes this promise different from every other promise in scripture: God does not merely promise it; He guarantees it with an **oath**. In the book of Hebrews, Paul explained that "because he could swear by no greater, he sware by himself" (Hebrews 6:13). When a person swears an oath, they swear by something greater than themselves. But nothing is greater than God, so He swears by His own name. That means it is absolutely impossible for this promise to fail, as long as you do your part.

This promise is available to **every** faithful Melchizedek Priesthood holder. It does not depend on how wealthy you are, how talented you are, or how famous you are. It depends on faithfulness. And that is something every person can choose.

The greatest thing God can give you is the chance to become like Him. And through the oath and covenant of the priesthood, that is exactly what He offers.
