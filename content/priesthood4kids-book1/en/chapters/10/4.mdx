### Lesson 10.4 -- The Warning

The oath and covenant of the priesthood contains the greatest promise in all of scripture, but it also comes with a serious **warning**. Greater promises bring greater responsibilities, and God wants us to understand exactly what is at stake.

In Doctrine and Covenants 84:41, the Lord says: "But whoso breaketh this covenant after he hath received it, and altogether turneth therefrom, shall not have forgiveness of sins in this world nor in the world to come." Those are strong words. They are meant to be. God is telling us that if someone receives this sacred covenant and then completely and totally turns away from it, the consequences are severe.

Now, let's be careful to understand what this **does not** mean. It does not mean that making a mistake here and there causes you to lose everything. Everyone stumbles. Everyone falls short sometimes. The Savior's Atonement covers our weaknesses when we repent. The key phrase is "altogether turneth therefrom." This is talking about someone who fully, knowingly, and completely abandons the covenant -- someone who turns their back on God after receiving His greatest promises and walks away on purpose.

Think of it this way. If your parents gave you the keys to their home and trusted you with everything they owned, and you deliberately burned the house down, that would be very different from accidentally breaking a window. God knows the difference between honest mistakes and total rebellion.

The purpose of this warning is not to scare you. It is to help you understand how **sacred** this covenant is. The Apostle Paul taught in Hebrews 10:29 that there is serious accountability for those who have "counted the blood of the covenant... an unholy thing." When you know how precious something is, you treat it with greater care.

This is actually a sign of respect from God. He does not hide the seriousness of the covenant. He tells you up front: "This is the greatest thing I can offer you. Treat it accordingly." A covenant backed by God's own oath deserves the deepest reverence and the most determined faithfulness you can give.

So the warning is not a reason to be afraid. It is a reason to be serious, to be faithful, and to hold sacred the incredible trust that God places in every priesthood holder. When you understand both the promise and the warning, you begin to see the priesthood for what it truly is: the most important responsibility and the most glorious opportunity you will ever receive.
