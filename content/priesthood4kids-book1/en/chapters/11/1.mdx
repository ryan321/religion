### Lesson 11.1 -- Why Ordinances Require Priesthood

An **ordinance** is a sacred physical act that carries deep spiritual meaning. Baptism, the sacrament, confirmation, temple sealings: these are all ordinances. They involve real, physical actions like going under water, breaking bread, or laying hands on someone's head. But behind each physical act is a spiritual promise from God. The question is: who has the right to perform them?

Imagine you needed surgery. Would you let just anyone pick up a scalpel and operate on you? Of course not. You would want a licensed, trained, authorized surgeon. The same principle applies to ordinances. They must be performed by someone who holds the proper **priesthood authority**, someone God has actually authorized to act in His name.

The fifth Article of Faith makes this crystal clear: "We believe that a man must be called of God, by prophecy, and by the laying on of hands by those who are in authority, to preach the Gospel and administer in the ordinances thereof." You can't just decide on your own to baptize someone. You can't walk up to a loaf of bread and declare it the sacrament. The authority must come from God through proper channels.

Why is this so important? Because an ordinance is not just a nice ceremony. It is a **binding agreement** between a person and God. Think of it like a legal contract. If someone signs a contract who has no legal authority to do so, the contract means nothing, no matter how sincere they were. In the same way, a baptism performed without priesthood authority may be sincere and heartfelt, but it is not recognized by God as a valid covenant.

This is one of the reasons the Restoration matters so much. When John the Baptist appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery and conferred the Aaronic Priesthood upon them (see Joseph Smith--History 1:68-72), he restored the **authority** that had been lost from the earth. Without that event, no one on earth would have had the right to perform a single valid ordinance.

Every time you see a priesthood holder perform an ordinance, you are witnessing a direct chain of authority that goes back through the laying on of hands all the way to Jesus Christ Himself. That is why ordinances require the priesthood, because God's covenants require God's authorization.
