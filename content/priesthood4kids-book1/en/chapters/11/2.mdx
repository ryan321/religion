### Lesson 11.2 -- Which Priesthood for Which Ordinance

Not every ordinance can be performed by every priesthood holder. God has organized His priesthood into two levels (the Aaronic and the Melchizedek), and each level is authorized to perform specific ordinances. Understanding which priesthood performs which ordinance helps you see the order and purpose behind God's plan.

The **Aaronic Priesthood** handles the ordinances connected to the preparatory gospel. Doctrine and Covenants 20:46 teaches that priests in the Aaronic Priesthood have authority to "baptize, and administer the sacrament." D&C 107:20 adds that the lesser priesthood holds "the key of the ministering of angels and the preparatory gospel." So the two main ordinances of the Aaronic Priesthood are **baptism** and the **sacrament**. These are foundational. They open the door to the gospel and allow members to renew their covenants weekly.

The **Melchizedek Priesthood** handles the higher ordinances. These include **confirmation** (making someone a member of the Church and bestowing the gift of the Holy Ghost), **blessings of healing** for the sick, **ordinations** to priesthood offices, **setting apart** for callings, and all **temple ordinances** including the endowment and sealings. These ordinances require the greater authority because they deal with higher covenants and eternal promises.

Here is a simple way to think about it. The Aaronic Priesthood brings people **into** the gospel through baptism and helps them **stay** in the gospel through the sacrament. The Melchizedek Priesthood takes them **deeper** into the gospel through confirmation, blessings, and temple covenants. Both are essential. You can't skip one and jump to the other.

Notice how the system builds step by step. A young man is baptized by a priest (Aaronic Priesthood), then confirmed by an elder (Melchizedek Priesthood). Each week he partakes of the sacrament blessed by a priest (Aaronic Priesthood). When he is sick, he receives a blessing from elders (Melchizedek Priesthood). When he enters the temple, Melchizedek Priesthood holders officiate. Every stage of a member's spiritual life is served by the right level of priesthood at the right time.

This is not random organization. It reflects God's perfect order. As Paul wrote, "God is not the author of confusion, but of peace" (1 Corinthians 14:33). Every ordinance has its place, every priesthood level has its role, and together they provide everything a person needs to return to Heavenly Father.
