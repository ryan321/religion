### Lesson 11.3 -- How Ordinances Work

Have you ever wondered what actually happens during an ordinance? How does a priesthood holder perform one, and why do some ordinances have exact words while others don't? Understanding the mechanics of ordinances helps you appreciate how carefully God has organized His work.

Most priesthood ordinances involve the **laying on of hands**. This means the person performing the ordinance places his hands on the head of the person receiving it. Confirmations, blessings of healing, ordinations, and setting apart all use this pattern. It is the same method used in ancient times. When Moses set Joshua apart to lead Israel, he "laid his hands upon him, and gave him a charge" (Numbers 27:23). The physical act of laying on hands is a visible sign that God's authority is being exercised.

Some ordinances have **prescribed words**, meaning the exact wording is given by revelation and must be spoken precisely. The sacrament prayers are the best example. Doctrine and Covenants 20:77 gives the exact prayer for the bread, and D&C 20:79 gives the exact prayer for the water. If a priest makes a mistake in the wording, he must repeat the prayer until it is correct. God cares about the details because these words represent His covenant with His people.

Other ordinances use words **given by the Spirit**. When an elder gives a blessing to a sick person, there is no script to follow. The elder anoints with oil (as taught in James 5:14-15: "Is any sick among you? let him call for the elders of the church; and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord"), then seals the anointing and speaks whatever the Holy Ghost directs. The words come through inspiration, not from a manual.

In every ordinance, the priesthood holder acts **in the name of Jesus Christ** and **by the authority of the priesthood**. He is not acting on his own. He is God's authorized representative, carrying out God's work with God's permission. It is like an ambassador who speaks for a king. The words carry the king's authority, not the ambassador's.

This is why preparation matters. A priesthood holder should approach every ordinance with prayer, faith, and worthiness. He is standing in for the Savior Himself. That is a responsibility worth taking seriously.
