### Lesson 11.4 -- What Priesthood Cannot Do

The priesthood is the power of God, and it is real and mighty. But it is not unlimited for the person who holds it. There are things the priesthood **cannot** do, and understanding these limits actually helps you appreciate it more, not less.

First, a priesthood blessing **does not guarantee a specific outcome**. When an elder blesses a sick person, he does not have the power to override God's will. The blessing is given according to the person's faith and according to **God's purposes**, not the desires of the person giving or receiving the blessing. Sometimes the Lord heals. Sometimes He does not. The Savior Himself prayed in Gethsemane, "Not my will, but thine, be done" (Luke 22:42). If even Jesus submitted His will to the Father, then every priesthood holder must do the same.

Second, the priesthood **cannot override agency**. God has given every person the right to choose for themselves. A father may hold the Melchizedek Priesthood, but he cannot use it to force his children to obey, believe, or repent. Agency is sacred, and the priesthood respects it completely. You can bless, invite, teach, and serve, but you cannot compel.

Third, the priesthood **cannot be used for personal gain or pride**. This is one of the most important warnings in all of scripture. Doctrine and Covenants 121:36-37 teaches: "The rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven, and the powers of heaven cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness. That they may be conferred upon us, it is true; but when we undertake to cover our sins, or to gratify our pride, our vain ambition, or to exercise control or dominion or compulsion upon the souls of the children of men, in any degree of unrighteousness, behold, the heavens withdraw themselves; the Spirit of the Lord is grieved." The moment someone tries to use the priesthood to show off, control others, or benefit himself, the power is gone. The heavens close. The authority becomes meaningless.

This is why humility is essential. The priesthood is God's power used on **God's terms** for **God's purposes**. A worthy priesthood holder is a servant, not a ruler. He follows the Spirit, submits to God's will, and uses his authority to bless others, never himself.

Understanding what the priesthood cannot do protects you from misusing it. And that protection is one of the greatest blessings a priesthood holder can have.
