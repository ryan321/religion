### Lesson 12.1 -- Aaronic Priesthood Offices

The Aaronic Priesthood has three offices that young men progress through as they grow: **deacon**, **teacher**, and **priest**. Each office builds on the one before it, and the duties **accumulate**, meaning when you move to a higher office, you keep all the responsibilities from the previous one and add new ones on top.

A young man can become a **deacon** at age twelve. Deacons have the responsibility to **pass the sacrament** to members of the congregation. They also collect **fast offerings** and **assist the bishop** in whatever he needs. It may seem like a small beginning, but passing the sacrament is sacred. You are delivering the emblems of the Savior's body and blood to the people. Doctrine and Covenants 84:111 teaches that deacons are to be "appointed to watch over the church."

At age fourteen, a young man can be ordained a **teacher**. Teachers keep all the duties of a deacon and add new ones. They help **prepare the sacrament** by setting up the bread and water before the meeting begins. They are also called to **minister** to members and to "watch over the church always, and be with and strengthen them" (D&C 20:53). Teachers are specifically commanded to "see that there is no iniquity in the church" and to "see that the church meet together often" (D&C 20:54-55). This is a responsibility of real spiritual leadership.

At age sixteen, a young man can be ordained a **priest**. Priests have powerful authority. They can **baptize** new members, **bless the sacrament**, and **ordain** other deacons, teachers, and priests (D&C 20:46-52). A priest can also "preach, teach, expound, exhort" and "visit the house of each member" (D&C 20:46-47). Priests carry every duty of a deacon and teacher plus these additional responsibilities.

Notice the pattern. God does not hand you everything at once. He lets you grow step by step. A twelve-year-old learns to serve by passing the sacrament. A fourteen-year-old learns to watch over others. A sixteen-year-old learns to perform sacred ordinances. By the time a young man is ready for the Melchizedek Priesthood, he has years of experience in faithful service behind him. Each office is preparation for the next, building a foundation of faithfulness one step at a time.
