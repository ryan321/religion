### Lesson 12.2 -- Elder and High Priest

When a man receives the Melchizedek Priesthood, he is typically ordained to the office of **elder**. This is the foundational office of the higher priesthood, and it carries significant authority and responsibility.

Doctrine and Covenants 20:38-45 outlines the duties of an elder in detail. Elders have the authority to **confirm** people as members of the Church and **bestow the gift of the Holy Ghost**. They can **bless the sick** through the laying on of hands and the anointing of oil. They can **ordain** other elders, priests, teachers, and deacons. They can **conduct meetings** as directed by the Holy Ghost and **administer the sacrament** when needed.

Most adult men in the Church are elders. This is not a lesser status; it is a calling of great power and trust. An elder holds every key needed to perform the essential ordinances that bless individuals and families. When your father or another elder gives you a priesthood blessing, he is exercising real Melchizedek Priesthood authority that comes directly from God.

The office of **high priest** represents a higher office within the same Melchizedek Priesthood. High priests hold all the authority of an elder plus additional responsibility for **presiding**. Bishops, stake presidents, and mission presidents are all ordained as high priests because their callings require them to preside over others.

It is important to understand that a high priest and an elder hold the **same priesthood**. The difference is in the **office**, not the priesthood itself. Think of it like this: two soldiers may both serve in the same army, but one might be a lieutenant and the other a captain. They serve in the same army with the same mission, but they have different roles and levels of responsibility.

The Apostle Paul taught that God gave the Church "apostles... prophets... evangelists... pastors and teachers; for the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry" (Ephesians 4:11-12). Each office exists to serve God's children, not to create a ranking system of importance. Whether a man serves as an elder or a high priest, his purpose is the same: to use the Melchizedek Priesthood to bless, serve, and bring others to Christ.

The office you hold matters less than how faithfully you fulfill it.
