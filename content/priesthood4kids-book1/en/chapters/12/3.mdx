### Lesson 12.3 -- Patriarch, Seventy, and Apostle

Beyond the offices of elder and high priest, the Melchizedek Priesthood includes three additional offices that serve unique and vital roles in the Church: **patriarch**, **seventy**, and **apostle**.

A **patriarch** is ordained to give **patriarchal blessings**. These are special, personal blessings that declare a person's lineage in the house of Israel and provide inspired guidance for their life. Every stake has an ordained patriarch. Doctrine and Covenants 107:39-56 explains that this office has existed since the days of Adam, passed down through the great patriarchs of the Old Testament. A patriarchal blessing is given once in a person's lifetime and is considered personal scripture, a message from God to that individual.

The **Seventy** are called as special witnesses who assist the Twelve Apostles in directing the work of the Church across the entire world. Doctrine and Covenants 107:25 describes them as those who should "preach the gospel, and to be especial witnesses unto the Gentiles and in all the world." D&C 107:34 adds that they act "in the name of the Lord, under the direction of the Twelve." Today there are multiple quorums of Seventy, and their members travel throughout the world organizing, teaching, and strengthening the Church in every nation. They help carry the enormous responsibility of a global church.

The **Apostle** holds the highest office in the Melchizedek Priesthood. Doctrine and Covenants 107:23 teaches that the Twelve Apostles are "special witnesses of the name of Christ in all the world." An apostle holds **all the keys** of the priesthood. This means he has the authority to direct every aspect of God's work on earth. The Twelve Apostles, together with the First Presidency, lead the entire Church of Jesus Christ.

The word "apostle" comes from a Greek word meaning "one who is sent." Just as Jesus sent His original Twelve Apostles to preach the gospel to all nations (see Matthew 28:19-20), today's apostles carry the same mission: to testify of Christ and direct His work throughout the world.

Each of these offices (patriarch, seventy, and apostle) serves a distinct purpose. Together they ensure that God's priesthood organization reaches every corner of the earth and blesses every one of His children.
