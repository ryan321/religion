### Lesson 12.4 -- The Prophet

At the head of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints stands one man who holds a calling unlike any other on earth: the **President of the Church**, who is also the **prophet, seer, and revelator** for the entire world.

The President of the Church is the only person on earth authorized to exercise **all priesthood keys** at the same time. Every key ever restored, from the keys John the Baptist brought to those given by Moses, Elias, and Elijah in the Kirtland Temple, rests with the prophet. Other apostles hold these keys as well, but only the President is authorized to direct their use for the whole Church. Doctrine and Covenants 107:91-92 explains: "And again, the duty of the President of the office of the High Priesthood is to preside over the whole church, and to be like unto Moses... to be a seer, a revelator, a translator, and a prophet, having all the gifts of God."

The prophet is always the **senior apostle**, the apostle who has served the longest in the Quorum of the Twelve. When a President of the Church passes away, the senior apostle is set apart as the new prophet. This creates an **unbroken line of succession** that stretches all the way back to Joseph Smith, who was called by God and ordained under the hands of angelic messengers.

What makes the prophet unique is that he **receives revelation for the entire Church**. Bishops receive revelation for their wards. Stake presidents receive revelation for their stakes. Parents receive revelation for their families. But only the prophet receives revelation that guides all of God's children everywhere. The Lord said through Amos, "Surely the Lord God will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets" (Amos 3:7).

This is the highest calling on earth. Not because the man is perfect, but because the calling carries God's full authority. Every prophet has been an ordinary man (a farmer, a businessman, a teacher) called by God to an extraordinary work. The power is not in the man. It is in the priesthood keys he holds and the God who called him.

When you sustain the prophet by raising your hand, you are declaring that you recognize God's chosen leader and that you will follow the revelation he receives. It is one of the most important things a member of the Church can do.
