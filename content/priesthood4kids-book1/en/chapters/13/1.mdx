### Lesson 13.1 -- What a Quorum Is

When you hear the word **quorum**, you might think it just means a class at church. But a quorum is so much more than that. A quorum is an **organized group of priesthood holders** who are called to work together, support each other, and serve others. It is a brotherhood, a team united by sacred authority.

Every quorum has a **president** who leads and directs the group. The members share duties and responsibilities. They look out for each other, teach one another, and serve together. In Doctrine and Covenants 107:85-89, the Lord explains how quorum presidents should sit in council with their members, teaching them "the duty of their office." This isn't just about sitting in a classroom. It's about learning how to magnify the priesthood together.

Here is something interesting about the word "quorum." In everyday language, a quorum means the **minimum number of people needed** to take official action. If you have a committee of ten people, maybe six have to show up before any votes count. That idea teaches us something powerful about priesthood quorums: **every member matters**. When someone is missing, the quorum is incomplete. Your presence isn't optional; it's essential.

Think about a basketball team. You need five players on the court. If only four show up, the team can't play. A priesthood quorum works the same way. Each person brings something important. When someone stops coming, the whole group feels the gap. That is why quorum members go looking for each other. They don't leave anyone behind.

So what does a quorum actually do? The purposes are simple but powerful. Quorum members **support** each other through hard times. They **teach** each other the gospel. They **serve** people in their ward and community. And they **lift** each other to become better disciples of Jesus Christ. The Lord designed quorums to make sure no priesthood holder ever has to stand alone.

The Apostle Paul compared the Church to a body, saying "the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee" (1 Corinthians 12:21). Every part is needed. In a quorum, every member, whether brand new or experienced, has something valuable to contribute.

A quorum is not a club, not a class, and not just a group of friends. It is a sacred brotherhood organized by God so that priesthood holders can grow stronger together than they ever could alone.
