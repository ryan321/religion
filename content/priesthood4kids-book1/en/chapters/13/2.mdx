### Lesson 13.2 -- Ward-Level Quorums

In every ward, the priesthood is organized into quorums so that the work of the Lord can move forward in an orderly way. Each quorum has a specific size, a specific leader, and a specific mission. Let's walk through the quorums you'll find at the ward level.

The **deacons quorum** can have up to **12 members**. Deacons are young men who hold the Aaronic Priesthood, usually ages 11 and 12. Their quorum is presided over by a **deacons quorum president** -- and here's something important: that president is chosen from among the deacons themselves. He is not an adult leader. He is a young man who is called, set apart, and given real responsibility. He has counselors and a secretary, just like other leaders in the Church.

The **teachers quorum** can have up to **24 members**. Teachers are usually ages 13 and 14 and also hold the Aaronic Priesthood. Like the deacons, the teachers quorum is led by a president chosen from among its own members.

The **priests quorum** can have up to **48 members**. Priests are usually ages 15 through 18. But here is a key difference: the priests quorum is presided over by the **bishop** of the ward. The Lord established this in Doctrine and Covenants 107:87-88, where He explains that the bishop is the president of the priests quorum. The bishop may call an assistant from among the priests to help, but the bishop himself holds the keys for that quorum.

For adult men, the **elders quorum** includes all **elders and high priests** in the ward. This quorum is led by an **elders quorum president** who is called and set apart by the stake president. The elders quorum handles much of the service and ministering work in the ward.

Each quorum president -- whether a twelve-year-old deacon or a grown man leading the elders -- holds **keys** for his quorum. Those keys give him the right to receive revelation for the quorum, direct its work, and watch over its members. As the Lord said, "I have given unto him the keys of the mysteries of the kingdom" (D&C 28:7). Every quorum leader acts under the direction of those with higher authority.

Ward-level quorums make sure that every priesthood holder has a place where he belongs, where he is taught, and where he can serve.
