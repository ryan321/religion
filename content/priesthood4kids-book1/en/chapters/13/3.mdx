### Lesson 13.3 -- Stake and General Church Quorums

The priesthood isn't organized only at the ward level. It extends upward through the stake and all the way to the leaders who preside over the entire Church. Each level of organization has priesthood quorums and leaders with specific responsibilities.

At the **stake level**, the **stake presidency** presides over all priesthood work in the stake. A stake usually covers several wards, and the stake president holds keys to direct the priesthood throughout all of them. He and his counselors train bishops, guide elders quorum presidents, and make sure priesthood ordinances and service are happening in every ward.

The stake also has a **patriarch** -- a Melchizedek Priesthood holder who is called and ordained to give **patriarchal blessings** to members of the stake. These blessings declare a person's lineage in the house of Israel and give personal promises and guidance from the Lord.

At the **general Church level**, the organization becomes even more significant. The **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles** is one of the most important governing bodies in the Church. Doctrine and Covenants 107:23-24 describes them as "twelve traveling councilors" who are "special witnesses of the name of Christ in all the world." They hold all the keys of the priesthood and travel throughout the earth teaching and testifying.

The **Quorums of the Seventy** assist the Twelve in their work. Doctrine and Covenants 107:25-26 explains that the Seventy act "in the name of the Lord, under the direction of the Twelve." They help administer the Church in different areas around the world, making sure that priesthood power reaches every nation.

The **Presiding Bishopric** oversees the temporal affairs of the Church -- things like finances, welfare, and Church buildings. They work under the direction of the First Presidency.

And at the very top is the **First Presidency**, which Doctrine and Covenants 107:22 calls "the highest council of the church" and "the highest quorum." The First Presidency consists of the prophet and his counselors. They hold all priesthood keys and direct the entire Church under the revelation of God.

This organized structure means that priesthood authority flows in a clear line from God through His prophets and apostles, down through stakes and wards, all the way to the newest deacon in the smallest branch. Nobody is overlooked. Everyone is connected.
