### Lesson 13.4 -- The First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve

At the head of the Church of Jesus Christ stands the **First Presidency** -- the prophet and his two counselors. Doctrine and Covenants 107:22 describes them as "three Presiding High Priests" who form "a quorum of the Presidency of the Church." They are the **highest governing body** on the earth, authorized to receive revelation for the entire Church and direct all its affairs.

Right beside them is the **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles**, the **second-highest governing body**. D&C 107:23-24 teaches that the Twelve are "equal in authority and power" to the First Presidency, meaning they hold the same priesthood keys. Together, the First Presidency and the Twelve hold **all the keys** of the priesthood and direct the Church under continuous revelation from God.

But what happens when the prophet dies? This is one of the most important parts of priesthood organization. When a prophet passes away, the **First Presidency is dissolved**. The counselors return to their places in the Quorum of the Twelve. At that point, the **Quorum of the Twelve**, under the leadership of the **senior apostle** -- the one who has served the longest -- presides over the Church.

The Twelve then meet and, when the Lord directs, the senior apostle is sustained and set apart as the new President of the Church. He calls counselors, and a new First Presidency is organized. This process has happened many times throughout Church history, and it has worked perfectly every time.

Why does this matter? Because it ensures that the **priesthood keys are never lost**. There is never a moment when the Church is without authorized leadership. The keys pass in an unbroken chain. Jesus Christ Himself stands at the head, and He has put a system in place so that His authority continues on the earth no matter what happens.

The Apostle Paul taught that Christ placed apostles and prophets in the Church "for the perfecting of the saints" and "till we all come in the unity of the faith" (Ephesians 4:11-13). The First Presidency and the Twelve are not just administrators -- they are prophets, seers, and revelators called to guide God's children home.

This is God's Church, and He has organized it so that it will never fall apart.
