### Lesson 14.1 -- Types of Priesthood Blessings

One of the most beautiful things about the priesthood is that it allows God's power to reach into our lives through **blessings**. There are several types of priesthood blessings, and each one serves a different purpose.

**Blessings of healing** are given to people who are sick or injured. The Apostle James taught, "Is any sick among you? let him call for the elders of the church; and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord: and the prayer of faith shall save the sick" (James 5:14-15). In these blessings, **consecrated oil** is used, oil that has been set apart for sacred purposes. One elder anoints the person's head with oil, and then another (or the same elder) seals the anointing and pronounces a blessing. The Lord confirmed this practice in Doctrine and Covenants 42:44, commanding the elders to bless the sick.

**Blessings of comfort and counsel** are given without oil. These are sometimes called simply "priesthood blessings." A father might give his child a blessing before a hard school year, before a big decision, or during a difficult time. These blessings offer guidance, peace, and reassurance from the Lord.

**Father's blessings** are a special tradition. A father who holds the Melchizedek Priesthood can place his hands on his child's head and pronounce a blessing at important milestones: the start of a school year, before a mission, or any time his child needs extra support. These blessings can be deeply personal and powerful.

**Baby blessings** happen when an infant is given a name and blessed, usually in sacrament meeting. Doctrine and Covenants 20:70 instructs that "every member of the church of Christ having children is to bring them unto the elders before the church, who are to lay their hands upon them in the name of Jesus Christ, and bless them in his name."

**Setting apart** happens when someone is called to a position in the Church. A priesthood leader lays hands on the person's head and sets them apart, giving them authority and blessings for their calling.

**Patriarchal blessings** are given once in a lifetime by an ordained patriarch. These blessings declare your lineage in the house of Israel and give personal promises from the Lord.

Each of these blessings is a way for Heavenly Father to speak directly into your life through His priesthood.
