### Lesson 14.3 -- Priesthood and Women

Some people wonder, "If women don't hold priesthood offices, does that mean the priesthood has nothing to do with them?" The answer is a strong **no**. Women are deeply connected to priesthood power, even though they are not ordained to priesthood offices.

First, it helps to understand an important truth that President Dallin H. Oaks taught: **"Men are not the priesthood."** The priesthood is God's power. Men who are ordained hold priesthood offices and perform ordinances, but the power itself belongs to God, and He shares it with all of His faithful children.

Women participate in priesthood power in several important ways. When a woman receives her **temple endowment**, she enters into sacred covenants that give her access to priesthood power and blessings. The temple is where this connection becomes especially clear. Women perform ordinances in the temple, exercise priesthood authority in those settings, and receive the same promises of exaltation that men do.

Women also serve in **callings** throughout the Church with **priesthood authority delegated through keys**. A Relief Society president, a Young Women leader, a Primary president: each of these women is set apart and given authority to act in her calling. She receives revelation for her stewardship. She leads and directs and serves with real, God-given authority.

The Prophet Joseph Smith said that the Relief Society was organized **"under the priesthood after the pattern of the priesthood."** From the very beginning, women in the Church were organized to work alongside priesthood holders in building the kingdom of God. The Apostle Paul taught, "Neither is the man without the woman, neither the woman without the man, in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 11:11). God's work requires both.

Women pray, serve, teach, lead, receive revelation, make covenants, and exercise faith, all of which draw on priesthood power. A mother who raises her children in the gospel is exercising enormous spiritual influence. A woman who serves a mission is called and set apart with priesthood authority. A sister who gives a talk in sacrament meeting speaks by the same Spirit that guides the entire Church.

The priesthood blesses everyone. It is not a wall that separates men and women. It is a bridge that connects all of God's children to His power.
