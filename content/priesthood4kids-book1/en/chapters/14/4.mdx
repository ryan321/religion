### Lesson 14.4 -- What the Priesthood Can and Cannot Do

The priesthood is incredibly powerful, but it is not unlimited in the way some people think. Understanding what the priesthood **can** and **cannot** do will help you respect it and use it the way God intends.

**What the priesthood CAN do:**

The priesthood can **heal** the sick through blessings of faith. It can **comfort** hearts that are breaking. It can **bless** children, families, and individuals with guidance from the Lord. It can **ordain** men to priesthood offices. It can **baptize** and **confirm** people as members of the Church. It can **seal** families together for eternity in temples. It can **authorize** people to serve in callings. It can **direct** the work of the Church through revelation. And priesthood holders can **receive revelation** for those they are called to serve.

That is a long and powerful list. When you think about everything the priesthood does, it touches every part of the gospel.

**What the priesthood CANNOT do:**

The priesthood cannot **override God's will**. A priesthood holder cannot command God to do something God has not chosen to do. Every blessing is subject to the will of the Father.

The priesthood cannot **force healing**. Sometimes faithful people receive blessings and are not healed in this life. That does not mean the blessing failed or that their faith was too small. It means God has a different plan, and His plan is always perfect.

The priesthood cannot **override agency**. No one can use the priesthood to force another person to believe, to repent, or to change. Agency is sacred and the priesthood respects it completely.

The priesthood cannot be used for **personal power or gain**. This is one of the most important scriptures in all of priesthood study. Doctrine and Covenants 121:36-37 warns: "The rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven, and the powers of heaven cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness. That they may be conferred upon us, it is true; but when we undertake to cover our sins, or to gratify our pride, our vain ambition ... the heavens withdraw themselves; the Spirit of the Lord is grieved."

The priesthood cannot **guarantee specific outcomes**. It is God's power on **God's terms**. When we use it with humility, faith, and righteousness, miracles happen. But we do not get to dictate the results.

The priesthood is a trust, not a trophy. It must be exercised with pure intent, or it simply does not work.
