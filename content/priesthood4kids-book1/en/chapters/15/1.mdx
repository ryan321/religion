### Lesson 15.1 -- The Sons of Levi

As we look toward the future, the scriptures tell us about amazing events that will happen with the priesthood before the Savior returns. One of the most fascinating is the prophecy about the **sons of Levi**.

In the Old Testament, the tribe of Levi was set apart to serve as priests in Israel. The Levites performed sacrifices, cared for the tabernacle and temple, and administered sacred ordinances. When the Aaronic Priesthood was restored in 1829, the angel who appeared was **John the Baptist** -- and the words he spoke included a remarkable promise. Doctrine and Covenants 13 records: "Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah, I confer the Priesthood of Aaron ... and this shall never be taken again from the earth, until the sons of Levi do offer again an offering unto the Lord in righteousness."

This same promise appears in Malachi 3:3, where the Lord says He will "purify the sons of Levi, and purge them as gold and silver, that they may offer unto the Lord an offering in righteousness."

So what does this mean? The Prophet Joseph Smith taught that the sons of Levi -- the descendants of that ancient tribe -- will one day make a special **offering unto the Lord in righteousness**. This will not be a return to regular animal sacrifices like those performed in the Old Testament. Joseph Smith clarified that while there may be a one-time sacrifice connected to this event, it does not mean the old system of daily and weekly sacrifices will be restored. The law of Moses has been fulfilled through Jesus Christ.

This offering by the sons of Levi is connected to the **final establishment of God's kingdom** on the earth. As Doctrine and Covenants 128:24 declares, the sons of Levi will make their offering "in a temple which shall be built unto the Lord." It is a future event, something that will happen as part of the grand events leading up to and including the Second Coming of Jesus Christ.

This prophecy reminds us that God keeps every promise. What He said thousands of years ago through prophets like Malachi -- and what the angel John the Baptist confirmed in 1829 -- will all be fulfilled. The priesthood story is not finished. Some of its most important chapters are still ahead.
