### Lesson 15.3 -- Returning the Keys to Christ

Throughout history, God has called prophets and given them priesthood keys to lead His work in their time. Moses held keys. Elijah held keys. Peter, James, and John held keys. Joseph Smith received keys from each of these ancient prophets. But one day, all of those keys will be returned to the One who gave them in the first place.

At the grand council at Adam-ondi-Ahman, every prophet who held keys in **any dispensation** will stand and account for his stewardship. Think about what that means. Noah will be there. Abraham will be there. Moses, Elijah, and all the prophets who restored keys in the Kirtland Temple (D&C 110) will be there. Joseph Smith and every prophet who followed him will be there. Each one will report on how he used the priesthood authority entrusted to him.

Then **Adam** -- who was the first mortal to hold priesthood keys -- will gather all of those keys together and deliver them back to **Jesus Christ**, the rightful holder of all priesthood power. The Prophet Joseph Smith taught that Adam "holds the keys of the dispensation of the fullness of times" and that "all the keys of every dispensation" will be delivered to Christ at this great meeting (see Teachings of the Prophet Joseph Smith).

Why does this matter? Because it means the priesthood has always had an owner, and that owner is Jesus Christ. The Savior said, "All power is given unto me in heaven and in earth" (Matthew 28:18). Every prophet who ever held keys was acting as a steward -- a caretaker of something that belonged to Christ. When the keys are returned, the stewardship is complete.

This event also **prepares the way for the Second Coming**. Once Christ has received all priesthood keys back, He comes in glory. He takes direct control of His kingdom. There is no more need for stewards because the **King Himself** has arrived.

Think of it like this: if a father leaves on a long trip and puts his eldest son in charge of the family business, the son runs things carefully. But when the father comes home, the son happily hands everything back and says, "Here is what I've done." That is what will happen when Adam returns the keys to Christ.

The priesthood was given to Adam in the beginning. It will return to the One who gave it. And with that, the greatest chapter of history begins.
