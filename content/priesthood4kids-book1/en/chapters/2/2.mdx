### Lesson 2.2 -- Adam and the Priesthood

Who was the very first person on earth to hold the priesthood? It was **Adam** -- the first man, the first father, and the first priesthood holder. And here's what makes it even more remarkable: Adam didn't receive the priesthood from another man. He received it directly from **God Himself**.

Doctrine and Covenants 84:16 tells us plainly that "the Lord confirmed a priesthood also upon Aaron and his seed, throughout all their generations," but before Aaron, before Moses, before anyone else, the priesthood was given to Adam. Verse 16 of that same section traces the line of authority and shows that it began with Adam, who was ordained by God personally. There was no one else to do it -- Adam was the first. So God, who holds the fulness of all priesthood power, placed His hands on Adam and gave him the **Melchizedek Priesthood**.

What did Adam do with this authority? He didn't just carry it around like a trophy. He **used** it. Adam taught his family the gospel. He performed **baptisms**. He conferred the **Holy Ghost**. He offered **sacrifices** that pointed forward to the coming of Jesus Christ. He led and guided his family as a patriarch -- a father who holds priesthood authority and uses it to bless his household.

Moses 5:58-59 tells us that Adam was baptized, received the Holy Ghost, and was "born of the Spirit." All of these ordinances were performed by priesthood power. Adam understood that the priesthood was not about him -- it was about serving God and helping his family come unto Christ.

Here's something incredible to think about: the same priesthood that Adam held is the same priesthood that exists in the Church today. It hasn't changed. It hasn't been upgraded. God's power is the same yesterday, today, and forever (Hebrews 13:8). When a young man today is ordained to the priesthood, he holds the same authority that Adam received in the Garden of Eden.

Adam also set the pattern for how the priesthood should work in families. He didn't just serve at church -- he served at **home**. He blessed his children, taught them the commandments, and helped them understand the plan of salvation. Priesthood service starts in the family, and Adam showed us that from the very beginning.

Adam was the first of many great priesthood holders. But he was also the model -- the example of what it means to receive God's power and use it to lift others.
