### Lesson 2.3 -- The Patriarchs

After Adam received the priesthood from God, what happened next? Did it disappear? Did people have to figure it out on their own? No. The priesthood was carefully **passed down** from father to son in an unbroken chain for generations. These faithful men are called the **patriarchs**, and their story is one of the most remarkable things in all of scripture.

Doctrine and Covenants 107:40-52 gives us the exact list. Pay attention, because this is the line of authority from Adam all the way to Noah: Adam ordained **Seth**, who ordained **Enos**, who ordained **Cainan**, who ordained **Mahalaleel**, who ordained **Jared**, who ordained **Enoch**, who ordained **Methuselah**, who ordained **Lamech**, who ordained **Noah**. Every single link in that chain is recorded. No gaps. No guesses. God kept a perfect record.

What's amazing about this list is that many of these men actually knew Adam personally. D&C 107:53 tells us that Adam gathered his righteous posterity together at **Adam-ondi-Ahman** three years before his death, and there he blessed them and gave them his final counsel. Several of the patriarchs in the chain were alive at that gathering. The priesthood wasn't passed through strangers; it was passed through family.

After Noah, the line continued through **Abraham**, who received the priesthood and was told by God, "In thee and in thy seed shall all the families of the earth be blessed" (Abraham 2:11). Abraham passed the priesthood to his son **Isaac**, who passed it to his son **Jacob**. The covenant and the authority stayed in the family, generation after generation.

This is why the priesthood has always been a **family affair**. It wasn't meant to be something separate from home life. Fathers blessed their children, taught them the gospel, and then ordained them to carry the work forward. The priesthood flowed naturally through righteous families.

Why does this matter to you? Because when you look at this chain of patriarchs, you see that God's plan for the priesthood has always been personal and connected. Each patriarch knew the one who ordained him. Each one had a face, a name, and a relationship with the man who passed the authority along. The priesthood is not a distant, impersonal force. It is deeply human, passed from one faithful person to another, all the way back to Adam, and through Adam, back to God.

You are part of that same story. The priesthood you will hold connects you to every name on that list.
