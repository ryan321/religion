### Lesson 2.4 -- Why This History Matters

You might be thinking, "Okay, Adam had the priesthood. The patriarchs passed it down. That's nice history, but why should I care?" Great question. And the answer might surprise you.

The priesthood you can hold today is the **exact same priesthood** that Adam held. Not a copy. Not a newer version. The same authority, from the same God, for the same purpose. The priesthood is not a modern invention. It is not something Joseph Smith came up with in the 1800s. It is God's power from the very **beginning** of the world, and it has been on the earth whenever God has had a people willing to receive it.

But here's the important part of the story: the priesthood was **lost**. After the time of Jesus Christ and His apostles, the world went through a period called the **Great Apostasy**. People changed the doctrines, rejected the prophets, and the authority of the priesthood was taken from the earth. For centuries, no one on earth held God's authorized priesthood. As the prophet Amos had foreseen, there was "a famine in the land, not a famine of bread, nor a thirst for water, but of hearing the words of the Lord" (Amos 8:11).

Then came the **Restoration**. In 1829, heavenly messengers brought the priesthood back. John the Baptist appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery and conferred the **Aaronic Priesthood** (D&C 13). Shortly after, the apostles **Peter, James, and John** appeared and conferred the **Melchizedek Priesthood** (D&C 27:12-13). These weren't random angels. These were men who had held the priesthood during their mortal lives and who had the authority to pass it on.

This means that your ordination to the priesthood, when it happens, won't just trace back to your bishop or stake president. It traces through an unbroken line all the way back to Peter, James, and John, who received it from **Jesus Christ**, who holds all priesthood power because He is the Son of God. And Christ's authority reaches back through the patriarchs to Adam, and through Adam to God the Father Himself.

That is your priesthood heritage. Every ordination in the Church today connects to God Himself. When you hold the priesthood, you are not holding something small or ordinary. You are holding an authority that stretches from eternity to eternity, the same power that created the heavens and the earth.

Understanding this history isn't just interesting. It changes what the priesthood means to you. It's not just a church assignment. It's your connection to God across all of time.
