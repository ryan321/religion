### Lesson 3.1 -- Why It's Called That

The Melchizedek Priesthood has an interesting name, and an even more interesting reason behind it. You might wonder, "If this is God's priesthood, why is it named after a man?" The answer goes back thousands of years.

The official name of the higher priesthood is actually the **"Holy Priesthood, after the Order of the Son of God"** (D&C 107:3). That's its real name. But early saints and prophets began calling it the "Melchizedek Priesthood" to avoid using the name of God too frequently. It was a matter of **reverence**. They didn't want to use God's sacred name so casually that it lost its meaning. So they chose the name of one of the greatest priesthood holders who ever lived: **Melchizedek**.

Who was Melchizedek? He was the **king of Salem** (an ancient city that many scholars believe later became Jerusalem) and a **high priest** of God. He lived during the time of Abraham, one of the most important figures in all of scripture. Genesis 14:18-20 tells us that after Abraham won a great battle, Melchizedek came out to meet him. He brought bread and wine, blessed Abraham, and Abraham paid **tithes** to him -- a tenth of everything he had.

Why would Abraham, who had received tremendous promises from God, pay tithes to Melchizedek? Because Melchizedek held the higher priesthood and was God's authorized servant. Abraham recognized Melchizedek's authority and showed respect for it.

The book of Hebrews in the New Testament goes into even more detail. Hebrews 7:1-3 describes Melchizedek as a "priest of the most high God" who was so righteous that he is compared to the Son of God. The chapter explains that the priesthood of Melchizedek is greater than the Levitical (or Aaronic) priesthood because it is eternal and not limited to one family line. Alma 13:17-18 in the Book of Mormon adds that Melchizedek's people had "waxed strong in iniquity," but through his faithful preaching and priesthood leadership, they repented and found peace. That's why he was called the "prince of peace."

So when you hear the name "Melchizedek Priesthood," remember that it honors a man who used God's power to bring an entire city from wickedness to righteousness. His name was chosen because he represented everything the higher priesthood is meant to do: lead people to God and help them find peace.
