### Lesson 3.2 -- What It Includes

The Melchizedek Priesthood is called the **higher priesthood** for a reason: it holds the authority to do things that the Aaronic Priesthood cannot. If the Aaronic Priesthood is like learning the basics, the Melchizedek Priesthood is the full picture. Together they make up the complete priesthood of God, but the Melchizedek Priesthood carries the greater responsibilities.

So what does the Melchizedek Priesthood include? Let's walk through it.

First, it holds the authority to **lead the Church**. Every prophet, apostle, stake president, and bishop operates under the authority of the Melchizedek Priesthood. The Lord explained in Doctrine and Covenants 107:8 that "the Melchizedek Priesthood holds the right of presidency, and has power and authority over all the offices in the church in all ages of the world." That means every calling, every organization, and every council in the Church functions under this priesthood.

Second, Melchizedek Priesthood holders can **confer the gift of the Holy Ghost**. After someone is baptized, an elder or higher authority lays hands on their head and confirms them a member of the Church, giving them the gift of the Holy Ghost. This is one of the most sacred ordinances in the gospel, and it requires the higher priesthood (D&C 33:15).

Third, this priesthood gives the authority to **ordain others** to the priesthood. Elders can ordain other elders. High priests can ordain high priests. The priesthood multiplies as worthy men are called and ordained.

Fourth, Melchizedek Priesthood holders can give **priesthood blessings**: blessings of comfort, healing, counsel, and guidance. James 5:14 teaches, "Is any sick among you? Let him call for the elders of the church; and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord." This practice comes directly from the New Testament and continues today through the Melchizedek Priesthood.

Fifth, this priesthood is required for **temple ordinances**. The endowment, sealings, and other sacred ceremonies performed in the temple all operate by the power and authority of the Melchizedek Priesthood.

Finally, those who hold the proper **keys** within the Melchizedek Priesthood can receive **revelation** for the areas they preside over. A bishop receives revelation for his ward. A prophet receives revelation for the entire Church. The keys determine the scope of that revelation.

The Melchizedek Priesthood is the fulness of God's authority on earth. It's the power that makes everything in the Church possible.
