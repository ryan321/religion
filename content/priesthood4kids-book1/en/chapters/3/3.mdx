### Lesson 3.3 -- Offices in the Melchizedek Priesthood

Here's something that surprises a lot of people: within the Melchizedek Priesthood, there isn't just one job. There are several different **offices**, each with its own specific duties and responsibilities. But here's the key: every person in these offices holds the **same priesthood**. The office determines what you do, not how much priesthood you have.

Doctrine and Covenants 107 lays out the offices clearly. Let's look at each one.

**Elder** is the foundational office of the Melchizedek Priesthood. When a young man receives the Melchizedek Priesthood, he is typically ordained an elder. Elders can give blessings, confirm new members, confer the Holy Ghost, ordain other elders, and teach the gospel. Most adult men in the Church hold this office. D&C 20:38-45 describes the elder's duties, including "teaching, expounding, exhorting, baptizing, and watching over the church."

**High Priest** is an office given to men called to certain leadership positions, such as bishops, stake presidents, and others who preside. The high priest holds the same Melchizedek Priesthood as an elder but is set apart to preside and administer in specific ways. Alma 13:1-2 teaches that high priests are "called and prepared from the foundation of the world," ordained according to God's foreknowledge.

**Patriarch** is a special office. Patriarchs are ordained to give **patriarchal blessings**, personal blessings that declare a person's lineage in the house of Israel and offer guidance and promises for their life. Each stake typically has an ordained patriarch (D&C 107:39).

**Seventy** are called to be special witnesses and missionaries, helping to spread the gospel throughout the world. The Lord organized the first Seventy during His mortal ministry (Luke 10:1), and they continue to serve today as General Authorities and Area Seventies.

**Apostle** is the highest office in the Melchizedek Priesthood. Apostles are special witnesses of Jesus Christ to all the world (D&C 107:23). The Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, along with the First Presidency, leads the Church under the direction of the Savior.

All of these offices hold the same Melchizedek Priesthood. The difference is in the **keys** and **responsibilities** that come with each calling. An elder is not "less than" an apostle in terms of priesthood; he simply has a different assignment. God organizes His work through these offices to make sure everything is done in order and with proper authority.
