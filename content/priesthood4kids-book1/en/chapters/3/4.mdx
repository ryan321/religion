### Lesson 3.4 -- The Melchizedek Priesthood and You

Everything we've talked about in this chapter -- the history, the authority, the offices -- is leading to one big point: the Melchizedek Priesthood is something you can actually hold someday. And that should fill you with both excitement and reverence.

Young men in the Church typically receive the Melchizedek Priesthood at age **18** and are ordained to the office of **elder**. This is a major step. The Aaronic Priesthood is called the "preparatory" priesthood for a reason -- it prepares you for this moment. When you move from the Aaronic to the Melchizedek Priesthood, you are stepping from the preparatory stage into the **fulness** of God's priesthood authority on earth.

What does that mean for your life? It means you can give **priesthood blessings** to family members and others who are sick or in need. Think about the power of that -- when your mom, your sibling, or a friend is struggling, you can lay your hands on their head and pronounce a blessing by the authority of God. James 5:14-15 promises that "the prayer of faith shall save the sick, and the Lord shall raise him up."

It means you can **confirm** people after their baptism and confer the gift of the Holy Ghost. You can **ordain** other worthy men to the priesthood. You can serve in leadership callings that require Melchizedek Priesthood authority. And eventually, you can officiate in **temple ordinances** -- some of the most sacred acts of service in all of God's work.

But here's the part that matters most: authority comes from **ordination**, but power comes from **how you live**. The Lord said in Doctrine and Covenants 121:41-42 that the priesthood should be exercised "only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned." No force. No pride. No showing off. Just quiet, steady, Christlike service.

The Melchizedek Priesthood is a **sacred trust**. When God allows you to hold His higher authority, He is saying, "I believe in you. I trust you to use this power the way my Son would." That trust is earned not by being perfect, but by being humble, repentant, and willing to serve.

Everything you do right now -- how you treat people, how you keep the commandments, how you serve in your Aaronic Priesthood duties -- is preparing you. The Melchizedek Priesthood is ahead of you, and it is one of the greatest blessings you will ever receive.
