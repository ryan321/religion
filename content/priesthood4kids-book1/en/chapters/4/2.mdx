### Lesson 4.2 -- The Levitical Priesthood

You may have heard the words "Aaronic Priesthood" and "Levitical Priesthood" used as if they mean exactly the same thing. They are closely related, but there is a difference worth understanding.

The **tribe of Levi** was one of the twelve tribes of Israel. When Moses organized the children of Israel in the wilderness, the Lord set the Levites apart for special service. Numbers 3:6-8 records that Moses was told to "bring the tribe of Levi near, and present them before Aaron the priest, that they may minister unto him." The Levites were given the responsibility of caring for the **tabernacle**, the portable temple the Israelites carried through the desert. They set it up, took it down, transported it, and guarded it.

Within the tribe of Levi, **Aaron's direct descendants** served as the **priests**. They performed the sacrifices, burned the incense, and carried out the sacred ordinances at the altar. So while all of Aaron's family were Levites, not all Levites were priests. The Levites who were not descendants of Aaron served in supporting roles, carrying the tabernacle furniture, singing in worship, and keeping the sacred spaces clean and orderly.

The **Levitical Priesthood** refers specifically to the priesthood as it functioned among the tribe of Levi under the **Law of Moses**. It was the Aaronic Priesthood in action within a particular time and setting. Think of it this way: "Aaronic Priesthood" is the broader name for the lesser priesthood that God established, while "Levitical Priesthood" describes how that same authority operated in ancient Israel through the tribe of Levi.

Hebrews 7:11 asks, "If therefore perfection were by the Levitical priesthood... what further need was there that another priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec?" This scripture shows that the Levitical system, while important, was never meant to be the final or complete priesthood. It pointed forward to Christ, the great High Priest.

Today we do not use the Levitical system because the Law of Moses was fulfilled by Jesus Christ. But the Aaronic Priesthood itself continues. When a young man blesses the sacrament or baptizes someone, he is exercising the same Aaronic Priesthood authority that once operated through the Levites, just in a different setting and without the animal sacrifices and temple rituals of the old law.

Understanding the Levitical Priesthood helps us appreciate how God organized His people anciently and how He continues to organize them today.
