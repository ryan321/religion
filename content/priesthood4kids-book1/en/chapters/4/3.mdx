### Lesson 4.3 -- Offices in the Aaronic Priesthood

The Aaronic Priesthood has three offices that young men can hold: **deacon**, **teacher**, and **priest**. Each office comes with specific duties, and each one builds on the responsibilities of the one before it. Doctrine and Covenants 20:46-60 describes these offices and what each one is expected to do.

A young man can be ordained a **deacon** at age **twelve**. Deacons have the privilege of **passing the sacrament** to the members of the congregation each Sunday. This is a sacred duty. They are helping people renew their covenants with Heavenly Father. Deacons also **collect fast offerings**, going to the homes of members to gather donations that help the poor and needy. They assist the bishop in looking after the temporal needs of the ward. Doctrine and Covenants 84:111 teaches that deacons are to be "appointed to watch over the church."

At age **fourteen**, a young man can be ordained a **teacher**. Teachers have all the duties of a deacon plus additional responsibilities. They **prepare the sacrament** by breaking the bread and filling the water trays before the meeting begins. Teachers are also called to **minister** to members and to "watch over the church always, and be with and strengthen them" (D&C 20:53). They help make sure that there is no contention or wrongdoing in the congregation. A teacher's role is focused on service and spiritual watchcare.

At age **sixteen**, a young man can be ordained a **priest**. Priests carry all the duties of deacons and teachers, plus they receive significant additional authority. A priest can **baptize** those who are entering the covenant. He can **bless the sacrament** by offering the sacred prayers over the bread and water. He can also **ordain other deacons, teachers, and priests** when authorized by the bishop. These are real, meaningful responsibilities that connect directly to the saving ordinances of the gospel.

Notice how each office builds on the one before. A deacon learns to serve faithfully. A teacher learns to watch over and strengthen others. A priest learns to perform sacred ordinances. This step-by-step pattern is part of why the Aaronic Priesthood is called "preparatory." It prepares young men for the greater responsibilities of the Melchizedek Priesthood and for a lifetime of service in the Lord's Church.

Each office matters. Whether you are twelve or sixteen, you are doing the Lord's work.
