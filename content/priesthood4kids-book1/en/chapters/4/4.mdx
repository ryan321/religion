### Lesson 4.4 -- The Bishop and the Aaronic Priesthood

Every ward in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has a **bishop**, and one of the bishop's most important roles involves the Aaronic Priesthood. Doctrine and Covenants 107:15 declares that "the bishopric is the presidency of this priesthood, and holds the keys thereof." This means the bishop is the **president of the Aaronic Priesthood** in his ward.

What does that look like in practice? The bishop oversees everything related to the Aaronic Priesthood in his ward. He holds the **keys** that authorize the administration of the **sacrament** each Sunday. He authorizes **baptisms** performed within the ward. He watches over the young men who hold the Aaronic Priesthood, making sure they are learning, growing, and fulfilling their duties. He calls leaders to serve in the Young Men organization and works with quorum advisers to support every deacon, teacher, and priest.

The bishop also serves as a **judge in Israel**. This means he has the authority and responsibility to help members repent and stay on the covenant path. Members who need to confess serious sins go to the bishop. He listens, counsels, and helps them access the Savior's Atonement. Doctrine and Covenants 107:72 explains that the bishop is to "judge his people by the testimony of the just." This is a weighty and sacred calling.

Beyond the ward level, the Church has a **Presiding Bishop**. The Presiding Bishopric presides over the Aaronic Priesthood for the **entire Church**. They oversee temporal affairs, including finances, welfare, humanitarian aid, meetinghouse construction, and other practical needs. Just as a ward bishop watches over the Aaronic Priesthood locally, the Presiding Bishop has a global responsibility.

It is worth remembering that the bishop himself holds the **Melchizedek Priesthood**. Specifically, he is ordained a **high priest**. But his calling as bishop gives him presidency over the Aaronic Priesthood. This shows how the two priesthoods work together. The higher priesthood directs and supports the lesser priesthood, and together they accomplish the Lord's work.

For a young man holding the Aaronic Priesthood, the bishop is a key leader and mentor. He is the one who interviews, counsels, and authorizes. When you understand the bishop's role, you see that the Aaronic Priesthood is not left on its own. It is guided by someone the Lord has called and given keys to lead.
