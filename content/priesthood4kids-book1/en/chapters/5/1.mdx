### Lesson 5.1 -- What Priesthood Keys Are

You already know that the priesthood is the authority to act in God's name. But having the priesthood is not the same as having permission to use it however and whenever you want. That is where **priesthood keys** come in.

**Keys** are the authority to **direct** the use of the priesthood within a specific area of responsibility. Think of it like a car. The priesthood is the engine; it has the power to move. But keys are the ignition key. Without turning the key, the engine does not start. A person can hold the priesthood, but he can only use it in official Church ordinances and callings when someone with the right keys authorizes him to do so.

For example, a priest in the Aaronic Priesthood has the authority to baptize. But he cannot just walk up to a pond and baptize anyone he wants. He needs to be **authorized** by someone who holds the keys. In his case, that's the bishop. The bishop holds the keys for baptisms in his ward, and he gives permission for the priest to perform that baptism. Without that authorization, the ordinance is not valid in the eyes of the Church.

The Lord established this pattern so that His Church would be orderly. Doctrine and Covenants 132:7 teaches that "all covenants, contracts, bonds, obligations... that are not made and entered into and sealed by the Holy Spirit of promise, of him who is anointed, both as well for time and for all eternity... are of no efficacy, virtue, or force." In other words, ordinances must be performed under proper authority and authorization to be binding.

Keys also determine **who** can authorize **what** and **where**. A bishop's keys work in his ward. A stake president's keys work in his stake. A mission president's keys work in his mission. No one can step outside the boundaries of his own stewardship. This keeps things organized and prevents confusion.

Understanding keys helps you see why the priesthood is not just about individual power. It is about a connected system of authority that flows from Jesus Christ through His appointed leaders. When an ordinance is performed by the right person with the right authorization, you can trust that it carries God's full approval.
