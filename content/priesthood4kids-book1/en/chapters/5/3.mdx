### Lesson 5.3 -- Who Holds Keys at Every Level

Priesthood keys exist at every level of the Church, from the very top down to a quorum of twelve-year-old deacons. Each leader holds keys for his specific **stewardship** -- the area he has been called to lead -- and no one else's.

At the highest level, the **prophet and President of the Church** holds **all** priesthood keys on the earth. Every key that has ever been restored belongs to him. He is the only person who can authorize the use of all of them. Doctrine and Covenants 132:7 teaches that there is always "one on the earth at a time on whom this power and the keys of this priesthood are conferred."

The **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles** also hold all the keys of the kingdom, but they exercise them **under the direction of the prophet**. They do not act independently. When the prophet dies, the senior apostle becomes the new president because the keys already rest with the Twelve.

At the **stake** level, the **stake president** holds the keys for his stake. He authorizes ordinations to the Melchizedek Priesthood, sets apart leaders, and oversees the spiritual welfare of everyone in his stake. His keys do not extend to another stake.

At the **ward** level, the **bishop** holds the keys for his ward. As we learned in a previous lesson, he presides over the Aaronic Priesthood, authorizes sacrament and baptisms, and serves as a judge in Israel. His keys apply only within his ward boundaries.

A **mission president** holds the keys for his mission. He directs the missionary work, authorizes baptisms within the mission, and presides over all the missionaries serving there.

At the **quorum** level, each quorum president holds keys for his quorum. The **deacons quorum president** holds keys that no one else in the entire Church holds -- the keys to direct the work of his specific quorum. This is remarkable. Doctrine and Covenants 107:85 says the president of the deacons quorum is to "sit in council with them, teaching them the duties of their office." A twelve-year-old can hold real priesthood keys.

This structure means the Lord's Church is never without clear leadership. Every member knows who presides, who authorizes, and who is responsible. It is God's pattern of order.
