### Lesson 5.4 -- Keys and Revelation

Priesthood keys are not just about giving permission for ordinances. They also carry something deeply spiritual: the right to **receive revelation** for a specific stewardship.

When the Lord gives someone priesthood keys, He also opens a channel of communication. The person holding keys can seek and receive **revelation from God** about the people and work he is responsible for. This is one of the most important principles in the Church.

The **prophet** holds the keys to receive revelation for the **entire Church**. He can declare doctrine, announce new temples, restructure organizations, and speak the mind of the Lord to all members everywhere. Doctrine and Covenants 28:2 makes this crystal clear: "No one shall be appointed to receive commandments and revelations in this church excepting my servant Joseph Smith, Jun." Today that same principle applies to the living prophet -- he is the only one authorized to receive revelation for the whole Church.

A **stake president** can receive revelation for his stake. He may feel prompted to call a specific person as a bishop or to emphasize a particular teaching in his stake. But he cannot receive revelation for another stake or for the whole Church. His revelation is bounded by his keys.

A **bishop** can receive revelation for his ward. He may feel guided about how to help a struggling family, who to call to a position, or how to address a challenge in his congregation. This is real and powerful, and it comes because he holds the keys for that ward.

A **mission president** receives revelation for his mission. A **quorum president** receives revelation for his quorum. Even a **father** -- while not holding formal priesthood keys in the same way -- presides in his home and can receive revelation for his family. Doctrine and Covenants 68:25 teaches that parents have a responsibility to teach their children the gospel, and the Lord helps them know how.

This pattern protects the Church from confusion. No one can claim to have revelation for an area outside his stewardship. If someone says he has received a revelation for the whole Church but is not the prophet, you know something is wrong. Keys and revelation go hand in hand, and they always stay within the proper boundaries.

When you understand this connection, you can have confidence that your bishop, your stake president, and your prophet are receiving guidance from God for the people they serve. The Lord speaks to His leaders through the keys He has given them.
