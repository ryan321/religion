### Lesson 6.2 -- The Major Dispensations

Throughout history, God has called seven major prophets to lead dispensations. Each one received priesthood keys and the gospel truths that were needed for his time. Let's walk through them.

**Adam** was the first. After being cast out of the Garden of Eden, Adam received the priesthood and the plan of salvation. He learned about the Atonement of Jesus Christ, was baptized, and taught his children the gospel. Moses 5:58-59 records that "the Gospel began to be preached, from the beginning." Adam's dispensation was the starting point for everything.

**Enoch** was called in a time of great wickedness. He preached with such power that he built a righteous city called Zion. His people were so faithful that the entire city was taken up to heaven (see Moses 7:21). Enoch held the priesthood and led a people who lived the gospel fully.

**Noah** received the priesthood and was commanded to preach repentance before the great flood. When the people refused to listen, God preserved Noah and his family through the ark. After the flood, Noah reestablished the gospel on a cleansed earth. Genesis 6:9 tells us Noah was "a just man and perfect in his generations, and Noah walked with God."

**Abraham** received a covenant from God that his descendants would carry the priesthood and bless all nations of the earth (see Abraham 2:9-11). This Abrahamic covenant remains central to God's plan today. Every priesthood blessing traces back to the promises God made to Abraham.

**Moses** led the children of Israel out of Egypt and received the law from God on Mount Sinai. He held the keys of the gathering of Israel and organized the priesthood among the Levites. Moses' dispensation established the patterns of temple worship and sacrifice.

**Jesus Christ** came in the meridian of time. He held all priesthood keys, organized His Church, called twelve apostles, and performed the Atonement. His dispensation brought the fulness of the gospel to the earth.

**Joseph Smith** was called to open the final dispensation. Doctrine and Covenants 112:30 describes his era as "the dispensation of the fulness of times." Every key, ordinance, and truth from every previous dispensation has been restored through him. We live in Joseph Smith's dispensation today.

Each prophet received the keys appropriate to his time, and each dispensation brought the world closer to the Savior's return.
