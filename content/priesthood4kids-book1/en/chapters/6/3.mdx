### Lesson 6.3 -- Which Keys Were Held When

Not every dispensation prophet held the same priesthood keys. God gave each prophet the keys that were needed for his time and mission. Tracing which keys were held when helps us understand why the Restoration was such a remarkable event.

**Adam** held the fulness of priesthood keys. As the first man, he received everything needed to establish God's plan on earth. Doctrine and Covenants 107:55 teaches that Adam will eventually preside over a great council of all dispensation leaders at Adam-ondi-Ahman.

**Moses** held specific keys that were vital to his mission. He held the **keys of the gathering of Israel** -- the authority to bring God's covenant people together. He used these keys to lead the Israelites out of Egypt and toward their promised land.

**Elijah** held the **sealing keys** -- the power to bind on earth what is bound in heaven. Malachi 4:5-6 prophesied that Elijah would return "before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord" to "turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers." These keys make eternal families possible.

The **apostles in Christ's time** held all the keys necessary to lead the Church. They baptized, confirmed, ordained, and administered the gospel throughout the known world. But when the apostles were killed and rejected, those keys were lost. The **Great Apostasy** settled over the earth, and for centuries no one held priesthood keys.

Then came the **Restoration**. The keys returned piece by piece through heavenly messengers. **John the Baptist** appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery in 1829 and restored the **Aaronic Priesthood** (see D&C 13). Shortly after, **Peter, James, and John** appeared and restored the **Melchizedek Priesthood** with its keys.

In **1836**, at the Kirtland Temple, three more messengers came. **Moses** returned the keys of the **gathering of Israel**. **Elias** committed the **dispensation of the gospel of Abraham**. **Elijah** restored the **sealing keys** (see D&C 110:11-16). Each visitor brought back something specific that had been lost.

This is why the Restoration is not just one event -- it is a series of events where every key from every previous dispensation was returned to the earth. Nothing was left out. Every key is now here.
