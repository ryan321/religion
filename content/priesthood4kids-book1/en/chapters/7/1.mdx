### Lesson 7.1 -- John the Baptist Restores the Aaronic Priesthood

By 1829, Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery had been translating the Book of Mormon together. As they read about baptism in the ancient record, a question came to their minds: Who has the authority to baptize today? They decided to pray and ask God.

On May 15, 1829, near the Susquehanna River in Pennsylvania, something incredible happened. A resurrected being appeared to them in a blaze of light. It was John the Baptist, the same man who had baptized Jesus Christ in the Jordan River nearly two thousand years earlier. He had been sent by the Apostles Peter, James, and John to restore something that had been lost from the earth.

John the Baptist laid his hands on Joseph's head and then on Oliver's head and spoke these words, recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 13: "Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah I confer the Priesthood of Aaron, which holds the keys of the ministering of angels, and of the gospel of repentance, and of baptism by immersion."

Think about what that means. The Aaronic Priesthood had not been on the earth with proper authority for centuries. And now, through a heavenly messenger, God was giving it back. Not through a book. Not through a vote. Through the laying on of hands by someone who already held that authority, a resurrected being sent directly from heaven.

After John the Baptist ordained them, he instructed Joseph and Oliver to baptize each other in the Susquehanna River. Joseph baptized Oliver first, and then Oliver baptized Joseph. They reported feeling the Holy Ghost immediately, and they began to understand the scriptures in ways they never had before.

This event matters because it shows how God restores His authority. He does not just announce it. He sends real messengers with real power, and that authority is passed from person to person by the laying on of hands, the same pattern used since the days of Moses and Aaron (see Numbers 27:18-23). The priesthood is not something people can simply decide to have. It must be given by someone who already holds it, and that chain of authority traces all the way back to heaven.
