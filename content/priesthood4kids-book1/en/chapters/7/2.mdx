### Lesson 7.2 -- Peter, James, and John Restore the Melchizedek Priesthood

The Aaronic Priesthood was a wonderful gift, but it was not enough to do everything God needed done. The Aaronic Priesthood gives authority to baptize, but it does not give authority to confirm people as members of the Church, bestow the gift of the Holy Ghost, ordain others to higher offices, or organize the Church of Jesus Christ. For all of that, a higher priesthood was needed -- the Melchizedek Priesthood.

Shortly after John the Baptist's visit -- sometime in late May or early June of 1829 -- three more heavenly messengers appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery. These were the Apostles Peter, James, and John. These were not just any three Apostles. These were the same three who had been with Jesus at the Transfiguration on the mount, where they saw Moses and Elijah and heard the voice of God the Father (see Matthew 17:1-5). These were the same three whom Jesus took deeper into the Garden of Gethsemane on the night of His suffering (see Matthew 26:36-37). They were the senior leaders of Christ's original Church.

Peter, James, and John laid their hands on Joseph and Oliver and conferred the Melchizedek Priesthood upon them. The Lord Himself confirmed this event in Doctrine and Covenants 27:12-13, saying that He had sent "Peter, and James, and John, whom I have sent unto you, by whom I have ordained you and confirmed you to be apostles, and especial witnesses of my name." He also said they held "the keys of your ministry and of the same things which I revealed unto them."

Now Joseph and Oliver had the full authority of God's priesthood. They could confirm people, bestow the Holy Ghost, ordain men to the priesthood, and organize the Church. Without this event, the Church of Jesus Christ could not have been officially established on April 6, 1830.

Notice the pattern again. God did not ask Joseph to simply start a church. He sent authorized messengers -- the very men who had led Christ's ancient Church -- to pass the authority along by the laying on of hands. The Melchizedek Priesthood connects the restored Church directly back to Jesus Christ and His original Apostles. As Paul taught in Hebrews 5:4, "No man taketh this honour unto himself, but he that is called of God, as was Aaron."
