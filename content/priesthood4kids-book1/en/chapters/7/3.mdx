### Lesson 7.3 -- Keys Restored at the Kirtland Temple

On April 3, 1836, something happened in the Kirtland Temple in Ohio that had never happened before in this dispensation, and it fulfilled ancient prophecy. Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery were praying behind a veil in the temple when they saw a vision of Jesus Christ Himself. The Lord accepted the temple and then sent three more heavenly messengers, one after another, each carrying specific priesthood keys.

This event is recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 110, and it is one of the most important sections in all of scripture.

First came Moses. He committed to Joseph and Oliver "the keys of the gathering of Israel from the four parts of the earth, and the leading of the ten tribes from the land of the north" (D&C 110:11). These are the keys that allow missionaries to go throughout the world and gather God's children into His Church. Every time missionaries knock on a door or teach a lesson, they are acting under the authority of these keys.

Next came Elias. He committed "the dispensation of the gospel of Abraham," saying that in Joseph and his seed "all generations after us should be blessed" (D&C 110:12). This restored the promises and covenants that God made with Abraham, that his descendants would bless all the families of the earth (see Genesis 22:18).

Finally came Elijah. His appearance was especially powerful because the prophet Malachi had foretold it centuries earlier: "Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord: and he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers" (Malachi 4:5-6). Elijah restored the keys of the sealing power (D&C 110:13-16). This is the authority that allows families to be sealed together for eternity in temples. Without Elijah's keys, temple ordinances like baptism for the dead and eternal marriage would not be possible.

These three visitors each brought something essential. Moses brought the power to gather. Elias brought the power of covenant. Elijah brought the power to seal. Together with the Aaronic and Melchizedek Priesthood already restored, the Church now had the authority to carry out God's full plan of salvation.
