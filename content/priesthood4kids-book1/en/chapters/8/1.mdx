### Lesson 8.1 -- Why This Matters Most

Of everything we have studied in this book so far, this chapter may be the most important. We have learned what the priesthood is, where it comes from, how it was restored, and how it is organized. All of that matters. But none of it matters if the priesthood is used the wrong way.

Here is the truth that every priesthood holder needs to understand: having the priesthood means nothing if you use it improperly. A person can hold every office, receive every ordination, and sit in every leadership position, and still have zero actual priesthood power. The power of the priesthood does not come from the title you hold. It comes from who you are.

The Lord taught this lesson in a powerful way. In 1839, the Prophet Joseph Smith was locked in a terrible dungeon called Liberty Jail in Missouri. He and other Church leaders had been imprisoned for months in freezing, miserable conditions for crimes they did not commit. Joseph cried out to the Lord, asking why the Saints were suffering so much. The Lord's answer is recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 121, and it contains the most definitive teaching in all of scripture about the proper and improper use of priesthood authority.

In those dark, cold prison walls, God did not just comfort Joseph. He taught him (and us) exactly how priesthood power works and exactly how it is lost. The Lord explained that many people receive the priesthood but few actually use it the way God intends (D&C 121:34). He explained that the moment a man tries to use his priesthood to control other people, the power leaves. And He explained what righteous priesthood leadership actually looks like.

We are going to spend this entire chapter and the next one studying D&C 121 carefully, because this is where the rubber meets the road. You can know everything about the priesthood and still fail as a priesthood holder if you do not understand these principles. As Jesus taught in Matthew 7:21, "Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that doeth the will of my Father which is in heaven."

The priesthood is not about power over people. It is about power to serve people. And there is a world of difference between the two.
