### Lesson 8.2 -- "Many Are Called, but Few Are Chosen"

The Lord begins His teaching on priesthood power with a sobering warning. In Doctrine and Covenants 121:34, He says: "Behold, there are many called, but few are chosen." Think about that. Many people receive the priesthood. They are called. But only a few actually become what God needs them to be. Only a few are chosen, not because God rejects them, but because they reject God's way of doing things.

Why do so many fail? The Lord answers in the very next verses. "Because their hearts are set so much upon the things of this world, and aspire to the honors of men, that they do not learn this one lesson: that the rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven, and that the powers of heaven cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness" (D&C 121:35-36).

Read that again slowly. The rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven. You cannot separate them. And the powers of heaven only work through righteousness. There is no shortcut. There is no way to cheat the system. A man cannot live selfishly and still expect God's power to flow through him.

The Lord then describes exactly what goes wrong. When a man tries to exercise "control or dominion or compulsion upon the souls of the children of men, in any degree of unrighteousness," the Spirit of the Lord withdraws (D&C 121:37). Notice the phrase "in any degree." Not just big abuses. Any degree. Even small acts of control, manipulation, or pride cause the Spirit to pull back.

This is called unrighteous dominion. Dominion means authority or control. Unrighteous dominion means using authority in a way that is selfish, controlling, or unkind. It is the opposite of what God intends the priesthood to be.

The Lord is teaching us something essential here: the priesthood is not a position of power to be used for your own benefit. It is a sacred trust. And the moment you start using it to get your way, to make yourself feel important, or to push people around, the actual power behind it disappears. You might still have the title, but the heavens are no longer backing you up.

As Mormon warned in Moroni 7:6-7, if a person does the right thing but does it for the wrong reasons, "it is not counted unto him for righteousness." Motives matter to God.
