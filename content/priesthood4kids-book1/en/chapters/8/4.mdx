### Lesson 8.4 -- "Amen to the Priesthood"

The Lord does not leave us guessing about what happens when someone exercises unrighteous dominion. He states the consequence clearly and directly in Doctrine and Covenants 121:37: "The heavens withdraw themselves; the Spirit of the Lord is grieved; and when it is withdrawn, Amen to the priesthood or the authority of that man."

Amen to the priesthood. That is one of the most powerful and sobering phrases in all of scripture. It means it is over. Done. Finished. The priesthood authority of that man is effectively gone.

Now here is something important to understand. God does not take away the ordination itself. The man may still hold the title of elder, or high priest, or bishop. His name may still be on the records. Other people might still call him by his priesthood office. But the actual power, the connection to heaven that makes the priesthood real, is gone. He has authority on paper but zero power in practice.

Think of it like a lamp that is unplugged from the wall. The lamp still looks like a lamp. It still sits on the table. It still has a bulb in it. But it gives no light because it is not connected to the source of power. A priesthood holder who drives away the Spirit through unrighteous dominion is like that unplugged lamp. Everything looks the same on the outside, but nothing works on the inside.

The priesthood only works through righteousness. There is no other way. You cannot force it. You cannot fake it. You cannot hold onto the power while living contrary to God's principles. As Alma taught his son, "Wickedness never was happiness" (Alma 41:10), and wickedness never was priesthood power either.

This is not meant to scare you. It is meant to help you understand how sacred the priesthood really is. God trusts priesthood holders with something precious: His own authority. And He expects that trust to be honored. When it is honored, the priesthood is the most powerful force for good on the earth. When it is abused, it becomes nothing at all.

The good news is that repentance is always available. A man who has lost priesthood power through unrighteous dominion can turn back to God, change his heart, and reconnect to heaven. The Lord is always willing to forgive those who sincerely repent (see D&C 58:42). But the first step is recognizing the problem and choosing to lead the way God commands, with love, not control.
