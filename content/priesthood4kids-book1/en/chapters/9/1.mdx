### Lesson 9.1 -- How the Priesthood Should Be Used

In the last chapter we learned how the priesthood should NOT be used -- through control, compulsion, and pride. Now the Lord teaches us the right way. And it is the exact opposite of everything we just studied.

In Doctrine and Covenants 121:41-42, the Lord gives us a list of principles that describe how priesthood power actually works: "No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned; by kindness, and pure knowledge."

Read that list again. Persuasion. Long-suffering. Gentleness. Meekness. Love unfeigned. Kindness. Pure knowledge. There is not a single word about force. Not a single word about control. Not a single word about demanding respect or obedience. Every single quality on this list is about how you treat other people -- not about the position you hold.

Notice how each quality is the direct opposite of unrighteous dominion. Instead of forcing people, you persuade them. Instead of being impatient and angry, you practice long-suffering. Instead of being harsh and demanding, you are gentle and meek. Instead of pretending to care while actually caring about yourself, you love people genuinely -- "unfeigned" means without faking it. Instead of twisting the truth to get your way, you share pure knowledge -- truth without manipulation.

This is how the Savior Himself led. Jesus never forced anyone to follow Him. He taught, He invited, He served, and He loved. When people chose to walk away, He let them go (see John 6:66-67). When Peter denied Him three times, Jesus did not punish him. He forgave him and gave him more responsibility (see John 21:15-17). That is the pattern for every priesthood holder.

The world often thinks that power means getting people to do what you want. But God's definition of power is completely different. In God's kingdom, the most powerful person in the room is the one who serves the most, loves the most, and gives the most -- not the one who controls the most. As Jesus taught in Matthew 20:26-27, "Whosoever will be great among you, let him be your minister; and whosoever will be chief among you, let him be your servant."
