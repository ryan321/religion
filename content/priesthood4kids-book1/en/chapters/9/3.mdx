### Lesson 9.3 -- Love Unfeigned and Pure Knowledge

The Lord continues His description of righteous priesthood use in Doctrine and Covenants 121:41-42 with two more qualities that go together like two sides of a coin: "love unfeigned" and "pure knowledge." Separately, each one is important. Together, they create the most powerful kind of leadership there is.

**Love unfeigned** means genuine love: love that is real, not fake, not performative, and not conditional. The word "unfeigned" literally means "not pretended." This is love that does not have hidden motives. It is not the kind of love that says "I care about you" but really means "I care about what you can do for me." It is not the kind of love that disappears the moment someone disagrees with you or disappoints you.

Love unfeigned means you care about someone even when it costs you something. You love them when they are difficult. You love them when they fail. You love them even when they are not lovable. This is the kind of love that the Savior showed throughout His ministry. In John 13:1, just before He washed His disciples' feet, the scripture says He "having loved his own which were in the world, he loved them unto the end." His love was not conditional. It did not have an expiration date. It was unfeigned.

**Pure knowledge** means truth without manipulation. It means sharing what is real and honest without twisting it to serve your own purposes. A leader with pure knowledge does not use the scriptures as a weapon to shame people. He does not bend the truth to win an argument. He does not withhold information to keep people under his control. He teaches truth clearly and honestly because he wants people to grow -- not because he wants to look smart or stay in charge.

When you combine real love with real truth, you get the most powerful form of leadership in the universe. That is how the Savior led. He loved people deeply and He told them the truth, even when the truth was hard. He told the rich young ruler to sell everything he had (see Matthew 19:21). That was hard truth. But He said it out of love, not to be cruel. Mark 10:21 says Jesus "beholding him loved him" before giving that difficult counsel.

Love without truth can become permissiveness, allowing people to make harmful choices without warning them. Truth without love can become cruelty, being technically correct but crushing people in the process. The priesthood requires both: genuine love and honest truth, working together. As Paul wrote in Ephesians 4:15, we should be "speaking the truth in love."
