### Lesson 9.4 -- "Let Thy Bowels Be Full of Charity"

The Lord saves the best for last. After teaching how priesthood power is lost and how it should be used, He gives us one of the most beautiful promises in all of scripture. In Doctrine and Covenants 121:45-46, He says:

"Let thy bowels also be full of charity towards all men, and to the household of faith, and let virtue garnish thy thoughts unceasingly; then shall thy confidence wax strong in the presence of God; and the doctrine of the priesthood shall distil upon thy soul as the dews from heaven. The Holy Ghost shall be thy constant companion."

Read that promise carefully. If you live with charity -- genuine, Christlike love for all people -- and if virtue garnishes your thoughts unceasingly -- meaning your mind is filled with pure, clean, good things, then three incredible things happen.

First, your confidence grows strong in the presence of God. You do not have to be afraid or ashamed when you approach Him in prayer. You come before Him with a clear conscience and a clean heart, and you know that He hears you.

Second, the doctrine of the priesthood distils upon your soul as the dews from heaven. It does not crash down like a thunderstorm. It settles gently and quietly, the way morning dew appears on the grass. Priesthood power and understanding flow naturally into the life of a person who lives righteously. You do not have to force it or manufacture it. It just comes.

Third, the Holy Ghost becomes your constant companion. Not an occasional visitor. Not someone who shows up now and then. A constant companion, always with you, guiding you, warning you, comforting you, and empowering you.

This is how the Savior wants every priesthood holder to live. Not grasping for power, not demanding respect, not controlling other people, but living with such charity and virtue that God's power flows through them as naturally as dew forms on a blade of grass at dawn.

Mormon taught that "charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever; and whoso is found possessed of it at the last day, it shall be well with him" (Moroni 7:47). Charity is not just something you do. It is something you become. And when you become a person of charity and virtue, the priesthood works through you the way God always intended: to bless, to heal, to serve, and to lead the way Christ leads: with love.

This is the standard. This is the goal. And with the Savior's help, every priesthood holder can reach it.
