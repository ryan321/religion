# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing The Priesthood for Kids. You now understand something that many adults in the Church don't fully grasp -- what the priesthood is, where it comes from, how it works, and how it should be used.

You learned that the priesthood is the power and authority of God -- eternal, not invented, held by Heavenly Father and delegated to His children since Adam. You traced it through the patriarchs, through Moses and Aaron, through Melchizedek, and through the apostles. You watched it be lost during the Apostasy and restored by heavenly messengers -- John the Baptist, Peter, James, John, Moses, Elias, and Elijah.

You learned the difference between the Aaronic and Melchizedek Priesthoods, what the Levitical Priesthood is, and what every office from deacon to apostle means. You learned what keys are, who holds them, and how they create order in the Church. You learned about dispensations and why we live in the most important one.

Most importantly, you learned how the priesthood should be used -- and how it should not. D&C 121 taught you that unrighteous dominion destroys priesthood power, and that the only way the priesthood works is through persuasion, long-suffering, gentleness, meekness, love unfeigned, kindness, and pure knowledge. If you remember nothing else from this book, remember that.

You learned about the oath and covenant -- the most sweeping promise God makes to any person. You learned how priesthood makes ordinances valid and what blessings can be given. You learned how the Church is organized, from the deacons quorum to the First Presidency.

And you looked ahead -- to the sons of Levi making their offering, to the grand council at Adam-ondi-Ahman, and to the day when all priesthood keys will be returned to Jesus Christ.

The priesthood is not just a topic for a lesson. It is the power by which God operates. And if you are faithful, it is the power by which you will serve Him -- now and forever.

Thank you for learning with us.
