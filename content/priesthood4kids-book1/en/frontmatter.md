## Introduction

Every Sunday, a twelve-year-old boy stands at the sacrament table and passes bread and water to the congregation. It looks simple. But what's actually happening is extraordinary: a young man is exercising the authority of God -- the same authority Adam held, the same authority Moses held, the same authority that was restored by an angel on the banks of the Susquehanna River.

That's the priesthood. And this book is going to teach you everything about it.

You'll start with what the priesthood is -- the actual power and authority of God, delegated to His children. You'll trace it from Heavenly Father through Adam, the patriarchs, and the prophets. You'll learn the difference between the Aaronic and Melchizedek Priesthoods, what keys are, what dispensations are, and how the priesthood was restored in the last days.

Then you'll spend two full chapters on D&C 121 -- the Lord's teaching on how the priesthood should and should not be used. This may be the most important thing you ever learn about the priesthood: that its power comes not from your office or title, but from the kind of person you are. Unrighteous dominion destroys priesthood power. Love, persuasion, and gentleness are how it actually works.

You'll learn the offices, the quorums, the organization, and how keys flow from the prophet through every level of the Church. And you'll look ahead to the future -- when all priesthood keys will be returned to Jesus Christ at Adam-ondi-Ahman.

## How to use this book

15 chapters, one per week, four lessons per chapter, test on the fifth day. Each lesson is about 15-20 minutes. Scripture references throughout -- keep your scriptures handy.

## Note to parents

This book covers the priesthood comprehensively. It is written for all kids -- young men who hold or are preparing to receive the priesthood will find it especially relevant, but young women need to understand the priesthood too. The two chapters on D&C 121 (chapters 8-9) are the heart of the book and deserve special attention.
