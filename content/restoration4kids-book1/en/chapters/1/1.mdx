### Lesson 1.1 -- What Jesus Established

When Jesus Christ was on the earth, He didn't just teach nice ideas and then leave. He built something. He organized a **church**, a real, structured organization with leaders, authority, and order. Understanding what He set up helps you see why the loss of it mattered so much.

First, Jesus called **twelve apostles** (Luke 6:13). These weren't just followers. They were men He specifically chose and gave authority to lead. He trained them, taught them, and gave them the power to act in His name. Think of them like the presidency of His Church, the ones responsible for keeping everything running after He was gone.

Second, Jesus gave His apostles the **priesthood**. The priesthood is God's authority to act in His name. Without it, you can't perform ordinances like baptism or bestow the gift of the Holy Ghost. Jesus didn't want just anyone doing these things however they felt like. There was a right way, done by the right people, with the right authority. He made that clear when He ordained His apostles and sent them out to preach and baptize (Matthew 28:19).

Third, Jesus taught **doctrine**: the truths about God, about the plan of salvation, about repentance, faith, and eternal life. These weren't opinions; they were revealed truths straight from Heavenly Father. Jesus said, "My doctrine is not mine, but his that sent me" (John 7:16). The apostles were responsible for keeping that doctrine pure and passing it on correctly.

Fourth, Jesus established **ordinances**: sacred acts like baptism, the sacrament, and the laying on of hands. These ordinances were done in specific ways for specific reasons. They were part of the covenant path that every person needed to follow.

So when Jesus ascended into heaven after His resurrection, He left behind a complete Church. The apostles kept leading. They called new leaders, performed ordinances, and taught the gospel across the known world (Acts 2:42). But it wasn't easy. From the very beginning, there was **fierce opposition**. The apostles were arrested, beaten, and threatened. Enemies of the Church worked hard to stop the gospel from spreading.

Still, as long as the apostles were alive, the Church had prophets to receive revelation and priesthood keys to keep things in order. The question is: what happens when those apostles are gone? That's what you'll learn about next.
