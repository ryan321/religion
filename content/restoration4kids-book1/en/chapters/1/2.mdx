### Lesson 1.2 -- The Apostles Are Killed

After Jesus ascended into heaven, the apostles got to work. They traveled, preached, baptized, and organized the Church across many lands. But they faced danger every single day. Powerful people (both religious leaders and government officials) wanted to stop them. And one by one, the apostles were killed.

The first apostle to be martyred was **James**, the brother of John. The Bible tells us directly that King Herod "killed James the brother of John with the sword" (Acts 12:2). This happened only about a decade after Jesus's resurrection. James didn't die of old age. He was executed by a king who wanted to please the enemies of the Church.

**Peter**, the senior apostle and the one who held the **keys of the kingdom**, was also killed. According to early Christian tradition, Peter was crucified in Rome. He asked to be crucified upside down because he didn't feel worthy to die in the same way Jesus did. Think about that for a moment. The leader of the entire Church, the man Jesus called "the rock" (Matthew 16:18), was put to death by the Roman government.

**Paul**, who had traveled thousands of miles to preach the gospel and had written so many of the letters we read in the New Testament, was beheaded in Rome. Paul had survived shipwrecks, beatings, imprisonment, and stoning, but eventually the Roman emperor had him executed.

The other apostles met similar fates. They were stoned, stabbed, crucified, or killed in various ways. Within a few decades of Jesus's resurrection, nearly all of the original apostles were dead.

Here is the critical point: when the apostles died, they took their **priesthood keys** with them. Priesthood keys are the authority to direct the work of the Church. Only apostles held these keys, and only apostles could call and ordain new apostles. Once they were gone, there was **no one left on earth** with the authority to lead the Church or to call new apostles. The chain of authority was broken.

It's like a relay race where the runners are taken off the track before they can pass the baton. The race doesn't continue. It stops. And that's exactly what happened to the authority of Christ's Church. The organization Jesus built still had members, but it no longer had authorized leaders. That missing authority would not return for a very long time.
