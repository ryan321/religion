### Lesson 10.1 -- The Dedicatory Prayer

On March 27, 1836, more than a thousand Saints crowded into the newly finished **Kirtland Temple** in Ohio. This was the first temple built in this dispensation, and the day had finally come to dedicate it to the Lord. The Saints had sacrificed so much to build it: their time, their money, and their labor. Now they would see if the Lord would accept what they had offered.

**Joseph Smith** stood before the congregation and offered a **dedicatory prayer**. This was not just any prayer Joseph had written on his own. The Lord had given him the words by revelation, and you can read the entire prayer today in **Doctrine and Covenants section 109**. It is one of the most beautiful prayers ever recorded.

In the prayer, Joseph asked the Lord to **accept the temple** as His house. He asked God to pour out His Spirit on all who would worship there. He prayed that the Saints would be blessed with power from on high and that the temple would be a place of learning, prayer, and revelation. He asked the Lord to protect the Saints and help them in their mission to share the gospel with the world.

The people in the temple that day felt something extraordinary. Many reported feeling the **Spirit** more powerfully than they ever had before. Some said they saw **angels** in the temple. Others heard what sounded like **heavenly choirs** singing along with the congregation. The entire building seemed to be filled with the glory of God.

Think about what that must have been like. You walk into a beautiful new building that you helped build with your own hands. The prophet offers a prayer that came straight from the Lord. And then you feel the Spirit so strongly that it almost takes your breath away. Some people outside the temple even reported seeing a bright light or fire resting on the building that evening.

The Kirtland Temple dedication showed the Saints that their sacrifices had been worth it. The Lord was pleased, and He was ready to pour out blessings on His people. This was not just a building. It was truly the **house of the Lord**.
