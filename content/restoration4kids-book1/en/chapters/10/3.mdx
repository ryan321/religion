### Lesson 10.3 -- The Savior Appears

One week after the Kirtland Temple was dedicated, something happened that changed everything. It was April 3, 1836 -- **Easter Sunday**. Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery were in the temple. They knelt behind a curtain (called a **veil**) and prayed quietly together.

As they prayed, a vision opened. **Jesus Christ Himself** appeared to them. He stood before them in glory. His eyes were like a flame of fire. His hair was white like pure snow. His face shone brighter than the sun. His voice was like the sound of rushing waters.

The Savior spoke to Joseph and Oliver and told them something they had been waiting to hear. He said: **"I have accepted this house, and my name shall be here"** (Doctrine and Covenants 110:7). The Lord was pleased with what the Saints had built. The Kirtland Temple was officially His house.

Think about how powerful that moment was. The God of Israel -- the same being who led Moses through the Red Sea, who spoke to prophets throughout the ages -- was standing in a building in Kirtland, Ohio. He had a **house on earth** again. After centuries without a temple, without prophets, and without priesthood authority, the Lord once more had a place where He could come and dwell among His people.

Jesus also told Joseph and Oliver to rejoice because He had **forgiven the sins** of His servants. He promised that the fame of this house would spread to foreign lands. It would be the beginning of blessings that would be poured out on the heads of His people (D&C 110:9-10).

This appearance of the Savior in the Kirtland Temple is one of the most important events in all of Church history. It confirmed that the Restoration was true. It confirmed that Joseph Smith was the Lord's prophet. And it confirmed that temples are sacred places where God Himself can come.

The Savior's visit was not the end of what happened that day, though. After He departed, three more heavenly messengers came -- and each one brought something important. You will learn about them in the next lesson.
