### Lesson 10.4 -- Moses, Elias, and Elijah

After the Savior departed from the Kirtland Temple on April 3, 1836, Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery were not left alone for long. Three more **heavenly messengers** appeared one after another, and each brought something specific and important. These visits are recorded in **Doctrine and Covenants 110:11-16**.

The first to appear was **Moses**. Yes, the same Moses who parted the Red Sea and led Israel out of Egypt. Moses came to give Joseph and Oliver the **keys of the gathering of Israel**. This meant the Church now had the authority to gather God's people from all over the earth. Every missionary who has ever served, every person who has been baptized from every nation -- that work traces back to the keys Moses restored in Kirtland.

Next came **Elias**. He brought what the scriptures call the **dispensation of the gospel of Abraham**. This included the promises and covenants God made to Abraham -- that his descendants would bless all the families of the earth and that the gospel would go to every nation. These blessings now belonged to the restored Church.

Finally, **Elijah** appeared. His mission was perhaps the most personal of all. Elijah brought the **sealing power** -- the priesthood authority to **bind families together for eternity**. Because of the keys Elijah restored, families can be sealed in temples so that their relationships last beyond death. A husband and wife, parents and children -- these bonds do not have to end when someone passes away.

Elijah's coming had been prophesied long before. The Old Testament prophet **Malachi** wrote: "Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord: And he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers" (Malachi 4:5-6). That prophecy was **fulfilled** in the Kirtland Temple.

This is why Latter-day Saints care so much about family history and temple work. The sealing power makes it possible for families to be together forever. Every temple sealing performed anywhere in the world is possible because Elijah came to Kirtland and gave those keys to Joseph Smith.

April 3, 1836, was one of the most important days in the history of the Restoration. The Savior accepted His temple, and three ancient prophets restored keys that the Church still uses today.
