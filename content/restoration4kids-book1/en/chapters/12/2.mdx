### Lesson 12.2 -- The Nauvoo Temple

Almost as soon as the Saints settled in Nauvoo, the Lord gave them another commandment: build a **temple**. This command is recorded in **Doctrine and Covenants 124:27-28**. The Lord made it very clear: if the Saints did not build the temple, they would be **rejected** as a people. This was not optional.

The **Nauvoo Temple** was going to be different from the Kirtland Temple. The Kirtland Temple had been a place for teaching, worship, and receiving priesthood keys. The Nauvoo Temple was designed for something more. The Lord wanted to introduce sacred **ordinances** that could only be performed in a temple.

First, there would be **baptism for the dead**. This is the practice of being baptized on behalf of someone who has already passed away, so that person can accept the gospel in the spirit world. The Saints even built a temporary **baptismal font** before the temple was finished so they could begin this work right away.

Second, the temple would be the place for the **endowment**, a sacred ceremony where members make covenants with God and receive instruction about His plan for them. The endowment teaches about the purpose of life, the creation, and the path back to Heavenly Father.

Third, **sealings** would be performed in the temple. These are the ordinances that bind husbands and wives, and parents and children, together for eternity, made possible by the sealing keys Elijah restored in Kirtland.

The Saints worked **constantly** on the Nauvoo Temple. They quarried limestone from a nearby bluff. They hauled timber. They donated their time and labor even while building their own homes and trying to feed their families. The temple was the **priority**. Everything else came second.

The Nauvoo Temple rose above the city on a bluff overlooking the Mississippi River. It was the most impressive building in the region. Its construction showed the world that the Saints were serious about their covenants with God. Even in the face of poverty and past persecution, they would build the Lord's house because He had asked them to.
