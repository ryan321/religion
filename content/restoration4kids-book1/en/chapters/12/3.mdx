### Lesson 12.3 -- The Relief Society

On **March 17, 1842**, Joseph Smith did something that he said was essential to the Church being fully organized. He established the **Female Relief Society of Nauvoo** -- the first official organization for women in the Church.

**Emma Smith**, Joseph's wife, was chosen as the first **president** of the Relief Society. She selected two counselors, and the organization began with about twenty women. Joseph told them that the Church had never been perfectly organized until the women were organized too. This was a powerful statement. It meant that the work of the Lord required women to be organized, leading, and serving right alongside the men.

The Relief Society had a clear purpose from the beginning. The women would practice **charity** -- the pure love of Christ. They would look after the poor and the sick. They would serve those in need. They would strengthen each other spiritually. Joseph told the women that they should be a **"select society, separate from all the evils of the world"** -- a group dedicated to doing good.

And that is exactly what they did. The women of the Relief Society visited families, brought food to the hungry, cared for the sick, sewed clothing for those who had none, and supported one another through the difficulties of frontier life. They were organized, active, and essential to the well-being of the entire Nauvoo community.

The Relief Society was not just a sewing circle or a social club. It was a **divinely inspired organization** with real authority and real responsibility. The women led their own meetings, collected donations, managed their own funds, and made decisions about how to serve.

Today, the Relief Society continues as one of the **largest women's organizations in the world**. Millions of women across the globe participate. The mission has not changed -- it is still about charity, service, and spiritual growth. Every time a Relief Society member takes a meal to a neighbor, visits someone in the hospital, or comforts a friend, she is continuing what Emma Smith and those first twenty women started in Nauvoo.

The founding of the Relief Society reminds us that the Lord's Church needs everyone -- men and women -- working together to build His kingdom.
