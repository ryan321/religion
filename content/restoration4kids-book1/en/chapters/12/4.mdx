### Lesson 12.4 -- Growth and Gathering

Nauvoo was growing fast, and a big reason for that growth was the success of **missionary work**. In 1837 and again in 1840, the Church sent missionaries to **England**. These missions were incredibly successful. Thousands of people in the British Isles joined the Church and then sailed across the Atlantic Ocean to gather with the Saints in Nauvoo.

The **Book of Mormon** was translated and published in **multiple languages** during this period. The gospel was no longer just for people in the United States. It was going to the world, just as Moses's restored keys of the gathering of Israel had made possible.

During the Nauvoo years, Joseph Smith also taught the Saints many important doctrines in greater detail than ever before. He taught them about the **temple endowment**, the sacred covenants and instruction received in the temple. He taught about the **sealing of families** for eternity. He explained the **plan of salvation**: where we came from before this life, why we are here on earth, and where we go after we die. These teachings gave the Saints a deeper understanding of God's plan for His children.

Nauvoo became a center of **learning, faith, and covenant-making**. The Saints studied the scriptures, attended meetings, worked on the temple, and prepared themselves spiritually. It was a time of tremendous growth, not just in numbers, but in understanding.

But even as the Church flourished, **opposition was building again**. People outside the Church grew nervous about how large and powerful Nauvoo was becoming. Some politicians saw the Saints as a political threat. Newspapers published attacks against Joseph Smith and the Church.

Even more troubling, some opposition came **from within**. A few Church members turned against Joseph, disagreeing with certain teachings and practices. They started their own newspaper to criticize him. The tension was rising, and dark days were ahead once again.

Still, what the Saints accomplished in Nauvoo was remarkable. In just a few years, they built a thriving city, sent missionaries around the world, received sacred temple ordinances, and deepened their understanding of the gospel. Nauvoo the Beautiful lived up to its name, even if the beauty would not last forever.
