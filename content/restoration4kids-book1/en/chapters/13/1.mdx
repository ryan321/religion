### Lesson 13.1 -- Who Joseph Was

You might picture Joseph Smith as a serious man standing at a pulpit. But the real Joseph was much more than that. He was warm, energetic, and full of life. He loved people, and people loved being around him. He played with children, wrestled with friends, and laughed easily. He wasn't stiff or distant -- he was the kind of person who would sit down next to you and make you feel like you mattered.

And he never claimed to be perfect. Joseph made mistakes throughout his life, and he was honest about that. He learned, he grew, and he relied on the Lord to guide him. He once said he never told anyone he was perfect -- only that the things God had revealed through him were true. That honesty is part of what makes his story so remarkable. He wasn't chosen because he had it all figured out. He was chosen because the Lord could work through him.

Think about where Joseph started. He was a **farm boy** from a poor family with very **limited formal education**. He didn't attend fancy schools. He didn't come from wealth or power. By the world's standards, he had nothing going for him. But by **God's power**, he accomplished things that no amount of education or money could explain.

Through the gift and power of God, Joseph **translated an ancient record** -- the Book of Mormon -- from golden plates written in a language no living person could read. He received **hundreds of revelations** now recorded in the Doctrine and Covenants. He built cities, including Nauvoo, which at its peak rivaled Chicago in size. He **organized the Church** of Jesus Christ in the latter days. He established **temples** where the most sacred ordinances could be performed.

No one in modern history did more to reveal the **nature of God** and the **plan of salvation** to the world. Joseph taught that God the Father and Jesus Christ are separate beings with glorified bodies. He taught that families can be together forever. He taught that every person who has ever lived will have the chance to accept the gospel. These weren't small ideas -- they changed everything.

All of this came through a young man with no advantages except one: the Lord was with him. That is the story of Joseph Smith -- not a perfect man, but a faithful one, called by God to do something no one else could do.
