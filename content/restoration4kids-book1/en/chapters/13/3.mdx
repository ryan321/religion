### Lesson 13.3 -- His Testimony

Joseph Smith endured **persecution** from the very beginning. From the moment he told people about the **First Vision**, he was mocked, threatened, attacked, and hunted. For over twenty years, enemies tried to silence him. They dragged him from his home, tarred and feathered him, threw him in jail, and took him to court again and again. His family suffered. His friends suffered. His life was in constant danger.

And through all of it, Joseph **never denied what he had seen**.

He described it this way: "I had seen a vision; I knew it, and I knew that God knew it, and I could not deny it, neither dared I do so" (Joseph Smith--History 1:25). Those words tell you everything about the kind of man Joseph was. He didn't waver. He didn't back down. He didn't change his story when it would have been easier to just say he made it all up.

Think about what he could have done. At any point, Joseph could have said, "I was wrong. There was no vision. Leave me alone." And the persecution would have stopped. He could have gone back to farming, lived a quiet life, and avoided all the suffering. But he never did that, not once, because he knew what he had experienced was real.

Joseph **testified of the Father and the Son**, who appeared to him in the Sacred Grove. He testified of **angels** (Moroni, John the Baptist, Peter, James, John, Moses, Elias, and Elijah) who came to restore authority and keys. He testified of the **Book of Mormon** as a true record translated by the gift and power of God. He testified of the **priesthood** and of every revelation the Lord gave him.

And in the end, Joseph **sealed his testimony with his life**. In the scriptures, when someone dies for their testimony, it carries a special weight (Hebrews 9:16-17). Joseph didn't die in battle or by accident. He was killed because of what he taught and what he testified was true. He gave everything, including his life, rather than deny the work God had given him.

That is the mark of a true witness. Not someone who backed down when things got hard, but someone who held firm to the very end. Joseph's testimony stands as one of the most powerful witnesses in all of scripture. He saw what he saw, he said what he said, and he never took it back.
