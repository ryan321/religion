### Lesson 13.4 -- Preparing the Church

In the final months of his life, Joseph Smith seemed to sense that his time was limited. He didn't know exactly when the end would come, but he knew the work had to be prepared to go forward without him. And so he did something critical -- he made sure the **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles** had everything they needed to lead the Church after he was gone.

Joseph had received many **priesthood keys** over the years -- keys of the gathering of Israel, keys of the sealing power, keys of the dispensation of the fulness of times. These keys gave authority to direct specific parts of the Lord's work. Without them, the Church could not function the way the Lord intended.

In early 1844, Joseph gathered the Twelve together and **conferred all of these keys** upon them. He didn't hold anything back. Every key, every authority, every power that he held as the prophet -- he passed it all to the apostles. He told them plainly what he was doing and why.

Joseph said something remarkable during this time. He told the Twelve: "I have lived until I have seen this burden, which has rested on my shoulders, **rolled on to the shoulders of other men**." He went on to say that the "**keys of the kingdom** are planted on the earth to be taken away no more forever." Think about what that means. Joseph was saying that even if something happened to him, the keys would remain. The Restoration would not die with the prophet.

This was one of the most important things Joseph ever did. If he had been killed without passing on those keys, the Church would have had no authorized leadership. The priesthood keys could have been lost again, just as they were lost in the **Great Apostasy**. But because Joseph was inspired to prepare the Twelve, the line of authority remained **unbroken**.

The Twelve didn't fully understand at the time why Joseph was being so urgent about this. But within a few months, they would understand completely. Joseph was preparing the Church to survive the most devastating blow it had ever faced -- the death of its founding prophet. And because he was faithful in this final preparation, the work of the Lord would go on.
