### Lesson 14.3 -- The Deaths of Joseph and Hyrum

As the mob fired through the door of the upstairs room, **Hyrum Smith** was the first to fall. A bullet struck him in the face, and he collapsed to the floor. His final words were, "I am a dead man." Joseph rushed to his brother's side. Hyrum was gone. He was 44 years old.

The mob continued to fire into the room. **John Taylor** tried to escape through the window but was struck by a bullet that hit a watch in his vest pocket, knocking him back inside. He was shot multiple times -- at least four bullets hit him -- but he survived. He crawled under the bed, seriously wounded but alive. **Willard Richards**, remarkably, was barely touched. Only a small bullet grazed his ear. He would later say it was a fulfillment of a blessing Joseph had given him.

With Hyrum dead, bullets flying, and the mob pressing into the room, **Joseph moved toward the window**. As he reached it, he was struck by bullets fired from both inside and outside the building. He fell from the **second-story window** to the ground below.

Joseph Smith, the Prophet of the Restoration, was dead. He was 38 years old.

The scriptures record: "In life they were not divided, and in death they were not separated" (Doctrine and Covenants 135:3). Joseph and Hyrum had served side by side through years of hardship, persecution, and faithful labor. Now they were taken together.

Joseph and Hyrum did not die as criminals. They were **martyrs** -- men who gave their lives rather than deny the truth God had revealed through them. The word "martyr" means witness, and their deaths stand as a final, powerful witness of the gospel they taught. They sealed their testimony with their own blood.

Doctrine and Covenants 135:3 says of Joseph: "He lived great, and he died great in the eyes of God and his people." He had done everything the Lord asked of him. He had been faithful to the end.

This is one of the most sacred and solemn events in the history of the Church. Two brothers, two servants of God, gave everything they had -- including their lives -- for the Restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ.
