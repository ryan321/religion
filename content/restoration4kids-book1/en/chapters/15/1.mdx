### Lesson 15.1 -- Who Will Lead?

With Joseph Smith gone, the most urgent question facing the Saints was simple but critical: **Who will lead the Church?**

Several people stepped forward with claims. **Sidney Rigdon**, who had served as a counselor in the First Presidency, came to Nauvoo and told the Saints he should be appointed as a "**guardian**" over the Church. He argued that as a member of the First Presidency, he had the right to step in and guide things until a new prophet could be chosen. Others also made claims to leadership, each with their own reasoning.

This was a **dangerous moment**. If the Saints made the wrong decision -- if they followed someone who did not hold the proper authority -- the Church could splinter permanently. Different groups could go different directions, each claiming to be the true Church. It had happened before in history. It could happen again.

But Joseph had already given the answer. In the months before his death, he had **conferred all priesthood keys** on the **Quorum of the Twelve Apostles**. He had told them plainly that the keys of the kingdom had been placed on their shoulders. The authority to lead was not with Sidney Rigdon or anyone else who made a claim. It was with the Twelve.

The **senior apostle** -- the one who had served longest in the quorum -- was **Brigham Young**. He was a bold, practical, and deeply faithful man. He had been with Joseph through years of hardship. He had served missions, organized the Saints, and been trusted by the prophet with the most important responsibilities.

When Brigham arrived back in Nauvoo in early August 1844, he quickly assessed the situation. He listened to Sidney Rigdon's claims and then called for a meeting of the entire Church membership so the Saints could hear from both sides and make their choice. Brigham was confident. He knew where the keys were, and he knew the Saints would recognize the truth when they heard it.

The meeting was set for **August 8, 1844**. What happened that day would settle the question once and for all.
