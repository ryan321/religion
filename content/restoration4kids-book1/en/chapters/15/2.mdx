### Lesson 15.2 -- The Mantle of the Prophet

On **August 8, 1844**, the Saints gathered in Nauvoo for one of the most important meetings in the history of the Church. The question before them was clear: Who held the authority to lead?

Sidney Rigdon spoke first. He stood before the congregation and made his case. He talked for about an hour and a half, arguing that he should serve as guardian of the Church. But his words did not carry the Spirit the Saints were hoping to feel. Many who listened felt uneasy. Something was missing.

Then **Brigham Young** stood to speak.

What happened next has been described by dozens of witnesses, and their accounts are remarkably consistent. As Brigham began to address the Saints, many in the congregation experienced something extraordinary. They testified that his **voice changed** -- it sounded like Joseph Smith's voice. His **manner and appearance** seemed to shift. Some said he even **looked like Joseph**. It was as if the prophet himself were standing before them one more time.

This experience became known as the "**mantle of the prophet**" falling upon Brigham Young. It was not something Brigham planned or manufactured. It was a witness from the Lord, given directly to the Saints so they could know where the authority rested. Many people recorded this experience in their journals and letters. They had no doubt about what they had seen and heard.

Brigham did not claim to be a prophet that day. He simply told the Saints the truth -- that Joseph had conferred all the keys on the Twelve, and that the Quorum of the Twelve held the authority to lead the Church. He asked the Saints to sustain the **Twelve Apostles** as the presiding body of the Church, and the vote was **nearly unanimous**.

The crisis was over. The Church would not be splintered. The Saints had recognized where the Lord's authority rested, and they followed it. Brigham Young was sustained as the **president of the Quorum of the Twelve**, and from that position he would lead the Saints through some of the most difficult years they had ever faced.

The mantle experience was a tender mercy from the Lord. At a moment when the Saints could have been lost in confusion, He gave them a clear and unmistakable sign.
