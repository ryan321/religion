### Lesson 15.3 -- Finishing the Nauvoo Temple

Even before Joseph's death, the Saints had been working to complete the **Nauvoo Temple** on the bluff overlooking the Mississippi River. It was the most ambitious building project the Church had ever undertaken -- far larger and grander than the Kirtland Temple. And now, with persecution intensifying and the Saints facing the very real possibility that they would have to leave Nauvoo, the question arose: Should they keep building?

**Brigham Young's** answer was clear and firm. **Yes**. They would finish the temple. No matter what was coming, the Saints needed the blessings that could only be received inside the house of the Lord.

This decision took remarkable faith. Enemies of the Church were burning outlying settlements. Mobs were threatening violence. It was becoming obvious that the Saints could not stay in Nauvoo much longer. Every practical instinct said to stop building and start packing. But Brigham knew that the **temple ordinances** -- the endowment, the sealings, baptisms for the dead -- were more important than anything else the Saints could do to prepare for the journey ahead.

The Saints worked **day and night** to finish the temple. They pushed through exhaustion, fear, and the bitter cold of an Illinois winter. In late **1845**, even before the building was fully complete, Brigham authorized the temple to be opened for ordinances. The need was too urgent to wait.

**Thousands of Saints** received their **endowments** and **sealings** in those final weeks and months in Nauvoo. They came in large numbers, and the temple operated nearly around the clock to accommodate as many people as possible. Brigham himself spent long days and nights in the temple, officiating and making sure every Saint who could come had the chance to receive these sacred blessings.

Why did this matter so much? Because the covenants the Saints made in the temple would **strengthen them** for everything that lay ahead. The trek across the plains, the suffering, the sacrifice -- all of it would be easier to bear because they had made promises to God and received His promises in return. The temple gave them spiritual power that nothing else could provide.

The Saints were about to leave almost everything they owned behind. But they would carry their covenants with them.
