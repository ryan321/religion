### Lesson 15.4 -- Looking West

By early **1846**, it was clear that the Saints could no longer stay in Nauvoo. The persecution had become too severe. Mobs were attacking outlying communities, burning homes, and threatening worse. The state of Illinois offered no protection. The Saints had to go.

But where? **Brigham Young** had been studying maps and reports from explorers and mountain men. He had a destination in mind -- the **Great Basin**, a vast and remote valley in the Rocky Mountains, far beyond the reach of the mobs and governments that had driven the Saints from place to place. It was so isolated that, as Brigham reasoned, no one else would want it. No one would drive them out again.

Brigham **organized the exodus** with the same practical, determined leadership that would define his entire ministry. He divided the Saints into companies. He assigned leaders. He made plans for supplies, wagons, livestock, and routes. This would not be a panicked flight -- it would be an organized migration of an entire people.

The first companies of Saints crossed the frozen **Mississippi River** in **February 1846**. It was bitterly cold. Families loaded what they could into wagons and left behind their homes, their farms, their businesses, and the beautiful temple they had just finished. Ahead of them lay more than **1,000 miles** of prairies, rivers, and mountains.

The journey would take months. It would test the Saints in every way imaginable -- physically, emotionally, and spiritually. There would be hunger, sickness, death, and heartbreak along the way. But the Saints pressed forward because they believed the Lord was leading them.

Brigham Young would eventually be sustained as the **second president** of the Church. Under his leadership, the Saints would settle the Salt Lake Valley, build cities across the West, and establish the Church on a foundation that endures to this day.

The Restoration had **survived the death** of its founding prophet. It would survive the loss of Nauvoo. It would survive the trek west. The work of the Lord, begun in a grove of trees by a fourteen-year-old boy, would roll forward. No unhallowed hand could stop it. The kingdom of God was here to stay.
