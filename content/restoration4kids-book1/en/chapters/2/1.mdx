### Lesson 2.1 -- The Dark Ages

After the Great Apostasy, the world entered a long period that historians often call the **Dark Ages** or the **Middle Ages**. This stretch lasted for roughly a thousand years, from about AD 500 to AD 1500. During this time, the gospel of Jesus Christ as He taught it was simply not on the earth. There were no prophets, no priesthood authority, and no revelation guiding the world.

The **Catholic Church** became the dominant religious power across Europe. It had enormous influence over religion, governments, education, and daily life. The pope in Rome was considered the highest authority on earth in matters of faith. Kings and queens often answered to him. If you lived in Europe during this time, there was basically one church, and you belonged to it.

Church services were conducted in **Latin**, a language that most ordinary people didn't speak or understand. Imagine sitting through a church meeting every week and not understanding a single word. You would just have to trust that the leaders were teaching you the right things. Most people couldn't check for themselves because they couldn't read, and even if they could, Bibles were extremely rare and expensive. Each copy had to be written out by hand, which took months or even years.

Because people had so little access to the scriptures, they depended entirely on priests and bishops to tell them what God wanted. Some of these leaders were sincere and good. Others took advantage of the situation. Some churches taught that you could **buy forgiveness** for your sins by paying money, a practice called **indulgences**. That's a long way from what Jesus taught about repentance.

If anyone questioned the teachings of the Church or tried to interpret the Bible differently, they faced serious **persecution**. People were imprisoned, tortured, or even killed for disagreeing with official Church doctrine. Free thinking about religion was dangerous.

Through all of this, there was **no revelation** flowing from heaven to earth, at least not to any prophet leading a church. God had not abandoned His children, but the time for the Restoration had not yet come. The world wasn't ready. People didn't have the freedom, the knowledge, or the tools they would need. But God was about to start changing that, one step at a time.
