### Lesson 3.1 -- The Smith Family

For over a thousand years, the world waited. The Apostasy had stripped away the gospel. The Reformation, the printing press, and the founding of America had prepared the way. Now, in a quiet corner of New England, God placed the family that would change everything.

**Joseph Smith Jr.** was born on **December 23, 1805**, in Sharon, Vermont. His parents were **Joseph Smith Sr.** and **Lucy Mack Smith**. They were a large family. Joseph Jr. had several brothers and sisters, and they were close. They worked together, looked out for each other, and stuck together through hard times.

The Smiths were a **hardworking** family. Joseph Sr. was a farmer and sometimes a merchant, but nothing ever seemed to go quite right financially. Bad business deals, crop failures, and tough luck kept the family struggling. They weren't poor because they were lazy; they worked incredibly hard. Life in early America was simply difficult for many families, and the Smiths had more than their share of setbacks.

They were also a **religious** family. Both Joseph Sr. and Lucy cared deeply about God and about raising their children to be faithful. Lucy leaned toward the **Presbyterian** faith and attended meetings. Joseph Sr. was more cautious about organized churches, but he had spiritual experiences of his own, including powerful dreams that he felt came from God. The children grew up in a home where God was real and faith mattered.

Around **1816**, the family moved to the area near **Palmyra, New York**. They were looking for better opportunities and a fresh start. The area was part of western New York, a region that was growing fast as settlers moved in from New England. The Smiths worked the land, tapped maple trees for sugar, and did whatever they could to get by.

Joseph Jr. was a normal boy in many ways. He worked alongside his father and brothers in the fields. He didn't have much formal education because schooling was limited in rural areas, and families needed their children to work. But Joseph was thoughtful, curious about religious matters, and deeply affected by what was happening around him.

This ordinary family in this ordinary place was exactly where God wanted them. You don't have to be wealthy, famous, or highly educated to be part of God's plan. The Smiths proved that.
