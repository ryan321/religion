### Lesson 3.4 -- A Question Forms

All the confusion, all the competing preachers, all the arguments -- they pushed Joseph Smith to a point where he had to make a decision. Not a decision about which church to join. A decision about how to find out which church was right.

Joseph was **fourteen years old**. He had no special education. He hadn't studied theology or philosophy. He was a **farm boy** from a struggling family in rural New York. By the world's standards, he was nobody important. But he had something that mattered more than degrees or money -- he had a **sincere question**, and he was willing to take it to God.

The question was simple and clear: **Which of all the churches is right, and which should I join?** Joseph didn't want someone else's opinion anymore. He had heard enough opinions. He wanted an answer from God Himself.

What happened next shows how God works. Joseph was reading the Bible one day when he came across a verse in the book of James: "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him" (James 1:5). That verse hit him with incredible force. He later said it entered "with great power into every feeling of my heart" (Joseph Smith--History 1:12).

Think about what that verse says. If you don't know something, ask God. He'll give you the answer freely. He won't scold you for asking. That was exactly what Joseph needed to hear. He didn't need another preacher. He didn't need a debate. He needed to **pray**.

Joseph decided he would do exactly what the Bible said. He would go somewhere private and ask God his question directly. It sounds simple, but it was extraordinary. A fourteen-year-old boy with no authority, no training, and no standing in any church was about to approach the God of the universe with a personal question.

He didn't know what would happen. He didn't expect to see a vision or receive a dramatic revelation. He just wanted an answer. But what was about to happen in a quiet grove of trees near his home would become the **most important event since the Resurrection of Jesus Christ**. It would launch the Restoration of the gospel, bring back the priesthood, open the heavens, and change the course of history forever.

It all started with a boy and a question.
