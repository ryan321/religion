### Lesson 4.1 -- James 1:5

Have you ever had a question that really mattered to you? Maybe you wondered about something so much that you just had to find the answer. That is exactly what happened to a young man named **Joseph Smith** when he was fourteen years old.

Joseph grew up in the early 1800s in a part of New York State where **religious revivals** were happening all around him. Preachers from different churches held big outdoor meetings, and each one said their church was the right one. Joseph's own family was divided. Some leaned toward one church, others toward a different one. It was confusing. Joseph wanted to join a church, but he did not know which one was true.

During this time of confusion, Joseph was reading the **Bible**, specifically the book of **James** in the New Testament. He came across a verse that changed his life forever. **James 1:5** says, "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him."

Think about what that verse is saying. If you do not know something, you can **ask God**, and He will answer you. He will not be upset with you for asking. He gives generously.

Joseph later wrote about this moment. He said, **"Never did any passage of scripture come with more power to the heart of man than this did at this time to mine"** (Joseph Smith--History 1:12). The words hit him with incredible force. He read it again and again. The answer seemed so simple: he should just ask God directly which church to join.

This is an important lesson for all of us. God wants us to **pray** and ask Him questions. He is not too busy for us. He does not think our questions are silly. James 1:5 is an invitation that is open to everyone, including you.

Joseph made a decision. He would find a quiet place, kneel down, and **pray out loud** for the first time in his life to ask God which church he should join. He did not know it yet, but that simple decision to take God at His word and ask was about to lead to one of the most important events in the history of the world.

It all started with a boy, a Bible, and one powerful verse.
