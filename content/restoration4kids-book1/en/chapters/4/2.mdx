### Lesson 4.2 -- The Sacred Grove

It was a beautiful morning in the **spring of 1820**. Joseph Smith, just fourteen years old, walked away from his family's farm and into a **grove of trees** nearby. He had made up his mind. He was going to pray and ask God which church to join.

This grove of trees is now called the **Sacred Grove**. It was a quiet, peaceful spot, the kind of place where a boy could be alone with his thoughts and with God. Joseph found a spot, looked around to make sure he was by himself, and then **knelt down** to pray.

But something unexpected happened. The moment Joseph began to pray, a terrible **darkness** came over him. It was not like a cloud blocking the sun. This was a real, powerful force that pressed down on him. He felt as if some dark power was trying to **destroy him**. His tongue felt stuck. He could not speak a single word.

Joseph was afraid, but he did not give up. He did not run away. Instead, he **called upon God with all his strength**, begging to be delivered from this awful power that held him. He used every bit of energy he had to keep praying.

And then, right at the moment when Joseph thought he might be completely overcome, something amazing happened. He saw a **pillar of light** appear above his head. It was brighter than the sun. The light slowly came down toward him until it rested upon him and the trees around him. As soon as the light appeared, the darkness was gone. Joseph was free.

Joseph described this light as being beyond anything he had ever seen. It filled the grove and seemed to come from heaven itself (Joseph Smith--History 1:16-17). As the light settled around him, Joseph saw something in the light that would change everything.

What happened next is one of the most sacred events in all of history. But notice something important about this experience. Joseph had to **push through difficulty** before the blessing came. He faced real opposition. He could have quit. He could have stood up and walked home. Instead, he kept praying. That took real **faith** and real **courage**.

Sometimes when we pray, answers do not come right away. Sometimes it feels hard. Joseph's experience in the Sacred Grove teaches us to **keep trying** and trust that God will answer.
