### Lesson 4.3 -- The Father and the Son

When the pillar of light rested on Joseph Smith in the Sacred Grove, he looked up and saw something no one else on earth had seen in a very long time. Standing above him in the light were **two personages**. They were glorious beyond description. Their brightness and glory were beyond anything Joseph could put into words.

One of them **called Joseph by name** and then pointed to the other, saying, **"This is My Beloved Son. Hear Him!"** (Joseph Smith--History 1:17).

Think about what just happened. **God the Father** spoke to Joseph and introduced His Son, **Jesus Christ**. The two of them -- two separate, real, glorified beings -- appeared to a fourteen-year-old boy in a grove of trees in upstate New York. This is called the **First Vision**.

Joseph now knew something incredibly important. God the Father and Jesus Christ are **two separate beings**. They have bodies. They can speak. They know your name. Many churches at that time taught that God was invisible, unknowable, and without a body. Joseph now knew that was not true. He had seen Them with his own eyes and heard Them with his own ears.

Joseph remembered why he had come to the grove in the first place. He asked the Savior which of all the churches he should join. The answer was clear -- he was told to **join none of them**. None of the churches on the earth at that time had the **fulness of the gospel**. They had some truth, but important things were missing. The complete gospel of Jesus Christ would be **restored** -- brought back to the earth in its fulness.

This was an enormous answer. God did not just tell Joseph which church door to walk through. He told Joseph that the true Church was not yet on the earth, but it would be. And Joseph would have a role to play in bringing it back.

The **First Vision** answers some of the biggest questions people have ever asked. Is God real? Yes. Does He hear prayers? Yes. Does He answer them? Yes. Does He know who you are? Yes -- He even knows your name. The First Vision is the foundation of the **Restoration of the gospel**. Everything that came after -- the Book of Mormon, the priesthood, the Church -- traces back to this sacred moment in the Sacred Grove.
