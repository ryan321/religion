### Lesson 5.3 -- Four Years of Preparation

From **1823 to 1827**, Joseph Smith returned to the Hill Cumorah every September to meet with the angel **Moroni**. Each year, Moroni taught him more about the work he would eventually do. These were years of **learning and growing** -- not just waiting around.

Think about how long four years is. For Joseph, it was roughly the time from age seventeen to age twenty-one. A lot happened in his life during those years, and not all of it was easy.

One of the hardest moments came when Joseph's **oldest brother, Alvin**, died unexpectedly in **November 1823** -- just two months after Joseph's first visit to the hill. Alvin had been excited about the gold plates and had encouraged Joseph to do everything the angel said. Losing Alvin was a heavy blow to the entire Smith family. Alvin was the oldest son and a leader in the household.

During these years, Joseph also had to **work hard** to help support his family. The Smiths were not wealthy. Joseph did farmwork and other jobs to earn money. He traveled to different areas looking for work, which is how he ended up in **Harmony, Pennsylvania**, where he met a young woman named **Emma Hale**.

Joseph and Emma fell in love, but Emma's father was not thrilled about Joseph. He thought Joseph was too poor and was suspicious of his talk about visions and gold plates. Despite her father's objections, Joseph and Emma were **married on January 18, 1827**.

All through these years, Joseph kept his annual visits to the hill. Each time, Moroni gave him **more instruction**. Joseph was being prepared -- spiritually, mentally, and emotionally -- for the enormous task ahead of him. He was learning to be obedient, to be patient, and to trust God's plan even when life was hard.

These four years teach us something valuable. God does not always give us everything at once. Sometimes He asks us to **prepare** first. He uses time, experiences, and even hardships to shape us into the people He needs us to be. Joseph's years of preparation were not wasted time. They were essential.
