### Lesson 6.1 -- Martin Harris and the 116 Pages

Once Joseph Smith had the gold plates, he needed someone to help him. Translating was a big job -- Joseph would read the words through the **Urim and Thummim** while someone else wrote them down. One of the first people to help was a local farmer named **Martin Harris**.

Martin was older than Joseph and well-respected in the community. He believed in Joseph's work and even gave him money to help with expenses. Martin served as Joseph's **scribe**, writing down the words as Joseph dictated the translation. Over time, they produced **116 pages** of manuscript.

But there was a problem. Martin's wife, **Lucy Harris**, was skeptical. She did not believe Joseph really had gold plates, and she was not happy about her husband spending time and money on this project. Martin wanted to prove to Lucy that the work was real. He begged Joseph to let him take the 116 translated pages home to show her.

Joseph asked the Lord through prayer if Martin could take the pages. The answer was **no**. But Martin kept pressing. Joseph asked **a second time**. Again, the answer was no. Martin pleaded. Joseph asked **a third time**, and this time the Lord permitted it -- but with strict conditions about who could see the manuscript.

Martin took the pages. And then the worst happened -- the **pages were lost**. Whether Lucy destroyed them or someone else took them, the 116 pages disappeared and were never recovered.

The Lord was not pleased. He **rebuked Joseph severely** for not listening the first two times. Joseph had feared man more than God. As a consequence, the **Urim and Thummim and the plates were taken away** from Joseph temporarily. He lost the gift of translation for a time. This experience is recorded in **Doctrine and Covenants sections 3 and 10**.

Joseph was heartbroken. He had made a serious mistake. But this was not the end. The Lord eventually **forgave Joseph** and returned the plates and the ability to translate. God gave Joseph a second chance -- but the lesson was clear. When God says no, He means no. We need to **listen the first time**.
