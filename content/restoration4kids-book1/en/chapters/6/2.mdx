### Lesson 6.2 -- Oliver Cowdery Arrives

After the loss of the 116 pages, Joseph Smith needed a new scribe. Martin Harris was no longer helping, and the work of translation still needed to be done. God had a plan.

In **April 1829**, a young schoolteacher named **Oliver Cowdery** showed up at Joseph's door in Harmony, Pennsylvania. Oliver had been boarding with the Smith family up in New York while teaching at a local school. He heard the family talk about Joseph, the gold plates, and the work of translation. Something stirred inside him. He prayed about it and felt strongly that he should travel to Harmony and offer his help.

Just two days after arriving, Oliver began serving as Joseph's **scribe**. The work moved forward with remarkable speed. Joseph and Oliver worked together day after day, with Joseph translating and Oliver writing. They developed a rhythm and a deep friendship.

Oliver later wrote about this experience in a letter that captures just how special those days were. He said they were **"days never to be forgotten -- to sit under the sound of a voice dictated by the inspiration of heaven"**. Imagine that. Oliver was sitting across from Joseph, listening to him speak the words of an ancient record by the power of God, and writing every word down. He knew he was part of something extraordinary.

Oliver was not just any helper. He was the **right person at the right time**. He was educated, he could write quickly and clearly, and he had a strong testimony of the work. God had prepared him for this role just as He had prepared Joseph.

The arrival of Oliver Cowdery was a turning point in the translation of the **Book of Mormon**. Before Oliver came, the work had stalled. After he arrived, things moved quickly. In fact, the bulk of the entire Book of Mormon was translated with Oliver as scribe.

This teaches us something about how God works. He puts the **right people in our path** at the right time. Oliver did not stumble into this by accident. God guided him to Joseph. And when we are trying to do good things, God will send people to help us too.
