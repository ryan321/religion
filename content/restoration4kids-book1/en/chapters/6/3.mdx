### Lesson 6.3 -- The Translation

How exactly did Joseph Smith translate the Book of Mormon? He did it **by the gift and power of God**. That is the short answer, and it is the most important one.

Joseph used the **Urim and Thummim** -- the special stones that were buried with the plates -- to translate the ancient writing. He also used a **seer stone** at times. Both of these instruments helped him receive the translation by revelation. The words came not from Joseph's own knowledge but from God.

Here is one of the most amazing facts about the Book of Mormon translation. The bulk of it was completed in roughly **65 working days**. That is just over two months of actual translating time. In that short period, Joseph and Oliver produced over **500 pages** of text. Think about that. Five hundred pages of detailed history, complex stories, sermons, prophecies, poetry, and doctrine -- all in about two months.

Joseph Smith had very **limited formal education**. He had attended school only briefly and was not a trained writer or scholar. He had no library of reference books. He did not go back and revise draft after draft. Witnesses who were present said Joseph would start each day's work **right where he left off** the day before, without reviewing what had already been translated. He simply picked up and kept going.

The Book of Mormon contains hundreds of **names, places, and dates**. It has complex storylines that weave together across centuries. It quotes and echoes the Bible in sophisticated ways. It contains detailed descriptions of **warfare, government, religious practices**, and family life. And it was produced by a young man in his early twenties with almost no education, in about two months.

Many people who have studied the translation process have concluded that it would be **essentially impossible** for any person to produce this book on their own in such a short time. The translation itself is powerful **evidence of divine assistance**. God helped Joseph do something that no ordinary human could have done alone.

The translation of the Book of Mormon is one of the great miracles of the Restoration. It shows what is possible when God gives someone a task and provides the power to accomplish it.
