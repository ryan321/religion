### Lesson 6.4 -- Publication

The translation was finished, but the Book of Mormon still needed to be **printed and published** so people could actually read it. That was not a simple task in 1830.

Joseph Smith found a printer named **E.B. Grandin** in **Palmyra, New York**, who agreed to print the book. But printing was expensive. Five thousand copies would cost **$3,000** -- an enormous sum of money at that time. Joseph did not have it.

Once again, **Martin Harris** stepped forward. Despite the painful lesson of the 116 lost pages, Martin still believed in the Book of Mormon. He **mortgaged his farm** to guarantee the printing costs. That took real sacrifice and real faith. Martin was putting his property -- his livelihood -- on the line for this book.

The printing process took several months. The book was finally published in **March 1830**. Those first **5,000 copies** of the Book of Mormon went out into the world, and the book has never been out of print since. Today it has been translated into over 100 languages and millions upon millions of copies have been distributed.

Before the book was published, something else important happened. God wanted people to know the gold plates were real, so He provided **witnesses**. The **Three Witnesses** -- **Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, and Martin Harris** -- were shown the plates by an angel. They saw the plates with their own eyes and heard the voice of God testifying that the translation was correct. Their testimony is printed in the front of every copy of the Book of Mormon you have ever seen.

In addition, **Eight Witnesses** were allowed to **see and handle** the plates physically. They held them, turned the pages, and examined the engravings. They were not shown the plates by an angel -- they handled them in a normal, everyday setting. Their testimony is also printed in every copy of the Book of Mormon.

None of the Three Witnesses or Eight Witnesses ever **denied their testimony**, even those who later left the Church. They always maintained that they had seen and handled the gold plates. That is powerful evidence.

The Book of Mormon was now in the hands of the people. The Restoration was moving forward. Just eleven days after the book was published, the **Church of Jesus Christ** would be officially organized. A new chapter in God's work on earth was about to begin.
