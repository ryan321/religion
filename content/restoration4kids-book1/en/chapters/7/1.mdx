### Lesson 7.1 -- Why Priesthood Matters

Have you ever tried to do something important but didn't have permission? Maybe you wanted to sign a document, but only an adult with the right authority could do it. It wouldn't matter how badly you wanted to sign it -- without the proper authority, the signature wouldn't count.

That is exactly how **priesthood** works. The priesthood is the **authority to act in God's name**. Without it, a person can pray, read scriptures, and try to follow Jesus. But they cannot perform **ordinances** -- like baptism or confirmation -- in a way that God recognizes. A good person baptizing someone without priesthood authority is like someone signing a contract they have no legal right to sign. The action happens, but it doesn't count.

This is why the **Great Apostasy** created such a serious problem. When the original Apostles were killed and the full truth was lost, the priesthood authority was taken from the earth (see Chapter 5). That means for hundreds of years, no one on earth had the right to baptize, confirm, ordain, or perform any ordinance in God's name. Churches existed. Good people prayed and studied the Bible. But the **authority** was gone.

Here is the important part: priesthood authority cannot be **claimed** or **invented**. You can't just decide you have it. You can't study your way into it. You can't vote on it. It has to be **given to you by someone who already holds it**, and that person has to have the right to pass it on. It is a chain of authority that goes all the way back to God.

So think about the problem Joseph Smith faced. By 1829, he and Oliver Cowdery were translating the Book of Mormon and learning about baptism and the need for authority. They wanted to be baptized, but who could do it? No one on earth had the priesthood. No minister, no priest, no bishop in any church could give them what had been lost.

The only solution was for **heavenly messengers** to bring it back. God would have to send someone who held the priesthood -- someone who had received it directly -- to restore it to the earth. That is exactly what happened, and it is one of the most important events in the Restoration.

The priesthood is not just a nice idea. It is the foundation of everything the Church does. Every baptism, every blessing, every temple ordinance depends on it. Without it, there is no Church. With it, God's work moves forward.
