### Lesson 7.2 -- John the Baptist and the Aaronic Priesthood

It was May 15, 1829. Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery had been working hard on the translation of the Book of Mormon. As they translated passages about baptism, a question came up that they couldn't answer on their own: Who has the authority to baptize? They decided to do what Joseph had learned to do -- they went to pray.

The two of them walked to a quiet spot near the **Susquehanna River** in Pennsylvania and knelt down. What happened next changed the world.

A **heavenly messenger** appeared to them. He was a **resurrected being** surrounded by light. He identified himself as **John the Baptist** -- the same John who had baptized Jesus Christ in the Jordan River nearly 1,800 years earlier. He had been sent by the Apostles Peter, James, and John, who held a higher authority.

John the Baptist placed his hands on Joseph's and Oliver's heads and spoke these words: **"Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah, I confer the Priesthood of Aaron, which holds the keys of the ministering of angels, and of the gospel of repentance, and of baptism by immersion for the remission of sins"** (D&C 13:1).

This was the **Aaronic Priesthood** -- the same priesthood that Aaron, the brother of Moses, had held in ancient Israel. It is sometimes called the **lesser priesthood**, not because it is unimportant, but because there is a higher priesthood with additional authority. The Aaronic Priesthood gives the authority to **baptize**, to administer the **sacrament**, and to perform other important duties.

After receiving this priesthood, John the Baptist instructed Joseph and Oliver to **baptize each other** in the Susquehanna River. Joseph baptized Oliver first, and then Oliver baptized Joseph. They also **ordained each other** to the Aaronic Priesthood as John directed.

Both Joseph and Oliver described feeling the **Holy Ghost** powerfully after their baptism. Scriptures they had been studying suddenly became clear. They understood prophecies that had been confusing before. The priesthood was already at work.

Think about what had just happened. For the first time in centuries, two men on earth held genuine priesthood authority given by a heavenly messenger. The chain of authority that had been broken during the Apostasy was being restored -- link by link.
