### Lesson 7.3 -- Peter, James, and John and the Melchizedek Priesthood

Shortly after John the Baptist restored the Aaronic Priesthood, another heavenly visit took place. This time, three messengers appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery: **Peter, James, and John** -- three of the original Apostles chosen by Jesus Christ.

These were not just any Apostles. These were the **same three** who had been with Jesus at some of the most sacred moments in His ministry. They were on the **Mount of Transfiguration** when Jesus appeared in glory (Matthew 17:1-3). They were with Him in the **Garden of Gethsemane** on the night before His crucifixion (Mark 14:33). Jesus had trusted them with the most important events, and now they were trusted to bring back the highest authority.

Peter, James, and John laid their hands on Joseph and Oliver and conferred the **Melchizedek Priesthood**. This is sometimes called the **higher priesthood**. The name comes from **Melchizedek**, a great high priest in the Old Testament who was so righteous that the priesthood was named after him (see Hebrews 7).

The Melchizedek Priesthood carries authority that the Aaronic Priesthood does not. It includes the power to **confirm** people as members of the Church, to bestow the **gift of the Holy Ghost**, to **ordain** others to the priesthood, to give **blessings of healing and comfort**, and to **lead the Church**. Without it, the Church could not be fully organized.

Now both priesthoods were on the earth again. The **Aaronic Priesthood**, restored by John the Baptist, gave authority for baptism and the sacrament. The **Melchizedek Priesthood**, restored by Peter, James, and John, gave authority for everything else -- confirmation, ordinations, temple ordinances, and the leadership of the Church.

This is an important pattern in the Restoration. God did not restore everything at once. He did it **step by step**. First the Aaronic Priesthood, then the Melchizedek Priesthood. Each piece built on the one before it.

With both priesthoods restored, Joseph and Oliver now had the full authority they needed. They could baptize, confirm, ordain, and eventually organize the Church of Jesus Christ. The foundation had been laid. The authority that had been missing for centuries was back on earth -- not because men had claimed it, but because God had sent it through His authorized servants.
