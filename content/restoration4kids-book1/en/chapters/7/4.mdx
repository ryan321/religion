### Lesson 7.4 -- What This Means for You

The restoration of the priesthood is not just a story from history. It is something that affects your life right now.

Think about the day you were **baptized**. The person who baptized you held the **Aaronic Priesthood** -- the very same priesthood that John the Baptist restored to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery near the Susquehanna River in 1829. The prayer that was said, the authority that was used, and the ordinance itself all trace directly back to that moment when a resurrected messenger from heaven placed his hands on two young men and said, "Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah, I confer the Priesthood of Aaron" (D&C 13:1).

Now think about your **confirmation**. After your baptism, you were confirmed a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and given the **gift of the Holy Ghost**. The men who placed their hands on your head held the **Melchizedek Priesthood** -- the same priesthood that Peter, James, and John brought back to the earth. Your confirmation is connected to three of Jesus Christ's original Apostles.

Every **sacrament** you take on Sunday is administered by the Aaronic Priesthood. Every **blessing** you receive -- whether a father's blessing before school starts, a blessing when you are sick, or a blessing of comfort during a hard time -- comes through the Melchizedek Priesthood. Every person who is **set apart** for a calling in the Church receives that setting apart through the same authority.

You can actually trace a line from any priesthood ordinance today all the way back to that riverbank in Pennsylvania. The man who baptized you was ordained by someone, who was ordained by someone, who was ordained by someone -- and that chain goes back to Joseph and Oliver, who received it from heavenly messengers, who received it from Jesus Christ Himself.

This is what Latter-day Saints mean when they say the Church has **priesthood authority**. It is not a feeling or a tradition. It is a real, traceable line of authority that connects you to Jesus Christ through His servants.

The next time you take the sacrament, remember where that authority comes from. The next time someone gives you a blessing, remember who restored that power to the earth. The priesthood is not just something that happened long ago. It is something that is working in your life today -- every single week.
