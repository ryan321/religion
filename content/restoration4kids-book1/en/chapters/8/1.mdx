### Lesson 8.1 -- April 6, 1830

For years, God had been preparing the way. Joseph Smith had received the gold plates, translated the Book of Mormon, and received the priesthood. Now it was time for the next step, the one everything had been building toward. It was time to **organize the Church**.

On **April 6, 1830**, a small group gathered in a **log home** in **Fayette, New York**. The home belonged to the **Whitmer family**, who had been supporters of Joseph's work. The meeting was simple and quiet. There were no huge crowds. No fancy buildings. Just a handful of believers who knew something important was about to happen.

New York state law at the time required a minimum of **six members** to officially organize a religious society. Exactly six people served as the founding members that day: Joseph Smith, Oliver Cowdery, Hyrum Smith, Peter Whitmer Jr., Samuel H. Smith, and David Whitmer. Others were present as well, but these six signed the legal documents.

Joseph Smith was **sustained** by the group as the **first elder** of the Church and as a **prophet, seer, and revelator**. Oliver Cowdery was sustained as the second elder. This wasn't Joseph choosing a title for himself. The members voted to accept him in the role God had called him to fill.

The **sacrament** was administered for the first time in the newly organized Church. Bread and wine were blessed and passed, just as Jesus had done with His Apostles at the Last Supper. Members who had already been baptized were **confirmed** as members and received the **gift of the Holy Ghost** through the Melchizedek Priesthood.

Think about how small this beginning was. Six official members in a log cabin. No money. No property. No influence. And yet Latter-day Saints believe this was the moment the **Church of Jesus Christ** was back on the earth, the same Church that Jesus had established during His ministry, now restored with proper authority and organization.

The name of the Church would later be given by revelation: **The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints** (D&C 115:4). But on that April day, the important thing was that it existed. The priesthood had been restored. The Book of Mormon was published. And now there was an organized body of believers, led by a prophet, authorized by God. The Restoration was no longer just preparation. It was real.
