### Lesson 8.2 -- Early Converts

Once the Church was organized, it began to grow, and it grew fast. The message of the Restoration was powerful, and people who were searching for truth recognized it.

Some of the earliest members were from **Joseph Smith's own family**. His parents, Joseph Sr. and Lucy Mack Smith, believed in his calling and were baptized. His brothers Hyrum and Samuel were faithful from the beginning. Having your own family support you mattered. Joseph didn't have to convince strangers first. The people who knew him best believed he was telling the truth.

One of the most important early converts was **Parley P. Pratt**. Parley was a preacher who had been searching for the true Church of Christ. One day, he came across a copy of the **Book of Mormon**. He started reading and could not stop. He later wrote that he read it day and night, that it gave him something no other book had ever given him. He was baptized almost immediately and became one of the greatest **missionaries** in the early Church. Parley would go on to travel thousands of miles, suffer imprisonment, and bring hundreds of people into the Church.

Another key convert was **Sidney Rigdon**. Sidney was a popular **minister** in Ohio with a large congregation. He was already teaching ideas that were close to what the restored gospel taught, including the need for faith, repentance, and baptism by immersion. When missionaries brought him the Book of Mormon, he studied it carefully, prayed about it, and became convinced it was true. He was baptized, and a large portion of his **congregation** followed him into the Church.

Within the first year, **hundreds** of people joined. That is remarkable when you think about it. There was no advertising. No social media. No money for campaigns. Just the Book of Mormon, the testimony of missionaries, and the power of the Holy Ghost confirming the truth to those who sought it.

These early converts gave up a lot. They left comfortable churches, lost friends, and faced mockery. But they had felt something they couldn't deny. They had read the Book of Mormon, prayed about it, and received answers. That personal witness, that testimony from the Holy Ghost, was the engine that powered the early Church.
