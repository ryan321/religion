### Lesson 8.3 -- Early Missionaries

Almost as soon as the Church was organized, missionary work began. The Restoration was not meant to be kept quiet. The Lord wanted the gospel taken to everyone who would listen.

One of the first major missions was led by **Oliver Cowdery**. In the fall of 1830, the Lord directed Oliver and a small group of missionaries to travel west to teach the gospel to **Native Americans** living near the frontier. This was a long, difficult journey on foot with very little money or supplies.

On their way west, the missionaries stopped in **Kirtland, Ohio**. This was where Sidney Rigdon had his congregation. The missionaries shared the Book of Mormon and their testimonies, and the results were astonishing. Within just a few weeks, they baptized **more than 100 people** in the Kirtland area. Entire families and neighbors joined together. It was one of the most successful missionary efforts in the early Church.

Think about how these missionaries traveled. There were no cars, no trains to most places, and no planes. They walked -- often **hundreds of miles** through forests, across rivers, and through harsh weather. They had little food, patched clothing, and no guarantee that anyone would listen to them. Many doors were slammed in their faces. People mocked them and called them fools.

But the missionaries kept going. They believed the message they carried was true, and they felt a duty to share it. When they found people who would listen, they taught simply and bore their **testimonies**. They offered copies of the Book of Mormon and invited people to read and pray about it.

Not everyone accepted the message. In fact, most people didn't. The missionaries faced **rejection** constantly. But the ones who did listen and who did pray often felt the same witness that Joseph Smith, Oliver Cowdery, and Parley P. Pratt had felt. The Holy Ghost confirmed the truth to them, and they were baptized.

This pattern -- sending missionaries out with nothing but the message and a testimony -- would become one of the defining features of the restored Church. It started in 1830 with a small group walking toward Ohio, and it has never stopped. Today, tens of thousands of missionaries serve around the world, carrying the same message those first missionaries carried: the gospel of Jesus Christ has been restored.
