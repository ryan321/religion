### Lesson 8.4 -- The Gathering Begins

As the Church grew, the Lord revealed an important principle through Joseph Smith: the Saints should **gather**. They were not meant to stay scattered in different towns and states, believing alone. They were supposed to come together, build communities, strengthen one another, and build **Zion** -- a place of unity and righteousness.

The first gathering place the Lord chose was **Kirtland, Ohio**. There were good reasons for this. The missionary work there had been incredibly successful. Over a hundred people in the Kirtland area had already been baptized. There was a ready-made community of believers waiting to receive the prophet.

In early **1831**, Joseph and Emma Smith left New York and moved to Kirtland. Other Saints from New York followed. It was not an easy move. They left behind homes, farms, and everything familiar. But they believed the Lord had spoken, and they obeyed.

Kirtland became the center of the Church. Joseph received revelations there, organized Church leadership, and began building something that would last. The Saints worked hard, built homes, planted crops, and worshiped together. For a time, things were good.

But a pattern was already forming -- one that would repeat several times in Church history. The Saints would **gather** in a place. They would **build** -- homes, communities, and eventually temples. Then **persecution** would come. Neighbors and local leaders would grow suspicious or hostile. And the Saints would be forced to **move** again.

This cycle of gathering, building, persecution, and moving would take the Saints from Kirtland to Missouri, from Missouri to Nauvoo, and eventually from Nauvoo across the plains to the Salt Lake Valley. Each time, they lost almost everything. And each time, they started over.

Why did the Lord ask them to gather? Because there is strength in numbers. Scattered believers can be picked off one by one. But when the Saints come together, they can **teach** each other, **support** each other in hard times, and build **temples** where sacred ordinances are performed. The gathering is not just about geography. It is about building the kingdom of God on earth.

Today, the gathering continues. Members all over the world gather in wards and stakes. They build temples on every continent. The principle is the same one the Lord taught in 1831: come together, strengthen one another, and build Zion wherever you are.
