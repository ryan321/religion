### Lesson 9.1 -- Revelations in Kirtland

When the Saints gathered to Kirtland, Ohio, something remarkable happened. It became a **season of learning**. The Lord poured out revelation after revelation through the Prophet Joseph Smith, teaching the young Church how to grow, how to organize, and how to live.

Many of the sections in the **Doctrine and Covenants** were received during the Kirtland period. These were not vague, general statements. They were specific **instructions** from the Lord on exactly how to run His Church. Think of it like a new employee starting a job. You need a manual, you need training, and you need someone to answer your questions. The Lord was doing that for Joseph and the Saints, one revelation at a time.

Some revelations dealt with **Church organization**. The Lord explained how the priesthood should be structured, what offices and callings were needed, and how decisions should be made. The Church was not supposed to be a loose group of believers doing whatever they wanted. It was supposed to be organized, with clear responsibilities, clear authority, and clear direction.

Other revelations focused on **missionary work**. The Lord called specific people by name and told them where to go and what to teach. These weren't suggestions. They were direct commands from God to His servants. "Go ye into all the world, preach the gospel to every creature" was not just a general idea; it was a marching order.

One of the most well-known revelations from this period was the **Word of Wisdom** (D&C 89). The Lord gave instructions about what the Saints should and should not put into their bodies. No tobacco. No alcohol. No hot drinks (understood to mean coffee and tea). Eat grains, fruits, vegetables, and meat sparingly. This wasn't just health advice; it was a commandment from God, given for the physical and spiritual well-being of His people.

The Lord also taught about the **law of consecration**, the idea that everything we have belongs to God and should be used to bless others. The Saints were asked to give their surplus to the Church to care for the poor and build up Zion.

Kirtland was like a school, and God was the teacher. Step by step, revelation by revelation, He was shaping a group of ordinary people into a functioning Church that could carry His work across the world.
