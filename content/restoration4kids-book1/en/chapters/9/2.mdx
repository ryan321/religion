### Lesson 9.2 -- The School of the Prophets

In 1833, the Lord directed Joseph Smith to establish something unusual: a **school**. But this wasn't a regular school for children. It was the **School of the Prophets**, a place where Church leaders could study, learn, and prepare for the work ahead.

The idea was simple but important. The men who would lead the Church and serve as missionaries needed to be **educated**. They needed to understand the gospel deeply. They needed to study the scriptures, learn languages, and understand history and the world around them. The Lord was not interested in having uninformed leaders. He wanted them sharp, prepared, and knowledgeable.

The school met in a small room above a store in Kirtland. Leaders gathered there to study the gospel, learn **Hebrew** and other languages, discuss doctrine, and receive instruction from Joseph Smith. The Lord Himself gave direction on what should be taught. One of the most famous scriptures from this time says, **"The glory of God is intelligence, or in other words, light and truth"** (D&C 93:36). That verse tells you everything about how God feels about learning.

Here is an interesting story from the School of the Prophets. When the men gathered for their meetings, many of them **smoked tobacco** and **chewed tobacco**. The room was small, and the smoke and mess bothered Emma Smith, who had to clean up after them. Joseph asked the Lord about it, and the answer was the **Word of Wisdom** (D&C 89), the revelation we learned about in the last lesson.

So the Word of Wisdom didn't come out of nowhere. It came because real people had a real problem, and the prophet asked God about it. That is how many revelations work. A question leads to prayer, and prayer leads to an answer from God.

The School of the Prophets shows something important about the Restoration. God cares about **education**. He wants His people to learn, not just about the gospel, but about the world. He wants them to read, study, think, and grow. This is why The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has always placed a high value on education. Brigham Young University, seminary, institute, and Church schools all trace back to this same principle: the glory of God is intelligence.

Learning is not separate from faith. It is part of it. The School of the Prophets taught the early Saints that lesson, and it still applies to you today.
