### Lesson 9.3 -- Building the Kirtland Temple

In 1833, the Lord gave a commandment that must have felt overwhelming to the small, poor community of Saints in Kirtland. He told them to **build a temple** (D&C 95). Not someday. Not when they could afford it. Now.

The Saints in Kirtland had very little. Most were farmers and laborers. They didn't have architects, professional builders, or wealthy donors. But the Lord had spoken, and they were determined to obey.

The work began, and everyone contributed. **Men** quarried stone from nearby hills and cut timber from the forests. They hauled heavy loads by hand and by oxcart. Many of them worked on the temple during the day and then worked their own farms at night just to feed their families. It was exhausting, but they did not complain -- at least not for long.

The **women** made an incredible sacrifice of their own. The exterior plaster of the temple needed something to make it strong and beautiful. The women of Kirtland donated their **fine china** -- dishes, plates, and cups that were among their most treasured possessions. The china was crushed and mixed into the plaster. When the walls dried, they **sparkled** in the sunlight. Every person who looked at the Kirtland Temple could see the sacrifice of those women built right into the walls.

It took **three years** to complete the temple. Three years of backbreaking labor, personal sacrifice, and faith. The Saints built it even as they faced poverty and persecution from their neighbors. Some people threatened to destroy the temple before it was finished. Men stood guard at night to protect the building.

The **Kirtland Temple** was the **first temple** of the Restoration. It was dedicated on March 27, 1836, in a meeting filled with the Spirit. Many members reported seeing **angels** and experiencing spiritual manifestations. Joseph Smith offered a dedicatory prayer that had been given to him by revelation (D&C 109).

This temple was not like the temples we have today -- it did not have the same ordinances like endowments and sealings as they would later be practiced. But it was the house of the Lord. It was a place where God could send heavenly messengers and restore essential priesthood **keys**. And that is exactly what happened just one week after the dedication, when the Savior Himself appeared in the temple along with Moses, Elias, and Elijah (D&C 110).

The Kirtland Temple stands as a monument to what ordinary people can do when they trust God and give everything they have.
