### Lesson 9.4 -- The Doctrine and Covenants

As Joseph Smith received revelation after revelation, a question arose: how should these revelations be preserved? They couldn't just live in people's memories. They needed to be written down, organized, and shared with the whole Church.

The first attempt to compile the revelations was the **Book of Commandments**, published in **1833**. This small book contained many of the revelations Joseph had received up to that point. But before most copies could be distributed, a mob in Missouri destroyed the printing press and scattered the pages. Some members -- including two brave young women named Mary Elizabeth Rollins and Caroline Rollins -- ran into the street and gathered as many pages as they could before the mob could destroy them all.

Two years later, in **1835**, the revelations were compiled again in a larger, more complete volume. This book was called the **Doctrine and Covenants**. It contained the earlier revelations from the Book of Commandments plus many additional ones. Over time, more sections were added as new revelations were received.

The Doctrine and Covenants is unlike any other book of scripture. The Bible contains ancient writings from prophets who lived thousands of years ago. The Book of Mormon is a record of ancient peoples in the Americas. But the Doctrine and Covenants is **modern revelation** -- God speaking directly to people in our time, in our language, about our circumstances.

When you read the Doctrine and Covenants, you are reading the actual words the Lord spoke to Joseph Smith and other early leaders. He calls people by name. He gives specific instructions. He warns, encourages, corrects, and teaches. He talks about how to organize the Church, how to live, how to handle money, how to treat the poor, how to prepare for His Second Coming, and dozens of other topics.

The Doctrine and Covenants also contains some of the most important **doctrines** of the Restoration. It teaches about the three degrees of glory (D&C 76), the nature of God, the purpose of temples, the gathering of Israel, and the plan of salvation. It includes **prophecies** about the future and **counsel** that applies to every member of the Church today.

This is one of the things that makes The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints different from other churches. Latter-day Saints believe that God did not stop speaking after the Bible was completed. He continues to speak through prophets. The Doctrine and Covenants is the proof. It is a book of God's words to His people -- not in ancient times, but in the last days.
