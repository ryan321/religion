# Epilogue

## You did it!

Great job completing The Restoration for Kids. Think about everything you've learned.

You started with the Great Apostasy -- understanding what was lost when the apostles were killed and why the gospel couldn't just be fixed from within. You watched God prepare the world over centuries: the printing press, the Reformation, religious freedom, and the founding of a nation where a boy could pray in a grove of trees without being punished for it.

You met Joseph Smith -- a farm boy with a sincere question and the courage to ask God directly. You were there in the Sacred Grove when the Father and the Son appeared. You followed Joseph through the angel Moroni's visits, the translation of the Book of Mormon, the restoration of the priesthood by John the Baptist, Peter, James, and John, and the organization of the Church on April 6, 1830.

You watched the Saints build and sacrifice in Kirtland, where the Savior Himself appeared in the temple. You followed them to Missouri, where they were driven out at gunpoint. You stood with Joseph in Liberty Jail, where some of the most powerful revelations in all of scripture were received. You walked with the Saints to Nauvoo, where they built a city and a temple. And you were there at Carthage, when Joseph and Hyrum sealed their testimonies with their lives.

You saw the Church survive. You saw Brigham Young take up the mantle. You saw the Saints finish the temple and prepare to cross a continent.

The Restoration is not just history. It is the reason your church exists. The priesthood that was restored by John the Baptist is the same priesthood that was used at your baptism. The keys that Elijah restored in the Kirtland Temple are the same keys that seal families in temples today. The Book of Mormon that Joseph translated is the same book you read at home.

You are part of this story. It didn't end in 1847 -- it continues every time a missionary teaches the gospel, every time a family is sealed in the temple, every time a young person bears testimony that Joseph Smith was a prophet of God.

Thank you for learning with us. Keep studying, keep praying, and keep building on what the early Saints sacrificed so much to give you.
