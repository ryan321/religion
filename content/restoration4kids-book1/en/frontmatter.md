## Introduction

Have you ever wondered why there needed to be a Restoration? Why couldn't someone just start a really good church and get it right?

The answer is authority. After Jesus Christ established His Church and His apostles were killed, the priesthood authority to lead the Church was lost from the earth. People could read the Bible, pray, and try to follow Jesus -- but no one had the authority to baptize, confirm, ordain, or receive revelation for the Church. The gospel had to be restored -- brought back by God Himself, through a prophet, with real priesthood authority.

That's what this book is about.

You'll start with the Great Apostasy -- what was lost and why it couldn't be fixed by human effort alone. You'll see how God spent centuries preparing the world: the printing press, the Reformation, religious freedom, and the founding of America. Then you'll meet Joseph Smith -- a fourteen-year-old farm boy who read a verse in the Bible and decided to ask God a question.

From there, the story moves fast: the First Vision, the angel Moroni, the gold plates, the translation of the Book of Mormon, the restoration of the priesthood, and the organization of the Church. You'll follow the Saints through Kirtland, Missouri, and Nauvoo -- through temples and persecution, revelation and heartbreak. And you'll be there at Carthage Jail when Joseph sealed his testimony with his life.

The story doesn't end there. Brigham Young and the Twelve Apostles led the Church forward, finished the Nauvoo Temple, and headed west. The Restoration survived -- because it was God's work, not just one man's.

This is the story of how everything you know about the Church began. It's your story.

## How to use this book

This book is designed as a workbook for self-study or guided by a parent or teacher. No expertise required -- the lessons explain everything you need to know.

The book is designed to be used over the course of a semester. Each chapter corresponds to a week, with a total of 15 weeks. Each chapter has 4 lessons which are meant to be done once a day, leaving a 5th day for review and the chapter test.

For example, if you started on Monday each week, you could do:

Monday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.1

Tuesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.2

Wednesday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.3

Thursday  Chapter 1, Lesson 1.4

Friday  Chapter 1, Review and test

Then the next Monday you would start Chapter 2, Lesson 2.1, and so on.

Each lesson has questions at the end, and each chapter has a test. Your AI tutor will help you check your answers and talk through anything you're unsure about.

After each lesson, there is a checkbox to mark when the lesson is completed to keep track of progress.

After 15 weeks (and 15 chapters) you'll understand the Restoration -- not as a list of events to memorize, but as a story you're part of.

## Note to parents

You don't need to be a Church history expert to help your child with this book. The lessons are written directly to the child in plain language. Everything is explained as it comes up.

For the best experience, we recommend going through each lesson with your child. The lessons are short -- about 15-20 minutes each. Some lessons cover difficult topics (persecution, the martyrdom) honestly but in an age-appropriate way. You may want to discuss those lessons together.

This book is written from a faithful Latter-day Saint perspective. Joseph Smith was a prophet. The First Vision happened. The Book of Mormon is true. The priesthood was restored. These are not debated in this book -- they are the foundation. The book is designed for families who are active members of the Church and want their children to understand how the Restoration happened and why it matters.

The goal is to help your child see the Restoration as their own story -- not something that happened to other people long ago, but the reason their church, their priesthood, their temples, and their covenants exist today.
