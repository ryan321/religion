# Best Subjects — Messaging Guide

## Name
Best Subjects (bestsubjects.school)

## One-liner
The subjects that matter most, taught well.

## The pitch (30 seconds)
Economics, civics, business, logic, rhetoric — these are the subjects that teach kids how to think clearly, make good decisions, and understand the world. They matter more than most of what fills a school day, but they rarely get the attention they deserve. Best Subjects teaches them in plain language, at a kid's pace, with an AI tutor built into every lesson. Read a lesson, talk it over, answer a few questions, move on when you've got it.

## Core message
These are the subjects that matter most. We wrote the best versions of them for kids, and built a tutor into every lesson.

## StoryBrand framework

The parent is the hero. Best Subjects is the guide. Everything on the site should be framed from the parent's perspective — their worry, their goal, their child's future.

### 1. Character (the hero)
A parent who wants their kid to have every advantage. They care deeply about their child's education, but they have a growing sense that what school provides — however well-intentioned — isn't enough on its own.

### 2. Problem

**External:** Their kid's education is thin on the subjects that actually matter for life — economics, civics, logic, how to think and decide. The curriculum is full, but full of what?

**Internal:** They feel uneasy. They sense something's missing but they can't teach it themselves. They're not economists or civics teachers. They feel stuck between "school should handle this" and "school clearly isn't handling this well enough."

**Philosophical:** Every kid deserves to learn the subjects that shape how they think, decide, and participate in the world. It shouldn't depend on what school they go to or whether their parents happen to have the right background.

### 3. Guide (Best Subjects)

**Empathy:** "You can tell something's missing. You're right."

**Authority:** "We wrote the books. We built the tutor. These subjects are what we do."

### 4. Plan
1. Pick a subject and start free
2. Your kid reads a short lesson and talks it over with the tutor
3. They answer questions in their own words and get real feedback
4. They build real understanding, one lesson at a time

### 5. Call to action

**Direct:** "Start a free lesson"

**Transitional:** "Browse subjects"

### 6. Failure (what happens if they don't act)
Their kid reaches adulthood without understanding how money works, how decisions compound, how to reason through an argument, or how to participate meaningfully in civic life. They're at a disadvantage — not because they weren't smart enough, but because nobody taught them.

### 7. Success
A kid who thinks clearly about decisions, understands how the world works, and has a foundation most adults wish they'd gotten. A parent who gave their child something school didn't.

## Three pillars

### 1. The best subjects
Economics, civics, business, logic, rhetoric. These are the subjects that shape how a person thinks, decides, and participates in the world. They matter more than most of what fills a school day. When they are taught, they're often buried in jargon or saved for college. We think kids can learn them now.

### 2. Taught well
No jargon. No charts. No math beyond counting. Each lesson is 15–20 minutes of plain language and examples from a kid's real world — allowance, snacks, recess, video games. This isn't dumbed down; it's cleared up. Simple as possible, but not simpler.

### 3. An AI tutor for every lesson
Every lesson comes with a tutor that knows the material, knows your child, and never loses patience. Ask it anything. It won't give away answers, but it will explain things ten different ways until it clicks. Your child's work is graded with real feedback, not just right/wrong.

## Voice principles
- **Direct.** Say what it is. "Kids already do economics every day — they just don't call it that."
- **Confident, not salesy.** The content speaks for itself. Claim quality plainly, like "Great Courses" does.
- **Warm but not cute.** We respect kids as thinkers. No cartoon language, no dumbing down.
- **Parent-honest.** "You don't need to know anything about economics to help your child with this book."
- **Hero-first.** Lead with the parent's concern, not our product. We show up as the guide, not the protagonist.

## Audience

### Primary: Parents
Parents of kids 7–12 who want their children to have every advantage. They look at what school provides and sense that something's missing — the practical, foundational subjects that shape how a person thinks and decides. They may not be able to articulate exactly what's lacking, but they feel it. They want something their kid can do somewhat independently, because they can't teach these subjects themselves.

**What they need to hear:** You're right that something's missing. You don't have to teach it yourself. We built this so your kid gets the foundation that most people never get.

### Secondary: The kids themselves
Curious kids who are ready to understand more about how the world works. They don't want to be talked down to. They want it to make sense.

**What they need to hear:** You're going to understand this. It's not as hard as people make it seem.

## Objection handling

**"My kid is too young for economics."**
Your kid already makes economic decisions every day — what to spend time on, whether to trade snacks, how to save allowance. This book just gives them the words for what they're already doing.

**"Can't they just learn this in school later?"**
Some schools teach these subjects, but often late and poorly — buried in jargon, or crammed into a single elective. Starting early, in plain language, builds intuition that lasts. And many kids never get the chance at all.

**"Is the AI safe / appropriate for kids?"**
The tutor only discusses lesson content. It's designed to explain and encourage, not to give answers. It remembers your child's interests to make examples relevant, but it stays within the material.

**"Is this just worksheets with ChatGPT?"**
No. Every lesson is written from scratch — real curriculum, not generated content. The AI tutor is built specifically for each lesson, with the full lesson context and a pedagogical approach designed for this age group. It's a tutor, not a chatbot.
