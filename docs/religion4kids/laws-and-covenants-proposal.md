# Laws and Covenants for Kids -- Book Proposal (Revised)

## Why This Book

Latter-day Saint kids hear about covenants constantly -- in baptism, the sacrament, the temple, general conference. They hear about the Law of Moses, the Abrahamic covenant, the baptismal covenant, the Word of Wisdom, the law of tithing, the oath and covenant of the priesthood, the law of chastity, the new and everlasting covenant, and the law of the fast. But they often can't distinguish between them, explain what each one is, or say when and why it was given.

This book covers every major law and covenant in the gospel in one volume -- some in full chapters, some grouped together -- so kids walk away with a clear, complete picture.

## Format

- 15 chapters, 4 lessons + 1 test per chapter
- Each lesson: ~300-500 words, conversational second-person prose
- Bold key terms, scripture references
- 3 questions per lesson, 4 per test
- Target age: 7-12

## Laws and Covenants for Kids

**Subtitle:** What God Promises and What He Asks

### Chapter 1: What Are Laws and Covenants?
- 1.1 -- What Is a Law
- 1.2 -- What Is a Covenant
- 1.3 -- How Laws and Covenants Work Together
- 1.4 -- Why God Uses Laws and Covenants

### Chapter 2: The Covenants with Adam and Noah
- 2.1 -- Adam and Eve: The Fall and the First Covenant (Garden, Fall, law of sacrifice, Moses 5:5-7)
- 2.2 -- What Adam Was Taught (fulness of gospel from the beginning, baptism, priesthood, Moses 5-6)
- 2.3 -- The Covenant with Noah (flood, new beginning, promise never destroy by flood, rainbow, Gen 9:11)
- 2.4 -- What These First Covenants Teach (pattern: God teaches, asks obedience, promises blessings; covenant of preservation; broadest covenant)

### Chapter 3: The Abrahamic Covenant
- 3.1 -- God Calls Abraham (Ur, sought priesthood, called to leave, Abraham 1:1-4)
- 3.2 -- The Four Promises (land, posterity, priesthood, gospel to all nations, Gen 12:1-3, Abraham 2:9-11)
- 3.3 -- Passed to Isaac, Jacob, and the Twelve Tribes
- 3.4 -- You Are Part of This Covenant (baptism enters you in, patriarchal blessing, literal or adopted)

### Chapter 4: The Covenant at Sinai and the Law of Moses
- 4.1 -- Israel at Mount Sinai (Exodus 19, national covenant, "kingdom of priests")
- 4.2 -- The Ten Commandments (Exodus 20, first four = God, last six = people)
- 4.3 -- Sacrifices, Festivals, and the Sabbath (burnt offerings, Passover, Day of Atonement, Sabbath as sign)
- 4.4 -- The Law as a Schoolmaster (higher law offered first, golden calf, lesser law given, Galatians 3:24, every element pointed to Christ)

### Chapter 5: The Davidic Covenant and the Prophets
- 5.1 -- The Covenant with David (throne forever, 2 Samuel 7:12-16, fulfilled in Christ, Luke 1:32-33)
- 5.2 -- Prophets as Covenant Enforcers (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel -- reminded people of promises)
- 5.3 -- The New Covenant Promised (Jeremiah 31:31-33, written on hearts, through the Messiah)
- 5.4 -- How Old Testament Covenants Connect (Adam through David, each builds, all point to Christ)

### Chapter 6: The New and Everlasting Covenant
- 6.1 -- What the New and Everlasting Covenant Is (D&C 66:2, the fulness of the gospel, "new" because restored, "everlasting" because from the beginning)
- 6.2 -- Christ Fulfills the Law of Moses (Matt 5:17, ritual laws end, moral laws deepened)
- 6.3 -- The Higher Law (heart over behavior, Matt 5:21-48, "be ye therefore perfect")
- 6.4 -- The Atonement: The Heart of Every Covenant (Gethsemane/cross, Alma 7:11-12, the covenant act that makes all other covenants possible, broken heart and contrite spirit)

### Chapter 7: The Baptismal Covenant
- 7.1 -- What Baptism Is (first saving ordinance, gate, 2 Nephi 31:17, by immersion, Aaronic Priesthood)
- 7.2 -- What You Promise (Mosiah 18:8-10, bear burdens, mourn, comfort, stand as witnesses, keep commandments, take Christ's name)
- 7.3 -- What God Promises (Holy Ghost, redemption, eternal life, D&C 20:77, Mosiah 18:9)
- 7.4 -- Baptism as the Beginning (not the end, confirmation follows, everything starts here, renewed weekly)

### Chapter 8: The Sacrament Covenant
- 8.1 -- What the Sacrament Is (bread and water, Last Supper, 3 Nephi 18, most sacred weekly ordinance)
- 8.2 -- The Sacrament Prayers (D&C 20:77,79, Moroni 4-5, word for word, three promises, one guarantee)
- 8.3 -- Renewing Your Covenant (weekly reset, repent/recommit/receive Spirit, no need for rebaptism)
- 8.4 -- Making the Sacrament Matter (not routine, most important minutes of your week, what to think about)

### Chapter 9: The Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood
- 9.1 -- What the Priesthood Is (authority to act in God's name, two priesthoods: Aaronic and Melchizedek, restored by heavenly messengers)
- 9.2 -- The Oath and Covenant (D&C 84:33-44, those who receive the priesthood covenant to magnify their callings, God covenants to give them "all that my Father hath")
- 9.3 -- What It Means to Magnify a Calling (not just showing up, serving with full effort, taking it seriously, growing into the responsibility)
- 9.4 -- The Priesthood and You (young men receive Aaronic Priesthood at 12, preparing for Melchizedek, young women benefit from priesthood blessings and power, priesthood serves everyone)

### Chapter 10: The Law of Tithing and the Law of the Fast
- 10.1 -- What Tithing Is (one-tenth, D&C 119:4, Abraham/Melchizedek, temple recommend requirement)
- 10.2 -- Malachi's Promise (Malachi 3:10, prove me, windows of heaven, tithing teaches trust)
- 10.3 -- The Law of the Fast (going without food/water for two meals, fasting with prayer, donating fast offerings to the poor, Isaiah 58:6-11)
- 10.4 -- What Fasting and Tithing Teach (both are about sacrifice and trust, giving up something physical to gain something spiritual, putting God and others before yourself)

### Chapter 11: The Word of Wisdom and the Law of Chastity
- 11.1 -- The Word of Wisdom (D&C 89, 1833 School of Prophets, prohibitions and encouragements, health code from God)
- 11.2 -- The Promises of the Word of Wisdom (D&C 89:18-21, health, wisdom, protection, ahead of science)
- 11.3 -- The Law of Chastity (sexual purity within marriage only, power of creation is sacred, protects families)
- 11.4 -- Living These Laws (both are about respecting the body God gave you, preparing now for a life of covenant keeping, world's standards vs. God's standards)

### Chapter 12: Temple Covenants
- 12.1 -- What Happens in the Temple (house of the Lord, endowment, sealings, highest covenants)
- 12.2 -- The Covenants of the Endowment (law of obedience, law of sacrifice, law of the gospel, law of chastity, law of consecration -- five covenants, each explained briefly)
- 12.3 -- The Law of Consecration (dedicating time/talents/everything to God's kingdom, highest economic/spiritual law, how we live it today)
- 12.4 -- Ordinances and Covenants: What's the Difference (ordinance = physical act performed by priesthood authority, covenant = the promise made during the ordinance, you can have an ordinance without a covenant if your heart isn't in it)

### Chapter 13: Sealing and Celestial Marriage
- 13.1 -- The Sealing Power (restored by Elijah at Kirtland Temple, D&C 110:13-16, Malachi 4:5-6, binds on earth and heaven)
- 13.2 -- Celestial Marriage (D&C 131:1-4, marriage in the temple is required for exaltation, "new and everlasting covenant of marriage," not just "till death do you part")
- 13.3 -- Families Are Forever (children sealed to parents, temple work for the dead, no one left out, the purpose of it all)
- 13.4 -- Why Sealing Is the Culmination (every covenant leads here, every ordinance points here, the whole plan is about bringing families home to God together)

### Chapter 14: When Covenants Are Broken
- 14.1 -- What Happens When You Break a Covenant (the Spirit withdraws, blessings are suspended, not destroyed -- but on pause until you repent, God doesn't give up on you)
- 14.2 -- Repentance: The Way Back (the Atonement covers covenant-breaking, repentance is real and available, the sacrament is the weekly mechanism for renewal, bishop's role for serious sins)
- 14.3 -- Dispensations: Covenants Lost and Restored (the pattern: God gives covenants, people break them, apostasy, God restores through a new prophet, Adam/Noah/Abraham/Moses/Christ/Joseph Smith)
- 14.4 -- God Never Breaks His Covenant (you might break yours, but God never breaks His, D&C 82:10, He is always willing to receive you back, the prodigal son, His arms are always open)

### Chapter 15: The Covenant Path
- 15.1 -- One Path, Many Covenants (the sequence: faith, repentance, baptism, Holy Ghost, priesthood, endowment, sealing, each builds on the last)
- 15.2 -- The Gathering of Israel (Abrahamic promise fulfilled, every missionary/baptism/sealing is gathering, you are part of it)
- 15.3 -- All the Laws and Covenants Together (quick review: what you've learned, how they connect, the big picture)
- 15.4 -- Your Place on the Path (where you are now, what's ahead, not a checklist but a way of life, leads to eternal life D&C 14:7)
