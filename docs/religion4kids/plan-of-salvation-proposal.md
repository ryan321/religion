# The Plan of Salvation for Kids -- Book Proposal

## Why This Book

The plan of salvation is THE central doctrine of the Restoration. Everything else -- priesthood, covenants, temples, the Atonement, the Restoration itself -- exists because of the plan. Yet most kids (and many adults) know only the basics: premortal life, earth life, afterlife. This book covers the full plan in detail -- from intelligences to the celestial earth, with scripture references throughout.

## Format

- 15 chapters, 4 lessons + 1 test per chapter
- Each lesson: ~300-500 words, conversational second-person prose
- Bold key terms, scripture references in EVERY lesson
- 3 questions per lesson, 4 per test
- Target age: 7-12, LDS perspective, matter-of-fact

## The Plan of Salvation for Kids

**Subtitle:** Where You Came From, Why You're Here, and Where You're Going

### Chapter 1: Why the Plan Was Needed

- 1.1 -- What Is the Plan of Salvation?
  - God has a plan for His children. It's called the plan of salvation, the plan of happiness, or the great plan of mercy (Alma 42:5, 8, 15). It explains where you came from, why you're here, and where you're going. It is the most important thing you will ever learn because it answers the biggest questions anyone can ask: Who am I? Why am I here? What happens when I die? Every doctrine, every ordinance, and every covenant in the gospel exists because of this plan.

- 1.2 -- Intelligences
  - Before you were a spirit child of God, you existed as an intelligence. Abraham saw in vision that God stood among the intelligences "before the world was" and said, "These I will make my rulers" (Abraham 3:22-23). The Doctrine and Covenants teaches that "intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created, neither indeed can be" (D&C 93:29). Something about you is eternal -- it has always existed. God organized intelligences into spirit beings. You have always existed in some form, and you always will.

- 1.3 -- It's All About Progression
  - The whole point of the plan is progression -- becoming more like God. You started as an intelligence. You became a spirit child of God. You came to earth to receive a physical body and be tested. If you are faithful, you will be resurrected with a glorified body and can eventually become like Heavenly Father. The Prophet Joseph Smith taught: "You have got to learn how to be gods yourselves...the same as all gods have done before you" (King Follett Discourse). The plan is a roadmap for eternal progression.

- 1.4 -- God's Work and Glory
  - Why does God do all this? Moses 1:39 gives the answer: "For behold, this is my work and my glory -- to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man." God's purpose is YOUR progression. He doesn't need anything from you. He is already exalted. But His greatest joy is helping His children become what He is. The plan of salvation is God's plan to make you like Him. Everything -- the creation, the fall, the Atonement, the covenants, the temple, the resurrection -- serves that purpose.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 2: The Premortal Life

- 2.1 -- You Lived Before You Were Born
  - Before you were born on earth, you lived as a spirit child of Heavenly Father in a premortal existence. You had a spirit body, a personality, and agency. You knew God. You knew Jesus Christ. You knew the other spirits who would become the people on earth. Jeremiah 1:5: "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee; and before thou camest forth out of the womb I sanctified thee." God knew you before you were born because you lived with Him.

- 2.2 -- Spirit Children of God
  - You are a literal spirit child of Heavenly Father. "We are the offspring of God" (Acts 17:29). "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God" (Romans 8:16). This is not a metaphor. God is your Father in the most real sense. You inherited the potential to become like Him, the way a child inherits the potential to become like a parent. Your divine nature -- your capacity to grow, learn, love, and create -- comes from Him.

- 2.3 -- Agency in the Premortal Life
  - Even in the premortal life, you had agency -- the ability to choose. Some spirits were more diligent and faithful than others (Abraham 3:22-23). Some were foreordained to specific missions on earth -- prophets, leaders, and servants of God. But no one was forced. Agency has always been the foundation of God's plan. You chose to follow God's plan. You chose to come to earth. That choice was yours.

- 2.4 -- Why We Couldn't Stay
  - If the premortal life was good, why leave? Because you couldn't progress further without a physical body and the experiences of mortality. You needed to be tested, to learn to walk by faith, to experience pain and joy, to make choices with real consequences. Staying in the premortal world would have been comfortable, but it would have meant no growth. The plan required leaving home -- like a child leaving for school. It's hard, but it's necessary.

### Chapter 3: The Council in Heaven

- 3.1 -- The Grand Council
  - Before the earth was created, Heavenly Father gathered His spirit children in a great council (Abraham 3:22-28). He presented His plan: His children would go to earth, receive physical bodies, be tested, and have the opportunity to return to His presence and become like Him. But there was a problem. Mortality would involve sin -- and no unclean thing can dwell in God's presence (1 Nephi 10:21). A Savior would be needed to pay the price for sin.

- 3.2 -- The Need for a Savior
  - God's plan required a Savior -- someone who would live a perfect life, take upon Himself the sins of every person, and make it possible for the rest of us to repent and be forgiven. Without a Savior, the plan could not work. Justice demanded a penalty for sin. Someone had to pay that penalty, or none of us could return to God. The question at the council was: Who would be the Savior?

- 3.3 -- Two Volunteers
  - Two beings volunteered. Jesus Christ, the Firstborn of the Father, said, "Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever" (Moses 4:2). He would follow the Father's plan, preserve agency, and give all glory to God. Lucifer also volunteered but said, "I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor" (Moses 4:1). Lucifer's plan would destroy agency -- forcing everyone to be "saved" -- and he wanted God's glory for himself.

- 3.4 -- The Father's Choice
  - Heavenly Father chose Jesus Christ. "This is my Beloved Son...hear him" (Abraham 3:27, Moses 4:2). Jesus was chosen because His plan preserved agency -- the foundation of all progression. Without the ability to choose, there is no growth, no testing, no genuine righteousness. Lucifer's plan was a counterfeit -- it promised safety but eliminated the very thing that makes salvation meaningful. God chose the plan that allowed His children to become like Him, not the plan that would keep them as they were.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 4: The War in Heaven

- 4.1 -- Lucifer's Rebellion
  - Lucifer was angry. He rejected the Father's decision. Revelation 12:7-9 describes what happened next: "There was war in heaven: Michael and his angels fought against the dragon; and the dragon fought and his angels." Michael (Adam) led the faithful spirits. Lucifer (the dragon) led the rebellious ones. This was not a war with swords -- it was a war of influence, testimony, and choice. Every spirit had to decide which side to support.

- 4.2 -- What Lucifer's Plan Really Involved
  - Lucifer's plan was not just an alternative. It was a fundamental rejection of God's plan. He wanted to destroy agency -- to force everyone to obey (Moses 4:3, 2 Nephi 2:27). He wanted God's glory for himself. And he wanted to overthrow God: "He sought to destroy the agency of man, which I, the Lord God, had given him, and also, that I should give unto him mine own power" (Moses 4:3). Lucifer's plan was rebellion disguised as compassion. "No one will be lost" sounds nice until you realize it means no one will be free.

- 4.3 -- One-Third Cast Out
  - Lucifer and one-third of the host of heaven were cast out (D&C 29:36, Revelation 12:4). They were denied physical bodies. They became Satan and his angels -- devils who would tempt and oppose God's children throughout earth's history. They chose to reject the plan and lost everything. They can never progress. They can never have bodies. They can never return to God. Their war against the plan continues, but their ultimate defeat is certain.

- 4.4 -- You Chose Right
  - You are here on earth. That means you chose the right side. You supported the Father's plan. You chose Jesus Christ as your Savior. You chose agency even though it meant risk -- the risk of sin, suffering, and failure. You chose growth over safety. You are not here by accident. You are here because you made a courageous choice before you were born. And the plan you chose is the plan you are living right now.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 5: The Creation

- 5.1 -- Why the Earth Was Created
  - The earth was created as part of the plan of salvation. It is the place where God's children would receive physical bodies, be tested, and have the opportunity to progress (Abraham 3:24-25). "We will go down, for there is space there, and we will take of these materials, and we will make an earth whereon these may dwell." The creation was not random. It was purposeful -- designed specifically to support the plan. Every mountain, ocean, and creature exists because God made a place for you.

- 5.2 -- The Need for Physical Bodies
  - One of the main reasons you came to earth was to receive a physical body. In the premortal life, you were a spirit. But the scriptures teach that "spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy; and when separated, man cannot receive a fulness of joy" (D&C 93:33-34). A body is not a burden -- it is a gift. God has a glorified body of flesh and bones (D&C 130:22). To become like Him, you need one too. Satan and his followers were denied bodies. Having a body is a blessing they will never receive.

- 5.3 -- The Creators
  - Jesus Christ, under the direction of the Father, created the earth. "All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made" (John 1:3). He was assisted by Michael (Adam) and possibly others. The creation took place over six creative periods (not necessarily literal 24-hour days). The account is given in Genesis 1-2, Moses 2-3, and Abraham 4-5. The creation was organized -- from light to land to plants to animals to man -- each step building on the last.

- 5.4 -- Adam and Eve
  - God created Adam from the dust of the earth and breathed life into him (Genesis 2:7, Moses 3:7). Eve was created from Adam's rib -- his companion, his equal, his partner (Genesis 2:21-22, Moses 3:21-22). They were placed in the Garden of Eden, a paradise where they walked with God. They were given two commandments: multiply and replenish the earth, and do not eat of the tree of knowledge of good and evil (Moses 2:28, 3:17). The stage was set for the next essential step in the plan: the Fall.

### Chapter 6: The Fall

- 6.1 -- The Two Commandments
  - In the Garden of Eden, Adam and Eve faced two commandments that seemed to conflict: "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth" and "Of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it" (Moses 2:28, 3:17). In their innocent state, they could not have children. And they could not have children without partaking of the fruit. This was not a mistake in the plan. It was the plan. The Fall had to happen by choice, not by force.

- 6.2 -- Eve's Choice
  - Eve partook of the fruit first. Latter-day Saints do not see this as a foolish act. Eve understood that the Fall was necessary for progression. She said, "Were it not for our transgression we never should have had seed, and never should have known good and evil, and the joy of our redemption" (Moses 5:11). The Fall brought death, pain, and separation from God -- but it also brought children, growth, knowledge, and the possibility of redemption. Eve's choice was courageous.

- 6.3 -- What the Fall Changed
  - The Fall changed everything. Adam and Eve became mortal -- subject to death, pain, sickness, and temptation. They were cast out of God's presence -- spiritual death. They could now have children. They could now experience opposition in all things (2 Nephi 2:11). Without the Fall, "all things must have remained in the same state in which they were...having no joy, for they knew no misery; doing no good, for they knew no sin" (2 Nephi 2:22-23). The Fall was not a disaster. It was a doorway.

- 6.4 -- The Fall and the Plan
  - The Fall was anticipated from the beginning. "Adam fell that men might be; and men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25). God did not react to the Fall -- He planned for it. The Atonement of Jesus Christ was prepared before the world was created to overcome the effects of the Fall. Physical death would be overcome through resurrection. Spiritual death would be overcome through repentance and the Atonement. The Fall made the Savior necessary, and the Savior made the Fall survivable.

### Chapter 7: The Need for a Savior

- 7.1 -- The Problem
  - Because of the Fall, two things happened to every person born on earth: (1) physical death -- your body will die, and (2) spiritual death -- you are separated from God's presence. On top of that, every person (except Jesus) sins. "All have sinned, and come short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23). And "no unclean thing can dwell with God" (1 Nephi 10:21). So how do you get back? You can't fix the problem yourself. You can't un-die. You can't make yourself perfectly clean. You need someone to do what you cannot.

- 7.2 -- Justice Demands a Price
  - God is perfectly just. Justice means that every law has a consequence. If you break a law, a penalty must be paid. "What, do ye suppose that mercy can rob justice? I say unto you, Nay; not one whit" (Alma 42:25). Justice cannot be ignored, waived, or cheated. If no one pays the price for sin, justice is violated and God ceases to be God. The penalty for sin is real and it must be satisfied.

- 7.3 -- Mercy Cannot Rob Justice
  - Here is the beauty of the plan: mercy does not rob justice -- it satisfies it. Jesus Christ, the sinless Son of God, volunteered to pay the penalty for everyone's sins. He took the punishment that justice demanded. Because He paid, mercy can be extended to you. "The demands of justice" are met, and "God himself atoneth for the sins of the world, to bring about the plan of mercy" (Alma 42:15). Justice gets its payment. Mercy gets its pardon. And you get to come home.

- 7.4 -- Why Only Jesus Could Do It
  - No ordinary person could be the Savior. Only someone who was sinless could pay for the sins of others -- a sinner paying for sin just adds to the debt. Only someone with power over death could overcome death for everyone. Jesus was the Only Begotten Son of God in the flesh -- divine enough to have power over death, mortal enough to experience it. He was the only being in all of creation who could do what needed to be done. And He volunteered before the world was created (Moses 4:2).

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 8: Laws, Covenants, and Ordinances

- 8.1 -- Why God Gives Laws
  - Laws are how God teaches His children to progress. Every commandment is a lesson in becoming more like Him. "Thou shalt not steal" teaches integrity. "Love thy neighbour" teaches charity. The Word of Wisdom teaches self-discipline. The law of chastity teaches reverence for sacred things. Laws are not arbitrary restrictions -- they are the curriculum of the plan. "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated" (D&C 130:20). Obedience unlocks blessings.

- 8.2 -- Covenants Help You Progress
  - Covenants are the mechanism by which God binds Himself to bless you. When you make a covenant, you promise to obey, and God promises to bless. Each covenant -- baptism, the sacrament, the temple -- moves you closer to becoming like God. Covenants are not just personal. God has made covenants with nations (Israel at Sinai) and with dispensations (the Abrahamic covenant that spans all of history). The covenant path is the progression path.

- 8.3 -- Ordinances Connect Heaven and Earth
  - Ordinances are sacred physical acts performed by priesthood authority that connect you to God's power. Baptism washes you clean. Confirmation gives you the Holy Ghost. The sacrament renews your covenant. The endowment gives you knowledge and power. Sealings bind families for eternity. Each ordinance is a step in the plan -- a visible, tangible way that God reaches into your mortal life and applies His power to your progression.

- 8.4 -- Dispensations and the Plan
  - God's plan has unfolded through dispensations -- periods when He gives the gospel and priesthood to a prophet. Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Christ, Joseph Smith -- each received the plan and taught it to their generation. When people fell away, God started again. We live in the dispensation of the fulness of times (D&C 112:30) -- the final dispensation when everything has been restored and the plan will be completed. The plan is the same in every dispensation. Only the time changes.

### Chapter 9: The Savior in the Meridian of Time

- 9.1 -- Why the "Meridian"
  - Jesus Christ came to earth in what the scriptures call the "meridian of time" -- the central point of history (D&C 20:26). Everything before His coming pointed forward to Him. Everything after points back. He is the center of the plan, the center of history, and the center of the covenant. His birth in Bethlehem, His ministry in Galilee and Judea, and His death and resurrection in Jerusalem are the most important events that have ever happened or ever will happen.

- 9.2 -- The Atonement
  - In Gethsemane and on the cross, Jesus Christ accomplished the Atonement -- the central act of the entire plan. He took upon Himself "the pains and the sicknesses of his people...their infirmities...the sins of his people" (Alma 7:11-12). He suffered every pain, every sorrow, every temptation, and every sin that any person would ever experience. He bled from every pore (D&C 19:18). He died on the cross. He overcame death through His resurrection. The Atonement makes repentance possible, resurrection universal, and exaltation available.

- 9.3 -- Universal Resurrection
  - Because of the Atonement, every person who has ever lived will be resurrected -- their spirit and body reunited permanently. "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" (1 Corinthians 15:22). Resurrection is a free gift. You don't have to earn it. It comes to everyone -- the righteous and the wicked, the faithful and the faithless. Physical death, introduced by the Fall, is completely overcome by the Resurrection. This is the first great blessing of the Atonement.

- 9.4 -- What the Atonement Makes Possible
  - The second great blessing of the Atonement is that it makes repentance possible. Because Jesus paid the price for sin, you can repent and be forgiven. You can be washed clean. You can return to God's presence. But unlike resurrection, this blessing is conditional -- you must exercise faith, repent, be baptized, receive the Holy Ghost, and endure to the end (2 Nephi 31:17-20). The Atonement opens the door. You have to walk through it. But you never walk alone -- the Savior walks with you.

### Chapter 10: Being Born Again

- 10.1 -- A Change of Heart
  - The plan doesn't just require you to do good things. It requires you to become a different person. King Benjamin's people experienced this: "The Spirit of the Lord wrought a mighty change in their hearts" (Mosiah 5:2). Alma asked, "Have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?" (Alma 5:14). Being born again is not just a metaphor. It is a real spiritual transformation -- your desires change, your nature changes, and you become a new person in Christ.

- 10.2 -- Born of Water and the Spirit
  - Jesus told Nicodemus, "Except a man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God" (John 3:5). Being born of water is baptism. Being born of the Spirit is receiving the Holy Ghost. Together, these ordinances begin the process of spiritual rebirth. But it doesn't end at baptism. Being born again is a lifelong process -- daily repentance, daily reliance on the Spirit, daily choosing to follow Christ. You become more like Him over time, not all at once.

- 10.3 -- Ordinances and Covenants Help You Become Like God
  - Every ordinance in the gospel is designed to change you. Baptism washes you clean. The sacrament renews that cleanliness. The endowment teaches you and empowers you. The sealing connects you to eternal relationships. Each ordinance and covenant moves you one step closer to becoming like Heavenly Father. They are not just checkboxes -- they are transformative experiences that, when received with real intent, actually change who you are.

- 10.4 -- Joint Heirs with Christ
  - The ultimate promise of the plan is staggering: "If children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" (Romans 8:17). A joint heir inherits everything the other heir inherits. If you are faithful, you will receive "all that my Father hath" (D&C 84:38). Not some of it. All of it. God's power, His glory, His knowledge, His eternal life. You will be like Him. This is the destination of the plan -- not just being saved, but being exalted. And it is available to every person who will follow the covenant path.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 11: The Spirit World

- 11.1 -- What Happens When You Die
  - When your body dies, your spirit goes to the spirit world -- a place where all spirits wait for the resurrection. The spirit world has two conditions: paradise and prison. Paradise is for the righteous -- those who accepted the gospel and kept their covenants. Spirit prison is for those who did not know the gospel or did not accept it. Both are temporary -- everyone will eventually be resurrected and judged (Alma 40:11-14).

- 11.2 -- Spirit Paradise and Spirit Prison
  - In paradise, the righteous rest from their labors and continue to learn and serve. In prison, spirits who did not hear the gospel on earth -- or who heard it and rejected it -- have another chance. "The gospel is preached to them that are dead" (1 Peter 4:6). President Joseph F. Smith saw in vision that Jesus organized the righteous spirits to teach those in prison (D&C 138:29-37). The missionary work continues beyond death.

- 11.3 -- Everyone Gets a Chance
  - One of the most beautiful parts of the plan is that everyone gets a fair chance. Billions of people have lived and died without hearing the gospel. Are they lost? No. The gospel is taught in the spirit world. Those who accept it there can receive ordinances performed on their behalf in temples on earth. Baptism for the dead (1 Corinthians 15:29, D&C 128:15-18) ensures that no one is denied an ordinance because of when or where they lived. God's plan is perfectly fair.

- 11.4 -- Temple Work for the Dead
  - This is why Latter-day Saints do temple work -- performing baptisms, endowments, and sealings for people who have died. You are not forcing anything on them. The ordinance is performed on earth, and the person in the spirit world is free to accept or reject it. They still have agency. But the ordinance is available if they want it. Family history work -- finding the names of your ancestors -- is directly connected to the plan of salvation. You are helping to make the plan work for everyone.

### Chapter 12: The Resurrection and Degrees of Glory

- 12.1 -- The Resurrection
  - Every person who has ever lived will be resurrected -- spirit and body reunited in a perfected, immortal form. "The spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its perfect form" (Alma 11:43). The righteous are resurrected first (the first resurrection), and the wicked later. But everyone is resurrected. This is a free gift of the Atonement. Jesus Christ was the first to be resurrected, and because He rose, all will rise (1 Corinthians 15:20-22).

- 12.2 -- The Celestial Kingdom
  - The highest degree of glory is the celestial kingdom -- where God the Father and Jesus Christ dwell (D&C 76:50-70). Those who receive the celestial kingdom are those who accepted the gospel, were baptized, received the Holy Ghost, kept their covenants, and endured to the end. They will receive "all that my Father hath" (D&C 84:38). They will live with God and their families forever. This is exaltation -- the goal of the plan.

- 12.3 -- The Terrestrial and Telestial Kingdoms
  - The terrestrial kingdom is for honorable people who did not fully accept the gospel (D&C 76:71-80). They receive the presence of the Son but not the Father. The telestial kingdom is for the wicked who did not repent until after they suffered for their sins (D&C 76:81-90, 98-106). Even the telestial kingdom "surpasses all understanding" in its glory. God's plan provides a place for everyone -- but the highest blessings require the highest faithfulness.

- 12.4 -- Perdition
  - There is one group who receives no glory: the sons of perdition (D&C 76:30-38). These are people who received a perfect knowledge of the truth -- who "knew my power, and have been made partakers thereof" -- and then chose to deny it and fight against God. This is not ordinary sin. It requires a level of knowledge and deliberate rebellion that very few people will ever reach. For virtually everyone, the plan of salvation provides a place of glory. Perdition is the rare and terrible exception.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 13: The Millennium and Final Judgment

- 13.1 -- The Second Coming
  - Jesus Christ will return to the earth in glory (Acts 1:11, D&C 29:11). He will come as "King of Kings, and Lord of Lords" (Revelation 19:16). The wicked will be destroyed. The righteous will be caught up to meet Him. He will reign personally on the earth for a thousand years. This is the Second Coming -- the event the whole plan has been building toward since the council in heaven.

- 13.2 -- The Millennium
  - The Millennium is a thousand-year period of peace that follows the Second Coming (Revelation 20:1-6). Satan will be bound -- not by chains, but because the people's righteousness gives him no power (1 Nephi 22:26). There will be no war, no contention, no wickedness. Christ will reign. Temple work will continue at an extraordinary pace -- ordinances for billions of deceased people will be completed. The earth will be renewed and receive its paradisiacal glory (Articles of Faith 10).

- 13.3 -- The Loosing of Satan and the Final Battle
  - At the end of the Millennium, Satan will be "loosed for a little season" (D&C 88:111, Revelation 20:7-8). He will gather his followers for one final battle against God's people. This final rebellion will be defeated swiftly and completely. Satan and his followers will be cast out forever (Revelation 20:10). The purpose of this loosing is the final test -- even after a thousand years of peace, agency remains, and every person must choose.

- 13.4 -- The Final Judgment
  - After the final battle, every person will stand before God to be judged (Revelation 20:12-13, 2 Nephi 9:15). You will be judged by your works, your desires, and the intents of your heart. The judgment is perfectly fair -- God knows everything about you, including your circumstances, your struggles, and your sincerity. After the judgment, everyone is assigned to their degree of glory (or perdition). The earth itself will be celestialized -- transformed into a celestial kingdom for those who inherit it (D&C 88:17-20, 130:9).

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 14: Perfection Through the Savior

- 14.1 -- Commanded to Be Perfect
  - Jesus said, "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect" (Matthew 5:48). After His resurrection, He told the Nephites, "Be perfect even as I, or your Father who is in heaven is perfect" (3 Nephi 12:48 -- notice He now includes Himself, because He had been perfected through the Resurrection). Perfection is the command. But it doesn't mean you have to be perfect right now. It means that perfection is the destination, and the Savior is how you get there.

- 14.2 -- He Makes Up the Difference
  - You cannot make yourself perfect. "We know that it is by grace that we are saved, after all we can do" (2 Nephi 25:23). You do your best -- you repent, you keep covenants, you serve, you try. And the Savior makes up the difference. His grace is not a reward for perfect performance. It is the power that transforms imperfect people into perfected beings. You bring what you have. He provides what you lack. Together, you become what neither could alone.

- 14.3 -- Free and Open to Everyone
  - The plan of salvation is not reserved for a special few. "He inviteth them all to come unto him and partake of his goodness; and he denieth none that come unto him, black and white, bond and free, male and female" (2 Nephi 26:33). Every person who has ever lived -- regardless of when, where, or how they lived -- will have a chance to hear the gospel, accept it, and receive the ordinances. Through temple work, even the dead receive the opportunity. God's plan excludes no one who is willing.

- 14.4 -- Sealed as Families
  - The final, crowning element of the plan is that families can be together forever. Sealing ordinances in the temple bind husbands and wives and parents and children for eternity (D&C 132:19-20). This is why Elijah restored the sealing keys (D&C 110:13-16). This is why we do family history and temple work. The plan of salvation is not about individual salvation alone -- it's about bringing entire families home to God. "The hearts of the children shall turn to their fathers" (Malachi 4:6). That prophecy is being fulfilled right now, every time someone enters a temple.

**Test:** 4 questions.

### Chapter 15: The Plan Is Yours

- 15.1 -- Where You Came From
  - You existed as an intelligence before the world was. God organized you into a spirit being -- His literal child. You lived with Him, knew Him, and loved Him. You participated in the council in heaven, chose the Father's plan, sustained Jesus Christ as the Savior, and fought on the right side in the war in heaven. You chose to come to earth, knowing it would be hard, because you understood that progression required it. You are not here by accident. You are here on purpose.

- 15.2 -- Why You're Here
  - You are here to receive a physical body, to be tested, and to grow. You face opposition because opposition is how you develop strength. You experience joy and sorrow because both teach you. You have agency because without it you cannot truly choose good. You are surrounded by covenants, ordinances, and the guidance of the Holy Ghost because God gave you every tool you need to succeed. Your life is not random. It is part of a plan designed by a loving Father who wants you to come home.

- 15.3 -- Where You're Going
  - If you follow the covenant path -- faith, repentance, baptism, the Holy Ghost, temple covenants, enduring to the end -- you will return to God's presence with a glorified, resurrected body. You will live with your family forever. You will receive "all that my Father hath." You will be a joint heir with Christ. The plan doesn't just save you from something (sin and death). It saves you for something (eternal life and exaltation). The destination is becoming like God.

- 15.4 -- The Plan Is Yours
